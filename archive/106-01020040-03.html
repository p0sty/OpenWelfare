Staff applying for Community Language Allowance (CLA)

Managers of staff applying for Community Language Allowance (CLA)

Staff applying for Community Language Allowance (CLA)

Table 1: this table describes the process for staff self-identifying as a potential CLA recipient.

Step

Action

1

Staff member identifies as potential CLA recipient + Read more ...

Bilingual staff can initiate the CLA application process.

To meet initial eligibility, staff must:

  • speak a language other than English. This includes:
    • international languages
    • Aboriginal and/or Torres Strait Islander languages, or
    • staff assisting customers who are deaf or hard of hearing, using Auslan and other sign languages
  • be an ongoing, non-ongoing or casual employee
  • not be a contractor or labour hire staff
  • not be a contracted interpreter or translator for Services Australia

Note: staff must not assist customers in language before their CLA eligibility is approved.

Is initial eligibility met?

  • Yes, staff member must discuss their interest in receiving CLA with their Manager, go to Step 2
  • No, procedure ends here

2

Demonstrate language proficiency + Read more ...

Staff must demonstrate their language proficiency to be eligible to receive CLA.

Staff can provide their Manager with a copy of their formal language certification or recognition, such as a:

  • National Accreditation Authority for Translators and Interpreters (NAATI) certification at the certified provisional interpreter level or higher, or
  • NAATI recognition as a practicing or recognised interpreter (only where NAATI certification is not available in that language), or
  • Certificate II in Auslan from TAFE, State Deaf Society or a Services Australia approved equivalent organisation

Is language proficiency met by a formal certification or recognition?

  • Yes, go to Step 4
  • No, go to Step 3

3

CLA Proficiency test + Read more ...

If staff do not have a formal certification or recognition of their language skills, they can sit a CLA Proficiency test to demonstrate language proficiency.

The National Accreditation Authority for Translators and Interpreters (NAATI) conduct the CLA Proficiency test on behalf of Services Australia.

To sit a CLA Proficiency test, staff:

  • must request their Manager complete a Request for Community Language Allowance Proficiency test (PP015) form (see link on Resources page)
  • must review the completed PP015 and sign if correct
  • are encouraged to prepare for the test. Staff can refer to the following documents on the Resources page:
    • NAATI CLA practice material
    • NAATI Guide to the CLA practice material

NAATI will:

  • contact the staff member and Manager to arrange a test date. The test is conducted via MS Teams
  • advise if no testing is available in the requested language. If this occurs, the staff member or Manager must email Language Services as soon as possible

Did the staff member do and pass the CLA Proficiency test?

  • Yes, go to Step 4
  • No, staff may consider sitting a re-test:
    • Services Australia may pay for a re-test after three months, (maximum of two re-tests), or
    • staff can pay for a re-test, if within three months of the latest test, or
    • staff can pay for a re-test after three failed attempts
    • procedure ends here

Note: Managers are responsible for organising re-tests. Services Australia will reimburse the cost if the staff member passes the re-test.

4

Obtaining CLA approval + Read more ...

Staff must obtain relevant delegation approval (APS5 and above for direct line Managers) before using their language skills in their usual duties. See Resources page for a link to delegation approval information.

Staff must:

  • seek written approval from the appropriate delegate within 21 days of submitting their CLA application
  • obtain approval in ESSentials from the appropriate delegate

Notes:

  • Managers enter and approve CLA in ESSentials
  • Staff receive CLA from the first payday after CLA is approved in ESSentials
  • Staff receive CLA during periods of Annual Leave, Personal Leave and Long Service Leave

5

Reviewing and retaining CLA + Read more ...

To retain CLA, staff must:

  • record instances when they assist customers in their relevant language on a Record of use of a language other than English for Community Language Allowance (CLA) including Indigenous language and Auslan (PP014) form (see link on Resources page)
  • send completed PP014 to their Manager on a monthly basis
  • store completed forms as per storage protocols. See Storing scanned documents

Note: if staff assist a customer group/seminar in language, this is recorded as a single instance.

Procedure ends here.

Managers of staff applying for Community Language Allowance (CLA)

Table 2: this table describes the process for Managers identifying or assessing bilingual staff as CLA recipients.

Step

Action

1

Identifying and assessing potential CLA recipients + Read more ...

Managers of bilingual staff can initiate or assess a staff member’s CLA application.

Managers:

  • may be asked to discuss CLA eligibility with staff
  • may receive CLA applications from staff
  • are encouraged to identify potential recipients
  • can view current CLA recipients in Office Locator > Teams > Interpreter Services
  • must have suitable delegation to approve CLA: APS5 or above for direct line Managers. See Resources page for a link to delegation approval information

To meet initial eligibility, Managers must confirm the staff member:

  • speaks a language other than English. This includes:
    • international languages
    • Aboriginal and/or Torres Strait Islander languages, or
    • staff assisting customers who are deaf or hard of hearing, using Auslan and other sign languages
  • is an ongoing, non-ongoing, or casual employee
  • is not a contractor or labour hire staff
  • is not a contracted interpreter or translator for Services Australia

Does the staff member meet initial eligibility?

  • Yes, and:
    • staff member initiated the application, go to Step 2
    • Manager initiated the application, go to Step 3
  • No, and:
    • staff member initiated the application. Discuss reasons why eligibility was not met and document in writing within 21 days of receiving their application. Procedure ends here
    • Manager initiated the application, procedure ends here

2

Assessing ongoing business need for the requested language skills + Read more ...

It is the Manager’s responsibility to assess if there is an ongoing business need for the staff member’s language skills.

Before approving CLA, Managers must consider a range of factors including local business needs.

Managers are encouraged to refer to local resources and reports.

Is there a clear, ongoing business need for the staff member’s language skills?

  • Yes, go to Step 3
  • No, discuss reasons with staff member and document in writing to them within 21 days of receiving CLA application. Procedure ends here

3

Confirming staff member’s language proficiency + Read more ...

Managers must confirm the staff member is proficient in the requested language.

To demonstrate language proficiency, Managers are encouraged to ask the staff member if they have a formal language certification or recognition, such as a:

  • National Accreditation Authority for Translators and Interpreters (NAATI) certification at the certified provisional interpreter level or higher, or
  • NAATI recognition as a practicing or recognised interpreter (only where NAATI certification is not available in that language), or
  • Certificate II in Auslan from TAFE, State Deaf Society or a Services Australia approved equivalent organisation

Does the staff member have a formal certification or recognition of their language skills?

  • Yes, go to Step 5
  • No, go to Step 4

4

Arranging CLA Proficiency test + Read more ...

Staff who do not have a formal certification or recognition of their language skills, can do a CLA Proficiency test to demonstrate language proficiency. Managers are responsible for arranging this test.

The National Accreditation Authority for Translators and Interpreters (NAATI) conduct the CLA Proficiency test on behalf of Services Australia.

To arrange the test, Managers must:

  • complete the Request for Community Language Allowance Proficiency test (PP015) form (see link on Resources page)
  • request the staff member to sign the completed PP015 form
  • email the completed PP015 to NAATI: clainfo@naati.com.au and cc Language Services
  • encourage staff to prepare for the test and refer to the:
    • NAATI CLA practice material, and
    • NAATI Guide to the CLA practice material
      on the Resources page

NAATI will:

  • contact the Manager and staff member to arrange a test date. The test is conducted via MS Teams
  • advise if no testing is available in the requested language. If this occurs, Managers must email Language Services as soon as possible

Did the staff member pass the CLA Proficiency test?

  • Yes, go to Step 5
  • No, discuss with the staff member the option of a re-test:
    • Services Australia will pay for a re-test after three months, (maximum of two re-tests), or
    • staff can pay for a re-test if within three months, or
    • staff can pay for a re-test after three failed attempts
    • procedure ends here

Note: Managers are responsible for organising re-tests. The agency will reimburse costs if the staff member passes the re-test.

5

Entering and approving CLA in ESSentials + Read more ...

After CLA eligibility is met, Managers must notify staff:

  • in writing within 21 days of receiving their application
  • to commence using their language skills in their usual duties
  • CLA will be paid from the first pay after it is approved in ESSentials
  • CLA will be paid during periods of Annual Leave, Personal Leave and Long Service Leave

To enter and approve CLA in ESSentials, Managers:

  • are encouraged to review the Allowances - Team Leader (ESS) task card
  • must enter an end-date that is no longer than 12 months from the start date
  • must enter CLA1 as the initial allowance rate and review on a monthly basis

Note: CLA may not be payable for months in which staff do not meet minimum usage. Refer to clause D1 of the Enterprise Agreement for specific rates. See Resources page for a link.

6

Reviewing staff member’s use of language skills + Read more ...

To retain CLA, staff must demonstrate their language skills are used to assist customers.

It is the Manager’s responsibility to review the CLA recipients’ language usage on a monthly basis.

Managers must:

  • request staff complete a Record of use of a language other than English for Community Language Allowance (CLA) including Indigenous language and Auslan (PP014) form (see link on Resources page)
  • review completed PP014 to determine CLA rate:
    • CLA1: staff use their language skills at least three times per month
    • CLA2: staff use their language skills at least eight times per month
  • review the need for CLA if the staff member’s role changes
  • advise staff in writing if:
    • language skills are no longer required
    • duties have changed and the language skills are not required
    • CLA rate changes, such as CLA1 to CLA2

Note: if staff assist a customer group/seminar in language, this is recorded as a single instance.
