Previous qualification + Read more ...

Does the customer have a disability or medical condition that previously qualified their carer for a Carer Allowance (CA) (child) Health Care Card for the customer on the day before the child turned 16?

  • Yes, if the customer is
    • aged 15 & 9 months - 25 years, go to Step 2
    • not between these ages, check eligibility for Low Income Health Care Card (LIC) or a Centrelink payment. Procedure ends here
  • No, check eligibility for LIC or a Centrelink payment. Procedure ends here

Note: customers can lodge completed claims up to 3 months before turning 16 years old.

2

Claiming options for customers + Read more ...

The preferred method of claiming EHC is online. If the customer:

  • is claiming online, go to Step 3
  • is unable or unsuitable to complete an online claim, offer staff assisted claim process. For:
    • Assisted Customer Claim (ACC), see Viewing and processing online and Assisted Customer Claim (ACC). Procedure ends here
  • declines online, go to Step 13
  • is submitting a paper claim, go to Step 14

3

Check access to online account + Read more ...

Check the customer is registered with an active Centrelink online account. Customers who do not have an active account must confirm their identity at a local service centre to register.

Tell customers how to create a myGov account and link to Centrelink services. Centrelink mail can be accessed through the customer's myGov inbox.

Service centre staff can help customers with online claiming through self service terminals.

Customers can use the Payment and Service Finder in their Centrelink online account or on Services Australia website to check which payment is appropriate for them, and to start their claim.

Intent to claim provisions apply to vulnerable customers.

4

Starting an online claim + Read more ...

Once registered with an 'active' online account, customers must:

  • sign into myGov and access their linked Centrelink online account
  • from the Menu, select the My Details > Personal and contact details > My profile to make updates:
    • the My profile page shows information on the Centrelink record
    • select Update (relevant service name) in the sections that need to be updated, i.e. Relationship or Interpreter details

To start a new claim:

  • select Payments and Claims > Claims > Make a claim from the Menu
  • on Make a Claim page select Get started under Concession Cards
  • answer the streaming questions in the Eligibility check section to decide the most suitable card.
  • if not eligible they are referred to the Payment and Service Finder or advised to contact Services Australia for a suitable service
  • select Back to return to Make a Claim page to start a new claim type
  • after completing the streaming questions, select Continue
  • the Next steps page tells the customer more information or documentation needed to submit the claim

To submit the claim customers must provide all documents needed and verify their identity if the Identity Confirmation Dashboard in Process Direct (PD) displays Not Confirmed. Exceptions are made for some vulnerable customers.

See Apply for a payment or concession card options online.

5

Electronic messaging + Read more ...

Customers who provide a mobile phone number or email address are automatically subscribed to Electronic Messaging (EM) services, unless they have previously declined EM.

Customers are:

  • asked to nominate a preferred contact method (SMS or email) for electronic messages
  • automatically subscribed to receive Centrelink mail online in the myGov Inbox

Customers can opt out of these services at any time.

6

To view or update a started claim online + Read more ...

To access an online claim, customers must:

  • sign in to myGov and access the linked Centrelink online account
  • select Payments and Claims > Claims > My online claim

A list of all online claims started by the customer will display.

If the status of the claim is incomplete, the customer can choose to:

  • Continue claim to display the next question set for completion, or
  • Cancel claim. A page will display to confirm. Once cancelled the customer cannot view or continue the claim. No claim information is recorded. The customer can start a new online claim at any time

7

Claim progress + Read more ...

The Claim navigation page shows the sections of the claim the customer must complete. These must be completed in order and before starting the next section.

Select Start to access sections.

Each section contains modules with relevant questions. On each question page, the customer can select Previous to go back to the previous question.

Customers can read the Oliver Digital Assistant text for help with the questions on the current page.

After completing each section, return to the Claim navigation page to select the next section.

Select Edit to correct answers of a previous section.

To return to the Claim navigation page, select the arrow under the last dot on the side navigation and Continue.

Customers can use the dots on the side navigation of each page to go between pages in the claim. Questions answered incorrectly will have a red cross in the dots on the right of the page. Select the red cross to go to the page with the error.

A claim does not need to be completed in one session. Customers can leave the Claim navigation page at any time and the responses will be saved.

All compulsory questions must be answered before submitting the claim.

Customers will be advised if they have a reduced question set. A summary of the following sections can be updated as required:

  • Your personal details
  • Your circumstances

8

Personal details and circumstances + Read more ...

Question sets include:

  • Personal details - Identity, Address, Contact details, Relationship
  • Contact Requirements - Permit to Enquire, Nominee
  • Australian Residence
  • Study details

9

Review and confirm + Read more ...

Outstanding items

This section will display if the customer has selected 'Provide details later' in any of the previous sections of the claim. The items can be selected and the customer will be navigated back to the relevant section of the claim to make the update.

Confirm details

A summary of all the information provided by the customer so they can check what they have entered is correct. The information displays in sections, e.g. Circumstance details.

A summary of My profile information also displays. Customers must make sure all information is correct.

To correct online claim or My profile information, the customer can select the Update button relevant to the answers to be changed. This takes them back to My profile or the relevant section of the claim to review/update the relevant claim questions and responses.

Declaration

Once all information is correct the customer must accept the declaration by ticking the box. Completing the declaration means a signed claim form is not required.

Acknowledgement

The customer must accept the acknowledgement by ticking the box. Completing the acknowledgement means the customer understands and agrees to the terms of their claim.

Confirm Information

The customer must select the Confirmation Information button to save all the information provided in the claim. No answers in the claim can be changed once this button is selected.

10

Next steps + Read more ...

At the Next steps section the customer can:

  • Upload documents
  • Submit claim

11

Uploading documents for online claims + Read more ...

Customers can access their started online claim to upload documents using:

  • Centrelink online account, or
  • Express Plus Centrelink mobile app
  • In the Next steps section, an Upload documents button is next to each listed task. Help the customer upload the documents and submit their claim online
  • Service Officers can scan the documents and submit the claim for the customer if needed:
    • scan the documents
    • run the Request and Manage Customer Tasks guided procedure to submit the claim
  • If the customer uploaded or gave documents before starting the claim or Assisted Customer Claim (ACC), the document displays as Required on the Next steps section. The customer can either:
  • re-upload the document on the Next steps section from the Documents and Forms section
  • contact by phone or in person to have the Request and Manage Customer Tasks guided procedure run to change the document status from Required to Provided

12

Submit the claim + Read more ...

Customers must:

  • answer all mandatory questions
  • provide all their required documents
  • verify their identify through the Identity Confirmation Dashboard in Process Direct (PD)

Exceptions will be made for some vulnerable customers.

Claims not submitted within 13 weeks will expire.

The claim cannot be submitted if the Required tasks are not complete.

Once the relevant tasks are completed, the Submit button will become available so the customer can submit their claim.

Procedure ends here.

13

Issuing a paper claim + Read more ...

Service Officers must initially promote online claiming or offer staff assisted claims.

Where a customer is unable or unsuitable to complete an online claim staff must offer staff assisted claim options. If unavailable issue an Ex-Carer Allowance (Child) Health Care Card (SS456).

Backdating of EHC claims

If seeking backdating of the EHC entitlement start date to a date earlier than the default start date, the claimant must:

  • provide evidence of a medical service or treatment received by the claimant on a relevant date
  • provide evidence that the claimant has been a full time student for the entire period from the relevant start date, and
  • satisfy all qualification criteria for the entire period from the relevant date

4

Backdate EHC claim + Read more ...

If the customer wants to backdate the EHC entitlement start date to a date earlier than the default start date, they must:

  • provide evidence of a medical service or treatment received by them on a relevant date
  • provide evidence they have been a full time student for the entire period from the relevant start date, and
  • satisfy all qualification criteria for the entire period from the relevant date

If the customer is requesting backdating prior to 40 weeks and appears eligible with evidence supplied, escalate the case to the Level 2 Policy Helpdesk for advice before making a decision on the start date.

If the customer is eligible for the EHC to be backdated, record the grant date on the Ex-Carer Allowance (Child) Health Care Card Assessment (EHCA) screen.

5

Finalise the claim + Read more ...

After coding the claim and addressing the data validations, Assess will display:

  • Select Assess
  • The Errors (SWE) screen will list any validation errors to address
  • Once the Message Log is clear, select Assess
  • The Entitlement (ELD) screen displays the outcome. Ensure the outcome is correct for the customer (and partner if applicable)
  • Select Finish
  • Review the Claim Outcome DOC. Ensure the text, Act reference and date are correct and meet the Getting it Right (GIR) Minimum Standards
  • Select Finalise
  • Select Finish. A box will confirm the claim has been successfully completed

Note: claims processed before the customer turns 16 years of age will issue a card on the 16th birthday if all other requirements are met.

Procedure ends here.

6

Streamline rejection + Read more ...

If a claim is streamline rejected, full claim coding is not required.

Go to the Benefit Action (BA) screen:

  • Select Add
  • Select the correct Benefit Type code
  • Select Benefit Action REJ-Reject from the drop down menu
  • Check there is a tick in the Streamline Rejection box and select the Save
  • From the Rejection Reason Code drop down menu select FSD - Failed to Supply Documents
  • Check the Date of Effect is correct
  • Select Save
  • Select Assess
  • The Errors (SWE) screen may list validation errors to address
  • Once the Message Log is clear, select Assess
  • The Entitlement (ELD) screen displays the outcome. Check the outcome is correct for the customer (and partner if applicable)
  • Select Finish
  • Review the Claim Outcome DOC. List the specific documents that were not supplied
  • Ensure the text, Act reference and date are correct and meet the Getting it Right (GIR) Minimum Standards
  • Select Finalise
