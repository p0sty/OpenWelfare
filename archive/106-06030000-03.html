1

Obtain residence information + Read more ...

The customer is asked to provide details of residence on either of the following forms:

  • an Agreement claim
  • a Module O - Life Residence in Australia and other countries (Mod O)
  • a Verification of working life residence in Australia (AUS27)
  • a Request for information (AUS168)

An agreement country may also request the information for their claim and may provide another form with similar questions.

These forms ask the customer to provide copies of documents to support their residence.

As a guide, it is reasonable for an absence under 3 years to be considered temporary, depending on all factors being considered. If it is confirmed through weighing up of evidence that the absence is temporary, this would not be recorded on the Country of Residence (CRES) screen. For example, discussion with the customer, other evidence provided and general residence factors. For more information, see Assessing if a customer is an Australian resident.

If the customer is lodging a claim for a Survivor or Widows pension from an agreement country, the WLR needs to be calculated for the deceased partner. The forms should have been completed providing details for the deceased partner.

2

Obtain details from the Immigration Datalink + Read more ...

The datalink should be activated to obtain details available directly from the Department of Home Affairs. Go to the Immigration Enquiry (RSIMME) screen.

Is the Customer Immigration Link Status: field Active?

  • Yes, go to Step 3
  • No, activate the Immigration Datalink on RSIMME screen. When activated, go to Step 3

3

Check the information from the Immigration Datalink + Read more ...

Go to the Legal Residence Details (RSLEG) screen. If the customer has Department of Home Affairs information from 1 September 1994, this screen can provide valuable information about the customer's legal residence status. For example, it will give the customer's citizenship including date of grant, visa subclasses and NZ residence status.

The Immigration Advised Movements (RSIM) screen will give details of any arrivals and departures in Australia since 1 September 1994. This will give the Service Officer information to help determine whether the customer was residing in Australia during this period.

Was the customer or deceased partner born in Australia and state that they have never left Australia since then for any reason other than a holiday, and the information received via the Immigration Datalink does not contradict this?

  • Yes, see Step 3 in Table 2
  • No, go to Step 4

4

Check documents which provide supporting evidence + Read more ...

As residence affects the rate of pension to which a person is entitled, it is preferable to obtain evidence of a customer or deceased partner's residence in Australia. This is not always possible and the Service Officer must recognise this and provide for the needs of customers who lack supporting evidence and have limited access to information sources within Australia.

Is there adequate evidence that verifies all permanent arrivals to and departures from Australia?

  • Yes, see Step 3 in Table 2
  • No, go to Step 5

5

Immigration data not available or customer or deceased partner arrived before 1 September 1994 + Read more ...

  • If access to the Immigration Movement Records (MR) database is available, search for the customer or deceased partner on this database. The database contains visa and movement records since 1981
  • Arrivals after 1965 need the customer or deceased partner's name, variations (if any) and date of arrival. Arrivals before 1965 also require the customer or deceased partner's ship or flight details and the port at which they arrived. Make sure that the Customer Person Detail (CPD) screen is updated with all relevant information
  • Old Arrival Records can be searched in the National Archives:
    • go to www.naa.gov.au
    • select Go to RecordSearch
    • select Passenger arrivals tab
    • key the surname of the customer. It is not usually necessary to include extra details such as full name, year, ship, port or flight number. This may limit the search
    • select Search
    • results will display the passenger name and date of arrival
    • identify the correct customer by checking the linked digital information
    • if the passenger name displays but has no date of arrival, select the digital copy of the passenger list manifesto. This has the date the ship/flight arrived. It is not necessary to scroll through the list to find the customerâ€™s name
  • CIS Service Officers can also:
    • check microfilm and microfiche if necessary, or
    • request Passenger Card details. These provide details the customer completed upon arrival into Australia. It may help in determining the customer's intention to reside in Australia. Note: specific access is required to obtain Passenger Cards. See Resources for a link to the Passenger Card Image System intranet page

For more information, including how to send a request for verification of arrival to CIS, see Verifying and recording Proof of Arrival (POA).

Hold the claim or activity pending return of the information.

Have all dates of arrival and departure been verified?

  • Yes, see Step 3 in Table 2
  • No, go to Step 6

Lodging a claim under a Social Security Agreement + Read more ...

Is the customer lodging a claim under an International Social Security Agreement?

  • Yes, compare residence with the period of contributions in the Agreement country. See the Resources page for tips to help verify residence. Go to Step 7 in Table 2
  • No, go to Step 7

Request verification from referees + Read more ...

Check if names of referees have been given on any forms, such as the AUS027. Contact the referees to try to verify information. Record the responses in a DOC.

Assessing residence + Read more ...

Without supporting evidence, the Service Officer must decide whether to accept the customer's statement. See Resources page for tips to help verify residence.

As the customer's rate of pension is based on their residence, verifying this can be as important as a customer verifying income or asset information.

Has any of the customer or deceased partner's residence been verified?

  • No, go to Step 2

2

Residence is not acceptable + Read more ...

Is the customer paid according to their current or former partner's WLR?

  • Yes, go to Step 4
  • No, advise the customer of the decision and record all the details in a DOC as to the reasons for the decision. Procedure ends here

3

Residence is acceptable + Read more ...

WLR is calculated automatically by the system.

  • Make sure that the customer or deceased partner's correct country of birth is on the Immigration Enquiry (RSIMME) screen and country of citizenship is held on the Legal Residence Details (RSLEG) screen. Also make sure correct visa information is held for customer or deceased partner who were not born in Australia. Without correct information, the system will not be able to correctly calculate WLR. For assistance coding this information, see Recording legal residence status
  • To calculate, enter the customer or deceased partner's residence information on the Country of Residence (CRES) screen.
  • Make sure that all advised permanent residence periods are entered. Do not record any temporary absences from Australia (e.g. overseas holidays) as residence overseas and do not record any time spent in Australia as a visitor as Australian residence
  • If the customer is departing permanently, record the departure on the CRES screen
  • Once this information is correctly coded, the system will work out the WLR and display on the Australian Working Life Residence (AWLR) screen. The WLR will also default into the Aust Working Life Residence: field on the Additional Residence Details (ARD) screen

4

Check if partner's WLR affects the customer + Read more ...

Where the customer's record is linked to the partner's record, the system will take the higher of the 2 WLR where applicable. Where the customer is paid according to their former partner's WLR, the system is unable to calculate the correct rate. The former partner's WLR must be manually recorded.

Will the customer be paid according to their current or former partner's WLR?

  • Yes, go to Step 5
  • No, continue with agreement claim procedure, portability decision or foreign claim. Procedure ends here

5

Possible use of partner's WLR + Read more ...

If the deceased partner has a record, the WLR can be calculated using the CRES and AWLR screens. If no record exists, manually calculate the deceased partner's WLR

  • Is the customer claiming:
    • a Survivor or Widow's pension from an agreement country, or
    • receiving PPS under an International agreement?
  • Yes, go to Step 6
  • No, customer should use their own WLR. Continue with Agreement claim procedure, portability decision or foreign claim. Procedure ends here

6

Lodging a foreign claim for a Widow or Survivor Pension from an Agreement country + Read more ...

Is the customer lodging a foreign claim for a Widow or Survivors Pension from an Agreement country?

  • Yes, advise the agreement country of the deceased partner's WLR
    • Continue with the foreign pension claim
    • Procedure ends here
  • No, go to the Additional Residence Details (ARD) screen
    • Record the higher of the customer's or their deceased partner's calculated WLR in the customer's Aust Working Life Residence: field
    • If using the deceased partner's WLR, record the reason for the difference in the WLR and the residence information and calculation of the WLR for the deceased partner in a DOC
    • Continue with agreement claim procedure or portability decision
