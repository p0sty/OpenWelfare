This is a Family and Domestic Violence Interaction Point. If the customer is with another person, on speaker phone, or already identified with family and domestic violence concerns, continue with the current business conversation. Otherwise, go to the Family and domestic violence procedure to conduct the risk identification and referral process.

YA or ABSTUDY customer separates

Step

Action

1

Support the customer to separate safely + Read more ...

When a customer separates, changes to their record must be made to reflect their new circumstances. These changes can result in a potential privacy breach if they are not done correctly. Customers must be supported to separate safely.

Discuss separating safely with the customer before updating any personal information

2

Notification of separation + Read more ...

Youth Allowance (YA) or ABSTUDY customer advises they are no longer living with their partner (separated).

If the customer advises their relationship status will change in future, do not code the change. Encourage the customer to use the online update relationship details option through their Centrelink online account at the time of the relationship status change. Within the activity, the customer can provide details of date of separation/divorce. Record details of contact on a DOC.

At risk assessment

To protect vulnerable customers, those considered being at risk of family and domestic violence may have their relationship status updated immediately. Verification will still be required, however can be supplied after the records have been unlinked. Ask the customer:

'Do you have any concerns for your safety and/or the safety of your children?'

  • Yes, go to Step 3
  • No, go to Step 4

3

Vulnerable customer at risk + Read more ...

Customers at risk should have their files updated immediately. Verification should be requested but Service Officers should not defer updating customer files, as the safety of the customer is paramount.

If a customer has advised of their new relationship status online, this activity must be processed through the inbox in Process Direct.

To avoid further risk to the customer, when completing the 'unlinking' activity via the Marital Status (MS) screen to update the relationship status due to separation, take care when updating the customer's address to ensure updates do not flow onto their former partner’s record.

  • enter 'MS' in the Next: field to access a new screen for updating
  • Event Date: field, record the date of separation
  • Marital Status: field, select separated marital status
  • PPE Cust and PPE Ptnr: field, update to 'N'
  • Qualifier Code: field, leave blank
  • Source: field, select relevant source of information
  • Receipt Date: field, date the information was recorded
  • selecting Continue will go directly to the Customer Details Task (CDTS) screen where the default of B (both customer and partner) must be changed to S (customer only) for the Address, Accommodation and Telephone fields. This ensures the address details do not flow to the former partner's record
  • on the Accommodation (AC) screen, code the appropriate sharer status code in the Sharer Status: field
  • Finalise on the Assessment Results (AR) screen
  • Go to Step 4

4

Verification of separation + Read more ...

Customers who have had their relationship status updated immediately due to family and domestic violence concerns are still required to provide evidence of the separation within 14 days.

Check the customer’s record to see if they have completed an online Update relationship details activity advising of the separation. Documentation is requested at the ‘next steps’ of the update for the customer to upload.

Is the customer able to provide evidence of the separation?

  • Yes,
    • Request evidence. See Requesting information (CLK)
    • Tell the customer they must supply verification of their change in relationship or their payment may be suspended or claim rejected
    • Record details on a DOC
    • Go to Step 5
  • No,
    • See the Verifying separation table in Change in relationship status from partnered to single
    • Go to Step 5

5

Update customer details + Read more ...

Do not update relationship status at this stage.

  • If necessary, update the Marital Status (MS) screen to change PPE or to allow address details to be updated. Start a new line:
    • Code the Event Date: field as the day before the separation
    • Re-code Marital Status: field (do not change it). When the MOD S is returned, the new relationship status will be recorded
    • Update PPE: field (person permitted to enquire) to 'N'
    • Code a relationship qualifier in the Qualifier Code: field
    • As the customer is considered partnered until verification of the separation has been processed, select from the Qualifiers of partnered relationship statuses
    • Select Continue
  • Update any other information from the Customer Details Task (CDTS) screen, such as change of address/change of payment destination
    • For change of address, on the Address Details (AD) screen change the Make Partner the Same? code 'N', if applicable
    • On the Accommodation Details (AC) screen, update the Accommodation Details for: field in Customer First or the Details For: field in Process Direct with Customer. See Determining proof of rent for the Verification field on the Accommodation Details (AC) screen
    • Note: the Accommodation (AC) screen can be changed for one partner only (that is, the customer) provided a code appears in the Relationship Qualifier: field
  • If the customer has their ex-partner appointed as their nominee, ask if they wish to cancel the nominee arrangement
  • Record details on a DOC

Finalise on the Assessment Results (AR) screen

Check if the customer is independent due to being a member of a YA couple or an ABSTUDY customer considered to be independent due to previously being married or in a registered relationship:

  • For YA customers, go to Step 6
  • For ABSTUDY customers, go to Step 7
  • No, go to Step 10

6

Determine if YA customer is independent + Read more ...

YA customers who have left a de facto relationship that has lasted as least 6 months due to special circumstances, (which can include an abusive relationship), may be considered independent.

For more information, see Assessing independence when a customer is/was married or a member of a couple.

Is the customer considered independent due to being a member of a YA couple?

  • Yes, go to Step 8
  • No, go to Step 10

 

7

Determine if ABSTUDY customer is independent + Read more ...

ABSTUDY customers who have previously been married or in a registered relationship are considered independent. For more information, see ABSTUDY customer currently or previously married or in a registered relationship.

ABSTUDY customers who have left a de facto relationship are no longer considered independent. For more information, see ABSTUDY customer in de facto relationship.

Is the ABSTUDY customer considered independent due to previously being married or in a registered relationship?

  • Yes, go to Step 9
  • No, go to Step 10

8

Customer is independent due to being a member of a YA couple + Read more ...

Update the Independent/Homeless/Away from Home Details (NIH) screen as follows:

  • On the existing Independence line with reason 'PCO' (currently a member of a qualifying YA couple), code End Date: field with date of separation
  • Code End Reason: field with 'NLE'
  • Code a new line with Act: field as 'IND', Start Date: field from day after separation and Start/Reject Reason: field coded as either:
    • 'PDF' (has previously been member of de facto relationship for at least 12 months), or
    • 'PDS' (has previously been a member of a de facto relationship for at least 6 months and special circumstances exist)
  • Go to Step 10

9

ABSTUDY customer considered independent due to previously being married or in a registered relationship + Read more ...

Update the Independent/Homeless/Away from Home Details (NIH) screen in the ISS system as follows:

  • Code a new line with Act: field as 'IND'
  • Start Date: field from day after separation
  • Start/Reject Reason: ‘PBM’
  • Source and Date of Receipt fields should be completed with appropriate details

Note: the End Date, End Reason, and Consent to Contact fields do not need to be completed.

Go to Step 10

10

Check ongoing entitlement + Read more ...

If the customer is no longer qualified for YA or ABSTUDY or it has been identified they may be eligible for a more appropriate payment such as Parenting Payment Single (PPS), the customer is to claim online.

If the customer is unable to complete an online claim, check details in Office Locator for the customer's local service centre and book a claim appointment to complete the Assisted Customer Claim (ACC) workflow.

If the customer's ex-partner is receiving Parenting Payment Partnered (PPP), see Claiming Parenting Payment (PP).

If the separated couple have a child in their care, check if there has been a change of care and/or if maintenance action is to be taken. See Family Tax Benefit (FTB) and/or Child Care Subsidy (CCS) customer separates.

Tell the customer of change of rate of payment. Customers receiving ABSTUDY Incidentals Allowance only (ABY/CUR) may be entitled to full ABSTUDY including Living Allowance following separation. For more information regarding the single rate of YA or ABSTUDY, see Ready Reckoners and rate charts.

Offer the option of changing the future payment destination if others (including ex-partners or joint holders) have access to the existing bank account. See Changing payment destinations.

11

Online Separation update or Separation Module S + Read more ...

Encourage the customer to use the online update relationship details option through their Centrelink online account (Menu > My details > Personal and contact details > Update relationship details). Within the activity, the customer can provide details of date of separation/divorce.

If a customer cannot use the online service, request a Separation details (MOD S) form (where the customer or their partner are not claiming or transferring to PPS). See Requesting information (CLK).

Follow up vacation of principal home where the customer has part or full legal ownership of that property.

Process the MOD S when returned, see Step 2 in the Process when MOD S form is returned table in Assessing the Separation details MOD S and online updates. Note: if the customer's ex-partner receives PP, the records must only be unlinked within the Benefit Transfer (BTR) activity to transfer the ex-partner to PPS. Otherwise, PPP will cancel and a new claim activity coded to transfer to PPS. The PP customer may suffer hardship as a result.
