Job seeker - Activity Review Online Task or Letter

Table 1: This table describes how to action an Approved Activity Review Online Task or Letter.

Step

Action

1

To conduct an Approved Activity Review (AAR) + Read more ...

If the job seeker contacting has Personalised Servicing, see Referring customers to and handling customer enquiries and correspondence for Personalised Services.

Is the job seeker contacting about an online task?

  • Yes,
    • Advise the job seeker they need to complete the task if they still meet the approved activity criteria and the activity will be extended
    • Assist job seeker online to complete if having difficulty. If they are not continuing, they do not need to do anything. We will be in contact after the expiry date
  • No, job seekers who have received a request to contact letter, go to Step 2

2

System process + Read more ...

System process once job seeker completes an activity that attracts AAR Contact

Once the job seeker completes an approved program or activity which attracts the AAR Contact, the job seeker will receive an online task or letter depending on whether they are signed up for Self Service.

The system will create an online task or issue a letter 28 days out from the expiry date.

If the job seeker fails to contact or complete the online task before the expiry of an approved activity, job seeker management requirements will commence and they will be referred to an Employment Services Provider.

The exception to this is for the approved requirements below:

  • Defence Force Reserve (DFR)
  • Job Seeker Full-time Student (STF)
  • Job Seeker Part-time Student (SPT)

Where an approved requirement DRF, STF or SPT expires, a work item is created that will be allocated to a Service Officer to contact the job seeker. See Table 2 for details.

3

Contact requirements + Read more ...

Review Activity and Exemption Summary (AEX) screen for the expiring approved activity.

Actions and discussion points for review appointments include:

  • assess outcomes from participation in a recent program or activity, and any follow up action required
  • investigate whether the job seeker's circumstances have changed since the last review was undertaken (for example, now homeless, have an illness or changed family circumstances resulting in a possible exemption for a period)
  • where necessary:
    • re-run the Job Seeker Snapshot, and
    • refer for an Employment Services Assessment (ESAt) as required
  • investigate whether all barriers to employment have been addressed
  • discuss with the job seeker other options in terms of further requirements the job seeker could undertake to improve employment prospects
  • for Centrelink managed job seekers, review the existing activity on the Activities and Exemption (AEX) screen and update it as necessary
  • if not already referred and it is appropriate, refer the job seeker to a suitable Workforce Australia Employment Services Provider. If the job seeker has:
    • a current and valid ESAt/JCA, this action should take into account any referral recommendations in the ESAt/JCA
    • already completed a period of participation in the recommended program - for example, exited from Disability Employment Services (DES) with an employment or education outcome - they would generally not be referred back to the same program on the basis of the original ESAt/JCA
  • ensure the job seeker is engaged with their provider. If not, and no future appointments already exist, encourage the job seeker to contact their provider as soon as possible to book an appointment to re-negotiate their Job Plan

4

Minimum outcomes + Read more ...

As a minimum outcome, each job seeker will:

  • receive written advice regarding the requirement to negotiate an Job Plan
  • have their Job Seeker Snapshot updated where necessary
  • if they are a Centrelink managed job seeker, they will have their activities and Job Plan reviewed. Approved activities are to be updated on the Activities and Exemptions (AEX) screen. The system will determine if a new Job Plan should be negotiated, and
  • if not already connected and if it is appropriate, they will be connected with a suitable Employment Services Provider

Actioning Online - AEX and Job Plan review work items

Table 2: This table describes how to action an AEX and Job Plan review work item.

Step

Action

1

To conduct an Approved Activity Review work item + Read more ...

There will be notes associated with the activity on the Participation Summary screen and the AEX screen will show the current Approved Activity that will be expiring shortly. The AAR - will create a work item:

  • AEX review, if the approved activity is either DFR, STF or SPT, go to Step 3
  • Online - AEX and Job Plan review, if it is an Assisted Online Task, go to Step 2

2

Assisted Online Task + Read more ...

On receiving the work item, check note from Activities/Activity Details for corresponding work item ID (Maintain Activities & Exemptions).

'Customer actioned their 'Approved Activity End date' review online task'

If Note commences with: 'Customer actioned their 'Approved Activity End date' review online task' then job seeker has advised they are continuing with activity.

If the job seeker is a DES participant with DPA or DPB coded on AEX, or has a current QTI coded, then complete the activity and do not update AEX or the Job Plan.

For all other current approved requirements recorded:

  • extend the approved activity 363 days from the current date and finalise the update
  • negotiate a new Job Plan
  • send the Job Plan for online agreement by the job seeker
  • complete the work item via the Activities or Interaction History tabs in Customer First:
    • select the relevant Transaction Type ‘CoC Interaction’ from a tab, and
    • change the status from ‘For manual action’ to ‘Completed’
  • no further action is required

There is no requirement for the job seeker to re-verify their ongoing participation in the approved activity e.g. Volunteer Work, Study etc. however, Service Officer discretion may be used to contact job seeker if any doubt exists before coding the extension.

'Customer used 'View approved activity or principal carer exemption' service'

If Note commences with: 'Customer used 'View approved activity or principal carer exemption' service' then job seeker is potentially advising of a change in circumstance to their recorded approved activity or exemption.

  • Review the remainder of the notes provided by the job seeker
  • Contact the job seeker to discuss their change in circumstances
  • Update the job seeker’s AEX screen. The system will determine if the Job Plan needs to be updated
  • Explain to the job seeker how the changes affect their mutual obligation requirements

If contact with job seeker is required and unable to contact, go to Step 3.

3

First outbound attempt + Read more ...

When it is necessary to contact a job seeker, make every reasonable and genuine effort to ensure contact is successful.

If the contact number recorded on the job seeker's record is a mobile number, Service Officers should create a pre-call notification (SMS only) using Desktop Electronic Messaging Capability (DEMC). Following creation of the SMS, wait 1 to 2 minutes before making the outbound attempt.

Was the contact successful?

  • Yes, go to Step 7
  • No:
    • If the first attempt to contact a job seeker is unsuccessful, hold the work item until the next calendar day (see Table 3 for details)
    • Key sufficient detail about the outbound contact attempt on the Notes tab to allow subsequent Service Officer to action the second attempt
    • Once finalised, go to Step 4

4

Second outbound attempt + Read more ...

If the first attempt to contact the job seeker is unsuccessful, make a second genuine attempt in line with current processes.

If the contact number, recorded on the job seeker's record, is a mobile number, Service Officers should create a pre-call notification (SMS only) using Desktop Electronic Messaging Capability (DEMC). Following creation of the SMS, wait 1 to 2 minutes before making the outbound attempt.

Was the contact successful?

  • Yes, go to Step 7
  • No, go to Step 5

5

Issue a Q164 letter after second unsuccessful contact attempt or where no contact details recorded for job seeker + Read more ...

Issue a Q164 letter asking the job seeker to contact Services Australia by phone within 14 days:

  • after a second unsuccessful outbound contact attempt, or
  • where there are no contact details or incorrect details are recorded (for example, the job seeker's phone number is not registered)

The job seeker has 14 days to respond after they receive the letter. Add days to allow extra time for mail delivery. Hold the work item until the day after the allowable time has expired (see Table 3 for details).

If the job seeker:

  • contacts because of the suspension, restore payment and action outstanding activities. Go to Step 7
  • fails to contact, go to Step 6

6

Job seeker fails to respond to the Q164 letter + Read more ...

If the job seeker fails to contact by the notification date +1 after the Q164 is issued, Service Officers must determine if suspension action can be taken. Before suspending a job seeker's payment, see Suspension of payments (CLK) for information about factors to consider.

If it is appropriate to manually suspend a job seeker for Failing to Respond to Correspondence (SUS-FRC):

  • go to the Benefit Action (BA) screen:
    • key the payment type as the type of benefit in the Svc Rsn field
    • key the action to be taken in the Action field
    • key the suspension reason 'FRC' in the Reason field
    • press [Enter] and the date of effect will default or key the date of effect of the suspension in the Effect Date field
    • complete the Source field and the date of receipt in the DOR field
  • include the following details on a DOC (where relevant):
    • decision made, payment type and the Act/Guide reference the decision was based on, see Creating, reviewing and deleting documents (including Fast Notes and DOA DOCs) and Online Document Recording (ODR)
    • date of effect of the decision
    • reasons for the decision, including why the person was not qualified or why the payment is not payable, for decisions made under section 80
    • follow-up action required on contact with the job seeker
    • if contact with the assessing Service Officer is required before restoration can be done or whether Smart Centre Telephony staff may restore the payment
  • go to the Assessment Results (AR) screen and finalise the activity

Where the job seeker's payment has:

  • been suspended for failing to contact:
    • no further action is required by Services Australia
    • hold the work item for 13 weeks from the suspension date. Do not cancel the work item
  • not been suspended as it not appropriate to suspend the job seeker's payment for failing to contact:
    • undertake job seeker contact, and
    • hold the work item as per the Q164 process. Do not cancel the work item

Note: see Table 3 for how to hold a work item.

7

System process + Read more ...

System process once job seeker completes an activity that attracts AAR Contact

Once the job seeker is nearing completion of an approved program or requirement which attracts the AAR Contact, the system will create a work item for activities DFR, STF and SPT or an Assisted Online Task.

The system will create a work item 28 days out from expiry date.

If we are unable to contact the job seeker or they fail to contact us after being issued with Q164 on expiry of approved activity, job seeker management activities will commence and they will be referred to an Employment Services Provider.

8

Contact requirements + Read more ...

Review AEX screen for the expiring approved activity.

Actions and discussion points for Review appointments include:

  • if job seeker is a DES participant on AEX (DPA, DPB) or has a current QTI coded, then complete the activity and do not update AEX or Job Plan
  • discuss with the job seeker other options in terms of further activities the job seeker could undertake to improve employment prospects
  • for Centrelink managed job seekers, review the approved activity on the AEX screen and update it as necessary

9

Minimum outcomes + Read more ...

As a minimum outcome, each job seeker required to undertake AAR will:

  • receive written advice regarding the requirement to negotiate an Job Plan
  • have their Job Seeker Snapshot updated where necessary
  • if they are a Centrelink managed job seeker, they will have their AEX screen reviewed. The system will determine if a new Job Plan should be negotiated, and
  • if not already connected and if it is appropriate, they will be connected with a suitable Employment Services Provider

Actioning a PAA Review Booked Appointment generated prior to 1st of July 2021

Table 4: This table describes how to action a PAA Review Job Plan Contact generated prior to 1st July 2021.

Item

Description

1

Job seeker requirements + Read more ...

The PAA Review Contact is about assessing outcomes from a job seeker's participation in an approved activity or program. It allows a Service Officer to review a job seeker's mutual obligation requirements. Job seekers who are selected to attend this appointment are required to:

  • attend the face-to-face appointment as scheduled
  • contact the Services Australia to book an appointment (where the system has been unable to automatically book an appointment), and
  • for a Centrelink managed job seeker, negotiate and sign a new Job Plan

2

Job seeker exemptions + Read more ...

Job seekers may be excluded from selection for the PAA Review Contact if they:

  • have commenced another approved activity, for example, Short Course (SHD) within 28 days of ceasing to participate in their previous activity
  • have become exempt from mutual obligation requirements within 28 days of ceasing to participate in their previous activity
  • have had a follow-up Job Plan review within the previous 3 months
  • are a job seeker with a partial capacity to work (PCW) who has suffered the loss or temporary incapacity of a carer (if granted for 4 or more weeks)
  • are a job seeker whose part-time work activity has been automatically updated on the AEX screen and their Job Plan has been updated accordingly

3

Mutual obligation requirements or programs which attract the PAA Review Contact + Read more ...

  • Adult Migrant English Program (AME)
  • Apprentice/Trainee (APT)
  • Armed Services Training Camp (ARM)

  • Defence Force Reserve (DFR)

4

System process + Read more ...

System process once job seeker completes an activity that attracts PAA Review Contact

Once the job seeker completes an approved program or activity which attracts the PAA Review Contact, the system will not select the job seeker for an appointment for at least 21 days.

  • The system will then check for activity type 'Jobsearch' (JSE) and search for an available appointment 14 to 28 days in the future
  • PAA reviews will not be created for CDP provider managed job seekers
  • If an appointment is made, the job seeker is sent an appointment notice
  • If no available appointment is found, an advice is issued requesting the job seeker to contact and make an appointment
  • If the job seeker fails to contact to make an appointment the job seeker's payment will be suspended. Automatic suspension occurs when a PAA Review has not been completed within 70 days from the date that the job seeker exited the program or activity
  • The job seeker's payment will be restorable upon completion of the PAA Review

5

Appointment requirements + Read more ...

In the Participation Summary workflow, a message will display in the Action Item table ‘Principal Carer Exemption or Approved requirements is about to end'. Service Officers will be able to complete or cancel the PAA Review as required via Customer First, which supports the process through discussion points and links to other workflows which may be required.

Actions and discussion points for PAA Review appointments include:

  • assess outcomes from participation in a recent program or activity, and any follow up action required
  • investigate whether the job seeker's circumstances have changed since the last review was undertaken (for example, now homeless, have an illness or changed family circumstances resulting in a possible exemption for a period)

Where necessary, re-run the Job Seeker Snapshot and refer for an Employment Services Assessment (ESAt) as required, including where:

  • there has been a change of circumstances
    • the QTI activity has expired, and the job seeker is likely to be eligible for QTI to be extended, as it appears they still have a Partial Capacity to Work (PCW) or Temporary Reduced Work Capacity (TRWC) of less than 15 hours per week
  • consider whether a referral is required for a Job Capacity Assessment (JCA)
  • investigate whether all barriers to employment have been addressed
  • discuss with the job seeker other options in terms of further requirements the job seeker could undertake to improve employment prospects
  • for Centrelink managed job seekers, review the existing Job Plan and update it as necessary
  • if not already referred and it is appropriate, refer the job seeker to a suitable Employment Services Provider. If the job seeker has a current and valid ESAt/JCA, this action should take into account any referral recommendations in the ESAt/JCA. If the job seeker has already completed a period of participation in the recommended program - for example, exited from Disability Employment Services (DES) with an employment or education outcome - the job seeker would generally not be referred back to the same program on the basis of the original ESAt/JCA
  • ensure the job seeker is engaged with their provider. If not, and no future appointments already exist, encourage the job seeker to contact their provider as soon as possible to book an appointment to re-negotiate their Job Plan

6

Minimum outcomes + Read more ...

As a minimum outcome, each job seeker required to attend a face-to-face PAA Review appointment will:

  • receive written advice regarding the requirement to negotiate a Job Plan
  • have their Job Seeker Snapshot updated where necessary
  • if they are a Centrelink managed job seeker, they will have their Job Plan reviewed. A new Job Plan should be negotiated to reflect the current situation and encompass further obligations and follow-up requirements as agreed to by both the job seeker and the Service Officer, and
  • if not already connected and if it is appropriate, they will be connected with a suitable Employment Services Provider

7

Failure to attend interview suspension processes + Read more ...

A manual suspension process will apply if selected job seekers fail to attend scheduled interviews without prior contact with Services Australia.

If a job seeker fails to attend the interview, two genuine attempts must be made to contact them and if they cannot be contacted, their payment should be suspended with the reason 'Failed to Attend Interview' (FAI).

Following successful contact with Services Australia, the interview must be rescheduled and, when the job seeker attends their appointment, payment can be restored. Service Officers must record a DOC containing reasons for non-attendance.

A fail-safe mechanism also operates to automatically suspend the payments of job seekers (suspension code 'PRO') who do not have a Job Plan negotiated within 70 days from the date they became eligible for the PAA Review. A suspension letter will be issued to the job seeker.
