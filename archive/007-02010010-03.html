PPL details for customers with children born or entering care both before and on or after 1 July 2023.

This page contains information on assessing claims for Parental Leave Pay (PPL) and/or Family Tax Benefit (FTB) from birth mothers who relinquish the care of their child.

PPL and FTB claims from birth mothers relinquishing care of child

Step

Action

1

Birth mothers who relinquish the care of their child may be eligible for PPL and/or FTB + Read more ...

FTB can include Newborn Supplement (NBS) and Newborn Upfront Payment (NBU) if the customer is not eligible for PPL.

Has the customer lodged a claim for payment?

  • Yes, go to Step 3
  • No, go to Step 2

2

How to claim + Read more ...

Encourage customers to complete an online claim for family assistance and PPL. This is the preferred service offer. It gives customers a streamlined experience.

Help the customer register for online services if necessary. Make sure they can access an 'active' online account before directing them to claim online.

If it is unreasonable for the customer to claim online, Service Officers can issue a Claim for Parental Leave Pay and Family Tax Benefit (FA100).

Extra assistance is available to help remote Indigenous customers make a claim.

3

Care of the child + Read more ...

Will the customer be the carer of the child after the birth before they relinquish care of the child?

  • Yes, they may be eligible for PPL and FTB for the child. Note: the birth mother/ carer may receive either PPL or NBS for the newborn child. Customers cannot be paid both PPL and NBS for the same child. Go to Step 4
  • No, go to Step 4

4

Cessation of care of the child + Read more ...

Birth mothers who cease to have care of their child immediately after birth may be eligible for PPL for maternal recovery purposes.

For children born before 1 July 2023, birth mothers may receive PPL up to 18 weeks following the birth of their child if they:

  • nominate a start date no later than the child's date of birth
  • claim and provide relevant documents within the 28 days following the birth

As the birth mother cannot receive PPL more than 18 weeks after the child’s birth, they should be advised to either:

  • connect all of their Flexible PPL days to the end of their PPL period, or
  • claim consecutive Flexible PPL days after their PPL Period

For children born on or after 1 July 2023, birth mothers may receive up to 20 weeks of PPL, in addition to the gaining parent/carer.

For the birth mother, PPL will only be payable during the first 20 weeks (140 calendar days) after the birth of the child.

Birth mothers cannot be paid PPL on a day they are performing paid work.

If they claim and provide relevant documents more than 100 days following the birth, PPL may only start within 100 days from the date all information is received. This means they may receive less than the full entitlement.

Has the customer lodged an effective claim and provided all relevant documents within 28 days of the birth of the child?

  • Yes, confirm the customer has selected the child's date of birth as their nominated start date for PPL. Go to Step 5
  • No, the customer may not receive the full PPL entitlement
    • Confirm the customer has selected the date of claim as their start date
    • If the customer has selected a later date, try to call the customer as a later start date will prevent them receiving the full PPL entitlement
    • Go to Step 5

5

Return to work + Read more ...

Note: return to work provisions apply to children born before 1 July 2023.

Has the customer returned to work since giving birth to the child?

The PPL payment will cease if the birth mother advises a return to work:

  • before the end of the PPL period, or
  • during the connected period

Tell the customer to claim Flexible PPL days before the child is 18 weeks old. See Parental Leave Pay (PPL) customer, for a child born before 1 July 2023, returns to work.

Any Flexible PPL days that are rejected or withdrawn after the customer has returned to work are returned to the customer’s balance of unclaimed Flexible PPL days. These can be claimed on another eligible date within 18 weeks of the child’s birth or adoption. See Claiming and managing Flexible Paid Parental Leave (PPL) days for children born or entering care before 1 July 2023.

  • Yes, they may not be eligible for PPL. See Parental Leave Pay (PPL) customer, for a child born before 1 July 2023, returns to work. Procedure ends here
  • No, go to Step 6

6

Child details + Read more ...

Children must be named, but if care of the child has been relinquished as part of the process of adoption or a surrogacy arrangement and the child was never in the care of the birth mother, a claim can be accepted for the child without a name being given.

Has the customer provided a name for the child?

  • Yes, add the child to the customer's record with the child's name details provided. Go to Step 7
  • No, add the child to the customer's record with a first name and surname of 'surrogate' (this applies for both adopted and surrogate children). Go to Step 7

7

Check/complete coding

For claim processing requirements, see Assessing family assistance and Paid Parental Leave scheme claims.

Once this has been completed

For claim processing in Customer First: + Read more ...

Select the new claim activity from the Activity List (AL) screen and complete any further coding required.

'S'elect the child from the Child Selection (CHS) screen. On the Child Task Selector (CHTS) screen, select the following screens to check/update details:

  • On the Child in Customer Care (CHC) screen, code 'ICC' (In Customer Care) in the Care Status: field
  • Child in Primary Care (!CiPC) screen. This page should be updated for PPL claims with:
    • Date of event
    • Carer: claimant
    • In Primary Care: No
    • Reason: Surrogacy/Adoption. This will still allow the payment to issue

There is no need to update:

  • Child Relationship (!CHRE) screen
  • Care Alignment Details (!CADDS) screen
  • Child Miscellaneous Circumstances (CHM) screen
  • Medicare Information (MEI) screen

For claim processing in Process Direct + Read more ...

Within the claim activity select the record to be updated from the

On the Child in Customer Care (CHC) screen, in Care Status select In Customer Care

Go to the Child in Primary Care (CIPC) screen. This screen should be updated for PPL claims with:

  • Date of event
  • Carer: Claimant
  • In Primary Care: No
  • Left Primary Care Reason: Surrogacy/Adoption. This will still allow the payment to issue

There is no need to update:

  • Child Relationship (CHRE) screen
  • Care Alignment Details (CADDS) screen
  • Child Miscellaneous Circumstances (CHM) screen
  • Medicare Information (MEI) screen

Note: Surrogacy arrangements + Read more ...

A surrogacy arrangement includes the formal surrogacy agreement or supporting evidence of a surrogacy agreement. If unable to make a determination using the information provided, access technical support who may determine that a referral to the Level 2 Policy Helpdesk via the Online Form is required. See the Resources page for a link to the form.

8

Refer to Service Delivery Support Team - Families & Child Care Portfolio + Read more ...

Once the claim has been assessed for the child, refer the child's Customer Reference Number (CRN) on the birth mother's record to Service Delivery Support Team - Families & Child Care Portfolio.

Insert 'New adopted child record' in the subject line.

The CRN will be promoted to a 'PER'son record (if it was a 'CHI'ld record) and will remain visible. Action by Service Delivery Support Team - Families & Child Care Portfolio will prevent any future use of that CRN.

Referral for other issues:

Other CRN issues to refer to the Families and Child Care helpdesk include:

  • pre-adoptive care
  • child names
  • aliases
  • privacy issues
