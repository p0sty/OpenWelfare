Customer contact details and pre-claim process + Read more ...

Claims lodged in Australia may:

  • already be indexed and scanned to record, or
  • lodged online and found within Process Direct

Residence will generally be coded and identity confirmed for these claims.

Agreement claims lodged inside Australia cannot be processed in Process Direct and will need to be indexed in Customer Record (CR). Claims lodged from outside Australia will need to be indexed in Customer Record.

Before indexing a claim:

  • Link or unlink customer and partner records if required
  • Update all contact details. For example, address, contact phone numbers including mobiles, email address
  • If customer has previously been subscribed to Electronic Messaging and staff are transferring them to Environment I, check Electronic Messaging and Online Letters (EMOL) screen in Customer First and unsubscribe customer if necessary
  • Check the customer is in the correct Environment and transfer (ICOA) if necessary. See Inter-environment change of address (ICoA) transfer of a customer record using Customer First or Customer Record.
  • If current income and assets information is provided with the claim, update income and assets if the customer has a partner and the partner is receiving payment
  • Classify any debt shell generated by the income and assets update to International Services .ZALL_INTL_IA_DEBT work item
    • Record the debt investigation details using the Fast Note, select Auto text option > International Services > Debt > select from options: Autocompleted SBO Debts, External Debt Referral (XWA) and Internal Debt Referral (UKD)
  • Make sure any person nominated as a Person Permitted to Enquire (PPE) in the claim form is coded on the customer record. See Person Permitted to Enquire (PPE) authority
  • Run the Nominee Action script or Nominee Action Guided Procedure if customer has provided nominee forms (AUS221/SS313). See Appointing nominees for more details if needed

Index the claim (if required), ensuring to add the appropriate keywords below. See Indexing a customer's claim on the system.

If a PD claim exists, this must be placed on hold while the claim is being processed in CR. This will make sure that the customer can still see the claim and its progress through their online account. Claims in PD are set to 'Completed' when the CR claim is finalised. See the Resources page for Claim status per programme

All agreement claims must have the below keywords added in Customer First/Customer Record.

Inside Australia keywords:

  • Choose payment type @AGE or @DSP or @CAR
  • Two letter country code
  • CLMDOM

Outside Australia Keywords

  • Choose payment type @AGE or @DSP or @CAR
  • Two letter country code
  • CLMINT

Note: when indexing claims lodged from outside Australia the Date of Receipt must be the date the claim was received in Australia.

Payment Destination Overseas (PAPO) screen including Special Banking Option (SBO) + Read more ...

The PAPO screen is used to record bank account details from outside Australia. Service Officers should always run the Payment Destination Update - Record Overseas Direct Deposit Details script to update this screen.

If the script is unavailable, PAPO may be coded manually after checking the country's banking requirements in Overseas Bank Account Details (OBAD). For more details, see Delivery of payments to Centrelink customers outside Australia.

Note: if the claim is to be rejected, for any reason, code the PAPO screen with:

  • 'CHQ' in Method of Payment field
  • 'NAC' in DCR Exempt Rsn field

This is to avoid customer payments being rejected if the claim is granted in the future with out-of-date overseas bank account details. Rejected payments will cause delays in payment delivery to the customer as well as cause potential financial hardship.

Note: there are mandatory coding requirements for US bank accounts. If the HOM address format is not correct, the payment will reject. See United States in the Overseas Bank Account Details (OBAD) table for mandatory coding and more action if needed.

Payments made to New Zealand + Read more ...

Customers paid into a bank account in New Zealand have two options available to them and the Embargo (EMB) may need to be coded.

The NZ authorities offer customers two methods of payment:

  • the Special Banking Option (SBO), which is a bank account in the customer's name, managed by the NZ authorities, and
  • the Direct Payment Method (DPM), which is a regular account, owned and managed by the customer

If a customer elects to be paid by SBO, pension payments from other countries, including Australia, are deposited into the SBO account (BSB 031530). The NZ authorities then pay the customer their full NZ entitlement directly into their 'regular' bank account. Note: 030595 is an old SBO BSB that is still valid for existing customers but should not be used for new customers. If this BSB is provided, MSD should be contacted for confirmation before proceeding.

SBO is coded using the Payment Destination Update - Record Overseas Direct Deposit Details script and can only be used if the SBO paperwork accompanies the claim. See:

  • New Zealand Agreement and foreign pension information
  • International Social Security Agreements

Disability Support Pension (DSP) claims inside Australia + Read more ...

Claims for DSP under agreements made inside Australia follow the domestic DSP streaming and medical assessment process, i.e. MAT assessment, then referral to Job Capacity Assessment (JCA) and Disability Medical Assessment (DMA) if appropriate. Most agreement claims then need a severely disabled assessment.

Once lodged and indexed, DSP claims are assessed through a streaming process. The streaming process determines if the claim can be manifestly granted, manifestly rejected, or needs a referral for a Job Capacity Assessment (JCA). It also identifies if there is any more details are needed to process the claim.

In most cases, the JCA/DMA assessment has been done before the claim is assessed by CIS. If CIS need to refer for a JCA, make sure identity is confirmed and all non-medical information is received and coded, including:

  • compensation clearances
  • Complex Assessment Officer (CAO) assessments, and
  • Member of a Couple assessment (MOCA)

JCA referrals for agreement claims must include the decision about where the CITW occurred. See Job Capacity Assessment (JCA) Referral.

Refer for JCA as per the MAT notes on the record, i.e. file assessment, phone assessment or F2F assessment.

Agreement claims requiring a severely disabled assessment will need the following notes added to the JCA referral:

  • 'Severely Disabled' assessment is needed for DSP under New Zealand Agreement. A work capacity of 0-7 hpw is needed for DSP qualification under the agreement. Contact referring officer if more details are needed

Note: text can be changed for other agreements if appropriate.

Follow the process in Assessing Disability Support Pension (DSP) claims until there is sufficient medical evidence to make a decision.

Manifest eligibility and severely disabled assessments + Read more ...

Customers who are manifestly eligible for DSP under the current rules are considered to be severely disabled without further review. See Granting Disability Support Pension (DSP) (manifest).

International Services (CIS) Service Officers are responsible for manifest decisions for agreement DSP claims lodged inside Australia and for DSP claims lodged outside Australia.

CIS specialist Disability Officers (IDOs) can be consulted for help with making a manifest decision. However, any appeal about a manifest decision is the responsibility of the claim processing team.

Coding the Manifest Code field on the Medical Conditions Details (MC) screen with the following will automatically update the Severely Disabled Code field on the Pension Disability Information (PDI) screen to 'Yes':

  • 'BLI' - permanently blind
  • 'INT' - intellectual disability
  • 'NHM' - nursing home level care
  • 'TPI' - receiving a Department of Veterans' Affairs (DVA) Disability Compensation Payment at the Special Rate (Totally and Permanently Incapacitated (TPI))
  • 'TRM' - terminal illness

Note: refer all potential manifest cases for Category 4 HIV/AIDS to the Health Professional Advisory Unit (HPAU) for consideration.

Is the customer manifestly eligible for DSP?

  • Yes:
    • The customer is taken to be severely disabled without further review
    • Make sure all details are correctly coded on the MC and PDI screens
    • See Step 1 of the Coding and finalising claims under an international agreement table
  • No:
    • An IDO has made a severely disabled decision, see Step 1 in Table 3
    • An IDO has not made a severely disabled decision, an assessment is needed. Refer to IDO. Run the International DOC Creation Script in Customer Record and select 'WON - DSP refer to IDO for medical decision'. Put the claim on hold for 14 days. If the claim is from outside Australia delete the inside keyword and replace with 'outside'. Procedure ends here

  • the customer lodged their FP claim before the AU claim was lodged (including early and deemed claim lodgement date), and

International specific coding - as needed

Foreign Insurance/Contribution Totalisation (FICT) screen + Read more ...

The residence qualification rules, including the totalisation rules for all agreement countries, are automated.

If totalisation is needed, the information used by the system for most countries is provided on the FICT screen. The values used by the system in the totalisation calculation are those shown in the Total Insurance and Useable Insurance for Totalisation fields, with the summary result being shown on the Totalisation Summary (TOS) screen.

If a customer fails to totalise, use the TOS screen to work out why.

The FICT screen is coded as follows:

  • Country: agreement country
  • Manual override: this field is only needed when the system outcome is incorrect, for instance when a decision has been set aside on appeal
  • Date From:/Date To: record the Insurance Periods (IP) provided
  • Years/Months/Weeks/Days: record the Insurance Values (IV) provided for the relevant IP. If only Days is provided by the agreement partner, convert to months by dividing by 30 and record it in the Months field
  • C/O?: Y if the customer's contributions have been refunded ('cashed out')
  • Press [Enter] if a new row is needed

Note:

  • New Zealand Agreement: the system uses information from the Country of Residence (CRES) and Legal Residence Details (RSLEG) screens in the totalisation calculation. This can be found on the New Zealand and India (NZI) screen instead of TOS. There is no need to code any IP/IV, though if needed the Manual override field may be used
  • Indian Agreement: AWLR for totalisation can be found on the New Zealand and India (NZI) screen instead of TOS. IP/IV will still need to be coded on the FICT screen for NZI to summarise correctly
  • System processing applies totalisation rules based on the agreement country as coded on the ARD screen. A customer cannot use periods of coverage in one country to totalise under another agreement

See International Social Security Agreements.

Embargo (EMB) screen - New Zealand only + Read more ...

Australian income support payments are directly deducted by NZ for mutual customers who are present long-term in NZ. Any Australian arrears period results in a debt being owed by the customer to NZ. To divert these arrears into a holding account to offset this debt, code the EMB screen.

If a claimant has requested their Australian payments be paid into their own account by the Direct Payment Method (DPM), the arrears from the Australian claim must be embargoed (within the new claim activity). There is no way to retrieve and embargo arrears if they are accidentally released to the customer. If this occurs, advise New Zealand in the Service Decision liaison that is sent when the claim is finalised. See Agreement liaisons, NZ CICs and exchange of information.

The EMB screen is coded as follows:

  • Embargo country: NZ
  • Apply Embargo Y/N: Y (defaults to 'N') before finalising the claim

If a customer is paid by SBO, the Australian arrears do not need to be embargoed. The ODR DOC that is created by the Agreement Pension Qualification Script must be changed however to reflect that the arrears have been paid into a SBO account. Instead of 'all arrears released to the customer', change this sentence to read 'all arrears released to customer's SBO account'

Income and assets coding + Read more ...

Make sure all income and assets from the claim have been coded. See:

  • Assessing and coding modules and forms
  • Coding income and assets for Centrelink payments and services
  • Foreign income and assets
  • Recording and correcting employment income details
  • Determining the Date of Event for employment income

Notes:

  • Coding of income and assets is not needed if the claim is to be rejected - FRC, or agreement specific reason (AGS, AGL, AGT) and the partner, where one exists, is not receiving, or is not going to receive, income support
  • For customers who have arrived from New Zealand and are receiving a defined NZ benefit, code an Assumed Rate on FIPS if granting or if partner, where one exists, is in receipt of income support. See New Zealand Agreement and foreign pension information

For customers and/or partners who are paid foreign employment income:

  • Foreign employment income is coded on the Foreign Employment Income Paid Details (FEIP) screen with the date of event as the date the employment income was paid by the employer
    • To code a new instance of foreign employment income, key 'FEIP' in Nxt: and press [Enter]
    • To change an existing occurrence, key a P next to the EAN entry on FIPS and press [Enter]
  • Frequency can be DAY, 1WE, 2WE, MTE or LOP. See Foreign income and assets
  • If there is foreign employment income to be coded before 7 December 2020 (including where the entitlement period commenced within the Changing the Income Assessment Model transition period from 24 November 2020), go to the Foreign Income/Assets Details (FID) screen
  • Code at least two entries for the first month:
    • code a Date of Event (DOV) for the earnings start date (Date of Event)/Start day, and
    • an additional, earlier, page for the first currency revaluation date in that month.
      For example, if a customer's start day is 14 September a page for the September revaluation date, (1 September) and a page for 14 September must be included. If this is not done, the income will be ignored until the following revaluation date

For customers and/or partners who are paid domestic (Australian) employment income:

  • Domestic employment income is coded on the Employment Income Paid Details (EAPP) screen with the date of event as the date the employment income was paid by the employer
  • 1WE or 2WE is to be coded if the customer is in Environment I with an overseas address and the customer and/or partner are paid the same amount weekly or fortnightly
  • MTE is to be coded for pension customers who meet the Pension Monthly Exception Rule
  • IOP or LOP is to be coded for all other domestic employment income. For coding assistance, including domestic employment income earned prior to 7 December 2020 See Recording and correcting employment income details

Code manual rate + Read more ...

Manual rate calculations will be required when customer and partner are paid under different agreements. See Non NSS systems coding for manual rate of payment.

Valid reason codes for agreement claims are:

  • AGI Agreement pensions income test
  • AGA Agreement pensions asset test

The following steps may help by getting the system to work out the rate to code on the Manual Assessment (MAS) screen:

  • Ensure all income and assets are coded for each member of the couple,
  • Go to the Additional Residence Details (ARD) screen and put both members of the couple on the customers agreement,
  • Go to AR and take note of the customers rate,
  • Go to ARD and put both members of the couple on the partners agreement,
  • Go to AR and take note of the partners rate,
  • Return to ARD and put each member of the couple on the correct agreement,

Finalise the new claim activity + Read more ...

Go to the Assessment Results (AR) screen and make sure the correct claim decision is showing.

If the claim is to be rejected, make sure that the rejection reason is correct.

Agreement claim rules are generally automated based on information coded:

  • Agreement Scope (AGS) - where the customer does not meet the scope of the agreement. That is, the payment is not covered by the agreement or the customer does not meet a payment specific rule. For example, not severely disabled for DSP claims or not the carer partner of an Australian pensioner for Carer Payment
  • Agreement Lodgement (AGL) - where the customer does not meet the lodgement rules under the agreement
  • Agreement Totalisation (AGT) - where the customer cannot meet the residence rules under the agreement using totalisation

For help with rejections for non-agreement reasons, see Rejecting a Centrelink claim.

Contact the customer to advise of the rejection and appeal rights. Two genuine attempts must be made. Contact is not needed for claims rejected 'Failed to respond to correspondence (FRC)' or 'Failed to supply documents (FSD)'. See Advising verbally of an unfavourable decision (CLK).

If the claim is to be granted, check the Pension Rate Calculation (PRC) screen to make sure the correct arrears are being paid for the entire arrears period, noting any rate calculation changes due to absences/returns to Australia are correct.

Check the following before finalising the claim and issuing the arrears:

For all customers:

  • Check CRES, RSCD, ARD, RSIMME, RSLEG
    • NZ Residence Status (RSLEG) may be incorrect if customer has more than 80 movements returned by the immigration datalink. Correct the status if necessary. See Australian Residence Rules for New Zealand citizens
    • If customer is travelling on a NZ passport and NZ citizenship is coded on RSLEG make sure the correct country of birth is coded on RSIMME and CRES. Customers born in Cook Islands, Niue and Tokelau may be NZ citizens but are not automatically residents of NZ and this can affect qualification.

For overseas residents:

  • Check that the rate for entire arrears period is
    • The correct AWLR/AWAR
    • The correct denominator (for example, 300,420, 528, 540)

For Australian residents:

  • Check that the rate for entire arrears period is the Direct Deduction rate/Comparison rate
  • Check RSS to confirm the rate is correct where the customer has travelled to/from Australia within the claim period
  • Check PNA to confirm the arrears start from the correct date
  • If the system is paying a direct deduction rate (customer residing in AU) and it should be a proportional rate because the customer lives overseas:
    • Check that RSCD has been coded with the last departure, no matter how long ago it was
    • Check Immigration limiting Date (ILD) and update to last departure before PNA date if needed

If the claimant has died after lodging the claim, arrears may need to be manually zeroed from the appropriate date and a specific manual grant letter sent to the surviving partner or to the customer's estate. See Table 4.

Inhibiting auto advice

In most cases, international specific letters must be manually issued and the automatic letter inhibited before the activity is completed. The only time automatic letters should be issued is:

  • for grants of all agreement claims inside Australia (except if the customer dies during the arrears period)

In all other cases, a manual letter will be needed. Navigate to the Assessment Consequences (ASC) screen and code the Let Act field with 'INH' to inhibit the automatic advice.

Liaison

Liaisons are usually generated by the Foreign Pension system by FGL with Australian residence periods and rate information (current and some historical) auto-populating. New Zealand uses Autonomy Process Automation (APA). However, FGL will still need to be coded manually to generate the ODR DOC for New Zealand liaisons.

A liaison must be sent to the agreement partner advising of the outcome of the claim in the following circumstances:

  • All claims lodged outside Australia. This includes claims lodged direct,
  • Claims lodged inside Australia under the New Zealand Agreement that are rejected and the customer receives a defined NZ benefit (NZS, NZI, NZV)
  • If the claim has been rejected because the customer cannot totalise, but is in receipt of a defined NZ benefit, a dummy AU notional rate must be provided in the liaison to New Zealand

If a claim has been lodged direct and the agreement partner is not known, send the liaison to the default fund.

Australian historical rates are not provided to New Zealand when a customer is granted. See New Zealand Agreement and foreign pension information.

All liaisons must be recorded on FGL to make sure an ODR DOC is created and they must also be uploaded to the customer's record by DOC Tools.
