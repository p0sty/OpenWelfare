Change of care scenarios

Table 1

Change of care

Description

Change of care where the customer is fleeing a family and domestic violence situation

Muriel flees a family and domestic violence situation and presents at the local women's refuge with child Amelia. Muriel has recently fled from the ex-partner who was receiving FTB for Amelia.

Muriel lodges a claim for FTB for Amelia, supported by a letter from the women's refuge that Muriel is caring for Amelia at that point in time. Muriel is concerned that contacting the ex-partner to confirm the change of care may place them at risk of harm.

The Service Officer needs to be satisfied there has been a change in care and that Amelia is now in Muriel's care. The Service Officer may need to seek other forms of verification that the change of care has occurred. This may require social worker involvement or Muriel may be requested to provide additional evidence. The letter from the women's refuge can be used as part of the evidence to support the change of care.

Ultimately the Service Officer needs to be satisfied that a change of care has occurred based on available evidence, without confirming with the ex-partner.

Change of care - supported by an interim court order

On 20 December 2013, Byron requested FTB due to now caring for 2 children. Byron's ex-partner Phoebe was contacted to confirm the details. Phoebe contested that the children were now in Byron's full-time care and claimed that they were only on an access visit.

Byron was able to produce an interim court order dated 18 December 2013 which stated the children were to reside with Byron from 18 December 2013 until further orders were issued.

Phoebe stated the matter was due to go back to court in January 2014 and intends to fight to get the children back and explains this absence is only temporary.

However, as the change of care is fully supported by the interim order dated 18 December 2013, Byron can be paid from the date of the order (18 December 2013) and Phoebe's payments for the children must be cancelled from this date.

No change of care - temporary visit during school holidays

Moira is a single parent who has the full care of 2 children. The children go to stay with their grandparents during the Christmas school holidays for 6 weeks and the grandparents lodge a claim for FTB.

Moira contested the change stating the children were only away temporarily and shows financial assistance was provided to the grandparents to care for the children during their visit. Moira also provided the children with pocket money, clothes and toiletries, etc. Moira regularly phones the children during their holiday and is consulted regarding the children's medical needs and activities.

On the basis of the information provided, it is decided that the children remain in Moira's care during their holiday, and the grandparent's FTB claim is rejected.

Child entered customer care

An additional child claim for FTB is received from Rosemary on 10 August for child Yon. Rosemary claims care for Yon and states the child has now left the care of Margaret.

The Service Officer calls Margaret, who is currently receiving FTB for Yon. Margaret disagrees that Yon has already left and confirms Yon is only leaving Margaret's care on the following Saturday, 15 August.

Child Support then calls Rosemary who agrees with Margaret's statement explaining that a mistake was made when writing the dates. Rosemary accidentally wrote the date claimed as the date of the change in care.

On Margaret's record, Yon is recorded as left care from 15 August. This cancels Margaret's FTB for Yon. Rosemary's claim is then processed and Yon is recorded in Rosemary's care from 15 August. Child Support also records a DOC on each customer's record and includes details of the contact with each person.

Correct date of effect for change of care

Anne and John have 1 child, Kevin, and are separated. Kevin enters Anne's care from 2 September 2013. John is sent letters and has been called several times (to check if Kevin has in fact left John's care). John's FTB is cancelled DPT + 1 on 9 October 2013 after no reply contact.

Anne is asked to supply evidence to prove that Kevin has entered Anne's care. Anne supplies evidence of the change in care on 13 October 2013. As evidence has been provided, a care decision can be made.

John's record is updated that Kevin left care on 2 September 2013 and to ensure correct processing, John's FTB must be restored as part of this activity and a debt will be raised for the FTB paid from 2 September to 9 October.

Anne's FTB must be updated using 2 September 2013 as the date that Kevin entered care, resulting in an arrears payment.

Change of care where the child is placed in formal foster care

Foster carer Jane has had care for child Billy since placed by the local welfare agency/state authority.

Jane lodges a claim for FTB for Billy. As part of the claim, Jane provides a letter from the welfare agency/state authority confirming that there has been a formal change in care of Billy and the court has placed Billy in care of the Minister. The letter outlines that Billy has been placed in Jane's care by the welfare agency/state authority under a foster care arrangement and advises the date this occurred.

If the Service Officer is satisfied based on the letter that a change of care has occurred, no further evidence should be required.

Child arrested - returned to parents care before court appearance

Jessie has been arrested on a Friday evening and placed in the local watch house for the weekend before being arraigned.

Once arraigned, Jessie is returned to parent Michelle's care before the court appearance.

Until Jessie has been remanded into the custody of a Youth Justice Centre, Jessie is still the legal responsibility of parent Michelle. Michelle retains eligibility for FTB.

Child arrested - sent to Youth Justice Centre before court appearance

Fiona has been arrested on a Friday evening and placed in the local watch house for the weekend before being arrested.

Once arraigned, Fiona is sent to a Youth Justice Centre while awaiting the court date.

As Fiona has been remanded into the custody of a Youth Justice Centre, Fiona becomes the legal responsibility of the Youth Justice Centre from the date Fiona is remanded.

FTB for Fiona is to be cancelled from the date Fiona was remanded.

Child removed from care by a state child protective service

Ashley has been removed from Vicky's care for 3 weeks by a state child protective service. Care of Ashley now sits with the relevant State Department.

This change is not considered a temporary absence as legal responsibility now belongs to the relevant state department.

Vicky's FTB for Fiona is to be cancelled from the date Ashley was removed from care.

Child placed in to care by parent for less than 4 weeks

Toni calls to advise they have placed their child Jill in to care with the Smith Family, an Approved Care Organisation (ACO), for 2 weeks while they are recuperating in hospital. As the care is only temporary and informal, Toni is able to retain their FTB entitlement.

Temporary care becomes permanent - advised before change in care

Andy calls to advise they have placed their child Scott into care with the Smith Family (an ACO) for 25 days while they are recuperating. As the care is less than 4 weeks and informal, a determination is made that temporary care provisions can apply.

On day 18, Andy calls to advise the care has become permanent. Scott is coded out of the parents' care from that day, as this was the first day it became permanent.

Temporary care becomes permanent - advised before change of care became indefinite

Jodie calls to advise their child Miguel is staying with grandparents for the next 3 weeks. As the care is less than 4 weeks and informal, a determination is made that temporary care provisions can apply.

Two weeks later, Jodie calls to advise that Miguel will now stay with the grandparents indefinitely. Miguel is coded out of the parents' care from the day the care became indefinite.

Temporary care becomes permanent - advised after change in care

Billie calls to advise their child Harrison has been living with grandparents for the last 6 weeks. Initially Harrison was only supposed to be with the grandparents for 2 weeks, however decided to stay permanently.

As there is no evidence to support the first 2 weeks were temporary care and Billie has advised after Harrison had permanently left, Harrison is coded out of Billie’s care from the day the child left.

Temporary care becomes permanent - advised after change in care

Wendy calls to advise that child Kimmy was removed from Wendy's care 4 weeks ago. Initially Kimmy was only supposed to be out of their parent's care for 2 weeks, however a court decision was handed down to remove the child for a longer term.

Initially Wendy was issued a letter to support the first 2 weeks as being temporary care, and although Wendy has advised after Kimmy was removed long term, temporary care provisions can be applied for the 2 weeks as this is supported by the evidence. Kimmy is coded out of Wendy’s care from the day the care became long term.

P24845 problem and workaround

The P24845 system issue may impact in the following scenarios:

  • Where a step parent is claiming FTB that has been transferred to them from a biological parent, who is also a mutual Child Support Customer, and the other biological parent also becomes entitled to receive FTB for any level of care
  • Where the biological parent transfers their FTB to a step parent and they later separate. Care details for the child will not be transferred correctly to Child Support if a past period care refresh is requested
  • The care may transfer to the step parent's child support record (with incorrect child relationship details) causing inaccurate child support assessments and impacting multiple parties

As a result, where FTB is transferred out to partner ('TOP' is keyed on the Child Override/Claim (CHOC) screen - from parent to step parent) and the customer is a Centrelink and Child Support mutual customer, these records are to be referred for investigation. See the Process page of Notification and assessment of shared care arrangements for Family Tax Benefit (FTB) for further information on workaround and text for Display on Access (DOA) DOCs.

Use this letter to advise carers of a shared care decision based on evidence provided by 1 of the carers only. That is, where the other carer has not responded to a request for evidence.

SSA Shared Care Principal Carer Determination

This letter must be sent to all carers in a shared care arrangement, where at least 1 carer is claiming/receiving an income support payment. The letter advises which carer is determined as the principal carer of the child/children.

This letter must be sent to members of a couple advising which parent is determined as the principal carer of the child/children.

Your care arrangement - Request for information

Use this letter to request information and/or evidence about care arrangements from any carer.

This can also be used for shared care dispute cases.
