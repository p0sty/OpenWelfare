This is a Family and Domestic Violence Interaction Point. If the customer is with another person, on speaker phone, or already identified with family and domestic violence concerns, continue with the current business conversation. Otherwise, go to the Family and domestic violence procedure to conduct the risk identification and referral process.

The Department of Social Services (DSS) have paused all Moving to an Area of Lower Employment Prospects (MALEP) assessments. Do not undertake any MALEP assessments.

This document outlines the procedure for customers who change their address details. For some payments, a change of address can affect a customer's rate and qualification for payment.

Change of address due to separation, including where the customer is at risk of family and domestic violence

There are different processes to follow, depending on whether the customer is at risk of family and domestic violence, when updating an address where the customer is advising of a change of address due to separation from their partner.

In all cases, care must be taken when updating the customer's address to ensure updates do not flow onto their former partner's record. All address types and contact details should be reviewed when making updates to make sure current details are up to date.

If a customer indicates they and/or their children are at risk of family and domestic violence and are concerned for their safety, their relationship status can be updated immediately and the Separation details (MOD S) form and/or verification supplied within 14 days.

For more information and updating instructions, see Change in relationship status from partnered to single.

Mail cannot be sent to a future address

For correspondence to be sent, Services Australia must be given an address where a customer can receive mail.

If a customer is advising of a future change of address, make sure they are aware that mail will not be sent to that address until after the future start date.

To help keep customer’s information secure, tell the customer that they may need to give us a postal address to send mail to until that future date. For further information on postal address options available, see Where customers are unable to provide a residential address.

Change of details

Customers should be advised that information used for their Centrelink, Child Support and Medicare payments and services is transferred within the agency. However, additional contact with one of the agency's programs separately may be required to update relevant information, depending on individual circumstances.

A customer's address (including postal address) and telephone details (including mobile phone numbers) must be checked at every contact, and updated if required.

If a customer has been selected for a Rent Assistance (RA) Profile Review, address updates must be done as part of the review. While a RA Profile Review is outstanding, customers and staff will be blocked from updating the address outside the review.

Where the customer has verified their identity via Interactive Voice Response (IVR), address details do not need to be confirmed.

The customer can view/update address, accommodation or contact details online.

Note: the home address on the system will not be changed just to issue a Health Care Card (HCC) or Low Income Health Care Card (LIC) with a different address (for example, term address for students). Customers can nominate a preference as to which address should be displayed on the HCC or LIC. See Preferences for concession cards for more information.

Change of postal address only

If the address change is a change of postal address only, entitlement will not be affected. All mail will go to the postal address. End dating a postal address using yesterday's date makes sure any future outgoing correspondence is sent to the correct address.

Sharing accommodation

If a person is sharing accommodation with another person who is not a family member:

  • The Living Arrangements question set needs to be asked, and
  • Request sharer details through a Rent Certificate (SU523) form using Request Documents. See Requesting information (CLK). Note: change of address details for dates in the future are unable to be coded for sharers. Advise these customers to re-contact once they have physically moved address

Change of address for customers assessed as separated under one roof

Two people who were previously partnered may be living separately and apart on a permanent or indefinite basis but continue to live under the same roof. This may be a temporary or permanent arrangement.

If a customer currently assessed as separated under one roof advises they have now moved out of the shared residence, the customer's record must be updated and the 'SUR' Relationship Qualifier Code must be ended on the Marital Status (MS) screen. For more information on how to end the 'SUR' qualifier code, see Member of a Couple (MoC) and Separated under one roof (SUOR) assessments.

Circumstances that may affect entitlement to all payments

  • Entitlement to Rent Assistance (RA) may change. For example, now sharing accommodation, purchased a home, under 25 and now living with parents or moved temporarily
  • If the customer is in receipt of Disability Support Pension (DSP) (Blind) or Age Pension (Blind) they must disclose details of their income and assets to be paid RA
  • Entitlement to Remote Area Allowance (RAA) may change
  • Relationship status changes. For example, separation, becoming partnered, illness separated
  • Dependent child changes due to separation or becoming partnered
  • A change in income and/or assets from real estate. For example, a homeowner becoming a non-homeowner and their previous home becoming an asset
  • Customer receiving Essential Medical Equipment Payment (EMEP) are required to reclaim for the new address

Essential Medical Equipment Payment (EMEP)

When the person receiving EMEP (or the equipment user they care for) changes address, the system will auto cancel their EMEP payment. This is due to EMEP being paid in respect of medical equipment and/or medically required heating/cooling being run at a specific address. When a person moves and continues to use that medical equipment at the new address, they must lodge a new claim for the EMEP.

The cancellation of EMEP must be documented with the change of address.

No home address or temporary/unstable accommodation

If the customer has indicated they no longer have a home address or that their new accommodation is temporary or unstable, consider recording a homelessness indicator.

For correspondence to be sent, Services Australia must be provided with an address where a customer can receive mail. Before updating a customer's residential address to no fixed address, staff must ensure an alternate postal address has been provided. See Where customers are unable to provide a residential address.

Non-current customers

As the customer is not current, accommodation information and telephone subscriber information is not required.

Note: in the situation where the non-current customer is a care receiver and they have had a change of address, eligibility for Carer Payment (CP) and/or Carer Allowance (CA) for the carer needs to be reviewed. See Change of address for Carer Payment (CP) and Carer Allowance (CA) for more information.

Current customers who cannot access Rent Assistance (RA)

Customer's receiving a Low Income Health Care Card (LIC), a Commonwealth Seniors Health Card (CSHC) or Child Care Subsidy (CCS) are deemed current customers. If the customer, and partner if applicable, are only in receipt of a LIC, CSHC or CCS, the customer's rent details (the Accommodation Details (AC) screen) do not need to be coded, as the customer cannot access RA.

Job seekers

Circumstances that may affect customer's entitlements include:

  • Moving to an area of lower employment prospects. Services Australia must determine if the job seeker has reduced their employment prospects by moving to a new location. If this is the case, the Service Officer should investigate a possible Moving to an Area of Lower Employment Prospects (MALEP) employment related exclusion period
  • Single customers under 25 years old, without dependent children, who return to live with a parent, do not qualify for Rent Assistance (RA)

Job seekers are able to give their Employment Services Provider their new address details. The Employment Services Provider will advise the job seeker they will need to contact Services Australia to provide their updated home address. When the job seeker contacts Services Australia, the Service Officer will need to confirm their home address details and determine if there are any potential impacts on eligibility and/or ongoing entitlement.

Note: when a temporary address is recorded, job seekers will remain connected to the same Employment Services Provider and will be required to comply with their mutual obligation requirements.

For more information, see Job seeker change of address.

ABSTUDY and Youth Allowance (YA)

If a customer is the applicant for an ABSTUDY student (for example, for Schooling A payments), or they act as a nominee for another person, their details will need to be updated on that customer's record as well.

Circumstances that may affect customer's entitlements include:

  • At home or accommodated independent person if living with parents
  • Away from home rate if required to live away from home for the purposes of engaging in study, training, employment, etc. for secondary and tertiary students
  • Independence on the grounds of state care. Independent customers that return to live in the parental home will be required to have their independent status reviewed. For more information, see Returning to the parental home when independent or qualified for the away from home rate of Youth Allowance (YA) or Returning to the parental home when in receipt of the away from home rate of ABSTUDY
  • Independence because it is unreasonable for the customer to live at the home of either parent; rate changes associated with leaving or returning to the parental home. Claims for the unreasonable to live at home (UTLAH) rate must be investigated by a social worker
  • Fares Allowance entitlement, if required to live away from home to study
  • Moving to an Area of Lower Employment Prospects (MALEP) - this can result in the application of a payment preclusion period

Self service

Customers contacting to provide a change of address are required to complete the transaction through their online account or Express Plus App if capable of self-management.

See Accessing and using Centrelink self service and Update address, accommodation or contact details online for more information.

Customers must be referred to self service to complete their updates if:

  • they receive payment for:
    • Austudy
    • Trainees
    • Australian Apprentice
    • Family Tax Benefit (FTB)
    • Parental Leave Pay
    • Dad and Partner Pay (DAP)
    • Child Care Subsidy (CCS)
    • JobSeeker payment (JSP)
    • Parenting Payment (PP) as a single or partnered customer
    • Youth Allowance (YA) (full time students)
    • Youth Allowance (YA) (job seeker), and
  • they are not excluded from using self service for change of address transactions

Unless:

  • self service channels are currently unavailable (confirmed by NNU, Newsflash and Bulletins), or
  • the Service Officer determines the customer is not capable of self-management in this instance

Note: if a customer is selected for a Rent Assistance (RA) Profile review, address updates must be done as part of the review. While an RA Profile review is outstanding, customers and staff will be blocked from updating an address outside the review.

Carer

There are specific qualification criteria in relation to where the carer and care receiver are living. When a carer or care receiver notifies a change of address, continued eligibility for payment needs to be confirmed. See Change of address for Carer Payment (CP) and Carer Allowance (CA) for more information.

Note: accommodation details (for example, rent paid or ownership) are not required if the carer is receiving CA only.

Disability Support Pension (DSP)

  • If the customer moves in with their carer, their principal home may remain an exempt asset for up to 2 years
  • They are exempt from the sharers' rate of Rent Assistance (RA)
  • If they are under 21 (receiving Youth Disability Support Pension (DSP)), the rate of payment will vary depending on whether they are living with their parents or independently
  • If the customer lives in disability accommodation this must be correctly recorded on their record
  • If the customer is in receipt of DSP (Blind) they must disclose details of their income and assets to be paid RA

Customers with outstanding debts

Customers who have an outstanding debt, have a legal obligation to notify Services Australia of any change of address within 14 days while a debt is still owing, even if they are no longer receiving a payment.

Family Tax Benefit (FTB) customers with debts stopped for manual intervention

Customers who have no current address or myGov inbox subscription will have the following stopped for manual intervention:

  • FTB reconciliation
  • lump sum claim
  • non-lodger activities
  • Newborn Upfront Payment (NBU) debt activities

See Manual intervention into family assistance reconciliation.

Income managed customers

When an income managed customer notifies a change in circumstances, including a change of address or change in rent, a review of their priority needs and expense allocations is required. For more information, see Changes to Income Management priority needs.

Income managed customers who move out of a declared Income Management (IM) area

Customers:

  • whose payments are currently income managed and who move indefinitely or permanently outside of a declared IM area will continue to be income managed
  • who have been identified for IM and move out of a declared IM area before IM commences, or provide a retrospective change of address, may still have their payments income managed

For more information, see Customer moves out of an Income Management area.

Customers who move into a declared Income Management (IM) area

When a customer moves into a declared IM area, they may be profiled or referred for IM if they meet the criteria for an IM measure available in that location.

The location criteria for IM eligibility is based on a customer's permanent residential address (that is, Home or Term address). Therefore it is important to confirm the customer's move is permanent and not temporary to avoid them being incorrectly identified for IM. The Resources page contains a link to more information about declared IM areas.

Enhanced Income Management customers

Customers may be placed on enhanced IM if their home or term address is in an enhanced IM location and they meet other eligibility criteria. If customers are only in the area temporarily (for example, they are travelling, attending a funeral or medical appointment), their address must be coded as Temporary to avoid being placed on enhanced IM in error.

If a customer wishes to change and/or backdate their address details, evidence supporting the change may be required from the customer before ending participation on enhanced IM is considered. This is to prevent customers from changing or backdating their address details to avoid participation.

Customers who permanently move out of an enhanced IM location will end participation in the program after 13 weeks. Updating or deleting home or term address details purely for the purposes of avoiding enhanced IM will not automatically end a customer's participation. Address updates done incorrectly in an effort to avoid enhanced IM may result in placing the customer at risk of overpayment for additional payments such as Rent Assistance or Remote Area Allowance.

Address updates for customers paid outside of Australia

Some countries have specific address format requirements in order for a customer to be paid correctly to an overseas bank account. Refer to Overseas Bank Account Details (OBAD) for country task cards and coding requirements. Updating a home address incorrectly for customers paid to certain countries may cause their payment to reject. Care needs to be taken before updating address details for these overseas customers.

Third party address updates

Generally, addresses must not be changed solely on information provided by a third party. However, there are some circumstances where it may be appropriate to action a change of address, if:

  • the partner of a Services Australia customer advises a change of address for both themselves and their partner, and they have permission to enquire, the address may be changed. The identity of the person providing the update and marital circumstances must be verified prior to the change. Extra care must to be exercised in these cases and if not convinced of the accuracy, the address must not be changed before checking with the affected person
  • a customer is admitted to a nursing home and their partner or an authorised third party has advised the change of address into residential aged care. Once the information is clarified with the nursing home, the address may be changed, see Change of address to an aged care home

In all cases, the Service Officer needs to be satisfied the third party is identified correctly before updates are made.
