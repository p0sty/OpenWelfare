This page has information on how to process a Low Income Heath Care Card (LIC) renewal in Process Direct.

On this page:

Renewal claim lodgement

Renewal pre-claim processing

Processing online LIC renewal

Renewal claim lodgement

Table 1

Step

Action

1

LIC renewal + Read more ...

The preferred method of renewing a LIC is online, if the customer is deemed unable or unsuitable to complete an online claim, staff should offer to complete an Assisted Customer Claim (ACC) with the customer.

If the customer declines an ACC, offer a replacement renewal form.

If:

  • a processing officer receives a LIC renewal work item, see Step 1 in Table 2
  • the customer is contacting about completing the renewal online, see Important dates and periods for assessing eligibility for LIC renewals
  • the customer requests assistance in completing the renewal, see Viewing and processing online and Assisted Customer Claim (ACC), go to step 4
  • the customer is requesting a replacement form, go to Step 2
  • the customer is lodging a LIC renewal form, go to Step 3
  • If the customer is requesting urgent processing, go to Step 4

2

Customer requests a replacement form + Read more ...

Service Officers must promote online claiming or offer Assisted Customer Claim (ACC) initially.

Customers can request a replacement form if:

  • their LIC is still current, or
  • it is within 13 weeks of the previous card expiry date

Services Officers can create and print a LIC renewal form (SS054M) manually then issue it personally in the Service Centre or post it to the customers address. See Renewing Your Health Care Card (SS054m) in Resources.

Service Officers must check the following details are correct before manually printing the form:

  • customer's name
  • address
  • Customer Reference Number (CRN)
  • renewal period
  • return address and
  • date of issue

Procedure ends here.

3

Lodging a renewal form + Read more ...

Customers can lodge a Renewing your Health Care Card (SS054) form or submit a LIC renewal online.

Is the LIC still current or within 13 weeks of the previous card's expiry date?

  • Yes,
    • check the renewal form to make sure it is fully completed and signed
    • scan the SS054 and any supporting documents onto the customer's record, go to Step 4
  • No, customer must submit a new claim for LIC, see Claiming Low Income Health Care Card (LIC). Procedure ends here

4

Urgent processing + Read more ...

Is the renewal to be processed urgently?

  • Yes, see Immediate new claim and non-new claim priority processing, go to Step 1 in Table 2
  • No, tell the customer the renewal will be allocated to processing staff to complete. Procedure ends here

Renewal pre-claim processing

Table 2

Step

Action

1

Locate claim + Read more ...

Locate the work item in Process Direct.

Select the Work Item to go to the Customer and Claim Information table on the Transaction Summary (TS) screen to review and process the customer's claim.

Paper claims

When a Claim for a Health Care card renewal (SS054) is scanned to the customer’s record, a Social Application (SOA) shell work item is generated on the customer’s record. This has no claim information apart from:

  • the Customer Reference Number CRN)
  • personal detail, and
  • receipt date

Claim information will need to be manually added into the work item using information from the scanned claim and documents.

2

Claim status + Read more ...

The claim status must be In Process before it can be processed.

Is the claim status In Process?

  • Yes, go to Step 3
  • No, for claim status definitions and action to take, see Process Direct navigation, common screens and functions, go to Step 3

To reassess a previously rejected claim:

  • if rejected FSD or POI, see Request to reassess a rejected claim
  • to re-index the claim, refer to directions for SAP online claims in Indexing, re-indexing and cancelling claim activities

3

Review claim summary + Read more ...

  • Select to view the claim details provided by the customer
  • Select to expand the Claim Summary in a new window if desired
  • Review the Claim summary. Key details include:
    • relationship status
    • Australian residence
    • income details
  • Select Documents to view the documents provided by the customer (uploaded online or scanned to their record)

4

Review scanned documents + Read more ...

To view supporting documents:

  • select links at the bottom of the expanded Claim Summary, or
  • select from the bottom of the open Process Direct window, or
  • select the Documents icon

Check the date the document was scanned.

There may also be older documents not related to the claim.

Has all relevant supporting documents been provided?

  • Yes, go to step 6
  • No,
    • if additional documents are needed, go to step 5
    • if documents have been requested at the time of claim or after and not returned, the claim must be rejected. See Rejecting a Low Income Health Care Card (LIC) claim

5

Request additional documents or information + Read more ...

If additional information or clarification of claim or submitted documents is needed to determine the customers eligibility:

Make 1 genuine attempt to contact the customer verbally, see Step 5 in Table 1 of Requesting information (CLK):

  • Issue a pre-call notification (SMS only) between 8:00 am and 8:00 pm using the customer's local time to let them know a Service Officer will call them today from a private number, see Centrelink letters online and Electronic Messaging

If contact was successful:

  • record the details of the conversation in the Progress of claim note
  • if all information to assess the claim has now been provided, go to Step 6
  • if information is still needed
    • encourage the customer to upload the information via the requested task in the online claim so their claim can be processed as soon as possible, see Upload documents service
    • hold the claim for the time allowed to respond. Procedure ends here

If the contact was unsuccessful or it is not appropriate for the customer to provide the information or evidence verbally:

  • Issue a request for information via > Request Documents

Note: documents that have been requested at Next Step in the claim must not be requested again, the claim must be rejected. See Rejecting a Low Income Health Care Card (LIC) claim.

  • Procedure ends here

6

Relationship details + Read more ...

These details must be reviewed/updated before selecting Process.

On the TS screen:

  • Link Summary (LS) table shows linked records, for example
    • Partner and any Relationship Qualifier Code
    • Children
  • Marital Status (MS) table shows the customer's relationship details. For help with coding, see Process Direct navigation, common screens and functions

Compare relationship details provided in the claim with the details already recorded in the Marital Status (MS) table. If the customer has given different information about their relationships in the claim, to the confirmed data in the MS table:

  • attempt to contact the customer by phone and
  • confirm the correct relationship details and dates

If phone contact is unsuccessful, the relationship details supplied in the claim should be applied.

If updates have been made to relationship details the claim will need to be regenerated.

7

More action needed + Read more ...

To:

  • regenerate claim (if needed), go to Step 8
  • refer to a Complex Assessment Officer (CAO), go to Step 9
  • otherwise, go to Step 1 in Table 3

8

Regenerate claim + Read more ...

When needed, select > Regenerate Claim to regenerate the claim, excluding claims with a status of Approved.

See Step 1 in Table 3.

9

Complex Assessment Officer (CAO) referral + Read more ...

If the customer's financial circumstances are complex, for example, they include a trust or company, a CAO referral is needed to make sure the income test is met.

Select > Referral:

  • Select Referral Type, select 'Complex Assessment Officer'
  • Referral Reason, example 'Trusts'
  • KPI Date
  • Expected Start Date
  • check the box for each document provided as part of the CAO assessment
  • write any relevant notes in the Notes field
  • Have all necessary documents been provided, select 'Yes'
  • Select Finish

Note: the claim will now have a status of On Hold with reason Referred to CAO.

Procedure ends here.

Processing online LIC renewal

Table 3

Step

Action

1

Claim Processing screen + Read more ...

Select Process at bottom right of the claim to start coding and assess the claim. Do not start coding before selecting Process. This will result in errors.

The Errors (SWE) screen will display.

Before coding any screens:

  • Select Assess to send the claim details to ISIS.
  • The Entitlement (ELD) screen will display
  • Return to the SWE screen to complete the claim coding

Message Log

This section displays any errors with existing coding. These must be fixed before the claim can be assessed. For help, see Using Digital Assistant Roxy in Process Direct.

Task Selectors

These list common screens. Task selectors that have mandatory screens are pre-selected.

Task selectors may not list all screens that must be checked or coded. Check screens to compare historical details with the claim details.

A flag will display against all screens that have provisional claim data. These may not need to be accessed to process the claim.

  • Select screens to be checked or updated. There is an option to select all screens
  • Select Next or press [Enter] to go through the selected screens

Use Super Key to go to screens. Key 'screens' to view a full list.

Go to next step

2

Check eligibility + Read more ...

Based on the information available, is a Streamline Rejection needed?

  • Yes, action the Streamline Rejection. See Rejecting a Low Income Health Care Card (LIC) claim. Procedure ends here
  • No, go to Step 3

3

Assess and code the LIC claim + Read more ...

Check information provided in the claim (provisional data) is accurate and in line with any evidence provided, including for income and financial investments. See the Resources page for help in determining the right Date of Event.

Select all relevant Task Selectors and select Next:

  • Enter/update required information for each relevant screen
  • Check there is no conflict between the provisional data and confirmed data. Correct if necessary
  • Check Immigration Advised Movements (RSIM) screen to confirm the customer was in Australia at the time of lodgement
  • Check nominee information has not been incorrectly ended, for example Public Trust nominees

Is the customer eligible?

  • Yes, go to Step 4
  • No, reject the claim. Procedure ends here

4

Finalise the claim + Read more ...

After coding the claim and addressing the data validations, Assess will display:

  • Select Assess
  • The Errors (SWE) screen will list any validation errors to address
  • Once the Message Log is clear, select Assess
  • The ELD Entitlement screen displays the outcome. Make sure the outcome is correct for the customer (and partner if applicable)
  • Select Finish
  • Review the Claim Outcome DOC and make sure the text, Act reference and date are correct and meet the Getting it Right (GIR) Minimum Standards
  • Select Finalise
  • Select Finish. A box will confirm the claim has been successfully completed
