PPL employer debt raising and recovery

Table 1

Item

Description

1

Debt identification + Read more ...

Services Australia raise and recover PPL debts from employees and/or employers where an excess payment has occurred.

Third parties (individuals or organisations) may also have a debt raised against them for any PPL received in error. See Requesting refunds from third parties.

2

Determining who owes the debt + Read more ...

The Paid Parental Leave Employer Processing Team (PPLEPT) issue a Q318 letter to the employer. This confirms the location of the PPL funding amounts for the debt period.

Where exceptional circumstances have occurred, such as the death of the PPL claimant, Paid Parental Leave Employer Processing Team (PPLEPT) staff will:

  • make 2 genuine attempts to contact the employer, whilst maintaining the privacy and confidentiality of the customer
  • request the employer to urgently return the letter to minimise debt impacts
  • issue the Q318 letter

The Q318 letter asks:

  • what payments the claimant received
  • how much tax the claimant paid, and
  • has the tax been remitted to the Australian Taxation Office (ATO)

Employers must supply this information in writing within 14 days. Issue a new letter if the employer advises they made an error.

When the employer provides PPL, raise the debt against the person who has the money. If the employer:

  • has not yet paid the money to the employee, the debt is recoverable from the employer
  • has paid the money to the employee, the money is recoverable from the employee

3

Employer debts - how they may occur + Read more ...

Raise a debt against the employer when:

  • there was an advance of PPL for a period where PPL is no longer payable
  • they have failed to provide the PPL to the employee
  • they have not made the agency aware of a change in circumstances
  • the employer receives an incorrect amount of PPL as a result of an administrative error
  • the agency receives an outcome from the Fair Work Ombudsman (FWO)
  • there is a duplicate payment
  • the debt is due to fraud and/or misrepresentation, for example in relation to the Income Test

Note: raise the whole amount as a debt if the employer fails to provide information requested in the Q318.

4

Employer debts + Read more ...

Check how much employer debt is owed.

An employer is to repay any PPL still held by them. This includes any withheld Pay As You Go (PAYG) deductions which have not been remitted to the ATO.

5

Employer debts - how they are recovered + Read more ...

The agency has the following options for recovering a PPL debt from an employer:

  • cash repayments
  • garnishee
  • referral to an External Collection Agent (ECA)
  • legal proceedings (civil recovery)

See Recovering debts from organisations.

Note: PPL employers/organisations can make payments by electronic funds transfer as a last resort. See Garnishee of continuing income to recover debts.

6

Further notice of debt letters + Read more ...

A letter issues when:

  • a PPL employer debt remains outstanding for 28 days after initial notice of debt
  • an existing debt payment arrangement becomes more than seven days overdue

PPL customer debt raising and recovery

Table 2

Item

Description

1

Customer debts - how they may occur + Read more ...

PPL debts may be raised if:

  • customer was not eligible for payment due to:
    • no longer being the primary carer
    • ceasing to be a permanent resident
    • PPL payability ceasing due to return to work
    • transfer of payment
    • payment made after death
    • working on a Flexible PPL day
    • duplicate payment
  • fraud and/or misrepresentation
  • when an employer is responsible for delivering PPL and any flexible PPL scheduled within 12 months of the child birth or adoption

2

How much will be raised? + Read more ...

The employee is to repay all amounts. This includes Pay As You Go (PAYG) deductions which have been remitted to the ATO.

3

How they are recovered + Read more ...

A customer can have their debt offset against the PPL payable to their current partner or other secondary claimant. This is for cases where payments are retrospective. In this situation, the secondary claimant has to request to offset the debt.

The agency has the following options for recovering a PPL debt from an employee:

  • cash repayments
  • withholding repayments from current Centrelink payments
  • offsetting against PPL arrears, where these occur in the same activity
  • garnishee
  • referral to an External Collection Agent (ECA)
  • legal proceedings (civil recovery)

See Debt recovery for Centrelink.

4

Further information for customers + Read more ...

Debt finalised in the current financial year

For payments delivered by the agency, customers can request a refund of the PAYG withholding from the agency. See Refunding Australian Taxation Office (ATO) excess tax deductions.

For payments delivered by their employer, customers can request a refund of the PAYG withholding amount from their employer. On their income tax return, customers must report the total PAYG withheld and total taxable income.

For more information, see Step 2 in Table 2 of Apportioning Parental Leave Pay (PPL) debts between employers and employees.

If portions of the debt were delivered by both the agency and their employer, see Apportioning Parental Leave Pay (PPL) debts between employers and employees.

Debt finalised after the end of the financial year in which it occurred but tax return not lodged

The customer will need to:

  • recover the PAYG amount from the Australian Taxation Office (ATO) when they complete their tax return
  • make an agreement with the agency allowing them to repay the PAYG amount upon receipt from the ATO, and
  • report the amended total PAYG withheld and total taxable information from the revised payment summary

For more information, see Step 3 in Table 2 of Apportioning Parental Leave Pay (PPL) debts between employers and employees.

Debt finalised after the end of the financial year in which the tax return has been lodged

The customer will need to lodge an amended assessment application to the ATO advising of the revised payment summary.

Note: when the agency does not provide the payment the employer is responsible for amending the payment summary. The Initial Notice of Debt states this information on it.

For more information, see Step 4 in Financial year and Australian Taxation Office (ATO) details when a PPL debt was actioned table.

5

Writing off debts + Read more ...

PPL debts may be written off as per current provisions.

6

Waiver of debts + Read more ...

PPL debts may be waived as per current provisions.

Use the waiver reason code of (LTW). This is PPL specific.

Apply the waiver where:

  • the outstanding balance of a PPL debt is equal to or more than $50, but less than $200, and
  • it is not cost effective to recover the remaining amount

This waiver reason is only for debt management staff. Do not use during debt raising as a waiver reason.

PPL debt offsetting, exemptions, and refunds

Table 3

Step

Action

1

Customer contact + Read more ...

Customer is requesting:

  • Refund to an offset amount, go to Step 2
  • Exemptions to their PPL offsetting, go to Step 3

2

Check offsetting screen + Read more ...

Locate the information via the offsetting screen.

The PPL Withholding Offsetting Summary (P1WOS) screen displays a summary view of current and historical PPL withholding and offsetting actions.

The information on this screen can be expanded by entering:

  • ‘O’ in the line to go to the PPL Offsetting Details screen (P1WOOD) screen to view the details of offsetting, including income support payment details
  • ‘W’ in the line to go to the P1WOWD to view Debt ID, withholding amounts and type (standard, non-standard)

Note: P1WOS can be accessed from Assessment Results (AR) screen by entering ‘L’ in the activity.

3

Request information + Read more ...

Customers must provide evidence to show that they are experiencing exceptional or unforeseen circumstance that may cause severe financial hardship.

Service Officers/Social workers should request information and refer this to a Subject Matter Expert for a quality check.

Do not refer directly to the Families Level 2 Helpdesk.

4

SME quality check + Read more ...

Regular living and medical expenses are not grounds for a refund/exemption, requests must meet the definition of exceptional or unforeseen.

Is there sufficient evidence?

  • Yes, refer to National Families and Child Care Level 2 Policy Helpdesk for further assessment. Go to Step 5
  • No:
    • Advise referrer that insufficient information is available, go to Step 3
    • If customer cannot provide any further information they do not meet the requirements for refund. Advise customer of decision and their rights to internal review via Authorised Review Officer (ARO)

5

Referral to Families Level 2 Helpdesk + Read more ...

Include the following information for the referral:

  • The refund amount requested
  • What the refund will be used for
  • Details of the unforeseen or exceptional circumstances
  • The costs and how these have contributed to financial hardship
  • Evidence to support the unforeseen or exceptional circumstances and the costs

The Resources page has a link to helpdesk.

6

Families level 2 Helpdesk Assessment + Read more ...

Note: the maximum period for an exemption to be in place is 12 months.

Does the customer meet requirements for a refund/exemption?

  • Yes, for:
    • Refunds - Advise customer and go to Step 7
    • Exemptions - Advise referring officer of outcome, referring officer to contact customer
  • No:
    • Advise referring officer of outcome. Referring officer to advise the customer of outcome and their right to seek further review by an ARO
    • DOC record (provide detail on why request was denied)
    • Procedure ends here

7

Refer to Debt staff + Read more ...

Create an open DOC. Use Fast Note - select Auto text, use Debts > Updates > Debt offset refund request.

Include the following information:

  • Helpdesk enquiry number (do not apply for ARO reviews)
  • If the customer is 'at risk' or experiencing hardship, add the URGENT keyword

System will include the ACTDRU keyword on DOC for allocation to Debt staff.

Debt staff to follow the Refunding debt payments procedure.
