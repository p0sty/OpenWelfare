Table 1: This table describes information on how to determine if a customer has regained entitlement and can resume JobSeeker Payment (JSP), Youth Allowance (YA), Parenting Payment Partnered (PPP), Parenting Payment Single (PPS), Farm Household Allowance (FHA) or Austudy.

Step

Action

1

Income support payment (ISP) status + Read more ...

If ISP has a status of:

  • CAN (cancelled), go to Step 2
  • REJ (rejected due to assessment of PPL or DAP income), the customer must re-claim to test their eligibility. Consideration may be made to run the Assisted Customer Claim (ACC) at point of contact if all required information is available. Procedure ends here

2

Previous ISP has a status of CAN (cancelled) + Read more ...

  • Display On Access (DOA) DOC review falls due or Work Item is allocated, go to Step 3
  • Customer initiates contact, see Step 1 in the Work Item received or contact initiated by customer table

3

Review DOA DOC + Read more ...

The customer's cancelled ISP may be able to be resumed if they have regained entitlement to their ISP following the end of their or their partners PPL/ DAP payment period.

In most circumstances there may be no requirement for the customer to lodge a new claim.

Is the review being completed within 13 weeks from the end of the PPL or DAP payment period?

  • Yes, make 2 genuine attempts to contact the customer to determine if continued payability has been maintained, go to Step 4
  • No, go to Step 8

4

Was the contact successful? + Read more ...

  • Yes, go to Step 5
  • No, annotate PPL/DAP income and ISP Display on Access (DOA) DOC detailing contact attempts, hold activity for 13 weeks from end date of the PPL/DAP period. Procedure ends here

5

Determine continued entitlement + Read more ...

Based on assessment of the customer's (and/or their partner's) current circumstances, can it be determined that continued eligibility for the ISP has been maintained since the PPL or DAP payment period ceased?

  • Yes, go to Step 6
  • No, go to Step 7

6

Customer remained eligible for ISP + Read more ...

Using the information on the customer's record, run Assisted Customer Claim (ACC) to generate and re-grant the cancelled ISP, (do not create a SOA shell or use 're-index' options).

  • The date of effect will be the date payability resumes (for PPL, viewable on the Benefit Status (XBS) screen, System PPL. For DAP, viewable on the XBS screen, System DAP):
    • PPL/DAP period end date +1
    • Last day of the PPL period or connected Flexible PPL days + 1, or
    • Flexible PPL day (or block of days) + 1
  • If required, the OWP must be waived with the reason Personal Financial Crisis Other Circumstances (PFO). The reason for the exemption must be recorded on a DOC on the customer's record. Commences must be included in the evidence provided section, for example, transfer from PPL or DAP
  • Jobseekers will have an automatic RapidConnect exemption applied and will resume prior placement status with their provider within ACC. There will be no requirement to run the Online Participation Interview
  • Expire the PPL/DAP income and ISP DOA DOC
  • In the customer's record in Customer First, use Fast Note to create a DOC as follows:
    • select Auto text, use Generic > Claims > Customer remains eligible for ISP
    • select Auto text, use Generic > Claims > Customer remains eligible for FHA
  • If the customer has submitted an Online Claim for the respective ISP:
    • Locate the claim in Process Direct
    • Mark as 'Not Required'
    • Annotate Progress of Claim DOC to refer to re-grant DOC dated xx/xx/xxxx
  • Procedure ends here

7

Customer did not remain eligible for income support payment + Read more ...

  • Advise the customer they will need to lodge a new claim if they wish to re-test their eligibility to an ISP in the future
  • Expire the PPL/DAP income and ISP DOA DOC
  • In the customer's record in Customer First, use Fast Note - select Auto text, use Generic > Claims > Continuation of payment not met
  • Procedure ends here

8

End of Review Period + Read more ...

The cancelled ISP cannot be resumed if contact is not established within 13 weeks of the end of the relevant PPL or DAP payment period.

The customer will need to lodge a new claim re-test their eligibility for the ISP.

  • Expire the PPL/DAP income and ISP DOA DOC
  • In the customer's record in Customer First, use Fast Note - select Auto text, use Generic > Claims > Continuation of payment not met
  • Procedure ends here

Table 2: This table describes the process to be followed when a customer contacts about resuming an income support payment (ISP) after the end of the Parental Leave Pay (PPL) or Dad and Partner Pay (DAP) payment period.

Step

Action

1

Customer initiates contact or Work Item received + Read more ...

The customer's (and/or their partner's) ISP may be able to be resumed if they have regained entitlement following:

  • the end of the DAP period
  • the end of the PPL period and any connected Flexible PPL days
  • untaken Flexible PPL days remaining
  • all of their Flexible PPL days have been paid

In most circumstances there may be no requirement for the customer to lodge a new claim.

Has contact been established within 13 weeks from the end of the relevant PPL or DAP payment period?

  • Yes:
    • new claim trained Service Officers, go to Step 3
    • all other Service Officers, go to Step 6
  • No, go to Step 2

2

Contact not established within 13 weeks + Read more ...

The ISP cannot resumed if contact has not been established within 13 weeks following the end of the customer or their partnerâ€™s PPL or DAP payment period. The customer will need to lodge a new claim to re-test their eligibility to a payment.

  • Expire the PPL/DAP income and ISP Display On Access (DOA) DOC (if applicable)
  • In the customer's record in Customer First, use Fast Note - select Auto text, use Generic > Claims > Continuation of payment not met
  • Procedure ends here

3

Customer initiated contact, new claim trained Service Officer + Read more ...

Based on assessment of the customer's current circumstances, has continued eligibility for the ISP has been maintained since of the end of the relevant PPL or DAP payment period?

  • Yes, go to Step 4
  • No, go to Step 5

Note: if the ISP has a status of REJ due to assessment of PPL or DAP income or if FHA has a status of CAN CLR, the customer must re-claim to test their eligibility. Consideration may be made to run the Assisted Customer Claim (ACC) at point of contact if all required information is available.

4

Continued eligibility for ISP has been maintained since cessation of PPL or DAP payment period + Read more ...

Using the information on the customer's record, run Assisted Customer Claim (ACC) to generate and re-grant the cancelled ISP, (do not create a SOA shell or use 're-index' options)

  • The date of effect will be the date payability resumes (For PPL, viewable on the Benefit Status (XBS) screen, System PPL. For DAP, viewable on the XBS screen, System DAP):
    • PPL/DAP period end date +1
    • Last day of the PPL period or connected Flexible PPL days + 1, or
    • Flexible PPL day (or block of days) + 1
  • If required, the OWP must be waived with the reason Personal Financial Crisis Other Circumstances (PFO). The reason for the exemption must be recorded on a DOC on the customer's record. Comments must be included in the evidence provided section, for example, transfer from PPL or DAP
  • Jobseekers will have an automatic RapidConnect exemption applied and will resume prior placement status with their provider within ACC. There will be no requirement to run the Online Participation Interview
  • Expire the PPL/DAP income and income support payment DOA DOC
  • In the customer's record in Customer First, use Fast Note to create a DOC as follows:
    • select Auto text, use Generic > Claims > Customer remains eligible for ISP
    • select Auto text, use Generic > Claims > Customer remains eligible for FHA
  • If the customer has submitted an Online Claim for the respective ISP:
    • Locate the Claim in Process Direct
    • Mark as 'Not Required'
    • Annotate Progress of Claim DOC to refer to re-grant DOC dated xx/xx/xxxx
  • Procedure ends here

5

Continued eligibility for payment has not been maintained since cessation of PPL or DAP payment period + Read more ...

  • Advise the customer they will need to lodge a new claim to re-test their eligibility to a payment in the future
  • Expire the PPL/DAP income and ISP DOA DOC
  • In the customer's record in Customer First, use Fast Note - select Auto text, use Generic > Claims > Continuation of payment not met
  • Procedure ends here

6

Customer initiated contact, non-new claim trained Service Officer + Read more ...

Check whether the payment has become payable since the customer was last on an ISP, that is, customer meets basic payment qualification and payability.

Note: an ISP may be cancelled under various reasons for example:

  • CAN-INC - where cancellation has occurred immediately due to PPL/DAP income,
  • CAN-E6F - where cancellation has occurred at the end of the 6 fortnight nil rate period if customer also had other income

If ISP has a status of REJ (rejected) due to assessment of PPL or DAP income the customer must re-claim to test their eligibility. Consideration may be made to run the Assisted Customer Claim (ACC) workflow at point of contact if all required information is available.

ABSTUDY customers will remain current after a 6 fortnight nil rate period. ABSTUDY payments will automatically resume at the end of the PPL/DAP period.

Is the payment now payable?

  • Yes, go to Step 7
  • No, go to Step 8

7

Payment is now payable + Read more ...

  • Annotate the PPL/DAP income and ISP DOA DOC with details confirming the customer and/or partners current circumstances including employment status
  • In the customer's record in Customer First, use Fast Note - select Auto text, use Families > Claims > PPL/DAP income and ISP.
    The work item will be automatically allocated to an appropriately trained staff member to assess eligibility/resumption for the relevant ISP
  • Add the appropriate keyword based on whether PPL or DAP granted and ISP payment type. For correct keyword, see Resources page of Processing standalone and combined claims for family assistance and/or Paid Parental Leave scheme payments
  • Advise the customer the resumption of their ISP will be assessed by a suitable trained Service Officer.

8

Payment is not payable at end of PPL/DAP + Read more ...

  • Annotate the PPL/DAP income and ISP DOA DOC with details confirming the customer and/or partners current circumstances including employment status
  • Direct the customer to the Payment and Service Finder and advise they may retest their eligibility for ISP by lodging a new claim
