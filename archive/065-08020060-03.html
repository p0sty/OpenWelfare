HEAS automated reviews + Read more ...

Reviews for loans paid through the HEAS automatically initiate and complete by the system for the following reasons:

  • fortnightly calculation of interest on the loan balance, go to Step 2

2

Fortnightly calculation of interest + Read more ...

When a loan is granted, the system automatically sets up a review to reassess the customer’s loan each fortnight. When the review runs:

  • the loan balance is updated with any loan payments issued during that fortnight, including:
    • regular loan payments
    • arrears, and
    • one off payments manually paid via the Payment Adjustment (PYAJ) screen
  • interest is calculated on the updated loan balance and added to the balance

Any loan repayments made during the fortnight will have already been taken into account by the time the fortnightly review runs, as the loan balance adjusts immediately when repayments are coded.

The updated loan balance, including accrued interest, is recorded on the HEAS Details (PLS/HEAS) screen.

The review runs one day after the Entitlement Period End Date (EPED) each fortnight. The Event Date for the update is the EPED of the fortnight that has just ended.

For example, if the entitlement period is 1 January 2020 - 14 January 2020, then:

  • the review for that fortnight runs on 15 January 2020 (the EPED+1 day), and
  • the new loan balance and interest calculation is updated on the PLS/HEAS screen using 14 January 2020 (the EPED) as the Event Date

The review continues to run each fortnight until the loan balance is repaid in full.

3

Annual reassessment of Maximum Loan Amount (MLA) + Read more ...

The MLA is calculated based on the value of a customer’s real assets and their age component. The age component for the HEAS is determined as follows:

  • if the customer is single, the age component is the customer’s age
  • if the customer is partnered, the age component is the age of the younger member of the couple

The system automatically recalculates the customer’s MLA each year when their age component increases. The age component increases on the customer’s birthday, or if they have a younger partner, on their partner’s birthday.

The MLA recalculation occurs at the end of the entitlement period in which the applicable birthday falls. The updated MLA is displayed on the HEAS Details (PLS/HEAS) screen in the Maximum Loan Amount field. The Event Date for the updated MLA is the Entitlement Period End Date (EPED) for that fortnight.

Upon completion of the review, a HEAS itemised loan statement with the updated MLA is automatically sent to the customer, unless the customer is deceased. If the customer is deceased, the letter is held on the BLST screen and an email is automatically sent to the Home Equity Access Scheme National team to review whether the statement may be released.

For more details on the manual review and release of the HEAS letters see Step 10 in Table 2.

Note: the system also automatically recalculates the MLA where the Loan Security value, Nominated Amount or Deductions are manually changed on the PLS/HEAS screen. Staff can also manually trigger a re-calculation of the MLA by entering 'Y' to the Recalc field on the PLS/HEAS screen.

4

Loan balance is approaching the maximum loan amount (MLA) + Read more ...

If a customer’s loan balance falls within $5,000 of their MLA, the system automatically generates a letter to advise the customer that they are approaching the MLA. The letter can be viewed on the History Summary (HS) screen.

The letter provides the customer with the opportunity to consider their financial circumstances and make necessary arrangements for when the payments cease. Some customers may decide to change their current loan arrangement to extend the period of time they can continue to receive loan payments.

For example:

  • customers may request to decrease their fortnightly loan amount, or
  • if a customer has more equity in Australian real estate, they may choose to:
    • offer this as more security for the loan, and
    • ask for the MLA be recalculated

Requests to change a loan arrangement must be made in writing and signed by the customer and their partner (if they have one). It is recommended that customers seek independent financial or legal advice before requesting an increase to their MLA.

5

Maximum loan amount (MLA) is reached + Read more ...

When a customer’s loan balance reaches their MLA, their loan payments automatically stop being paid. The following automatically occurs on their record:

  • The Cease Loan Indicator on the HEAS Details (PLS/HEAS) screen is set to ‘Y’ to prevent further loan payments from being issued
  • The Reject/Cease Reason field on the PLS/HEAS screen is updated to display cessation reason MBL (MLA reached)
  • A letter is generated to advise the customer that payments have stopped. The letter can be viewed on the History Summary (HS) screen

The system continues to calculate interest on the balance of the loan each fortnight until the loan has been repaid in full.

The customer may contact to offer additional security for the loan and request the MLA be recalculated.

The preferred option is for customers to use their Centrelink online account. If they are unable to use the online self service, they can complete the Home Equity Access Scheme Variation (SA497) form.

See Escalating enquiries to CAO's for more details.

It is recommended that customers seek independent financial or legal advice before requesting an increase to their MLA.

6

Consumer Price Index (CPI) reviews + Read more ...

CPI reviews are automatically actioned by the system in March, July and September of each year.

An exception to the automated process occurs if the HEAS customer is paid via manual rate. In this case, a Manual Follow Up (MFU) review is created for staff to manually apply the CPI update. See Step 8 in Table 2 for details about processing HEAS MFU’s for CPI updates.

For details about current payment rates see Rates and Thresholds.

2

Customer becomes deceased + Read more ...

An MFU generates when a death action is recorded and the customer has an outstanding HEAS loan balance. The MFU has the following attributes:

  • Activity notes: I012PH - HEAS customer has passed away
  • Keywords: PLSMFU, PLSDEA

The Complex Assessment Officer (CAO) is to determine the appropriate actions. This may include:

  • ensuring fortnightly loan payments have ceased to be paid:
    • For example check that the Cease Loan Indicator on the HEAS Details (PLS/HEAS) screen has automatically set to Y at the next fortnightly LNA review
    • This is the customer's next EPSD
  • determining appropriate action for recovery and settlement of the HEAS loan. See Initiating recovery of a Home Equity Access Scheme (HEAS) outstanding loan amount
  • assisting in the manual calculation of a Lump Sum Bereavement Payment (LBP), where required. The HEAS loan components must be excluded from the calculation of LBP

In some circumstances after a customer has passed away, the surviving partner or a third party may need a transmission of title.

  • Surviving partner - if the secured property is owned with a surviving partner, either as joint tenants or tenants in common, the surviving partner may request to have the property title transferred into their name only
  • Third party transfers - if the deceased customer has willed any part of the secured property that they owned to their surviving partner and/or a third party or parties, the third party or parties may request their share be transferred into their name/s. Appropriate recovery action must be taken

In the above cases, issue a tasking request to the Property legal team. Use the relevant transmission of title template.

The Resources page contains the templates.

3

Single customer becomes partnered + Read more ...

An MFU generates when a single customer becomes linked to a partner and:

  • the customer currently receives HEAS loan payments, or
  • has an outstanding loan balance
    Examples of reasons a customer is linked to a partner include marriage, or entering a de facto relationship

The MFU has the following attributes:

  • Activity notes: I005PH - MFU partner linked
  • Keywords: PLSMFU, PLSLNK

Complex Assessment Officers (CAO) must review the impacts (if any) for the purposes of the HEAS, including:

  • assessing the customer’s continuing eligibility to receive loan payments
  • taking into account any changes in the combined asset values, for example:
    • as a result of the new partner bringing assets into the relationship, or
    • the customer’s share in the ownership of their own assets being reduced
  • checking changes to real estate asset(s) used as security for the loan and revising the loan security value (as appropriate)
  • identifying the new partner’s intentions, if any, to apply for a loan under the HEAS

4

Partnered customer becomes single + Read more ...

An MFU generates when a partnered customer is unlinked from their partner, and:

  • the customer currently receives HEAS loan payments, or
  • has an outstanding loan balance
    Examples of reasons a customer may become unlinked from their partner include divorce, separation or death of their partner

The MFU has the following attributes:

  • Activity notes: I004PH - MFU unlinked from partner
  • Keywords: PLSMFU, PLSUNL

Complex Assessment Officers (CAO) must review the impacts (if any) for the purposes of the HEAS, including if:

  • the customer/partner has ongoing eligibility for loan payments
  • the asset(s) securing the loan will be disposed of or redistributed
  • recovery action for the loan must be initiated. See Initiating recovery of a Home Equity Access Scheme (HEAS) outstanding loan amount
  • the legal formalisation of the end of a marriage or marriage-like relationship has resulted in a court order to transfer the HEAS debt to one person.
    See Transfer of the Home Equity Access Scheme (HEAS) debt based on Court Order for the transmission of title template and more details

If the customer was receiving the HEAS loan payments at the time of being unlinked from their partner, their loan payments may automatically stop if the customer is no longer qualified.

For example, if the customer is under Age Pension age and had qualified by virtue of their partner was of Age Pension age. The customer must qualify for the HEAS in their own right to continue receiving loan payments.

5

Qualifying payment stops for Age Pension age customer + Read more ...

An MFU generates when a customer’s qualifying payment is cancelled and all the following criteria are met:

  • The customer is Age Pension age
  • Their qualifying payment is not Age Pension
  • The HEAS payments cancelled when the qualifying payment cancelled
  • The customer has an outstanding loan balance

For example, the customer is Age Pension age and they receive the loans payments as well as Carer Payment (CAR) or Disability Support Pension (DSP). If the HEAS stops when the CAR or DSP stop, the MFU is created to determine if the customer may have ongoing eligibility for the HEAS via the Age Pension qualification rules.

The MFU has the following attributes:

  • Activity notes: I008PH - HEAS customer APA
  • Keywords: PLSMFU, PLSAPA

The Complex Assessment Officer (CAO) is to determine whether the customer may be payable as a ‘loan only’ arrangement using Age Pension qualification.

If restoration of the HEAS payments is possible, contact with the customer is required to determine their desired outcome, noting it may be their preference to no longer receive loan payments. The customer may wish to test their eligibility for the Age Pension first, in which case they must lodge an Age Pension claim. The outcome of the pension claim must be determined before recommencing loan payments through the HEAS.

For details about how to start loan payments see Home Equity Access Scheme (HEAS) cancellation, restorations and rejections. When starting loan payments as a loan-only payment, staff must set the HEAS Only Claim indicator to Y on the HEAS Details (PLS/HEAS) screen. This prevents pension components from being incorrectly paid.

6

Qualifying payment stops for a payability based reason + Read more ...

An MFU generates if a customer’s qualifying payment is cancelled for a payability based reason. When the qualifying payment cancels for one of these reasons, the loan payments continue to be paid as a loan only arrangement. The qualifying payment, although cancelled, remains notionally ‘current’ in the system at a zero rate to facilitate the loans payments.

This does not override the legislative decision that the customer is no longer eligible for a payable rate of the qualifying payment. Therefore manual action is required to prevent any pension components from becoming payable.

The MFU has the following attributes:

  • Activity notes: I013PH - HEAS Qualifying Payment stopped (HEAS ongoing)
  • Keywords: PLSMFU, PLSQPC

The Complex Assessment Officer (CAO) is to set the HEAS Only Claim indicator on the HEAS Details (PLS/HEAS) screen to Y. This will prevent the system from incorrectly auto-restoring the qualifying payment in response to coding updates on the customer’s records. For example, where the customer makes an update via their online account, or staff code an income or asset update.

Where the system has notionally ‘overridden’ a pension cancellation reason for the purposes of paying the loan only, staff can see the cancellation reason for the qualifying payment as follows:

  • Go to the XPDETR screen
  • Type PHPS in the Group field and press [Enter]
  • The pension cancellation details will be indicated by the following:
    • The PHPS.PEN.STS.CODE will be CAN
    • The PHPS.PEN.RSN.CODE will display the cancellation reason code (i.e. one of the payability based reasons)

If the qualifying payment has already been restored by the time the MFU is actioned, check whether the restoration action was correct. The qualifying pension must only be restored if qualification for payment has been re-established. Customers generally have to lodge a new application unless a decision has been made to reinstate the payment. For guidance see Restoration of payments (CLK). If payment has been correctly restored there is no need to change the HEAS Only Claim indicator. Incorrect auto-restoration of the qualifying payment may require corrective action (for example, manual cancellation) and a debt raised for the pension components.

7

Qualifying payment stops for certain other reasons + Read more ...

An MFU generates for customers with an outstanding loan balance when the qualifying payment stops for one of the below reasons and as a result loan payments stop on the same day:

  • CLR - Claimant request/Voluntary surrender
  • VST - Voluntary surrender trust and company
  • CMI - Compensation settlement under investigation
  • CMP - Precluded due to Compensation direct deductions
  • COM - Compensation preclusion period
  • FCC - Fail to claim compensation

The MFU has the following attributes:

  • Activity notes: I011PH - HEAS Qualifying Payment stopped
  • Keywords: PLSMFU, PLSQPS

The Complex Assessment Officer (CAO) is to review whether the customer can continue receiving loan payments as a loan only arrangement.

Based on the cancellation reason being payability-related or a voluntary cessation, the customer may still be eligible to continue getting loan payments. Staff must establish ongoing eligibility for the HEAS, before making a decision to re-start loan payments.

Loan payments must only be started as a loan only payment with the consent of the customer. Contact with the customer is required to determine their desired outcome, noting it may be their preference to no longer receive loan payments.

For details about how to start loan payments see Change of circumstances – Home Equity Access Scheme (HEAS).

When starting loan payments as a loan-only payment, staff must set the HEAS Only Claim indicator to Y on the HEAS Details (PLS/HEAS) screen. This prevents pension components from being incorrectly paid.

8

Consumer Price Index (CPI) reviews + Read more ...

CPI reviews are actioned automatically by the system in March, July and September of each year. An MFU only generates for the purposes of the HEAS if the customer is paid via manual rate, as manual rates need adjusting after each CPI.

The MFU has the following attributes:

  • Keywords: PLSCPI

To manually adjust the loan rate, in Customer First:

  • Select the MFU and go to the HEAS Details (PLS/HEAS) screen to confirm the new loan component payable
  • Go to the Assessment Results (AR) screen and place a 'T' next to the assessment line for the customer (if the customer is partnered only one person's record can be updated at a time) and press [Enter]
  • This will go to the Daily Rates Summary (RATS) screen where a row will display for the relevant period requiring a manual rate. Place an ‘M’ next to the applicable row (noting there may be more than one) and press [Enter]
  • Code the daily rate for the loan component for each applicable period and press [Enter] to accept the change. Repeat for any other rows requiring manual adjustment
  • Once all lines are completed return to the AR screen. If the customer is partnered, repeat the manual rate coding for the partner, then finalise the activity

For details about current payment rates see Rates and Thresholds.

CPI updates may result in a partial entitlement to a qualifying payment. If there is an increase in basic entitlement, manual CPI reviews must be conducted as soon as the new rate information is available to make sure the correct rate of the HEAS is calculated.

Retirement Villages

Where a customer has previously advised that they live in a Retirement Village please request the customer and/or the partners Title Certificate before finalising their review.

The customer’s or their partner’s name must be on the Land Title Certificate that make use of the:

  • land lease
  • leasehold, or
  • loan licence arrangements

Ownership is determined by the placement on information on the Title Certificate

  • Typically ownership is at the top as first schedule/registered owner
  • If the customer or partner information is second schedule, they may not own the property outright

Retirement Villages do not generally provide land ownership. This means that they cannot be used as security for the purposes of the HEAS and/or the Property Title Certificate to be eligible for the HEAS and must be cancelled REA (Australian real estate requirements not met). See Home Equity Access Scheme (HEAS) cancellation, restorations and rejections.

Self-Managed Superannuation Funds (SMSF)

Where a customer has previously advised that they have used SMSF as security for their HEAS loan, request a copy of their funds that were used to secure their HEAS Loan.

A HEAS claimant should not use property held by a SMSF as security for their HEAS loan as it is a requirement to have sufficient real assets to secure and repay the loan and should be cancelled DIS (Secretary discretion). See Home Equity Access Scheme (HEAS) cancellation, restorations and rejections.

Note: Self-Managed Superannuation Fund (SMSF) that require cancellation should include the following information:

“SMSF utilised as security for a HEAS Loan negates the Superannuation Industry (SIS) Act prohibition, borrowing money when a person is a trustee of a regulated superannuation fund.”

10

Letters on deceased records + Read more ...

All letters generated for the HEAS on deceased records are held for manual review by a Complex Assessment Officer (CAO). The system places letters on hold automatically, on the Select Cobs Record (BLST) screen. A system-generated email report is sent to the Home Equity Access Scheme National team mailbox for investigation of whether it is appropriate to issue the letter or if a manual letter needs to be created.

To determine if it is appropriate to release the letter, review the letter type and Document List (DL) for any details about who correspondence is to be sent to, following the customer’s death. Examples of reasons it may not be appropriate to release the letter include:

  • a bereavement period may be in place, during which no correspondence must be sent to the customer, surviving partner or nominee
  • an instruction has been documented to send correspondence to another person. For example:
    • the surviving partner
    • a nominee, or
    • the executor of the deceased estate

If doubt exists, consult a Team Leader and/or determine an appropriate escalation avenue for assistance.

To release a held letter:

  • go to the BLST screen and locate the held letter
  • enter ‘R’ in the selection box for the row and press [Enter]
  • to check the letter has been issued, await overnight processing then check the History Summary (HS) screen

To delete a held letter:

  • go to the BLST screen and locate the held letter
  • enter ‘D’ in the selection box for the row and press [Enter]. The letter will be immediately deleted
  • determine if a manual letter needs to be sent in place of the deleted letter

Note: only staff with access to process bereavement letters can delete or release a letter on a deceased record.

If staff need to issue a manual letter, issue a deceased loan statement or deceased estate letter from Process Direct via the Home Equity Access Scheme letters (HEASL) screen:

  • issue Deceased loan statements to the surviving partner
  • issue Deceased estate letters to:
    • the estate of a deceased customer who was single
    • the estate of a surviving partner after their death
    • the executor of an estate. Note: Services Australia must have evidence of an arrangement

11

Reviews for saved cases + Read more ...

Customers who have a loan under the provisions of the 1985 ‘old scheme’ are saved cases and require annual review. There are no system-generated reviews created on saved cases, therefore review actions must be set up and conducted manually.

These manual reviews are created and actioned by a Home Equity Access Scheme National Complex Assessment Officer (CAO). The manual actions required are:

  • create an MFU review on the customer’s record to mature annually
  • on maturity of the review:
    • Manually calculate the interest that has accrued in the previous 12 months. Note: the interest calculation applicable to the old scheme is different to the current HEAS. See the Social Security Guide, 3.4.5.100 Saved Cases - PLS before 10 July 1996 for details
    • Update the DOC on the Document List (DL) screen with the updated interest amount and loan balance
    • Create and issue a loan statement to the customer (or where applicable, their surviving partner or executor) setting out the amount of the outstanding loan balance to date
  • Upon completion of the review, create a new MFU to mature in 12 months' time

As the HEAS loan amount (including interest) is an allowable deduction from the assessable asset value of the real estate used as security for the loan, update the Mortgage/Loans field on the Real Estate Detail (RE) screen with the loan amount. See Security for the Home Equity Access Scheme (HEAS) for details about how to assess and apply the deduction.

To create a manual review see How to code and action a manual review. Add keyword PLSMFU to manual reviews created for the purposes of the HEAS.

12

HEAS only customer claims a pension + Read more ...

Service Officers:

  • update Income and Asset details provided from claim via a change of circumstances (CoC) transaction
  • send a Fast Note to Complex Assessment Officers (CAO) to advise that a HEAS only customer has claimed a pension

Upon allocation of the Fast Note, CAOs review circumstances and contact the customer to discuss impact of change.

If a pension is payable (for example, income and assets are below the applicable cut off thresholds):

  • in Customer First, code a reassessment (REA) line on the Benefit Action (BA) screen, using the date of claim as the:
    • date of receipt, and
    • date of event
  • change the HEAS only claim indicator on the HEAS Details (PLS/HEAS) screen to N
  • pension payments display on Payment Summary (PS) screen from the date of claim
  • finalise the activity
  • DOC action taken - use Fast Note - select Auto Text, use Older Australians > Home Equity Access Scheme > HEAS Notification of Change of Circs (CoC)
  • send a Fast Note back to the Service Officer to advise:
    • HEAS review has been completed
    • create a manual grant DOC
    • change the claim status in Process Direct to Complete
    • if pension payments have not started from date of claim, manually calculate the pension arrears and pay via the Payment Adjustment (PYAJ) screen
    • create an Online Advice (OLA) letter Q011 to the customer (and correspondence nominee if applicable)

If a pension is not payable (for example, income and assets are above the applicable cut off thresholds):

  • DOC action taken - use Fast Note - select Auto Text, use Older Australians > Home Equity Access Scheme > HEAS Notification of Change of Circs (CoC)
  • send a Fast Note back to the Service Officer to advise:
    • HEAS review has been completed and customer has elected to remain as HEAS only
    • create a manual rejection DOC
    • change the claim status in Process Direct to Complete
    • create an Online Advice (OLA) rejection letter Q134 to the customer (and correspondence nominee if applicable)

1

Task locking conflicts + Read more ...

When an advance payment of a loan is issued through the HEAS, trigger processing is used to automatically make the following updates:

  • add the advance payment to the customer’s loan balance
  • code the advance payment as investment type HEA on the Direct Investments (SVDI) screen. The investment is exempt from the asset test for a period of 90 days
  • removal of the asset test exemption from the HEAS investment on the SVDI screen after 90 days

Manual Follow Up (MFU) activities are created when trigger activities cannot be completed automatically. The MFU displays as a PEN/Manual Followup review on the Activity List (AL) screen and allocated via the Workload Management System for the Home Equity Access Scheme National team to action.

To identify the reason the MFU has created, review the Activity Details (AY) screen. If the keyword is:

  • PLONA, go to Step 2
  • HEASAVC, go to Step 3
  • HEASAVR, go to Step 4

See Advance payments for Home Equity Access Scheme (HEAS) loans for more details.

2

PLONA task lock / Error MFU + Read more ...

A PLONA trigger is created when an advance payment is granted. The trigger runs overnight and attempts to add to the advance payment to the customer’s loan balance on the HEAS Details (PLS/HEAS) screen.

If the PLS/HEAS screen is locked by a STArted activity, the PLONA trigger cannot process the automatic loan balance update. The trigger will retry for 7 days before creating an MFU for manual follow up.

The MFU includes the following attributes:

  • Activity notes: Unable to update loan balance
  • Keywords: PLONA, HEAMFU

To action the MFU, the Home Equity Access Scheme National team must:

  • select the MFU via the AL screen, and
  • manually increase the loan balance on the PLS/HEAS screen by adding the advance payment amount

The advance payment amount populates in the notes on the Activity Details (AY) screen, including the advance grant date.

The Effect Date for the loan balance update on the PLS/HEAS screen must match the advance grant date.

Once the coding updates are complete:

  • finalise the MFU via the Assessment Results (AR) screen, and
  • record the outcome in a DOC

Procedure ends here.

3

HEASAV task lock / Error MFU + Read more ...

A HEASAV trigger is created when an advance payment is granted and the customer’s loan is secured against their principal home.

The trigger runs overnight and attempts to code the advance payment as investment type HEA (HEAS-ADVANCE) on the Direct Investments (SVDI) screen.

The update includes coding an asset test exempt amount in the Loan/Encumbrance Amount $: field (Asset Test Deduction Amt: field in Customer First), as advance payments of loans paid through the HEAS are exempt from the assets test for 90 days.

Note: do not code an asset test deduction against the New Home Deduction Amt $: field. These amounts are exempt from the Sale of Home provisions for sales finalised on/after 1 January 2023. See Selling a home.

If the SVDI screen is locked by a STArted activity, the HEASAV trigger cannot process the automatic coding update.

The trigger will retry for 14 days before creating an MFU for manual follow up.

The MFU includes the following attributes:

  • Activity notes: Unable to update savings for HEAS Advance
  • Keywords: HEASAVC, HEAMFU

To action the MFU, the Home Equity Access Scheme National team must:

  • select the MFU via the AL screen, and
  • manually record the advance payments as an investment on the SVDI screen as follows. Note: it is critical the details are recorded in this way to ensure future reviews run correctly:
    • Account name: HEAS-ADVANCE
    • Type: HEA
    • Balance: full amount of the advance payment
    • Event Date: equal to the grant date of the advance payments
    • Loan/Encumbrance Amount $: field (Asset Test Dedn Amt $: field in Customer First): if applicable, key the full amount of the advance payment

The advance payment amount populates in the notes on the Activity Details (AY) screen, including the advance grant date.

Once the coding updates are complete, finalise the MFU via the Assessment Results (AR) screen and record the outcome in a DOC.

For more details about the automated review process for ending the 90 day asset test exemption, go to Step 4.

Otherwise, the procedure ends here.

4

90 Day Review task lock / Error MFU + Read more ...

In the advance payment has been recorded on the Direct Investments (SVDI) screen, an automated process runs after 90 days to remove the asset test exemption from the investment.

If the SVDI screen is locked by a STArted activity, the system cannot process the automatic coding update.

The system immediately creates an MFU for manual follow up.

Note: due to the possible effects on the customer’s pension rate, the update must be completed as soon as possible to prevent rate adjustments.

The MFU includes the following attributes:

  • Activity notes: Unable to Update Savings - HEAS investments no longer asset exempt
  • Keywords: HEASAVR, HEAMFU

To action the MFU, the Home Equity Access Scheme National team must:

  • Select the MFU via the AL screen, and
  • Go to the Savings Summary (SVS) screen
  • Select the HEAS-ADVANCE account, and
  • Manually review the details to determine if the Loan/Encumbrance Amount $: field (Asset Test Dedn Amt $: in Customer First) field in Process Direct/Customer First needs to be manually set to zero
  • Check the Balance field
    • if the amount is zero, no coding updates are required
    • if the Balance is more than zero, update the investment details per the below instruction
  • Balance: no change
  • Event Date: equal to the grant date of the advance payments + 90 days
  • Loan/Encumbrance Amount $ field (Asset Test Dedn Amt $: field in Customer Record): if applicable, key the balance of the loan/encumbrance which is deductible from the investment value in whole dollars
    Only code an amount while the customer is entitled to have all or part of the asset exempted under the Assets Test

Note: do not code an asset test deduction against the New Home Deduction Amt $: field. These amounts are exempt from the Sale of Home provisions for sales finalised on/after 1 January 2023.

The advance payment amount populates in the notes on the Activity Details (AY) screen, including the advance grant date.

Once the coding updates are complete:

  • finalise the MFU via the Assessment Results (AR) screen, and
  • record the outcome in a DOC

Procedure ends here.
