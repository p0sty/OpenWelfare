Medicare online accounts

On this Tab:

Basic troubleshooting

Escalation

Basic troubleshooting

Table 1

Item

Description

1

Access to Medicare online account using myGov + Read more ...

Check with the customer:

  • Have they created a myGov account?
  • Have they linked Medicare services to their myGov account?
  • Have they been able to access their Medicare online account in the past?
  • What screen or prompt did they get to?

If they are having trouble accessing their myGov account, see Troubleshooting myGov.

Check status and eligibility

After authenticating the customer, use CDMS to check the customer's online account status is Active.

Check for CMBO, MPI, Investigation or Return Mail flags on the customer’s record.

If they have a Return Mail flag, help them verify their address. This will remove the flag and the customer will be able to link to their Medicare online account.

Check if the customer has prevented access to their Medicare online account.

Check mainframe to confirm information relating to claiming.

For help, see Access to Medicare self service - set up, checking, cancelling and enabling.

2

Service not available + Read more ...

Check with the customer:

  • Have they been able to access their Medicare online account in the past?
  • What date and time were they unable to access the service?

Service interruptions

To confirm if there is a scheduled service interruption of Medicare online account, customers can check for system outage notification messaging on the myGov and Medicare online account home pages. A banner notification message may display:

  • myGov: advising of a planned whole of service outage to a member service
  • Medicare online account: advising of a planned outage of a service within Medicare online account

If Medicare online account is currently unavailable due to an unscheduled outage, an error message will display informing customers to try again later.

For information about disruptions to services and scheduled maintenance times, see Network News Update (NNU).

Internet connection

Check the customer can access other internet sites, e.g. their email.

If they are accessing their Medicare online account using a mobile device, check the customer has stable phone reception and internet connection.

This can be using phone data or Wi-Fi:

  • If using phone data, ask them:
    • to check if they have reached their monthly data limit
    • if their service provider has any outages in the area
  • If using Wi-Fi, ask them:
    • to check they have the appropriate access to the access point
    • if their service provider has any outages in the area

They can clear all temporary internet files in their browser settings.

If they continue having trouble connecting to the internet, they should seek help from their internet provider.

3

Session time out + Read more ...

If the customer stops using their Medicare online account for more than 10 minutes, their session will time out.

This is a security measure to protect their information.

They will see a sign out message. Then can select OK and then sign in again.

4

Supported internet browsers and settings + Read more ...

Customers need one of these internet browsers:

  • Internet Explorer version 9 +
  • Mozilla Firefox version 30 +
  • Google Chrome version 39 +
  • Safari Version 5 + (Apple only)

Other browsers (including Beta versions) may work but have not been tested. They are not supported.

Customers should refer to their browser help or settings to check they have the latest version.

Browser settings

Customers need to have cookies and JavaScript enabled in their browser for their Medicare online account to work correctly.

They should clear all temporary internet files in their browser settings.

See Resources page for links to internet browser help sites.

5

myGov app - supported mobile operating systems + Read more ...

The Resources page contains links to using the myGov app, forms, publications and user guides relating to the myGov app digital wallet.

6

Display issues - screen resolution + Read more ...

Pages may not display correctly due to the screen resolution or small font setting.

A high resolution makes everything smaller.

If using a PC, advise the customer to adjust the resolution to 600 x 800.

They may be able to press [CTRL+].on their keyboard to zoom in.

7

Using service options + Read more ...

See Using Medicare self service for:

  • information about online service options
  • links to online guides and staff task cards that show how customers use self service

8

Further troubleshooting help + Read more ...

Use all information available to complete basic troubleshooting.

For more help, see Resources page for links to more information and error messages with an explanation and action to take.

Table 2

Step

Action

Service Officer + Read more ...

If the basic troubleshooting does not resolve the technical issue, escalate to a Programme Support Officer (PSO).

Procedure ends here for Service Officers.

2

PSO + Read more ...

Ensure the Service Officer has used available help and recorded what they have done to try to resolve the issue.

If unable to resolve issue

Refer the issue to Level 2 Business Support by submitting the 'Medicare Online Accounts General Enquiries issues' mySupport request. Take note of any receipt or reference code. See Resources page, Level 2 Business Support for a link to mySupport.

The Level 2 Business Support team is not an ICT helpdesk. It helps PSOs by escalating incidents for technical issues with Medicare online accounts. These include errors, faults or outages. Do not give the customer any timeframe for a response.

Access to Medicare online account using myGov + Read more ...

To use the app, customers must have linked to Medicare using their myGov account.

Check with the customer:

  • Have they created a myGov account?
  • Have they linked Medicare to their myGov account?
  • Have they been able to access the app in the past?
  • What screen or prompt did they get to?

If they are having trouble accessing their myGov account, see Troubleshooting myGov.

Check status and eligibility

After authenticating the customer, use CDMS to check the customer's online account status is Active.

Check for CMBO, MPI, Investigation or Return Mail flags on the customer’s record.

Check if the customer has prevented access to their Medicare online account.

2

Your account has been revoked + Read more ...

If the customer gets this error when signing into the app:

  • If they cannot sign in to myGov, they will need to reset their username or password or create a new myGov account
  • In myGov they can access Account settings menu option to get their username and update their secret questions and answers
  • If there is no Medicare tile on their screen, they will need to re-link Medicare services to their myGov account
  • If the Medicare tile
    • does not open their Medicare online account, they will need to re-link Medicare services to their myGov account
    • opens their Medicare online account, they need to reset their PIN using their myGov username and secret question and answer

For more information, see Using myGov accounts.

3

This service is unavailable, please try again later + Read more ...

If they get this alert, they can update their device by going to the App Store or Google Play for version updates. See Item 7 for versions needed to use the app.

They can also check their signal or wireless connection.

If they still get the error message, check for service interruptions.

4

Service interruptions + Read more ...

For information about disruptions to services and scheduled maintenance times, see:

  • Services Australia Website, Customer service changes
  • Network News Update (NNU)

5

Session timeout + Read more ...

If the customer stops using the app for more than 15 minutes, their session will time out.

This is a security measure to protect their information.

They will see a sign out message. Then can select OK and login again.

6

Internet connection + Read more ...

Check the customer has stable phone reception and internet connection. This can be using phone data or Wi-Fi:

  • If using phone data, ask them:
    • to check if they have reached their monthly data limit
    • if their service provider has any outages in the area
  • If using Wi-Fi, ask them:
    • to check they have the appropriate access to the access point
    • if their service provider has any outages in the area

They can clear all temporary internet files in their browser settings.

If they continue having trouble connecting to the internet, they may need to seek help from their internet provider.

7

Mobile device + Read more ...

The app is available for:

  • iOS (Apple) devices with a minimum operating system of iOS9
  • Android devices with a minimum operating system of 4.4.0

Customers should download updates when they are available. They can change their device settings to allow automatic updates.

If they have an outdated device or operating system, they can either:

  • update the latest software on their device
  • use their Medicare online account

App can only be used on one device

If the customer wants to use the app on more than one device, they will need to change their PIN each time.

If they get a new device, they will need to download the app and create a new PIN.

They will lose the list of transactions in History if they start using a new device.

8

Device permissions + Read more ...

For the app to work, the customer needs to set permissions:

  • Telephone number so they can securely store data on the device and share the login PIN with the Child Support app
  • Camera so they can take photos of documents to upload
  • Photo library so they can upload supporting documents

9

Out of memory + Read more ...

If this message displays, their device's memory is full.

This may stop them from using some of the app's features.

They can try to create space on their device by:

  • removing apps they do not use
  • deleting videos

10

App keeps freezing, times out or has slow response times + Read more ...

Advise the customer to:

  • check the device has the latest version of the app (they can see the latest version for each platform on the relevant app page)
  • shut down the device and restart
  • check their internet connection
  • confirm the service is available - see Item 4

Android devices

At logon some customers may experience:

  • the app opening and closing after keying PIN
  • an error message: Login failed – Service not available. Please try again later

Ask the customer to use the sign in as someone else function on their device. This will require re-authentication of their myGov credentials.

11

Using service options + Read more ...

See Using Medicare self service for:

  • information about services available using the app
  • links to online guides and staff task cards that show how customers complete the service option

12

Further troubleshooting help + Read more ...

Use all information available to complete basic troubleshooting.

If none of the options above work, ask the customer to reset their PIN and set up the app again.

For more help, see Resources page for links to more information and error messages with an explanation and action to take.
