Requesting documented verification from the customer

Table 1

Step

Action

1

Customer to supply verification + Read more ...

The customer must be given the opportunity to provide suitable verification documents before contacting third parties.

Before requesting documented verification from the customer:

  • If the customer is unable to provide the information during the initial phone conversation, give the customer the opportunity to source the required information
  • Advise the customer they must call back within the negotiated timeframe (preferably no more than 7 days or their Expected Response Date if that is more than 7 days from the date of contact) with the information
  • Advise the customer exactly what information they need to supply
  • Clearly document the verbal updates requested from the customer so the next Compliance Officer (CO) can action the intervention
  • All Private Company assessment and Family Day Care (FDC) matches from the Department of Education, Skills and Employment (DoE) need written verification of income

A Request for Information (RFI) letter is sent to the customer to request documents for verification where they have:

  • Refused to discuss intervention
  • Advised they can provide the documents required to verify circumstances
  • Undisclosed property. The customer is required to complete a MOD R and provide all associated supporting documents prior to requesting current/historical valuations (the exception to this is for Timeshare. For more information on Timeshare see the UII Trigger and Discussion Resource on the Resources page)
  • Undisclosed or undeclared Interest in a Private Company. The customer is required to complete a MOD PC and provide all associated supporting documents
  • Been involved as a Family Day Care Educator as verification of their income is required

Request the documents to make an assessment on the customer's record. For more information about what documents are needed see the UII Trigger and Discussion Resource on the Resources page.

Uploading documents online

If it has been determined verification is required, encourage the customer to upload the documents online by accessing their Centrelink services using myGov or Express Plus mobile app.

  • To issue a QSS32 Free Text Questions to the customer regarding Family Day Care only, go to Step 2
  • To issue a Request for Information (RFI) letter, go to Step 3
  • To check the return of requested documents, go to Step 5

2

Further information from Family Day Care Educators + Read more ...

When a customer is unable to answer questions over the telephone, they will be sent a QSS32 with the required questions.

Manually complete and send the QSS32 letters:

  • Complete the blank fields
  • Include suggested questions for FDC educators (you can have up to 10 free text questions). For further information on questions to be included see the UII Trigger and Discussion Resource on the Resources page
  • Finalise the letter (enter 'FIN' in the command box)
  • Annotate the Intervention DOC
  • Manually code the IRCD screen to place the intervention on HLD for the QSS32

Note: once the QSS32 has been returned further documents must be requested from the customer or third party. See the UII Trigger and Discussion Resource on the Resources page.

Procedure ends here until documents are returned.

3

Issuing the customer with a Request for Information (RFI) letter + Read more ...

Advise the customer the following information:

Suggested dialogue:

  • 'You will be issued with a request to provide the information required as per our telephone conversation today'
  • (If verbal updates can be accepted) 'You will have 21 days to respond to this request. If you wish to call back with the information, please contact us'
  • 'Once all the required information has been received, your financial income and asset details will be updated accordingly'
  • 'Where there is no change to your rate of payment a letter will be sent advising you when the intervention has been finalised'
  • 'You will be contacted and advised of the outcome and of any adverse decisions'
  • 'If documents are not returned by due date your payments may be suspended'

Telephony Action Processing Script (TAPS)

Telephony Intervention Processing script (TAPS) does not actually issue an RFI letter to the customer.

  • CO must select the option Further investigation required in TAPS
  • Do not select 'Request verification from Customer'. This will make sure that Review Contact Details (IRCD) screen is not coded with the letter code Q002 (as this letter is obsolete)

Issue a Request for Information (RFI) letter using the RFI guided procedure in Customer First

The letter does not send forms to the customer. If forms are to be completed (e.g. a MOD PC and Profit and Loss Statement) tell the customer that these can be downloaded on the Services Australia website.

Before issuing the RFI letter fully explain to the customer the information required, including the periods the documents are required for. Select the relevant documents from within the guided procedure.

The RFI is issued via Customer First > Workspace > Guided Procedures > Request for Information > select option 'a completed {form description} form and all supporting documents'.

Make sure the following information has been advised and documented:

  • details as to what information is needed
  • timeframe for response
  • consequences of non-compliance
  • the relevant legislative reference under which the request has been made (Section 63 of the Social Security (Administration) Act 1999)

Use the Mail Forms guided procedure in Customer First if the customer is unable to download the forms from the website. Make sure to also send the RFI. It contains the relevant legislation and consequences for not responding.

Note: for information relating to what source documents are needed for an update, see the UII Trigger and Discussion Resource on the Resources page.

4

Place the review on hold + Read more ...

To place on hold, see Step 3 in Table 1 Information about what needs to be coded on the IRS contact screen in Coding the Contact screen in IRS.

To add a keyword:

  • Go to the Review Summary (IRRS) screen
  • 'T' the IRS review (this will display the Manage Reviews (IRMR) screen)
  • Add the keyword 'UIIRFI'
  • Press [Enter]

Once placed on hold, 'W' the intervention on the Review Summary (IRRS) screen.

Record what documents have been requested on the intervention DOC.

Procedure ends here until documents are returned.

5

Check if customer has provided documents + Read more ...

How to determine if the documents have been returned:

  • The document will be scanned or uploaded by the customer and can be found in Document Tools in Customer First

When the response is received, it may create a work item for example, ZDMS_QSS32 - Free Text Questionnaire, once the document has been viewed, it must be closed

  • See Viewing Centrelink customer's digital image for more details on completing a digital work item in Customer First or in Process Direct

Where the documents have been returned:

  • finalise the work item once the document has been actioned
  • annotate the intervention DOC to show the documents have been returned
  • update the Review Contact Details (IRCD) screen with 'Y' in the Response field and enter the 'Date of response'

Has customer provided the documents requested?

  • Yes, see Assessing and finalising Unearned Income Interventions (UII)
  • No, go to Step 6

6

Manual suspension + Read more ...

If the customer has not responded in time, the intervention will be referred to staff to decide if manual suspension is needed.

Manual suspension action is needed if the customer has not responded to the QSS32 or RFI.

Before proceeding be aware of the procedures to refer a customer to a:

  • social worker in Payment and Integrity, or
  • identifying customer vulnerability and risk issues

Follow the phone contact requirements. Record all attempts to contact the customer on the intervention DOC and update the Review Contact Details (IRCD) screen. See the Attempted customer contact (outbound call attempts) DOC template in the Resources page for the documenting requirements.

Date of Effect (DOE)

If the customer fails to give the information when responding to a section 63 or section 68 notice, the date of effect of this adverse determination is generally the day following the expiration of the period allowed.

Where the customer has already been paid for this date, the date of effect for the suspension will be Date Paid To (DPT) plus 1.

See the Resources page in Suspension of payments (CLK) for examples.

Suspend payment

Access the customer's record:

  • Select the review in IRS. This makes sure the CO is within the IRS review before making any updates to the customer record. This is also known as being within the 'review image'. See 'Updating a customer record whilst in the review image' under User Guides on the Resources page for more information
  • Go to the Benefit Action (BA) screen. Code the following:
    • Key (payment type, for example, JSP) as the type of benefit in the Svc Rsn field
    • Key 'SUS' in the Action field
    • Key 'FRC' in the Reason field
    • Press [Enter] and the date of effect will default or enter the date of effect of the suspension in the Effect Date field
    • Complete footer details, Source field and date of receipt in the DOR field
    • Go to the Assessment Results (AR) screen
    • Finalise the activity
    • Update the letter contact on IRCD screen to 'N' the customer did not contact
    • Record a new contact on the IRCS/IRCD screen to place the intervention on hold (HLD) for 28 days, pending contact from the customer

To create a contact record, go to the IRCS screen and code the following:

  • Method type: key 'OUT'
  • Date Contacted: key 'T' for today
  • Press [Enter], when the Confirm Y/N box appears, key 'Y'
  • Press [Enter] and the IRCD screen will be displayed

On IRCD screen, code the following:

  • Person Contacted: key 'customer'
  • Expected response date: 28 days in the future
  • Text: 'customer suspended (FRC) - auto advice issued. Resubmit to follow up'
  • Add a keyword to the IRS intervention. In Customer Record:
    • Go to Review Summary (IRRS) screen
    • 'T' the intervention (this will display the Manage Reviews (IRMR) screen)
    • Add the keyword 'SUSHLD'
    • Press [Enter]
    • Record the suspension action in the intervention DOC. See the Customer suspended (FRC) - Fail to contact (Manual suspension only) DOC template in the Resources page for the documenting requirements
    • Generate a Display on Access (DOA) DOC on the customer record to make sure that if/when the customer contacts or comes back onto payment, that the customer is referred to the Compliance Risk Branch for discussion before any restoration. See Table 4 under the Customer First tab in -Creating, reviewing and deleting documents (Fast Note, Callbacks, ACTDOCs and DOA DOCs) for the process to add a DOA DOC

Note: if customer responds to the suspension letter, the DOA DOC must be end dated.

If the customer is already cancelled outside CRB action:

  • 2 phone call attempts are required as per manual suspension action
  • do not place the intervention on HLD for 28 days
  • Third Party verification is required
  • see Step 1 in Table 2

Procedure ends here until the customer either:

  • contacts after suspension, go to Step 7
  • does not contact, go to Step 8

7

Customer contacts in response to suspension of payment + Read more ...

During conversations be aware of the procedures to refer a customer to a:

  • social worker in Payment and Integrity, or
  • identifying customer vulnerability and risk issues

If the customer contacts in relation to the suspension action, follow the agency's phone contact requirements - Introduction, Identity Confirmation and Purpose. Once assessed whether the customer has retained eligibility for the payment, then payments can be restored.

Complete the Benefit Action (BA) screen to restore. All actions and decisions made are to be fully explained and documented within the intervention DOC

If the customer contacts after third party verification has been requested:

  • tell the customer third party information has been requested
  • discuss the customer's income and asset details making sure all the required information has been requested
  • for FDC match data with DoE cases only, the customer should be asked to provide complete Income Tax Returns

If the customer contacts before third party verification has been requested discuss the documents required.

If the customer states they still can provide verification, tell them it must be provided within 14 days.

  • As a written request for information has been received by the customer tell them if they fail to give the information by the new agreed due date their payments may be suspended
  • Manually update the Request for Information letter response date on the IRCD screen to 14 days in the future (if needed)
  • If the customer has already been given an additional 14 days since the original suspension to provide information, tell the customer that third party verification (where possible) will now be requested

If the customer states they cannot provide verification, determine if there is enough information to assess the intervention or if a request for verification should be sent to a third party:

  • check the customer record
  • scanned images
  • partner record, if applicable

All actions and decisions must be fully explained and documented on the customer record.

Is third party verification needed?

  • Yes, see Step 1 in Table 2
  • No, see Assessing and finalising Unearned Income Interventions (UII)

8

Customer does not contact in response to Suspension Action + Read more ...

Before proceeding be aware of the procedures to refer a customer to a:

  • social worker in Payment and Integrity, or
  • identifying customer vulnerability and risk issues

If the customer has not responded in the required time, make 2 genuine attempts to contact the customer. Assign the work item via Hold to User process in Table 5 of Work Optimiser for staff. Follow the phone contact requirements. Record all attempts to contact the customer on the intervention DOC and update the Review Contact Details (IRCD) screen. See:

  • the Attempted customer contact (outbound call attempts) DOC template in the Resources page for the documenting requirements
  • Coding the Contact screen in the Integrated Review System (IRS)

To manually cancel the payment, in the customer's record:

  • Select the review in IRS. This will make sure the CO is within the IRS review, before making any updates to the customer record. This is also known as being within the 'review image'.
  • Go to the Benefit Action (BA) screen. Code the following:
    • Key (payment type, for example, JSP) as the type of benefit in the Svc Rsn field
    • Key 'CAN' in the Action field
    • Key 'FRC' in the Reason field
    • Press [Enter] and the date of effect will default or enter the date of effect of the suspension in the Effect Date field
    • Complete footer details, Source field and date of receipt in the DOR field
  • Go to the Assessment Results (AR) screen
  • Finalise the activity
  • Update the letter contact on IRCD screen to 'N' the customer did not contact

Record the cancellation action in the intervention DOC. See the Customer payment cancelled - Failed to respond DOC template in the Resources page for the documenting requirements.

Once the cancellation process has been actioned, determine if third party verification is needed. See Step 1 in Table 2.

Note: if the contact are successful, continue to discuss the intervention with the customer.

Requesting verification from third parties

Table 2

Step

Action

1

Third party verification needed + Read more ...

A request for third party verification is made when the customer:

  • fails to respond to request to contact letter by the due date
  • is unable to supply verbal updates
  • fails to provide suitable verification documents for the Unearned Income Intervention (UII) by the due date

Verification can be requested from the third parties or in some cases directly from the Australian Taxation Office (ATO). FDC may require both third party and ATO verification.

All reasonable attempts must be made to gather the information from third parties before requesting the information from the ATO.

Add any phone numbers located/used to the intervention DOC in case future contact is required.

See the Resources page for more information on what documents are to be requested and information included in the third party requests:

  • UII Triggers and Discussion Resource
  • UII Suggested Text and Dialogue for Customer and Third Party Verification

Requesting third party information:

  • For earned income verification issue a Request for Information - Employment Details (Q146) request, go to Step 2
  • For unearned income verification issue a Questions for third party (QSS40) request, and Electronic Data Exchange Financial Institutions (FIE), go to Step 3

To request information from the ATO see Table 3

  • To check the return of a request, go to Step 5

2

Earned income verification requests + Read more ...

The verification request can be made using the General Review Action script by selecting 'Q146 - Employer Report.

The General Review Action script will:

  • annotate the intervention DOC
  • create an entry on the IRCD screen
  • place the review on hold (HLD) pending return of the information requested

Note: often the customer will have a contact telephone number for the employer that can be used to get the correct address. This will avoid going back to the customer if the employer does not supply the required information.

Add any phone numbers located/used to the intervention DOC in case future contact is needed.

If the customer receives Disability Support Pension (DSP) or Carer Payment (CP), they are subject to allowable hours rules.

Note: these rules do not apply to Carer Payment (XWP)

Determine if the customer receives DSP/CAR and the number of hours working does not exceed the allowable limits:

  • For DSP customers, see Commencing or returning to work Disability Support Pension (DSP)
  • For CP customers, see Carer Payment (CP) and Carer Allowance (CA) carer undertakes paid employment, self-employment, voluntary work, education or training activities
  • For CP customers where the care receiver commences or returns to work

Record discussion in the Intervention DOC.

To check the return of a request, go to Step 5.

3

Unearned income verification requests + Read more ...

The following steps are a guide to use as a reference for acceptable standards of verification, as determined by the:

  • nature of the investment/asset type, and
  • preliminary customer contact

For correct coding practice, all staff must adhere to the relevant assessment and coding instructions.

Telephony Intervention Processing script (TAPS)

Issuing a QSS40

  • TAPS will create and issue one QSS40 as part of the script flow
  • Manually enter the questions for the QSS40. See the UII text for customer and third party verification guide on the Resources page
  • If multiple QSS40s are needed, run the General Review Action Script post completion of TAPS to issue any further QSS40s
  • Any contact with third parties must be done using the General Review Action Script or Data Exchange Facility if:
    • the initial phone conversation has not occurred (due to suspension and cancellation), and
    • TAPS has not been run on the customer's record
  • TAPS can only be initially run on during the phone conversation
  • The General Review Action Script can be accessed from the Customer Record 'Script Selector' link. Requests via the Electronic Data Exchange Financial Institutions tool are made through Customer First

Note: TAPS cannot be used for FDC interventions at this point, the General Review Action Script must be used.

Requesting information via the Electronic Data Exchange Financial Institutions tool (for Commonwealth Bank requests only)

TAPS does not issue Electronic Data Exchange Financial Institutions Requests. Complete the following action:

  • in TAPS select 'Request verification Via Electronic' via the Action dialogue box this will add a task to the Task List as a reminder to action the FIE request once TAPS has complete
  • once TAPS is completed access the Financial Institution Exchange (FIE) Tool in Customer First

For more information see:

  • Electronic Data Exchange with Financial Institutions tool
  • contact information in Electronic Data Exchange

To place the intervention on hold, go to the IRCS screen and code the following:

  • Method type: key 'ONL'
  • Date Contacted: key 'T' for today
  • Press [Enter], when the Confirm Y/N box appears, key 'Y'
  • Press [Enter] and the IRCD screen will be displayed

On IRCD screen code the following:

  • Expected response date: 21 days in the future
  • Person contacted: FIE REQUEST
  • Once the intervention has been placed on hold, 'W' the intervention via the Review Summary (IRRS) screen

General Review Action Script

If more than one QSS40 to third party/institution to request documents for verification is required:

  • access the Script
  • select option QSS40/QSS41 (dom circs)
  • select the option for type of information being requested

Note: for all third party requests (except financial institution, employer and real estate requests) the following action is to be done when issuing a QSS40:

  • select employer information option where the script stops at 'Issue Review of Domestic Circumstances Regarding'. This option brings up the pro-forma of the letter. Manually change the questions. (See the UII text for customer and third party verification guide on the Resources page)
  • enter name of company and postal address
  • click on option 'NO' to finalise letter
  • when the script stops, it will ask if another QSS40 is to be issued. Do not click next. From here view the letter and make the necessary changes
  • delete the automated questions and replace with the relevant questions. See the UII text for customer and third party verification guide on the Resources page
  • check the letter is correct (in particular check the correct contact phone number is included in the letter)
  • once all information has been included in the letter, bring the script up and click Next
  • select 'Annotate the DOC', where the DOC has already been created for the intervention and change the resubmit date for 30 days in the future
  • finalise the QSS40 manually
  • the script will record the contact in IRS, change the expected response date on IRCD to 30 days in the future
  • once the intervention has been placed on hold, 'W' the intervention via the Review Summary (IRRS) screen

For Financial Institution Exchange (FIE) tool requests

5

Response received to third party information request + Read more ...

When the response is received, it may create a work item, for example:

  • ZDMS_Q146 - Question - employer requesting emp. Details Q146, or
  • ZDMS_QSS40 - 3rd party Questions - up to 10 free text QSS40, or
  • ZIAM_CRB_SCN - Cust Compliance Scan

Once the document has been viewed, the Digital Image Work Item must be closed. See Viewing Centrelink customers digital images on how to close the work item in Customer First or in Process Direct.

Make sure the intervention DOC has been annotated with the details provided including the name of the third party and update the Review Contact Details (IRCD) screen with 'Y' in the Response field and enter the 'Date of response'.

Once the information is returned, make sure the record is updated if there is a risk of customer incurring further overpayment while waiting for other correspondence.

If all information has been received, the intervention is ready to be assessed and finalised. See Assessing and finalising Unearned Income Interventions (UII).

6

Information has not been provided QSS40 requests + Read more ...

Before contacting the third parties about failure to return the requests make sure:

  • the original request was sent
  • the request has not been returned

Phone the third party to ascertain the correct postal address and identify any reasons for delay in responding.

If the third party says they have the request and need more time to send the required information, get a time frame and extend the review on IRCD screen.

Offer to send the request by fax instead.

To issue another QSS40 request to the third party/institution:

  • select General Review Action Script
  • select option QSS40/QSS41 (dom circs)
  • under the 'Issue Review of Domestic Circumstances Regarding 2nd' is selected. The date of the original previous request is required to be placed in the date box
  • make sure the original scope dates are used
  • check the letter is correct (in particular check the correct contact phone number is included in the letter)
  • if a 13 number is displayed, enter DATA in the Command box, and add the appropriate UII 1800 number or relevant phone number for FDC cases
  • finalise the letter (enter 'FIN' in the Command box)
  • the script will create/update the DOC, change the resubmit date for 30 days in the future
  • select 'Annotate the DOC' where the DOC has already been created for the Intervention
  • the script will record the contact in IRS, change the expected response date on IRCD to 30 days in the future

Note: for Business Income and FDC cases, send an ATO information request at this point. See Table 3.

7

Information has not been provided via the FIE tool request + Read more ...

If the bank has not responded by the expected response date, refer the intervention to the Service Desk. The Resources page contains a link to the CADO Service Desk Online form.

8

Information has not been provided Q146 Employer Report requests + Read more ...

Before contacting the third parties about failure to return the requests make sure:

  • the original request was sent
  • the request has not been returned

If there is no evidence the Q146 has been returned contact the employer by phone to see if there is an issue providing the information.

All reasonable attempts must be made to try and contact or identify employers.

If a phone call to the employer has been made and two Q146 have been issued and not returned, it is considered that all avenues have been exhausted.

Where needed reissue the Q146 using the General Review Action script.

Note: do not send a second request where it is obvious there will be no response.

9

Check all third party information has been returned + Read more ...

Is all the third party information returned and the customer's record can be progressed?

  • Yes, see Assessing and finalising Unearned Income Interventions (UII)
  • No:
    • request more information, or
    • a decision must be made whether or not to proceed using the information on hand based on the inability to verify any further information. This option must be fully DOC'd, explaining the basis for the decision
    • See Assessing and finalising Unearned Income Interventions (UII)

ATO request for information process for CO and QDO

Table 3

Item

Description

1

ATO request for information + Read more ...

The ATO request for information form can be used where it has been determined tax return information is needed to verify a customer's circumstances and no other option is available to get the information.

Income tax returns for the customer are needed for assessment of self-employment/other income.

The Resources page contains a link to the ATO information request form.

2

CO completes template + Read more ...

The ATO request for information template is to be completed by the requesting Compliance Officer (CO).

  • All compulsory fields must be completed (CO full name, customer details and CRN)
  • Request information required for the verification. If information in match is for the 2010/2011 financial year, only request tax return for this financial year and subsequent years. For FDC request information for the year prior to the match year if within the verification period
  • Only complete the Full Income Tax Return section on the ATO request form
  • In 'other box' on ATO Request Form, insert - Require accountant details
  • The request is then emailed to the site Quality and Development Officer (QDO)
  • Manually code the IRCD screen to place the intervention on HLD for 30 days
  • Generate a Display on Access (DOA) DOC, advising customer is not to be restored and must be transferred to UII prior to restoration or re-grant of payment/s

Note: if the form has not been completed correctly, QDO needs to return it to the CO for correction.

3

QDO actions + Read more ...

When an ATO request for information referral is received, the QDO is required to:

  • Check the request and make sure all details have been provided on the form, including CO details and contact number
  • Check the information requested is needed for the intervention. If not return the request back to CO
  • Once all details have been checked, the request is to be emailed to the Business Income and Bank Interest (BIBI) interventions mail box

Once the information is returned from the ATO:

  • DOC the return of the request
  • Update the IRCD screen with 'Y' in the Response field and enter the 'Date of response'
  • Reallocate to original CO

4

CO actions information received + Read more ...

Update the customer record with information received.

See Assessing and finalising Unearned Income Interventions (UII).

5

Information has not been provided - ATO information requests + Read more ...

Where an ATO information request has not been returned by the ATO by the expected response date, this date can be extended for a further 28 days.

If the ATO is unable to verify the customer's details, refer the match to a QDO for forwarding to the CADO Service Desk.

The Service Desk will follow up the match and advise what action is to be taken.
