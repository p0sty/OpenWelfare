Extension of time decision

Table 1

Step

Action

1

Extension of time decision (EOT) + Read more ...

If the application relates to a care percentage decision with date of event on or after 1 July 2010 (3 March 2011 for WA ex nups), see the Special circumstances and Date of Effect table.

An objection must be received in writing within 28 days (or 90 days for customers living in a reciprocating jurisdiction) after notification of the original decision, except an objection to a care percentage decision made after 1 July 2010 which can be made verbally (this includes where a customer objects to the date of event of a change in care). See Taking care objections by phone (CS). An additional 12 business days are added to the 28/90 calendar day timeframe to allow the customer to receive the decision.

A customer can request an extension of time to lodge an objection either verbally or in writing. The extension of time (EOT) application can be made with the objection or after the objection has been lodged.

Note: the additional 12 days for postage is not added to the timeframe if the applicant received notification of the decision via Child Support online.

2

Determining whether an EOT is required + Read more ...

Enter the date the original decision was made into the extension of time calculator, which will automatically add an additional 12 business days to the 28/90 day timeframe to allow for postage. Check the date the objection was received to determine whether an EOT is required.

For information about making an extension of time decision, the References page contains a link to the Child Support Guide: 4.1.5 Extension of time to lodge objections.

Is the objection received outside the 28/90 day time frame?

  • Yes, go to Step 3
  • No, the EOT is received at the same time the objection is lodged. Go to Step 5

3

EOT is required + Read more ...

Contact with the applicant is required, to advise them an EOT is required.

Make 3 outbound contact attempt on all numbers and use SMS, before issuing a

MX0-1 Unique letter using the UQ50.02 Request call re Objection grounds or EOT – Applicant letter to invite the applicant to apply for an EOT within a maximum of 14 days. If contact is unsuccessful and the response timeframe lapses, make an additional 2 attempts at different times of the day within 24 hours before finalising the objection.

4

Taking a verbal EOT application + Read more ...

If initial contact with the applicant was over the phone and an EOT is required:

  • advise the objection was lodged outside the required timeframe and can only be considered if an EOT application is made and granted
  • offer to take a verbal EOT application over the phone
  • advise that the respondent may be contacted as part of the EOT process
    If phone contact with the applicant is unsuccessful, send an MX0-1 Unique letter using the UQ50.02 - Request call re-Objection grounds or EOT - Applicant letter. See Letters Cuba Process Help

Using the Objection First Contact macro to document the conversation, ask the applicant the circumstances that led to the objection being lodged late.

Document the customer’s circumstances and reasons in their own words. Read the document back to the customer and ask for confirmation that their reasons have been correctly documented.

Go to Step 5.

5

Consider EOT application + Read more ...

Consider the following when making a decision on an EOT application:

  • the reasons for the delay and whether the applicant took action to make Child Support aware the decision was being contested
  • whether granting the EOT will cause prejudice to the other party, including any difficulties they may experience in providing evidence as a result of the delay
  • whether granting the EOT will cause prejudice to the general public, such as the fairness in granting an extension of time to the applicant when compared to other persons in a similar position and possible implications for income tested pensions, allowances or benefits
  • the merits of the objection

For information about the facts to consider in making an extension of time decision, the References page contains a link to the Child Support Guide: 4.1.5 Extension of time to lodge objections.

6

Contact respondent + Read more ...

If considering granting the EOT, contact the respondent to give them an opportunity to respond to the information that is being taken into account. Make 1 outbound contact attempt on all available numbers. If phone contact is unsuccessful, send an MX0-1 Unique letter with the UQ50.03 - EOT Request Respondent letter.

7

Finalising EOT request + Read more ...

EOT applications should be finalised within 7 business days to ensure timely resolution of the customer’s issues. Legislatively, a decision must be made within 60 days (120 days if the applicant is a resident of a reciprocating jurisdiction). A customer may apply to the AAT for review of the refusal of their EOT decision. See Extension of time (EOT) review lodged against Child Support decisions.

8

Granting an EOT request + Read more ...

If the EOT is granted, contact the applicant to inform them of the outcome. Use this opportunity to request additional evidence to support their objection if required and:

  • complete the Extension of time granted template
  • complete the acknowledgement of objection (with extension of time) data in the Objections letters macro
  • copy and paste the applicant text generated from the Objections advice/EOT macro into a MX0-1 Unique letter and attach the EOT report. Send to the applicant
  • copy and paste the respondent text generated from the Objections letter macro into a MX0-1 Unique letter. Send to the respondent
  • update the Objection Maintenance window to show the EOT is accepted and copy the UQ50.01 - EOT Decision and Right of Response letter into a new notepad with the subject heading 'EOT granted'. Documentation Cuba Process Help, Create or pend a new note

See Contact customer regarding Child Support objection, Contact respondent.

9

Refusing an EOT request + Read more ...

If the objection is not properly made due to the objection being out of time and an extension of time application was made and refused:

  • make 3 contact attempts within 48 hours to the applicant only
  • complete the EOT report template
  • complete the acknowledgement of objection (with extension of time) data in the Objections Letters macro
  • copy and paste the applicant text generated from the Objections advice/EOT macro into an MX0-1 Unique letter and attach the EOT refused template. Send to the applicant
  • update the Objection Maintenance window to show the EOT is refused and copy the EOT Refused letter into a new notepad with the subject heading ‘EOT refused’
  • ensure the file contains all the relevant documents listed in the objections file checklist and has been batch filed according to the objection records management toolkit
  • undertake follow up action in accordance with the Customer Management Approach (CMA) for Child Support

If the respondent has not been advised of the EOT application and cannot be disadvantaged by its refusal, do not send them a copy of the letter or report.

If the respondent has been notified of the EOT, copy and paste the respondent text generated from the Objections advice/EOT macro into an MX0-1 Unique letter. Send to the respondent.

Special circumstances and Date of Effect

Table 2: This table describes the process for dealing with Special Circumstances and Date of Effect for care percentage decisions with date of event on or after 1 July 2010 - (3 March 2011 for WA ex-nups).

Step

Action

1

Special circumstances and Date of Effect (Care decisions with date of event on or after 1 July 2010 (3 March 2011 for WA ex-nups) + Read more ...

From 1 July 2010, objections to care percentage decisions with date of event on or after 1 July 2010 are not subject to the extension of time rules that apply to all other objections. See Objections Cuba Process Help, Create an objection record for information about recording a ‘special circumstances’ application.

Child Support can make a determination that special circumstances exist which prevented the customer from lodging an objection or requesting a review with Centrelink within 28 days of notification of the original decision (or 90 days if the person resides in a reciprocating jurisdiction). In determining whether the customer lodged their objection within 28 days, allow 12 working days for letters to be delivered by post.

Determining the date, the customer was notified of the care percentage decision

Always use the letter issued by Child Support as the first time the customer is notified - even when Family Assistance made the original care decision. Family Assistance only provide written notification to their customers in very limited situations and do not notify the other party of the outcome of an original care decision if that person is not receiving FTB for the child. Therefore, the first notification of a care decision a customer may receive is more likely to be the Child Support letter. If the customer confirms that Child Support notified them of the decision prior to the date of the original decision letter from Child Support, for example, they were notified first by phone, the date of the phone call is the date of notification.

If a customer makes a special circumstances application, determine whether it is appropriate in the circumstances to extend the lodgement period. If required contact the customer to obtain further information regarding the special circumstances. If the customer cannot be contacted by phone, issue letter UQ50.02 - Request call re-Objection grounds or EOT - Applicant.

  • If successful, the objection or an application for Centrelink review can then be deemed to have been received within the extended timeframe for lodgement
  • The customer may object to multiple decisions regarding multiple care periods. Determine whether special circumstances exist for each decision
  • If considering granting Special Circumstances, contact the respondent to give them an opportunity to respond to the information being taken into account

2

Determine if special circumstances exist + Read more ...

Consider the information and available evidence to determine if special circumstances exist.

Examples of special circumstances may include that the parent:

  • was seriously ill or had an accident that stopped them from lodging an objection
  • suffered a personal trauma such as a death in the family or a natural disaster that caused damage to the parent’s property
  • had communication difficulties because of, or including, isolation, illiteracy or poor English-language skills
  • reasonably relied upon inaccurate or misleading information

Have special circumstances been established?

  • Yes, go to Step 3
  • No, go to Step 4

3

Determine if it is appropriate to extend the lodgement period + Read more ...

If it is determined that special circumstances exist, a decision must be made whether to extend the lodgement period.

Consider:

  • if extending the period for lodgement will prejudice the other parent. For example, will an extension that results in an earlier date of effect for the objection decision create a significant overpayment or arrears of child support?
  • whether the applicant made any efforts to lodge the objection earlier, communicate to Child Support or Family Assistance that the decision was being contested or raised their concerns in other ways. For example, a complaint to Family Assistance, Child Support or the Ombudsman

Go to Step 5.

4

No special circumstances + Read more ...

If it is determined:

  • there were no special circumstances which prevented the customer from lodging their objection within the prescribed timeframe, or
  • it is not appropriate to extend the lodgement period

the objection or review by Centrelink will affect the child support assessment from the date the customer lodged their objection or request for review

Update the special circumstances in the Objection Maintenance window and record the outcome. See Objections Cuba Process Help.

Proceed to Make decision and finalise Child Support objection.

5

Special circumstances exist + Read more ...

If special circumstances exist and it is appropriate to extend the lodgement period, if the substantive objection decision alters or replaces the original care percentage decision, the objection decision will replace the original care percentage decision. See Make decision and finalise Child Support objection, Centrelink care decisions and the Objections Cuba Process Help, View and update objection records.

Example:

  • M and F are assessed for the costs of the child A, who is in the shared care of M and F
  • M notified Child Support on 13 August that the care of A had changed on 1 August. Child Support made a care percentage decision on 26 August, deciding that a change had occurred, that M now had 40% care and F had 60% care
  • F objected to that decision on 11 December, explaining that they were not able to lodge the objection earlier. Child Support considered the objection and decided on 6 February to allow the objection, as M’s care percentage is 36% and F’s care percentage is 64%
  • Child Support then considered if special circumstances prevented F from lodging the objection within 28 days. Child Support is satisfied that special circumstances did exist. Child Support then considered if the discretion to extend the period should be exercised. Child Support is satisfied that F did not rest on their rights and that M would not be prejudiced by a decision to extend the period in which to lodge the objection. Child Support makes a determination under s87AA(2) that the objection was lodged within time
  • The assessment is amended from 1 August to use the objection decision percentages (M 36% and F 64%) to replace the original care percentage decision

If special circumstances exist, but it is not appropriate to extend the lodgement period to the date that the objection was lodged, do not exercise the discretion to extend the lodgement period. Process the decision as if there are no special circumstances. Go to Step 4.

Advise both customers they have appeal rights to the AAT on the objection decision and on the determination regarding the date of effect under s87AA(2).

The customer may lodge an application to have the objection decision, the date of effect or both components of the objection reviewed by AAT.

If further assistance is required to determine whether special circumstances exist, contact a Service Support Officer (SSO).

Update the special circumstances in the Objection Maintenance window and record the outcome.

Note: if the applicant has applied for special circumstances and the date of effect needs to be determined for the objection decision, consideration of special circumstances must be finalised in time for the objection to be processed within the legislated timeframes.

When a date of effect is determined, proceed to Make decision and finalise Child Support objection.
