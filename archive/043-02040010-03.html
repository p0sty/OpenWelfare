Employer registration process for PPL

Table 1: portions of this process can be completed by the Paid Parental Leave Employer Processing Team (PPLEPT) only.

Step

Action

1

Employer online registration + Read more ...

To access PPL Services in Business Hub:

  • employers with Australian based users must register online for a Provider Digital Access (PRODA) account
  • examples of Australian based users are personnel and Human Resources (HR) staff

Existing users registered in PRODA can access PPL Services through their myGovID.

To participate in the PPL scheme employers must have an Australian Business, confirmed by an Australian Business Number (ABN).

The Resources page has links to:

  • Business Hub website
  • PRODA website, and
  • Business Hub User Guides. These include:
    • Registering your organisation for Business Hub
    • Individual PRODA registration for Business Hub
    • Managing Personnel
    • Registering for Paid Parental Leave Services
    • Paid Parental Leave Services

If an Australian organisation has users who work overseas, these users may not be able to register for PRODA to access Business Hub. This is because they are unable to satisfy PRODA authentication requirements.

For example, an overseas payroll section working for an Australian organisation and the overseas user is unable to meet Australian identity checks.

Is the employer able to register for PRODA to access Business Hub online?

  • Yes, go to Step 2
  • No:
    • non PPLEPT staff refer the employer to the PPL Employer Processing Team. Procedure ends here
    • PPLEPT staff go to Step 3

2

Employer registration for PRODA + Read more ...

If the employer does not have overseas users, procedure ends here.

  • If the employer has overseas users and they are unable to register for PRODA:
    • non PPLEPT staff refer the employer to the PPL Employer Processing Team. Procedure ends here
    • PPLEPT staff, go to Step 4

3

Employer cannot register for PRODA to access Business Hub online + Read more ...

In some instances an employer may not be able to, or does not want to, register for online services. This may be due to the employer’s location being remote with limited or no internet access.

The PPLEPT helps the employer choose the registration services they need to manage their PPL employer obligations.

If an employer has:

  • overseas based users - for example HR and personnel. If the employer has difficulties registering for PRODA to access Business Hub online, refer them to the:
    • PRODA helpdesk. The eBusiness staff will confirm the employer and overseas users have the necessary Australian identification requirements to register for PRODA and/or
    • user guides for more PRODA registration information. Resources has a link

Procedure ends here

  • overseas users who cannot register for PRODA to access Business Hub, go to Step 4
  • limited or no internet access - for example the employer is in a remote area. If they cannot do their PPL business online or refuse to use online services, go to Step 6

4

Centrelink Business Online Services (CBOS) registration for overseas users + Read more ...

If an associate of an organisation advises the PPLEPT that the PRODA helpdesk has confirmed their overseas users cannot register for PRODA:

  • the PPLEPT must tell the employer that access to CBOS for their overseas user will only be provided if the organisation has:
    • registered for PRODA and PPLS in Business Hub. Refer the employer to the Business Hub User Guides for registration, and
    • replied to the Request for information to access CBOS for PPL purposes email template and provided the required details

Once registered for Business Hub the organisation will have access to both systems and continue to use CBOS until overseas users are transitioned.

After the employer confirms the individual’s PRODA registration, the PPLEPT will:

  • ask for the associate of the organisation’s email address
  • advise the agency will email them requesting their contact details and those of their overseas users

When the email address is confirmed:

  • update the address in the system, if required
  • send the associate the email template - Request for information to access CBOS for PPL purposes from the PPLINFO mailbox. Resources has a link

Note: the template allows the employer to give their own details and those of their overseas users who they nominate to act on behalf of the organisation. Do not change the template in any way. Always email the template from the PPLINFO mailbox.

DOC the employer record to reflect the discussion.

Create a new organisation record + Read more ...

Before creating a new organisation record, search for the organisation as set out in Step 7

Obtain the following details of the organisation:

  • Australian Business Number (ABN)
  • business name
  • address
  • Access Manager contact details, and
  • payment destination details
  • pay cycle details

To create a new PPE organisation record, in Customer First Environment B:

  • access the Organisation Search (ON) screen
  • enter PPL in the Stakeholder Code: field
  • enter the full name of the organisation in the Org Name: field
  • enter ADD in the Act: field
  • complete the Source: and DOR: fields
  • enter PPE in the Svc Rsn: fields
  • press [Enter]
  • a message should appear I046CU - No records found meeting the search criteria
  • select Add Organisation, or enter AO in the Nxt: field and press [Enter]

Add organisation details + Read more ...

Access the Add Organisation (AO) screen and complete:

  • the BUS Name: TRA name: and ABN fields
  • add the organisation’s physical and postal addresses
  • if required, add the nominated overseas user’s details and their position from the email sent by the associate of the organisation. Services Officers must code the Position: field
    • add the phone number or follow up with organisation if not provided
    • add the email details of the nominated overseas user
  • select Add Organisation, or type Y in the Add Organisation (Y/N): field, and press [Enter]

The Paid Parental Leave Organisation Selector (P1OTS) screen will be displayed:

The following options will be pre-selected on this screen:

  • OSCS Organisation Contact
  • ADS Organisation Address
  • P1BL Paid Parental Leave Business Line

Select the Complete Activity check box, then select Continue

  • the Organisation Contact Summary (!OSCS) screen will display, complete the details for the contact person on the business. Select Continue
  • the Organisation Address (!ADS) screen will display, complete the address details for the business. Note: only a postal address is required. Select Continue
  • the Paid Parental Leave Business Line (P1BL) screen will display, select the relevant option from the employer opt in status and number of employees dropdown. Select Continue

If the Complete Activity check box was selected on the P1OTS screen, this activity will auto complete, otherwise manually select the activity from the AL screen to be finalised.

Step

1

Request to add or update an overseas user + Read more ...

Most employers will do their PPL business with Services Australia through Business Hub.

Some Australian Businesses who have overseas users will need to update user or employer details in Registration and Access Management for Business (RAMB).

Requests to add an overseas-based user to RAMB can be received by phone or in writing.

All overseas users must try to register with PRODA. Some overseas users may have Australian identification documents to confirm their identity in PRODA.

An overseas user will confirm with PRODA helpdesk - eBusiness they are not able to register. This is because they do not meet Australian Proof of Identity requirements.

If an associate of an organisation has confirmed their overseas users cannot register for PRODA:

  • The PPLEPT must tell the employer that access to CBOS for their overseas user will only be provided if the organisation has:
    • registered for PRODA and PPLS in Business Hub. Refer the employer to the Business Hub User Guides for registration, and
    • replied to the Request for information to access CBOS for PPL purposes email template and provided the required details

Once registered for Business Hub the organisation will have access to both systems and continue to use CBOS until overseas users are transitioned.

Before creating a new user ID, the overseas user must provide details to pass an identity check.

Proof of identity can be obtained verbally, and can include:

  • full name and their association with the business
  • organisation's Customer Reference Number (CRN)
  • Australian Business Number (ABN)
  • trading name
  • business address
  • date of birth
  • driver's licence or passport number
  • contact details such as their email, phone number and country where they are located

If a new user ID is created or if the overseas user has an existing user ID:

  • check the user’s record in RAMB, and
  • update if required:
  • enter International as the employee ID, and
  • PPL as the service code

Note: including the word International will identify the user is located overseas and not Australian based

Has their identity and association with the business been confirmed?

  • Yes, go to Step 2
  • No, the user cannot be added. Do not create a user ID
    • make one genuine attempt to contact the employer to advise of the information required to add a user. If unable to establish contact, send the 'Please contact us' approved email text. The 'Please contact us' email must only be used in approved circumstances
    • DOC the record
    • close the request
    • procedure ends here

Managing the Organisation Contact Summary (OSCS) screen

Table 6: this process is for PPLEPT staff only

Step

Action

1

Management of an authorised representative + Read more ...

When contact is made with an employer, check the Organisation Contact Summary (OSCS) screen in Customer First to confirm the listed authorised representative. See Resources for information on access role definitions.

If the organisation is registered for Business Hub, compare the details under the Business Hub Access Management tile in Process Direct.

Centrelink Business Online Services (CBOS) transitioned employers

When Business Hub replaced CBOS, employers had a period to transition to Business Hub before CBOS was locked. In this transition, the authorised officer/contact roles were not auto selected by the system, therefore Process Direct and the OSCS screen are not aligned. If an authorised contact has transitioned to Business Hub from CBOS, select the relevant roles in Process Direct to reflect correctly on the OSCS screen.

Authorised officer/contact updates on the OSCS screen:

  • if an existing authorised contact is no longer an authorised representative for the organisation, go to Step 2
  • if an authorised representative displays multiple times on the OSCS screen, go to Step 3
  • if the organisation has Business Hub access, but the individual authorised contact has not transitioned to Business Hub, go to Step 4

2

Existing contact is no longer an authorised representative for the employer + Read more ...

The employer may advise that an existing authorised representative is no longer acting in that role for the organisation.

Does the organisation have Business Hub access?

  • Yes, for updating employer contacts in Business Hub, see Managing personnel and updating details in Business Hub. Procedure ends here
  • No, go to Step 5

3

Contact is displaying multiple times on OSCS screen + Read more ...

If an authorised representative has multiple access roles (for example, contact officer and authorised officer), duplicate lines will display on the OSCS screen.

Duplicate lines may create where a previous Centrelink Business Online Services (CBOS) user becomes a Business Hub user. The duplicating line can result in email notifications generating for each access role the contact is assigned.

If multiple entries are displaying on the OSCS screen for the authorised representative, and:

  • the authorised officer role is one of the entries, keep the authorised officer role and end date all other entries on the OSCS screen, go to Step 5
  • the authorised officer role is not one of the entries, keep the contact officer role and end date all other entries on the OSCS screen, go to Step 5

4

Individual contact not transitioned to Business Hub + Read more ...

Where the organisation has transitioned to Business Hub, there may be authorised contacts listed on the OSCS screen who have not transitioned to Business Hub.

Before any updates can be made to the OSCS screen, in Process Direct (PD), select the Business Hub Access Management tile to check if there is a current authorised officer. An individual contact may advise that the authorised officer has left the business and no one else was given that role. An employer contact is unable to assign the authorised officer role to themselves.

Is there a current authorised officer?

  • Yes, end date contacts not listed in Business Hub Access Management. Go to Step 5
  • No, and in PD, authorised officers listed:
    • tell the employer the organisation needs to nominate an authorised officer and an access manager in Business Hub
    • DOC the record, including no updates required
    • procedure ends here
  • No, and in PD, no other authorised officers are listed:
    • the PPLEPT service officer (SO) will need to do a POI check and check the users association with the organisation, refer to Managing personnel and updating details in Business Hub,
    • PPLEPT SO if satisfied will need to tick the authorised officer role on behalf of the employer contact.
    • DOC the record
    • procedure ends here

5

Updating Organisation Contact Summary (OSCS) screen + Read more ...

In Customer First:

  • Select the Organisation Contact Summary (OSCS) screen to end date the contact
  • DOC the record, including the contact details updated
  • Procedure ends here
