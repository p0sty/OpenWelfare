Reasons for issuing a notice of refusal

Table 1

Reason

Use this option when…

Current NOPB exists – verified

a verified NOPB is currently valid and the claimant is seeking another NTC.

Current NOPB exists – deemed

a deemed NOPB is currently valid and the claimant is seeking another NTC.

Insufficient reason or medical evidence for failing to submit the statement by the due date – NOC deemed

a claimant is seeking to amend either the Care Costs or Medical Services without evidence (as per current process). This option is not restricted to NOC deemed – this wording will be updated in the future.

Failed to submit the statement within 2 years from DOJS – NOC deemed

the claimant is seeking to amend a deemed NOC more than 2 years from the date of judgment or settlement.

Pre or post judgment or settlement –evidence and delegations to amend notices

Table 2: outlines evidence and delegations required to amend a notice of past benefits (NOPB) or notice o charge (NOC) at either pre or post settlement stage.

Reason for amendment

Delegation and evidence required

There is an identified processing error and services or costs have been incorrectly included

Service Delivery APS1 and all levels above can approve to correct the error.

No evidence is required.

Service has already been paid by the compensation payer

Service Delivery APS5 or above can approve to remove the service with:

  • a copy of invoice and receipt that was paid by the compensation payer

See Escalations and case notes for more information on escalating a work item to the Service Support Officer (APS5).

A care cost amount (in full or part) does not relate to the compensable injury or illness

Program Management APS5 or above can approve to review the costs with:

  • a letter from treating doctor that the services specifying:
    • some or all of the care costs do not relate to the compensable injury or illness, or
    • the increase in the level of care was not related to the compensable injury or illness

See Escalations and case notes for more information on escalating a work item to Program Management (APS5).

Pre judgment or settlement – evidence and delegations to amend notices

Table 3: outlines the evidence and delegations required to amend a notice of past benefits (NOPB) at pre judgment or settlement stage.

Reason for amendment

Delegation and evidence required

Valid NOPB all services taken

Generally, a notice of refusal will be issued in these circumstances, however a Service Delivery APS5 or above can approve to process on a case by case basis:

  • a recompleted statement by claimant with
  • written evidence from the compensation payer or claimant’s solicitor that the claim cannot be finalised without an accurate notice and must be endorsed by:
    • a judge and the date has been set, or
    • the Public Trustee

See Escalations and case notes for more information on escalating a work item to the Service Support Officer (APS5).

Valid verified NOPB

Consider a review if:

  • there are processing errors, Service Delivery APS1 can approve
  • there is an invoice/receipt from compensation payer that they have paid the account, Service Delivery APS 3 can approve
  • care costs do not relate, escalate to Programme Management with the letter from the treating doctor. See Escalations and case notes for more information on escalating a work item to Program Management (APS5)

Otherwise amend at expiry of notice or post settlement by processing new statement by claimant.

Expired NOPB

Service Delivery APS 3 can approve to process a statement to remove previously verified services with a clear statement by the claimant.

If unsure, consult a Programme Support Officer.

Note: this is not an amended NOPB. It is completed in statement by claimant processing.

Post judgment or settlement – evidence and delegations to amend notices

Table 4: outlines the evidence and delegations required to amend a notice of past benefits (NOPB) or notice of charge (NOC) at post judgment or settlement stage.

Reason for amendment

Delegation and evidence required

Within 2 years of a judgment or settlement being fixed, a request to reduce NOPB or NOCH due to apportionment of liability to the injured person

Service Delivery APS3 can approve to reduce the NOC/NOPB-AP by the percentage of apportionment applied where written evidence has been supplied by the compensation payer.

A change only in the date of injury or illness (DOI) at date of judgment or settlement (DOJS)
  • Consider if a new notice to claimant is required, see Medicare Compensation Recovery for more information
  • If the only change is the DOI advised on the notice of judgement or settlement (NOJS) is later than the previous registered date, Service Delivery APS3 can amend DOI and re-process to remove any services before the new date of injury

NOC all services taken
  • The Health and Other Services (Compensation) Act 1995 (the HOSC Act) provides for a review within 2 years of DOS
  • Service Delivery APS1 and above can process a valid statement by claimant.

Verified NOC – valid at DOJS
  • No provision in the HOSC Act to review a notice resulting from a verified statement, however consideration for review will be given within 2 years of DOJS
  • Injured person/claimant must apply in writing and evidence may be required

Possible scenarios

Previously verified services are clearly unrelated to the injury or illness, Service Delivery APS5 can approve to process a recompleted statement by claimant.

Previously verified services that might relate to the injury or illness:

  • Service Delivery APS5 can approve to process:
    • a recompleted statement by claimant
    • a statutory declaration by the claimant that the services do not relate

There has been a change in the nature of the injury or illness at DOS:

  • Service Delivery APS5 can approve to process:
    • a recompleted statement by claimant
    • a statutory declaration by the claimant to explain the change in injury/illness and that the services do not relate
    • supporting valid NOJS

See Escalations and case notes for more information on escalating a work item to the Service Support Officer (APS5). If unsure, escalate to Programme Management.

Verified NOPB – expired at DOJS
  • No provision in the HOSC Act to review a notice resulting from a verified statement, however consideration for review will be given within 2 years of DOS
  • Injured person/claimant must apply in writing and evidence may be required

Possible scenarios

Previously verified services that are clearly unrelated to the injury or illness:

  • Service Delivery APS3 can:
    • process a statement by claimant requesting to remove previously verified services
    • consult a Programme Support Officer or Team Leader if unsure

Previously verified services that might relate to the injury or illness:

  • Service Delivery APS5 can approve to process:
    • a recompleted statement by claimant with
    • a statutory declaration by the claimant that the services do not relate
    • See Escalations and case notes for more information on escalating a work item to the Service Support Officer (APS5). If unsure, escalate to Programme Management

There has been a change in the nature of the injury or illness at DOS:

  • Service Delivery APS5 can approve to process:
    • a recompleted statement by claimant with
    • supporting valid NOJS
    • See Escalations and case notes for more information on escalating a work item to the Service Support Officer (APS5). If unsure, escalate to Programme Management
