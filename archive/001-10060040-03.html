Investigating a potential UNPP

Table 1

Step

Action

1

UNPP failure generated + Read more ...

Selecting the failure from the Participation Compliance hub will open the Compliance Investigation workflow and display Other related information.

Service Officers working in the service centre with a UNPP security resource are responsible for investigating and determining whether a UNPP at new claim has occurred.

PST-skilled Service Officers (WNPPD) investigate and determine if a UNPP has occurred while a CDP participant is in receipt of a participation payment.

2

Establish contact with the CDP participant + Read more ...

If the CDP participant's statement is not recorded within the Compliance Investigation workflow, the CDP participant must be provided with an opportunity to explain their reasons for current unemployment. The UNPP decision maker is required to talk with the CDP participant before making a determination to apply a UNPP.

If the UNPP investigation forms part of a CDP participant's new claim, service centre Service Officers with a UNPP security resource must consider if the new claim is likely to be granted or rejected.

If the CDP participant's new claim is to be rejected, the UNPP failure must also be rejected. This decision must be recorded in a DOC. For further information, see Request to reassess a rejected claim.

If the CDP participant applies for a formal review of the decision to reject the claim, or reclaims payment within 12 weeks of becoming unemployed, the UNPP failure investigation is to be regenerated.

For more details about attempting to contact a CDP participant to discuss the potential UNPP, see Job seeker contacts to discuss non-compliance or circumstances impacting compliance.

3

Accessing the Compliance Investigation workflow + Read more ...

In Process Direct:

  • select Customer Summary from the Process Direct landing page
  • enter the customer's CRN, select Go
  • Go to the Participation Compliance Hub (BIPCW) screen
  • select Compliance Investigation on the outstanding compliance action to start the compliance investigation and display the Key Information screen
  • select Next to be taken to the Key Information screen

The Key Information screen provides relevant CDP participant information and details of the UNPP failure, including:

  • details of the employment the UNPP relates to
  • information from the Provider Report, if one exists, and
  • a link to the Circumstances Impacting Compliance screen

The Service Officer (with UNPP or WNPPD security resource) is required to:

  • record if an interpreter was used or not
  • check, and (if required) update the CDP participant's phone and email details
  • select Next to continue the workflow

4

Commence the investigation + Read more ...

The Investigation screen will present. Commence investigating the circumstances under which the CDP participant left employment.

Access and review the Participation Report if one exists by selecting from the Other related information tab and select the relevant hyperlink to review other related information. When finished reviewing select the Investigation tab.

The Investigation screen will be pre-populated with details of the failure date and the reason for the failure.

The Service Officer (with UNPP or WNPPD security resource) will be prompted to outline the failure under investigation and read the privacy statement to the CDP participant.

If appropriate, record a response to 'Has the provider discussed the failure with the job seeker?' If required, record in free text the reason the CDP participant gave the provider.

Discuss with the CDP participant the explanation and record any more details provided in the appropriate free text field.

Consider the reasons given and answer the question 'Does the job seeker's statement suggest ongoing circumstances had a direct and significant impact on the failure?'.

If the answer is 'Yes' or 'Unsure', a link to the Circumstances Impacting Compliance screen will allow this page to be viewed and circumstances can be added and/or updated if required. See Circumstances impacting job seeker compliance.

5

Select the appropriate Participant Reason + Read more ...

Record the CDP participant's main excuse for the failure on the Investigation screen.

The displayed options will include only options appropriate to the failure being investigated.

  • Select a category
  • Select a reason for the category selected that best describes the reasons the CDP participant has given (or that has been further determined) as their main excuse

More details may display depending on the choice of job seeker's reason. More information may include:

  • Details of reported income
  • Medical certificates and exemptions
  • Address details

6

Gathering information and considering guidelines in order to make a determination + Read more ...

For participants who are Principle Carers, use the Rate Estimator to determine financial suitability if applicable (refer to Financial suitability for Principal Carers) and record the following:

  • Net fortnightly income excluding employment costs and income for the job
  • Net fortnightly income including employment costs and income for the job
  • Is the participant (more than $50.00 per fortnight) financially better off if they accept the job?

Record No in response to the question: Is this a relocation UNPP?

The Investigation screen will display information from the Employment Separation Certificate (SU1) for Services Australia generated UNPPs if this had been provided when the failure was generated.

Consider the reasons the CDP participant has provided for the failure and the reasonable excuse or other guidelines, see:

  • Unemployment due to a voluntary act or misconduct
  • Refusing or failing to accept a suitable job offer
  • Assessing reasonable excuse for non-compliance with mutual obligation requirements
  • Suitable/unsuitable work

Record the most appropriate response to questions as they present.

Is this an acceptable reason that warrants further investigation?

  • Yes or Unsure, further investigation is required. Go to Step 7
  • No, further investigation is not required. See Step 1 in Table 2

7

Direct and significant impact on the failure + Read more ...

Does the excuse indicate it had a direct and significant impact on the failure?

  • Yes or Unsure, further investigation is required. Go to Step 8
  • No, further investigation is not required. See Step 1 in Table 2

8

Avoidable failure + Read more ...

Does the excuse indicate it was within the participant's control and they could have avoided the failure?

  • Yes, further investigation is not required. See Step 1 in Table 2
  • No or Unsure, further investigation is required. Go to Step 9

9

Sufficient evidence + Read more ...

Does sufficient evidence already exist to confirm the excuse for the failure?

  • Yes, further evidence is not required. See Step 1 in Table 2
  • No, go to Step 10

10

Further evidence is required + Read more ...

Service Officers (with UNPP or WNPPD security resource) make evidence based decisions. When further evidence is required to reach a decision, the Service Officer needs to ensure any evidence collected as part of the UNPP investigation should be specific to the incident, and focus on the issues to be considered for this decision under the applicable legislation and policy.

When the Service Officer is requesting/awaiting further details or evidence to confirm the excuse for failure, the following will display for entry of information:

  • Evidence Due date:

When the Service Officer selects the option No - Phone call required to confirm the excuse for failure, the following will display for entry of information:

  • Evidence due date
  • Who do you need to contact about the participant's excuse
  • Summary of evidence provided

When evidence is required and a future date is set for its return, Save and Exit the screen flow using the 'Evidence required for JSCF failure' option. If appropriate, the contact trigger will be removed but will be re-instated on the Evidence due date + 1.

Procedure ends here.

Assistance required when making the decision to apply a UNPP + Read more ...

A Service Officer with a UNPP or WNPPD security resource can finalise a decision to apply a UNPP. Before finalising a decision to apply a UNPP if the decision maker:

  • needs assistance identifying or understanding the impact of a CDP participant's personal factors or barriers on the non-compliance event

PST Virtual Support Queue staff should use all available reference material to research their query and if more assistance is required, the Level 2 Policy Helpdesk should be contacted.

Determine if failure occurred + Read more ...

  • No, go to Step 3

Errors or omissions in the report + Read more ...

Have you identified any significant errors or omissions in the report submitted by the provider/failure submitted?

What were the answers to these questions?

  • Yes to both, go to Step 7
  • No to either, go to Step 6

Rejection reason + Read more ...

Service Officers must be satisfied this is the correct decision before going any further. This may require Service Officers to reconsider and confirm the main reason for non-compliance has been correctly captured earlier in the workflow.

Select the appropriate rejection reason.

Clearly record an explanation for rejecting the compliance action in the free text area provided.

Record further information for the provider to explain the reason for rejection, if appropriate.

7

Penalty applied + Read more ...

The penalty will be applied automatically when the questions are answered as above. It is essential the decision maker is satisfied this is the correct determination before going any further. If this is not the correct determination, the decision maker may need to satisfy themselves they have correctly captured the main reason recorded for non-compliance earlier in the workflow.

When the penalty is applied, as the decision makers, Service Officers must be satisfied the decision is correct under the Social Security (Administration) Act 1999. This may require Service Officers to reconsider and confirm the main reason for non-compliance has been correctly captured within the Compliance Investigation workflow.

When the penalty is applied, the penalty and penalty periods will be automatically calculated.

Note: any information recorded can be obtained by the Community Development Program (CDP) participant under the Freedom of Information Act 1982. The information which can be lawfully provided by Services Australia to Employment Service Providers is covered by Section 202 of the Social Security (Administration) Act 1999.

The Service Officer (UNPP/WNPPD) will:

  • check the penalty period and adjust the failure period start and end dates if required, see Manually adjusting non-payment period start and end dates
  • record a summary of the reason why the decision has been made, including any evidence or circumstances that have been considered
  • advise the CDP participant of the decision and their review and appeal rights

8

Waiver provisions + Read more ...

The Service Officer (UNPP/WNPPD) will:

  • advise the participant of waiver provisions when a penalty is applied
  • record answers to the following:
    • Are you satisfied the job seeker falls into the class of persons specified by the Secretary of the Department of Employment and Workplace Relations?
    • Will serving the UNPP cause severe financial hardship?

Is 'yes' recorded for both above questions?

  • Yes, the system will automatically record UNPP hardship waiver for entire duration of UNPP period and display the waiver decision. See Step 1 in Table 3 for more information
  • No, the job seeker does not meet the UNPP hardship provisions. Go to Step 9

9

Community Development Program (CDP) participant wants to apply for a formal review of decision + Read more ...

The Service Officer (UNPP/WNPPD) will record a response to:

  • Have they indicated they intend to immediately appeal the decision?
  • Record the application for formal review and code Payment Pending Review (PPR) if appropriate, see Non-compliance with compulsory requirements - review and appeals and Payment Pending Review (PPR) of decision to apply a penalty for non-compliance

10

Existing circumstances impacting compliance require review to confirm they are up to date + Read more ...

The Service Officer (UNPP/WNPPD) will be required to complete outstanding reviews or assessments of existing circumstances impacting compliance. See Circumstances impacting job seeker compliance

Is a new claim currently pending the outcome of the UNPP decision?

  • Yes, go to Step 11
  • No, go to Step 12

11

Advise service centre of UNPP decision + Read more ...

When the new claim cannot be finalised at the same time the UNPP decision is made, annotate the Progress of Claim DOC confirming the UNPP decision has been finalised. Service Officers are to include any other details that may help with the finalisation of the outstanding new claim.

Once granted, the payment status will be current at zero rate (CZR) for the duration of the UNPP when it is applied.

The CDP participant will not be required to comply with mutual obligation requirements during the period they are subject to the UNPP, however may incur a further UNPP if they voluntarily leave or are dismissed from other suitable work during the UNPP.

When the UNPP occurred while the CDP participant was in receipt of a participation payment, they should be encouraged to complete all reporting up to the start date of the UNPP. Reporting will not be required during the UNPP.

12

Further advice for the Community Development Program (CDP) participant + Read more ...

When the CDP participant chooses to serve the UNPP, advise the CDP participant:

  • 2 weeks before the end of their UNPP they will receive a message via SMS or online account, or a letter advising the CDP participant of their:
    • mutual obligation requirements once the penalty ends, and
    • if they are not sure what their mutual obligation requirements are to contact their provider to discuss their Job Plan

Considering waiver and hardship provisions for participants with a UNPP

Table 3

Step

Action

1

Payment under hardship provisions + Read more ...

Eligibility for payment under hardship provisions must be considered when:

  • investigating and finalising the UNPP, or
  • the Community Development Program (CDP) participants contacts either before the UNPP commences or at any time during the UNPP to advise they are in severe financial hardship

CDP participants currently serving a UNPP who contact a service centre regarding changes in circumstances should be referred to a Participation Solutions Team (PST)-skilled Service Officer (WNPPD) if a Service Officer with a UNPP security resource is not available at the site.

A consultation with an APS 5 is encouraged if the Service Officer making the determination is uncertain as to whether a hardship waiver should be applied.

When a decision maker identifies that an APS 5 consult is appropriate:

  • Smart Centre staff can select PST RJCP CCA Assessments from the Services Australia Workspace transfer menu
  • Service centre staff can use the local APS 5 consultation processes or contact a PST-skilled Service Officer 5 for assistance

Service Officer with UNPP/WNPPD security resource commences review action + Read more ...

When finalising a decision to apply a UNPP on the Decision screen, select one of the following options to commence waiver action:

  • adjust penalty period and waive, or
  • waive only

Has the CDP participant requested a review under the 'hardship provisions' while serving the UNPP?

  • Yes, go to Step 3
  • No, upon finalisation of the UNPP decision the Waiver Consideration screen will be presented. Go to Step 4

3

Go to the Review Decision screen + Read more ...

The Review Decision screen is accessed by navigating to the Participation Compliance Hub and choosing the Review workflow from the Compliance Investigation workflow for the completed UNPP.

Select one of the following options from the Actions required: field:

  • adjust penalty period and waive, or
  • waive only

Record reasons and supporting evidence obtained to substantiate the decision to make payment during the UNPP under hardship provisions.

Completion of the Review Decision screen will automatically go to the Waiver Consideration screen.

Complete Waiver Consideration screen + Read more ...

Record the date the CDP participant contacted to request payment under hardship provisions in the What was the date of contact for assessment of waiver?: field.

Note: this field will pre-populate with the system date, however, when a CDP participant requested payment under UNPP hardship provisions earlier than this date the date of contact should be recorded. This information can be located in the Evidence UNPP Hardship DOC on the Document List (DL) screen.

5

Assess severe financial hardship + Read more ...

Gather evidence of the CDP participant's liquid assets to determine if they would be in severe financial hardship as a result of the UNPP.

Severe financial hardship means the CDP participant has the following liquid assets:

  • $5,000 where they are a member of a couple, or have a dependent child. This includes the partner's liquid assets
  • $2,500 for all others

When the UNPP is determined while processing a new claim, evidence of liquid assets provided by the CDP participant as part of the new claim should be used when the amounts have been verified. If the liquid assets have only been verbally declared, verification will be required. When the CDP participant is currently in receipt of payment, information recorded on their record and other evidence may be used to determine the level of liquid assets available.

When a CDP participant is re-testing their eligibility under UNPP hardship provisions because they did not qualify for hardship in the first instance due to the level of their liquid assets, the unavoidable or reasonable expenditure for the Liquid Assets Test policy guidelines must be applied.

Will serving the UNPP cause the CDP participant to be in severe financial hardship?

  • Yes, go to Step 6
  • No, see Step 1 in Table 4

6

Establish whether the CDP participant falls within a 'class of person' specified by the Secretary of the Department of Employment and Workplace Relations + Read more ...

Gather evidence to establish whether the CDP participant:

  • has significant family and caring responsibilities, including persons with a dependent child
  • has an illness, impairment or condition that requires treatment and cannot afford this treatment after meeting their essential expenses
  • has a diagnosed cognitive, neurological, psychiatric or psychological impairment or mental illness, or
  • does not have access to safe, secure and adequate housing (which includes using emergency accommodation or an inability to meet accommodation expenses)

Evidence must be collected or be available to substantiate the assessment that the CDP participant falls within a 'class of person' specified by the Secretary of the Department of Employment and Workplace Relations.

Examples of evidence include but are not limited to:

  • verifying that the CDP participant or their partner has care of a dependent child based on the family assistance record
  • medical certificate, verification of cost of prescriptions or other medical expenses as well as other essential expenses to confirm that the CDP participant cannot afford treatment after meeting essential expenses
  • medical certificate, doctor's report or psychologist assessment verifying a cognitive, neurological, psychiatric/psychological impairment or mental illness
  • verification of residency in a refuge, social work assessment or other evidence that verifies CDP participant does not have access to safe, secure and adequate housing

Note: the 'class of person' must be re-established if the CDP participant is re-testing their eligibility for payment under hardship provisions.

If the CDP participant is required to provide evidence to support a hardship waiver, use the Request for Information workflow Evidence - UNPP Hardship. This Note will record the details of the request. The Resources page contains the contents for the free text update.

Is a waiver being considered as the CDP participant is unable to meet their accommodation expenses?

  • Yes, go to Step 7
  • No:
    • and the CDP participant satisfies the class of persons criteria for other reasons, see Step 2 in Table 4
    • and the CDP participant does not satisfy the class of persons criteria, see Step 1 in Table 4

7

Affordability of housing + Read more ...

The safe, secure and adequate housing provision allows for payment to be reinstated when the CDP participant is at risk of homelessness as they cannot afford their current accommodation and there are no other feasible housing options.

As participation payments are made in arrears, a CDP participant is able to have their payment reinstated at the point their liquid assets are equivalent, or less than, the combined amount of their next accommodation payment plus one fortnightly participation payment at the maximum basic rate.

To establish eligibility under this provision confirm the CDP participant's regular accommodation expenses including the amount payable and frequency of payment.

Examples of evidence for a non-homeowner:

  • Verified details recorded on the Accommodation Circumstances (AC) screen. Further verification would be required if the CDP participant asserts that this does not correctly reflect their circumstances, or
  • Rent receipts or a lease agreement or other written evidence of accommodation costs when a formal arrangement exists. A verbal statement from the supplier of accommodation (hostel, boarding house, parents if living at home) will be sufficient for informal rent or board

Examples of evidence for a homeowner:

  • a recent letter or statement indicating a regular mortgage payment is required
  • when the mortgage is being paid as a line of credit then hardship may be established if a regular payment is required to be paid

Confirm the CDP participant is at risk of homelessness if they cannot meet their regular accommodation expenses. The delegate is to be satisfied the inability to pay accommodation expenses is likely to result in the person's accommodation being put at risk. The amount of information and evidence will vary according to the type of accommodation and the terms the accommodation is offered. It would be expected if the CDP participant has informal accommodation expenses, that is, rent/board paid to family or friends, verification may be required to a greater extent to assure the delegate that the inability to pay is likely to result in eviction.

Determine if the CDP participant has sufficient liquid assets to cover immediate accommodation/living expenses by:

  • calculating the liquid assets currently available to the CDP participant and their partner (if applicable)
  • determining the maximum fortnightly payment the CDP participant may receive (basic rate not including add-ons)

Is the liquid asset amount less than or equal to the fortnightly payment amount plus the next accommodation payment due?

  • Yes, the UNPP can be waived. See Step 2 in Table 4
  • No, then a hardship waiver cannot be recorded at this point. Advise the CDP participant to make contact as soon as their liquid assets fall under this benchmark in order to assess a hardship waiver. The CDP participant should be warned that a reduction in liquid assets will be assessed against the unavoidable or reasonable expenditure test. When a critical or unavoidable expense is not incurred and the assets are depleted more rapidly than allowed, a waiver may not be approved or may be deferred despite the CDP participant not being able to pay their accommodation costs. See Step 1 in Table 4

3

Select the reason for making payment under hardship provisions + Read more ...

In the 'Please select the reason for waiving the penalty period': field, select one of the following:

  • Unemployment non-payment period hardship - full
    This option removes the entire non-payment period. It should only be selected if the CDP participant requests assessment of eligibility:
    • Before the UNPP start date, or
    • As soon as they become aware of the UNPP being applied or reapplied. Generally this means the CDP participant has contacted within 48 hours of receiving verbal notification of the UNPP decision. If verbal notification did not occur then CDP participant contact should be within 2 weeks of the date the UNPP decision was recorded. This is to allow the person time to receive and act on the notice when they have not been verbally notified of the application of the penalty and the availability of waiver
  • Unemployment non-payment period hardship - partial
    Select this option if the CDP participant requests an assessment of eligibility while currently serving a UNPP and the provisions for a full waiver have not been met. When this option is selected a new UNPP end date must be recorded. This date should be the later of:
    • the day before the waiver was requested, or
    • the day before the CDP participant met eligibility for hardship provisions

If necessary, further relevant information should also be included in the free text box to indicate the reason/s why the waiver was recorded.

4

Start date of payment under hardship provisions + Read more ...

  • If the waiver reason 'Unemployment non-payment period hardship - full' is selected then payment will recommence from the start date of the UNPP
  • If the waiver reason 'Unemployment non-payment period hardship - partial' is selected then payment will recommence the day after the UNPP end date recorded

Select Next to continue.

Check warnings and errors and correct any errors. Once all errors have been fixed select Assess.

Check the Entitlements and select Finish.

Add any comments to the Notes and select Finalise.

    • undertake normal job search requirements
    • continue to comply with current terms of their Job Plan unless an exemption from mutual obligation requirements is granted, and
    • record that the CDP participant is aware of these requirements within a DOC
