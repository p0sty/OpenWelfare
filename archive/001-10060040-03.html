Investigating a potential UNPP

Table 1

Step

Action

1

UNPP failure generated + Read more ...

Selecting the failure from the Participation Compliance workflow will open the Compliance Investigation workflow and display the Key Information screen.

Participation Solutions Team (PST)-skilled Service Officers (Eight Week Non-Payment Period Decision (WNPPD)) are responsible for investigating and determining whether a relocation UNPP applies.

For all other UNPPs:

  • Service Officers working in the service centre with a UNPP security resource are responsible for investigating and determining whether a UNPP at new claim has occurred
  • PST-skilled Service Officers (WNPPD) investigate and determine if a UNPP has occurred while a CDP job seeker is in receipt of a participation payment

2

Establish contact with the CDP job seeker + Read more ...

If the CDP job seeker's statement is not recorded within the Compliance Investigation workflow, the CDP job seeker must be provided with an opportunity to explain their reasons for current unemployment. The UNPP decision maker is required to talk with the CDP job seeker before making a determination to apply a UNPP.

If the UNPP investigation forms part of a CDP job seeker's new claim, service centre Service Officers with a UNPP security resource must consider if the new claim is likely to be granted or rejected.

If the CDP job seeker's new claim is to be rejected, the UNPP failure must also be rejected. This decision must be recorded in a DOC. For further information see Requested documentation not provided resulting in rejection of new claim.

If the CDP job seeker applies for a formal review of the decision to reject the claim, or reclaims payment within 12 weeks of becoming unemployed, the UNPP failure investigation is to be regenerated.

For more details about attempting to contact a CDP job seeker to discuss the potential UNPP, see Establishing contact with the job seeker to discuss non-compliance or potential barriers to compliance.

3

Accessing the Compliance Investigation workflow + Read more ...

Selecting the failure from the Participation Compliance workflow will open the Compliance Investigation workflow and display the Key Information screen.

The Key Information screen provides relevant CDP job seeker information and details of the UNPP failure, including:

  • details of the employment the UNPP relates to
  • information from the Provider Report, if one exists, and
  • a link to the Circumstances Impacting Compliance screen

The Service Officer (UNPP/WNPPD) is required to:

  • record if an interpreter was used or not
  • check, and (if required) update the CDP job seeker's phone and email details

4

Commence the investigation + Read more ...

Go to the Investigation screen and commence investigating the circumstances under which the CDP job seeker left employment.

Access and review the Participation Report if one exists.

The Investigation screen will be pre-populated with details of the failure date and the reason for the failure.

When it is identified the CDP job seeker received relocation assistance, the failure must be investigated by a Service Officer with WNPPD security resource.

The Service Officer (UNPP/WNPPD) will be prompted to outline the failure under investigation and read privacy statement to the CDP job seeker.

If appropriate, record a response to 'Has the provider discussed the failure with the job seeker?' If required, record in free text what excuse the CDP job seeker gave the provider.

Discuss with the CDP job seeker the explanation and record any more details provided in the appropriate free text field.

Consider the reasons given and answer the question 'Does the job seeker's statement suggest ongoing circumstances had a direct and significant impact on the failure?'.

If the answer is 'Yes' or 'Unsure', a link to the Circumstances Impacting Compliance screen will allow this page to be viewed and circumstances can be added and/or updated if required. See Circumstances impacting capacity to comply with compulsory requirement.

5

Select the appropriate Job Seeker Reason + Read more ...

Record the CDP job seeker's main excuse for the failure on the Investigation screen.

The displayed options will include only options appropriate to the failure being investigated.

  • Choose a category from the drop down list
  • Select a reason from the drop down list for the category selected that best describes the reasons the CDP job seeker has given (or that has been further determined) as their main excuse

More details may display depending on the choice of job seeker's reason. More information may include:

  • Details of reported income
  • Medical certificates and exemptions
  • Address details

6

Gathering information and considering guidelines in order to make a determination + Read more ...

Job seekers who are Principle Carers, use the Rate Estimator to determine financial suitability if applicable (refer to Financial suitability for Principal Carers) and record the following:

  • Net fortnightly income excluding employment costs and income for the job
  • Net fortnightly income including employment costs and income for the job
  • Is the job seeker (more than $50.00 per fortnight) financially better off if they accept the job?

Record a response to the question: Is this a relocation UNPP?

The Investigation screen will display information from the Employment Separation Certificate (SU1) for Services Australia generated UNPPs if this had been provided when the failure was generated.

Consider the reasons the CDP job seeker has provided for the failure and the reasonable excuse or other guidelines, see:

  • Unemployment due to a voluntary act or misconduct
  • Refusing or failing to accept a suitable job offer
  • Assessing reasonable excuse for non-compliance with mutual obligation requirements
  • Suitable/unsuitable work

Record the most appropriate response to questions as they present.

Is this an acceptable reason that warrants further investigation?

  • Yes or Unsure, further investigation is required. Go to Step 7
  • No, further investigation is not required. See Step 1 in Table 2

7

Direct and significant impact on the failure + Read more ...

Does the excuse indicate it had a direct and significant impact on the failure?

  • Yes or Unsure, further investigation is required. Go to Step 8
  • No, further investigation is not required. See Step 1 in Table 2

8

Avoidable failure + Read more ...

Does the excuse indicate it was within the job seeker's control and they could have avoided the failure?

  • Yes, further investigation is not required. See Step 1 in Table 2
  • No or Unsure, further investigation is required. Go to Step 9

9

Sufficient evidence + Read more ...

Does sufficient evidence already exist to confirm the excuse for the failure?

  • Yes, further evidence is not required. See Step 1 in Table 2
  • No, go to Step 10

10

Further evidence is required + Read more ...

Service Officers (UNPP/WNPPD) make evidence based decisions. When further evidence is required to reach a decision, the Service Officer needs to ensure any evidence collected as part of the UNPP investigation should be specific to the incident, and focus on the issues to be considered for this decision under the applicable legislation and policy.

When the Service Officer is requesting/awaiting further details or evidence to confirm the excuse for failure, the following will display for entry of information:

  • Evidence Due date:

When the Service Officer selects the option No - Phone call required to confirm the excuse for failure, the following will display for entry of information:

  • Evidence due date
  • Who do you need to contact about the job seeker's excuse
  • Summary of evidence provided

When evidence is required and a future date is set for its return, Save and Exit the screen flow using the 'Evidence required for JSCM failure' option. If appropriate, the contact trigger will be removed but will be re-instated on the Evidence due date + 1.

Procedure ends here.

Making a decision about a UNPP

Table 2

Step

Action

1

Consider consulting Level 1 Support if the Service Officer making the decision to apply a UNPP is not an APS5 + Read more ...

A Service Officer with a UNPP or WNPPD security resource can finalise a decision to apply a UNPP. However, when this staff member is not an APS5 or above, consultation with a Level 1 contact is encouraged before finalising a decision to apply a UNPP if the decision maker:

  • needs assistance identifying or understanding the impact of a CDP job seeker's personal factors or barriers on the non-compliance event
  • needs guidance to make a decision
  • is uncertain as to whether a hardship waiver should be applied

Level 1 contacts should use all available reference material to research their query and if more assistance is required, Level 1 support should contact the Level 2 Policy Helpdesk.

On the Decision and Penalty screen record a response to questions as they present.

Have you confirmed the requirement or job is not suitable, it is explicitly precluded by policy or social security law, or they can't be compelled to meet the requirement for another reason?

Errors or omissions in the report + Read more ...

Misconduct + Read more ...

What were the answers to these questions?

  • Yes to both, go to Step 7
  • No to either, go to Step 6

Rejection reason + Read more ...

Service Officers must be satisfied this is the correct decision before going any further. This may require Service Officers to reconsider and confirm the main reason for non-compliance has been correctly captured earlier in the workflow.

Select the appropriate rejection reason from the drop down list.

Clearly record an explanation for rejecting the compliance action in the free text area provided.

Record further information for the provider to explain the reason for rejection, if appropriate.

Note: any information recorded can be obtained by the CDP job seeker under the Freedom of Information Act 1982. The information which can be lawfully provided by Services Australia to Employment Services Providers is covered by Section 202 of the Social Security (Administration) Act 1999.

7

Penalty applied + Read more ...

The penalty will be applied automatically when the questions are answered as above. It is essential the decision maker is satisfied this is the correct determination before going any further. If this is not the correct determination, the decision maker may need to satisfy themselves they have correctly captured the main reason recorded for non-compliance earlier in the workflow.

When the penalty is applied, as the decision maker Service Officers must be satisfied the decision is correct under the Social Security (Administration) Act 1999. This may require Service Officers to reconsider and confirm the main reason for non-compliance has been correctly captured within the Compliance Investigation workflow.

When the penalty is applied, the penalty and penalty periods will be automatically calculated.

Note: any information recorded can be obtained by the Community Development Program (CDP) job seeker under the Freedom of Information Act 1982. The information which can be lawfully provided by Services Australia to Employment Service Providers is covered by Section 202 of the Social Security (Administration) Act 1999.

The Service Officer (UNPP/WNPPD) will:

  • check the penalty period and adjust the failure period start and end dates if required, see Manually adjusting non-payment period start and end dates
  • record a summary of the reason why the decision has been made, including any evidence or circumstances that have been considered
  • advise the CDP job seeker of the decision and their review and appeal rights

8

Waiver provisions + Read more ...

The Service Officer (UNPP/WNPPD) will:

  • advise the job seeker of waiver provisions when a penalty is applied
  • record answers to the following:
    • Are you satisfied the job seeker falls into the class of persons specified by the Secretary of the Department of Education, Skills and Employment?
    • Will serving the UNPP cause severe financial hardship?

Is 'yes' recorded for both above questions?

  • Yes, the system will automatically record UNPP hardship waiver for entire duration of UNPP period and display the waiver decision. See Step 1 in Table 3 for more information
  • No, the job seeker does not meet the UNPP hardship provisions. Go to Step 9

9

Community Development Program (CDP) job seeker wants to apply for a formal review of decision + Read more ...

The Service Officer (UNPP/WNPPD) will record a response to:

  • Have they indicated they intend to immediately appeal the decision?
  • Record the application for formal review and code Payment Pending Review (PPR) if appropriate, see Non-compliance with compulsory requirements - review and appeals and Payment Pending Review (PPR) of decision to apply a penalty for non-compliance

10

Existing circumstances impacting compliance require review to confirm they are up to date + Read more ...

The Service Officer (UNPP/WNPPD) will be required to complete outstanding reviews or assessments of existing circumstances impacting compliance. See Circumstances impacting capacity to comply with compulsory requirement

Is a new claim currently pending the outcome of the UNPP decision?

  • Yes, go to Step 11
  • No, go to Step 12

11

Advise service centre of UNPP decision + Read more ...

When the new claim cannot be finalised at the same time the UNPP decision is made, annotate the Progress of Claim DOC confirming the UNPP decision has been finalised. Service Officers are to include any other details that may help with the finalisation of the outstanding new claim.

Once granted, the payment status will be current at zero rate (CZR) for the duration of the UNPP when it is applied.

The CDP job seeker will not be required to comply with mutual obligation requirements during the period they are subject to the UNPP, however may incur a further UNPP if they voluntarily leave or are dismissed from other suitable work during the UNPP.

When the UNPP occurred while the CDP job seeker was in receipt of a participation payment, they should be encouraged to complete all reporting up to the start date of the UNPP. Reporting will not be required during the UNPP.

12

Participation Compliance workflow submitted to QOL + Read more ...

Due to system limitation, Quality On Line (QOL) may not display all workflow screens if the failure investigation is completed over more than 1 contact. Any screens not displayed in QOL can be viewed through the Participation Compliance workflow by selecting the Date of Event (DOV) of the submitted failure on the Participation Compliance workflow.

13

Further advice for the Community Development Program (CDP) job seeker + Read more ...

When the CDP job seeker chooses to serve the UNPP, advise the CDP job seeker:

  • 2 weeks before the end of their UNPP they will receive a message via SMS or online account, or a letter advising the CDP job seeker of their:
    • mutual obligation requirements once the penalty ends, and
    • if they are not sure what their mutual obligation requirements are to contact their provider to discuss their Job Plan

Considering waiver and hardship provisions for customers with a UNPP

Table 3

Action

1

Payment under hardship provisions + Read more ...

Eligibility for payment under hardship provisions must be considered when:

  • investigating and finalising the UNPP, or
  • the Community Development Program (CDP) job seeker contacts either before the UNPP commences or at any time during the UNPP to advise they are in severe financial hardship

CDP job seekers currently serving a UNPP who contact a service centre regarding changes in circumstances should be referred to a Participation Solutions Team (PST)-skilled Service Officer (WNPPD) if a Service Officer with a UNPP security resource is not available at the site.

A consultation with a Level 1 contact is encouraged if the Service Officer making the determination is uncertain as to whether a hardship waiver should be applied.

When a decision maker identifies that a consult is appropriate:

  • Smart Centre staff can select PST Virtual Support from the Softphone transfer menu
  • Service centre staff can use the direct number for these consults. The direct number must not be given to customers and is available by using Office Locator, searching Participation Solutions Team. The direct number for the Virtual Support Queue is identified on the 'Teams at' page and then select PST in the left hand column menu

Service Officer with UNPP/WNPPD security resource commences review action + Read more ...

  • waive only

Has the CDP job seeker requested a review under the 'hardship provisions' while serving the UNPP?

Go to the Review Decision screen + Read more ...

The Review Decision screen is accessed by navigating to the Participation Compliance workflow and choosing the Review workflow for the completed UNPP.

Complete Waiver Consideration screen + Read more ...

This information can be located in the Evidence UNPP Hardship DOC on the Document List (DL) screen.

5

Assess severe financial hardship + Read more ...

Gather evidence of the CDP job seeker's liquid assets to determine if they would be in severe financial hardship as a result of the UNPP.

Severe financial hardship means the CDP job seeker has the following liquid assets:

  • $5,000 where they are a member of a couple, or have a dependent child. This includes the partner's liquid assets
  • $2,500 for all others

When the UNPP is determined while processing a new claim, evidence of liquid assets provided by the CDP job seeker as part of the new claim should be used when the amounts have been verified. If the liquid assets have only been verbally declared, verification will be required. When the CDP job seeker is currently in receipt of payment, information recorded on their record and other evidence may be used to determine the level of liquid assets available.

When a CDP job seeker is re-testing their eligibility under UNPP hardship provisions because they did not qualify for hardship in the first instance due to the level of their liquid assets, the unavoidable or reasonable expenditure for the Liquid Assets Test policy guidelines must be applied.

Will serving the UNPP cause the CDP job seeker to be in severe financial hardship?

  • Yes, go to Step 6
  • No, see Step 1 in Table 4

6

Establish whether the CDP job seeker falls within a 'class of person' specified by the Secretary of Department of Education, Skills and Employment + Read more ...

Gather evidence to establish whether the CDP job seeker:

  • has significant family and caring responsibilities, including persons with a dependent child
  • has an illness, impairment or condition that requires treatment and cannot afford this treatment after meeting their essential expenses
  • has a diagnosed cognitive, neurological, psychiatric or psychological impairment or mental illness or,
  • does not have access to safe, secure and adequate housing (which includes using emergency accommodation or an inability to meet accommodation expenses)

Evidence must be collected or be available to substantiate the assessment that the CDP job seeker falls within a 'class of person' specified by the Secretary of the Department of Education, Skills and Employment.

Examples of evidence include but are not limited to:

  • verifying that the CDP job seeker or their partner has care of a dependent child based on the family assistance record
  • medical certificate, verification of cost of prescriptions or other medical expenses as well as other essential expenses to confirm that the CDP job seeker cannot afford treatment after meeting essential expenses
  • medical certificate, doctor's report or psychologist assessment verifying a cognitive, neurological, psychiatric/psychological impairment or mental illness
  • verification of residency in a refuge, social work assessment or other evidence that verifies CDP job seeker does not have access to safe, secure and adequate housing

Note: the 'class of person' must be re-established if the CDP job seeker is re-testing their eligibility for payment under hardship provisions.

If the CDP job seeker is required to provide evidence to support a hardship waiver, a Fast Note must be recorded using the Fast Note Template: Evidence - UNPP Hardship. This Fast Note will record the details of the request. The Resources page contains the contents for a manual DOC to be recorded when Fast Note is unavailable.

Is a waiver being considered as the CDP job seeker is unable to meet their accommodation expenses?

  • Yes, go to Step 7
  • No:
    • and the CDP job seeker satisfies the class of persons criteria for other reasons, see Step 2 in Table 4
    • and the CDP job seeker does not satisfy the class of persons criteria, see Step 1 in Table 4

7

Affordability of housing + Read more ...

The safe, secure and adequate housing provision allows for payment to be reinstated when the CDP job seeker is at risk of homelessness as they cannot afford their current accommodation and there are no other feasible housing options.

As participation payments are made in arrears, a CDP job seeker is able to have their payment reinstated at the point their liquid assets are equivalent, or less than, the combined amount of their next accommodation payment plus 1 fortnightly participation payment at the maximum basic rate.

To establish eligibility under this provision confirm the CDP job seeker's regular accommodation expenses including the amount payable and frequency of payment.

Examples of evidence for a non-homeowner:

  • Verified details recorded on the Accommodation Circumstances (AC) screen. Further verification would be required if the CDP job seeker asserts that this does not correctly reflect their circumstances, or
  • Rent receipts or a lease agreement or other written evidence of accommodation costs when a formal arrangement exists. A verbal statement from the supplier of accommodation (hostel, boarding house, parents if living at home) will be sufficient for informal rent or board

Examples of evidence for a homeowner:

  • a recent letter or statement indicating a regular mortgage payment is required
  • when the mortgage is being paid as a line of credit then hardship may be established if a regular payment is required to be paid

Confirm the CDP job seeker is at risk of homelessness if they cannot meet their regular accommodation expenses. The delegate is to be satisfied the inability to pay accommodation expenses is likely to result in the person's accommodation being put at risk. The amount of information and evidence will vary according to the type of accommodation and the terms the accommodation is offered. It would be expected if the CDP job seeker has informal accommodation expenses, that is, rent/board paid to family or friends, verification may be required to a greater extent to assure the delegate that the inability to pay is likely to result in eviction.

Determine if the CDP job seeker has sufficient liquid assets to cover immediate accommodation/living expenses by:

  • calculating the liquid assets currently available to the CDP job seeker and their partner (if applicable)
  • determining the maximum fortnightly payment the CDP job seeker may receive (basic rate not including add-ons)

Is the liquid asset amount less than or equal to the fortnightly payment amount plus the next accommodation payment due?

  • Yes, the UNPP can be waived. See Step 2 in Table 4
  • No, then a hardship waiver cannot be recorded at this point. Advise the CDP job seeker to make contact as soon as their liquid assets fall under this benchmark in order to assess a hardship waiver. The CDP job seeker should be warned that a reduction in liquid assets will be assessed against the unavoidable or reasonable expenditure test. When a critical or unavoidable expense is not incurred and the assets are depleted more rapidly than allowed, a waiver may not be approved or may be deferred despite the CDP job seeker not being able to pay their accommodation costs. See Step 1 in Table 4

Making a decision regarding UNPP hardship provisions

Table 4

Step

Action

1

Community Development Program (CDP) job seeker not eligible for payment under hardship provisions + Read more ...

Record the assessment of eligibility for payment under hardship provision using the following fields on the Waiver Consideration screen:

  • Is the customer in a 'class of person' specified by the Secretary?
  • Will serving the UNPP cause the customer severe financial hardship?

When a response of no has been selected for either of these questions, the system will automatically default the field 'What is the decision on this waiver action?' to 'Do not waive the non-payment period'.

If necessary, further relevant information should be included in the free text box to indicate the reason/s why a waiver could not be recorded.

Select the check box to indicate the CDP job seeker has been notified of the decision and use the Continue button to go to the Assessment Results (AR) screen.

Procedure ends here.

2

CDP job seeker eligible for payment under hardship provisions + Read more ...

Record the assessment of eligibility for payment under hardship provision using the following fields on the Waiver Consideration screen:

  • Is the customer in a 'class of person' specified by the Secretary?
  • Will serving the UNPP cause the customer severe financial hardship?

When a response of yes has been selected for both of these questions, the system will automatically default the field 'What is the decision on this waiver action?' to 'Waiver the non-payment period'.

3

Select the reason for making payment under hardship provisions + Read more ...

In the 'Please select the reason for waiving the penalty period': field, select 1 of the following:

  • Unemployment non-payment period hardship - full
    This option removes the entire non-payment period. It should only be selected if the CDP job seeker requests assessment of eligibility:
    • Before the UNPP start date or,
    • As soon as they become aware of the UNPP being applied or reapplied. Generally this means the CDP job seeker has contacted within 48 hours of receiving verbal notification of the UNPP decision. If verbal notification did not occur then CDP job seeker contact should be within 2 weeks of the date the UNPP decision was recorded. This is to allow the person time to receive and act on the notice when they have not been verbally notified of the application of the penalty and the availability of waiver
  • Unemployment non-payment period hardship - partial
    Select this option if the CDP job seeker requests an assessment of eligibility while currently serving a UNPP and the provisions for a full waiver have not been met. When this option is selected a new UNPP end date must be recorded. This date should be the later of:
    • the day before the waiver was requested or,
    • the day before the CDP job seeker met eligibility for hardship provisions

If necessary, further relevant information should also be included in the free text box to indicate the reason/s why the waiver was recorded.

4

Start date of payment under hardship provisions + Read more ...

  • If the waiver reason 'Unemployment non-payment period hardship - full' is selected then payment will recommence from the start date of the UNPP
  • If the waiver reason 'Unemployment non-payment period hardship - partial' is selected then payment will recommence the day after the UNPP end date recorded

Select Continue to go to the AR screen and finalise the activity.

5

Remind the CDP job seeker of mutual obligations requirements + Read more ...

Was the UNPP generated by Services Australia?

  • Yes, a 'RapidConnect' appointment may be required. For further information, see RapidConnect
  • No, when the CDP job seeker is present or on the phone notify the CDP job seeker that while receiving payment under hardship provisions they will be required to:
    • undertake normal job search requirements
    • continue to comply with current terms of their Job Plan unless an exemption from mutual obligation requirements is granted, and
    • record that the CDP job seeker is aware of these requirements within a DOC
