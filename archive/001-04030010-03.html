Assessing and recording a customer as the principal carer of a dependent child

Step

Action

1

Caring for a dependent child + Read more ...

Customer advises they are caring for a dependent child aged under 16.

  • If the customer is not in receipt of an income support payment, the Online Participation workflow is run at the initial contact with the customer to identify the appropriate service offer and to refer the customer to an Employment Services Provider if appropriate
  • If the customer has not been in receipt of a Centrelink payment and is claiming a disaster/emergency payment, a principal carer determination may need to be completed. Only one person can be the principal carer of a dependent child. Go to Step 3
  • A customer may remain the principal carer of a child during a temporary absence from the child's care, including where care of the child is contested or the child is abducted. See Child leaves customer's care/custody
  • If a child leaves the customer's care or enters their care, their entitlement to payments and services, principal carer status and mutual obligation requirements may be affected. A change of care must be confirmed before reassessing the new carer's payments
  • If the customer is a member of a couple (with a child already in their care) and wishes to swap principal carer status from/to their partner, confirmation of a change of care is not required. Go to Step 4
  • If the customer is currently in receipt of payment, go to Step 5

For help with the definition of a principal carer of a dependent child, see the References page.

2

Share the care of a child + Read more ...

Does the customer share the care of a child with another person?

A shared care assessment is required to determine which carer is the principal carer of the child. This applies even if one carer has not claimed income support. Only one person can be the principal carer of a child at any time. Customers with shared care who do not qualify as a principal carer but have a dependent child may still qualify for the 'with child' rate of benefit. Parenting Payment Single (PPS) customers are eligible to receive an additional income free area for any children for whom they have shared care but who are not in their principal care.

JobSeeker Payment (JSP) customers with shared care who are the principal carer are qualified for the 'with child' rate and have access to a separate income test.

Is a shared care assessment required?

  • Yes, and the Service Officer:
    • is suitably trained, see Principal carer determination in shared care arrangements
    • is not suitably trained, run the Principal Carer Determination Required Fast Note to refer the customer for a Shared Care/Principal Carer assessment. Select Auto Text, use Families > Updates > Principal Carer Determination Required. Select Confirm. Place claim on hold
  • No, go to Step 3

3

Process claim/reassessment activity + Read more ...

For a new claim, the Child Selection (CHS) screen is included in the screen flow.

For help with coding to assess a child already linked to the customer or to link/add a new child, including where the child is a customer or their record is in another environment, see Linking a child to a customer's record.

On the CHS screen, 'S'elect each child the customer is claiming for to present the Child Task Selector (CHTS) screen.

Ensure each child is:

  • included in the customer's claim assessment, and
  • updated as required for changes to principal care assessments

For help, see Child Override/Claim (CHOC) screen.

For JobSeeker Payment, Youth Allowance (job seeker) or Special Benefit under JSP conditions, go to the Principal Carer Circumstance Details (PCCD) screen (in NSS system).

An E551NM will occur if the Service Officer adds a child or changes details and the principal carer status (PCCD) has not been coded.

'E551NM - Child details recorded but no PCCD screen update'.

The Service Officer must code the Date of Event (DOV) and principal carer indicator 'Y' when adding a child or changing child details on a customer's record, and:

  • the customer is claiming or in receipt of JobSeeker Payment, Youth Allowance (job seeker) or Special Benefit under JSP conditions, and
  • the customer is a principal carer, and
  • there is no coding on the PCCD screen

If the customer is not the principal carer, and the SSA Shared Care (SSC) screen principal carer indicator is 'No', the PCCD screen should be updated to 'N' for principal carer status.

Note: if processing a Family Tax Benefit (FTB) claim in the FAO system, and the customer is in receipt of a NSS system payment, check the Principal Carer Circumstances Details (PCCD):

  • Update if required
  • This may need to be actioned outside the new claim and in the NSS system

For PPS if the customer is not the principal carer of that child, go to the child's Child Override/Claim (CHOC) screen and code reason 'OTN' to ensure the child is not assessed as a PP child. The child may only continue to be assessed for the purpose of the additional income free area if the customer retains entitlement to PPS for another child, or other child/ren. If this is the last remaining PP child, this will ensure PPS cancels.

Go to Step 5.

4

Change in principal carer status + Read more ...

If a customer gains principal carer status, they may become eligible for more exemptions and flexibility in their mutual obligation requirements, depending on their circumstances. It may also be appropriate to reassess whether the customer is on the most appropriate payment, taking into consideration their change in principal carer status. The customer may also be eligible for Family Tax Benefit (if they are not already receiving it). Issue a Q888 inviting the customer to claim Family Tax Benefit (FTB). See Resources page for standard text.

Check whether any other person is recorded as the principal carer of the child.

If the principal carer status is changing from a person that is not the customer's current partner, it is generally necessary to code the current Principal Carer 'off' before granting and/or coding the new carer 'on' to avoid system errors. For NSS system payments, updates to the customer's Child Override/Claim (CHOC) screen and Principal Carer Circumstance Details (PCCD) screen (in the NSS system) must also be completed. For Parenting Payment (PP) updates to the CHOC screen may be required, especially for PPS, as the Pension (PEN) system may still read the child as a 'PP Child (even if the level of care is not enough to be' See Child Override/Claim (CHOC) screen for assistance.

Ensure all current child details are coded correctly on the Child in Customer Care (CHC), Child Miscellaneous Circumstances (CHM) and the CHOC screens. Note: if the CHC screen already has a current In Customer Care (ICC) line - do not code a new ICC line.

Ensure shared care percentages are recorded correctly and are the same on the FTB Shared Care (FSC) and the SSA Shared Care (SSC) screens.

If the care decision is already present on the FTB Shared Care (FSC) screen for the customer or their partner - do not code a new care line from a later date.

If the customer's current partner is showing as the principal carer of the child and is in receipt of FTB, the FSC and SSC screens should not be updated, as this will cancel the partner's FTB.

Job Plans

If the Principal Carer is a Centrelink managed job seeker, their Job Plan may need to be updated to reflect their new mutual obligation requirements.

Centrelink managed job seekers needing to re-negotiate their Job Plan may have a new plan negotiated over the phone (if they have an Online Account) or may attend a service centre. If referring the job seeker to a service centre, make an Enquiry appointment type for the customer. Check Office Locator for service centre appointment requirements. The Resources page contains a link to Office Locator.

Where necessary, renegotiate the customer's Job Plan and ensure the Activity and Exemption Summary (AEX) screen is updated accordingly.

5

CHTS screen coding + Read more ...

From the Child Task Selector (CHTS) screen these screens must be coded:

  • CHC screen with the date the child entered the customer's care. Note: if the CHC screen already has a current In Customer Care (ICC) line - do not code a new ICC line
  • CHOC screen to include a line with the claim type from the start date of the claim or child's date of birth if required. Claim type is NSS for JSP, Youth Allowance (YA) and Special Benefit (SpB) and PPP for Parenting Payment Partnered. A child may be coded as a dependent child of the customer but may not be a principal carer child due to other child circumstances, for example, shared care percentage. In these cases for PPS, the code Other - Eligibility no (OTN) is to be coded. For help, see CHOC screen
  • Code the customer's relationship to the child in the CHM screen using the appropriate code in the Principal Carer Type: field
  • If the customer has adopted the child or is a registered foster carer, code the Child Miscellaneous Circumstances (CHM) screen
  • Shared care details must be coded on the SSC screen (whether customer has principal care or not) and also recorded for family assistance payments on the FSC screen.
    If the care decision is already present on the FSC screen for the customer or their partner - do not code a new care line from a later date

Note: a problem (P24845) has been identified where shared care is not being correctly applied when a child is in the care of a parent who has transferred their Family Tax Benefit (FTB) to their current partner (step parent). The Resources page contains more information.

  • Check/update dependent child income on the Child Student/Income Circumstances (CHSI) screen
  • For NSS system payments, go to Step 6
  • All other payment types, go to Step 7

Note: if there is no change in care assessment, and an error displays when coding 'Y' on the PCCD screen, insert a new line with the DOV as the claim and the previously coded percentage.

6

NSS system payments, PCCD screen + Read more ...

For NSS system payments, code the principal carer status of the customer on the PCCD screen:

  • Start Date: field, code the date the assessment is to start from (for example, date of claim, date child entered care)
  • PC Indicator: field, code Y if the customer has at least one qualifying principal carer child in their care, otherwise code N

If customer is not a principal carer, code the reason in the Ineligibility reason: field:

  • 'NDE' (No dependent children)
  • 'PPC' (Partner is Principal Carer)
  • 'NPC' (Not Principal Carer)

7

Check child eligibility details on Assessment Explanation (AX) screen + Read more ...

Before finalising the activity on the Assessment Results (AR) screen, check child eligibility details are correct on the:

  • AX screen for the service reason (AXNSS screen for NSS payments)
  • Pensions Child Summary (PNKDS) screen for pension children
  • PPP Child Summary (PGKDS) screen for PPP children

Record details on a DOC.

8

Advise the customer of any changes + Read more ...

Advise the customer of any changes as a result of a change to their principal carer status.

Advise the customer of their mutual obligation requirements.

If the job seeker is Centrelink managed and their requirements have changed negotiate a new Job Plan.

Check if an exemption from mutual obligation requirements is appropriate, for example, due to special family circumstances.

Customers with a child under 6 can choose to participate in ParentsNext.

Advise the customer of any change in their rate or other entitlements/concessions as a result of their principal carer status.

Principal carers may be entitled to:

  • 'With child' or 'higher' rate of basic payment as a single principal carer
  • Family Tax Benefit
  • Child Care Subsidy (CCS) and Additional Child Care Subsidy (ACCS) if they need extra help with the cost of child care
  • Youth Disability Supplement (YDS) for YA job seekers with a partial capacity to work
  • Telephone Allowance (TAL) (eligibility requirements differ depending on the income support payment and whether the principal carer is partnered or single)

Single principal carers are also entitled to:

  • Pensioner Education Supplement (PES) if undertaking qualifying study while on Parenting Payment Single (PPS), JSP (single principal carer), or if they transferred to YA from PPS at the time they were receiving PES and continue to study in the same course
  • Pharmaceutical Allowance (PhA)
  • Pensioner Concession Card (PCC)
  • More generous principal carer Allowance Income Test for JSP single principal carers

Single carers with at least 14% care of a child (under 16 years of age)

  • JSP or YA (jobseeker) only - may be entitled to the 'with child' rate of payment if they have at least 14% of a child under 16 years of age

Partnered principal carers receiving Parenting Payment Partnered (PPP) who claimed payment on or after 1 July 2006 and have an assessed partial capacity to work due to a disability can access the following supplementary concessions from 1 January 2008:

  • PCC
  • PhA
  • PES (where continuing to study the same course)
  • TAL

Principal carer customers who:

  • have transferred to PPP from the DSP transition group
  • have been assessed as having a partial capacity to work, and
  • were in receipt of DSS PES or ABSTUDY PES will only remain eligible for PES if they remain in the same category (that is, partial capacity to work) and are still completing the same course of education or study for which they were receiving PES while receiving DSP

9

Advise decision in writing + Read more ...

If 2 or more people are caring for the child, if the principal carer determination is changed, each person must be advised in writing of the principal carer determination, even if they have not claimed income support payment. For standard text to be used in Free Text (Q808 for a partnered person or Q807 for a single person) after a shared care assessment, see Principal carer determination in shared care arrangements.

When each member of a couple is claiming or receiving payment based on being a principal carer, ensure each person is advised in writing of the principal carer determination. The Resources page contains standard text.
