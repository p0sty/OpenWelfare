Debt allocation for various business areas

Category

Description

Smart Centre Call created activities + Read more ...

  • Document undetermined debt activity
  • Send to region XWA for allocation

2

Smart Centre Call identifies possible debt + Read more ...

Smart Centre Call staff may identify cases:

  • of an incurred debt
  • when corrective coding (which generates a debt activity) is not possible
  • that need further investigation or action

In these cases:

  • record an ACTDOC, and
  • refer to the customer's local service centre for action

3

Service centre created activities + Read more ...

Debt activities created due to a customer record update at a service centre are allocated to XWA for action.

Ripple debt activities

A ripple debt occurs when an action or activity on a record impacts on a connected record. For example, a child's record attached to a parent's record. The activity carried out on the child's record may result in a change on the parent's record. This is a ripple effect.

In cases where an activity creates a ripple debt activity, the customer's home office (service centre):

  • finalises the activity
  • documents the reasons for the debt
  • allocates to XWA

4

Debt Recovery Team created activities + Read more ...

This team:

  • does not have delegation to reassess records
  • cannot raise debts
  • transfers undetermined debt activities to XWA

5

Consolidated Team created activities + Read more ...

This applies to all consolidated functions:

  • Transfer debts from non-Customer Compliance Service Profiling reviews to XWA
  • Debts from Customer Compliance Service Profiling reviews are investigated by the team conducting the review
  • Transfer debt activities created from updating a customer's record by a Consolidated Team to XWA for action

6

Specialist Team created activities + Read more ...

The appropriate Processing Services raises debts on the following payments:

  • ABSTUDY
  • Assistance for Isolated Children Scheme (AIC)
  • Assurance of Support (AOS)
  • Community Development Employment Projects (CDEP)
  • Pensioner Education Supplement (PES)
  • Paid Parental Leave (PPL) (for debts raised against the employer)
  • Mobility Allowance (MOB)
  • Australian Apprenticeships
  • Status Resolution Support Services (SRSS), and
  • Farm Household Assistance (FHA).

Debts created by:

  • Centrelink Academic Reassessment Transformation (CART) reviews are finalised as part of the CART processing team review
  • Tertiary Collection of Student Information (TCSI) reviews are finalised by the Study Load Intervention (SLI) team

Identifying the Original Decision Maker (ODM) + Read more ...

The ODM is the staff member or team who made the original decision on a customerâ€™s record.

If a customer contacts with questions about a determined debt, the ODM must be known before an appeal can be actioned.

The ODM can be a:

  • team or staff member from a Business area, when:
    • questions relate to the cause of the overpayment. For example, start dates, rent details. The customer is not appealing the debt, but the decision that caused the debt
    • the debt was not raised by a Debt Raising Team (region codes starting with X, HU1 or HU2). This includes auto raised debts and FTB Reconciliation debts
  • Debt Raising Team, when questions relate to the decision to recover the debt or the calculated amount. For example:
    • the method used to calculate the debt, or
    • the application of a waiver when the debt was because of administrative error or unusual circumstances

8

Centrelink compensation debts + Read more ...

Centrelink compensation debts are either 'compensation payer debts' or 'customer debts', and raised by Compensation Recovery Teams (CRT). See Compensation Recovery Teams.

Compensation payer debts are recovered by the CRT.

Payment of customer debts is arranged with the Debt Recovery Team. See Recovery of Centrelink compensation debts.
