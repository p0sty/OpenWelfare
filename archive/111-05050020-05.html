Examples of appropriate and inappropriate Callback and ACTDOC work items

Table 1

Item

Description

1

Appropriate Callback work items

  • A customer has contacted to talk about a rejected claim. After discussing the decision using information on the record, the customer wants a further explanation. This is outlined in Request for an explanation or application for a formal review
  • Appointment profiles for a service centre state 'local booking only' and there is a requirement for the service centre to book an appointment and notify customer of the time
  • Customer has contacted to speak to a Financial Information Service (FIS) Officer, and no FIS officer is currently available to accept the call immediately

2

Inappropriate Callback work items

  • Customer enquires about the progress of a claim that is outside timeliness standards and has supplied all the required information. See Progress of Claim
  • Customer wants an explanation of a decision and the Service Officer has not tried to discuss the decision based on information on the file
  • Customer asks to speak to a certain Service Officer because they prefer to deal with that Service Officer
  • Customer requests to speak to a Service Officer whose name is at the bottom of a letter they have received and the reason for the call is not clarified to determine if the call can be finalised at first point of contact
  • Customer has enquired about progress of claim and claim is still within the timeliness standard
  • Customer has enquired about actioning scanned documents that can be processed at point of contact
  • The Callback work item contains information only and includes no request for a call back
  • There is an existing Callback work item regarding the same issue already on customer file

3

Appropriate ACTDOC work items

  • An Operational Blueprint procedure specifically states an ACTDOC work item must be sent to another site
  • A customer is responding to a request for further information that needs to be actioned on Customer Record, and that information cannot be coded at point of contact
  • A rescan request
  • A Disability Support Pension (DSP) customer advises they want to voluntarily register for employment assistance

4

Inappropriate ACTDOC work items

  • A claim needs to be reindexed
  • Customer requests to have claim withdrawn
  • Request to action a scanned document that can be processed at point of contact
  • Request to action an outstanding Customer Declaration Form (CDF) when a customer has already attended a CDF appointment or has a current Job Plan
  • Request to restore a customer with mutual obligation requirements whose payment is CAN/DNL (Did not lodge a Reporting Statement) or FRP (Failed to Report)
  • The ACTDOC work item contains information only and includes no request for action
  • There is an existing ACTDOC work item regarding the same issue already on customer file

Guidelines for using URGENT check box for Callbacks/ACTDOC work items

Table 3

Payment type

Urgent cases

Action

Education and Employment customers

'At risk' customers - where a customer is in financial hardship and meets at least 1 of the following:

  • has an eviction notice (or 1 expected shortly)
  • has dependants
  • is homeless or has applied for Unreasonable to Live At Home (UTLAH)
  • recent detention release
  • family breakdown recently occurred (risk of homelessness/danger)
  • advises foreseeable situation that may place them at risk if claim delayed

Determine the customer is experiencing financial hardship.

Create a Callback work item using the guided procedure. Select the checkbox to add the keyword 'URGENT'.

Service Officers need to add details in the Callback work item about what situation the customer can foresee and what risk they will be placed in if the claim or action is delayed.

Families customers

Indicators of hardship, include (but are not limited to):

  • homelessness indicator
  • recently arrived migrant

Additional indicators that a Families customer is in crisis or financial hardship, or at risk of being so, include:

  • customer is in receipt of an Income Support Payment (ISP) or has an ISP claim pending
  • the Paid Parental Leave (PPL) claim is past the nominated start date and the customer has advised they need the payment urgently
  • child or birth mother is ill
  • bereavement (child or parent)
  • change of care where carer has not had care of this child before (for example unexpectedly taken into care of grandparent)

Determine the customer is experiencing financial hardship.

For claim related enquiries, see Helping customers in crisis or financial hardship claim family assistance.

For non-claim related enquiries, create an ACTDOC work item and add the keyword 'URGENT'. The same indicators of hardship must be met.

Service Officers need to add details in the ACTDOC work item DOC about what situation the customer can foresee and what risk they will be placed in if the claim/action is delayed.

Older Australians, Disability and Carer customers

Indicators of hardship include:

  • homelessness indicator, or
  • recently arrived migrant

Additional indicators that this customer is in crisis or financial hardship, or at risk of being so, include:

  • customer is not current on any Income Support payment (ISP)
  • the claim is past the Key Performance Indicator (KPI) date
  • customer or care receiver is terminal
  • bereavement/death

Transfers to Age Pension

Make sure that customers who will soon lose qualification for their current payment (e.g. allowance to pension) are processed as a priority and at the earliest touch point to avoid placing them in financial hardship

Determine the customer is experiencing financial hardship.

Social workers assessing mutual obligation exemptions

A Callback work item must be sent if a Social Worker has assessed a job seeker as meeting the criteria for an exemption from mutual obligation requirements due to a â€˜Major Personal Crisisâ€™.

This applies to JobSeeker Payment (JSP), Youth Allowance (YA) (job seeker), Parenting Payment (PP) and Special Benefit (SpB) customers.

Using the Callback/ACTDOC workflow, social workers must

  • Select:
    • the correct Service Type
    • Office - 'JCS'
    • Category - 'Nosub'
    • Item Name - 'Manual Callback Template'
    • Urgent Enquiry - 'Yes'
    • URGENT check box
  • Provide more information in the Callback work item DOC to tell the Service Officer the timeframe and reasons for the exemption

See Exempting a job seeker from mutual obligation requirements due to special circumstances.
