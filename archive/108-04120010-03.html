Initial assessment of a claim for consideration under hardship

Table 1

Step

Action

1

Complex Assessment Officer (CAO) reassessments + Read more ...

Is the customer or partner being paid under assets hardship provisions and has there been a change in their income or assets?

  • Yes,
    • create a Fast Note, select Auto Text. Use Complex Assessment > Request for CAO action > CAO Asset hardship
    • include details of the change in income or assets
    • record how the notification was provided (verbal advice can be accepted)
    • Procedure ends here
  • No, go to Step 2

2

Assessment of customer circumstances + Read more ...

Check all customer income and asset details are current and correctly recorded.

If the customer's assets include investments, offer a Financial Information Services (FIS) appointment. A FIS can provide information about rearranging investments.

The case is ready to be referred to a CAO if:

  • a CAO has already reviewed the case and has advised a claim for assets hardship can be lodged, go to Step 9
  • a claim for assets hardship has been lodged by the customer
  • a real estate valuation has been completed in preparation for an assets hardship assessment, go to Step 7

For all other cases, go to Step 3

3

Pension affected by the assets test + Read more ...

Has the customer's claim for a pension been rejected, or have their payments been cancelled or reduced due to the application of the assets test?

  • Yes, go to Step 4
  • No:
    • the hardship provisions do not apply
    • procedure ends here

4

Income less than fortnightly pension + Read more ...

Is the total of all other income less than the customer's maximum fortnightly rate of pension?

  • Yes, go to Step 5
  • No, make a note of the income circumstances of the customer for discussion with the CAO later in the process. Go to Step 5

5

Amount of available funds limit + Read more ...

Are the customer's and partner's, if applicable, available funds less than the readily available funds limit. Note: the limits vary depending on the customer's income support payment.

  • Yes, go to Step 6
  • No, make a note of the customer's circumstances regarding their available funds for discussion with the CAO later in the process. Go to Step 6

6

Real estate assets + Read more ...

If the customer's assets do not include real estate, go to Step 8.

Does the customer want a review of the value of real estate:

  • Yes:
    • follow the process to request the valuation
    • when the review is complete, the case will be sent to a CAO for assessment if the customer's payments are still asset effected
    • record the information gathered for the CAO on an open DOC
    • re-submit the DOC to when the valuation is expected to be available
    • Procedure ends here until the valuation is complete
  • No, go to Step 8

7

Valuation of real estate returned + Read more ...

Check for a DOC on the customer record with information relevant to assets hardship.

If the value of the real estate has changed, complete an update, including any follow-up action. For example, restoration of payments.

An assets hardship assessment can continue if, after any real estate update, the customer's assets still mean they are:

  • over the assets limit and a claim has been rejected or payments cancelled, or
  • being paid at a reduced rate

Is an assets hardship assessment still needed?

  • Yes, go to Step 8
  • No:
    • close any unneeded DOCs
    • Procedure ends here

8

Contact Complex Assessment Officer (CAO) + Read more ...

Before inviting a claim under the assets hardship provisions, the case must be discussed with a CAO in the Asset Hardship Team.

A list of CAOs in the Asset Hardship Team can be found within Office Locator. See Resources page for instructions and a link.

Was contact made with a CAO?

  • Yes, go to Step 9
  • No:
    • check the customer's contact details are correct
    • create a Fast Note, select Auto Text. Use Complex Assessment > Request for Action > CAO Asset hardship
    • include information relevant to the assets hardship assessment including any items noted at Steps 3 and 4. If the referral is being made after a real estate valuation, this information should be on a DOC
    • advise the customer a CAO will call. It will be from a private number
    • Procedure ends here

9

Claim for consideration under the assets hardship provisions + Read more ...

If a CAO supports a claim under the assets hardship provisions:

  • Issue Claim for consideration under hardship form (SA233), to the customer. If both members of a couple wish to apply, each will need to complete a claim. They can lodge separate or dual claims
  • Advise the customer supporting documentation will be needed - see the Resources page which contains examples of circumstances where it may be unreasonable to sell an asset
  • Advise the customer to return the SA233 to Services Australia as soon as possible.

The delegation to make decisions under the assets hardship provisions lies with the CAO.

Recording the claim for assets hardship and finalising the process

Table 2

Step

Action

1

Progress of assets hardship claim + Read more ...

  • If the SA233 - Claim for consideration under hardship (SA233) has been returned and the claim is ready to be referred to a CAO, go to Step 2
  • If the CAO assessment of assets hardship has been completed and it has been:
    • approved, go to Step 4
    • not approved, go to Step 6

2

SA233 form returned + Read more ...

If customer is not currently receiving a pension, a claim needs to be indexed or a Social Application (SOA) shell created, whichever is the correct process for the claim type.

Check that the customer has lodged supporting documents confirming they are unable to sell assets, or that it is unreasonable to expect them to sell.

See the Resources page which contains examples of circumstances where it may be unreasonable to sell an asset.

3

Referral of claim + Read more ...

The delegation to grant or reject claims under the assets test hardship provisions is at the APS5 level, and all claims must be determined by a CAO

  • When the decision is made by the CAO under the Assets Test hardship provisions, it can be used to reassess a claim for payment or the rate of pension paid
  • Ensure all relevant documents are scanned. Refer the claim to the Complex Assessment Officer (CAO)
  • Record referral details in the CAO Referral Guided Procedure DOC
  • Procedure ends here

4

Customer circumstance change + Read more ...

Check the customer record. Has the customer had a change in circumstances since the case was assessed by a CAO? For example, notification of earnings.

  • Yes:
    • A CAO reassessment is needed
    • Ensure all relevant documents are scanned. Refer the claim to the Complex Assessment Officer (CAO)
    • Record referral details in the CAO Referral Guided Procedure DOC
    • Procedure ends here
  • No, go to Step 5

5

Assess claim or rate of payment + Read more ...

Go through new claim assessment if undetermined, as well as updating all income and assets before the manual rate is done on the Manual Assessment (MAS) screen

  • While in the customer's record, go to the MAS screen by coding 'MAS' in the Nxt: field
  • Code the date from which the manual rate applies in the Effect Date: field
  • Code 'MAN' in the Type: field
  • Code the reason code 'HAR' in the Manual Rate Reason: field
  • Code the Source and DOR. Press [Enter]
  • Code DOC in the Nxt: field
  • Record the details on the DOC of the manual rate, giving full details of the CAO recommendations, and review details
  • Code 'AR' in the Nxt: field
  • Select the assessment line with a 'T'. This goes to the RATS screen
  • Select the required assessment line with a 'M'. This goes to the MAR screen
  • Code the Frequency: file to 'DLY' to code a daily manual rate, '2WE' to code a fortnightly manual rate, or 'ANN' to code an annual manual rate. Note: annual frequency only available in the PEN system
  • If the customer is under Age pension age, code the Working Credit Rate Override (WCRC) screen
  • Code the manual rate payable for each component, as advised by the CAO in the New: field. If a component is no longer payable, code a new rate of zero. Press [Enter]
  • Check the result on the AR screen is correct
  • Code 'Y' to approve on the AR screen. Press [Enter]

6

Record details + Read more ...

Record all manual reviews as advised by the CAO.

  • Record the details of the assessment on a DOC
  • Advise the customer of the outcome
