Assessing a permanent blindness claim

Step

Action

1

Is the customer getting Disability Support Pension (DSP) or Age Pension (AGE)? + Read more ...

  • Yes, if a Request for Ophthalmologist/Optometrist Report (SA013) or equivalent information has not been received, issue an SA013 using the Request for Information workflow. See Resources for a link to the form. Go to Step 3
  • No, go to Step 2

2

Has the customer lodged a claim for DSP or Age? + Read more ...

  • Yes, go to Step 3
  • No,
    • Advise the customer no further action can be undertaken until a claim has been lodged
      Claiming DSP
      Claiming and re-claiming Age Pension
    • Discuss the importance of providing an SA013 or similar report from their ophthalmologist.

Also see:

  • Eligibility for DSP
  • Qualification for Age Pension (Blind)

Procedure ends here.

3

Assess permanent blindness + Read more ...

To be assessed as permanently blind, the customer must have one of the following:

  • Visual acuity as measured on the Snellen Scale after correction by suitable lenses of less than 6/60 in both eyes
  • A field of vision constricted to 10 degrees or less of arc around central fixation in the better eye, irrespective of corrected visual acuity (equivalent to 1/100 white test object), or
  • A combination of visual defects resulting in the same degree of visual impairment as described in the 2 points above

See the Resources page for information on whether other ratings on the Snellen Scale qualify a customer as permanently blind.

Does the customer clearly meet the necessary criteria for permanent blindness?

  • Yes, DSP Processing Smart Centre staff process the SA013:
    • Granting Disability Support Pension (DSP) (manifest), or
    • Transferring to Disability Support Pension (DSP) (Blind), or
    • Assessing Age Pension (Blind) claims, or
    • Age Pension (Blind) transfer provisions

Procedure ends here

  • No, go to Step 4

4

Customer does not meet criteria for permanent blindness + Read more ...

The SA013/report indicates:

  • the customer clearly does not meet the criteria, go to Step 5
  • on further assessment, the customer may meet the age and medical criteria for DSP, go to Step 6

5

Customer is not considered permanently blind + Read more ...

Is the customer current on DSP or Age?

  • Yes,
    • a Q999 must be issued advising the customer they do not meet the criteria for blind pension and their payment will continue. Include the Appeal paragraph in the letter
    • Create a DOC detailing the outcome and the reason for the decision (see Resources for DOC text). Procedure ends here
  • No, go to Step 6

6

Customer is under Age Pension age and may be eligible for DSP + Read more ...

If the customer has lodged a DSP claim, it is assigned to the Medical Assessment Team (MAT). See Medical Assessment Team (MAT).

The Assessor advises one of the following:

  • manifest medical eligibility criteria is met, including permanent blindness. See Disability Support Pension (DSP) manifest eligibility
  • the customer is manifestly medically ineligible
  • insufficient medical evidence prevents assessment of the customer's medical condition/s and functional impacts
  • there are doubts about the medical evidence authenticity. Note: MAT or the Health Professional Advisory Unit (HPAU) must investigate further by contacting health professionals listed in the evidence to confirm the diagnosis and prognosis
  • a current and valid assessment can be used for the claim determination, or a Job Capacity Assessment (JCA) referral is required to determine medical eligibility
