    FAQs for individuals

    Table 5

    Item

    Description

    1

    Question: Where can I find out more about the COVID-19 vaccine program?

    Answer: For information about Australia's vaccine rollout plan or any other COVID-19 updates, visit health.gov.au.

    2

    Question: Are COVID-19 vaccines safe?

    Answer: If you have questions about getting COVID-19 vaccines, you should contact your health professional or the National Coronavirus Helpline on 1800 020 080.

    3

    Question: I heard that a new COVID-19 vaccine was approved. When can I get it?

    Answer: You should speak with your healthcare provider to discuss vaccinations or call the National Coronavirus Helpline on 1800 020 080.

    For information about the vaccine rollout plan or any other COVID-19 updates, visit health.gov.au.

    4

    Question: I work in frontline or essential services, do I have to get a COVID-19 vaccine or booster dose?

    Answer: For information about the vaccine rollout plan or any other COVID-19 updates, visit health.gov.au.

    You can also call the National Coronavirus Helpline on 1800 020 080 for information about getting vaccinated.

    5

    Question: Do I/my kids have to have a COVID-19 vaccine or booster dose for school, work or travel?

    Answer: You should speak with your healthcare provider to discuss vaccinations or call the National Coronavirus Helpline on 1800 020 080.

    For information about the vaccine rollout plan or any other COVID-19 updates, visit health.gov.au.

    6

    Question: Can I/ my kids get an exemption from having COVID-19 vaccinations?

    Answer: You may be able to get a medical contraindication recorded for a COVID-19 vaccine if there is a valid medical reason.

    Medical contraindications can be recorded on the AIR by an eligible health professional. If you have one recorded, it will show on your immunisation history statement and your COVID-19 digital certificate.

    Speak to your health professional for information about medical contraindications.

    For more information see Immunisation medical exemptions in Australian Immunisation Register (AIR).

    7

    Question: Do I/ my kids have to show proof of vaccinations to enter businesses or travel interstate?

    Answer: You should check state or territory government websites for information about requirements for proof of vaccinations.

    8

    Question: When can I/ my kids can be vaccinated and when will I be told?

    Answer: To stay up to date with Australia's vaccine rollout plan or any other COVID-19 updates, visit health.gov.au

    To check if you can book a COVID-19 vaccination, go to the COVID-19 Vaccine Clinic Finder.

    9

    Question: Where do I/my kids go to be vaccinated?

    Answer: For information about where you can get vaccinated, go to the COVID-19 Vaccine Clinic Finder

    10

    Question: I’m/my kids are booked in for the first dose of a COVID-19 vaccine, what do I do?

    Answer: You should contact your vaccination provider to discuss your vaccination.

    You can also call the National Coronavirus Helpline on 1800 020 080 for more information about getting vaccinated

    11

    Question: What if I’m worried about side effects? I/my kids have had a COVID-19 vaccination and I think I’m having side effects.

    Answer: If you’re worried about side effects, you should contact your doctor or the National Coronavirus Helpline on 1800 020 080.

    You can also use the COVID-19 Vaccine Side Effect Checker on the HealthDirect website.

    12

    Question: How do I prove I/my kids have been vaccinated?

    Answer: Every vaccination you get shows on your immunisation history statement after it has been reported to the Australian Immunisation Register (AIR) by a vaccination provider. This includes COVID-19, flu, NIP and travel vaccines.

    If you’ve had at least one dose of an approved or recognised COVID-19 vaccine, you can also get a COVID-19 digital certificate. The digital certificate will only show your COVID-19 vaccines.

    If you’re travelling overseas and had at least one COVID-19 vaccination recorded on the AIR, you can request an International COVID-19 Vaccination Certificate online.

    13

    Question: How do I get proof of my vaccinations?

    Answer: If you're eligible for Medicare, you can get an immunisation history statement, COVID-19 digital certificate or International COVID-19 Vaccination Certificate online by:

    • making sure you have a myGov account
    • linking Medicare online

    If you're not eligible for Medicare, you can get an immunisation history statement, COVID-19 digital certificate or International COVID-19 Vaccination Certificate online by:

    • registering for an Individual Healthcare Identifier (IHI)
    • using the IHI service or register for a My Health Record account using your IHI in myGov

    You can also:

    • check your immunisation record
    • update your details, or
    • request proof of vaccinations by going to a service centre or calling the Australian Immunisation Register on 1800 653 809

    14

    Question: Can I get proof of my child’s/children’s COVID-19 vaccinations?

    Answer: If your child/children is/are under 14 years, you can get proof of their vaccinations online through myGov.

    If your child is 14 or older, you won't be able to access their vaccination information. People who are 14 or older can access their information:

    • online, or
    • give consent over the phone or in person for their parent or guardian to access this information on their behalf. This consent is valid for that instance only

    15

    Question: I have caring responsibility for someone. Can I get proof of their vaccinations?

    Answer: You can get proof of vaccinations for someone who is unable to manage their affairs if you have the following registered with Services Australia:

    • Power of Attorney
    • a guardian or administrative order
    • other legal arrangement

    If none of these are in place, you can submit Authorisation to act on an incapacitated person’s behalf for Medicare purposes form MO050.

    16

    Question: I have safety concerns in storing my COVID-19 vaccination proof. What can I do to make sure others cannot see it without my permission?

    Answer: You can add your COVID-19 digital certificate to a digital wallet. Both Apple Wallet and Google Wallet require a passcode to use and you can also add extra security with Face ID, Touch ID or an unlock pattern. You can change or update these via the settings in the app.

    17

    Question: How do I show proof of my/my child’s vaccinations?

    Answer: If you have a Medicare online account linked to your myGov, you can:

    • download and save proof of your vaccinations to your mobile device,
    • save them to your Express Plus Medicare app to use offline, or
    • add your COVID-19 digital certificate to your Apple Wallet and Google Wallet

    If you have an IHI and have linked the IHI service in myGov, you can:

    • download and save your proof to your mobile device, or
    • add your digital certificate to Apple Wallet or Google Wallet

    More information is available at What types of proof there are.

    18

    Question: How do I/my kids get an IHI?

    Answer: An Individual Healthcare Identifier (IHI) is a unique number used to identify an individual for health care purposes.

    You already have an IHI if any of the following apply:

    • you have a Medicare card
    • you have a DVA card
    • you’re enrolled in Medicare

    If you’re not eligible for Medicare or a DVA benefit you can request an IHI through the Individual Healthcare Identifiers (IHI) Service in myGov. You will first need to create a myGov account to access the IHI service.

    You can also request an IHI by completing the Request an Individual Healthcare Identifier form (MS003).

    You can find more information about IHIs by going to servicesaustralia.gov.au/hi or calling the HI Service on 1300 361 457.

    Note: Service Officers should check if the customer is eligible to enrol in Medicare before offering IHI, see Who can enrol.

    19

    Question: I/my kids have got an IHI and I need to update the personal details. How can I do this?

    Answer: You can update your personal details by:

    • contacting the Healthcare Identifiers (HI) Service on 1300 361 457, or
    • completing the Request an Individual Healthcare Identifier form (MS003)

    20

    Question: I linked a Medicare account/HI Service/My Health Record to myGov. Why can’t I see anything on the proof of vaccinations?

    Answer: When you link your Medicare account to myGov, your proof of vaccinations might not show any information for a few days. You can log on at any time to check whether your record has been updated.

    If you think your vaccinations are missing from your record, speak to your vaccination provider and ask them to record them on the AIR.

    21

    Question: I/my kids had a COVID-19 vaccination yesterday/today. Why can’t I see it on the vaccination evidence?

    Answer: It can take up to 10 days for any new vaccinations to appear on your immunisation history.

    Making sure your personal and contact details are up to date can help. If you have a Medicare Online Account, you can update your details using myGov or the Express Plus Medicare mobile app.

    If you are not eligible for Medicare, you can check or update your details with the AIR.

    If over 10 days, advise the individual to ask their vaccination provider to send their immunisation history to the AIR.

    If their provider has done this already, and it has been a number of days since, it may be a pended record. Check the vaccination record to see if it needs to be updated.

    22

    Question: I/my kids got COVID-19 vaccines overseas. Can I get them recorded on the AIR?

    Answer: The AIR can record COVID-19 vaccines that are recognised in Australia. They can be recorded if they were given on or after 1 March 2020:

    • AstraZeneca COVISHIELD (ASTCOV)
    • AstraZeneca Vaxzevria (COVAST)
    • Bharat Biotech Covaxin (BHACOV)
    • Gamaleya Sputnik V (GAMSPU)
    • Jansen-Cilag COVID Vaccine (JANSSEN)
    • Moderna Spikevax (MODERN)
    • Moderna Spikevax Biv BA.1 ((MODBIV)
    • Moderna Spikevax Biv BA.4-5 (MODBBA)
    • Novavax NUVAXOVID (NOVNUV)
    • Pfizer Comirnaty (COMIRN)
    • Pfizer Comirnaty Biv BA.1 (COMBIV)
    • Pfizer Comirnaty Biv BA.4-5 (COMBBA)
    • Sinopharm BBIBP-CorV (SINBBI)
    • Sinovac Coronavac (SINCOR)

    You can ask a recognised vaccination provider in Australia to record your vaccinations on the AIR if the vaccine you’ve had is:

    • approved or recognised to be recorded on the AIR, and
    • you received it on or after 1 March 2020

    Note: you’ll need documents in English that show what vaccinations you’ve had.

    It is also important that your vaccination provider records the country the vaccine was given. This will make sure that your vaccines are displayed correctly on your proof of vaccinations. If no country is recorded or it is recorded as ‘unknown’, the vaccination won’t display on your international certificate.

    23

    Question: Does my vaccine batch or serial number need to be recorded?

    Answer: Under legislation, it is mandatory that your vaccination provider records the batch numbers for COVID-19, flu and NIP vaccines.

    Some vaccines, such as Pfizer and AstraZeneca, have a lot number instead of a batch number. The lot number can be entered if there is no batch number.

    If the provider does not know the batch number, the will should contact their state or territory health department for those details.

    24

    Question: I got a COVID-19 vaccine brand overseas that we don’t have in Australia. Can other brands be added to the register?

    Answer: The Therapeutic Goods Administration (TGA) and Department of Health and Aged Care decide which vaccines are recognised in Australia and can be recorded on the Australian Immunisation Register (AIR).

    For more information about COVID-19 vaccines approved in Australia, visit the TGA website at tga.gov.au

    25

    Question: How does the new legislation about mandatory reporting affect vaccinations?

    Answer: It is mandatory for vaccination providers to report flu, COVID-19, National Immunisation Program (NIP) and Japanese encephalitis virus (JEV) vaccinations to the AIR within 10 days of administering them.

    You don’t have to do anything differently. It just means that all your flu, COVID-19, NIP and JEV vaccinations will be recorded on the AIR and show on your immunisation history statement.

    26

    Question: Is my/my kids information safe?

    Answer: Yes, Services Australia takes the integrity of the Medicare system and the Australian Immunisation Register seriously and has cybersecurity in place to protect your personal information.

    27

    Question: How does vaccination information get used?

    Answer: Information on the AIR is used to monitor immunisation coverage levels in the community to understand areas of need.

    Health professionals can access information on the AIR to check vaccines that have been given to their patients.

    28

    Question: Can I opt out of the AIR?

    Answer: You can opt out from receiving correspondence from the AIR and from third parties (such as health professionals) being able to access your information held by us.

    You can download a form from the Services Australia website (search ‘IM017’).

    Note: Service Officers can see Amend individual registration on the Australian Immunisation Register (AIR) for further information.

    29

    Question: Why doesn’t the immunisation history statement show all the immunisations I have/my child has received?

    Answer: If any immunisations are missing from your immunisation history statement, speak to your vaccination provider and ask them to record them on the AIR. All immunisations recorded on the AIR show on your immunisation history statement.

    The COVID-19 digital certificate will display once you’ve had at least one dose of an approved or recognised COVID-19 vaccine. The digital certificate only shows COVID-19 vaccines.

    30

    Question: What does my COVID-19 digital certificate show?

    Answer: If they are recorded on the Australian Immunisation Register, the COVID-19 digital certificate shows:

    • all the COVID-19 vaccines you’ve had, including any booster doses, or
    • a medical contraindication status

    A digital certificate is available when:

    • you’ve had at least one does of an approved or recognised COVID-19 vaccination, or
    • an eligible health professional has recorded a medical contraindication for all COVID-19 vaccine brands on the AIR

    Note: the digital certificate will show all of a customer’s COVID-19 vaccinations, including any booster doses.

    31

    Question: How does my International COVID-19 Vaccination Certificate work?

    Answer: The certificate shows proof of your COVID-19 vaccinations recorded on the Australian Immunisation Register and has a QR code. This QR code contains all the vaccination information shown on your certificate.

    The QR code can be read by the passport control systems the same as your passport when you travel overseas.

    32

    Question: I am travelling to Europe, can I use my International COVID-19 Vaccination Certificate when I am in foreign countries?

    Answer: International COVID-19 Vaccination Certificates are generally acknowledged around the world.

    The International COVID-19 Vaccination Certificate will allow you to enter and leave Australia. Other countries have different requirements and rules change quickly. It is critical that you understand the current travel requirements so you don't get caught off guard.

    Visit smartraveller.gov.au for up to date information.

    33

    Question: I received a letter that says I need to update my COVID-19 vaccination because my overseas vaccination is missing the ‘Country of Immunisation’, what do I have to do?

    Answer: The International COVID-19 Vaccination Certificate will only display overseas vaccinations that have a ‘Country of Immunisation’ reported.

    You will need to contact a recognised vaccination provider and ask them to update your overseas COVID-19 vaccination information with the Country of Immunisation. If required, you can contact a different provider. They may request evidence of your overseas COVID-19 vaccination details.

    34

    Question: My/my kids proof of vaccinations don’t match the passport. Can you fix it?

    Answer: The immunisation history statement and COVID-19 digital certificate show vaccinations you’ve had that are recorded on the Australian Immunisation Register.

    They’re used for healthcare purposes and aren’t meant to be used for personal identification.

    Your details on your International COVID-19 Vaccination Certificate show the details you have recorded with Services Australia. If you need the details on your international certificate to match the passport you’re travelling with and you’re enrolled in Medicare, you can contact Medicare enquiries.

    If you’re not eligible for Medicare, you can update your IHI details with the HI Service on 1300 361 457 or contact the Australian Immunisation Register on 1800 653 809.

    35

    Question: Do I need to print my International COVID-19 Vaccination Certificate or is a digital copy okay?

    Answer: This depends on your airline or the country you’re visiting. Some may accept digital copies but it’s recommended you carry printed ones too. You should check with your airline and the countries you plan on visiting.

    36

    Question: Is my International COVID-19 Vaccination Certificate linked to a central database?

    Answer: No, your certificate isn’t linked to a database.

    Authorities authenticate your international certificate by scanning the QR code to verify the information in the code matches the information shown on your certificate.

    37

    Question: Does my chip in my passport contain the information from my International COVID-19 Vaccination certificate?

    Answer: No but they both use the same technology. When you get your certificate nothing happens to your passport chip, they’re not linked. You’ll need to use both as separate documents when travelling overseas.

    38

    Question: What does Department of Foreign Affairs and Trade (DFAT) do with my personal information?

    Answer: You need to agree to consent to share your information with DFAT before a certificate can be issued. For privacy reasons, DFAT won’t keep a copy of your certificate once it’s issued.

    39

    Question: I’ve got an international vaccine certificate issued by another country. Do I still need to get the Australian one?

    Answer: This depends on the country you’re visiting. You may be able to use it but you should check with your airline and go to the Smart Traveller website for more information.

    40

    Question: I would like an International COVID-19 Vaccination Certificate but I don’t have a passport. What can I do?

    Answer: You need to have a valid passport to request an International COVID-19 Vaccination Certificate.

    To apply for a new passport, or to renew your current passport visit the Australian Passport Office at passports.gov.au

    41

    Question: I would like an International COVID-19 Vaccination Certificate but I only have a partial date of birth on my passport. What can I do?

    Answer: When you request your certificate online, you can enter a partial DOB to match your passport. For example, ‘MM/YYYY’ or ‘YYYY’.

    42

    Question: I’m about to travel overseas but I can’t have COVID-19 vaccine due to a medical condition. Can I get an international certificate?

    Answer: If you have a medical contraindication this means you can’t be vaccinated against COVID-19 for health reasons. The international certificate shows proof of your COVID-19 vaccines recorded on the Australian Immunisation Register.

    If you can’t be vaccinated for COVID-19, you can use your immunisation history statement. You will also need to seek advice from your airline and the country you want to travel to for acceptable proof.

    43

    Question: Can you email my proof of vaccination?

    Answer: The fastest and easiest way to request a certificate is using your Medicare online account through myGov. You can also visit a Service Centre or we can post one to you but it can take up to 14 days to arrive in the mail.

    Emailing certificates can only be done in exceptional circumstances, where all other options have been ruled out. If customers can’t access any of the options above, Service Officers should refer to Emailing proof of vaccination.

    Note: only AIR Programme Support can approve to email proof of vaccination for customers in Australia or overseas. Service Officers and PSOs are not to do this unless it is for a customer referred by a Department of Foreign Affairs and Trade (DFAT) consulate staff member.

    44

    Question: I have a hardcopy of my immunisation history statement/COVID-19 digital certificate/International COVID-19 Vaccination Certificate. If I lose it, do I need to pay a fee?

    Answer: No, proof of vaccination is free but you will need to request a new copy using your linked services through myGov.

    You can also attend a service centre or request a copy be mailed but these can take up to 14 days to arrive.

    45

    Question: Is there an expiry date on the International COVID-19 Vaccination Certificate?

    Answer: No, there’s only a ‘valid from’ date. International certificates don’t expire but if your personal or passport details or vaccination information changes, you’ll need to request a new one.

    46

    Question: Do I need proof of COVID-19 vaccinations to enter Australia?

    Answer: You can find information about travelling to Australia on the Smart Traveller web site.

    47

    Question: I’m overseas and I’ve lost or damaged my International COVID-19 Vaccination Certificate, what can I do?

    Answer: If you lose or damage your international certificate, you need to get a new one through myGov. Find out more about accessing myGov while travelling overseas on the myGov website.

    If you can’t use myGov, contact the Australian Immunisation Register to request a new certificate.

    48

    Question: I’ve got more than one passport. Which passport do I use for the International COVID-19 Vaccination Certificate?

    Answer: You should request an international certificate for the passport you want to enter foreign countries with.

    You can request certificates for both your passports but you can only request a certificate using one passport at a time.

    49

    Question: I recently received a new foreign passport, but the Department of Home Affairs has yet to attach my visa to it. What do I need to do to get my International COVID-19 Vaccination Certificate?

    Answer: To get your visa linked, you'll need to submit your updated passport data to Home Affairs. For more information, read the 'Your passport details have changed' page on the Home Affairs website.

    50

    Question: I'm having trouble getting my International COVID-19 Vaccination Certificate online. What should I do? My passport is not recognised, is it because I have two foreign passports?

    Answer: If you have two foreign passports from separate nations, make sure you are filling out the information for the passport that is linked to your visa. We can help you get an international certificate with the other passport if you need one.

    51

    Question: Do I need my International COVID-19 certificate to depart Australia and re-enter?

    Answer: Yes, you will need your international certificate plus usual travel documents.

    See these websites for more information:

    • smartraveller.gov.au
    • covid19.homeaffairs.gov.au
    • health.gov.au

    FAQs for health professionals

    Table 6

    Item

    Description

    1

    Question: Are COVID-19 vaccines mandatory in Australia?

    Answer: All vaccinations in Australia are voluntary, including COVID-19 vaccines and boosters. People maintain the option to choose. This will apply to any COVID-19 vaccination that may become available.

    2

    Question: Can I administer COVID-19 vaccines?

    Answer: The Commonwealth Department of Health and Aged Care, or your state or territory health department, are best placed to advise which health professionals will administer COVID-19 vaccines and boosters.

    For information about the vaccine rollout plan or any other COVID-19 updates, visit health.gov.au

    3

    Question: Are there any forms I need to fill out to receive COVID-19 vaccines?

    Answer: You may need to complete some forms to receive COVID-19 vaccines. When completing these forms, you need to use your AIR Provider number.

    The Department of Health and Aged Care has resources for health professionals containing information and advice about administering COVID-19 vaccinations. For further information, go to health.gov.au

    4

    Question: Do I have to get all my patients to sign a COVID-19 vaccination consent form?

    Answer: The Department of Health and Aged Care has resources for health professionals containing information and advice about administering COVID-19 vaccinations.

    For further information, go to health.gov.au

    5

    Question: My patient doesn’t know their Medicare number. Can I just use a special or emergency Medicare number?

    Answer: It’s important to correctly identify your patient so their immunisation history is up to date and they can get proof of their vaccinations.

    If a patient can’t find their Medicare card they can use the Express Plus Medicare app to view their digital Medicare card.

    You can also go to HPOS to use the:

    • Find a Patient to find their Medicare details
    • Healthcare Identifier Service for their IHI if they’re not eligible for Medicare or DVA
    • AIR site and enter their full name and date of birth

    6

    Question: My patient wants a specific COVID-19 vaccine/brand. What do I do?

    Answer: The Department of Health and Aged Care has resources for health professionals containing information and advice about administering COVID-19 vaccinations.

    For further information, go to health.gov.au

    7

    Question: Can my patient get an exemption for COVID-19 vaccines?

    Answer: Medical contraindications can be recorded on the AIR for COVID-19 vaccines if there is a valid medical reason as outlined in the Australian Immunisation Handbook. If a medical contraindication is only recorded for 1 COVID-19 vaccine brand, the exemption will only apply to that vaccine. This means they won’t be able to download a COVID-19 digital certificate with a medical contraindication status but it will show on their immunisation history statement.

    If your patient can’t receive any COVID-19 vaccines, you will need to record a medical contraindication for each brand on the AIR.

    Only eligible health professionals are authorised to record medical contraindications. For more about recording contraindications visit AIR for health professionals.

    8

    Question: Do I have to record COVID-19 vaccinations on the AIR?

    Answer: Yes. Legislation was passed making it mandatory to report flu, COVID-19 and National Immunisation Program (NIP) vaccinations to the AIR. For further information, go to health.gov.au

    9

    Question: What are the rules for mandatory reporting to the AIR?

    Answer: The Department of Health and Aged Care is best placed to provide advice about the new mandatory reporting legislation. For further information, go to health.gov.au

    10

    Question: Do I have to record a vaccine batch or serial number?

    Answer: Yes. Under legislation, it is mandatory to record the batch numbers for COVID-19, flu and NIP vaccines.

    Some vaccines, such as Pfizer and AstraZeneca, have a lot number instead of a batch number. Enter the lot number if there is no batch number.

    If you don’t know the batch number, contact your state or territory health department for assistance.

    11

    Question: Can I record COVID-19 vaccines my patient had overseas?

    Answer: You can record vaccines your patient had overseas if they’re a brand that’s recognised by the Therapeutic Good Administration (TGA) and they were given on or after 1 March 2020. Your patient must provide evidence of the vaccinations and the English translations if appropriate.

    Recording COVID-19 vaccines your patient had overseas will help them to access up to date proof of their vaccinations.

    It is also important to record the country in which the vaccine/s were given. This will make sure that your patient’s vaccines will display correctly on their proof of vaccinations. If no country is recorded, the vaccination won’t display on the patient’s international certificate.

    12

    Question: Do I have to record a country of vaccination for COVID-19 vaccines given overseas?

    Answer: Yes, you must select a country from the drop down list. If you don’t record a country or select ‘unknown’ the vaccine won’t display on your patient’s International COVID-19 Vaccination Certificate or they may not be able to request an international certificate at all.

    If you don’t know the country of vaccination, check your patient’s medical record or contact your patient to ask for more information.

    13

    Question: My patient received a letter saying they need to update their COVID-19 vaccination details because it is missing the country code. Can you please give me more information?

    Answer: There are a few reasons for this. It may be because the patient’s vaccination was recorded before the international certificate was made available, or the Country of Immunisation has been recorded as unknown.

    You will need to update their overseas COVID-19 vaccination encounter to include the Country of Immunisation. If you had originally reported this vaccination to the AIR you can update it by using:

    • the AIR site; or
    • your AIR compatible software if it has been updated to the latest version of web services. You can speak to your software developer/provider to confirm whether you can add the Country of Immunisation

    Ensure you are using the same provider number that you used to originally report the vaccination

    14

    Question: Do I need a different provider number to report COVID-19 vaccinations to the AIR?

    Answer: If you’re already registered with the AIR, you just need to use your Medicare provider number or existing AIR provider number to report COVID-19 immunisations to the AIR.

    If you’re not already registered with the AIR, you can find more information at servicesaustralia.gov.au/AIR for health professionals

    15

    Question: I’ve tried using the AIR site but couldn’t edit/update the encounter

    Answer: If you are not the original provider that reported the vaccination, you cannot update the information online and will need to resubmit the vaccination.

    If you are the provider that reported the vaccination, you will need to use the same provider number that you originally used to report the vaccination. You can update if you can see the pencil. If you cannot update this information via the AIR site, you can resubmit the vaccination and include the country of immunisation.

    16

    Question: I’m a Nurse Practitioner giving COVID-19 vaccinations. Do I need to get my own provider number?

    Answer: You don’t need your own provider number to record vaccinations. You should use the organisation’s AIR provider number if you’re working at a:

    • Vaccination Hub
    • hospital
    • GP Respiratory Clinic
    • ACCHO
    • AMS

    17

    Question: How do I record COVID-19 vaccinations on the AIR?

    Answer: COVID-19 vaccinations are recorded on the AIR using the same systems as other vaccines.

    You can use:

    • your practice management or clinical software that is integrated with our systems
    • the free AIR site via HPOS

    18

    Question: Do I need to get a PRODA account?

    Answer: Yes, you need a PRODA account to record vaccinations on the AIR.

    The Services Australia website has more information about registering for PRODA based on your organisation structure and practice model.

    19

    Question: How do I access the AIR site?

    Answer: If you have a Medicare provider number, you’ll need a PRODA individual account and to add HPOS as a service. From there, you can access the AIR site.

    If you are an organisation, you will need a PRODA organisation account and to add HPOS as a service. You can then link to the AIR site using your AIR provider number and AIR linking code.

    You can go to AIR for health professionals and select How to set up your access to the AIR for more information.

    20

    Question: Do I have to link my Healthcare Provider Identifier (HPI) number or My Health Record (MHR) to the AIR?

    Answer: No, you don’t need to link your HPI-I/HPI-O number or My Health Record to the AIR. If you have already added HPOS to your PRODA account and linked to the AIR, you have access to the AIR site.

    When you log onto the AIR site through HPOS, you’ll see an AIR screen where you can enter your HPI numbers. This is not mandatory information. You can select Next to continue to search for a patient on the AIR site.

    21

    Question: How do I record COVID-19 vaccinations if I’m having trouble with my software or AIR site access?

    Answer: The fastest way to record vaccinations on the AIR is by using the AIR site or your software.

    If you can't submit information online, use forms:

    • AIR immunisation encounter (IM018), or
    • AIR immunisation history form (IM013)

    Vaccines recorded using forms can take some time to appear on the AIR.

    22

    Question: How do I get proof of vaccination for my patient?

    Answer: You can download proof of vaccinations for your patient using the AIR site.

    For more information go to AIR for health professionals and select 'How to view and print reports and statements'.

    23

    Question: Where can I find information to provide to my patients (e.g. brochures or posters)?

    Answer: The Department of Health and Aged Care COVID-19 vaccines page has a range of resources that can be printed and displayed for patients.

    24

    Question: I’m a pharmacy how do I correctly record COVID-19 vaccines on the Australian Immunisation Register (AIR)?

    Answer: When you give a patient a COVID-19 vaccine, you’ll need to record it on the AIR. It’s important to correctly identify your patient so their immunisation history is up to date. This means they’ll be able to get proof of their vaccinations if needed.

    Don’t use the ‘Urgent Clinical Need’ special Medicare number for reporting COVID-19 vaccinations to the AIR. If you do this, the vaccination won’t appear on the patient's immunisation history statement.

    To find a record on the AIR, you’ll need your patient’s Medicare card and Individual Reference Number (IRN). If a patient can’t find their Medicare card, they can use the Express Plus Medicare app to view their digital Medicare card.

    25

    Question: I am a pharmacist. How am I paid for administering COVID-19 vaccines?

    Answer: Services Australia is not administering payments for pharmacists who give COVID-19 vaccines.

    Pharmacists can see Information for COVID-19 vaccination providers on the Department of Health and Aged Care website for information about the vaccination rollout.

    Answer: The system automatically changes the dose number to ‘V’ for individuals over 20 years old. This does not affect the generation of a COVID-19 status.

    Question: Can COVID-19 vaccines given overseas be recorded on the AIR?

If they’re a brand that’s recognised by the Therapeutic Good Administration (TGA) and they were given on or after 1 March 2020. Customers must provide evidence of the vaccinations and the English translations if appropriate to a vaccination provider in Australia for them to record on the AIR.

    Question: I’m processing pended COVID-19 vaccine encounters on AIR mainframe. Can I call the vaccination provider to confirm information?

    Answer: If contacting a COVID-19 vaccine hub see Questions 9 and 10 below.

    You can call all other vaccination providers to confirm critical information needed to process the immunisation encounter to an ‘A’ or ‘Y’ status. This includes information such as:

    • the date of service
    • the individual’s name or date of birth
    • vaccine brand and dose

    Note: changes to legislation make it mandatory for vaccination providers to record batch numbers for COVID-19 and flu vaccines. Service Officers who call a vaccination provider to confirm vaccine information and see a vaccine batch number isn’t recorded, should ask the provider for the details.

    Note: vaccination provider personal contact details must not be released to customers.

    Information such as the individual’s sex/gender isn't needed to process an immunisation encounter. Vaccination providers can still search for the individual and record additional vaccines for them on the AIR.

    AIR Service Officers can view the individual’s record by going to the YCEI – Individual enquiry screen in AIR Mainframe.

    The agency sends spreadsheets of all pended records to the hub team for clarification. Go to the SPS Library SharePoint page for more information.

    For any other COVID-19 vaccine hub, check the comments on their AIR programme registration in PDS for contact details.

    Question: What can I do if customers say they are having problems linking from myGov to Medicare?

    11

    Question: What can I do if customers can’t see their COVID-19 vaccinations (or other vaccinations) on their proof of vaccination?

    Answer: Vaccination providers have up to 10 days to record vaccines on the AIR. Making sure their personal and contact details are up to date can help to speed up the process.

    When customers first link their Medicare account to myGov their proof of vaccinations may not show for a few days. Encourage customers to link their account early to avoid delays in getting their proof of vaccination once they’ve had a COVID-19 vaccine.

    12

    Question: I don’t have access to the AIR site

    Answer: Refer all access requests to your Team leader.

    13

    Question: If a guardian of a child under 14 is on a different Medicare card, can they request proof of vaccinations?

    Answer: Yes, they can request proof of vaccinations for children in their care if they pass a security check and the address details are the same.

    14

    Question: Can I print a customer’s proof of vaccinations for them?

    Answer: Yes. Customers can access print friendly versions of their proof of vaccinations through myGov in the Medicare or IHI service. They can also request printed copies by attending a service centre or calling the AIR and asking for one to be posted. These can take up to 14 days to arrive in the mail.

    For more information, see Proof of vaccination and the Australian Immunisation Register.

    15

    Question: When printing immunisation history statements, COVID-19 digital certificates and international certificates, do we need to print in colour or black and white?

    Answer: Printing in colour is preferred. Customers may have issues with employers or businesses accepting black and white printed copies as valid.

    16

    Question: Will recording an ‘opt out’ indicator on a record stop a central printing hardcopy being sent?

    Answer: No, this won’t stop a hardcopy being printed and sent.

    17

    Question: The customer says their proof of vaccination doesn’t match their passport. Can we amend it?

    Answer: The immunisation history statement and COVID-19 digital certificate show vaccination information customers have recorded on the AIR. They’re for use in Australia and aren’t meant to be used for personal identification.

    The personal details displayed on the International COVID-19 Vaccination Certificate must exactly match the information shown on the customer’s passport. Customers can choose to update their personal details to match their passport or can contact the AIR for assistance to update the certificate details. You can enter a partial DOB to match customer’s passport, and certificates can be requested for customers with an ‘onlyname’.

    18

    Question: When requesting an international certificate for a customer, can I update the middle name based on what the customer says or do I need to complete a DVS check first?

    Answer: If a customer advises that their name has been spelt incorrectly on CDMS and it's a minor variation, you can change it without documentation.

    If the change is a major variation to their recorded legal name, customers must provide supporting documents to verify the required changes (verified via DVS check).

    See Change legal name on Medicare record.

    19

    Question: I’m requesting an International COVID-19 Vaccination Certificate for a customer but they don’t have a passport with an Australian visa and don’t have any Australian photo identification. Can I request a certificate?

    Answer: Yes, the customer can get an international certificate but you must verify their identity by using the Document Verification System (DVS) to validate another identity document.

    See Table 7, Proof of vaccination and the Australian Immunisation Register (AIR).

    Note: the identity document must be in the name that is displayed on the passport the customer is using to travel overseas.

    20

    Question: A customer is requesting an International COVID-19 Vaccination Certificate but they are not travelling to Australia. Can I request a certificate?

    Answer: No, the customer must be travelling either to or from Australia.

    If the customer is not travelling to or from Australia, either:

    • help them get online and request an International COVID-19 Vaccination Certificate themselves
    • offer them an immunisation history statement or COVID-19 digital certificate instead

    21

    Question: I’m requesting an international certificate for a customer. In the Immunisation Document System (IDS), does it default to the correct consent message based on what type of passport I’ve chosen?

    Answer: The correct disclaimer consent message will display depending on what type of passport you’ve selected for the customer.

    22

    Question : What if a customer doesn’t give consent to share their information with DFAT when requesting an International COVID-19 Vaccination Certificate?

    Answer: International certificates are generated by Department of Foreign Affairs and Trade (DFAT) using vaccination information the AIR shares. If the customer doesn’t consent to their information being shared with DFAT, we’re unable to proceed with the certificate request.

    23

    Question: What is the cap on the letters for CDMS? What happens when the name is too long for the International COVID-19 Vaccination Certificate?

    Answer: The maximum length of the customer’s first and middle names combined that can be sent to Department of Foreign Affairs and Trade (DFAT) is 39 characters. If the name is too long, it will need to be shortened to match the name on the passport.

    24

    Question: What if the customer has removed consent to share their vaccinations with third parties? Can we still view?

    Answer: Yes, use the AIR site to view proof of vaccinations for customers who have removed consent.
