1

I need to go into Aged Care. What do I do?

My Aged Care is the starting point to access government funded aged care services.

Assist the caller to register for Aged Care Services on the My Aged Care website or offer to book a face-to-face appointment with an Aged Care Specialist Officer (ACSO) if there is one located in their area or warm transfer the customer to the My Aged Care phone service. The website provides information on topics such as:

  • the types of aged care services available
  • eligibility for services
  • local service providers
  • Aged care costs
  • advocacy services
  • how to make a complaint

2

Do I need to complete a calculation of your cost of care?

It is not compulsory for a care recipient to complete a calculation of your cost of care for either home or residential care.

If the care recipient chooses not to disclose their income and/or assets information, they may be liable to pay the maximum fee depending on their care needs.

Individual circumstances will determine whether completing an application will be beneficial. See Aged care means assessment for more information.

3

Which aged care application will I need?

Customer First will receive notification that a care recipient has entered permanent residential care (residential care), or has commenced receiving a home care package. If Services Australia or where applicable, the Department of Veterans' Affairs (DVA) does not hold sufficient information to complete an assessment, a letter will be sent to the care recipient from the Aged Care Management Payment System (ACMPS) advising that:

  • They have not provided their means assessment information
  • If they wish to provide their information, they should complete an Aged Care Calculation of your cost of care application
  • The completed application needs to be returned to Services Australia (or DVA where applicable)
  • If the application is not returned, the maximum rate of fees and charges for their level of care may apply

Types of applications

The following forms are applicable to care recipients who are:

  • Receiving an income support payment from Services Australia (ISP customer)
  • Receiving a payment from the Department of Veterans' Affairs (DVA customer)
  • Self-funded retirees

Note: in some circumstances, a form is not required and a verbal application may be completed. Refer to Aged care means assessment for information regarding when a calculation of your cost of care (means assessment) application is required.

  • Aged Care Calculation of your cost of care
    • Is used to determine the type and level of contribution a care recipient must make towards their aged care costs
  • Aged Care - Claim for financial hardship assistance (SA462)
    • For care recipients who do not have sufficient means to pay aged care costs and/or accommodation payments due to circumstances beyond their control
  • Aged Care Financial Disclosure Consent form (SA480)
    • Is required for the release of financial information to a provider if this consent was not given in versions 1711 (prior to May 2019) of the SA456 or SA457 forms
    • See Aged care - consent and disclosure for further information if required
  • Nominee form (SS313 and AC019)
    • Refer to question 12 for details of the 'Authorising a person or organisation to enquire or act on your behalf form (SS313)' and the 'Aged Care Request for a nominee for Department of Veterans' Affairs customers form (AC019)'

If both members of a couple require an assessment they will each need to complete an application.

Further information is available in Aged care means assessment if required. This includes information regarding:

  • When a calculation of your cost of care is required
  • Home care and residential care means assessment processing

Where to find aged care forms

Aged care forms can be accessed from the Services Australia website. The Resources page has a link to the forms. The care recipient should be encouraged to access the forms themselves where possible.

The Aged Care Calculation of your cost of care forms are available as either a digital form or paper forms.

The benefits of accessing the Calculation of your cost of care forms themselves should be explained to the care recipient. That is, by accessing the form from servicesaustralia.gov.au/agedcare The care recipient will initially be presented with a digital Aged Care - Calculation of your cost of care form which is an intuitive form that will only ask them questions applicable to their care type and circumstances.

Note: advise the care recipient that the digital form cannot be printed until all questions have been answered (as the answers they provide will generate the next set of relevant questions).

If the care recipient is unable to complete the digital form they can print and complete 1 of the paper forms.

If a care recipient advises they are unable to access the required form online, the form may be issued via the Customer First Mail Forms Guided Procedure.

5

Is my home included as an asset?

For home care

The principal home is exempt.

For residential care

The principal home is included as an asset unless it is occupied by a protected person at the relevant date.

  • A protected person is a:
    • partner or dependent child
    • carer who has lived with the care recipient in home for the past 2 years and the carer is eligible for an Australian income support payment at the relevant date
    • close relative (such as a sister, brother, parent, child or grandchild) who has lived with the care recipient in their home for the past 5 years and is eligible for an Australian income support payment at the relevant date
  • The relevant date is either:
    • the date the assessment is completed (where the care recipient has not yet entered care), or
    • the date the care recipient entered care (where the assessment is requested after their admission)

The exemption of the principal home for aged care means assessment purposes will cease if the protected person who had been occupying the home at the relevant date subsequently moves out, or if applicable, their income support payment eligibility is lost.

If the principal home is included as an asset, its value for the purposes of calculating the means tested fee will be capped at the 'First Asset Threshold'.

Refer to the Department of Health's Schedule of Fees and Charges for Residential and Home Care for the current 'first asset threshold' rate.

If required, the care recipient may be booked in with an Aged care Specialist Officer (ACSO) if there is one located in their area, or referred to the My Aged Care website or phone service for:

  • information on how the home is assessed
  • access to the My Aged Care Residential Care Fee Estimator

What are the fees for home care and how are they calculated?

My Aged Care provides assistance with estimating fees for aged care services.

The caller can be booked with an Aged Care Specialist Officer (ACSO) if there is one located in their area, or referred to the My Aged Care website or phone service for assistance with estimating the cost of their care.

  • For an overview and estimate of home care fees, offer to book the caller in for an appointment with an Aged Care Specialist Officer (ACSO) if there is one located in their area
  • For an overview of home care fees, refer callers to the My Aged Care - Home Care Package costs and fees home page
  • To estimate home care fees, refer callers to the My Aged Care - Home Care Fee Estimator

Care recipients commencing a home care service on or after 1 July 2014 may be asked to pay:

  • a basic daily fee (BDF) only, or
  • a BDF plus an income-tested care fee (ITCF)

Basic Daily Fee (BDF)

From 1 July 2019, there are 4 levels of BDF, aligned to the 4 home care package levels (level 1, 2, 3 and 4), replacing the existing single BDF. Refer to the Department of Health's Schedule of Fees and Charges for the current home care BDF.

Pre 1 July 2019, the BDF was equal to 17.5% of the single basic Age Pension across all care levels. Refer to the Department of Health's Schedule of Fees and Charges for Residential and Home Care for archived rates.

Income-tested care fee (ITCF)

Services Australia (or DVA if applicable), are responsible for the assessment of income to determine liability for an ITCF.

A care recipient may be required to pay an ITCF if they have income over the relevant income free area. If the care recipient is:

  • receiving a full pension they will not pay an ITCF
    • they will only pay a BDF
  • a part pensioner, their ITCF will be the lowest of:
    • 50% of their income above the income threshold
    • the cost of their care, or
    • the daily cap. Refer to the Department of Health's Schedule of Fees and Charges for 'Caps on Income Tested Care Fees in Home Care' - 'First Cap' for the current rate
  • a self-funded retiree, their ITCF will be the lowest of:
    • the daily cap for a part pensioner PLUS 50% of their income above the income for a part pensioner, or
    • the cost of their care, or
    • the daily cap. Refer to the Department of Health's Schedule of Fees and Charges for 'Caps on Income Tested Care Fees in Home Care' - 'Second Cap' for the current rate

Annual and lifetime caps apply to the ITCF

Any means-tested care fee (MTCF) paid during an admission to residential care also contributes to home care caps. See the Department of Health's Schedule of Fees and Charges for current cap rates.

  • The annual cap is located under 'Caps on Income Tested Care Fees in Home Care'. Different cap rates apply depending on whether the care recipient is a full or part pensioner
  • The lifetime cap is located under 'Lifetime Cap on Means-Tested Care Fees in Residential Care and Income Tested Care Fees in Home Care'

What are the fees for permanent residential care (post 1 July 2014 assessment rules) and how are they calculated?

My Aged Care provides assistance with estimating fees for aged care services.

The caller can be booked in with an Aged Care Specialist Officer (ACSO) if there is one located in their area, referred to the My Aged Care website or warm transferred to the My Aged Care  phone service  for assistance with estimating fees:

  • For an overview and estimate of home care fees, offer to book an appointment with an Aged Care Specialist Officer (ACSO) if there is one located in their area
  • For an overview of residential care fees, refer them to the My Aged Care - Aged care home costs and fees home page
  • To estimate residential care fees, refer them to the My Aged Care - Residential Care Fee Estimator

Care recipients commencing a permanent residential care service on or after 1 July 2014 may be asked to pay a combination of fees depending upon their circumstances. Fee types include:

  • Basic Daily Fee (BDF)
  • Means-Tested Care Fee (MTCF)
  • Accommodation Payment or Accommodation Contribution
  • Extra Service Fee (if the care recipient has elected to receive extra services as part of their agreement with the service provider)

Services Australia (or DVA if applicable), are responsible for the assessment of assets and income to determine liability for:

  • an MTCF (to contribute to the cost of care); and
  • accommodation costs

Basic daily fee

This fee applies to all care recipients and is equal to 85% of the single basic age pension.

The BDF covers daily living costs such as meals, laundry, cleaning, and utilities such as power and phones.

Refer to the Department of Health's Schedule of Fees and Charges for the current residential care BDF.

Means-tested care fee (MTCF)

The MTCF is a contribution towards the cost of care. It is determined by an assessment of combined income and assets and by the level of care that is required.

Accommodation payment

An accommodation payment covers accommodation costs.

It may be payable if the means-tested amount (as determined by the means assessment) on the date of entry to care is equal to or more than the maximum accommodation supplement (as determined in the Department of Health's Schedule of Fees and Charges). In this case:

  • the care recipient will be assessed as 'not low means'
  • they will not receive government assistance with their accommodation costs
  • an accommodation payment will not be affected by changes to income and assets

Care recipients have 28 days from the date (or proposed date) of entry to care to choose how the accommodation payment will be paid. It can be paid either as a:

  • Refundable Accommodation Deposit (RAD)
    • paid in full, as a lump sum, or
  • Daily Accommodation Payment (DAP);
    • paid as a daily amount which calculated following conversion of the RAD amount, or
  • combination of the RAD and DAP

Accommodation contribution

An accommodation contribution is a contribution towards accommodation costs.

It may be payable if the means tested amount (as determined by the means assessment) on the date of entry to care is less than the maximum accommodation supplement amount (as determined in the Department of Health's Schedule of Fees and Charges). In this case:

  • the care recipient will be assessed as 'low means'
  • the government will contribute towards accommodation costs
  • an accommodation contribution will be affected by changes to income and assets

Care recipients have 28 days from the date (or proposed date) of entry to care to choose how the accommodation contribution will be paid. It can be paid either as a:

  • refundable accommodation contribution (RAC)
    • paid in full, as a lump sum; or
  • daily accommodation contribution (DAC)
    • paid as a daily amount; or
  • combination of the RAC and DAC

Extra service fees

This fee is paid if the care recipient has elected to receive extra services as part of their agreement with the service provider. Extra services are available in extra service facilities only. These facilities are required to publish their extra service fees on the My Aged Care website.

Extra service facilities provide a higher standard of accommodation, not extra nursing care. They cover services such as:

  • onsite hairdressing
  • pay television
  • special therapies such as massage
  • improved choice of meals and inclusions such as beer and wine

What will my fees be if I opt in to the post 1 July 2014 assessment rules?

Care recipients can opt into the post 1 July 2014 assessment rules if:

  • they received a home care or permanent residential care service prior to 1 July 2014, and
  • they will be moving to a new service on or after 1 July 2014, and
  • they have not yet moved to the new service, and
  • the new service type is the same as the previous service type (that is, moving from home care into home care, or residential care into residential care), and
  • they have not spent more than 28 days outside of care (other than on approved leave)

If the care recipient requests only an estimate of fees if they opt in offer to book the customer an appointment with an Aged Care Specialist Officer (ACSO), or refer them to the My Aged Care website or phone service for assistance with estimating fees when opting in. The website contains Fee Estimators for both home care and residential care.

Where the care recipient requests written advice of fees if they opt in

The care recipient will need to undergo an aged care means assessment (unless they elect not to disclose their financial details, in which case they will be deemed Means Not Disclosed and will pay the maximum fees applicable to their care type and level of care).

The care recipient may qualify for a verbal application or they may need to complete a calculation of your cost of care.

See Aged care means assessment for information regarding when a calculation of your cost of care is required.

How to opt in

In order to opt in, the care recipient must complete a Continuing Care Recipient opting into the New Aged Care Arrangements from 1 July 2014 form (AC022).

  • this form must be lodged with the new service provider before moving to the new service

Note: completing a calculation of your cost of care is not considered to be a formal request to opt in.

What is the progress of my assessment?

To check the progress of an aged care means assessment, check the care recipient's Benefit Status line in Customer First.

RCA/CAN:

  • Check the Benefit Status (XBS) screen which displays a status history for aged care, or
  • Check the DL screen for a DOC stating Cancellation of Assessment
  • These checks will show date of cancellation and reason

RCA/CAN-EXP:

  • Check the DL screen for a DOC stating Care Assessment - FIN
  • This DOC will indicate what date the means assessment was completed
    • A means assessment is valid for 120 days
    • If the assessment was completed more than 120 days prior to the date of the query, a new assessment will be required if there has been no entry into care within the 120 validity period

RCA/CUR:

  • Check the DL screen for a DOC stating Care Assessment - FIN
  • This DOC should include details relating to completion of the assessment, including the date that the assessment was finalised
  • A care recipient can expect to receive a letter within 14 days of the completion of an assessment. If the care recipient has not received a letter within this timeframe, review the record (in all applicable systems) to determine why the letter has not automatically generated. ICT escalation may be required
  • A manual letter may be requested if:
    • All systems contain correct information
    • Links between systems are current
    • A letter was not issued within 5 working days of an assessment being completed
  • For assistance with requesting a manual letter, refer to the Aged care letters task card in Aged care letters

RCA/NCL or no RCA status:

  • Check the DL and AL screens for a DOC stating Progress of Assessment
    • This DOC should state what, if any, additional information has been requested from the care recipient, or any other reason why the assessment has not yet been completed, for example, Complex Assessment Officer (CAO) referral or property valuation
  • Advise the care recipient why the assessment has not been completed and of any information that has been requested from them
  • If the care recipient advises that all information has now been provided:
    • Confirm that all information has been returned and is able to be viewed via Document Tools, and/or confirm that DL states that an internal referral has been completed
    • If the care recipient receives an income support payment and the assessment can be completed within 20 minutes, complete the assessment while the care recipient is on the phone
    • If the assessment is deemed urgent add the keyword of RCAPRI to increase the priority of the work item. An additional ACTDOC is not required
    • An assessment is deemed urgent if the care recipient will lose an Aged Care placement if it is delayed, or they are experiencing financial hardship and require an urgent assessment/reassessment of income and assets

Can I receive a letter urgently?

A letter can be provided urgently if the 'Aged Care URGENT Letter Request' criteria have been met.

Refer to the Aged care letters task card in Aged care letters for information regarding the criteria and the process required to request an urgent manual letter.

Care recipient details including income and assets, type of care, fees and charges and entry details need to be confirmed in all relevant aged care systems prior to requesting a manual letter.

12

Why haven't my fees and charges been updated after I have advised of a change in my circumstances?

Changes in aged care fees following a change in circumstances will not usually occur until the next Quarterly Review (QR).

  • The QR is a reconciliation process that aligns the fees that a care recipient has paid, with the fees associated with their care needs, over the past quarter
  • The care recipient will need to wait for the QR for the fee change to be applied, and for any refund owing to be paid

Care recipients who receive an Income Support Payment (ISP) may be confused about the need to wait until the next QR for their fees to change. When there is a change in circumstances related to their ISP, the system applies the change immediately and the care recipient can see an immediate effect on their ISP. This is not the case for aged care fees and charges.

If it is identified that a care recipient would be significantly disadvantaged (for example, placed in financial hardship) if required to wait until the next QR for a change in ongoing fees and charges following a change in circumstances, they may request an immediate Ad Hoc Review of their ongoing fees and charges.

  • Ad hoc reviews can occur automatically (for residential care only, where there has been a significant change) or be undertaken manually (for both residential and home care)
  • a manual ad hoc review should only be undertaken where it has been determined that:
    • an automatic ad hoc review will not occur
    • there has been a significant change in circumstances
    • the care recipient would be significantly disadvantaged if an ad hoc review is not undertaken
  • an ad hoc review will not generate a refund (where a refund is due)
    • the care recipient will be required to wait until the QR for any refund owing to be calculated and paid

For further information regarding Quarterly Reviews, see Aged care reviews - quarterly reviews.

I am a nominee (or an authorised representative) and I want to discuss aged care

Arrangements can be made that allow a third party to enquire or act on behalf of a care recipient when dealing with Services Australia. This is referred to as a nominee arrangement.

The type of form used to create a nominee arrangement will determine:

  • what information can be accepted from or provided to a third party for aged care purposes
  • what correspondence the third party may receive from each of the aged care systems (Customer First, Aged Care Management and Payment System (ACMPS) and System for the Payment of Aged Residential Care (SPARC))

There are 3 form types which will allow the appointment of a nominee:

Authorising a person or organisation to enquire or act on your behalf form (SS313)

This enables care recipients to nominate a person or organisation to enquire, act or update on their behalf for Centrelink payments and services including aged care.

See Appointing nominees.

Note: from 11 November 2020 the SS313 is to be used by aged care recipients to appoint a nominee (excluding DVA care recipients).

Aged Care Request for a nominee for Department of Veterans' Affairs customers form (AC019)

This enables DVA care recipients to nominate a person or organisation to enquire, act or update on their behalf regarding their aged care costs.

To appoint an aged care nominee email the digital image of the AC019 to the Aged Care - Payments Team.

See Aged care request for a nominee.

Note: prior to 11 November 2020 the AC019 was used for all aged care recipients to appoint a nominee.

Aged Care Calculation of your cost of care (means assessment) forms

Aged Care Calculation of your cost of care forms contain an 'Authorising a person or organisation to enquire or act on your behalf form (SS313) section which allows for the appointment of a nominee.

See Aged care means assessment.

I am an aged care provider and I need assistance

Aged care providers may contact either the Aged Care Means Testing Line or the Aged Care Provider Enquiry line directly.

Calls may also be transferred (announced) from the Aged Care Provider Enquiry Line to the Aged Care Means Testing Line when the original caller is on the line and the query relates to means testing (for example, the fee letter they have received appears to be incorrect).

  • When this occurs, a Softphone screen pop will appear and staff on the Provider Enquiry Line will have obtained the required information from the service provider to document the record
  • If the Provider Enquiry Line Service Officer does not have the service provider on the line, the correct procedure is for the Provider Enquiry Line Service Officer to contact the Aged Care means assessment team via email
    • For contact details, see Office Locator (search RCA)

Aged care providers are considered to have 'implied consent', however care must be taken to ensure the care recipient's privacy is maintained. See Implied consent regarding third parties for further information if required.

Where an aged care provider enquiry is not related to a means assessment, the call should be transferred (announced) to the Aged Care Provider Enquiry line.

The following information must be obtained from the aged care provider and documented in the care recipient record:

  • The service provider's service ID and service name
  • Return contact details of the service provider (phone and email if possible) and the details of the care recipient (name, DOB and reference number)

When an aged care provider contacts as they have not received a fee advice:

  • Check the applicable aged care system to determine whether a fee letter has been issued, and whether the information in the letter is correct. If required, re-issue the fee letter to all parties
  • See Aged care letters task card in Aged care letters for assistance if required
  • Do not reissue any letters that have been manually stopped (for residential care, the Letter Sent Ind in the System for the Payment of Aged Residential Care (SPARC) screen 2.13 will show as M)

Considerations when providing information to an aged care provider regarding fees:

  • If the fee letter is incorrect, remediation of the care recipient's record should be undertaken to correct the issue and allow an updated letter to be generated as soon as possible. It is not necessary to seek further information from the care recipient where an administration error occurred
  • Services Australia is prevented from releasing any personal information about the care recipient to the provider
    • The only exception is with regards to income and asset details used for means assessment purposes. This information can only be released if financial disclosure consent has been provided
    • See the Services Australia website for more information on aged care financial disclosure
    • Information regarding financial disclosure is also available in Aged care - consent and disclosure
  • Do not ask the service provider why they think that information contained in a fee letter is incorrect
    • If the service provider claims that the care recipient has additional income and/or assets that have not been reported to Centrelink, suggest that the service provider advises the care recipient to contact Services Australia to provide their correct information
    • If the care recipient declines, the service provider should be directed to the Australian Government Services Fraud Tip-off Line
