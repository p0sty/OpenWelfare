DRA and NZ DRA average weekly income cut-off points

Based on the Rate Calculator Determination 2020.

Average weekly income is the Average Weekly Ordinary Time Earnings (AWOTE) for a full-time adult in Australia according to the Australian Bureau of Statistics.

The Australian Bureau of Statistics updates the AWOTE in May each year. It applies for the following year from 1 January to 31 December.

Table 3

Step

Action

1

To assess a customer’s LGA

  • Open the WA Live Feed App
  • Place the address given by the customer in the search box
  • Select Search (magnifying glass icon)
  • Select the payment the customer is claiming in the Layers menu on the right hand side. Use AGDRP for NZ DRP claims and DRA for NZ DRA claims
  • If the address provided by the customer is:
    • in a declared LGA, the name will show in the middle of the LGA. Note: staff may need to zoom out until the LGA name shows
    • not in a declared LGA, to identify the name scroll down the Layers menu on the right hand side and select LGA 2020

Does reasonably close to the major disaster need to be assessed?

  • Yes, go to Step 2
  • No, continue to assess the claim:
    • Assessing AGDRP and NZ DRP claims
    • Assessing DRA and NZ DRA claims

2

To assess reasonably close to the major disaster

  • Select the Measure icon (right angle triangle) under the search bar
  • Select the Distance icon (second icon in the dark blue box on the right)
  • Left mouse click on the customer's address
  • Drag the mouse to the nearest burnscar
  • Note the distance listed under measurement result. This will be shown in km

Do not use Google maps to assess reasonably close to the major disaster.

Continue to assess the claim:

  • Assessing AGDRP and NZ DRP claims
  • Assessing DRA and NZ DRA claims
