For Compensation Recovery Teams (CRT) only.

Request for specific compensation information

Step

Action

1

Correspondence from solicitor + Read more ...

If the correspondence is from:

  • customer's solicitor, go to Step 2
  • defendant or compensation payers solicitor, go to Step 3

2

Release of information to the customer's solicitor + Read more ...

The customer's solicitor has implied authority to get information on the effect the compensation claim has on Centrelink payments. See Actioning compensation interest checks (CLK).

Procedure ends here.

3

Advising of a compensation claim + Read more ...

If the agency has an interest in the compensation claim, a copy of any notices sent to the compensation payer can be sent to their legal representatives. Sending a copy of the notices can be considered providing a copy to the original addressee.

Notices sent to compensation payers include:

  • Preliminary Notice, or
  • Recovery Notice, or
  • Release Notice

If the solicitor contacts by phone to discuss information about a customer (current or former), only disclose information concerning the compensation claim (for example, compensation charge). This is not general permission to disclose any protected information.

Staff must be satisfied that the solicitor is acting for the compensation payer or insurer.

Is the delegate satisfied the solicitor is acting for the defendant?

  • Yes, go to Step 4
  • No, go to Step 5

4

Interest checks + Read more ...

Whether or not agency interest exists, fax a copy of the notice addressed to the compensation payer to their legal representatives.

The notice must be addressed to the compensation payer or insurer.

  • Agency interest, see Step 6 in the What to do when an interest check is received Table
  • No agency interest, see Step 7 in the What to do when an interest check is received Table

Procedure ends here.

5

Do not send notices to the solicitor + Read more ...

Do not disclose any information or give a copy of the notices to the solicitor. Fax the notices to the compensation payer or insurer.

See Faxing letters using Outlook and Customer First.

  • Agency interest, see Step 6 in the What to do when an interest check is received Table
  • No agency interest, see Step 7 in the What to do when an interest check is received Table

Procedure ends here.
