Completing the Claim for ABSTUDY Allowances and Part-time Award (SY021) 010-02010090

  • Background
  • Process

Expand all


This document outlines the process for Service Officers assisting Aboriginal and Torres Strait Islander students and Australian Apprentices complete a paper Claim for ABSTUDY Allowances and Part-time Award (SY021).

When to use this claim form

The Claim for ABSTUDY Allowances and Part-time Award (SY021) should be completed by Aboriginal and Torres Strait Islander:

  • part-time students
  • full-time students claiming the non-means-tested ABSTUDY allowances only (including Incidentals Allowance), for example the customer's income will preclude payment of ABSTUDY Living Allowance
  • Australian Apprentices claiming Incidentals Allowance only, or
  • customers claiming assistance to travel away from home to:
    • attend a selection test or interview for entry into a tertiary, Masters or Doctorate level course, or
    • determine their suitability for the Indigenous Youth Mobility Programme

If an Australian Apprentice or full-time student is unsure what to claim or wishes to test their eligibility for Living Allowance, they should complete the Claim for ABSTUDY (SY019).

Completing the paper claim

Points to note in completing the claim:

  • The claim must be completed in blue or black pen
  • All relevant questions on the claim should be answered. Many questions only require a 'Yes' or 'No' tick
  • Identity Confirmation is not required when lodging a SY021

Note: Service Officers assisting customers to complete a claim must use a separate sheet of paper (attached to the claim), to capture any notations or explanations to questions. No underline or comments of any sort are to be added to a claim that is to be signed by a customer and lodged with Centrelink.

Lodging the paper claim

ABSTUDY paper claims and supporting documents can be lodged by using any of the following options:

  • Online - using the Upload documents service
  • Fax - see Processing Services details for: ABSTUDY and ABSTUDY PES
  • Post - via a reply paid envelope or by posting to Services Australia. See Processing Services details for: ABSTUDY and ABSTUDY PES
  • In person - at a service centre, or in remote areas, with a Centrelink field officer
