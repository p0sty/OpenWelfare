Frequently asked questions and answers for cardiac MRI

Table 5

Item

Eligible providers

1

Question: Who is eligible for this Medicare rebate?

Answer: Patients who present with symptoms of Arrhythmogenic Right Ventricular Cardiomyopathy (ARVC) or patients who are asymptomatic with a family history of confirmed ARVC in a first degree blood relative.

2

Question: Who can request and perform the cardiac MRI scan?

Answer: A specialist in diagnostic radiology or consultant physician, who is recognised by the Conjoint Committee for Certification in Cardiac MRI may provide cardiac MRI items 63395 to 63398.

All MRI requests must be made in writing, identifying the clinical indications for the service. Generally, MRI items cannot be self-determined. However, items 63395 to 63398 may be self-determined where the treating consultant physician who is recognised by the Conjoint Committee for Certification in Cardiac MRI deems the scan necessary for the management of their patient’s condition.

3

Question: How do I gain accreditation / recognition?

Answer: The Conjoint Committee for Certification in Cardiac MRI is comprised of specialists from the Royal Australian and New Zealand College of Radiologists (RANZCR) and the Cardiac Society of Australia and New Zealand (CSANZ).

For information about applying for Certification in Cardiac MRI visit:

The Royal Australian and New Zealand College of Radiologists’ website

4

Question: Are there limits on how many cardiac MRI scans a patient can have?

Answer: Patients with symptoms of Arrhythmogenic Right Ventricular Cardiomyopathy (ARVC) can receive the Medicare rebate for a cardiac MRI scan once in any 12-month period.

Patients who are asymptomatic with a family history of confirmed ARVC in a first degree blood relative can receive the Medicare rebate for a cardiac MRI scan once in any 36-month period.

5

Question: Where can practitioners and patients find out more about MRI services?

Answer: MBS Online and the Department of Health’s website.
