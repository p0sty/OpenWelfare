Information displayed on AIR immunisation history statements

Table 1

Note: customers and staff can request either a complete immunisation history statement or a COVID-19 and influenza immunisation history statement.

Item

Field

Description

1

As at (date)

Date the immunisation history statement was produced.

2

For (name)

Individual the immunisation history statement is for.

3

Date of birth

Individual date of birth, recorded by Medicare or the AIR for individuals not enrolled in Medicare.

4

NIP immunisation status

(only for individuals under 20)

NIP Immunisation status:

  • up to date - if an individual is not overdue for any immunisations
  • up to date/not up to date - Catch-up schedule active until dd/mm/yyyy
  • not up to date - if an individual is overdue for at least one immunisation

5

Schedule

(only for individuals under 20)

Age at which the vaccines are listed on the NIP Schedule (for example 2, 4, or 6 months).

6

Immunisation

Disease antigens immunised against (for example, diphtheria).

7

Date given

Date the vaccine/s was given.

8

Brand name given

Vaccination brand name given (for example, Infanrix-IPV). All recorded vaccination episodes that are at an A or Y status are included on immunisation history statements.

9

Next NIP immunisation/s due

Diseases the individual needs to be immunised against next. If there are multiple overdue dates, only single antigens will be listed (e.g. diphtheria, polio).

10

Date due

(only for individuals under 20)

Date when the next immunisations are or were due. This date may be in future (due) or past (overdue).

11

Notice/s

Notice/s appear under specific circumstances.

See the Examples of notices attachment for images of how these may appear.

FAQ for Service Officers

Table 4

Note: this information must not be released externally.

Item

Question and response

1

Why does the immunisation history statement not show all my or my child’s immunisations?

If any immunisations are missing from your immunisation history statement, speak to your vaccination provider and ask them to record them on the AIR. All immunisations recorded on the AIR show on your immunisation history statement.

The AIR does not record vaccines given before 1 January 1996.

The AIR records National Immunisation Program (NIP) Schedule vaccines, private vaccines (such as for influenza or travel), vaccines given through school programs and vaccines given overseas.

2

Why are my or my child's personal details incorrect on the proof of vaccination?

Personal details on the AIR come from your Medicare enrolment. You can change the details with Medicare and the AIR will update overnight.

The next day, you can access proof of your vaccinations using your Express Plus Medicare mobile app or through myGov using your Medicare online account, or My Health Record.

If you’re not enrolled in Medicare, your personal details come from:

  • your IHI record,
  • or your vaccination provider when they notified the AIR of your vaccinations

You can update your personal details with the AIR and your immunisation record will be updated. You can then access proof of your vaccinations using the IHI service or My Health Record in myGov.

3

My or my child’s proof of vaccination has wrong information on it. How do I get this fixed?

If your or your child’s vaccination history is showing incorrect information, speak with your vaccination provider and ask them to update the AIR.

You can take evidence of your immunisations to a vaccination provider and ask them to record them on the AIR.

For help to correct your vaccination history, contact the AIR public enquiry line.

4

My child is 14 or older, can I get proof of their vaccinations?

If your child is 14 or older, you will not be able to access their vaccination history.

Individuals 14 or older can get proof of their own vaccinations online. For contact details, see AIR enquiries.

An individual 14 years or older can also give consent for their parent or guardian to access their vaccination information. They can do this over the phone or in person at a service centre. This consent is only valid for that instance.

If a:

  • Power of Attorney,
  • guardianship order,
  • administrative order, or
  • other legal arrangement is in place, and
  • is registered with Medicare, the parent or guardian has legal authority to act on behalf of the person

Parents, guardians or carers of individuals 14 or older who cannot manage their own affairs can submit an:

  • Authorisation to act on an incapacitated person’s behalf for Medicare purposes form (MO050) if no legal arrangement is in place

5

Why can I not get my 14 or older child’s vaccination information?

The AIR is administered in accordance with the Australian Immunisation Register Act 2015, the Privacy Act 1988 and the Services Australia privacy policy.

Parents and guardians are able to access their child’s vaccination information until the child turns 14. When an individual is 14 or older, Services Australia requires their consent to provide their vaccination information. This includes requests for proof of vaccination.

6

How do vaccination providers link to the AIR?

Providers with a Medicare provider number already have access to the AIR. They can use their PRODA individual account to add HPOS as a service and access the AIR site.

See Australian Immunisation Register (AIR) site access in HPOS.

7

Do vaccination providers have to link their Healthcare Provider Identifier (HPI) numbers or My Health Record (MHR) to the AIR?

No, vaccination providers do not need to link their HPI-I/HPI-O number or My Health Record to the AIR. If they need help linking to the AIR, information is available on the Services Australia website.

See Australian Immunisation Register (AIR) site functions.
