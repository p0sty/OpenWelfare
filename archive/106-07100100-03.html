Checking the returned MOD P

Step

Action

1

Assess customer's current circumstances + Read more ...

  • If a Request for Information review activity has matured for the return of the MOD P. Note: this includes when additional forms were required, and when a Request for Information letter was issued for an incomplete MOD P, e.g. Q004. Go to Step 2
  • If a customer uploads a MOD P (Work Item ZDMS_MODP - MOD P Partner Details):
    • Check the Activity List (AL) screen to identify the form code on the left of the activity
    • Check the Document List (DL) screen to identify if there was a triggering event that created this activity
    • Go to Step 3
  • If the customer has returned a MOD P, go to Step 3

2

A Request for Information review activity has matured for MOD P to be returned + Read more ...

The customer has contacted before to advise they are partnered and a Request for Information (RFI) letter was issued for the partner to complete and return a MOD P..

A Request for Information review activity for the return of incomplete MOD P may also have been created if the customer has previously returned an incomplete MOD P.

When the review activity matures, check the customer's record for the return of MOD P via the Document Tools screen.

Has the MOD P been lodged?

  • Yes, go to Step 3
  • No, as a request to provide information was sent to the customer and the allowable time has passed:
    • Suspend the income support payment reason FRC (failing to reply to correspondence)
    • Record details on a DOC
    • Procedure ends here

3

MOD P lodged + Read more ...

Check:

  • Both the customer and their partner have signed the form
  • The form has been fully completed and can be clearly read. The Resources page has a link to the MOD P
  • No further forms need to be issued

Is the MOD P complete (no further form or clarification needed from the partner)?

  • Yes, go to Step 10
  • No,
    • If the form is not signed by the customer, go to Step 4
    • If the form is not signed by the partner, go to Step 5
    • If the form is incomplete or clarification is needed, go to Step 5
    • If further forms are required, go to Step 9

4

Contact customer + Read more ...

Service Officers can accept and use verbal information from the customer if information is missing or needs clarifying.

Try to contact the customer to verify the relationship.

Was contact successful?

  • Yes, go to Step 6
  • No, go to Step 8

5

Incomplete form or clarification needed + Read more ...

Service Officers can accept and use verbal information from the partner if information is missing or needs clarifying.

If the start date of the relationship has not been completed or there is conflicting information around the start date of the relationship, contact the partner to clarify the correct relationship start date.

The partner is under no obligation to provide information verbally. Services Australia will not be liable for any charge or fee related to gathering information authorised under social security law.

Try to contact the partner to verbally confirm the information.

Was contact successful?

  • Yes, and the partner:
    • agrees to verbally confirm the missing information (or confirm the relationship if they did not sign the form), go to Step 6
    • does not agree to give the information over the phone, go to Step 7
  • No, go to Step 7

6

Verbal information + Read more ...

The privacy statement and declaration about false and misleading information must be read to the customer/partner and recorded in the DOC to confirm these have been read.

Privacy and your personal information

'Your personal information is protected by law (including the Privacy Act 1988) and is collected by the Australian Government agency Services Australia for the assessment and administration of payments and services. Your information may be used by the agency, or given to other parties where you have agreed to that, or where it is required or authorised by law (including for the purpose of research or conducting investigations). You can get more information about the way in which the agency will manage your personal information, including our privacy policy, at servicesaustralia.gov.au/privacy.'

After obtaining details verbally, read the following Declaration to the partner:

Declaration about false and misleading information

'Do you declare that the information you have provided is complete and correct and understand that giving false or misleading information is a serious offence?'

When collecting information verbally, Service Officers must record the contact, conversations and information given on a DOC. If the customer or partner has not signed the form, the Service Officer must confirm with the person who has not signed the form that they are in a relationship.

Text to include in the DOC

Ext Details: verbal info regarding MOD P

Text: verbal information obtained from the customer/partner (delete option that does not apply) via phone on (dd/mm/yyyy) in relation to incomplete Partner details (MOD P) form lodged. Information sought in relation to Partner details (MOD P) form, question: (question number). Details obtained: (free text). Responses verbally confirmed. The Privacy Statement and Declaration about false or misleading information have been read to the partner.

Go to Step 10.

7

Partner does not want to provide information + Read more ...

Establish why the partner does not want to provide the information verbally.

If the partner says:

  • they do not want to provide the information over the phone, tell them they will be sent a formal request for the required information. This may cause delays to the customerâ€™s claim or result in a debt if the customer or partner is receiving a payment. Go to Step 8
  • they are not in a relationship with the customer, a Member of a Couple (MoC) assessment may be required. Follow the process when the partner does not confirm the relationship. Procedure ends here

8

Request information in writing + Read more ...

Service Officers may issue the following letters to the customer or partner, depending on what information is required:

  • Request for Information (RFI) - if selecting the required document in the RFI guided procedure (e.g. TFN from the partner). To issue the RFI letter, see Requesting information (CLK
  • Request customer to complete Form or Questionnaire (Q004) - if it is not possible or practical to obtain incomplete information verbally, or when the required information is not available in the RFI guided procedure. For example, if the:
    • customer and/or partner's name is missing
    • form has not been signed
    • partner refuses to provide information via phone, or
    • contact with partner has not been established

To issue the Q004, see Creating an Online Advice (OLA), including within an existing activity, noting the following:

  • print the letter locally
  • print the incomplete MOD P and insert it with the Q004
  • set a manual review to mature when the allowable time has passed to follow up the customer contact

Procedure ends here until the required information is returned.

9

Further forms required + Read more ...

The need for further forms (such as MOD IA or MOD F) will depend on the responses on the MOD P. Question 89 on the MOD P will list additional forms needed.

Check the customer's (and/or partner's) record. Make sure the required forms have not been issued or returned.

If additional forms are required:

  • direct the customer/partner to the Services Australia website to download the forms online
  • issue a request for additional required form/s. If using:
    • Customer First, use the Request for Information guided procedure
    • Process Direct, request additional forms via Request Document

An automatic review will create to follow up the return of information. This review will mature in 14 days on the Activity List (AL) screen. Resubmit the review activity to fall due when the allowable time has passed to follow up on the return of the form.

Procedure ends here.

10

Completed MOD P returned or information verified verbally + Read more ...

Is the MOD P a required task as part of an online claim or ACC?

  • Yes, set the Partner Confirmation or MOD P task to Provided. Continue assessing the claim. Procedure ends here
  • No, go to Step 11

11

Check if payment is suspended + Read more ...

Does the customer or partner's payment status show as SUS (suspended)?

  • Yes,
    • Do not update and link the records until after:
      investigating the reason for the suspension, and
      restoring the payment
      Note: linking records before restoring the payment may result in a limiting date error when restoring payment
    • After restoring payment, go to Step 12
  • No, go to Step 12

12

Action MOD P + Read more ...

If the customer is:

  • single and receiving Parenting Payment (PP), see Transfer from Parenting Payment Single (PPS) to Parenting Payment Partnered (PPP)
  • partnered and transferring to Parenting Payment (PP) from another payment, see Claiming Parenting Payment (PP)
  • receiving Youth Allowance (YA), see Youth Allowance (YA) or ABSTUDY customer becomes partnered
  • only receiving Family Tax Benefit (FTB) and/or Child Care Subsidy (CCS), see to FTB/CCS customer becomes partnered

In all other circumstances, update relationship status and link customer and partner records.
