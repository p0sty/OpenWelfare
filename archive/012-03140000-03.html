This page contains the process to follow when an organisation applies to the Healthcare Identifiers (HI) Service.

Processing evidence of identity (EOI) for HI Service

This table provides the process a Service Officer follows when processing EOI when an organisation applies to the HI Service.

Step

Action

1

Received notice issued by Register of the Australian Business Register (ABR) + Read more ...

Has an original or certified copy of the notice issued by the ABR bearing the business entity’s name and Australian Business Number (ABN) been provided, with the Responsible Officer's (RO) name listed as the Public Officer:

  • Yes, go to Step 3
  • No, go to Step 2

2

Check for other legal or regulatory document + Read more ...

Other types of acceptable documentary evidence could include a certified copy of any one of the following documents:

  • certificate of registration of a company issued by the Australian Securities and Investments Commission and the RO's is name listed as the Public Officer
  • the notice issued by the Registrar of the Australian Business Register (ABR) bearing the business entity’s name, Australian Business Number and the RO’s name is listed as the Public Officer
  • the organisation’s appointment as a trustee (if the legal structure is a trust) with the RO as a stakeholder
  • contract for sale or purchase of business addressed to the RO
  • statement of transaction issued by a financial institution in the name of the company, addressed to the RO and less than 1 year old
  • lease agreement for the organisation’s primary place of business addressed to the RO
  • rates notice for the organisation’s primary place of business addressed to the RO
  • certificate of change of name for the organisation issued by the Australian Securities and Investments Commission and addressed to the RO
  • a document issued by the Australian Taxation Office with the organisation’s name and tax file number and addressed to the RO
  • if the business is a Pharmacy, Pharmaceutical Benefits Scheme (PBS) approval number, the applicant’s name must be one of the approved persons relating to the approval record
  • an affidavit or statutory declaration that the RO can commit the business, sworn by a member of the board or executive of the seed organisation
  • a deed of appointment appointing the RO as being able to commit the business, where the appointer is listed on the ABR as the Public Officer, the CEO or the Director
  • charity registration letter issued by the Australian Charities and Not-for-profits Commission and addressed to the RO
  • a Workforce Incentive Program (WIP) - Practice Stream or Practice Incentive Program (PIP) confirmation statement outlining:
    • full practice details
    • Ownership details including the applicants (RO’s name) listed as the Owner/Partner/Associates name
    • Note: a WIP – Practice Stream or PIP confirmation statement cannot be used as an Authority to Act (A2A) for a person listed as a contact person only

Note: if the applicant has not previously been named as a person who can commit the pharmacy, or works for another type of organisation, the applicant needs to include an option from the above dot points or Step 1.

Has one of the above documents been provided:

  • Yes, go to Step 3
  • No, go to Step 5

3

Verify the business’s Australian Business Number (ABN) + Read more ...

Verify the business’s ABN to its business name via the ABR website (Resources page contains a link to the ABR website).

4

Process in eHealth Program (EHP) system + Read more ...

From the drop down box in EHP:

  • select EOI supplied
  • end of procedure

5

Put application on hold + Read more ...

If one of the acceptable legal documents has not been received:

  • put the application on hold
  • escalate to Provider HI Service and My Health Record Subject Matter Expert (SME) for confirmation
