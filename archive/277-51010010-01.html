This document outlines information about applying to Child Support for a child support assessment.

Reasonable maintenance action

Even if a customer's personal income means they will only receive base rate Family Tax Benefit (FTB) Part A, they must be encouraged to take reasonable action to obtain child support. It is every parent's responsibility to provide financial support for their children.

Customers may only apply for a child support assessment for an eligible child. However, it is not always appropriate for the customer to apply - first check that an application for child support assessment is needed.

Maintenance Action Test

For parents, there is a 13 week (91 day) timeframe for the customer to take reasonable action to obtain child support so Family Tax Benefit (FTB) Part A can continue to be paid at more than the base rate for a child. Information should be given to the customer about the effect of taking action to obtain child support and maintenance income on their rate of FTB Part A.

How to apply

Customers must contact Child Support directly, either online or by phone, to apply for a child support assessment.

Customers should make an application for a child support assessment online, either via:

  • the Services Australia website, or
  • their myGov account, if they are an existing Child Support customer

Links to the Services Australia website and myGov are located on the Resources page

After the application is submitted, Child Support will contact the applicant and other party wherever possible to:

  • ensure all relevant information is provided, and
  • discuss the child support process and provide support options where required

Customers that apply for child support online or via their myGov account are issued a receipt. The receipt includes their application number and the date and time the application was submitted. Customers can provide this information verbally to Centrelink as evidence of taking maintenance action.

If the customer contacts Centrelink to discuss applying for a child support assessment, refer the customer to Child Support. This allows all required information to be obtained and advice given to the customer about their child support options. For contact details, see Child Support.

Customers contacting Child Support by phone will be provided with the following information and will then be 'cold transferred' to Centrelink to allow maintenance action details to be recorded on their record:

  • Child Support Case Number
  • Child/ren's names the case applies to, and
  • Start Date of Child Support case

There is no requirement for staff to confirm these details with Child Support unless the Maintenance Action Test (MAT) codes (APP or CAC) have previously been coded in the past 3 to 6 months. In these cases, evidence of action should be sought before updating the customer's record.

Payer lives overseas

If the customer advises they are privately collecting 100% of an overseas court ordered amount, and:

  • the payer is overseas in either a non-reciprocating or excluded jurisdiction, or
  • the payer is in a reciprocating jurisdiction

Refer to Action to obtain child support when the paying parent is overseas and the Maintenance Action Test (MAT) for further information and what to advise the customer.

Shared care

From 1 July 2008, as part of the Child Support Scheme Reforms, Child Support changed how they assess customers for child support purposes. The main change is where customers share the care of a child or each have one or more children in care, there is now only one child support case rather than 2.

Parents and carers under 18 years of age

As part of the Commonwealth Child Safe Framework, Services Australia has developed Child Safe Policy May 2019. The policy outlines the agencyâ€™s commitment to creating an environment that safeguards children under 18 years of age during their interactions with the agency.

The Parent Support Team (PST) manages Child Support customers who are a parent or carer and under 18 years of age. PST management continues until the customer turns 18 years of age.

To transfer customers on the phone to PST who fit the criteria, see Customer Referral Guidelines for Child Support customers.

Non-parent carers

If a customer provides care for a child and they are not the parent, they may be able to apply for Child Support from both of the child's parents. Examples of non-parent carers include legal guardians, grandparents or other family members.

There is no MAT requirement for these customers if they elect not to apply, however they still may wish to apply for an assessment. If an assessment is commenced for a non-parent carer the Maintenance Income Test (MIT) will apply as per normal.

Customers can apply for a non-parent carer child support assessment if they:

  • care for a child 128 nights or more a year (35 per cent or more care), and
  • are not considered to be a member of a couple with one of the child's parents
