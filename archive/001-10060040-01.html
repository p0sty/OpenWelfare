Applying an Unemployment Non-Payment Period (UNPP) and determining hardship provisions 001-10060040

This document explains the processes for deciding if a Job Seeker Compliance Framework Unemployment Non-Payment Period (UNPP) applies because of voluntary unemployment, or dismissal because of misconduct for a Community Development Program (CDP) participant/new claimant. It also explains when the payment can be reinstated under hardship provisions if a UNPP is applied.

Reasons for applying a UNPP

A UNPP applies when a CDP participant:

  • voluntarily ceases suitable employment without a reasonable excuse, or
  • is dismissed from suitable employment due to misconduct as a worker

When a CDP participant does not commence suitable work, a serious failure may apply.

Duration and start date of UNPP

When a decision is made to apply a UNPP, unless the CDP participant is eligible for payment under hardship provisions, participation payments will not be payable, for a period of 8 weeks.

The start date of the UNPP will depend on whether the CDP participant was receiving a participation payment at the time they became unemployed.

  • If the CDP participant was not in receipt of a participation payment at the time they became unemployed, the UNPP commences from the date the CDP participant became unemployed
  • If the CDP participant was receiving a participation payment at the time they became unemployed, the UNPP commences from the date that is more beneficial, either the date of unemployment or the start date of the instalment period following the date the determination is made that a UNPP applies

Serving a UNPP with other waiting periods or exclusion periods

A UNPP may be served concurrently with other waiting periods including:

  • Liquid Assets Waiting Period (LAWP)
  • Income Maintenance Period (IMP)
  • Seasonal Workers Preclusion Period (SWPP)
  • Moving to an Area of Lower Employment Prospects (MALEP)

When other waiting or exclusion periods extend beyond a UNPP, there is no requirement to proceed with the UNPP investigation.

MALEP and UNPPs

When a CDP participant has left work voluntarily (or is unemployed as a result of misconduct) and Moved to an Area of Lower Employment Prospects (MALEP) both a UNPP and a MALEP must be considered. Any periods of the UNPP and MALEP exclusion period that overlap can be served concurrently.

MALEP may apply if the CDP participant moves to a new location after they become unemployed. See Moving to an Area of Lower Employment Prospects (MALEP) employment related exclusion period.

Mutual obligation requirements and reporting during UNPP

CDP participants who are serving a UNPP are not required to comply with mutual obligation requirements and are not required to report for the duration of a UNPP.

Responsibility for investigating a UNPP and determining hardship provisions

The responsibility for investigating a UNPP is dependent on various factors including how the participant became unemployed and when the UNPP was identified.

Service Officers who apply a UNPP are also responsible for:

  • determining whether a CDP participant is eligible for payment under hardship provisions during a UNPP, and
  • ending the UNPP if hardship provisions apply

The assessment of hardship may occur either during the course of finalising the determination to apply a UNPP or at any stage while the CDP participant is serving the UNPP.

UNPP hardship provisions

Payment may be reinstated during a UNPP under hardship provisions if a CDP participant:

  • falls within a class of person specified by the Secretary of the Department of Employment and Workplace Relations, and
  • is in severe financial hardship as a result of the UNPP

Class of person specified by the Secretary of Department of Employment and Workplace Relations

For the purposes of reinstating payment during a UNPP, a CDP participant is in a class of person specified by the Secretary of the Department of Employment and Workplace Relations when they meet at least one of the following conditions:

  • have significant family and caring responsibilities, including persons with a dependent child
  • have an illness, impairment or condition that requires treatment and cannot afford this treatment after meeting their essential expenses
  • have a diagnosed cognitive, neurological, psychiatric or psychological impairment or mental illness
  • do not have access to safe, secure and adequate housing or are using emergency accommodation or refuge

Severe financial hardship

Severe financial hardship means a CDP participant who:

  • has a dependent child or is a member of a couple with liquid assets below $5000 (including partner's liquid assets)
  • is not a member of a couple and has liquid assets below $2,500

Making payment under hardship provisions

CDP participants can test their eligibility for payment under hardship provisions at any time during a UNPP. If a CDP participant does not initially qualify due the level of their liquid assets, they can approach Services Australia at any time during the UNPP for reassessment of hardship. The rules that relate to unavoidable or reasonable expenditure for the Liquid Assets Test do apply in these situations.

When a CDP participant, meets the criteria to be eligible for payment under hardship provisions, payment will be reinstated from the later of:

  • commencement of the UNPP, or
  • the date the CDP participant requested the assessment of eligibility for hardship provisions, and met the eligibility criteria

When payment is reinstated under hardship provisions, the CDP participant will be required to comply with mutual obligation requirements, including the requirement to connect with the CDP provider unless an exemption is granted.
