Customers not covered by this procedure + Read more ...

Family assistance customers may get a TFN override if they are experiencing an urgent or extreme circumstance.

Special Benefit customer under the age of 16 years + Read more ...

If a customer under 16 years of age meets all eligibility criteria for Special Benefit except TFN, record a TFN exemption code of U16 on the TFN screen.

If the customer or their partner lives overseas, and a TFN is:

  • provided, record this on their record

Newly arrived refugee + Read more ...

Refugee customers can fast track a TFN application, if they:

  • claim Family Tax Benefit (FTB), and
  • apply for a TFN online

2

Lost or forgotten TFN + Read more ...

If the TFN details are on record, confirm Proof of Record Ownership (PoRO) before giving a customer their TFN. Go to Step 3.

If the customer asks for assistance obtaining their TFN via:

  • service centre, they can:
    • use an Access Centre Front of House (FoH) phone
    • contact the ATO Personal Tax Enquiries line to get their TFN, and
    • give it in writing to Services Australia
  • Smart Centre, advise the customer to:
    • contact the ATO Personal Tax Enquiries line to get their TFN, and
    • give it in writing to Services Australia, or
    • request a Tax file number - application or enquiry for individuals (NAT 1432) form or Tax file number application or enquiry for Aboriginal and Torres Strait Islander people (NAT 1589) form from the ATO via the ATO website or phone, and return completed to the ATO or Centrelink Service Centre within 14 days

See the ATO website for more information.

Did the customer obtain their TFN details in writing?

  • Yes, key the details. See Table 3
  • No, the customer must return the form (NAT 1432 or NAT 1589) within 14 days, see Step 2 in Table 2

3

TFN used for previous claim + Read more ...

The authorisation defaults to 'Yes' if a customer provided their TFN for a previous claim. This will allow the claim to process without auto cancellation for non-provision of a TFN.

Procedure ends here.

4

How to provide a TFN + Read more ...

Customers must provide a TFN in writing when they make a claim. They can:

  • upload their TFN online through the Express Plus app or Centrelink Services Online

Customers who do not have a TFN can:

  • Apply online via the ATO website. See Step 5 in Table 2
  • Attend a Centrelink Service Centre and a Service Officer will issue a:
    • Tax file number - application or enquiry for individuals (NAT 1432) form, or
    • Tax file number - application or enquiry for Aboriginal and Torres Strait Islander people (NAT1589). Note: only use this form if the customer does not have enough identity documents to apply for a TFN using the NAT 1432 form
    • The customer must return the form within 14 days, see Step 2 in Table 2

Is processing a TFN exemption review Manual Follow-up (MFU) activity required?

  • Yes, go to Step 5
  • No, see Step 1 in Table 2

5

How to process Manual Follow-up (MFU) activities to review TFN exemption + Read more ...

A MFU activity is created when a temporary TFN exemption is recorded on the customer's record.

To review a written request for a TFN, go to Step 8.

Make 2 genuine attempts to contact the customer.

Can the customer be contacted?

  • Yes, go to Step 6
  • No, issue a written request for a TFN, see Request for Information (RFI) letter. Place the MFU on hold for 21 days. (Allow extra time for mail delivery)

6

Successful customer contact + Read more ...

Note: A TFN must be provided in writing, it cannot be provided verbally.

Can the customer or their representative provide a TFN in writing?

  • Yes,
    • Advise the customer to provide a TFN by uploading it online or attend the service centre within 14 days or their payment will stop
    • Record a DOC within the MFU activity outlining the discussion
    • Place the MFU on hold for 21 days (Allow extra time for mail delivery)
    • Procedure ends here
  • No, go to Step 7

7

TFN unable to be provided in writing + Read more ...

If the customer or partner is:

  • COVID-19 affected and this is preventing the customer from providing a TFN or applying for a TFN, the DIS TFN exemption can be re-applied for an additional 3 months. A subsequent review will apply. See the Resources page for code details
    • If the customer's payment is cancelled or suspended for a reason relating to a TFN, see Restoration of payments (CLK)
  • Not affected by COVID 19:
    • Advise the customer to provide a TFN or evidence of a TFN application within 14 days or their payment will stop
    • The TFN or evidence of a TFN application can be uploaded online or provided at a service centre
    • Record a DOC within the MFU activity outlining the discussion
    • Issue a written request for the TFN, see Request for Information (RFI) letter
    • Place the MFU on hold for 21 days (Allow extra time for mail delivery)

Procedure ends here.

8

How to review written request for TFN + Read more ...

Select the activity on the Activity List (AL) screen in Customer First.

Has the customer provided a TFN, or evidence of a TFN application in the time requested?

  • Yes,
    • If they have provided a TFN, see Step 1 in Table 3
    • If they have provided evidence a TFN application has been lodged
      Code the exemption reason 'CAT' (TFN request from ATO) on the TFN screen
      The ATO will provide the TFN within 28 days which will auto update the record
    • Complete the MFU activity
    • Procedure ends here
  • No,
    • Suspend the customers payment
    • Record a DOC within the MFU activity
    • Complete the MFU activity
    • Procedure ends here

Processing TFN application

Table 2

Step

Action

1

Customer has provided TFN details + Read more ...

Did the customer provide TFN details in writing when making their claim?

  • Yes, key the details. See Table 3
  • No,
    • Issue the customer a Request for Information (RFI) letter using the Request for Information guided procedure. Request TFN details within 14 days. The guided procedure will create a DOC and a review
    • Hold the customer's claim pending the return of the TFN details
    • Claim will reject if no response in 14 days. The claim can be reassessed from the date of rejection, if the TFN is supplied within 13 weeks of the rejection notice being sent

2

Customer returns the form + Read more ...

Did the customer return the tax file number application or enquiry form (NAT 1432 or NAT 1589) within 14 days?

  • Yes, go to Step 3
  • No, the claim will auto reject and a letter will issue. The claim can be reassessed from the date of rejection, if the TFN or TFN application is provided within 13 weeks

3

Customer returns the TFN application or enquiry form (NAT 1432 or NAT 1589) within 14 days + Read more ...

If customer is not a Centrelink customer, go to Step 7

Check form + Read more ...

On date of receipt at service centre:

  • Do not scan or forward to a processing team for completion
  • Check the applicant has:
    • signed and dated the form if they are over 16
    • completed the form in blue or black ink and the writing is within the boxes
    • not used whiteout or corrective tape
    • signed all alterations
    • answered all of the questions
    • authorised the release of the TFN to the agency at Question 3
  • Check and confirm:
    • date of birth
    • spouse details if relevant, and
    • postal and home address. Note: update a home address if the postal address is a post office box
  • Complete Date of Birth (Office Use Only) field
    • only if the date of birth is blank, or
    • the date given does not match the identity documents provided
  • Check the identity documents they provide. See the Resources page for ATO requirements. The ATO identity document codes are different to the agency's
  • If the applicant is:
    • over 16 years of age, they need to provide 3 identity documents. One must be a Primary Document
    • under 16 years of age, they need to provide 2 identity documents. One must be a Primary Document
  • Fill in the 'office use only' section. See Resources for code details.
    • Add all documents the ATO will accept
    • Contact Identity Program for help filling in the 'office use only' section if required
  • Stamp the form within the 'office stamp' box
  • Sign and print name

Alternative Identity + Read more ...

If granting the customer under an Alternative Identity assessment:

  • Record the details of all identity documents they provide
  • Attach certified copies of all identity documents
  • Record the reasons for using Alternative Identity Confirmation in the 'Notes' box. For example, homeless customers
  • Give detailed notes in the Notes section of the TFN application
  • Confirm contact details are correct
  • Advise the customer that the ATO may contact to discuss

The ATO will decide to issue a TFN or not.

Insufficient identity provided + Read more ...

If the customer does not have the identity documents the ATO needs, ask if they can provide:

  • a recent bank statement with their name and address shown
  • bank account card
  • student ID card

The ATO accepts bank account card (not credit card) as an acceptable document.

If the customer does not have the required identity documents on them, give the form back to the customer. Tell them to bring the form back with all required identity documents.

Note: for NAT 1589 form, staff may act as a referee for the customer if they meet all criteria. See Proof of identity reference section on the form. To avoid a conflict of interest, another Service Officer must complete the Office Use Only section.

Recently released from prison + Read more ...

  • If the customer has recently come out of prison or detention and cannot meet ATO identity requirements:
  • Write 'Custody release' in the Office Use Only section
  • Document any acceptable identity documents (as per standard), and
  • Document or photocopy, certify and attach all other non-standard identity documents

Go to Step 4

4

Send form to the ATO + Read more ...

  • Send the original application and identity documents to the ATO TFN processing address. For contact details, see Australian Taxation Office (ATO) - tax file numbers
  • Record the 'CAT' TFN exemption code (TFN request from ATO) on the TFN screen on the customer's record
  • Record details on a DOC including TFN application received and forwarded to the ATO
  • The ATO will provide the TFN within 28 days and will automatically update
  • Go to Table 3

5

Customer uses the ATO online TFN application + Read more ...

Customers can apply online for a TFN. They will need to:

  • select the reason for applying:
    • To give to Centrelink, and
    • I have authorised Centrelink to receive this TFN from the ATO
  • enter their CRN
  • make an electronic declaration

Application Summary

After completing the online form, the customer must:

  • print the Application Summary
  • go to an Australia Post outlet, and show the:
    • Application Summary form, and
    • original identity documents

Australia Post will:

  • verify the customers identity documents
  • issue a receipt with a statement of authorisation attached to the Application Summary

The customer needs to provide a copy of the Application Summary and receipt to the agency. They can:

  • go to a service centre, go to Step 6, or
  • upload via the Express Plus app or Centrelink Services Online. See Table 3 for coding

6

Customer attends service centre with an Application Summary and receipt + Read more ...

  • Check the barcode on the Application Summary form. It needs to match the reference number on the Australia Post receipt. For example:
    • The Application Summary barcode - 4709 Z E 00000012001 14
    • The Australia Post receipt reference no - 00000012001
    • Note: the bolded numbers are the numbers that must match. The Resources page contains an example of an Australia Post receipt
  • Record details on a DOC, add:
    • Application Summary barcode
    • Australia Post receipt reference numbers. Note: only record the 'Reference Number' from the receipt. The 'unique reference number' is not traceable by the ATO
  • Record the 'CAT' TFN exemption code (TFN request from ATO) on the TFN screen on the customer's record (excluding family assistance customers). See Table 3 for coding
  • The Application Summary and receipt do not need to be photocopied and stored on file
  • The ATO ( when authorised by the customer) will provide the customer's TFN within 28 days

Note: the ATO will not receive an online TFN application until at least 2 business days after the interview at Australia Post.

7

Customer not in receipt of a Centrelink payment + Read more ...

On date of receipt at service centre:

  • Do not scan or forward to a processing team for completion
  • Check the applicant has:
    • signed and dated the form if they are over 16
    • completed the form in blue or black ink and the writing is within the boxes
    • not used whiteout or corrective tape
    • signed all alterations
    • answered all of the questions
  • Check and confirm:
    • date of birth
    • spouse details if relevant, and
    • postal and home address. Note: update the home address if the postal address is a post office box
  • Check all Identity documents provided. See the Resources page for ATO requirements. Note: ATO identity document codes are different to the agency's
  • Write 'Customer not in receipt of a Centrelink payment' on the top of Page 1
  • Copy all identity documents and note on the copies 'Originals sighted by Services Australia'
  • Do not complete the 'Office Use Only' section of the form
  • Do not record the identity details
  • Attach the identity documents copies to the form
  • Stamp the form

Send form to ATO

  • Send the application and identity documents to the ATO TFN processing address. For contact details, see Australian Taxation Office (ATO) - tax file numbers

8

ATO returns NAT 1432 or NAT 1589 due to insufficient identity + Read more ...

  • Record a TFN exemption reason of 'GRA' on the customers TFN screen. Note: change the exemption code from 'CAT' to 'GRA' if claim already granted. See Table 3 for coding. Once coded return to this step to continue
  • Contact the customer by phone and tell them:
    • the ATOs proof of identity documents required
    • to show the correct identity documents at a service centre within 14 days
  • If requested, send a new:
    • Tax file number - application or enquiry for individuals (NAT 1432), or
    • Tax file number - application or enquiry for Aboriginal and Torres Strait Islander people (NAT 1589)
  • Do not send the application form or covering letter to the customer to update
  • If unable to contact via phone:
    • issue the customer a Request for Information (RFI) letter using the Request for Information guided procedure. Request TFN details within 14 days
    • The guided procedure will create a DOC and a review
  • The customer's claim/payment will reject/cancel if they do not respond within 14 days. New claim reassessment will apply from the date of rejection, if the TFN is supplied within 13 weeks of the rejection notice being sent. See Cancellation of payments

Note: contact Customer Details:

  • for assistance with TFN applications returned from the ATO, or
  • to refer feedback

Do not contact ATO directly.

The agency is responsible for the completion of a TFN application once lodged.

To record a TFN exemption, see Table 3.

Record the TFN or exemption details in Customer First - Current customers and new claims + Read more ...

All customers must provide a TFN to Services Australia unless they have a valid exemption.

Note: exemptions are not applicable to family assistance payments.

Customer TFN

From the TFN screen, code:

  • TFN field: Enter TFN. The Auth field will default to 'Y' for the service reason being assessed, or

  • Receipt date field

Partner TFN

If the customer has a partner, repeat the above steps for their TFN details

Customer is a dependent

  • If the customer is a dependent, their parents' TFN is recorded during the PIT linking process. See Linking parent records with dependent student, Australian Apprentice and job seeker recipients or claimants - Parental Income Test (PIT)

2

Record the TFN details in Process Direct - Exemption reason + Read more ...

Customer's TFN - Exemption reason

  • Go to the TFN table
  • Record the customer's TFN in the Tax File Number field and the Authorisation field as 'Y', or
  • the exemption code in the Exemption Reason field, and
  • select Simulate

Partner TFN - Exemption reason

Code the partner's TFN:

  • Select the partner from the Relationship menu icon
  • Navigate to the TFN table
  • Record the partner's TFN in the Tax File Number field and the Authorisation field as 'Y', or
  • the exemption code in the Exemption Reason field, and
  • Select Simulate
  • Navigate back to the customer record by selecting the customer in the Relationship menu

Customer is a dependent

If the customer is Youth Allowance (YA) dependent, record the parent's TFN:

  • Select the relevant parent/guardian from the Relationship menu icon
  • Navigate to the TFN table
  • Record the parent's TFN in the Tax File Number field and the:
    • Authorisation field as 'Y', or
    • the exemption code in the Exemption Reason field, and
  • Select Simulate
  • Navigate back to the customer record by selecting the customer in the Relationship menu

For linking dependent customers to parent records, see Linking parent records with dependent student, Australian Apprentice and jobseeker recipients or claimants - Parental Income Test (PIT).

If the customer's payment is cancelled or suspended for a reason relating to a TFN, see Restoration of payments (CLK).

Procedure ends here.
