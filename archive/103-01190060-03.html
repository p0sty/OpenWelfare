This page contains the procedure to be used by Service Officers who are conducting a Disbursement interview for customers exiting Voluntary Income Management. Portions of this process must be completed by an Income Management Contact Officer (IMCO).

On this page:

Disbursement interview for Voluntary Income Management

Disbursement interview for Voluntary Income Management

Table 1: This table describes the process and actions for a Disbursement interview for customers who are ending Voluntary Income Management.

Step

Action

1

Disbursement interview + Read more ...

A Disbursement interview is only conducted at the customer's request, and can be held face-to-face or via telephone.

If the customer requires an interpreter, see Booking an interpreter for an appointment.

Disbursement interviews should be conducted by Income Management trained Service Officers.

Service Officers trained in Income Management, go to Step 2.

Service Officers not trained in Income Management:

Service Centre Service Officers:

  • Use this process to conduct a Disbursement interview
  • Seek assistance from a specialised Service Officer (if available) or Service Support Officer, or
  • Contact the Access Support Centre (select Income Management option) for assistance to with conducting the interview
  • See the Resources page for a link to the Access Support Centre. Go to Step 2

Cold transfer the customer to:

  • the Income Management line
  • the Indigenous Services queue (if appropriate), or
  • Multilingual Call (if appropriate)

See the Resources page for a link to the National Transfer numbers. Procedure ends here.

2

Reason for exiting Voluntary Income Management + Read more ...

Determine the reason for the Voluntary Income Management ending:

  • If the customer requests to exit Voluntary Income Management, go to Step 4
  • If the agency terminates Voluntary Income Management, go to Step 3
  • If the customer has moved permanently into a Cashless Debit Card area, see Income Management customer in a Cashless Debit Card (CDC) areas. Procedure ends here

3

Agency terminates Voluntary Income Management + Read more ...

When the customer no longer meets the legislative requirements for Voluntary Income Management, the agency will terminate their Agreement.

Has Income Management been automatically ended?

  • Yes, customer has attended interview, go to Step 6
  • Yes, customer has not attended interview, the Auto Disbursement rules will apply. Procedure ends here
  • No, Income Management is to be manually ended, go to Step 7

4

Customer requests to exit from Voluntary Income Management + Read more ...

A customer may request to exit Voluntary Income Management at any time after they have completed the mandatory 13 week Income Management period.

Advise the customer:

  • when they end their Agreement, they will not be able to enter into a new Agreement for 21 days
  • they can only request to terminate their Voluntary Income Management Agreement a maximum of 4 times in any 12 month period

Has the customer completed the mandatory 13 week period on Voluntary Income Management?

  • Yes, and the customer:
    • wishes to proceed with ending their Voluntary Income Management Agreement, go to Step 5
    • does not wish to proceed with an exit from Voluntary Income Management, record details of the contact in a DOC. Procedure ends here
  • No, tell the customer they must complete the mandatory 13 week period of Voluntary Income Management before they can end their Agreement. Remind the customer they can apply for a formal review of an Income Management decisions. Record details of the contact on a DOC. Procedure ends here

5

Customer wishes to end their Voluntary Income Management Agreement + Read more ...

Eligible customers can end Voluntary Income Management online via their Centrelink Online Account.

To be eligible for this online service customers must have an active Voluntary Income Management Agreement and have completed the mandatory 13 week period.

The following are excluded from this online service:

  • customers referred to Voluntary Income Management by a state authority
  • customers who would be eligible for Income Management under the Vulnerable Youth or Crisis categories when their Agreement ends
  • customers who have a Payment Nominee

Does the customer want to end their Voluntary Income Management Agreement online?

  • Yes, confirm their eligibility to use the service, then direct the customer to their Centrelink Online Account to end their Agreement. Procedure ends here
  • No, continue with the process below to end the customer's Voluntary Income Management agreement

The customer's reason for requesting to exit from their Voluntary Income Management must be recorded in the interview DOC.

  • Reason of 'customer request' is not sufficient
  • Full details of the customer's reason for exit must be documented, for example, wants access to more discretionary cash

6

Check the customer's Income Management account balance + Read more ...

This information is found on the Income Management Summary screen under the Income Management option.

Does the customer have funds in their Income Management account?

  • Yes, go to Step 7
  • No, and the customer:
    • is present, go to Step 8
    • is not present, go to Step 1 in Table 2

7

Confirm GPY Payment Destination details + Read more ...

Before starting the Voluntary IM Assessment workflow or Disbursements workflow:

  • Go to the Payment Destination Summary (PAS) screen
  • Confirm the payment destination recorded for Service Reason GPY matches the customer's most recent payment type

If there are no details recorded, or the account number is incorrect, select GPY to go to the Payment Destination Details (PAD) screen to update account details. For more information see Changing payment destination

Is the customer present?

  • Yes, go to Step 8
  • No, go to Step 1 in Table 2

8

Meeting ongoing priority needs + Read more ...

Customers are to be informed of the impact of Income Management ending. This includes Income Management allocations ending and options available to continue meeting their ongoing expenses. The following should be discussed with the customer.

  • Advise the customer they will now be responsible for organising the payment of expenses previously paid from their Income Management funds
  • Encourage customers to use Centrepay to continue paying for ongoing expenses where the Third Party Organisation is registered for Centrepay, or arrange for direct debits from their bank account
  • Customers who were having rent deductions made via Income Management to their State Housing Authority (SHA) should continue to have their rent paid via the Rent Deduction Scheme (RDS). Send a Fast Note to Income Management Payment and Contact Team (IMPACT) asking that the RDS deduction be started or increased to meet the full rent amount. Select Auto Text, use Income Management > Update > State Housing Authority deductions
  • Advise customers they can visit the 'Managing your money' page at servicesaustralia.gov.au/managingmoney, for useful information and resources to help manage finances
  • Check to see if the customer has an active BasicsCard and the current balance. The customer can continue to use the BasicsCard until the balance is depleted. However, on day 61 after Income Management has ended, the card will be automatically closed. Any remaining balance will be transferred into the customer's Income Management account. Alternatively, customers can close their BasicsCard and have the balance recalled immediately into their Income Management account to be included in the disbursement calculation
    • If the customer chooses to close their BasicsCard, this must be actioned before the disbursement process. For assistance, see BasicsCard close an account
  • Advise the customer they can transfer available funds to their BasicsCard or request one off payments to TPOs for non-excluded items during the disbursement period
  • Advise the customer of their option to appoint a payment nominee

Go to Step 1 in Table 2

Using Voluntary IM Assessment and Disbursement workflows

Table 2: This table describes how to use the workflows required to finalise the Disbursements interview for Voluntary Income Management. Portions of this process are to be completed by an Income Management Contact Officer (IMCO) only.

Step

Action

1

Ending Voluntary Income Management + Read more ...

The authority to end Voluntary Income Management depends on the reason for ending the Agreement, and whether the customer has completed the mandatory 13 week period on Income Management.

Mandatory 13 weeks of Voluntary Income Management completed

  • Service Officers can complete the Voluntary IM Assessment workflow to terminate Voluntary Income Management, selecting the appropriate reason. Go to Step 2

Customer has not completed the mandatory 13 weeks of Voluntary Income Management

  • Terminating or revoking a Voluntary Income Management Agreement before the customer has completed the mandatory 13 week period is restricted to Income Management Contact Officers (IMCOs) and Level 2 Income Management Helpdesk staff with INMV13 Security Resource
  • If an Agreement is to be terminated before the mandatory period, Service Officers must send a Fast Note to the area IMCO requesting termination of the Voluntary Income Management Agreement, including the reason for termination. Select Auto Text, use Income Management > Update > Voluntary IM termination request. Where there is no area IMCO, send details via webform to the Income Management Helpdesk. See the Resources page for a link to the Income Management helpdesk webform
  • Note: IMCOs are only authorised to terminate a Voluntary Income Management Agreement where it relates to an excluded payment nominee arrangement. For all other reasons, IMCOs are to refer the request for termination via webform to the Income Management Helpdesk for approval and action
  • Go to Step 2

2

Completing the Voluntary IM Assessment + Read more ...

Launch the Voluntary IM Assessment workflow.

Note: If an access error displays, no updates will be permitted. For assistance, contact your area Income Management Contact Officer (IMCO).

Update as follows:

Voluntary Agreement Details page:

  • Status: Select 'Terminated' from the drop down list
    • Note: 'Revoked' is only to be selected where the Voluntary Income Management Agreement was incorrectly commenced (for example, incorrect CRN, customer was not legislatively eligible at time of agreement)
  • Customer referred by: This will display as 'Not referred' or with the state or non-state authority details where the customer was originally referred for Voluntary Income Management
  • Agreement Termination Reasons: Select the appropriate reason from the following list (more than one reason may be selected if appropriate):
    • Customer has payment nominee who is not subject to IM
    • Customer not in receipt of Category H payment
    • Customer has DVA agent or trustee who is not subject to IM
    • Social worker intervention
    • No longer feasible to income manage
    • Customer request
    • Customer is DVA agent/trustee-no principal consent
    • Administrative error
  • Interpreter requirements: Customer's spoken language will display, complete if an interpreter is required
  • Income Management Agreement Termination: The text must be read to the customer, then tick the check box to confirm the customer understands and wishes to end their Agreement. Note: if the customer does not understand the impacts of terminating their Agreement, Voluntary Income Management should not be ended at this time
  • Select Continue

IM Decision page:

  • Check the details showing are correct and select Continue

3

Residual Income Management balance + Read more ...

Does the customer have any residual funds in their Income Management account?

  • Yes, go to Step 4
  • No, go to Step 6

4

Disbursements workflow + Read more ...

The Disbursements workflow will only present where the customer has funds remaining in their Income Management account.

Note: If conducting a Disbursement interview after Income Management has ended, launch the Disbursements workflow by selecting the workflow from Income Management option menu in Customer First.

Update as follows:

  • Is it likely Income Management will resume within 60 days of IM ending? Select Yes or No as appropriate
    • If Yes is selected, disbursement of residual funds will not be paid until 60 days after Income Management ends
    • If No is selected, residual funds will be paid immediately
  • Basics Card: If the customer has an active BasicsCard, messaging will prompt Service Officer to have appropriate discussions with the customer
  • Expenses: Messaging will prompt Service Officers to have appropriate discussions with the customer
  • How would the customer like their money disbursed? This will default to 'lump sum'. However, the customer can choose to have funds disbursed by instalments if the balance is $210 or more
  • Select Continue

5

Continue workflow: Expense Management Summary screen + Read more ...

Information will appear at the top of the Expense Management Summary screen. For example:

  • Customer is no longer on Income Management. The regular expenses will automatically cease on DD MMM YYYY, unless ceased earlier
  • Disbursement of IM account available balance back to customer will commence on DD MMM YYYY

Further discussions regarding ongoing priority needs must be held with the customer:

  • Advise the customer what regular expenses have ceased
  • If it has been less than 15 days since Income Management ended, ask the customer if they want the regular expenses to be ceased immediately. If so, cease regular expenses, see Coding Income Management expenses, otherwise, expenses will automatically cease 14 days after Income Management ends
  • Transfer funds to the BasicsCard if requested
  • Issue one-off payments to Third Party Organisations if requested. For more information about paying one off expenses, see Coding Income Management expenses

Select Continue.

  • Does the customer wish to pay any one-off expenses from the residual funds? : option, select Yes or No
    • If 'Yes' is selected the Manage Expenses workflow is launched and the one-off payment or Expense Request can be made
  • Once any one-off payments have been coded, select the Calculate Repayments button. This will display the amount and date(s) funds will be disbursed. Advise customer of this information

For more information on paying one-off payments, see Changes to Income Management priority needs.

6

Finalise workflow: Income Management Summary screen + Read more ...

The Income Management Summary screen will display a message advising how residual funds will be disbursed. Finalise any Work Items which may be displayed in the Work Items section.

Action any outstanding Work Items and finalise the activity on the Income Management Summary page.

If Income Management is ending at the customer's request, a DOC is automatically generated by the workflow. If required, annotate the DOC with any other relevant information.

If the Agreement is being terminated for a reason other than at the customer's request, a DOC is not generated by the workflow. The information given to the customer should be recorded in the Activity Notes on the Income Management Summary screen, or in a DOC:

  • Date Income Management ended
  • Reason for termination
  • If an interpreter was required but not used, how the interview was conducted (for example, relative, friend, Agent)
  • How funds will be disbursed.(lump sum or instalments)
  • Offer of Centrepay/Rent Deduction Scheme
  • Any Information required under the guidelines for adding a DOC to a customer's record

Finalise the activity on the Income Management Summary page.

Note: a letter is not issued confirming Voluntary Income Management has ended when the reason for ending is 'Customer request'.

7

Centrepay deductions + Read more ...

If the customer decides to use Centrepay for their ongoing priority needs, see:

  • Starting, changing and cancelling a Centrepay deduction at the request of a customer
  • Deductions options online

8

Payment Nominee + Read more ...

If the customer wishes to appoint a payment nominee, issue the customer with Authorising a person or organisation to enquire or act on your behalf (SS313). For more information on nominees, see Appointing nominees.
