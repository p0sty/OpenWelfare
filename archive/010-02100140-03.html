Identifying if compassionate travel is applicable

Table 1

Step

Action

1

Variations to school vacation travel + Read more ...

Does the student have school vacation travel booked within 2 weeks of the requested compassionate travel date?

  • Yes, the request should be treated as a variation to the original school vacation travel. See Fares Allowance (FAA) authorised in advance of travel for ABSTUDY
  • No, go to Step 2

2

Eligibility + Read more ...

To be eligible for FAA for compassionate travel the student must:

  • be eligible for Fares Allowance and
  • not travelling for an Away from Base activity and
    • meet one of the circumstances justifying travel for compassionate reasons, or
    • be an excluded (expelled) ABSTUDY student

Does the student meet the eligibility criteria for FAA for compassionate travel?

  • Yes, go to Step 3
  • No, see Step 9 in Table 2

3

Excluded or expelled students + Read more ...

Is the student applying for compassionate travel due to being an expelled or excluded secondary school student?

  • Yes, go to Step 6
  • No, go to Step 4

4

Circumstances justifying compassionate travel + Read more ...

Does the reason given by the student meet one of the circumstances justifying compassionate travel?

  • Yes, go to Step 5
  • No, see Step 9 in Table 2

5

Compassionate travel is limited to 2 return trips per student per year + Read more ...

Trips for excluded students are not included in the limit of return trips for compassionate travel.

Have the 2 trips been claimed in the current calendar year?

  • Yes,
    • if response received from Level 2 Policy Helpdesk provides approval to pay ABSTUDY Additional Assistance, go to Step1 in Table 2
    • if response received from Level 2 Policy Helpdesk declines the request to pay ABSTUDY Additional Assistance, go to Step 9 in Table 2
    • all other circumstances go to Step 7
  • No, see Step 1 in Table 2

6

Education institutions applying for travel for an excluded student + Read more ...

Education institutions applying for travel for an excluded student must complete the ABSTUDY expulsion checklist. See the Resources page for the checklist.

Compassionate travel for an excluded ABSTUDY student to return to their permanent home can be granted if the school confirms all the following:

  • The student is considered to be at harm to themselves or others, as defined in the respective state/territory requirements
  • The school has followed necessary steps outlined in the state/territory legislative requirements, including contacting parent(s)/guardian(s) and the respective authorities, like the Police, Department of Child Safety/Children services
  • Alternative local solutions, such as relocating the young person with family members, have been exhausted
  • There is no other source of funding available
  • The school confirms all these steps have been taken

Note: excluded students required to travel home under supervision, may have a supervisor travel with them. This can be a parent/guardian or a representative from the educational institution. People taking this type of travel will be approved as supervisor travellers under non-student travellers.

Does the completed ABSTUDY expulsion checklist confirm all the above criteria have been met?

  • Yes, see Step 8 in Table 2
  • No, see Step 9 in Table 2

7

Additional Assistance in lieu of Fares Allowance + Read more ...

The student or approved traveller may be eligible for ABSTUDY Additional Assistance in lieu of ABSTUDY Fares Allowance at the discretion of the Department for Social Services (DSS).

Escalate the enquiry to the Level 2 Policy Helpdesk, via Local Peer Support (LPS) and include the following details:

  • Request for approval of ABSTUDY Additional Assistance - Fares Allowance for compassionate travel to attend (event)
  • Two compassionate travel return trips been claimed for 2023: Yes/No
  • Summary of case
    • Travel Dates:
    • From destination:
    • To destination:
    • Traveller:
    • ABSTUDY Award of student:
    • Is the traveller seeking additional costs for ACM/MEA: Yes/No
    • Current supporting document located:
    • Reason for request:

Evidence needed to support a compassionate travel claim

Table 2

Step

Action

1

Reason for compassionate travel + Read more ...

If the compassionate travel is for:

  • the student to attend a funeral, go to Step 2
  • a non-student traveller to visit an ill or injured student, go to Step 3
  • the student to return home for any of the following reasons, go to Step 4:
    • critical illness or injury of an immediate family member
    • critical illness or injury to the student
    • to participate in a community activity under Aboriginal or Torres Strait Islander lore or kinship obligations

2

Compassionate travel to attend a funeral + Read more ...

In some Indigenous communities, mentioning the name of a deceased person may cause distress and/or offence.

Attempt to verify the funeral date using the Indigenous Funeral Notices homepage. The Resources page has a link. If unavailable, try to contact the nearest service centre and liaise with the local community or Indigenous Service Officer (ISO) to confirm the funeral details.

Note: Students travelling for this reason are still subject to mandatory supervision requirements. A family member may travel as a supervisor if they are escorting the student to the travel location.

Can the funeral be confirmed?

  • Yes, go to Step 6
  • No, go to Step 4

3

Travel for non-student traveller to visit ill or injured student + Read more ...

FAA travel may be approved for a family or community member when the student is ill or injured. This can be to visit the student or to accompany them to specialist appointments when it is compulsory for the parent/guardian to attend the appointment.

To approve this travel, the student must:

  • be ill or injured and it has been deemed appropriate for the family or community member to visit at the term address
  • provide a medical evidence advising:
    • the nature of the illness or injury
    • if the student is able to travel
    • the period of the illness or injury

Where the student is suffering from a terminal illness, both parents/guardians may be approved for FAA.

Has the above been confirmed?

  • Yes, go to Step 8
  • No, go to Step 4

4

Inform student they must provide evidence + Read more ...

Inform the student they must provide documentary evidence to support their compassionate travel claim.

Evidence can include, but is not limited to, a statement from:

  • the education or board provider
  • a doctor
  • the hospital
  • a community authority, or
  • a community person responsible for cultural matters (if the travel request is for the student being required to return home for a cultural activity)

These documents are required to grant FAA for compassionate travel. If the evidence:

  • cannot be provided prior to travel, go to Step 5
  • has been provided or verified and
    • travel is between their usual study location and permanent home address, go to Step 8
    • the student is requesting travel from or to an alternate destination, go to Step 7

5

In some circumstances evidence will not be available until after the student's travel + Read more ...

Documentary evidence can be provided after the travel has occurred if:

  • the Service Officer thinks the basis for the compassionate travel request is genuine and
  • providing evidence would delay the student returning home

Note: ensure cultural sensitivity is taken into account with the decision. Refer to an Indigenous Specialist Officer (ISO) for assistance.

Can documentary evidence be provided after the journey?

  • Yes, go to Step 6
  • No, go to Step 9

6

FAA approved + Read more ...

If FAA is to be approved, inform the student that if evidence is not provided (or is not sufficient), an overpayment will be raised to recover the FAA.

If required:

  • issue a Q999 requesting evidence to support their claim. See the Resources page for template. Allow extra time for mail delivery
  • create a DOC with advised details, use Students, ABSTUDY & Apprentices > Fares Allowance > Evidence for Compassionate Travel > Confirm
  • place the open Fast Note on hold for 21 days

Is the student travelling between their usual study location and permanent home address?

  • Yes, go to Step 8
  • No, go to Step 7

7

Check for approved alternate destination + Read more ...

Advise the student compassionate travel is usually approved only between the permanent home and the study location.

Does the student have exceptional circumstances where travel can be approved to a location other than the permanent home address or usual study address? See Determining the appropriate travel destination for ABSTUDY Fares Allowance (FAA).

  • Yes, go to Step 8
  • No, go to Step 9

8

FAA payable + Read more ...

FAA for ABSTUDY can be approved for:

  • a student or
  • a non-student traveller that is travelling to visit the student due to illness or injury of the student

Determine if the FAA is to be:

  • approved for someone other than the student, see Non-student travellers for ABSTUDY. Procedure ends here
  • authorised and booked before travel, or
  • costs reimbursed after travel

Excluded Students

Secondary school boarding students, who discontinue study during a school term, cease to be eligible for the Away from Home rate from the date they discontinue study. See Cessation of the ABSTUDY away from home rate.

Travel Service Officers are required to cancel any future travel that is booked or requested for an excluded secondary boarding student that is not required.

Send an ABSTUDY action request Fast Note to the ABSTUDY Smart Centre Processing Team to reassess the student's ABSTUDY rate and include the date the student discontinued. Select Auto text > Students, ABSTUDY & Apprentices > Update > ABSTUDY Action request > Confirm.

Procedure ends here.

9

FAA is not payable + Read more ...

Advise the claimant the student is not eligible for FAA for compassionate travel.

If the travel request was received by:

  • ABSTUDY Smart Centre telephony, record details of the discussion on a DOC. Explain their review and appeal rights. See Initial contact about a decision and the review of decision process
  • the ABSTUDY Travel Team, finalise the travel request in the Travel Management Application (TMA) with a Booking status of 'Rejected' and record details of the decision in the Notes tab. See Coding pre-booked Fares Allowance (FAA) entitlements for ABSTUDY and ABSTUDY Pensioner Education Supplement (PES).

Check if the student's circumstances entitle them to FAA under other criteria.

See ABSTUDY Fares Allowance (FAA).
