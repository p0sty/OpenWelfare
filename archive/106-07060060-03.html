Rejected claim + Read more ...

Claims may be reassessed when all outstanding requested documents have been provided within 13 weeks of the rejection notice. 6

Check if all requested documents have been provided + Read more ...

Check the customer's record to confirm that all outstanding requested documentation has been provided for the rejected claim.

Depending on the claim type:

  • check the Progress of Claim DOC or Rejection DOC to view notes related to a claim on
    • DL/Notes in Process Direct, or
    • Document List (DL) screen in Customer First
  • check for all requested documentation
    • for CCM aligned claims, see Circumstance Change Monitor (CCM)
    • for non-CCM claims or paper claims, check Document Tools in Customer First for a scanned paper claim and/or documentation
  • compare the documents provided by the customer to ensure all appropriate documents have been provided for claim finalisation. See Viewing Centrelink customers' digital images

A Secretary initiated review of decision can be applied to reassess the first claim when a customer:

  • lodges a second claim within 13 weeks of the date the rejection notice was given for the first claim, and
  • includes previously requested and outstanding documents for the rejected first claim

Scan any documentation provided by the customer onto their record. Attach electronic documents to the customer's record using Document Tools in Customer First.

Have all outstanding requested documents been provided?

  • Yes, go to Step 7
  • No, go to Step 10

7

All outstanding requested documents have been provided + Read more ...

If a claim was rejected FRC, FSD, POI or IME (insufficient/no medical evidence for DSP), it can be reopened and the customer could be paid from the original start date if all of the following are met:

  • the claim was rejected because the customer did not supply the documentation requested
  • the customer was entitled to the payment or concession at the time they made the claim or early claim provisions apply, and
  • the customer provided all the requested documentation within 13 weeks from the date the notice was given to reject the claim

In these situations, the customer does not need to lodge a new claim.

The start day for the payment will be affected if the customer is subject to:

  • RapidConnect and they did not attend their initial RapidConnect appointment
  • an exclusion period

Has the customer provided all of the requested documentation within 13 weeks of the date the notice was given to reject the claim?

  • Yes,
    • Service Officers fully skilled in processing the rejected claim type and currently scheduled for processing, go to Step 8
    • All other Service Officers, see Step 1 in Table 2
  • No, go to Step 9

8

Reassess rejected claim + Read more ...

Re-index the original rejected claim and reassess the customer's entitlement based on the documentation provided.

The claim can be granted from the original start date if all of the following are met:

  • the claim was rejected FRC/FSD/POI or IME (insufficient/no medical evidence for DSP) because the customer did not supply the requested documentation
  • the customer was entitled to the payment or concession at the time they made the claim or early claim provisions apply, and
  • the customer provided all the requested documentation for the claim within 13 weeks of the date the notice was given to reject the claim

The following also applies:

  • Change of circumstances advised by the customer since they submitted their original claim. This information can be given verbally and may need to be clarified when reassessing the claim. Make one genuine attempt to contact if required
  • The start day for the payment may not be the original contact date if the customer is subject to RapidConnect and they did not attend their initial RapidConnect appointment, or an exclusion period applies
  • If a customer lodges documents as part of a second claim and these documents were lodged within 13 weeks of the notice to reject the first claim, these documents can be used to reassess the first claim as part of a Secretary initiated review of decision
  • If more information is required to finalise the customer's claim, make one genuine attempt to contact. If unsuccessful, issue a separate request for information and place the claim on 'hold'

Follow relevant claim processing procedures for the payment or concession type:

  • Age Pension and Pension Bonus
  • Assistance for Isolated Children (AIC) Scheme
  • Carer Payment (CP) and/or Carer Allowance (CA)
  • Commonwealth Seniors Health Card (CSHC)
  • Disability Support Pension (DSP)
  • Fares Allowance (FAA) for YA, Austudy and PES
  • JobSeeker Payment (JSP)
  • Low Income Health Care Card (LIC)
  • Parenting Payment (PP)
  • Home Equity Access Scheme
  • Tertiary Access Payment (TAP)
  • Youth Allowance (YA) job seeker
  • Youth Allowance (YA) student or apprentice

Procedure ends here.

9

All outstanding requested documents provided after 13 weeks + Read more ...

If the customer provides all outstanding requested documentation after 13 weeks of the rejection notice being given, generally a new claim must be lodged.

However, a new claim does not need to be lodged and the original claim can be reopened if:

  • the customer:
    • applied for a formal review within 13 weeks of the rejection notice being given, and
    • provides all the previously requested documentation, even if provided more than 13 weeks after the rejection notice is given, and
    • is entitled to the payment or concession at the time they made their claim or early claim provisions apply
  • the customer applied for Assistance for Isolated Children scheme (AIC) or Tertiary Access Payment (TAP). See Step 1 in Table 2

If the customer is present

  • Tell the customer:
    • the claim remains rejected
    • their review and appeal rights, and if they need to lodge a new claim (if applicable)
    • they can apply for a formal review of the decision at any time. However, if they apply more than 13 weeks after the rejection notice was given, payment or concession may be affected by date of effect rules, See Customer initiated review of decision
  • Annotate the Rejection Decision DOC/Note with the details
  • Complete all related digital image Work Items
  • Procedure ends here

If the customer is not present

Go to Step 11.

10

Outstanding requested documents not provided + Read more ...

Before the rejected claim can be assessed, the customer must provide all outstanding requested documents within 13 weeks of the rejection notice.

Do not send a request to reassess a claim if some but not all of the requested documentation has been provided.

If the customer is present

Tell the customer:

  • what outstanding information is still required
  • their claim will not be reopened unless all requested documents are provided within 13 weeks of the rejection notice
  • they can apply for a formal review of the decision at any time. This may be before or after providing more information or evidence. If they apply for a formal review more than 13 weeks after the rejection notice was given, payment or concession may be affected by date of effect rules. See Customer initiated review of decision

Procedure ends here.

If the customer is not present

Go to Step 11.

11

Customer is not present + Read more ...

If the customer has not provided all documentation, complete these actions when allocated the following work items.

Reassess Rejected Claim Fast Note

  • Make a genuine attempt to contact the customer to advise the claim cannot be reopened. See Calling a customer or returning a customer's call
  • If contact by phone is unsuccessful, issue a manual Q999 letter to the customer (and correspondence nominee if applicable) See the Resources page for suggested text
  • Annotate the Reassess Rejected Claim DOC/Note or Rejection Decision DOC/Note with the reasons the claim cannot be reassessed, including:
    • what outstanding information is still required, and
    • details of the attempts made to contact the customer (if unsuccessful)
  • Complete/cancel related work items - Fast Note, Re-indexed claim
  • If the claim has been reindexed in error in Process Direct, change the claim status to Not Required or Complete depending on the payment type. See:
    • Process Direct navigation, common screens and functions, and
    • Viewing Centrelink customers' digital images
  • Consider issuing feedback via the Staff Feedback Tool if an inappropriate referral has been received

Scanned image

  • Do not reassess the customer's rejected claim as all outstanding documentation has not been provided and requirements have not been met. A new rejection decision should not be made as the original rejection decision and notice still applies
  • As the customer did not formally request their rejected claim be reassessed there is no need to contact the customer. The customer has already been advised of the requirements for their claim to be reassessed
  • Annotate the Rejection Decision DOC/Note with the details including what outstanding information is still required
  • Complete all related work items. See Viewing Centrelink customers' digital images

Procedure ends here.

Requesting rejected claims to be reopened and reassessed

Table 2

Step

Action

1

Customer has provided all requested documents, rejected claim can be reopened + Read more ...

A rejected claim can be reopened if the customer has provided all requested documents within 13 weeks. Exceptions: the 13 week provision does not apply to Assistance for Isolated Children scheme (AIC) or Tertiary Access Payment (TAP) claims. For these claims there is no time limit.

Re-indexing the rejected claim must only be completed by Service Officers:

  • fully skilled in processing the relevant rejected claim type, and
  • not currently scheduled for processing

All other Service Officers must complete the appropriate Fast Note.

Is the Service Officer fully skilled for processing the relevant rejected claim type?

  • Yes, go to Step 2
  • No, go to Step 3

2

Re-indexing the rejected claim + Read more ...

Process Direct

  • Re-index rejected claim in Process Direct
  • Create a Progress of Claim (POC) Note on the customer's record advising:
    • customer has provided all requested documentation/identity within 13 weeks of [insert claim type] claim being rejected and is available on the customer's record
    • rejected claim has been re-indexed as customer has requested claim be reassessed
    • original Claim ID: [insert claim ID]
    • re-indexed CoC interaction ID: [insert CoC ID]
  • If the claim is for payment under an international agreement and processed by International Services (CIS), see Claims for Australian payments under International Agreements
  • If customer meets financial hardship provisions, follow Request for urgent processing of a new claim when experiencing financial hardship
  • If customer is identified as vulnerable and/or has complex needs, follow Immediate new claim and non-new claim priority processing
  • If customer is not identified as being vulnerable or in hardship, and does not have complex needs, advise the customer of processing timeframes
  • Complete all related digital image Work Items
  • If applicable, unassign the re-indexed work item in Work Optimiser using the reason User Unavailable

Customer First

Note: claims which must be re-indexed in Process Direct must not be regenerated in Customer First. This results in a loss of claim data.

  • Re-index rejected claim
  • Record a closed DOC on the customer's record advising:
    • customer has provided all requested documentation/identity within 13 weeks of [insert claim type] claim being rejected and is available on the customer's record
    • rejected claim has been re-indexed as customer has requested claim be reassessed
  • If the claim is for payment under an international agreement and processed by International Services (CIS), see Claims for Australian payments under International Agreements
  • Advise the customer of processing timeframes. If customer meets financial hardship provisions, follow Request for urgent processing of a new claim when experiencing financial hardship
  • Complete all related digital image Work Items
  • If applicable, unassign the re-indexed work item in Work Optimiser using the reason User Unavailable

Procedure ends here.

Other claim types + Read more ...

Create an open work item using the Fast Note, with the following information:

  • Service Reason must match the claim type, e.g. CAR - Carer Payment
    For Home Equity Access Scheme claims, use the Service Reason of the qualifying payment, such as AGE - Age Pension or DSP - Disability Support Pension
    Do not leave it as CCD-Customer Contact Details or use a non-claim type
    The Service Reason cannot be changed once the Fast Note is completed. Fast Notes completed with CCD or other non-claim type service reasons will create a WLM_OWN work item in the Service Officer's inbox and will not be allocated for processing. To clear this WLM_OWN activity from their inbox, the Service Officer must delete the incorrect Fast Note and create a new Reassess Rejected Claim Fast Note using the correct service reason
  • Select Auto Text, use Generic > Claims > Reassess Rejected Claim
