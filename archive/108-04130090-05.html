Services Australia Valuation Request (SA470)

The SA470 is used for situations such as gifting, historical valuations (for example, undisclosed properties), properties owned by private trusts & private companies, and Residential Care Assessments.

Note: this is an internal agency form to be filled out by staff. It is not to be issued to customers to complete.

Services Australia Valuation Request form instructions

  • Open the link to the SA470 form
  • 'Save as' the document to a local drive
  • Complete the form on the screen - written or scanned SA470s must not be provided
  • ‘Save’ the form, ensuring the text within the form remains in an editable format for the valuation request to progress to the valuer
  • Provide details of any supporting documents scanned to the customers record in Qu.16, including date, name document is scanned under, details of the document (rates notice, title etc) and the page number of the scan where it can be found
  • The SA470 must be sent as an editable document, do not send scans or photocopies of this form

Once complete APS5 or above to email the SA470 to the Valuation Helpdesk.

Include ‘Valuation request’ and the customer reference number in the subject line of the email and the reason for the valuation request in the body of the email.

If request is urgent, please include ‘Urgent’ in the summary line and reason in the body of the email.

Information on taxi licences

State/territory

Description

Australian Capital Territory
  • The ACT taxi licencing process underwent major reforms effective from 1 August 2016
  • This meant some licences were transferable and some were not transferable
  • A transferable licence can only be transferred with the consent of the Road Transport Authority
  • The customer should be able to provide evidence of the purchase/trade price

Queensland

 Taxi licence transfer/sale prices for the last 12 months can be obtained at Queensland Government website

Tasmania
  • During 2018-19 all new licences were sold for the same non-negotiable price
  • There are currently no new owner operator taxi licences being issued in Tasmania between 2020 and 2024
  • A licence can only be obtained by purchasing from an existing licence holder with consent from the Transport Commission for the transfer of the licence
  • The customer should be able to provide evidence of the purchase price

Western Australia
  • The WA taxi industry underwent major reforms effective from 1 April 2019
  • There was a buy back scheme for all licences in Perth metropolitan area, Murray and Mandurah districts. There is no assessable asset value for these licences
  • Taxi licences in other areas of WA are only tradeable under a public tender process
  • The customer should be able to provide evidence of the purchase/trade price

Unreasonable estimated property values

Number

Example

1

Parmesh has a 2 bedroom unit in an outer suburb. On the new claim, the estimated property value has been provided as $45,000. It is likely that this was a typing error and perhaps meant to be $450,000.

This is considered unreasonable and a valuation should be requested before granting the claim.

2

Mackenzie has provided an estimated property value of $350,000. The rates notice also provided shows that the local council are assessing a land value of $325,000. Based on the rates notice amount, it is likely the whole property may be worth more than the estimate provided.

This is considered unreasonable and a valuation should be requested before granting the claim.

3

Brian lives on a 50 hectare property and provided an estimated property value of $1,200,000 and House and Curtilage (home and surrounding 2 hectares) amount of $1,150,000. It is unlikely the 48 hectares of land are valued at $50,000.

This is considered unreasonable and a valuation should be requested before granting the claim.

4

Wendy is a single non- homeowner claiming the Age Pension and advised in the online claim that they own a vacant block of land in a rural town. Wendy has provided an estimated value of $25,000 for the block. Wendy also has $50,000 in financial assets and a $10,000 car.

The current assets limit is $543,750, before the rate of Age Pension would start reducing.

Although the valuation provided seems unreasonable, it is unlikely a valuation would increase the block’s value enough to have an impact on the customer's payment under the assets test.

No valuation is required before granting the claim.
