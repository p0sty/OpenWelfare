Table 1: This table describes how to assess the carer and care receiver's income, assets and family circumstances when determining eligibility to CP when care is provided for a disabled adult and their dependent child.

Step

Action

1

Does the adult care receiver provide care for a dependent child? + Read more ...

  • Yes, go to Step 2
  • No, the carer (of the adult care receiver) cannot qualify for CP under this provision. To see if the adult care receiver can qualify under the 'full' qualification provisions, see Eligibility for Carer Payment (CP) when a care receiver is aged 16 years or over. At least 2 genuine attempts to contact must be made to contact the carer to advise of the unfavourable decision. Record details on a DOC in the carer's record. For more information, see Advising verbally of an unfavourable decision (CLK). Procedure ends here

2

Is the dependent child aged 6 or over? + Read more ...

  • Yes, go to Step 3
  • No, go to Step 5

3

Does the dependent child attract payment of Carer Allowance (CA) (child)? + Read more ...

  • Yes, go to Step 4
  • No, the carer (of the adult care receiver) cannot qualify for CP under this provision. Check Eligibility for Carer Payment (CP) when a care receiver is aged 16 years or over. At least 2 genuine attempts to contact must be made to contact the carer to advise of the unfavourable decision. Record details on a DOC in the carer's record. For more information, see Advising verbally of an unfavourable decision (CLK). Procedure ends here

4

Care receiver has a dependent child + Read more ...

To reach this step, the care receiver will have a dependent child who is either:

  • aged under 6, or
  • aged 6 to less than 16 and who is attracting a payment of CA (child)

Note: from this step onwards, the care receiver referred to is the adult care receiver, and the carer is the carer of the adult care receiver.

5

Is the care provided in the residence of the care receiver? + Read more ...

  • Yes, go to Step 7
  • No, go to Step 6

6

Care not provided in the residence of the care receiver + Read more ...

Is the care not being provided in the residence of the care receiver because the care receiver is absent from the home as they are hospitalised and the carer is participating in their treatment?

  • Yes, go to Step 7
  • No, the carer cannot qualify for CP. The CP claim should be manually rejected. At least 2 genuine attempts to contact must be made to contact the carer to advise of the unfavourable decision. Record details on a DOC in the carer's record. For more information, see Advising verbally of an unfavourable decision (CLK). Procedure ends here

7

Pensions income and assets test for the carer + Read more ...

Does the carer meet the pensions income and assets test? Note: if the carer is currently on another pension type and paid under the transitional rules the income test is not the same as the current pension income test.

  • Yes go to Step 8
  • No, the carer cannot qualify for CP. The CP claim should be manually rejected. At least 2 genuine attempts to contact must be made to contact the carer to advise of the unfavourable decision. Record details on a DOC in the carer's record. For more information, see Advising verbally of an unfavourable decision (CLK). Procedure ends here

8

Financial circumstances of the care receiver + Read more ...

Is the care receiver either:

  • in receipt of a Social Security pension or benefit, or a Service Pension or Income Support Supplement (ISS) or Veteran Payment, or
  • would be receiving such a payment if they had been an Australian resident for a long enough period?

Note: CA is not 1 of the payment types mentioned above.

  • Yes, see Step 1 in the Assessment of the medical report for CP claims table
  • No, go to Step 9

9

Does the care receiver meet the non-customer care receiver Income and Assets Test? + Read more ...

For more information, see Determining the appropriate tax year for the non-customer Care Receiver Income and Assets (CRIA) test.

  • Yes, see Step 1 in the Assessment of the medical report for CP claims table
  • No, the carer cannot qualify for CP. The CP claim should be manually rejected. At least 2 genuine attempts to contact must be made to contact the carer to advise of the unfavourable decision. Record details on a DOC in both the carer's and care receiver's records. For more information, see Advising verbally of an unfavourable decision (CLK). Procedure ends here

Assessment of the medical report for CP claims

Table 2: This table describes how to assess a Carer Payment and/or Carer Allowance - Medical Report (for a person 16 years or over) form (SA332a).

Step

Action

1

Medical report + Read more ...

Has the SA332(a) form been returned?

  • Yes, go to Step 2
  • No, processing of the new claim cannot continue until the Medical Report is returned. Inform the carer of this fact, and issue the SA332(a). When the SA332(a) is returned, go to Step 2

2

Do the Treating Health Professional's (THP) responses on the form indicate that care is required for at least 6 months? + Read more ...

  • Yes, calculate the ADAT score based solely on the health professional's responses. Go to Step 3
  • No, then the CP claim should be manually rejected on the Benefit Action (BA) screen, using the reason code 'NMQ' (not medically qualified). At least 2 genuine attempts to contact must be made to contact the carer to advise of the unfavourable decision. Record details on a DOC in the carer's record. For more information, see Advising verbally of an unfavourable decision (CLK). Procedure ends here

3

What is the THP''s score? + Read more ...

  • Less than 8, go to Step 4
  • 8 or greater, go to Step 5

4

ADAT score based on THP's score does not attract CP for carer + Read more ...

The CP claim should be manually rejected.

At least 2 genuine attempts to contact must be made to contact the carer to advise of the unfavourable decision. Record details on a DOC in the carer's record. For more information, see Advising verbally of an unfavourable decision (CLK).

Procedure ends here.

5

Calculate the total ADAT score + Read more ...

Calculate the total ADAT score, which is the total achieved when the scores from the THP and carer questionnaires are combined.

  • Less than 20, go to Step 6
  • 20 or greater, go to Step 7

6

Total ADAT score not sufficient to attract CP + Read more ...

The total ADAT score is not sufficient to attract CP for the carer. The CP claim should be manually rejected. At least 2 genuine attempts to contact must be made to contact the carer to advise of the unfavourable decision. Record details on a DOC in the carer's record. For more information, see Advising verbally of an unfavourable decision (CLK).

Procedure ends here.

7

Check THP's responses on SA332(a) + Read more ...

Has the THP responded No or Unsure on the SA332(a) to either of the following questions:

'Does the person require help on a daily basis because of their disability and/or medical condition(s) to carry out routine personal activities OR because they may be at risk to themselves or to others?'

'Is this care required for a significant period each day (at least the equivalent of a normal working day)?'

  • Yes, go to Step 8
  • No, go to Step 9

8

Making an assessment + Read more ...

  • The THP's responses should not be an automatic assessment that constant care is not being provided
  • The decision maker (DM) will need to make an assessment based on the total level of care provided to the care receiver, that is, a carer should reasonably be expected to provide at least the equivalent of a normal working day in personal care
  • When coding the CP claim the review officer's determination if the constant care qualifications are met should be recorded on the Care Receiver Care & Institution Details (CRCI) screen

9

Code the claim for CP + Read more ...

For more information, see Coding a paper claim for Carer Payment (CP) where the care receiver is 16 years of age or over.

Record details on a DOC in the carer's record.
