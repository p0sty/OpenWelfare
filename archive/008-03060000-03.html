For SCCRT or DSP Processing team

If the customer is in psychiatric confinement due to being charged with an offence:

  • to support decision making and recording procedures for customers claiming DSP while in psychiatric confinement, the SA379 form requests relevant information about the circumstances of the customer's confinement
  • the form must be completed by, or verified with, a Health or Allied Health Professional such as a Medical Practitioner, Registered Nurse or social worker. If not completed by a health professional, the contact details of the person responsible for the plan should be provided. Alternative written advice from the relevant correctional authority/facility covering sentence status and rehabilitation (as detailed in the form) may be used. It should be placed on the customer's file as a record of the decision taken and the reason for it
  • if the SA379 or equivalent information is not provided for a new claim, reject the claim as pay
  • ability cannot be assessed
  • payment must not be continued for a DSP recipient who is in psychiatric confinement, and does not provide an SA379 or equivalent information
  • for complex queries about the SA379 and continuing DSP payability, contact the Level 2 Helpdesk
  • to assist decision makers, see the References page for a link to the Payability During Periods in Gaol or Psychiatric Confinement policy
  • scan the SA379 form onto the customer's record. Go to Step 2

2

Imprisoned or confined due to criminal charges + Read more ...

Is the person in psychiatric confinement due to criminal charges?

  • Yes, go to Step 4
  • No, go to Step 3

3

Person confined not related to an offence + Read more ...

If the person is in psychiatric confinement not in connection with an offence (for example, because they are a danger to themselves and/or others) and they are not serving a term of imprisonment for any crime, DSP is payable, subject to the customer meeting all other eligibility criteria.

Record the details of the decision on a DOC.

Procedure ends here.

4

Determine if customer is serving a term of imprisonment for any crime + Read more ...

Is the customer serving a term of imprisonment for any crime, whether it is the basis for psychiatric confinement or not?

  • Yes, DSP is not payable. Go to Step 9
  • No, go to Step 5

5

Person in psychiatric confinement due to criminal charges + Read more ...

New claims to be processed by SCCRT (OA8) only

Reviews to be actioned by DSP Processing team (DIS)

Psychiatric confinement may occur due to the customer having:

  • undergone a period of custody pending trial or sentencing for an offence (mental fitness to stand trial being assessed)
  • been found guilty of an offence, but no conviction recorded
  • been found unfit for trial due to mental impairment (no conviction recorded), or
  • been found not guilty by reason of mental impairment (no conviction recorded)

Is the customer in psychiatric confinement due to 1 of the above reasons?

  • Yes, go to Step 6
  • No, go to Step 9

6

Determine course of rehabilitation + Read more ...

To determine if a person is undertaking a course of rehabilitation, obtain written advice from the correctional authority (certified by a health or allied health professional) to confirm this. Services Australia staff should accept advice from the correctional authority on this matter without further investigation. The Customer in Psychiatric Confinement (SA379) form should generally provide sufficient detail to make the decision.

Is the person undertaking a course of rehabilitation?

  • Yes, go to Step 8
  • No, go to Step 7

7

Person is not undertaking a course of rehabilitation + Read more ...

DSP is not payable (under the Social Security Act 1991, section 1158(b)).

See the References page for a link to the Payability During Periods in Gaol or Psychiatric Confinement policy.

Go to Step 10.

8

Person is undertaking a course of rehabilitation + Read more ...

DSP is payable (under the Social Security Act 1991, section 23(9)).

See the References page for a link to the Payability During Periods in Gaol or Psychiatric Confinement policy.

Rehabilitation plans and/or activities are not required to be reviewed at any set intervals. However, correctional authorities are required to notify Services Australia immediately (within 14 days) if there is a significant change to the customer's circumstances affecting payability.

Where an expected end date for the course of rehabilitation has been provided, set up a manual review.

A manual review must be set for 12 months (or whenever a change of circumstances is expected) for any person who is being paid DSP while in psychiatric confinement.

See Step 1 in Table 2.

9

DSP is not payable because a person is in gaol or psychiatric confinement + Read more ...

DSP is not payable (under the Social Security Act 1991, sections 1158(a), and 23(5)(a) for prison / 23(5) for a place other than prison).

See the References page for a link to the Payability During Periods in Gaol or Psychiatric Confinement policy.

Note: the date of effect of suspension for a customer/partner who is imprisoned or in psychiatric confinement will be the day after the customer entered custody.

If the customer has a partner, see Prison admission process and claims during incarceration.

Go to Step 10.

10

Action to reject a claim or stop payment + Read more ...

Where it is determined DSP is not payable and the customer is currently receiving DSP, the date of effect of suspension is the day after the customer entered custody. The customer does not lose eligibility until he/she has spent the whole day (midnight to midnight) imprisoned.

Where a claim was being assessed, reject DSP.

Where the person was receiving payment, suspend DSP.

If the customer has a partner, see Prison admission process and claims during incarceration.

See Step 1 in Table 2.

2

Customer currently in receipt of DSP + Read more ...

If DSP is no longer payable due to the customer being in gaol, serving a term of imprisonment for any crime or in psychiatric confinement and not undertaking a course of rehabilitation, DSP must be suspended.

Go to the Benefit Action (BA) screen and in the following fields:

  • Svc Rsn, key DSP
  • Action, key SUS
  • Reason, key IMP
  • Effect Date, key the date the suspension is to take effect
  • Source, key details
  • DOR, key the date of receipt

Finalise the activity on the Assessment Results (AR) screen.

Create a Display on Access DOC with the following wording:

  • Subject Line - PLEASE READ RE DSP ELIGIBILITY
  • Document text - Based on (information lodged), customer does not meet S23 (9) of the SSA therefore DSP is no longer payable. Payment has been suspended as per [insert]

If coded, remove 'Y' indicator in S23 (9) field on the Pension Disability Information (PDI) screen

Go to Step 4.

3

Customer has lodged DSP claim + Read more ...

New claim to be processed by SCCRT (OA8) only

Note: as part of the new claim process, when the Centralised Team is organising the JCA referral, include a note in the free text field stating customer is currently in psychiatric confinement. If a DMA is required, the Centralised Team must email the Government-contracted doctors (GCD) mailbox advising a DMA is required for a customer in psychiatric confinement. Once a response is received, the Centralised Team will undertake the DMA referral.

Once DSP claim has been processed and granted:

  • key the Event Date and add 'Y' indicator in S23(9) field on the Pension Disability Information (PDI) screen
  • record a Display on Access DOC indicating the customer's current S23 (9) status. Approved text:
    • Subject Line - PLEASE READ RE DSP ELIGIBILITY
    • Document text - Customer is in psychiatric confinement in [insert] because they have been charged with (but not convicted of) an offence. Customer is undertaking a course of rehabilitation. Subject to meeting all other eligibility criteria, DSP remains payable under Section 23(9) of the Social Security Act 1991. Determination made based on [evidence/date]. PDI screen updated. Circumstances of psychiatric confinement to be reviewed on [date] by requesting new SA379 form - manual review scheduled. Any changes to current circumstances must be notified immediately to Services Australia, including exit from psychiatric confinement, cessation of rehabilitation or commencement of a term of imprisonment due to conviction for an offence
    • add keyword - DSPREHAB
  • set up a future review on the Review Registration (RVR) screen to ensure ongoing S23 (9) eligibility is checked. Where the customer qualifies under S23 (9) because their mental fitness to stand trial is being assessed, review 6 monthly. Review all other customers annually. Approved text:
    • Service Reason: DSP
    • Review Reason: Manual Assessment Entitlement
    • Notes: Review whether DSP remains payable under S23(9) of the SSA 1991. Refer to DOA DOC. Request institution to provide new SA379 to review if customer is still in psychiatric confinement because they have been charged (but not convicted of) and offence, and undertaking a course of rehabilitation. If circumstances unchanged, DOA DOC to remain and set further review for 12 months
    • add keyword - DSPREHAB
