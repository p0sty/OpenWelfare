Referral Workflow

Making a referral to SEE

Table 1

Step

Action

1

Determine if the customer receives an eligible income support payment + Read more ...

Is the customer in receipt of an eligible income support payment (i.e. JobSeeker Payment, Youth Allowance (job seeker), Disability Support Pension, Parenting Payment (Single or Partnered) or Special Benefit)?

  • Yes, and they are aged:
    • 15 years to under 65 years? Go to Step 2
    • 65 years or older? Go to Step 5
  • No, go to Step 5

2

Determine if job seeker is Centrelink or provider managed + Read more ...

The Customer Information field on the Activity and Exemption Summary (AEX) screen shows if a customer is Centrelink or provider managed. For:

  • Centrelink managed job seekers, go to Step 3
  • Provider managed job seekers, go to Step 4

3

Centrelink managed job seekers + Read more ...

The Service Officer must check the following screens before starting the referral process. This assists with determining eligibility:

  • Legal Residence Details (RSLEG) screen - for Citizenship and Visa sub class
  • Residence Results Display (RSRD) screen - for permanent visa details
  • Period of Australian Presence (RSPAP) screen - for end date of the Newly Arrived Resident's Waiting Period (NARWP)

For job seekers without mutual obligation requirements, go to Step 5

For job seekers with mutual obligation requirements:

  • advise these job seekers of their obligation to participate in SEE, if assessed as suitable for training at the Pre-Training Assessment
  • use the Referral Workflow to refer them to SEE and arrange a Pre-Training Assessment appointment with the SEE provider while the job seeker is present

See Step 1 in Table 2.

4

Provider managed job seekers + Read more ...

Customer is eligible for SEE.

  • Tell the job seeker they are eligible to be referred to SEE. If the job seeker agrees to the referral, contact the job seeker's Employment Services Provider
  • Discuss any impact on the activities already in the Job Plan, if a referral is made to SEE. This discussion can be with any staff member of the provider, not just the job seeker's Case Manager
  • If the provider has reasonable grounds to oppose a referral, tell the job seeker to contact their Case Manager. Do not proceed with the referral

If the provider agrees, continue with the referral:

  • Obtain the jobseeker's consent to pass on their personal details to the SEE provider
  • Use the Referral Workflow to refer the job seeker to SEE

See Step 1 in Table 2.

5

System limitations + Read more ...

Job seekers aged 65 years or older, customers not in receipt of eligible income support payments, and customers who are not in receipt of any income support payment (volunteer job seekers), are eligible for SEE but are unable to be referred to SEE through Services Australia systems.

When the job seeker is in receipt of an eligible income support payment and aged 65 years or older, they should be advised to ask a provider for a referral to SEE. Procedure ends here.

The following customers will only be able to access SEE as a volunteer job seeker when the Services Australia system does not allow a referral to SEE:

  • the customer is in receipt of an income support payment other than JobSeeker Payment, Youth Allowance (job seeker), Disability Support Pension, Parenting Payment (single or partnered), or Special Benefit, or
  • the customer is not in receipt of any income support payment

If they want to be referred to SEE:

  • register them as a volunteer job seeker. This will automatically refer them to Online Employment Services
  • tell the volunteer job seeker they can only access a maximum of 12 months of Online Employment Services as a volunteer. Their access to the service offer will stop after that time, however, they will be able to continue in SEE until they complete the activity
  • tell the volunteer job seeker to contact the Digital Services Contact Centre and request a referral to SEE
  • if the customer cannot, or does not want to be referred as a volunteer job seeker, they cannot be referred to SEE. Procedure ends here

Referral Workflow

Table 2

Step

Action

1

Start the Referral Workflow + Read more ...

From Workspace, select Referral Summary. Ensure there is no current SEE referral, then select Create new referral:

  • Selecting Create new referral (or Create Referral from Workspace) launches the Referrals Workflow and displays the Referral Type screen
  • Enter the referral date of effect
  • Select the Referral Type: 'Skills for Education and Employment', then Continue

The Skills for Education and Employment Eligibility screen is displayed. This screen requires completion of a SEE eligibility checklist to ensure the job seeker is eligible for a referral to SEE.

  • Answer each question. The Eligibility Result displays at the end of the checklist
    • For the question Aged 15 years or more and looking for full-time work (dependent on individual mutual obligation requirements), select Yes for Principal Carers, or job seekers with a reduced work capacity
  • Enter the Source and Receipt date, then Continue

If the job seeker is:

  • eligible, go to Step 2
  • not eligible, go to Step 3

2

Job seeker is eligible for SEE + Read more ...

The Service Provider screen displays all SEE service providers and distance education providers in the job seeker's region. The Resources page contains a list of distance education providers.

Is a suitable provider or distance educator available?

  • Yes, go to Step 5
  • No, go to Step 4

3

Job seeker is not eligible for SEE + Read more ...

A job seeker is not eligible when they:

  • are in New Enterprise Incentive Scheme (NEIS)
  • are in Adult Migrant Education Program (AMEP)
  • have recently participated in SEE and the last program exit date is within 12 weeks of the next referral date:
    • When it's a new provider, the workflow completes and creates a DOC advising the date when the job seeker can be referred back to SEE
    • Job seekers wanting to go back to their previous SEE provider can contact the provider themselves to re-start SEE
    • When a Service Officer attempts to code another referral to the same provider within the 12 week period will receive the following error ''E409RR - Not Eligible, can be referred to SEE after <Due Date>'. These job seekers are not eligible for a new referral by Services Australia before the date recorded against the previous finalisation
  • successful completion. This is shown by the latest SEE status of Lang/Lit/Num Successful on the Referral Summary screen. Customers who have completed the program will be eligible again after a 12 week break
  • other reasons, the workflow completes and creates a DOC with the text Customer is not eligible for SEE

Job seeker has changed address:

The job seeker can:

  • contact their previous provider to advise they have relocated. They should request their record be updated in the provider's system with Transfer to new provider. Once updated, a referral to the new provider is possible
  • wait until 12 weeks from their last exit date, then seek a new referral, or
  • resume with the previous SEE provider by contacting the provider directly

Job seeker wants to change providers:

  • wait until 12 weeks from their last exit date, then seek a new referral

If launched from the Job Plan, the Referral Workflow then returns to the Job Plan to continue. This is for job seekers with mutual obligation requirements. See Negotiating Job Plans.

If the referral is not being made via the Job Plan, return to the Referral Summary screen to finalise the activity.

Procedure ends here.

4

No suitable providers are available + Read more ...

Ask the job seeker if they can attend a SEE provider at a nearby location. A job seeker may choose to undertake the program at another location due to:

  • transportation difficulties
  • a disability
  • caring responsibilities
  • cultural constraints
  • no accessible SEE provider

To search for other SEE providers in nearby locations, place a tick in the Include nearby regions checkbox. The list expands to include SEE providers in surrounding regions.

If the job seeker wishes to choose a provider outside their surrounding region, select Provider Search to manually search by region code, postcode or town/suburb.

Is a referral to another suitable SEE provider an option for the job seeker?

  • Yes, go to Step 5
  • No, when there are no suitable service providers available for the job seeker, a service gap is to be recorded:
    • Click in the Record service gap checkbox
    • Select a service gap reason from the drop down list options. When this option is selected, the list of service providers is disabled
    • Continue, then Finalise

Procedure ends here.

5

Suitable provider is available + Read more ...

Ask the job seeker if they are interested in undertaking SEE by distance education, if the suitable provider list includes distance education providers. If not, only select local providers.

Select the job seeker's preferred SEE provider. Select More Info... to obtain provider contact details.

Arrange an appointment with the SEE provider for the Pre-Training Assessment. This is mandatory for all SEE referrals, whether the referral is coded through the Job Plan or the Create Referral workflow. Ensure the job seeker knows of the compliance consequences if they do not attend their Pre-Training Assessment appointment.

Service Officers should not discuss the job seeker's circumstances with the provider. Only disclose sufficient information to book the appointment.

If referring to a distance education provider, go to Step 6.

If referring to local providers:

  • check if the job seeker has a silent phone number â€“ if so, seek their consent to pass the number to the provider over the phone as the referral system will exclude it from the online referral
  • phone the provider and make a Pre-Training Assessment appointment
  • advise the job seeker of the date, time and location of the appointment
  • once the SEE provider's contact details have been noted, select Back , then Continue

Go to Step 7.

6

Referral to distance education provider + Read more ...

Job seekers may choose to undertake SEE by distance learning.

For all SEE courses through a distance education provider, job seekers require:

  • a referral from Services Australia or an Employment Services Provider, and
  • an assessment and phone interview with the distance education provider to assess language or literacy and numeracy needs

Services Australia initiated referral to a distance education provider

  • Contact TAFE SA (Distance) to give the job seeker's details. Telephone numbers are available by selecting More Info... on the Service Provider screen
  • Ensure the job seeker is present to obtain consent to disclose their relevant personal details
  • Advise the job seeker that the provider will contact them to arrange a Pre-Training Assessment appointment either by direct visit, online, telephone interview and/or mailed response

Job seeker initiated referral to a distance education provider

If the job seeker has already contacted TAFE SA (Distance) and requires a referral from Services Australia:

  • confirm the job seeker is eligible for SEE
  • contact the distance education provider, and
  • record the referral

For further details about SEE distance education providers, including site codes, see the link on the Resources page.

Go to Step 7.

7

Code referral to SEE + Read more ...

Select a service provider, and on the Create Referral screen, select from the dropdown boxes:

  • Reason for referral - the reason why the job seeker has been referred to this Service Type
  • Status - Referred (REF) is displayed, as it is the initial referral status
  • Status Reason - the reasons for a status will be displayed, select Awaiting Contact
  • If a referral is being made to a SEE provider outside the job seeker's region, choose the appropriate option from the following:
    • Client Preference
    • Family Reasons
    • No Service Available
    • Significant Disability
    • Travel Distance
  • Referral Channel - the method the job seeker used in contacting Services Australia
  • Requested by - the person/organisation that requested the referral (for example, Services Australia, jobactive, etc.)
  • Referral effective date - the date the referral is to take effect (this is entered on the Service Type screen)
  • Due Date - the due date of the referral is automatically populated
  • If required, enter the Source and Receipt Date details and click Continue

The Confirm Referral screen is displayed. Details can be changed before confirming the referral.

Select Confirm Referral. The workflow returns to the Job Plan workflow for Centrelink managed job seekers with mutual obligation requirements. Otherwise, finalise the referral via the Assessment Results (AR) screen.

Go to Step 8.

8

Job Plan Workflow + Read more ...

For Centrelink managed job seekers with mutual obligation requirements, record the following in the Job Plan fields:

  • Assessment Type: select ESL pre training appointment assessment activity, language, literacy or numeracy depending on the job seeker's needs
  • Total Hours: add one hour, which is the amount of time to participate in the Pre-Training Assessment appointment
  • Start date and End date fields should automatically populate. The job seeker must have their Pre-Training Assessment appointment with the SEE provider within 3 weeks

Select Add and continue through the Job Plan workflow as required. See Negotiating Job Plans.

See Participation in Skills for Education and Employment (SEE) for details of the process to update the Job Plan when a job seeker commences SEE:

  • Once the job seeker has started SEE, an issue is created on their record
  • The issue advises the Job Plan must be updated
  • The period of the activity should match the details of the due date of the placement. This shows in the Referral Details (RRRD) screen. Access RRRD via the Referral Summary (RRSUM) screen
