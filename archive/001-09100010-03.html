Authenticating a job seeker + Read more ...

When a call is answered, the Service Officer can advise their name and that they are calling from Services Australia. Service Officers must inform the caller at the beginning of every outbound call that the call may be recorded for quality assurance and training purposes.

The Service Officer must be satisfied the customer record being accessed belongs to the correct person prior to assisting with a customer enquiry or before commencing the job seeker claim appointment. See Authenticating a Centrelink customer.

Note: it is not appropriate for the Service Officer to disclose the reason for the call or to identify the team they work with, as this could reveal the type of business the person being sought has with the agency, and therefore affect their privacy. Contacting the job seeker + Read more ...

6

First outbound attempt + Read more ...

When it is necessary to contact a job seeker, make every reasonable and genuine effort to ensure contact is successful.

If the contact number recorded on the job seeker's record is a mobile number, Service Officers should create a pre-call notification (SMS only) using Desktop Electronic Messaging Capability (DEMC). Following creation of the SMS, wait 1 to 2 minutes before making the outbound attempt.

Was the contact successful?

  • Yes, see Step 1 in Table 3
  • No,
    • First attempt to contact a job seeker is unsuccessful, hold the work item until the next calendar day (see Step 1 in Table 5 for details)
    • Key sufficient detail about the outbound contact attempt on the Notes tab to allow subsequent Service Officer to action the second attempt
    • Once finalised, go to Step 8

7

Second outbound attempt + Read more ...

If the first attempt to contact the job seeker is unsuccessful, make a second genuine attempt in line with current processes.

If the contact number, recorded on the job seeker's record, is a mobile number, Service Officers should create a pre-call notification (SMS only) using Desktop Electronic Messaging Capability (DEMC). Following creation of the SMS, wait 1 to 2 minutes before making the outbound attempt.

Was the contact successful?

  • Yes, see Step 1 in Table 3
  • No, go to Step 8

8

Issue a Q164 letter after second unsuccessful contact attempt or where no contact details recorded for job seeker + Read more ...

Issue a Q164 letter asking the customer to contact Services Australia by phone within 14 days:

  • after a second unsuccessful outbound contact attempt, or
  • where there are no contact details or incorrect details are recorded (for example, the job seeker's phone number is not registered)

The customer has 14 days to respond after they receive the letter. Add days to allow extra time for mail delivery. Hold the work item until the day after the allowable time has expired (see Step 1 in Table 5 for details).

If the job seeker:

  • contacts because of the suspension, restore payment and action outstanding activities. see Step 1 in Table 3
  • fails to contact, go to Step 9

To manually issue a Q164 letter, see Step 2 Table 5

9

Job seeker fails to respond to the Q164 letter + Read more ...

If the job seeker fails to contact by the notification provision date +1 after the Q164 is issued, Service Officers must determine if suspension action can be taken. Before suspending a customer's payment, see Suspension of payments (CLK) for information about factors to consider.

If it is appropriate to manually suspend a customer for Failing to Respond to Correspondence (SUS-FRC):

  • Go to the Benefit Action (BA) screen:
    • Key the payment type as the type of benefit in the Svc Rsn field
    • Key the action to be taken in the Action field
    • Key the suspension reason 'FRC' in the Reason field
    • Press [Enter] and the date of effect will default or key the date of effect of the suspension in the Effect Date field
    • Complete the Source field and the date of receipt in the DOR field
  • Include the following details on a DOC (where relevant):
    • Decision made, payment type and the Act/Guide reference the decision was based on, see Creating documents (Fast Note, Callbacks and ACTDOCs) and Online Document Recording (ODR)
    • Date of effect of the decision
    • Reasons for the decision, including why the person was not qualified or why the payment is not payable, for decisions made under section 80
    • Follow-up action required on contact with the customer
    • If contact with the assessing Service Officer is required before restoration can be done or whether Smart Centre Telephony staff may restore the payment
  • Go to the Assessment Results (AR) screen and finalise the activity

Where the job seeker's payment has:

  • been suspended for failing to contact:
    • no further action is required by Services Australia
    • hold the work item for 13 weeks from the suspension date. Do not cancel the work item
  • not been suspended as it is not appropriate to suspend the job seeker's payment for failing to contact:
    • undertake job seeker contact, and
    • hold the work item as per the Q164 process. Do not cancel the work item

Note: see Step 1 in Table 5 for how to hold a work item.

Requirement and timeframes for Job Plan + Read more ...

Negotiate a Job Plan within 4 business days for all job seekers starting on an income support payment with mutual obligation requirements, unless they have an exemption. Where a job seeker has a change of circumstances and needs a new Job Plan, negotiate it within 14 days of their change of circumstances.

If Job Plan negotiation and agreement is incomplete within 28 calendar days, suspension of the job seeker's income support payments will happen.

A job seeker with an exemption from their mutual obligation requirements cannot negotiate a compulsory Job Plan.

Does the job seeker have mutual obligation requirements?

  • Yes:
    • and their payments are suspended as they have not replied to correspondence about a Job Plan, go to Step 7
    • for all other job seekers, go to Step 2
  • No, go to Step 9

2

Under 18 and at risk + Read more ...

Is the job seeker under 18 years and do they have 'At Risk' issues?

  • Yes, see Eligibility and registration for employment assistance as a Vulnerable Youth (VY) or Vulnerable Youth (Student) (VY(S)). Procedure ends here
  • No, go to Step 3

3

Remote job seekers + Read more ...

Is the job seeker a remote job seeker?

  • Yes, and the job seeker is:
    • in a Community Development Program (CDP) region and is provider managed, the CDP provider is responsible for negotiating the Job Plan. Procedure ends here
    • in a CDP region and is Centrelink managed. Centrelink is responsible for negotiating the Job Plan. Arrange a phone interview to discuss their Job Plan. See Step 1 in Table 3
    • managed by Centrelink and does not live in a CDP region. Arrange a phone interview to negotiate a Job Plan. See Step 1 in Table 3
  • No, go to Step 4

4

Waiting period or Income Maintenance Period (IMP) + Read more ...

Does the job seeker have a waiting period or IMP of more than 14 days from the date of interview?

  • Yes:
    • The job seeker is not required to agree to a Job Plan immediately. The job seeker can agree to a Job Plan now or at the end of the waiting period
    • An action item will be generated for the Centrelink managed job seeker's record at the end of their waiting period and the Service Officer must negotiate a Job Plan with the job seeker at their next contact
    • If Job Plan negotiation within 28 calendar days after the start of income support payments does not happen, the job seeker's payment may be suspended. Procedure ends here
  • No, go to Step 5

5

Break in income support payment + Read more ...

Has the job seeker had a break in their income support payment?

  • Yes:
    • If there is a Job Plan from before a break in payment, it remains current until another Job Plan is created
    • When the job seeker resumes payment, negotiated a new Job Plan based on their current circumstances. Go to Step 6
  • No, go to Step 6

6

Determining who is responsible for negotiating the Job Plan + Read more ...

The Participation Summary and Job Plan Summary pages display whether the job seeker is:

  • provider managed, or
  • Centrelink managed

Centrelink managed

Service Officers need to negotiate a Job Plan with Centrelink managed job seekers, see Step 1 in Table 3.

Provider managed or in Online Employment Services and not doing an approved activity that will fully meet their mutual obligation requirements

The provider or OES will negotiate the Job Plan. For new claim job seekers this will usually occur at the initial interview with their provider or OES. Refer the job seeker back to their provider or OES. Procedure ends here.

Provider managed or in Online Employment Services and doing an approved activity which will fully meet their mutual obligation requirements

Job seekers who are provider managed and are fully meeting their mutual obligation requirements by doing approved activities, will become Centrelink managed.

Note: For OES job seekers the Job Plan will only be presented to them once they complete the Career Profile, unless exempt from RapidConnect.

Is the job seeker now Centrelink managed, and able to provide all the information and verification needed?

  • Yes, see Step 1 in Table 3
  • No, tell the job seeker they must provide verification of the approved activity before the Job Plan can be completed

7

If a Job Plan is not negotiated + Read more ...

A job seeker's income support payment will be suspended Failure to Respond to Correspondence (FRC) if they do not have a Job Plan:

  • negotiated within 4 business days of the date their first regular income support payment, or
  • within 28 days of becoming subject to mutual obligation requirements (for Parenting Payment Single (PPS) job seekers), or
  • within 14 days of a change in circumstances, or
  • agreed within 4 days of negotiating and submitting their Job Plan in their online account

If a job seeker contacts about an outstanding Job Plan, Service Officers should first establish if the job seeker is agency or provider managed, and if a new Job Plan is pending negotiation on the Job Plan Status page. See Step 6 to decide who is responsible for negotiating a Job Plan.

Are they a provider managed job seeker?

  • Yes, the provider will negotiate the Job Plan. Make sure the referral of the job seeker to a provider and an appointment has been booked within 2 working days, before restoring job seeker payment. Procedure ends here
  • No, go to Step 8

8

Centrelink managed and payment suspended FRC + Read more ...

Tell the job seeker that their failure to agree to the Job Plan has resulted in suspension of their payment. Agreeing to the Job Plan will allow restoration of their payment.

Job seekers are able to go online and restore their payment by completing the outstanding 'agree to job plan' task in their online account.

Service Centre Service Officers, see Step 1 in Table 3.

Smart Centre Service Officers who are suitably skilled may negotiate a Job Plan with the job seeker by phone, see Step 1 in Table 3.

Otherwise, the Smart Centre Service Officer must:

  • check Office Locator and determine if it is appropriate to refer the job seeker as a walk-in or book a scheduled appointment
  • where appropriate, book a Compulsory Participation appointment, and
  • if appointment booked, tell the job seeker of the time, date, and purpose of the appointment
  • tell the job seeker they must notify the agency if their contact number changes so they can be contacted
  • tell the job seeker they must negotiate and agree to a Job Plan before their income support payments can be restored

Record a DOC confirming the advice given, and any other relevant information disclosed by the job seeker.

If, after 13 weeks of suspension, a jobseeker still does not have a current Job Plan their payment will auto-cancel.

Procedure ends here.

9

Voluntary Job Plan + Read more ...

The job seeker may volunteer to participate

Job seekers who do not have, or are exempt from, mutual obligation requirements may volunteer for Employment Provider Services. Volunteer job seekers may be subject to restrictions by the Department of Education, Skills and Employment and only be allowed to participate in jobactive for up to 6 months unless their circumstances change. See Eligibility to access employment assistance services through jobactive. Procedure ends here.

Job seeker is applying for Additional Child Care Subsidy (ACCS) (Transition to Work)

The job seeker must be doing an Additional Child Care Subsidy (ACCS) (Transition to Work) approved activity that is included in a Job Plan. A Job Plan must be negotiated to include an ACCS (Transition to Work) approved activity as a voluntary activity. See Step 1 in Table 3.

Note: the job seeker may volunteer to connect with a provider who will negotiate a Job Plan for ACCS (Transition to Work) purposes. Procedure ends here.

Completing a Job Plan

Table 3

Step

Action

1

Negotiate the Job Plan + Read more ...

Voluntary Job Plans

Voluntary Job Plans may also be negotiated for Additional Child Care Subsidy (ACCS) (Transition to Work) purposes. The steps below for negotiating compulsory Job Plans for job seekers with mutual obligation requirements are only relevant for voluntary Job Plan purposes in as much as they describe the Job Plan workflow and finalisation process. This includes the option for the job seeker to finalise the Job Plan via their online account (not including the Centrelink app).

For job seekers seeking a voluntary Job Plan, go to Step 2.

Compulsory Job Plans

Explain the purpose of the interview, and tell the job seeker about the requirement to enter into a Job Plan, their rights and obligations in relation to the terms of the Job Plan, and information about compliance:

  • Restate the job seeker's mutual obligation requirements
  • In accordance with the job seeker's participation category, discuss with the job seeker what types of activities may be included in the plan as well as their options for overcoming any employment barriers. Make any relevant referrals to appropriate programs or activities. The Job Plan workflow will auto-code most referrals in the referrals cluster at the same time as they are included in the Job Plan
  • If the job seeker needs to negotiate a Job Plan as part of a new claim but the new claim cannot be finalised, the Service Officer may choose to negotiate a pre grant Job Plan if they are confident the job seeker will be Centrelink managed and they are confident which participation category the job seeker will fall into

Note: if a Service Officer prints the Job Plan for the job seeker, the system will check if there is a preferred written language other than English recorded on the Other Contact Details (OCD) page. It will check the language recorded against available translations of the 'Information you need to know' and if appropriate, a link displays to the Service Officer allowing them to print a translated copy of the 'Information you need to know' part of the Job Plan. The English version of this information will continue to form part of the Job Plan.

2

Begin a Job Plan or complete a started Job Plan + Read more ...

To begin or continue a started Job Plan:

  • In Customer First, under the Participation heading, select Job Plan
  • Answer any prompts displayed in relation to provider managed job seekers
  • Navigate to the Job Plan Status page
  • Select Negotiate Plan to open the Overview page. The Overview page enables a Service Officer to create or update a Job Plan via the Job Plan workflow

If the job seeker is provider managed, the Negotiate reason page will display. There will need to be a reason why negotiating a Job Plan for a provider managed job seeker. If the intention is to make the job seeker Centrelink managed, select Other and key this as the reason in the free text section.

If the job seeker has a pending new claim, the Pre-Grant Profile page will display.

If the job seeker is Centrelink managed, the first selected category from the Overview page will display.

The Activity Category page will display the job seekers activities and allow access to the individual activity pages. Appropriate activities should be added, modified or deleted from the individual activities pages by following the system prompts. This includes coding:

  • whether the activity requirement is compulsory or voluntary
  • the start and end date of the activity (where applicable)
  • the number of hours of participation (where applicable)

Note: jobseekers with compulsory participation requirements must have at least one compulsory activity included in the job plan.

The Plan Summary page enables a Service Officer to:

  • indicate if an interpreter was used when negotiating the Job Plan
  • view/print a PDF copy of the Job Plan for the job seeker to sign. Note: the Job Plan must be viewed using the View/Print button and checked, prior to the Service Officers' submission of the Job Plan to the job seeker's online account or certifying that the job seeker has signed the plan
  • specify if the job seeker signed their plan in person or elected to agree to their Job Plan in their online account, and
  • finalise the Job Plan

Once finalised the details of the Job Plan will be sent to the Department of Education, Skills and Employment.

Note: some Centrelink Managed job seekers signed up for Online Services who are fully meeting their mutual obligation requirements through part-time work can have their Job Plan updated automatically when the activity end date is reached by completing an Online Task. These job seekers then use the 'View and Agree Job Plan' online service to approve their Job Plan.

In cases where a Centrelink managed job seekers have attempted to update their approved activity and/or Job Plan online and the activity has failed to update, Service Officers are allocated a 'Change of Circumstances' work item to investigate the reason automation has failed. Once resolved, complete the COC activity, select the 'Online MOR & Job Plan or AEX Review' COC Interaction from the Interaction History and change the status from 'For manual action' to 'Completed'.

If the job seeker has an online account and wishes to agree to the Job Plan via their online account, they must agree to their Job Plan in their online account within 4 business days of it being submitted online. The Job Plan will be available in their online account immediately allowing Job Plans negotiated over the phone to be finalised during the contact.

If the job seeker elects to finalise their Job Plan:

  • in person, go to Step 3
  • using their online account, go to Step 6

3

Job Plan is finalised in person + Read more ...

Issue the printed Job Plan to the job seeker and explain their rights and obligations:

  • They can request a variation or negotiation of their current Job Plan at any time
  • They can apply for a formal review of any decision in relation to the Job Plan
  • They must notify the agency of changes in their circumstances
  • Job seekers with mutual obligation requirements must comply with the terms of the Job Plan
  • Job seekers who are participating voluntarily will not be subject to compliance action
  • Answer any questions the job seeker may have
  • Ensure the job seeker signs the Job Plan

Note: for remote job seekers where the Job Plan was negotiated over the phone, print 2 copies of the Job Plan. These should be sent with a Request for Information (RFI) letter requesting 1 copy be signed and returned by the job seeker. If the job seeker fails to return the Job Plan, see Refusing to enter into a Job Plan or Participation Plan. Procedure ends here.

Has the job seeker signed the Job Plan?

  • Yes, go to Step 5
  • No, and the job seeker:
    • is refusing to enter an agreement, go to Step 4
    • has expressed concerns and would like to think further before signing the Job Plan, go to Step 8

4

Job seeker refuses to enter into compulsory Job Plan + Read more ...

If the job seeker continues to refuse to enter into a Job Plan, despite being advised that it is a compulsory requirement to receive a participation payment, the job seeker must be advised:

  • refusing to enter into a Job Plan means they are not qualified for payment, and
  • cancellation of payment will occur because of their refusal to enter into a Job Plan

If the job seeker:

  • subsequently advises they are willing to enter into the Job Plan, go to Step 5
  • subsequently expresses concerns and would like to think further before signing the Job Plan, go to Step 8
  • still refuses to agree to the Job Plan, payment must be cancelled FSA 'Failed to Sign Activity Agreement (Job Plan) and the decision documented. Procedure ends here

5

Job Plan is signed by job seeker in person + Read more ...

Sign the Job Plan as the delegated Service Officer and complete the following:

  • Give (fax/post for remote job seekers) the signed Job Plan to the job seeker
  • Return to the Job Plan workflow to finalise the Job Plan. Select Yes to Has the job seeker signed the Job Plan?
  • key the date signed in the required field (for remote job seekers, the date signed will be the date the Job Plan was generated)
  • select Finalise - this navigates to the Assessment and Warning Error (AWE) page
  • continue through AWE page and Assessment Result (AR) page to complete the WAP/PLN activity

Procedure ends here.

6

Job seeker agrees, disagrees or refuses to agree to a Job Plan in their online account + Read more ...

The Job Plan will be available to finalise in the online account immediately (this cannot be done in the Centrelink app). The job seeker does not have to wait to receive notification. Job Plans negotiated over the phone should be finalised online immediately while the job seeker is still on the phone.

In their online account, the job seeker has the option to:

  • agree to their Job Plan
  • disagree with their Job Plan, for example if there is an error
  • refuse to agree to the Job Plan
  • do nothing

Job seeker agrees

Agreement with the Job Plan online is the same as signing the Job Plan. It becomes current and the job seeker must do the activities in the Job Plan. Go to Step 7.

Job seeker disagrees

If the job seeker disagrees with their Job Plan, a WAP/MFU activity will be created along with an associated action item on the Participation Summary page. This is to alert a Service Officer to contact the job seeker to re-negotiate their Job Plan. The WAP/MFU cannot be cancelled.

A work item 'Online-MOR & Job Plan or AEX Review' will also be created and allocated with notes in the interaction history for Service Officers to contact job seeker and discuss circumstances.

Job seeker refuses to agree in their online account

If the job seeker refuses to agree to the Job Plan in their online account, payment will either reject or cancel. The WAP/PLN activity will remain on the job seeker's record.

A work item 'Online-MOR & Job Plan or AEX Review' will also be created and allocated with notes in the interaction history for Service Officers to contact job seeker and discuss circumstances.

Job seeker does nothing

If the job seeker has an online account and wishes to agree to the Job Plan via their online account, they must agree to their Job Plan within 4 business days of submission of the Job Plan to the online account. If they have not agreed and there is no plan on the job seeker's record, the No Job Plan process will apply.
Note: the No Job Plan process does not apply to voluntary job seekers or if they do not have compulsory mutual obligation requirements.

If the job seeker:

  • after 2 days, has not agreed to their Job Plan in their online account, a reminder will be sent to the job seeker if they are registered for Electronic Messaging
  • after 4 days, does not agree to their Job Plan, the Job Plan Review (JPR) and the Job Plan Suspension (JPS) review processing is triggered. The job seeker will be sent a letter 14 days after the JPR is triggered, requesting them to contact the agency to negotiate a Job Plan
  • responds to the letter, check the status of the Job Plan in their online account via the Customer Portal Staff View. Tell the job seeker of the required action to finalise the plan (for example, sign in person at a service centre or use their online account)

requires a restoration, a new Job Plan must be negotiated and agreed to

If necessary, assist the job seeker to finalise their Job Plan via the job seeker's online account.

Note: the WAP/PLN activity will stay on the job seeker's record and they will be able to agree to their Job Plan in their online account.

If the job seeker does not respond to this letter, their payment will suspend 14 days later. The reason will be Failing to Reply to Correspondence. If they still do not have a current Job Plan after 13 weeks, their payment will auto-cancel.

The Resources page has information about the 'View and Agree to Job Plan' service.

7

Activity and Exemption Summary + Read more ...

Check the Activity and Exemption Summary (AEX) page to ensure it reflects the activities included in the Job Plan, including the activity start and end dates.

If the system has not auto-coded the AEX page, an Action Item will be displayed on the Participation Summary page to remind the Service Officer to check and update the AEX page to reflect the activities on the Job Plan and their start and end dates.

Note: some Centrelink Managed job seekers signed up for Online Services who are fully meeting their mutual obligation requirements through part-time work can have their Job Plan updated automatically when the activity end date is reached by completing an Online Task. These job seekers then use the 'View and Agree Job Plan' online service to approve their Job Plan. In cases where Centrelink Managed job seekers have attempted to update their approved activity and/or Job Plan online and the activity has failed to update, Service Officers are allocated a 'Change of Circumstances' work item to investigate the reason automation has failed. Once resolved, complete the COC activity, select the 'Online-MOR & Job Plan or AEX Review' COC Interaction from the Interaction History and change the status from 'For manual action' to 'Completed'.

Procedure ends here.

8

Think time + Read more ...

If the job seeker indicates they have concerns or expresses the need to think further about the terms of the Job Plan, discuss those issues with them.

If the job seeker still maintains they wish to think further about the terms (for example to consult with a third party before signing), the Service Officer should offer think time to the job seeker.

Remote job seekers

Arrange a phone interview for 2 days' time and either submit the Job Plan to the job seeker's online account or fax/post 1 copy of the draft Job Plan if required, to the job seeker to think about the terms being negotiated.
Note: the Job Plan may, if necessary, be held pending for up to 21 days where job seeker is considered remote.

Non-remote job seekers

For non-remote job seekers:

  • make an appointment or tell them to return in 2 working days' time
  • create a manual review if no appointment was booked
  • give a copy of the draft Job Plan to the job seeker to think about the terms being negotiated
  • alternatively, submit the Job Plan to the job seeker's online account for finalisation after they have had think time, avoiding the need for a further appointment

All job seekers

Tell the job seeker that failure to enter into a Job Plan within 2 days may result in payment suspension.

Do not finalise the Job Plan on the system until it has been returned and signed.

Step

Action

When to cancel a started Job Plan + Read more ...

It is only appropriate for a Service Officer to cancel a Job Plan where a pending plan has been started but not finalised and:

  • the job seeker is now provider managed or
  • the job seeker's participation activities have changed or
  • the job seeker's circumstances have changed and they no longer require a Job Plan (e.g. transferred to a non-participation payment or the job seeker is exempt)

When the Job Plan is cancelled, all updates are deleted and the job seeker's previous plan is reinstated. Note: a started Job Plan can only be cancelled once per day.

Is it appropriate to cancel the started Job Plan?

  • Yes, go to Step 2
  • No, negotiate and complete the started Job Plan as per Table 3 Completing a Job Plan

2

How old is the started Job Plan + Read more ...

Started Job Plans that are over 60 days old will need to be referred to ICT as Service Officers will be unable to cancel or delete these plans. Note: started Job Plans for job seekers who are now on a non-participation payment are automatically cancelled after 60 days and should not require Service Officer action.

Has the started Job Plan been outstanding for 60 days or more?

  • Yes, go to Step 4
  • No, go to Step 3

3

Cancelling the started Job Plan + Read more ...

  • Open the Job Plan Workflow
  • Select the started Job Plan
  • Select the Cancel button on the workflow page. A message will appear to confirm the cancellation of the workflow. Select OK
  • Select the Delete option
    • This will present a warning
      'Department of Employment edits control the deletion of a started (pending) Job Plan. Users may not delete two subsequent pending Job Plans on the same CRN. If you decide to delete this Job Plan now, no Services Australia user will be able to cancel a subsequent Job Plan for this customer.'
  • Select the appropriate reason for cancellation
  • Select Continue

This will complete the cancellation process.

  • of the age of the plan
  • a plan has already been cancelled and this is a second cancellation
  • there is a system issue/edit

  • Select the CoC Interaction Record by clicking on the ID
  • Select Edit from the top menu
  • Go to the Dates tab and click on Expand
  • Find the On Hold Expiry Date line, update the hold date by clicking on the calendar icon in the date column and select the next calendar day
  • Select the activity status and change status to On Hold from the drop down list
  • Select Save from the top menu

Note: do not place Interaction Record on hold without an expiry date. This will place the work item on Hold indefinitely, resulting in the item never reallocating.

Creating a manual Q164 letter + Read more ...

In the customers record:

  • Go to the OLA Accept Service Reasons (OASR) screen. Key OASR in Next and press enter
  • Code the required Service Reason, for example, JSP, YAL
  • Code the letter code Q164 in the Advice Letter code: field and press enter

The Letter Display (LDT) screen will display, with the selected code in the Letter Code: field.

If the customer has a correspondence nominee, issue the same letter to the correspondence nominee.

To finalise the letter:

  • Complete the required fields in the letter by selecting each appropriate option. Press [Continue/Enter]. Use [Previous] and [Next] buttons to go through the letter until all fields are completed
  • View letter variables by keying 'DATA' in the Command: field. Press [Enter] to display the Variable Data (LDD) screen. If 'free text' is required, enter this on the LLD screen
  • Enter or amend details as required, for example, address, contact details
  • When completed, key 'FIN'alise in the Command: field and press [Enter]
  • The system will prompt if fields still need to be completed. To view fields still to be completed, go to the LDT screen, code OPT in the Command: field and press [Enter]. Make any updates and code 'FIN' in the Command: field and press [Enter]
  • Record details on a DOC
