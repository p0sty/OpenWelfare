For Centrelink International Services (CIS) staff only.

This page contains information to support International Services (CIS) staff to process requests for recovery of New Zealand debts by embargo under the Agreement with New Zealand including reconciliation of schedules of recovered amounts.

Embargo of NZ debts from Australian arrears

Reconciliation of NZ debts recovered by embargo from Australian arrears

This table describes the process to send Australian Arrears Schedule and authorise payment to the New Zealand authorities.

Step

Action

1

Access Group Deduction Notice (GDN) + Read more ...

Information on accessing CIS positional mailboxes and use of secure local drives is available for staff in CIS through the International Services homepage. See the Resources page for a link.

At the beginning of each month, a Group Deduction Notice (GDN) of all individual embargoed amounts for the previous month is automatically sent to CIS via email.

Note: all information for processing NZ embargoes should be available in the first week of each month. The agreed arrangement is for Australia to send the monthly schedule and payment to New Zealand by the 15th of each month.

  • Access the CIS positional mailbox
  • Locate the Group Deduction Notice (GDN) for that month

Is the GDN for the current month available?

  • Yes, save the GDN to the secure local drive and archive in the mailbox. Go to Step 2
  • No, report the incident to the ICT Service Desk and await resolution. Escalate to the International Programme if required

2

Receipted amounts + Read more ...

Contact details for the Finance Team in Zone Tasmania are available for staff in CIS through the International Services homepage. See the Resources page for a link.

The Finance Team receipts all amounts that are deposited in the embargo holding account and email a copy of the receipts to CIS.

  • Access the CIS positional mailbox
  • Locate the embargo receipts for the previous month

Are receipts available?

  • Yes, go to Step 3
  • No, contact the Finance Team to resolve. Escalate to the International Programme if required

3

Check receipts + Read more ...

The GDN includes the totals of all bulk amounts deposited into the holding account. This is usually from the four weekly international payment strips but may also occur from regular strips in other environments.

All receipts must be checked to ensure they match the total amounts on the GDN.

  • Access the CIS positional mailbox
  • Open the GDN
  • Check that the receipted amounts match the total amounts in the GDN for each strip

Do the receipted amounts match the amounts in the GDN?

  • Yes, archive receipts in the mailbox. Go to Step 6
  • No, go to Step 4

4

Check embargo information in Customer First + Read more ...

Information on accessing embargo information in Customer First is available for staff in CIS through the International Services homepage. See the Resources page for a link.

The NZ Embargo Organisation record details the information for all embargoed amounts. The information on the record must be checked to ensure it matches the information in the GDN.

  • Access embargo information in Customer First
  • Open the GDN
  • Check that the information in Customer First matches the GDN

Does the embargo information in Customer First match the GDN?

  • Yes, go to Step 5
  • No, report the incident to the ICT Service Desk and await resolution. Escalate to the International Programme if required

5

Missing/wrong receipts + Read more ...

Contact details for the Finance Team in Zone Tasmania are available for staff in CIS through the International Services homepage. See Resources for a link.

If the embargo information in Customer First matches the GDN but amounts don't match the receipts, check with the Finance Team to ensure receipts are for the correct dates and that no receipts are missing.

Do the receipted amounts now match the amounts in the GDN?

  • Yes, archive receipts in the mailbox. Go to Step 6
  • No, report the incident to the Finance Team and await resolution. Escalate to the International Programme if required

6

Authorise Payment + Read more ...

Access to the form 'Request to Pay - NZ EMBARGO' is available for staff in CIS through the International Services homepage. See the Resources page for a link.

Once confirmed, staff in CIS are required to authorise the Finance Team to remit the embargoed amounts to New Zealand. The form 'Request to Pay - NZ OUTGOING EMBARGO' is used for this purpose.

Once received, the Finance Team arrange to pay the monies to nominated New Zealand account and send confirmation back to CIS.

  • Open the GDN and note the total amount
  • Access the 'Request to Pay - NZ OUTGOING EMBARGO' form
  • Complete the MONTH: and YEAR: and TOTAL: amount (from the GDN) to be paid to New Zealand
  • Print and sign the form, send to the Finance Team via internal mail and await confirmation of payment

Has confirmation of payment been received?

  • Yes, go to Step 7
  • No, contact the Finance Team to resolve. Escalate to the International Programme if required

7

Arrears Schedule to New Zealand + Read more ...

Information on electronic records management, including local secure drives, and accessing the electronic liaison system with New Zealand is available for staff in CIS through the International Services homepage. See the Resources page for a link.

Staff in CIS send a schedule to NZ detailing the individual embargoed amounts via secure electronic liaison.

  • Open the GDN for that cycle.
  • Reformat the GDN:
    • Delete all information above the 'Customer name' row
    • Insert 'SCH ID: AMMMYYYYAU' where MMMYYYY is the month and year of the schedule
    • Insert title 'CENTRELINK ARREARS SCHEDULE'
    • Where necessary, delete all rows between payment strips and amend the overall total to reflect the amount to be paid to New Zealand
    • Insert the exchange rate and total amount in NZD, as advised by the Finance Team
  • Save the GDN to the local secure drive as 'AUS ARREARS SCHEDULE YYYYNN' where YYYYNN is the current year (numeric) and month (numeric)
  • Access the electronic liaison system with New Zealand
  • Attach the schedule to an 'Australian Arrears Schedule' liaison form and send to New Zealand
