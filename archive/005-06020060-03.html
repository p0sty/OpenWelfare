This page outlines how to confirm the death of a Status Resolution Support Services (SRSS) customer or their child and what action to take once the death is confirmed.

On this page:

Actioning advice of death of SRSS customer or their child

Coding the death action

Actioning advice of death of SRSS customer or their child

Table 1: This table describes what to do when notification of the death of a SRSS customer or their child is received.

Step

Action

1

A person has advised that an SRSS customer or their dependent child has died + Read more ...

  • if the person providing the information is distressed, offer a private interview before proceeding
  • offer the services of a social worker (if distressed). See Social work service referral

2

Confirm death of the deceased SRSS customer or child + Read more ...

Confirmation of death can be accepted for a person where the person's full name is provided together with 2 of the following items:

  • deceased customer's last address in full
  • date of birth
  • Customer Reference Number (CRN)

Can the informant supply enough information to confirm death?

  • Yes, go to Step 4
  • No, go to Step 3

3

Death cannot be confirmed at initial contact + Read more ...

Obtain all the relevant details from the informant, including their own name and contact number.

Where death cannot be confirmed and the customer's whereabouts cannot be established, suspend payment pending further investigation.

Advise the SRSS Helpdesk of the unconfirmed death via an urgent phone call and provide all relevant information obtained, including the CRN and date of death, so the death can be confirmed by the Department of Home Affairs.

Note: it is imperative that the death is confirmed and actioned as a matter of urgency.

Procedure ends here until the death is confirmed.

4

Death has been confirmed + Read more ...

Was the deceased a customer or their child?

  • Customer, go to Step 5
  • Customer's child, see Step 5 in the Coding the death action table

5

Was income earned or did any other change occur in the reporting period? + Read more ...

  • Yes, go to Step 6
  • No, or the informant is unaware of any income or asset changes, see Step 1 in the Coding the death action table

6

Recording earnings and changes + Read more ...

  • select the required screens to record earnings or any other changes for the deceased customer
  • finalise the activity in the Social Service Plan

To continue see Step 1 in the Coding the death action table.

Coding the death action

Table 2: This table describes the coding to be completed when confirmation of the death of a SRSS customer or their child has been confirmed.

Step

Action

1

Before coding the death action for a customer + Read more ...

A death action cannot be completed if there are activities on the Activity List (AL) screen.

Where possible ensure that any activities are completed and then run the daily driver.

If there is a 'STA' debt activity on the AL screen, contact the SRSS Helpdesk.

If it is not possible to complete an activity, urgently call the SRSS Helpdesk. In these instances the customer's record should be suspended pending processing of the death.

Was the deceased customer receiving Dependent Child Allowance for any children?

  • Yes, do not unlink the children. Urgently escalate the case to the SRSS Helpdesk via a phone referral. This team will refer the case to the Department of Home Affairs for an urgent decision in relation to the care of dependent children. Go to Step 2
  • No, go to Step 2

2

Manually code the death action + Read more ...

Using the SRSS Information guided procedure, end date the Family Group assignment block from the date prior to the date of death.

On the Death/Executor (DEA) screen type 'I' in the Action: field to the left of the Date of Death: field, then key the following:

  • Enter the date the customer passed away in the Date of Death: field
  • Enter the date Services Australia was advised in the Date of Notification: field
  • Enter 'I' in the Action: field to the left of the Date Executor Recorded: field
  • If the Public Trustee is the executor, key the Public Trustee code in the Public Trustee: field.
    If the Public Trustee is not the executor, key the details of the executor into the Executor Details section of the screen
  • Enter the Source: and DOR: fields. Note: when a customer away and they have a surviving partner, record DOR as later of date of death or date paid to + 1
  • Ensure the update has applied on the Relationships Tab (SAP system). If not, use mySupport to refer to ICT

Note: If the executor is unknown, leave this blank

  • Select [Enter]

The Death Confirmation (DAC) screen will display a warning:

  • To confirm action, type 'Y' in SHOULD DEATH ACTION PROCEED: field and select [Enter]. Links with other persons or organisations are ended with a relationship end date of the date of death. Relationship status is updated to 'widowed' if the customer was partnered
  • Type 'N' to abandon the death action and to return to the DEA screen

If confirmed on the DAC screen, finalise activity on the Assessment Results (AR) screen.

  • if 'Y' is coded on the Death Confirmation (DAC) screen, the death is recorded, and any outstanding entitlement will be automatically issued
  • if 'N', the death recording is abandoned and the Death/Executor (DEA) screen will redisplay with the new message 'I033CU - Death Action cancelled - changes have been backed out'
  • For an adult go to Step 3
  • For a child go to Step 5

3

Finalise the death action + Read more ...

Access and approve the Social Service Plan.

Note: updating executor details after recording the death can be done if details are provided at this stage.

Record the details of the bereavement on a DOC including:

  • date of death
  • name and contact details of the informant
  • executor details (if known)

If the deceased customer has a surviving partner who is a SRSS customer, go to Step 4.

If the deceased is single, refer all cases involving the death of a customer immediately via a phone referral to the SRSS Helpdesk so advice can be provided to the Department of Home Affairs.

Procedure ends here.

4

Deceased customer has a surviving partner who is a SRSS customer + Read more ...

If the deceased customer has a surviving partner who is a SRSS customer:

  • Access and approve the partnerâ€™s Social Service Plan.
  • Issue Q062 letter. See Resources for the letter template
  • raise an SD via mySupport to apply a Do not send Filter on the SRSS reassessment letter and refer immediately to the SRSS Helpdesk via phone for an urgent escalation to ICT

5

Update record to reflect death of child + Read more ...

  • manually code death action on the child's record as per Step 2
  • record details on a DOC
  • finalise activity on the Assessment Results (AR) screen
  • access and approve the Social Service Plan on both parents' records
  • issue the Q062 letter
  • raise an SD via mySupport to apply a Do not send Filter on the SRSS reassessment letter and refer immediately to the SRSS Helpdesk via a phone call for an urgent escalation to ICT

Procedure ends here.
