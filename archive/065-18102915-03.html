This page contains information on processing CSHC online claims, assisted customer claims and paper claims.

Staff trained in Process Direct select the Process Direct tab, staff not yet trained select Customer First.

Relationship Details + Read more ...

Start processing + Read more ...

From the TS screen, select Process.

The Errors (SWE) screen will display a Message Log and Task Selector:

  • Select messages to view/update the relevant details. An error or warning will display if information has not loaded correctly from the online claim or ACC
  • The Task Selector will display a flag against screens that contain provisional data (data uploaded from the online claim or ACC or updated by staff)
  • Select the screens to be checked/ updated
  • Select Next to proceed through the selected screens

The Task Selector does not list all screens that may contain provisional or confirmed data. Check the Claim Overview to see if other information has been provided that may need to be updated. To check confirmed data, navigate to the individual screens.

For paper claims (SOA shells), select the screens to manually code details from the claim.

Check/update customer (and if applicable partner) personal details by selecting:

  • Customer Personal Details (CPDS)
  • Address (AD)
  • Telephone Details (TDS)
  • Other Contact Details (OCD)
  • Tax File Number Authorisation (TFN)
  • Office Code (OC)
  • Proof of Identity Details (POI)

To update partner/linked records, select to view the Relations menu. Select the person to access the record. Ensure the correct person's name is displayed before updating. Check/update details from the SWE screen. For help, see Process Direct navigation, common screens and functions.

Initiate environment transfer to link the partner's record if they are not in the same environment as the customer.

For help, see Inter-environment change of address (ICoA) transfer of a customer record. The Digital Assistant (Roxy) can be used to help address issues or errors within Process Direct, see Using Digital Assistant Roxy in Process Direct.

6

Residence + Read more ...

Residence information must be recorded if the claim is to be rejected for a reason other than residence, otherwise the system will override any other rejection reason and the claim will incorrectly reject for a residence relate reason.

For coding help, see Residence and Portability screens.

The customer must generally be in Australia when the claim is made, or meet an exception to the lodgement inside Australia rule.

7

SHC Assessment (SHA) screen + Read more ...

The SHA is a compulsory screen where the start date will generally default to the Date of Receipt. Do not manually change the start date on the SHC Assessment (SHA) screen.

The SHA date should not be changed to a date after the determined start date.

Early claim provisions do not apply to the CSHC. Any CSHC claims lodged prior to the customer being eligible (including before they reach Age Pension age) before the cancellation date of their income support payment has been reached or before the end of a NARWP, must be rejected.

Check the start date is correct, or change to an earlier date using the 'general start date' provisions.

The CSHC date of claim will be automatically taken as the immigration advised date of return if:

  • the customer lodges a CSHC re-claim within 14 days of the date of return to Australia, and
  • the date of return is less than 26 weeks since their CSHC cancellation date

This can apply to CSHC online claims, customised claims, paper claims and verbal re-claims. Customers whose CSHC has been cancelled for more than 26 weeks will need to complete a full new claim.

Press [Next].

10

Seniors Health Card income summary and details (SHIS) screen + Read more ...

Income Details

Income Details; records the customer (and partner):

  • Date of event
  • Reference tax year
  • Income type
  • Adjusted Taxable Income (ATI)

The deemed income amount will not display until after adding or updating an account-based income stream, navigating to the Entitlement (ELD) screen and then returning to the SHIS/SHID screen.

Date of Event (DOV)

For new claims, the DOV must be the same as the Date of receipt (DOR). The DOV cannot be a date after the DOR and should be the date of claim as recorded on the SHA screen.

Reference tax year

  • The reference tax year is the financial year the customer has declared their income and the date of receipt (DOR) of the claim determines the reference tax year
    • The reference tax year for TNA or NRL, must be within 2 years of the current financial year. Use the year the financial year ended, e.g. for 2020/21, code 2021
  • Members of a couple need to provide information relating to the same financial year
  • The reference tax year for an estimate (EST) is the current financial year. Record the year that the current financial year is to end, for example, for 2021/22 code 2022. After the estimate (EST) and income details have been recorded, a second entry is required for the reference year of the NOA using type TNA and the date before the claim DOV, that is, DOV - 1 day

Income Type:

  • TNA for a NOA and for customers who have been granted an ATO lodgement extension
  • NRL when a customer does not have a NOA because they are not required to lodge, for example their income was below the tax free threshold or as a result of an ATO tax offset. NRL must not be used when the customer is required to lodge. For example, the customer has income above the tax lodgement threshold, or had tax withheld from any income in that financial year.
  • EST – Estimate customer (or partner) declared due to an acceptable condition, they are providing an estimate of income for the current financial year.

11

Adjusted taxable income + Read more ...

Adjusted Taxable Income (ATI) details declared by the customer (and partner) should pre-populate for online claims.

Record or accept income details as declared by the customer in the claim, Where the income declared by the customer may not result in the correct claim outcome, code or correct/edit the income details to reflect the income details provided in the verification of income documentation.

Taxable Income

Taxable income details are mandatory and where coding is required code 1 of the following:

  • The amount on the NOA
  • The current year estimate, or
  • The customer's declared income, when not required to lodge

Note: when a customer provides an estimate, a superannuation withdrawal under the First Home Super Saver (FHSS) Scheme is not counted towards their taxable income.

Adjusted Taxable Income (ATI) additional components

The following fields are optional and must only be completed if they are relevant to the customer's claim: See Commonwealth Seniors Health Card (CSHC) income test and reference tax year (dept.local)

  • Foreign Income: key the Australian dollar value
  • Foreign Pension coding – coded by International Services
  • Net Investment Loss: key net rental property or financial investment loss details
  • Employer Provided Benefit: key fringe benefits amount in excess of $1,000, e.g. if the customer received $3,200, only record $2,200
  • Reportable Super Contributions: key reportable employer or personal deductable superannuation contribution details
  • Deemed Income Amount: This is a protected field that cannot be updated manually

Note: the deemed income amount will not display until after adding or updating an account-based income stream, navigating to the Entitlement (ELD) screen and then returning to the SHIS/SHID screen.

Once all information is updated select Next.

Handover function + Read more ...

Note: handover should only be completed after raising an incident in Roxy and ICT have responded with Claim is to be completed in ISIS.

Once selected the claim can no longer be accessed in Process Direct, unless the claim is handed back from Customer First/Customer Record to Process Direct by cancelling the activity and entering the reason Process in PD (PPD).

Is a handover is required?

    • Select > Handover to CF/CR

    • Once Handover is completed a warning will display advising the claim has been handed over to Customer First/Customer Record for processing

When a CSHC claim is finalised using Process Direct, an automatic Note is created on the customer's record to reflect the outcome.

Service Officers will need to check that the Note meets:

  • Getting it Right (GIR)
  • Online Document Recording (ODR)

If a customer already holds a Low Income Health Care Card (LIC) the CSHC will not be automatically issued as the LIC is a higher priority card than the CSHC. If the customer wants the CSHC issued this can be done by manually issuing a non-priority concession card. For more information, see Preferences for concession cards.

8

SHC Income Details (SHID) screen + Read more ...

This is a compulsory screen, except for manual rejections.

This screen records all customer and partner income details. It will display any deemed income from account based income streams automatically once the assessment has been completed on the Assessment Results (AR) screen.

The Initiate DVA Check (if displayed), Date of Event, Reference Tax Year, Income Type and Taxable Income: fields are mandatory:

  • Date of Event (DOV): For new claims this DOV must be the same as the Date of receipt (DOR). The DOV cannot be a date after the DOR and should be the date of claim as recorded on the SHA screen
  • Receiving a DVA Service Pension?: This is auto loaded from the Veterans Affairs pension screen (DVA). All DVA payment data must be coded on the DVA screen. For more information on how to record DVA income see Payments from the Department of Veterans' Affairs (DVA) and referrals to the DVA Clearance Team
  • Reference Tax Year. Note: (1) Members of a couple need to provide information relating to the same financial year, and (2) the date of receipt (DOR) of the claim determines the reference tax year. Where a claim is started before 1 July, but lodged on or after 1 July, the reference tax year selected may no longer be valid. Where this occurs contact the customer and if required, make a further request for information to obtain the correct income details to assess the claim
    • TNA - Tax Notice of Assessment: Record the financial year of the Notice of Assessment (NOA) or the ATO lodgement extension, which must be within 2 years of the current financial year. The most recent NOA should be used. Use the year the financial year ended, e.g. for 2019/20, code 2020
    • NRL - Not required to lodge an income tax return: Record the relevant financial year. This must be 1 of the previous 2 financial years, not the current financial year
    • EST - Estimate: If an estimate is used it needs to be for the current financial year and for an acceptable condition for using an estimate of income for the CSHC income test. Record the year that the current financial year is to end, for example, for 2020/21 code 2021
  • Income Type: Code 1 of the following:
    • TNA for a NOA and for customers who have been granted an ATO lodgement extension
    • NRL when a customer does not have a NOA because their income is either below the tax lodgement threshold or as a result of an ATO tax offset. NRL must not be used when the customer has income above the tax lodgement threshold, or when the customer has had TAX withheld from any income, in that financial year
    • EST for an estimate. If the customer was not required to lodge a tax return either because their income was below the tax free threshold or as a result of an ATO tax offset, it is not considered an estimate (EST), code as NRL

Adjusted Taxable Income (ATI) details declared by the customer (and partner) should pre-populate for online claims.

Income details are to be coded, or accepted as declared by the customer in the claim, Where the income declared by the customer may not result in the correct claim outcome, code or correct/edit the income details to reflect the income details provided in the verification of income documentation.

  • Taxable Income: Code 1 of the following:
    • Enter the amount on the NOA or for customers been granted an ATO lodgement extension an estimate of their taxable income. Note: when a customer provides an estimate, a superannuation withdrawal under the First Home Super Saver (FHSS) Scheme is not counted towards their taxable income
    • The current year estimate, or
    • The customer's income below the tax lodgement threshold

Customers who have provided an accepted estimate for the current financial year must also have their verified income that was over the income limit from either of the last 2 financial years recorded on the SHID screen. After the estimate has been recorded using the above coding, record the NOA using the date before the claim DOV, that is, DOV - 1 day.

Customers who are required to lodge a tax return but cannot provide income details from either of the previous 2 financial years because they have not yet lodged their tax return with the ATO and do not have an ATO lodgement extension, are not permitted to use a current year estimate for their income. Manually reject their CSHC claim. See Rejecting a claim for CSHC.

The following fields are optional and must only be completed if they are relevant to the customer claim:

  • Foreign Income: key the Australian dollar value
  • Foreign Pension coding – coded by International Services
  • Net Investment Loss: key net rental property or financial investment loss details
  • Employer Provided Benefit: key fringe benefits amount in excess of $1,000, e.g. if the customer received $3,200, only record $2,200
  • Reportable Super Contributions: key reportable employer or personal deductable superannuation contribution details
  • Complete the details as required

Once all information is updated press [Next].

If CSHC is rejected due to an income estimate for the current financial year being over the limit, they cannot lodge a second claim and provide previous financial year income details. The customer must provide a new estimate and reasons why their estimate has been changed. The revised estimate should only be accepted if details provided and the new estimate is reasonable in comparison with the previous estimate.

Note: the SHID screen includes a Deemed Income Amount: field. This is a protected field that cannot be updated manually. If a customer or partner has account-based income streams recorded that are subject to deeming, the claim activity will need to be taken through assessment to ensure the deemed income figure (if applicable) on the SHID screen is correct. To do this, go to the AR screen and then return to the SHID screen to view the updated Deemed Income Amount.
