Incapacitated customers claiming JSP/YA

Step

Action

1

Customer with temporary medical condition claims JSP or YA + Read more ...

  • Follow the process to determine the most suitable service offer on the basis of the customer's age, incapacity and job seeker or student status.
  • For customers identified as job seekers with a temporary incapacity who are:
    • able and willing to lodge a claim via online, go to Step 2
    • unable or unwilling to lodge a claim via online, go to Step 3
  • For information about:
    • exemption from mutual obligation requirements and medical evidence, go to Step 4
    • assessing a claim when medical evidence not supplied, go to Step 4
    • backdating start date of claim, go to Step 5

2

Online accounts + Read more ...

Online accounts are available for online claims for JSP and online claims for YA (job seeker). A correspondence nominee can submit a job seeker online claim on behalf of their principal.

Discuss this option with the customer and register where appropriate:

  • Customer must have an active self-service registration
  • Advise the customer the online claim needs to be submitted as soon as possible to be paid from the earliest date
  • The customer must have a contact phone number
  • Record a DOC on the customerâ€™s record
  • Advise the customer to lodge supporting documents via Upload documents or if not able to scan supporting document including medical certificate

Procedure ends here.

3

Claim completed using Assisted Customer Claim (ACC) + Read more ...

In most cases, Smart Centre staff:

  • record the contact on a DOC
  • book an appointment at the customer's local service centre to have the ACC completed. Refer to Office Locator for relevant JobSeeker Payment or Youth Allowance appointment type Short, Medium or Long

There are times where it is appropriate for Smart Centre staff to run the ACC for the customer to collect the information and submit the claim

At the scheduled interview, service centre Service Officer obtains customer's details via ACC for JSP or YAL (job seeker).

4

Exemption from their mutual obligation requirements and medical evidence + Read more ...

The customer's medical condition may mean they:

  • are exempt from mutual obligation requirements if they:
    • have a temporarily incapacity for all work, (unable to work at least 8 hours a week), or
    • are unable to undertake their normal work or study due to a medical condition, injury or illness, but have a job or study to return to. This includes customers with a concessional study load
  • have modified mutual obligation requirements if they are able to work at least 8 hours a week but less than 30 hours a week.

Medical evidence is needed or normal mutual obligation requirements may apply

Advise the customer to obtain approved medical evidence from their medical practitioner. Documents can be lodged using Upload Documents or taken to a service centre to be scanned to the record.

Customer's who may be granted:

  • an exemption from mutual obligations should lodge a Centrelink Medical Certificate (SU415 or SU683)
  • modified mutual obligation requirements, should lodge a Verification of Medical Conditions (SU684) form

Once the evidence is lodged, assess whether a temporary incapacity exemption can be granted.

If medical evidence is not lodged:

Customer has a job or study to return to:

  • medical evidence must be supplied before the claim can be assessed

Customer does not have a job or study to return to:

  • The JSP or YA new claim cannot be rejected for this reason alone. JSP or YA may still be granted, but the customer may be subject to normal mutual obligation requirements
  • For this reason, the request for a medical certificate should not be included in any list of documents or information sent to the customer as a requirement to determine the new claim. However, the customer should be strongly encouraged to provide this information as soon as possible

For staff finalising claims, if the medical certificate has not been scanned onto the customer's record and the customer:

  • has a job or study to return to:
    • reject the claim Failed to Supply Documents (FSD). Qualification for payment cannot be determined
  • does not have a job or study to return to:
    • Advise the customer verbally to provide the medical certificate within 15 days of the date they lodged their claim. It is needed to consider backdating the start date and/or an exemption from the mutual obligation requirements
    • If the customer does not provide the medical certificate within 15 days of the claim submitted date, and all other documentation has been provided, the claim is assessed without the medical information

5

Backdating start date of claim + Read more ...

If granting JSP or YA (incapacitated), backdating provisions can apply.

  • If a person claims within five weeks of their date of incapacity, they may have their payment backdated to the first day on which they were qualified for payment. Provisions apply:
    • The start date cannot be before the day they became incapacitated for work
    • Relevant waiting or exclusion periods apply
  • If a person claims more than five weeks after their date of incapacity, they may have their payment backdated to the first day on which they were qualified for payment. Provisions apply:
    • The start date cannot be backdated more than 4 weeks from the date of claim
    • The medical condition must the sole or principal cause of their failure to make the claim. If there is more than one possible cause for the delay, the main cause must be the medical condition
    • Relevant waiting or exclusion periods apply

See Calculating the start day - incapacitated customer for more information.
