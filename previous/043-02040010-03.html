Employer registration process for PPL

Table 1: Portions of this process can be completed by the Paid Parental Leave Employer Processing Team (PPLEPT) only.

Step

Action

1

Employer online registration + Read more ...

To access PPL Services in Business Hub:

  • employers with Australian based users must register online for a Provider Digital Access (PRODA) account
  • examples of Australian based users are personnel and Human Resources (HR) staff

Existing users registered in PRODA can access PPL Services through their myGovID.

To participate in the PPL scheme employers must have an Australian Business, confirmed by an Australian Business Number (ABN).

The Resources page has links to:

  • Business Hub website
  • PRODA website, and
  • Business Hub User Guides. These include:
    • Registering your organisation for Business Hub
    • Individual PRODA registration for Business Hub
    • Managing Personnel
    • Registering for Paid Parental Leave Services
    • Paid Parental Leave Services

If an Australian organisation has users who work overseas, these users may not be able to register for PRODA to access Business Hub. This is because they are unable to satisfy PRODA authentication requirements.

For example, an overseas payroll section working for an Australian organisation and the overseas user is unable to meet Australian identity checks.

Is the employer able to register for PRODA to access Business Hub online?

  • Yes, go to Step 2
  • No:
    • Non PPLEPT staff refer the employer to the PPL Employer Processing Team. Procedure ends here
    • PPLEPT staff go to Step 3

2

Employer registration for PRODA + Read more ...

If the employer does not have overseas users, procedure ends here.

  • If the employer has overseas users and they are unable to register for PRODA:
    • Non PPLEPT staff refer the employer to the PPL Employer Processing Team. Procedure ends here
    • PPLEPT staff, go to Step 4

3

Employer cannot register for PRODA to access Business Hub online + Read more ...

In some instances an employer may not be able to, or does not want to, register for online services. This may be due to the employer’s location being remote with limited or no internet access.

The PPLEPT helps the employer choose the registration services they need to manage their PPL employer obligations.

If an employer has:

  • Overseas based users - for example HR and personnel. If the employer has difficulties registering for PRODA to access Business Hub online, refer them to the:
    • PRODA helpdesk. The eBusiness staff will confirm the employer and overseas users have the necessary Australian identification requirements to register for PRODA and/or
    • User guides for more PRODA registration information. Resources has a link

Procedure ends here

  • Overseas users who cannot register for PRODA to access Business Hub, go to Step 4
  • Limited or no internet access - for example the employer is in a remote area. If they cannot do their PPL business online or refuse to use online services, go to Step 6

4

Centrelink Business Online Services (CBOS) registration for overseas users + Read more ...

If an associate of an organisation advises the PRODA helpdesk has confirmed their overseas users cannot register for PRODA:

  • The PPLEPT must tell the employer that access to CBOS for their overseas user will only be provided if the organisation has:
    • registered for PRODA and PPLS in Business Hub. Refer the employer to the Business Hub User Guides for registration, and
    • replied to the Request for information to access CBOS for PPL purposes email template and provided the required details

Once registered for Business Hub the organisation will have access to both systems and continue to use CBOS until overseas users are transitioned.

After the employer confirms the individual’s PRODA registration, the PPLEPT will:

  • ask for the associate of the organisation’s email address
  • advise the agency will email them requesting their contact details and those of their overseas users

When the email address is confirmed:

  • update the address in the system, if required
  • send the associate the email template - Request for information to access CBOS for PPL purposes from the PPLINFO mailbox. Resources has a link

Note: the template allows the employer to provide their own details and those of their overseas users who they nominate to act on behalf of the organisation. Do not change the template in any way. Always email the template from the PPLINFO mailbox.

DOC the employer record to reflect the discussion.

5

Reply from the organisation + Read more ...

Has the associate of the organisation replied to the email template providing their own and the nominated overseas user’s details?

  • Yes:
    • Upload and attach to the employer’s record the email template that lists the associate of the organisation and nominated overseas user’s details
    • Go to Step 6
  • No:
    • DOC the record
    • Identify whether Centrelink Assisted Dispute Resolution (CADR) process should commence. See Assisted dispute resolution for employer determination for Parental Leave Pay
    • Procedure ends here

6

Organisation online registration requirements and identifying or registering overseas users for CBOS + Read more ...

To determine if an organisation has registered for PRODA and PPL Services in Business Hub and to identify the organisation’s nominated users, navigate to:

  • Process Direct
  • select Business Hub Access Management
  • search using the PPE organisation CRN (10 characters with no spaces in between)
  • select the organisation
  • select Organisation Details
  • Paid Parental Leave displays under Business Function/s
  • select Personnel (to identify users linked to the organisation)\

Is the organisation registered for PRODA and PPL Services in Business Hub?

  • Yes, and it is:
    • already registered for CBOS, see Step 2 in Table 2
    • not registered for CBOS, the PPLEPT must register the organisation for CBOS. Go to Step 7
  • No, the employer is not able to or refuses to register for PRODA and PPL Services in Business Hub, go to Step 7

7

Search for the organisation + Read more ...

Access the Organisation List (OL) screen to search for any matches.

Are both PPE and PPX records for the organisation located?

  • Yes:
    • Type S beside the PPE organisation record and select Enter
    • The Organisation Details (ORD) screen will display
    • Check the details to ensure the correct record had been found. Go to Step 12
  • No:
    • If only PPE exists, create a new PPX record. Go to Step 8
    • If neither PPE nor PPX records exist, create a new PPE and PPX record. Go to Step 8

8

Create a new organisation record + Read more ...

Before creating a new organisation record, search for the organisation as set out in Step 7

Obtain the following details of the organisation:

  • Australian Business Number (ABN)
  • business name
  • address
  • authorised person contact details, and
  • payment destination details

To create a new PPE organisation record, in Environment B:

  • access the Organisation Search (ON) screen
  • enter the full name of the organisation in the Org Name: field
  • enter ADD in the Act: field
  • enter PPL in the Stakeholder Code: field
  • complete the Source: and DOR: fields
  • enter PPE in the Svc Rsns: fields
  • press [Enter]
  • a message should appear I046CU - No records found meeting the search criteria
  • enter AO in the Nxt: field
  • press [Enter]

To create a new PPX organisation record, in Environment B:

  • access the Organisation Search (ON) screen
  • enter the full name of the organisation in the Org Name: field
  • enter ADD in the Act: field
  • enter PPL in the Stakeholder Code: field
  • complete the Source: and DOR: fields
  • enter PPX in the Svc Rsns: fields
  • press [Enter]
  • enter Payment Destination Name
  • enter Payment Delivery Frequency
  • enter Pay Cycle details
  • enter Processing cut-off date
  • enter the employee’s next pay date and paid to date

9

Add organisation details + Read more ...

Access the Add Organisation (AO) screen and complete:

  • the BUS Name: and TRA name: fields if required
  • add the organisation’s physical and postal addresses
  • add the nominated overseas user’s details and their position from the email sent by the associate of the organisation. Services Officers must code the Position: field
  • add the phone number or follow up with organisation if it has not been provided
  • add the email details of the nominated overseas user
  • type Y in the Add Organisation (Y/N): field
  • press [Enter]

Additional organisation details + Read more ...

  • The Organisation Task Selector (OTS) screen will display
  • Review and if required update the following selections which will default by the system:
  • OSCS Organisation Contact
  • OSAS Agreement Details
  • OSBS Bank Details
  • Complete the Activity screen
  • Press [Enter] to progress through the screen flow
  • Update the Org Service Contact Details (OSCD) if there are additional contact details
  • The Org Service Agreement Summary (OSAS) screen should not require any updates - press [Enter]
  • The Org Service Bank Summary (OSBS) screen will require updating if payments are to be made to this organisation
  • Press [Enter] to the PPL Payment Destination Details (P1PD) screen
  • Press [Enter] to the Org Service Bank Details (OSBD) screen
  • Enter the organisation’s bank account details
  • Press [Enter] to the Report Output Method (OSRM) screen
  • Enter the Report type method (PDF)

11

Update bank details for an organisation + Read more ...

  • Access the Organisation Name Search (ON) screen
  • Record the organisation name in the Org name field, press [Enter]
  • On the Organisation List (OL) screen, select the PPX record for the organisation and press [Enter]
  • The Organisation Details (ORD) screen will display
  • Navigate to the PPL Org Task Selector (P1OTS) screen

  • Enter I in the Action: field, and press [Enter]

Check the organisation receives correspondence + Read more ...

Overseas based users registering for CBOS will get correspondence via Organisational Online Mail (OOM). See Step 2 in Table 2.

Ensure employers who are unable or refuse to register for business online services receive correspondence via mail. Navigate to and take action in Customer First:

  • Access the Organisation Contact Summary (OSCS) screen
  • Select Contact Name
  • Navigate to the Organisation Service Contact Details (OSCD) screen
  • Set the preferred method of contact to mail (MAI) instead of entering an email address
  • Check P1BL screen to ensure the record has updated correctly with the number of employees

Updating organisation roles in Registration and Access Management for Business (RAMB) for overseas user's only

Table 4: This process is for PPLEPT staff only when adding or updating details for users located overseas.

Step

Action

1

Request to add or update an overseas user + Read more ...

Most employers will do their PPL business with Services Australia through Business Hub.

Some Australian Businesses who have overseas users will need to update user or employer details in Registration and Access Management for Business (RAMB).

Requests to add an overseas based user to RAMB can be received by phone or in writing.

All overseas users must try to register with PRODA. Some overseas users may have Australian identification documents to confirm their identity in PRODA.

An overseas user will confirm with PRODA helpdesk - eBusiness they are not able to register. This is because they do not meet Australian Proof of Identity requirements.

Before creating a new user ID, the overseas user must provide details to pass an identity check.

Proof of identity can be obtained verbally, and can include:

  • Full name and their association with the business
  • Organisation's Customer Reference Number (CRN)
  • Australian Business Number (ABN)
  • Trading Name
  • Business address
  • Date of birth
  • Driver's licence or passport number
  • Contact details such as their email, phone number and country where they are located

If a new user ID is created or if the overseas user has an existing user ID:

  • check the user’s record in RAMB and
  • update if required:
  • enter International as the employee ID and
  • PPL as the service code

Note: including the word International will identify the user is located overseas and not Australian based

Has their identity and association with the business been confirmed?

  • Yes, go to Step 2
  • No, the user cannot be added. Do not create a user ID
    • Make 1 genuine attempt to contact the employer to advise of the information required to add a user. If unable to establish contact, send the 'Please contact us' approved email text. The 'Please contact us' email must only be used in approved circumstances
    • DOC the record
    • Close the request
    • Procedure ends here

Accessing and navigating in RAMB + Read more ...

Registering a new overseas contact in RAMB + Read more ...

To register a new overseas contact:

  • Select View/Update User under the Manage User section, re-enter the 4 character Organisation ID
  • Select Continue
  • Check the list of registered users
  • If the new overseas contact does not exist they must give details so their identity can be checked. This is so a user ID can be created. Proof of identity can be done verbally, and can include:
    • Full name and their association with the business
    • Organisation's Customers Reference Number (CRN)
    • Australian Business Number (ABN)
    • Trading Name
    • Business address
    • Date of birth
    • Driver's licence or passport number
    • Contact details such as email, phone number and the country where they are located
  • Once sufficient information has been provided:
    • select Register User under the Manage User section
  • Enter the user’s details:
    • First name
    • Last name
    • Employee ID (must enter the word International and use service code PPL), and
    • Contact details

Add the relevant roles in RAMB as requested by the overseas contact.

  • Select Continue
  • Tick the I Accept box

5

Providing a user ID and temporary password to the overseas contact + Read more ...

Give the overseas contact a user ID and temporary password verbally only if they:

  • have logged in to their computer, and
  • change their temporary password whilst on the phone for confirmation

Can the overseas user change the temporary password immediately?

  • Yes, give them the user ID and temporary password verbally. Stay on the phone until they confirm they have changed their temporary password. Go to Step 6
  • No, the user ID can be provided verbally but the password must be emailed
    • Use the approved Temporary Password email template on the Resources page
    • Send it from the PPLINFO mailbox to the email address provided in the CBOS registration
    • DOC the record. Do not record the password in the DOC
    • Create a new temporary password, if required.
    • Procedure ends here

Final check and DOC the record + Read more ...

  • The user’s full name

  • Driver’s licence number
  • Country of origin

Update the OSCS screen where required.

If the request was received via email:

  • Add the business' ABN to the Subject line of the email
  • Select Save icon in Outlook
  • File the email in the 'Additional contact' folder in the PPLEPT mailbox

If the organisation has requested more overseas users to have access, go back to Step 1.

3

Updating details for a registered overseas user for PPL - RAMB + Read more ...

To action a request to amend or add details to an already registered user, PPLEPT must do an identity check with the overseas user.

Proof of identity can include:

  • Full name and their association with the business
  • Organisation's Customers Reference Number (CRN)
  • Australian Business Number (ABN)
  • Trading name
  • Business address
  • Date of birth
  • Driver's licence or passport number
  • Contact details such as email, phone number and the country where they are located

If PPLEFT is not satisfied with the identity details:

  • try to contact the user. Use the known contact details already on record
    • if a phone attempt is unsuccessful, send the 'Please contact us' approved email text from the PPLINFO mailbox

Are identity requirements satisfied?

  • Yes:
    • follow normal procedures to identify the user
    • confirm the requested information and update overseas user details in RAMB
    • go to Step 4
  • No:
    • DOC the record and do not change or update user details
    • Procedure end here

4

  • Enter International in the employee ID field and PPL as the service code
  • Select an appropriate 'change reason' from the dropdown menu

Customer First

Procedure ends here.

Password reset - RAMB + Read more ...

Before issuing a new temporary password check:

  • the contact person's details against the user ID

    • Full name and their association with the business
    • Organisation's Customers Reference Number (CRN)
    • Australian Business Number (ABN)
    • Trading name
    • Business address
    • Date of birth
    • Driver's licence or passport number
    • Contact details such as email, phone number and the country where they are located

From the Organisation screen in RAMB:

  • Select View/Update User
  • Enter the Organisation ID to display all users or in the User Search box, enter the full user ID, if available. Select Continue

Select the user ID:

  • Enter International in the employee ID field
  • Select Reset Password
  • Tick the box Reset the eBusiness User's Password
  • Select Finalise
  • Email the temporary password using the approved Temporary Password email template on the Resources page

Go to Customer First and DOC the record, including the name of the person requesting the password reset.

Procedure ends here.

  • Select Finalise

Customer First
