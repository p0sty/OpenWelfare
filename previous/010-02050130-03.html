Determining eligibility and processing Additional Assistance claim

Table 1

Step

Action

1

Eligibility for Additional Assistance + Read more ...

Check if any of the following apply to the student/Australian Apprentice:

  • customer is eligible for ABSTUDY Living Allowance and it is unfair or harsh to limit assistance to ABSTUDY entitlement
  • customer's circumstances are unique or apply to a limited group. The grant of Additional Assistance will not result in inconsistent delivery of benefits
  • customer is:
    • changing their residential college before the end of their contract and has incurred additional accommodation charges, and
  • tried to negotiate removal or reduction of the charges with the college, and
  • is at risk of withdrawing from study due to financial hardship
  • student meets one of the criteria for students without parental support:
    • Orphanhood
    • Parents unable to exercise parental responsibilities
    • Unreasonable to Live at Home
    • Special Adult Status
    • Returning to an Indigenous community
    • Non-custodial parent granted under parenthood
    • Previous Lawful Custody (only secondary students aged 18 or over qualify under this criterion), and
    • All their ABSTUDY entitlements (apart from small residual payments) are paid direct to a board provider including during school vacations
  • student is single, and eligible for ABSTUDY:
  • under other Independence criteria applicable to single students - see Independence for ABSTUDY customers, or
  • as a student who was in State Care, and are no longer in State Care because of their age, and not in the care or custody of a parent or other person, and
  • all their ABSTUDY entitlements (apart from small residual payments) are paid direct to board provider. Students will need to demonstrate they have no access to financial support during the school vacations
  • the grant of Additional Assistance will overcome the hardship
  • the circumstances have not been caused or contributed to by action taken by the customer which may be regarded as negligent or imprudent, and there is no other Commonwealth or State Government agency or private organisation that is a more appropriate source of assistance
  • the customer meets the Away from Home or Away from Base criteria and has been impacted by an emergency or disaster situation

While general advice based on information provided by the customer can be given over the phone, the customer should be encouraged to test their eligibility, particularly in cases of discretionary decisions.

Do any of the above apply?

  • Yes, go to Step 2
  • No, go to Step 10

2

Additional Assistance for a limited group of customers + Read more ...

Check if any of the following apply to the customer:

  • The student is in the process of changing residential colleges before the end of contract and has incurred additional accommodation charges
  • the student meets one of the special independence criteria for students without parental support (mentioned in Step1), or
  • the student is single and eligible for ABSTUDY:
    • under other Independence criteria applicable to single students - see Independence for ABSTUDY customers, or
    • students who were in State Care, and they are no longer because of their age, and are currently not in the care or custody of a parent or other person
  • and all ABSTUDY entitlements (apart from small residual payments) are paid direct to a board provider including during school vacations. This means no access to financial support as would normally be provided by parent(s)/guardian(s) or other family members

Do any of the above apply?

  • Yes, see Step 1 in Table 2
  • No, go to Step 3

3

Emergency or disaster situation + Read more ...

Is the customer claiming additional assistance due to an emergency or disaster situation?

  • Yes, go to Step 5
  • No, go to Step 4

4

Student/Australian Apprentice lodge a claim + Read more ...

If the customer is claiming Additional Assistance:

  • Due to an emergency or disaster situation, go to Step 5
  • For any other reason, the customer will need to submit a claim verbally, ensure the following information recorded in a DOC on the customers record:
    • a description of circumstances relating to customer's study/training program which have caused financial difficulty, and
    • a statement of customer's or family's fortnightly income and expenditure, and
    • corroborative evidence of the appropriate situation, for example, from education institutions, boarding establishments, welfare agencies, and
    • an indication of the amount of assistance that is needed, go to Step 6

Services Australia may request evidence to support income and expenditure item claims.

5

Lodging a claim due to emergency or disaster impacts + Read more ...

If the customer is not eligible for Away from Home, or Away from Base entitlements, and impacted by an emergency or disaster situation, go to Step 9.

All other claims will be assessed by DSS on a case-by-case basis. When a claim for Additional Assistance (Emergency) is submitted, Service Officers must:

  • escalate the claim via email to ABSTUDY travel team. Record a Fast Note on the customer's record - select Auto text, use Students, ABSTUDY & Apprentices > Claims - ABSTUDY > Additional Assistance (emergency) requested
  • ensure the following information is included in both:
    • advising the claim has been accepted and escalated for assessment by DSS
    • a description of how the emergency or disaster situation has impacted the student’s or apprentice’s ability to undertake study or training
    • an indication of the amount of assistance that is needed
    • details of individual student/s or apprentice/s impacted (CRN, name, emergency contact)
    • details of the applicant
    • details of whom the assistance is to be paid to

Evidence is required to assess Additional Assistance (Emergency):

  • Confirmation of emergency or disaster event via verbal confirmation, or through reputable website
  • Confirmation of costs (such as: a statutory declaration, written confirmation from a Provider, or an invoice)

When DSS provide the outcome decision, go to Step 6.

6

Assessment outcome + Read more ...

Reject or approve the claim and advise the customer of the outcome.

  • If decision is to grant, go to Step 7
  • If decision is to reject, advise the customer of the decision and their review and appeal rights. Go to Step 10

7

Setting Additional Assistance amount + Read more ...

The amount of Additional Assistance will be set at the lesser of that amount required to:

  • meet the cost of the particular factor associated with the customer's study/training program which has contributed to financial difficulties, or
  • overcome the hardship, that is, address the imbalance between income and essential expenditure

8

Options in providing Additional Assistance + Read more ...

Additional Assistance may be provided in the form of:

  • a regular supplement to an allowance the customer is entitled to, or
  • a once only payment which will overcome the hardship

Additional Assistance is taxable if paid as a regular supplement, whereby if Additional Assistance payments are once only they are non-taxable.

9

Processing + Read more ...

Complete the following fields on the Supplementary Education (EPSP) screen:

  • Institution: field - should default from the Education Course Details (EDC) screen if there is only one current course, or as per the selection from the Education Course History (EDCH) screen
  • Event Date: field - record either the processing date, or the date the selected course is current. Note: any Additional Assistance regular supplement payments will start from this date
  • Payment Type: field - type 'ADD'
  • Reason: field - type either 'EXC' (exceptional circumstances) or 'ABL' (away from base Living Allowance)
  • Amount Claimed: field - input the amount of money student has claimed for
  • Amount Paid: field - record the amount which is to be paid to the customer
  • Payee: field - type 'CUS' (customer)
  • Claim Details: field - record details which are relevant to the customer's situation relating to the payment they are claiming. Note: this is an optional free text field
  • Frequency: field - record details to reflect the frequency the Additional Assistance payment is to be paid (that is, either 'ONE' (one off) or '2WE' (fortnightly)
  • End date: field - record the last date for '2WE' (fortnightly) payments (if relevant)

Ensure details are recorded in a DOC, including the relevant ABSTUDY policy reference. For Additional Assistance (emergency) Annotate the Additional Assistance (emergency) requested DOC regarding the claim for Additional Assistance (emergency) on the customers record with the following details:

  • Claim outcome (to be completed once DSS decision advised): Grant/Reject
  • If Rejected, reason for rejection:
    • Amount paid
    • Paid to
    • Q999 issued: Yes/No
    • APM: 99.2

Issue the customer, applicant, or third party notification of the decision via a Q999, ensure the appeal paragraph is included, and the ABSTUDY policy reference. See Resources for text.

Procedure ends here.

10

Not meeting criteria + Read more ...

Customer is not eligible for Additional Assistance.

Service Officers must:

  • Record decision and details in DOC, including the relevant ABSTUDY policy reference. Ensure details are recorded in a DOC, including the relevant ABSTUDY policy reference. For Additional Assistance (emergency) Annotate the Additional Assistance (emergency) requested DOC regarding the claim for Additional Assistance (emergency) on the customers record with the following details:
  • Claim outcome (to be completed once DSS decision advised): Grant/Reject
  • If Rejected, reason for rejection:
    • Amount paid
    • Paid to
    • Q999 issued: Yes/No
    • APM: 99.2

Contact the customer/applicant or third party to advise of the outcome or decision, including appeal rights.

Issue the customer, applicant or third party notification of the decision via a Q999, ensure the appeal paragraph is included, and the ABSTUDY policy reference. See Resources for text.

Procedure ends here.

Eligibility assessment of Additional Assistance for a limited group of customers

Table 2:

Step

Action

1

Identifying eligible students and whether they need to lodge a claim + Read more ...

A claim for Additional Assistance will not be required in all cases.

Students meeting one of the special independence criteria for students without parental support (listed in Step 1 Table 1), will be identified by the ABSTUDY Smart Centre. They will not need to lodge a claim. Confirmation that they have no access to financial support will be established by Centrelink records showing they meet one of those independence criteria and their ABSTUDY entitlements are paid direct to the board provider, including during school vacations.

Note: Students who continue to meet one of the special independence criteria without parental support can qualify for Additional Assistance during school vacations (subject to personal income test) while ABSTUDY Living Allowance and other boarding related payments are paid directly to their board provider. A social worker assessing continued eligibility of a student under the UTLAH criteria will look at any financial support they receive. If that support or assistance is determined as not continuous, reliable or meaningful and does not affect eligibility under the UTLAH criteria, the student's qualification for Additional Assistance is not affected assuming the other requirements are met.

Single students eligible for ABSTUDY under other independence criteria or those who were in State Care (but no longer in State Care only because of their age) will need to lodge a claim each year.

  • The claim needs to include confirmation they have no access to financial support from parent(s)/guardian(s), other family members or other sources during school vacations
  • For students formerly in State Care, their claim will need to show they have no access to financial support and are not in the care or custody of a parent or other person during school vacations

2

Amount of Additional Assistance payable + Read more ...

The amount of Additional Assistance for customers if approved, would be paid at an equivalent rate of the following entitlements:

  • Living Allowance,
  • Rent Assistance and/or
  • Remote Area Allowance

That they would otherwise be entitled to for the relevant vacation period(s) during the calendar year they are not residing at the boarding provider (school or hostel). Any residual payment of these allowances that is made to the customer will be deducted from the amount or rate of Additional Assistance payable.

3

Review eligible customers + Read more ...

Check whether the customers meet the eligibility conditions for Additional Assistance in Step 1 of Table 2.

4

Assessment outcome + Read more ...

Reject or approve the claim and advise the customer of the outcome.

  • If decision is to grant, go to Step 5
  • If decision is to reject, tell the customer of the decision and their review and appeal rights. Go to Step 8

5

Setting Additional Assistance amount + Read more ...

In the case of customers who meet one of the criteria in Step 1, their Additional Assistance rate would be paid at a rate equivalent of the following entitlements:

  • Living Allowance,
  • Rent Assistance,
  • Remote Area Allowance,
  • Energy Supplement and/or Pharmaceutical Allowance

This is the same rate they would otherwise be entitled to (that the boarding provider receives). See Away from home rate for ABSTUDY.

Additional Assistance rate is paid for relevant vacation period (s) during the calendar year they are not residing at the boarding school or hostel.

The payment period of Additional Assistance starts when the student has left the boarding provider's care, normally it is within the last few days of the term. The period will end on the day the student returns to the boarding provider. A beneficial interpretation is used in determining the period the student is not in the boarding providers care.

The rate of Additional Assistance would be subject to the personal income test, and income bank applicable to the Living Allowance, and any Rent Assistance, Remote Area Allowance and any other income affected boarding related components being paid to the board provider. See Personal Income Test and Income Bank for ABSTUDY, Austudy and Youth Allowance (YA) students and Australian Apprentices

Any residual payment of these allowances that is made to the student will be deducted from the amount or rate of Additional Assistance payable.

6

Options in providing Additional Assistance + Read more ...

In the case of students who meet one of the criteria in Step 1, their Additional Assistance rate would be paid:

  • in advance as single instalments for the period of the (three) short vacations that they are not boarding, and/or
  • fortnightly in arrears for the period of the long vacation (For example From 1 January to the commencement of boarding in term one and from the end of boarding in term four to 31 December)

Additional Assistance is taxable if paid as a regular supplement, whereby if Additional Assistance payments are once only they are non-taxable.

7

Processing + Read more ...

Complete the following fields on the Supplementary Education (EPSP) screen:

  • Institution: field - should default from the Education Course Details (EDC) screen if there is only one current course, or as per the selection from the Education Course History (EDCH) screen
  • Event Date: field - record either the processing date, or the date the selected course is current. Note: any Additional Assistance regular supplement payments will start from this date
  • Payment Type: field - type 'ADD'
  • Reason: field - type either 'EXC' (exceptional circumstances)
  • Amount Claimed: field - leave blank
  • Amount Paid: field - record the amount which is to be paid to the customer
  • Payee: field - type 'CUS' (customer)
  • Claim Details: field - record details which are relevant to the customer's situation relating to the payment they are claiming. Note: this is an optional free text field
  • Frequency: field - record details to reflect the frequency the Additional Assistance payment is to be paid (that is, either 'ONE' (one off) or '2WE' (fortnightly)
  • End date: field - record the last date for '2WE' (fortnightly) payments (if relevant)

For students who meet one of the criteria in Step 1, a review is to be set up to pay the Additional Assistance for the next vacation period after Payment on Advice has been checked to confirm student has resumed study.

Issue the customer, applicant, or third party notification of the decision vis a Q999, ensure the appeal paragraph is included, and the ABSTUDY policy reference. See Resources for text.

Go to Step 9.

8

Not meeting criteria + Read more ...

Customer is not considered to meet the criteria for being granted Additional Assistance.

Issue the customer, applicant, or third party notification of the decision via a Q999, ensure the appeal paragraph is included, and the ABSTUDY policy reference. See Resources for text.

While general advice based on information provided by the customer can be given over the phone, the customer should be encouraged to test their eligibility, particularly in cases of discretionary decisions.

9

Record decision + Read more ...

Record details of decision on a DOC.
