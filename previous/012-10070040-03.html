Assess the IP017 form

Table 1

Step

Action

1

Log into systems and check practice details + Read more ...

Log on to PIP Online

Assess the practice details on the form. The practice does not need to provide an ABN at Question 3.The practice must provide their:

  • Practice ID at Question 1
  • Practice name at Question 2, and
  • Full practice address at Question 4

If the above practice details or pages of the form are missing check the form version and the rest of the form for incomplete questions only, then go to Step 11

If the practice cannot be identified as the Practice details page is missing, mark the case as complete in Processing and National Demand Allocation (PaNDA), no further action is required.

Note: the practice must use all pages of the current form version.

2

Search for the practice + Read more ...

Search for the practice in PIP Online.

On the Practice Search screen:

  • Key the Practice ID
  • Select Search. The Search Results will show

If no Search Results show, the practice ID is incorrect. Check the form version and the rest of the form for incomplete questions only, and then go to Step 11.

Check if the practice name and address on the practice profile match the details on the form. An address variance may be accepted

If the details:

  • match, go to Step 3
  • do not match, check the company or trading name:
    • Select the Practice ID. The Practice Summary screen will show
    • Select Practice Details from the Main menu
    • Select Ownership Details from the drop down. The Ownership Details screen will show

Do these details match the details on the form?

  • Yes, go to Step 3
  • No, check the form version and the rest of the form for incomplete questions only, then go to Step 11

3

Practice status and form version + Read more ...

  • On the Practice Search screen, check the Search Results Status
  • Check the form version number on the bottom left corner of the form. Check if this is acceptable in the Practice Incentives forms list

Does the status show as Approved and is the form version acceptable?

  • Yes, go to Step 4
  • No, check the rest of the form to see if any other details are needed before returning the form to the practice. Go to Step 4

4

Current patient registration + Read more ...

Check patient registration at Question 5. The practice does not need to submit the IP017 if the patient has been registered online.

Has the patient been registered for the PIP IHI through PIP Online?

  • Yes, continue checking the form
  • No, or left blank, continue checking the form

Note: if the practice has answered ‘yes’, the form may be a duplicate or the patient registration may need to be updated.

5

Chronic disease or mental disorder and health check + Read more ...

Are Questions 6 and 7 complete?

If the answer to either question is no, the patient is not eligible to be registered for the IHI. Check the rest of the form for incomplete details before returning the form to the practice.

  • Yes, go to Step 6
  • No, check the rest of the form for incomplete details before returning the form to the practice

6

Patient details + Read more ...

Questions 8-11 are the patient details. Check that the details are complete:

  • Medicare card number at Question 8
  • Patient’s name at Question 9
  • Date of birth at Question 10
  • Gender at Question 11

If the Ref No on the Medicare card number is zero or not stated, check CDMS to find the active card.

If the above patient details are incomplete, see if the patient can be found using 2 of the fields to search CDMS:

  • If so, this is acceptable, go to Step 7
  • If the patient is not found, or details are incorrect, check the rest of the form to see if any other details are needed before returning the form to the practice. Go to Step 7

7

Patient descent + Read more ...

Is Question 12 complete?

  • Yes, and the answer is:
    • ‘yes’, go to Step 8
    • ‘no’, the patient is not eligible to be registered for the IHI. Check the rest of the form for incomplete details before returning the form to the practice
  • No, check the rest of the form to see if any other details are needed before returning the form to the practice. Go to Step 8

Note: the practice can select ‘Yes’ is for both Aboriginal and Torres Strait Islander Australians.

8

Patient consent + Read more ...

The patient must answer ‘yes’ to question 13 to be eligible for the IHI.

Has the patient answered yes to question 13?

  • Yes, go to Step 9
  • No, or not answered, check the rest of the form for incomplete details before returning the form to the practice

9

Check the patient declaration + Read more ...

Check the patient declaration details on the form at Question 16. There must be:

  • The patient or parent/guardian’s full name
    • If there are no name details, check the signature. Accept the form if the name can be identified from the signature
  • The patient or parent/guardian’s signature
    • for patients under 15 years, the declaration must be signed by a parent or guardian
  • the date of the signature. The date must
    • be clear and complete
    • not be blank, invalid, future dated or American style
    • be after the practice was registered for IHI
    • in the current registration period
  • patient or parent/guardian check box must be selected.

If any of the above details are missing, check the rest of the form for incomplete details before returning the form to the practice.

Note: the registration period is the timeframe practices have to register patients for the current year. This timeframe is from 1 November of the previous year to 31 December of the current year.

See the Resources page for more information about acceptable signatures.

10

Check the practice declaration + Read more ...

Check the practice declaration details on the form at Question 17. There must be:

  • the General Practitioner's (GP’s) full name
    • If the last name only has been provided, this is acceptable
    • If there are no name details, check the signature. Accept the form if the name can be identified from the signature
  • the signature of a GP
  • the GP’s date of signature. The date must:
    • be clear and complete
    • not be blank, invalid, future dated or American style
    • be after the practice was registered for IHI
    • not be before the registration period
  • the signature of at least one registered owner or authorised contact person
    Do not check these details if the practice ID is missing, incorrect or the practice cannot be identified
  • their full name
    • If the last name has been provided accept the form even if the first name has not been provided
    • If there are no name details, check the signature. Accept the form if the name can be identified from the signature
  • the owner or authorised contact person’s date of signature. The date:
    • must be clear and complete
    • must not be blank, invalid, future dated or American style
    • must be after the practice was registered for IHI
    • can be outside the current registration period

The registration period is the timeframe practices have to register patients for the current year. This timeframe is from 1 November of the previous year to 31 December of the current year.

Are the declaration details complete?

  • Yes, go to Table 2
  • No, go to Step 11

Note: if an authorised contact or owner is not registered on the profile, check the comments and associated documents for changes to the Authorised Contact Persons and/or practice ownership. If the signatory was authorised at the time of signing, the form can be accepted.

11

Returning an incomplete form + Read more ...

Return the form to the practice using the PIP IHI patient registration - more information is needed (Z1704) letter when the form needs more information to register the patient for the IHI

Return the form to the practice using the PIP IHI patient registration - patient/s not registered (Z1874) letter when the patients cannot be registered for the IHI

If the letter is being completed for more than one patient, include all relevant reasons in the letter.

If the practice ID is missing, incorrect or the practice cannot be identified, use the name and address on the form. If there is no name, use ‘Practice Manager’.

3

Search for the patient + Read more ...

Select Indigenous Health Incentive from the main menu.

Select Patient Search/Register/Update from the drop down. The Patient Registration - Search Patient screen will show.

If a warning message displays, check the Policy for registration warning messages located on the Resources page.

If the patient's Medicare card number is on the form, use Search criteria 1:

  • Key the below patient details:
    • Medicare card number
    • Individual Reference Number (IRN)
    • First name
  • Select “I declare that this search is for Indigenous Health Incentive patient registration related purposes only”
  • Select Search. The Patient Registration - Register/Update screen will show. Go to Step 4

If the patient’s Medicare card number is not on the form, or Search criteria 1 is not successful, use Search criteria 2:

  • Key the below patient details:
    • First Name
    • Select the tick box if the patient is legally known only by one name
    • Last Name
    • Date of Birth
  • Select “I declare that this search is for IHI patient registration related purposes only”
  • Select Search

4

Check current patient registration + Read more ...

If the patient does not have a current registration at the practice, the Patient Registration - Register/Update screen will show. Go to Step 5

If the patient has a current registration at the practice, the Patient Details table will show a status of Registered. The patient registration may need to be updated. Select Registration under Action and see Step 4 in Table 3 to check the registration details.

5

Register patient details + Read more ...

Under Answer the following questions select either Yes/No to all of the patient registration questions.

  • Does this patient have a chronic disease or mental disorder?
  • Has this patient had, or been offered, the appropriate health check?
  • Aboriginal and/or Torres Strait Islander Australian descent
    If the response is both Aboriginal and Torres Strait Islander Australian, select Yes for both options
  • Practice to be the usual care provider to look after their chronic disease or mental disorder

If the patient, parent or guardian answered No to any of the above questions, the patient is not eligible to participate in the PIP IHI.

6

Patient consent details and registration submission + Read more ...

Key the patient declaration date in the When did the patient sign the Patient Consent Form? field and select Submit Registration.

Question 7 will return. Select who signed the declaration from the drop down and select submit registration.

  • If the patient is under 15 years, Parent and Guardian will be the only available options
  • For patients over 15, the options are Patient, Parent or Guardian. A parent or guardian can provide consent for a patient over 15 years

If the form is being processed after 31 January and the patient consent date is between 1 November and 31 December of the previous year, see Patient Consent dates workaround on the Resources page.

If the patient is registered at another practice, a confirmation window will appear. Select yes to continue. Patients can be registered at multiple practices.

If registration is:

  • successful, the system will return a message confirming the patient registration
  • unsuccessful, an error message will show. Take action as detailed in the error message shown

7

Finalise the work item + Read more ...

Finalise the Work Item as Complete in PaNDA.

Procedure ends here.

Note: do not upload successful registration forms to the practice profile.

8

Return an incomplete form + Read more ...

Return the form to the practice using the PIP IHI patient registration - more information is needed (Z1704) letter when the form needs more details to register patients for the IHI an old or unacceptable form version has been submitted and more information is needed

Return the form to the practice using the PIP IHI and PBS Co-payment Measure patient registration - patient/s not registered (Z1874) letter when:

  • the patients cannot be registered for the IHI
  • an old or unacceptable version of the form has been submitted

To upload the letter and the form to the practice profile see Step 3 in the Upload associated documents in PIP and WIP – Practice Stream Online table.

Finalise the Work Item as Complete, Request rejected in PaNDA

See Associated documents and comments for PIP and WIP – Practice Stream for more information.

Search patient record + Read more ...

  • Select Indigenous Health Incentive from the main menu

There are 2 search criteria available to search for a patient

  • Search criteria 1: enter the patient Medicare number and IRN, agree to the declaration and select Search. The individual patient details will be returned

    • The declaration is the only mandatory field

4

Compare registration details + Read more ...

Compare the below current patient registration details to the details on the form:

  • Does the patient have a chronic disease or mental disorder?
  • Has the patient had, or been offered, the appropriate health check?
  • Aboriginal and/or Torres Strait Islander Australian descent
  • Practice to be the usual care provider to look after their chronic disease
  • Participation in the IHI

If the patient consent date on the form is after 31 October of the current year, the registration is not considered a duplicate. Process the registration form as this will then register the patient for the upcoming calendar year. Return to Step 5 in Table 1

Do the details match?

  • Yes, the record is a duplicate go to Step 6
  • No, the record needs updating, go to Step 5

5

Withdraw the patient to re-register them with new details + Read more ...

  • Close the Patient Registration Details window
  • The Patient List screen will show

Under Patient List:

  • Find the patient
  • Select Update from the Action column. The Patient Registration - Register/Update Patient screen will show

Under Withdraw Patient:

  • In the Patient withdrawal consent date field, key in the day before the consent date of the new registration form
  • Select As per patient request from the Withdrawal reason drop down
  • Select Submit Withdrawal. A message will show to confirm withdrawal of the record

The patient registration screen will show.

  • Re-register the patient. Use the new patient registration details on the new registration form
  • Key the new patient consent date as it is written on the new registration form

Do not delete the patient record as part of this process. Service Officers need to withdraw and re-register the patient as described above to maintain continuity of the record. If the original record has been entered with incorrect details (e.g. typographical or numeric error), escalate to the PSO for further investigation.

6

Finalise work item + Read more ...

If the registration is a duplicate, finalise the Work Item as Complete, Already Complete in PaNDA.

If the patient is withdrawn and re-registered, add a comment to the practice profile indicating the patient name and advising the patient was withdrawn from IHI and re-registered with updated details.

Finalise the work item as Complete in PaNDA.

Search and update a registration using the IHI Patient Search tab

Table 6

Step

Action

1

Search patient record + Read more ...

Select the IHI Patient Search tab in PIP Online.

There are 2 options for searching a patient:

  • Search criteria 1 searches on the patient’s Medicare number. Only records linked to the Medicare number will be returned. Using the IRN will limit the search further
  • Search criteria 2 allows multiple search options:
    • The declaration is the only mandatory field
    • Partial name entries with * at the end can be used for a wildcard search
    • Search results will vary depending on the search criteria used

Search criteria 2 will return a maximum of 50 records. Expanding the data entered will reduce the number of records returned.

Patient registrations will appear in a Search Results table. This table will show the PIP ID and practice name for each registration, the patient details, and the registration status.

2

Review the outcomes items for the patient + Read more ...

The assessment details screen will show the outcomes assessment period. Paid or Not Paid will show against each tier heading for the period.

Outcomes payments are calculated on the relevant Medicare items billed for the patient during a 12-month assessment period. The assessment period starts with the processing of an eligible tier 1 item.

To review the Medicare item numbers for each outcomes tier select Expand All or the arrow to the left of each Tier heading.

The list of Medicare items billed for the patient in the assessment period will show.

3

Confirm Tier 1 payment eligibility + Read more ...

To be eligible for a Tier 1 payment, the practice must have met the chronic disease and mental health management requirements for the patient. See the IHI guidelines on the Resources page for more information.

Has the practice billed the minimum eligible services?

  • Yes, the patient may be included in the tier 1 outcomes payment in the next payment quarter
  • No, the practice has not met the minimum requirements for the payment

Note: eligibility for Tier 1 outcome payments is determined by the date relevant MBS items are processed not the date of service. Eligible items will not show on the Assessment Details screen until processed by Medicare.

4

Confirm Tier 2 payment eligibility + Read more ...

To be eligible for a Tier 2 payment, the practice must have provided a minimum of 5 eligible Medicare services during the reference period.

Have at least 5 relevant MBS items been processed in the relevant assessment period?

  • Yes, the patient may be included in the tier 2 outcomes payment in the next payment quarter
  • No, the practice has not met the minimum requirement to trigger a payment
