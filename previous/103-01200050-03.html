Preparing for Initial Assessment interview

Table 1: This table outlines the steps required before proceeding with the Initial Assessment interview for SPaR Income Management.

Step

Action

1

Is the customer eligible for SPaR Income Management? + Read more ...

Customers will only be income managed under this measure if Services Australia, receives a notice from a Supporting People at Risk (SPaR) approved referring authority, requesting the customer to be placed on Income Management.

The Notice to Commence Income Management is displayed on the Income Management Summary screen under the 'Notice/Agreement' block. The twistie can be expanded to view the 'inactive' notice for 'Supporting People at Risk'.

When a customer is identified for Income Management:

  • they are advised in writing to contact the agency to arrange an Initial Assessment interview within the Income Management engagement period, and
  • a Work Item I053Q1 Income Management Assessment is required will be displayed on the Income Management Summary screen and Issues page

Service Officers not trained in Income Management - cold transfer the customer to the Income Management line, Indigenous Services line (if appropriate), or Multilingual Call (if appropriate). See the Resources page for a link to the National Transfer numbers. Procedure ends here.

Income Management trained Service Officers go to Step 2.

Go to Step 2.

2

Initial Assessment interview + Read more ...

Initial Assessment interviews are generally held within two weeks of receiving the Notice. It is preferable for this appointment to be conducted face-to-face.

If the interview cannot be held within 14 days, the Income Management Contact Officer (IMCO) will liaise with the approved referring authority, to determine if the interview can be conducted by phone or wait for the next face to face contact.

If conducted via phone interview, the Service Officer or IMCO must arrange a face to face interview as soon as possible to review the customer's allocations and ensure they understand the Income Management process.

To determine required action, select applicable customer circumstances:

  • customer has requested to book an appointment, go to Step 4
  • customer has requested to reschedule their appointment, go to Step 3
  • customer has attended interview, go to Step 6
  • customer has not attended a booked interview, go to Step 5

3

Rescheduling Initial Assessment interview + Read more ...

An Initial Assessment interview can be rescheduled once, only through a Smart Centre or Service Centre.

Is this the first time the appointment is being rescheduled?

  • Yes, and the customer:
    • requests the appointment be held at their treatment centre, or
    • is unable to attend a Service Centre
    • requests to attend an appointment with the Remote Servicing Team
    • Go to Step 4
  • Yes, and the appointment is to be held at a Service Centre, reschedule the appointment to a date within 14 days of the request. Tell the customer:
    • the time, date and location of their new appointment
    • if they fail to attend this appointment, Income Management will be applied
    • procedure ends here
  • No, go to Step 4

4

Referral to Income Management Contact Officer (IMCO) + Read more ...

Warm transfer the customer to the IMCO Zone Northern Australia when the customer:

  • requests to reschedule their appointment for a second time
  • lives in a remote location and is unable to attend a service centre
  • has not attended an appointment made with the Remote Servicing Team
  • requests to reschedule their appointment so it can occur at their place of treatment, or
  • does not have an appointment booked

See Resources page for a link to the Income Management Contact Officers (IMCO) intranet page.

Procedure ends here.

The IMCO will:

  • book/reschedule the appointment as appropriate (at a service centre, with the Remote Servicing Team or at the customer's place of treatment)
  • contact the referring authority to discuss whether a phone interview can be conducted (if appropriate)
  • tell the customer:
    • of the date, time and location of their new appointment
    • if they do not attend their interview their payments will be income managed

Procedure ends here.

5

Customer did not attend Initial Assessment interview + Read more ...

If a customer fails to attend an Initial Assessment interview, Auto Income Management is to be applied.

Was the appointment with a Remote Visiting Service?

  • Yes, reschedule the appointment to the next community visiting date. See Step 1 in Table 3
  • No, change the appointment status to 'Did Not Attend' via the Edit button in the DHS Appointment system (DAS). See Step 1 in Table 3

6

Service Officers not trained in Income Management + Read more ...

Initial Assessment interviews are only to be conducted by Service Officers trained in Income Management.

Is the Service Officer Income Management trained?

  • Yes, go to Step 7
  • No, follow this process, and if required:
    • Seek assistance from a specialised Service Officer (if available) or Service Support Officer, or
    • Contact the Technical Support Line (select Income Management option) for help

7

Has a referring authority contact person attended the interview? + Read more ...

  • Yes, the Service Officer must have a private discussion with the customer only, to confirm they agree to the approved referring authority contact person being present for the interview.
    Note: if the customer does not wish to have the contact person present, the contact person must not participate in the interview. Go to Step 8
  • No, go to Step 8

8

Confirm income support payment is current + Read more ...

Before starting the interview, check the customer's income support payment has not been suspended or cancelled.

  • Payment is suspended: Determine if:
    • payment is likely to be restored
    • there is no entitlement to the suspended payment, or
    • there is no entitlement to a payment
  • Payment is cancelled: Determine if:
    • payment is likely to be restored
    • the customer needs to re-claim or claim another payment type, or
    • there is no entitlement to a payment

Is the income support payment to be restored or re-granted?

  • Yes, go to Step 9
  • No, the customer cannot be placed on Income Management. The Income Management Notice will remain 'inactive' until it expires or is revoked by the referring authority. Tell the customer if they receive an eligible income support payment while an 'inactive' Notice is in place, they will become eligible for Income Management

Procedure ends here.

9

Interpreter requirements + Read more ...

If the customer requires an interpreter, only continue with the Initial Assessment when an interpreter is present. See Booking an interpreter for an appointment.

Can the Initial Assessment interview proceed?

  • Yes, see Step 1 in Table 2
  • No, book an interpreter for a later date. Procedure ends here

Step

Action

1

Check customer's nominee status + Read more ...

Go to the Nominee Link Summary (NOLS) screen to see if the customer has or is a payment nominee (NOP).

Note: correspondence nominee (NOC) arrangements do not exclude a customer from Income Management.

Is there a payment nominee arrangement in place?

  • Yes, go to Step 2
  • No, see Step 2 in Table 3

2

Review Nominee Arrangement + Read more ...

Customer referred for Income Management should have their payment nominee relationship reviewed. For more information, see Nominee arrangements under Income Management.

Service Officers are to review nominee arrangements to confirm the arrangement is appropriate and in the customer's best interests.

Note: having a NOC only does not exclude a customer from Income Management.

Is the payment nominee arrangement to continue?

  • Yes, go to Step 3
  • No, cancel the nominee arrangement. See Step 2 in Table 3

Note: if the customer is a payment nominee for another person, tell the customer the person for whom they are a payment nominee may now be eligible for Income Management. See Nominee arrangements under Income Management.

3

Does the customer have an excluded payment nominee? + Read more ...

  • Yes, the customer is not eligible for Income Management if they have an excluded payment nominee for Income Management purposes. Go to Step 4
  • No, tell the customer their payment nominee must be present for the Initial Assessment interview as they are responsible for managing their funds. Go to Step 5

4

Excluded payment nominee + Read more ...

  • Tell the customer their payments will not be income managed due to their payment nominee arrangement. However, if the arrangement changes, they may become eligible for Income Management
  • Create a DOC on the customer's record with the following information:
    • 'Customer has an excluded payment nominee and cannot be subject to Income Management'
  • Contact the local Income Management Contact Officer (IMCO) to tell of the nominee arrangements. The Resources page contains a link to Income Management Contact Officers intranet page
  • The IMCO will record the customer as ineligible and notify the referring authority of the outcome
  • The Income Management Notice will remain 'inactive' until the Notice expires or it is revoked by the referring authority. If the customer no longer has an excluded payment nominee while an 'inactive' Notice is in place, the customer will become eligible for Income Management
  • See Step 5 in Table 4

The IMCO will record the customer as ineligible for Income Management.

Launch the Supporting People at Risk IM assessment workflow.

On the Decision screen:

  • Is the customer ineligible for Supporting People at Risk Income Management? Select Yes
  • Select Excluded Payment Nominee from the dropdown menu
  • Continue workflow
  • Tick the Initial Assessment Work Item on the Expense Management Summary screen
  • Select Continue then Finalise

Procedure ends here.

5

Is the Payment nominee present? + Read more ...

  • Yes, see Step 2 in Table 3
  • No, book another appointment at a suitable time for the payment nominee as it is mandatory for the payment nominee to attend an Initial Assessment interview. Although it is not compulsory for the customer to attend, it is preferred to ensure they are aware of how Income Management will affect them

Note: Service Officers should consider whether it is more appropriate for an IMCO to book the appointment, due to the customer's location or any previously rescheduled appointments.

Procedure ends here.

Conducting the Initial Assessment interview

Table 3: This table contains steps to conduct the Initial Assessment interview.

Step

Action

1

Customer did not attend interview - apply Automatic Income Management + Read more ...

Launch the Supporting People at Risk IM assessment workflow.

  • In the Notice Section:
    • Receipt Date, Start Date, Period of Notice and End Date: fields will be pre-populated. Do not change
    • Status: change the field to 'Active'
    • Period of Notice and End date: these fields are protected, do not change
    • Organisation Contact details: These fields will be pre-populated. Do not change
  • Select Continue to navigate to the Income Management Initial Interview screen and complete the following:
    • Select 'Yes' to the question 'Does the customer need to be switched on Auto Income Management?'
  • In the Interview Details fields:
    • Logon ID interviewer: field defaults to the user. If this is not the Service Officer who is conducting the interview, change to the correct logon ID
    • Date: field will display as today's date. This should only be changed where the Initial Assessment interview was scheduled for a previous date and should be updated to the date the interview was conducted. Note: although the interview may be coded for a previous date, the date of effect will be the Entitlement Period End Date (EPED) following an assessment being completed
  • Select Continue

2

Conduct Initial Assessment interview + Read more ...

During the interview, the following must be covered:

  • Explain Income Management and how delivery of their payments will change
  • Discuss Income Management being put in place, as the agency received advice from an approved referring authority to place the customer on Income Management.
    Note: the agency is not advised why customers have been referred for Income Management. If customers have questions regarding this, refer them back to the referring authority
  • Explain how long Income Management will be applied to their payment. The end date of the Notice can be found on the Income Management summary screen under 'Notice/agreement'
  • Explain what happens if the customer moves out of an Income Management area
  • Discuss how income managed money is accessed. For more information, see Coding Income Management expenses
  • Explain what income managed money can be used for, that is, School Meals Program (if applicable), food, rent/housing, utilities, clothing, medical needs. Discuss the priorities applicable to the customer. See Determining a person's priority needs for Income Management
  • Explain what goods cannot be purchased from income managed funds, that is, prohibited goods and services - alcohol, home brew concentrate, home brew kits, tobacco products, pornographic material or gambling
  • Explain how money can be allocated to a BasicsCard:
    • Residual Payment method (default), or
    • They can choose the Partial Payment method, and
    • Immediate transfers (where Income Management funds are available)
  • If applicable, discuss changing existing Centrepay deductions to an income managed expense. Explain the impact of Income Management on any ongoing Centrepay deductions
  • Review and discuss any existing Rent Deduction Scheme payment and whether the customer would like to pay their State Housing Authority (SHA) rent from:
    • their income managed funds
    • their discretionary funds, or
    • a combination of both
  • Explain how money can be accessed for Income Management customers accessing funds while travelling
  • Explain the process for contacting the agency, including self-service, in regards to Income Management and BasicsCard. See Income Management and BasicsCard self service options. Offer to register the customer for self-service
  • Explain how common changes in circumstances may affect Income Management and the importance of notifying the agency of any changes in their expenses
  • Tell the customer of the impact on expense payments if income support payments are reduced due to earnings (for example, from employment or a payment penalty applied), and the priority needs payment order when there are insufficient funds to cover an expense
  • Explain how lump sum payments such as Advances, arrears and Family Tax Benefit (FTB) reconciliation payments are 100% income managed
  • When Income Management ends, all Income Management expenses will stop. The customer must make arrangements to continue paying these expenses themselves, or contact the agency to discuss alternative payment options (for example, Centrepay)
  • Tell the customer of their right to review Income Management decisions
    Note: if the customer wants to appeal the decision to be placed on Income Management, they must be referred back to the relevant approved referring authority

3

Discuss with customer how Priority Needs will be met + Read more ...

Regular expense allocations will be established to meet their priority needs and those of their family.

Discuss with the customer what ongoing expenses they have (for example, rent, food, utility bills etc.). If the expense meets a priority need, this can be paid from their income managed funds.

For more information, see Determining a person's priority needs for Income Management.

4

Supporting People at Risk Assessment IM assessment workflow + Read more ...

Launch the Supporting People at Risk IM assessment workflow.

In the Notice Section:

  • Receipt Date, Start Date, Period of Notice and End Date: fields will be pre-populated. Do not change
  • Status: change field to Active
  • Period of Notice and End date: fields are protected. Do not change
  • Contact: field will be pre-populated. Do not change
  • Select Continue to navigate to the Income Management Initial Interview screen
  • Interview Details
    • Does the customer need to be switched on Auto Income Management? this will default to No.
      Note: if the customer had been placed on auto Income Management previously, this must be changed to Manual when completing the Supporting People at Risk IM assessment workflow

The following fields have default values:

  • Logon ID interviewer: if this is not the Service Officer who is conducting the interview change to the correct logon ID
  • Date: displays as today's date. This should only be changed where the Initial Assessment interview was held on a previous date. Note: although the interview may be coded for a previous date, the date of effect will be the EPED following an assessment being completed
  • Channel: field defaults to CSC if interview conducted in Service Centre or will not display if interview conducted by Smart Centre
  • Location: field defaults to CSC if interview conducted in a service centre

Interpreter Requirements

Details of interpreter requirements must be recorded for interview purposes.

Where no information is recorded on the Other Contact Details (OCD) screen, exit the assessment workflow and update the OCD screen before proceeding with the interview. See Booking an interpreter for an appointment.

Customer's Spoken Language: defaults to the interpreter language recorded on the OCD screen.

  • Interpreter Required: defaults to:
    • Yes if the OCD screen indicates an interpreter is required
    • No if the interpreter language is recorded as English, and cannot be changed. If an interpreter is needed for the interview, the Service Officer must exit the workflow and update the OCD screen as appropriate

Is an interpreter being used? this will only present if the customer's language is not English. Select Yes, No, Not Required or Not Available from the dropdown menu as appropriate.

  • If Yes, no other information is required, select Continue and go to Step 5
  • If No, select the applicable reason from the dropdown menu:
    • Appointment not booked
    • Conflict of interest
    • Customer refused an interpreter
    • Customer wishes to use agent or family member
    • Deaf or hearing impaired
    • Services Australia does not have an interpreter who speaks the customer's language
  • If Not available is selected, select the applicable reason from the dropdown menu:
    • Interpreter did not attend interview
    • Interpreter service for that language is not available
    • Same gender interpreter not available
  • If Not required is selected, select the applicable reason from the dropdown menu:
    • No contact required
    • Speaks and understands English
  • More information: if an interpreter is not available, not being used or not required, additional information can be recorded in addition to the standard reasons selected above. For example, where 'Customer wishes to use agent or family member' is selected, the agent or family member's details can be recorded in this field

It is not recommended the interview proceed without an interpreter.

Confirm the customer has been read the Privacy Notice and the Third Party Disclosure Information by selecting the Yes radio button. This must be read and understood by the customer before proceeding.

Select Continue.

5

Continue workflow: IM Decision screen + Read more ...

The IM Decision screen will display the following:

  • Initiative: SPaR will be pre-populated and protected
  • Notice section will be pre-populated and protected
  • Complete the Income Management section as required:
    • Start Date: will be pre-populated and protected
    • End Date: will be pre-populated and protected
  • IM Ineligibility Reasons: check the box if customer is no longer eligible for Income Management. For further information on SPaR Income Management eligibility, see Eligibility for Supporting People at Risk (SPaR) Income Management
  • The Percentages section will default to 70% and is protected. If the customer is in receipt of ABSTUDY Living Allowance or ABSTUDY PES, it will default to 100%
  • Select Continue

6

Continue workflow: Expense Management Summary Screen + Read more ...

This page is a summary of the customer's payments and deductions and used to:

  • commence new Income Management expenses (regular or one-off)
  • vary or cease existing Income Management expenses
  • vary or cease existing Centrepay (voluntary) deductions
  • access the Priority Needs Expenses screen

7

Centrepay and Rent Deduction Scheme (RDS) + Read more ...

Does the customer have any Centrepay deductions or deductions to a State Housing Authority (SHA)?

Check the Voluntary Deduction for All Benefits on the Expense Management Summary screen to display deductions being paid from the customer's gross discretionary funds.

  • Yes, go to Step 8
  • No, see Step 1 in Table 4

8

Is the customer being placed on Auto Income Management? + Read more ...

  • Yes:
    • Centrepay deductions that meet a priority need can be changed to an Income Management expense, for example, accommodation, electricity
    • Check to see if the customer is paying a rent deduction to a State Housing Authority (SHA). This deduction can be changed to an Income Management deduction. Send a Fast Note to (Income Management Payment and Contact Team (IMPACT), asking that the Rent Deduction Scheme (RDS) deduction be paid from the customer's Income Management funds. Select Auto Text, use Income Management > Update > State Housing Authority deductions
    • Go to Step 9
  • No, go to Step 9

9

Changing Centrepay / Rent Deduction Scheme payments to Income Management expenses + Read more ...

Centrepay:

Discuss with the customer whether current Centrepay deductions should be:

  • converted to an Income Management expense
  • maintained as they are
  • varied, or
  • ceased

Search the Income Management Third Party Organisation (TPO) using Office Locator to determine the preferred payment methods for the TPO. Check for any payment notes that might impact converting the deduction from Centrepay to Income Management.

To change a Centrepay deduction to be paid in full or partially from income managed funds:

  • obtain any additional information, such as account details, from the Centrepay deduction to be cancelled or varied
  • action Centrepay deduction as appropriate:
    • cease the deduction where it will be paid in full from income managed funds, or
    • vary the deduction as discussed with the customer. For more information, see Starting, changing and cancelling a Centrepay deduction at the request of a customer
  • code new Income Management expense

Rent Deduction Scheme:

Discuss with the customer whether they would like their rent to continue being paid from discretionary funds, changed to an Income Management expense or a combination of both. For more information, see Actioning a request to update existing Rent Deduction Scheme (RDS) deduction for a customer who has commenced Income Management.

See Step 1 in Table 4.

Finalising the Initial Assessment interview

Table 4: This table outlines the steps for finalising an Initial Assessment interview.

Step

Action

1

Continue workflow: Code expenses + Read more ...

As per the priority needs discussion with the customer during the interview, set up the regular expenses to be paid from Income Management funds.

If there are offset payments note the EPED (as shown on Expense Management Summary screen) as offset payments affect how expenses are coded. The next EPED date will be the start date for each expense allocation.

For new expenses:

  • Select Add regular expenses from the Please select the expense you wish to add: dropdown list
  • For details on how to code expenses, see Coding Income Management expenses
  • For details on how to code School Meals Program expenses, see Payment of School Meals Program (SMP) expenses from Income Management funds or Centrepay

For customers who wish to pay their expenses via BasicsCard and choose the Partial Payment method, regular expenses must be set up once the assessment is finalised.

Note: if the customer is being changed from 'Auto' to 'Manual' Income Management, any existing Income Management expenses set up at the time Income Management commenced, can be viewed under the 'Regular Expenses' section. Expenses should be reviewed and updated as appropriate. For details on how to code changes to existing expenses, see Coding Income Management expenses.

Is the customer being placed on Auto Income Management?

  • Yes, when all expenses have been coded, go to Step 3
  • No, when all expenses have been added, click on the Priority Needs hyperlink, go to Step 2

2

Continue workflow: Priority Needs Discussion Result + Read more ...

The results of the priority needs discussion must be recorded on the Priority Needs Expenses screen when conducting an Initial Assessment interview.

As this would be the first discussion for a customer commencing Income Management, an answer of Yes or No must be given to each of the Priority Needs types. If priority needs have been recorded previously, two questions will be displayed:

  • Is a discussion being held? Select Yes as a discussion must be recorded as part of an Initial Assessment interview
  • Are there changes? Select Yes or No as appropriate. If the answer is Yes then a change must be made to either the priority expense/s need/s and/or how the expense/s is/are being met

Priority Needs Expense Category

Customer Response: field records how the customer will or will not meet the priority need. The field appears only after Yes or No is selected against the priority expense type, and appears against each expense type field when selected. When the field appears, a payment method type must be selected.

  • Yes, select the most appropriate value
  • No, select the appropriate value:
    • Not applicable to customer
    • Not met

If this is not the first discussion, Service Officers can view historical discussions under the Priority Needs Discussion History block. Past discussions can be selected and viewed but the details are protected.

Select Continue to be directed back to the Expense Management Summary screen.

3

Finalise workflow: Income Management Summary screen + Read more ...

If there are Work Items that need to be actioned, these will be displayed on the Income Management Summary screen in the Existing Work Items section.

If Work Items cannot be actioned within the workflow, investigate and action while the customer is present, once the workflow has been finalised. If the customer is not present the Work Items should be left for a Service Officer to action at the next customer contact. For more information on Work Items, see Income Management and BasicsCard Work Items.

Review the Income Management Summary screen to confirm:

  • Income Management has been correctly applied ('Qualification Details' block), and
  • payments and expenses are correctly reflected in the 'Fortnightly (14 days) balance details' block

Finalise the activity on the Income Management Summary page.

The workflow is complete. Manually record a DOC with the following information:

  • Customer has been advised and understands:
    • the Privacy Notice and Third Party Disclosure
    • how to request reviews of Income Management decisions
    • if applicable, the impact on Income Management if they are, or have a payment nominee
    • if applicable, why they have been transferred to this measure of Income Management and the impacts of transferring between measures
  • Any other information required under the guidelines for adding a DOC to a customer's record
  • Any other relevant information, for example, communication products given to the customer

Is the customer being placed on Auto Income Management?

  • Yes, procedure ends here
  • No, go to Step 4

4

Does the customer require a BasicsCard + Read more ...

  • Yes:
    • Issue the BasicsCard. See Initial offer or replacement of BasicsCard
    • Explain how funds will be transferred to the BasicsCard:
      • the Residual Payment method (default), or
      • they can choose the Partial Payment method. Set up any regular expenses to the BasicsCard as required. See Coding Income Management expenses
    • Go to Step 5
  • No, go to Step 5

5

Does the customer require any specialist referrals, for example, social worker, Multicultural Service officer, etc.? + Read more ...

  • Yes, refer customer to appropriate specialist as per current business process. For more information, see Specialist officers and other internal supports for customers. Go to Step 6
  • No, go to Step 6

6

Finalise interview + Read more ...

Provide the customer with details of their expense allocations and any other relevant information about Income Management.

Tell the customer they should contact the agency if they have any problems with allocations or wish to change them.
