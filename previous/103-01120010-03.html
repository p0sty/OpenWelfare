For Income Management Contact Officers (IMCOs) only.

This page contains information to assist the Income Management Contact Officer (IMCO), when a request is received from a State or Territory Child Protection Authority via the Unified Government Gateway (UGG), to commence income management of a customer.

On this page:

Viewing Disclosure of Information and Expense Allocations

Processing request for Income Management from Child Protection Authority

Table 1: This table describes how an IMCO is to action a request from Child Protection Authority to commence Income Management on a mutual customer.

Step

Action

1

Request received + Read more ...

Requests have been submitted by a State or Territory Child Protection Authority via the Unified Government Gateway (UGG).

The Income Management Contact Officer (IMCO) can view all requests sent by a Child Protection Authority by viewing the Third Party Information Summary Organisation (TPISO) screen in Customer First.

Navigate to the TPISO screen.

Note: the TPISO screen must only be viewed in Environment B. If an attempt is made to view via any other system or environment, the information displayed may be incomplete or incorrect.

2

Searching for requests + Read more ...

Searching the TPISO screen - B Environment

The TPISO screen shows all recent requests submitted by the organisation through the UGG.

To search for requests, complete the following fields:

  • Application Type: Select Welfare Payment Reform
  • Business Function: Select Child Protection
  • Information Type: Select the desired search function
  • Status: Select the desired search function, that is, Pending, Processed, Unprocessed etc

or:

  • Organisation CRN: Input appropriate Organisation Customer Reference Number (CRN) each Child Protection Authority district has an assigned CRN
  • Date Range: Input desired date range

Click Search

3

Actioning Child Protection Authority requests + Read more ...

To view any Child Protection Authority request, click on the hypertext under the Information Type heading, to see detailed information.

  • To action an unprocessed Mutual Customer search, go to Step 4
  • To action an unprocessed Disclosure of Information (DOI) eligibility check, See Step 1 in the Disclosure of Information via UGG table
  • To action an unprocessed or auto processed Notice of Commencement of Income Management, See Step 1 in the Notice to Commence Income Management table
  • To action an unprocessed or auto processed Revocation of Income Management, See Step 1 in the Notice to Revoke Income Management table
  • To action a referral for Voluntary Income Management, See Step 1 in the Voluntary Income Management table
  • To view DOIs that have been submitted by the Child Protection Authorities, See Step 1 in the Viewing Disclosure of Information and Expense Allocations table
  • To view Expense allocation requests made by the Child Protection Authorities, See Step 2 in the Viewing Disclosure of Information and Expense Allocation table

4

Undertake a Find Mutual Customer Search + Read more ...

In some cases when a Child Protection Authority undertakes a Mutual Customer search it results in a 'no' match. The Child Protection Authority case worker will then refer it to Services Australia for follow up.

The IMCO will need to investigate these requests and provide a response to the Child Protection Authority.

Mutual Customer searches that have returned a 'no' match will display as 'unprocessed'.

To view all unprocessed Mutual Customer searches submitted by Child Protection Authorities complete the following:

Navigate to the TPISO screen.

Code the following fields:

  • Information Type: 'Find Mutual Customer'
  • Date Range: set to preferred period of time
  • Status: 'Unprocessed'

Click Search and a list of results will be displayed for this period.

5

Actioning an unprocessed Mutual Customer Search + Read more ...

Click on the hyperlink Find Mutual Customer text under the Information Type column for the unmatched customer. This will load the Find Mutual Customer screen.

This screen displays the information entered into the UGG by the Child Protection Authority to locate a customer. Take note of the Mutual Client Summary details on the screen.

  • Conduct an Index Search in Customer First for this person, for example, Name, Date of Birth (DOB)
  • If there is no match in Customer First, a Department of Veterans' Affairs (DVA) search must be undertaken. To do this, contact the Centrelink DVA Clearance Team (SCS). see the Resources page for a link to the homepage

Has the customer been identified via the Index Search or the DVA search?

  • Yes, go to Step 7
  • No, go to Step 6

6

Advice outcome sent to Child Protection Authority - No match + Read more ...

In the Record Outcome section of the Find Mutual Customer screen:

  • select 'no' as the response to the question 'Did you uniquely match the Customer'?
  • add any additional notes
  • tick the Declaration box and press Submit

An 'Advice Outcome' will be automatically sent to the Child Protection Authority via their daily reports, indicating that the customer has not been matched to a Services Australia or DVA record.

Procedure ends here.

7

Advice outcome sent to Child Protection authority - match + Read more ...

In the Record Outcome section of the Find Mutual Customer screen:

  • select 'yes' as the response to the question 'Did you uniquely match the Customer'?
  • type in the CRN, and provide any additional notes.
  • tick the Declaration box and press Submit

An 'Advice Outcome" will be automatically sent to the Child Protection Authority via their daily reports, stating that the details provided have been matched to a Services Australia or DVA record.

The IMCO will then need to complete a Disclosure of Information (DOI) to be sent to the Child Protection Authority via the UGG.

Notice to Commence Income Management

Table 3: This table describes how an Income Management Contact Officer (IMCO) is to action a notice to commence Income Management.

Step

Action

1

Actioning a Notice to Commence Income Management + Read more ...

On receipt of a Notice to Commence Income Management or the Cashless Debit Card the system will automatically create an 'inactive notice' on the customer record and place an automatic DOC on the record stating a notice has been received. When this occurs, the Status of the notice in the UGG will set to 'auto processing'.

If Auto Processing fails and an 'inactive' notice cannot be created, the Status of the notice in the UGG will be set to 'unprocessed' and a reason for rejection will be displayed advising why an 'inactive' notice could not be created.

In circumstances where Auto Processing fails, manual processing must be completed:

  • search the TPISO screen for an 'unprocessed' Notice to Commence Income Management
  • launch the Child Protection Assessment workflow and record an 'inactive' notice on the Child Protection Notice details screen
  • if the customer is eligible for Child Protection Income Management, schedule an Initial Assessment interview for the customer. Update the status of the Notice to Commence Income Management or the Cashless Debit Card via the TPISO screen from 'unprocessed' to â€˜pending'
  • if the customer is not eligible for Child Protection Income Management, update the status of the Notice to Commence Income Management via the TPISO screen from 'unprocessed' to 'processed' and record the outcome

To record the outcome of the Income Management decision, go to Step 2.

2

Record outcome of the Income Management decision + Read more ...

Do not record outcomes until a formal decision about Income Management has been made (i.e. customer has been switched on or a decision has been made not to income manage the customer (for example, not eligible)).

Once the decision has been made, record the outcome which will be one of the following:

  • No IM switch on (customer not eligible)
  • IM switch on (manual)
  • IM switch on (auto)

Access the TPISO screen again and select the relevant notice from the list of search results. This will display the Income Management notice screen where the Record Outcomes section of the notice is to be completed.

Note: the Third Party Information Summary (TPIS) screen can be accessed from the customer's record. Any activity that appears on the TPISO screen that is relevant to a specific customer will be displayed on that customer's TPIS screen.

Has the customer been placed on Income Management?

  • Yes, go to Step 3
  • No, go to Step 4

3

Customer has been placed on Income Management + Read more ...

The customer's Income Management status will be automatically updated on the TPISO screen, and the notice changed from 'unprocessed' to 'pending' (Auto IM) or 'processed' (Manual IM) in the following circumstances:

  • When the customer is placed on Auto Income Management by the system, or
  • When the customer attends their Initial Assessment interview and the Child Protection Assessment workflow is run placing the customer on Manual Income Management.

This automated processing will send an advice outcome to the relevant State or Territory Child Protection Authority via the daily report advising of the change in Income Management status.

If the automated process is not successful the 'unprocessed' notice will need to be manually updated on the TPISO screen.

Scroll to the bottom of the Notice to the Record Outcome section.

Answer the question to 'Has the Client been switched on to Income Management under the Child Protection measure'?

If Yes, a sub question appears 'Client was switched onto Income Management select Auto or manual'.

  • Auto: customer has not yet attended their Initial Assessment interview so they have been automatically switched onto Child Protection Income Management
  • Manual: customer has attended their Initial Assessment interview and been switched on

This sends an Income Management Commencement advice outcome to the Child Protection Authority advising of the decision to switch the customer on to Income Management.

Procedure ends here.

4

Customer has not been placed on Income Management + Read more ...

Select the 'unprocessed' notice from the TPISO screen.

Scroll to the bottom of the notice to the Record outcome section.

Answer the question to 'Has the Client been switched on to Income Management under the Child Protection measure'?

If 'No' is selected, a sub question appears 'Why has no action been taken to switch the Client on to Income Management under the Child Protection measure'?

Select the most appropriate reason from drop down list:

  • not income managed
  • not receiving a trigger payment
  • excluded Nominee
  • income managed by another authority
  • income managed - not currently active
  • other type of Income Management

Click Submit

This sends an advice outcome to the Child Protection Authority via the daily report advising that the customer was not switched on to Income Management and the selected reason.

Procedure ends here.

Extending period of income management

Table 4: This table describes the steps that are to be followed by IMCO when a request is received from Child Protection Authority to extend the period of income management of a mutual customer.

Step

Action

1

A Continuation Notice + Read more ...

A Child Protection Authority may lodge a new Notice to Commence Income Management or the Cashless Debit Card to extend the period of Income Management beyond the current notice expiry date, or a notice may be received from a different Child Protection authority.

Is the notice from the same Child Protection Authority who lodged the current active notice?

  • Yes, go to Step 2
  • No, go to Step 3

2

Notice from the same Child Protection Authority + Read more ...

A Continuation notice will be automatically created when a customer with an active notice receives a new Notice to Commence Income Management or the Cashless Debit Card within 60 days prior to the current notice's end date.

The new notice will have an inactive status as there can only be one active notice on a customer's record at any time.

When the customer's current notice expires, the continuation notice will automatically become active and Income Management will continue uninterrupted.

A message will display under Description on the TPISO screen indicating the results of the automated processing, including where the automated process was unsuccessful in which case manual processing is required.

Was the automated processing of the continuation notice successful?

  • Yes, book an appointment for the customer to discuss the extension of Income Management and review their circumstances. Procedure ends here
  • No, manually record the continuation notice, go to Step 4

3

Notice from a different Child Protection Authority + Read more ...

If a new Notice to Commence Income Management or the Cashless Debit Card is received from a different Child Protection Authority than the existing active notice, an inactive notice will be automatically created on the customer's record (subject to usual business rules being satisfied).

A message will display under Description on the TPISO screen indicating the results of the automated processing, including where the automated process was unsuccessful and manual processing is required.

Note: if the notice includes an Unrestricted Cash Payment (UCP) service offer, the customer will be identified as eligible for the service offer in the automated process.

If the automated process is not successful, it will have a Status of 'unprocessed' on the TPISO screen and an inactive notice will need to be created on the customer's record using the Child Protection assessment workflow.

Was the automated processing of the continuation notice successful?

  • Yes, book an appointment for the customer to discuss the continuation of Income Management as referred by the new Child Protection Authority and to review their circumstances. Procedure ends here
  • No, manually record the continuation notice. Go to Step 4

4

Manually coding Commencement/Continuation Notice + Read more ...

In workflow open the Child Protection Assessment workflow.

On the Notice screen complete the following fields:

  • Receipt Date: will default to today's Date, do not change
  • Period of Notice: select 3, 6, 9 or 12 months. (This information will be provided on the notice)
  • Status: Inactive
  • Start Date: will default and is protected, do not change
  • End date: will be calculated from period of notice (field is protected and cannot be changed)
  • State/Territory: Select appropriate state or territory
  • Organisation/Office: Select appropriate agency/office from dropdown list
  • Contact: select appropriate contact from dropdown list
  • Expense Priorities: replicate priority needs ranked by the case worker in the notice received

Click 'Continue'

Service Offer screen

Is the customer present?

  • select 'No' if the customer is not present
  • select 'Yes' if the customer is present and discuss available service offers. (Note: there would be limited circumstances in which the IMCO would be conducting the Initial Assessment interview). See Initial Assessment interview for Child Protection Income Management

Is customer eligible for the 20% Unrestricted Cash Payment (UCP)? (Refer to the advice provided on the notice).

  • select 'Yes' or 'No' depending on the information provided in the notice

Click 'Continue'

Finalise the assessment.

In the 'Annotated Document Text' field, include any additional information provided on the notice.

Note: if the case worker provided information in the notice that refers to reasons why the customer was referred for Income Management, the IMCO should contact the case worker to request the notice be re-sent without this information.

Click 'Finalise' to complete the activity.

The customer's Income Management status will be automatically updated on the TPISO screen, and the notice changed from 'unprocessed' to 'processed'. An advice outcome will be automatically sent to the relevant State or Territory Child Protection Authority via the daily report.

Notice to Revoke Income Management

Table 5: This table describes how an Income Management Contact Officer (IMCO) is to action a request to revoke Income Management.

Step

Action

1

Actioning a request for Revocation of Income Management + Read more ...

When a State or Territory Child Protection Authority lodges a Revocation of Income Management or Cashless Debit Card notice (Revoke notice) via the UGG the system will automatically send the details to Centrelink system and the Status of the notice will be set to 'auto processing'. Depending on the individual circumstances, the system will perform one of the following actions:

  • automatically revoke any inactive notice/s on the customer's record
  • automatically create a 28 day review and Work Item for any currently active notice
  • create a Work Item for any revoke notices that are received for a currently active notice that is ending in less than or equal to 28 days

If the Auto Processing fails, the revoke notice will be set to 'unprocessed' and the rejection reason will be displayed advising why the notice could not be revoked.

In circumstances where the revoke notice is not automatically processed , the revoke notice must be manually recorded:

  • search the TPISO screen for unprocessed Revocation of Income Management or Cashless Debit Card notices, and select the relevant notice from the search results
  • Income Management Contact Officer (IMCO) will run the Child Protection Assessment workflow and update the 'active' notice to 'revoked'. This will end the customer's Child Protection Income Management and, if applicable will transfer the customer to another Income Management measure. If the customer is not eligible for another compulsory measure of Income Management, customers should be offered Voluntary Income Management if appropriate

Revoking the customer's notice via the Child Protection Assessment workflow will automatically update the Status of the notice on the TPISO screen to 'processed'. Was the automated processing of the revoke notice successful?

  • Yes, procedure ends here
  • No, manually update the revoke notice in the UGG. Go to Step 2

2

Manually updating the revoke notice + Read more ...

Access the Child Protection Assessment workflow and select the current active notice. Select Revoke and complete the workflow.

No action should be taken to record the outcome of a Revocation notice until a formal decision has been made about revocation of Income Management (that is, the revocation has been coded on the customer's record).

Search the TPISO screen for unprocessed revocations of Income Management. Select the relevant notice from the search results.

Scroll down to the Record Outcome section of the notice and answer the question 'Has the client's Income Management been revoked?' with 'Yes'.

Tick the Declaration

This will send an advice outcome to the Child Protection Authority advising that Income Management has been revoked.

Procedure ends here.

Voluntary Income Management referrals

Table 6: This table describes how an Income Management Contact Officer (IMCO) is to action a Voluntary Income Management (VIM) referral.

Step

Action

1

Voluntary Income Management (VIM) Referrals + Read more ...

To view Voluntary Income Management (VIM) referrals submitted by Child Protection Authorities, navigate to the TPISO screen.

Code the following fields:

  • Information Type: select 'Child Protection Voluntary Income Management'
  • Date Range: set to preferred period of time
  • Status: 'Unprocessed'

Take note of whether customer consent has been provided

Note: for 'unmatched' customers indicate whether unique matching has been successful. If yes, enter the customer's Customer Reference Number (CRN).

Any additional comments can be entered into the notes text box.

If customer consent has been provided, this must be recorded on the customer's record.

In the customer's record select the IM Third Party Consent Details from the IM menu.

On the Consent Details screen code the following:

  • Organisation: Select radio button for the relevant state or territory Child Protection Authority
  • Does customer consent to the following information being exchanged with the organisation? Select 'Yes' radio button
  • What does the consent cover? Tick the appropriate options

Click continue to finalise the workflow

Return to the TPISO screen and locate the request. Tick the checkbox declaring 'I confirm that I have completed the actions required to process this request'.

Any additional comments can be entered into the notes text box.

Note: do not organise an initial interview for the customer as the referral is voluntary. Customers are encouraged by the Child Protection Authority to attend. Services Australia will not actively follow up with the customer unless the customer initiates the contact.
