Applicant for permanent residency

Table 1

Step

Action

1

Documents needed to enrol + Read more ...

Customers must provide the following documents. They must provide these for each person on the enrolment form:

  • current passport or Immicard, and
  • proof from the Department of Home Affairs that they have applied for permanent residency
    Note: this can be from a Migration Agent, and must have details about the category of visa subclass, application ID and date of lodgement Service Officers must:
  • complete a VEVO check, and
  • record all documents in CDMS when registering a customer's Medicare entitlement

If the Medicare enrolment form (MS004) is incomplete or documents are missing:

  • attempt to contact the customer once to advise what is required, and
  • resubmit their complete application again, if applicable

If contact is unsuccessful, issue the appropriate standard letter including the customer’s application and documents.

2

Document Verification Service (DVS) + Read more ...

  • To verify documents for enrolments, see the Document Verification Service (DVS) table
  • If appropriate, see the Customer fails DVS check and attends service centre with issued letter table

3

Register the customer and enter the entitlement details + Read more ...

Register the customer for Medicare. Enter their entitlement details as per the below.

To enrol an applicant for permanent residency (including subclass 191):

  • Entitlement Type - conditional migrant
  • Country of relevance - this is the previous country of residence before arriving in Australia
  • Start Date - use the date the customer:
    • lodged their visa application (onshore)
    • entered Australia to live (offshore)
  • End Date - 12 months from the start date
  • End reason - entitlement ceased

Note: the applicant does not have permission to work if the entitlement type shows a dash (–) or non alpha on the CDMS link. Go to Step 4.

4

Applicant does not have permission to work + Read more ...

If they do not have work rights they must show proof of one of the below:

  • a relationship with a parent, de facto, spouse, or child that is:
    • an Australian citizen
    • permanent resident of Australia, or
    • New Zealand citizen that lives in Australia

Enter the relationship documents in the Consumer Directory Maintenance System (CDMS).

Note: if only one person in the family has work rights, only that person will be able to enrol in Medicare.

Eligibility is not based on the customer's passport or citizenship. It is based on the country they were living in before arriving in Australia. See Document types for Medicare.

5

Change of name on an interim Medicare card + Read more ...

Customers must provide their change of name documents.

For an initial enrolment:

  • Search for the name recorded with the Department of Home Affairs (Home Affairs). This will help match the customer to Home Affairs
  • Link the customer to their Home Affairs record. See Table 6
  • When the link is complete, change the customer's name

Any changes from Home Affairs will now update in the Medicare system.

If there is no Home Affairs link, the applicant's name may be changed once the enrolment is affected.

For a previously enrolled customer:

  • Change the customer's name if they are currently enrolled under the name held by the Home Affairs

A referee may be a:

  • minister of religion

Appeals to a visa refusal

Table 3

Step

Action

1

Appeals to a visa refusal + Read more ...

A customer may have their visa application refused by the Department of Home Affairs. They can appeal this decision with the Administrative Appeals Tribunal (AAT).

They are eligible for Medicare while appealing the decision.

Has the customer lodged an appeal with the AAT for their visa refusal?

  • Yes, go to Step 2
  • No, the customer is ineligible for Medicare entitlements. Procedure ends here

2

Documents required + Read more ...

To re-enrol, customers will need to provide:

  • proof of an appeal. They must show one of the below:
    • Administrative Appeals Tribunal (AAT) document
    • Federal Court or High Court document
    • Ministerial Intervention document or Visa Entitlement Verification Online (VEVO) to confirm Ministerial Intervention has begun
      Note: Step 3 has the proof of an appeal requirements
  • a valid visa with permission to work via one of the below:
    • in their passport
    • in the form of a travel document
    • an email or a letter from the Department of Home Affairs
  • an identification document via one of the below:
    • passport
    • travel document
    • Immicard
    • Current Australian driver’s licence (only if the passport, travel document or Immicard was previously recorded in CDMS as the initial enrolment identification)

Service Officers must do a VEVO check. Customers must hold a bridging visa with work rights. If the customer does not have work rights, they must supply relationship documents to be eligible for Medicare.

Expired foreign passport

To extend a Medicare card when a visa is refused, the agency can accept an expired foreign passport up to a maximum of 2 years from the expiry date providing this passport number:

  • was recorded in CDMS at the initial enrolment, and
  • can be verified in VEVO when they present

Note: an expired Immi Card is still a valid identity document. Do not accept an expired Immi Card as evidence of identity for initial enrolments.

3

Proof of the appeal documents + Read more ...

Administrative Appeals Tribunal (AAT) documents

The letter must be dated within the last 2 years of date of processing.

If it is over 2 years old, ask the customer to provide a current letter. It must show that their appeal is still ongoing.

Federal Court or High Court documents

Federal Court documents must have one of the below:

  • a court hearing date, or
  • advice that a court hearing date will be provided in the future

This document must identify the customer by name. If it only has a pseudonym, ask the customer to provide an additional letter that has:

  • their name, and
  • that they are referred to by the pseudonym

Ministerial Intervention documents

Ministerial Intervention documents must have one of the below:

  • Document confirming Ministerial Intervention is in place, or
  • VEVO confirming Ministerial Intervention has begun

Do not reject or refuse an application if a customer cannot provide evidence of an appeal.

Note: Attempt to contact customers to request evidence of appeal.

Escalate application via PaNDA to ME_Escalations (a PSO will escalate to the Medicare Consumers Program Support team if required) if:

  • they cannot determine that the appeal is ongoing
  • the customer cannot provide evidence of an appeal
  • the Service Officer cannot determine if there is a letter confirming the person’s pseudonyms
  • the Service Officer is unable to a get VEVO confirmation

Customers can apply for a Ministerial Intervention. They can do this with Department of Home Affairs Minister’s office.

Extend a Medicare card

Table 4

Step

Action

1

Log in to Consumer Directory Maintenance System (CDMS) + Read more ...

On the CDMS home page:

  • Select Amend on consumer details
  • Key the Medicare card number in ID Number field
  • Select Group

Note: a Medicare Payment Integrity (MPI) restriction may be on the record. This will prevent any changes. If this occurs, see the Comments in Group or contact a Medicare Eligibility Programme Support Officers (PSO).

2

Extend the entitlement and issue a replacement + Read more ...

The Resources page has extension periods.

To extend the entitlement details:

  • Select the Card Requests tab

In the Group Eligibility end date field:

  • key the extended date
  • tick the checkbox to replace the current card
  • Select the Replacement Reason from the drop down
  • Select the Card Request Source from the drop down
  • Select Comments and key the below if the visa is refused:
    • 'AAT letter dated dd/mm/yy, case number, extended for --mths'
  • Select Validate
  • Select Validate
  • Select Update. This will confirm the details

This date will now show in Entitlement. A new entitlement does not need to be added or amended.

Do not change the Group Eligibility Start Date.

Staff must:

  • verify identity documents in the Document Verification Service (DVS), and
  • enter new documents in CDMS

Note: when making updates in CDMS after the initial enrolment, try to locate the immigration data link and add it to the Medicare record. This will make sure immigration updates show in CDMS.

3

Adding documents to CDMS + Read more ...

On the CDMS home page:

  • Select Amend Consumer Details
  • Key the Medicare card number in ID Number field
  • Select Entitlement
  • Select Documents tab

Where a person has more than one Entitlement type

  • Select the radio button against an entitlement
  • Select Amend
  • Select Documents tab

The Eligibility and Residency Documents screen will display.

Document Types

  • Select or key the details of each of the customers eligibility documents:
    • Eligibility Document (mandatory)
    • Residency Document (if applicable)
    • Reference Text (number or text of the document, if required)
    • Reference Date (if required)
  • Select Validate

These documents will now appear in document reference. Repeat if other documents are to be added.

It is not a requirement to record the same document details if they are recorded in the CDMS document tab. For example, same passport submitted in new application to extend a Medicare card.

Note: when relationship documents have been previously submitted and recorded in CDMS which are still relevant, it is not a requirement to request these again.

See Documents required to register a customer for Medicare in CDMS for more details.
