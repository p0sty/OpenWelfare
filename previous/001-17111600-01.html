This document outlines the processes for approving drug or alcohol dependency treatment programs, or interventions as approved activities for job seekers.

Drug and alcohol treatments/interventions (treatment activities) as an approved activity

Drug and alcohol treatment programs/interventions (treatment activities) can be undertaken as approved activities by job seekers in Workforce Australia Employment Services.

Treatment activities may or may not contribute to mutual obligation or annual activity requirements, depending on whether a job seeker is provider or Centrelink managed.

Provider managed job seekers

Provider managed job seekers can discuss appropriate and available treatment programs or interventions with their provider for consideration in developing their points target.

If the job seeker is undertaking intensive treatment (such as residential rehabilitation) and is unable to participate in other activities, the treatment activity will fully meet their mutual obligation requirements.

The job seeker will remain provider managed and the treatment activity will be included in their points target.

If they are undertaking less intensive treatment activities (such as a fortnightly counselling session), this may contribute to meeting their mutual obligation or annual activity requirements. The job seeker will need to undertake other activities, such as job search or training in order to meet their requirements. However, the provider may reduce their points target as appropriate, based on the job seeker's individual circumstances.

Workforce Australia Employment Services Providers help job seekers by having access to the Employment Fund, which can be used to pay for professional services, such as drug and alcohol counselling or rehabilitation.

Disability Employment Services Providers are able to use their service fees to pay for appropriate types of assistance tailored to the individual job seeker's circumstances. This assistance may include interventions such as drug and alcohol counselling or rehabilitation.

If a job seeker fails to meet their compulsory requirements, they may be subject to compliance action under the Targeted Compliance Framework.

Centrelink managed job seekers

Centrelink managed job seekers may undertake treatment activities. Participation in these treatment activities does not reduce a Centrelink managed job seeker's mutual obligation requirements. They are additional voluntary activities only.

Centrelink managed job seekers must still undertake the required compulsory activities that have been approved by Services Australia, for example:

  • part-time work, voluntary work, or
  • quarterly participation interviews

The voluntary treatment activities must not impact on their ability to meet compulsory requirements. These job seekers are not be subject to compliance action if they fail to attend or cease their treatment activity. However, they must continue to meet their compulsory mutual obligation requirements.

If the job seeker wishes to reduce or fully satisfy their mutual obligation requirements through participation in a drug/alcohol treatment activity, they should be referred to an Employment Services Provider for assistance. Providers can access a greater network of services and resources to support job seekers to overcome their barriers to employment.

Services Australia does not have access to funding to pay for treatment activities. If a job seeker wishes to discuss treatment options, they should be directed to speak with their treating health professional.

Treatment activities

Provider managed job seekers

If a provider contacts to discuss treatment activities for a job seeker, direct them to their Account Manager for assistance.

Centrelink managed job seekers

Where the job seeker is seeking to add a treatment activity as an additional voluntary activity, Service Officers are not required to recommend or determine appropriate treatment activities.

Inclusion of a treatment activity in the job seeker's requirements is guided by evidence from treating health professionals and/or disclosure by the job seeker.
