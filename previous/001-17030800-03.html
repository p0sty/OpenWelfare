Eligibility and notification for the Youth Bonus Wage Subsidy (YBWS) 26 week suspension period

Table 1

Step

Action

1

Eligibility for the YBWS + Read more ...

The Department of Education, Skills and Employment will be responsible for determining a job seeker's eligibility for the YBWS.

A job seeker will be eligible to participate in the YBWS initiative if they satisfy all of the following:

  • they are under 25 years of age at the time of entering the YBWS initiative
  • in receipt of JobSeeker Payment (JSP) or Youth Allowance (YA), and
  • is provider managed by a jobactive or Transition to Work (TTW) provider

Job seekers participating in the Community Development Program (CDP) or with Disability Employment Services (DES) are excluded from this initiative.

Note: customers undertaking an apprenticeship are ineligible for YBWS as they are not considered to be job seekers.

2

Notification for the YBWS + Read more ...

The Department of Education, Skills and Employment will send through an electronic notification to Services Australia when a job seeker is placed into employment under the YBWS initiative:

  • information will display on the Participation Summary screen in Customer First, under the Subsidy heading and will include the job seeker's start date with the initiative
  • the YBWS information will display as 'no current information' where the job seeker is not a current YBWS participant. This could be because they have had payment restored within the 26 week suspension period or where their income support payment is cancelled at the end of the 26 week suspension period
  • previous periods in the YBWS will be included under the History heading
  • YBWS information may be displayed on the Participation Summary screen even though the customer may not be suspended (SUS) or cancelled (CAN), that is, they could be reporting income within a 6 fortnight nil rate period

Youth Bonus Wage Subsidy (YBWS) 26 week suspension period

Table 2

Step

Action

1

Commencement of YBWS 26 week suspension period + Read more ...

Notification from the Department of Education, Skills and Employment will be received, advising the job seeker has commenced employment under the YBWS. The job seeker's income support payment will auto-suspend for a period of 26 weeks when the job seeker loses qualification for payment. The income support payment will suspend using reason 'YBS' and a notification letter will be issued advising the job seeker that they have commenced the suspension period.

To identify if a job seeker is employed under the YBWS initiative, Service Officers can review the Participation Summary screen in Customer First under the Subsidy tab.

During the YBWS 26 week suspension period, job seekers will not:

  • be considered to be 'ON' income support for the purposes of calculating Family Tax Benefit (FTB), Child Care Subsidy (CCS), partner rent assistance, partner income test, supplements or concession cards
  • be required to report - this includes all reporting frequencies
  • have any ongoing mutual obligation requirements, other than their employment
  • accrue Working Credits

Note: Income Managed job seekers will be treated as being on income support during the suspension period and continue to be income managed. They can continue to use their BasicsCard during this period as for current periods of suspension/cancellation.

Job seekers placed in the YBWS initiative will be exited from their jobactive or Transition to Work (TTW) provider when they commence the 26 week suspension period. They will not become Services Australia managed. The YBWS job seeker will remain provider managed until their income support payment cancels or they return to their income support payment.

Has the job seeker contacted Services Australia within the 26 week suspension period?

  • Yes, and:
    • is participating in the YBWS, go to Step 3
    • it is confirmed the job seeker has not commenced under the YBWS, go to Step 4
  • No, go to Step 2

2

End of the 26 week suspension period - no job seeker contact + Read more ...

If there is no contact from the job seeker at the end of the 26 week suspension period, the job seeker's payment will auto-change from suspended to cancelled with the reason 'YBE'. A cancellation advice letter will be issued to the job seeker.

Once this has happened, their income support payment cannot be restored and the job seeker will have to reclaim if they wish to claim an income support payment.

The customer can apply for a formal review of the cancellation decision. For more details about reviews and appeals, see Request for an explanation, quality check or review (CLK).

Procedure ends here.

3

Job seeker contacts within the 26 week suspension period + Read more ...

If at any time during the suspension period, a job seeker wants to return to their existing income support payment, they will be able to contact Services Australia via:

  • online - by accessing the 'Advise End of Employment service' option from the Menu in their online account. Note: this service is only available for customers who are suspended YBS
  • phone, or
  • face to face

If contact is via:

  • online, go to Step 5
  • phone or face to face, go to Step 6

4

Restoration of payment when system is incorrectly coded as Suspend YBS + Read more ...

Follow the restoration process details in jobactive/Community Development Program (CDP) registration inactivated and cancellation of income support in error where it is determined the job seeker:

  • is not full-time ongoing and this has been recorded incorrectly, or
  • did not commence employment and it has been confirmed by contact with both the provider and job seeker that the Suspend YBS has been coded in error, that is, not advised by provider or job seeker

Note: do not use the Verify Employment Separation guided procedure.

When restoring a YBS suspended job seeker within 13 weeks of the suspension, the reason code 'YBS' should be used and the restoration date and Date of Receipt (DOR) must be the same as the date of the suspension in order to pay arrears owed. Clearly document the reason for restoration in a DOC.

If the customer contacts more than 13 weeks from the date of being notified of the decision they can only be restored from the date of contact by the customer.

Update all the outstanding reporting statements at the same time as the restoration to stop the record from auto-suspending for failure to report.

5

Contact Online via 'Advise End of Employment service' + Read more ...

If the job seeker notifies of ceasing employment via the online account option, the information will be populated in a Cease Employment - Change of Circumstance activity which can only be completed via the Verify Employment Separation guided procedure.

The Verify Employment Separation guided procedure can be accessed via the workspace or from the Staff Tasks tab within the Cease Employment - Change of Circumstance activity.

If the customer:

  • has not provided the Employment Separation Certificate (SU001), or verification from their employer, after completing the 'Advise End of Employment service', the Re-index Claim (ZCoC) activity is set to 'For Customer Action'
  • uploads the SU001 or verification document through the 'Upload Documents Online Service' the status of the ZCoC is set to 'For Manual Action'

Note: only Service Officers with the skill tag of 'Job Seeker Claims 50386849' and a proficiency level of 'Established' will be able to action these Change of Circumstance activities.

When the 'Advise End of Employment service' has been completed and the Service Officer is ready to complete the Verify Employment Separation guided procedure, all the mandatory fields will need to be completed before the work item can be completed.

If a customer advises their work hours have changed from full-time to part-time or casual, or they are on leave without pay (LWOP), they should still use the 'Advise End of Employment service' option where possible. The reason for ceasing work should be recorded as 'shortage of work' and proof must be provided.

Once this has been completed, Service Officers are to use the same reason (shortage of work) to cancel the YBS suspension via the Verify Employment Separation guided procedure.

In all instances, DOC the reason for the restoration. For example, 'Customer reduced to casual/part time hours', or 'On LWOP and not incapacitated'.

A SUS-YBS customer can have their payment restored if they:

  • become temporarily incapacitated, and
  • provide suitable medical evidence

To restore payment for this reason, go to Step 13.

Otherwise, go to Step 7.

6

Contact via phone or face to face + Read more ...

If the job seeker notifies via phone or in person, they are ceasing employment, or they are on leave without pay (LWOP), Service Officers must use the Verify Employment Separation guided procedure to record that the job seeker has ceased YBWS employment.

If the job seeker is advising their employment hours have reduced or changed to casual or part time hours, go to Step 12.

The commencement of the Verify Employment Separation guided procedure generates a Cease Employment - Change of Circumstance activity in Workload manager.

If the customer:

  • has provided evidence, complete all the appropriate fields in the Verify Employment Separation guided procedure. Go to Step 7
  • does not have evidence to verify ceasing employment, the preferred option is to tell the customer to complete the online 'Advise End of Employment service'

If the customer chooses not to do this, key the following on the Verify Employment Separation guided procedure, pending evidence being provided:

  • Employment Separation/Verification Details section:
  • Employer name and date employment ceased - record details provided by customer
  • Source - select SU1 from the dropdown list
  • Advise End of Employment section:
  • Reason for separation - select appropriate reason as advised by customer
  • Further Details of Separation - record 'Evidence to be provided'
  • Action code - this will auto-populate based on reason recorded unless 'OTHER' is recorded for reason for separation. In these cases the action code will have to be manually recorded. Note: valid values are RES for Restoration or CAN for Cancellation
  • Date of Effect - will default to date depending on whether restored (RES) or cancelled (CAN)

Once coding is complete, select Save and Exit. Do not select Finish. The Change of Circumstances (CoC) transaction will be set as 'For Customer Action'.

If information is not updated, the customer's payment may not restore and will remain suspended (SUS-YBS).

A SUS-YBS customer can have their payment restored if they:

  • become temporarily incapacitated, and
  • provide suitable medical evidence

To restore payment for this reason, go to Step 13.

Otherwise, go to Step 7.

7

Job seeker to provide information/evidence within 14 days of contact + Read more ...

The job seeker or their employer must provide the agency with information/evidence on the cessation of their employment, see Employment Separation Certificate or Letter from employer, to determine if restoration of the payment will occur or if they must submit a new claim.

The evidence must include information on any leave or redundancy payments received by the customer on termination from the employer.

Note: this evidence may be attached as a document in the Customer Tasks tab in the Cease Employment - Change of Circumstance activity.

If the customer task is not completed and the document is not attached to the Cease Employment - Change of Circumstance activity, ensure that a search for the document is undertaken in the Upload documents service. There may be instances where the document did not attach to the Cease Employment - Change of Circumstance activity.

Has the job seeker provided information/evidence within 14 days of contact?

  • Yes, go to Step 8
  • No, go to Step 11

8

Reason for ceasing employment + Read more ...

Where the job seeker's employment has ceased due to no fault of their own, their income support payment can be restored. If the reason for ceasing employment was the job seeker's fault, their income support payment will be cancelled. An unemployment failure should be generated if payment is reclaimed within 6 weeks of voluntarily leaving work or being dismissed due to misconduct.

Service Officers assessing a job seeker's reason for ceasing employment must obtain evidence and make a determination based on the following reasons:

  • Shortage of work
  • Unsuitability for the type of work
  • Unsatisfactory work performance
  • Misconduct as an employee
  • Voluntarily ceased work
  • Redundancy
  • End of season or contract
  • Other

Reasons that would be considered the job seeker's fault include:

  • Unsatisfactory work performance
  • Misconduct as an employee
  • Voluntarily ceased work
  • Other (further investigation needed)

Was the job seeker at fault for ceasing employment?

  • Yes, go to Step 9
  • No, go to Step 10

9

Job seeker at fault + Read more ...

Where the reason for ceasing employment is the fault of the job seeker, the payment is to remain suspended until the reason for ceasing employment has been confirmed by evidence on the Employment Separation Certificate (SU001) or from other evidence provided.

If it is determined the payment is not to be restored:

  • cancel the payment from the date the job seeker ceased employment using the Verify Employment Separation guided procedure, and
  • attempt to contact and tell the job seeker:
    • to submit a new claim if they want to receive an income support payment, and
    • that an unemployment failure will be investigated and an unemployment preclusion period may affect the new claim start date. For further information, see Unemployment due to a voluntary act or misconduct

Cancelling the income support payment

The Verify Employment Separation guided procedure must be used to correctly cancel the job seeker's payment.

The evidence provided by the job seeker or their employer must include information on any leave or redundancy payments received by the job seeker on termination from the employer. These payments are to be assessed and recorded on the Maintain screen in the guided procedure and applied under the existing Income Maintenance Period (IMP) rules.

Note: the Verify Employment Separation guided procedure may display the IMP leave types in a different order on the Review screen compared to the Income Maintenance Period Summary (IMPS) screen. The overall IMP calculated start and end period in the guided procedure will be calculated correctly and display as per the IMPS screen calculation for the start and end date of the applied IMP.

Finish the Verify Employment Separation guided procedure and include comments within the Notes tab of the Cease Employment - Change of Circumstance activity advising the reason for cancellation.

The job seeker's payment will auto-change from suspended to cancelled with the reason 'YBS'. A cancellation advice will be issued to the job seeker.

Once this has occurred their income support payment cannot be restored and the job seeker will have to submit a new claim if they wish to claim income support payment.

The customer can apply for a formal review of the cancellation decision. For more details about reviews and appeals, see Request for an explanation, quality check or review (CLK).

Contact the job seeker

Attempt to contact the job seeker by phone to tell them their income support payment has been cancelled and if they would like to receive an income support payment they must reclaim. The job seeker must be advised that an unemployment preclusion period may apply, if an unemployment failure has been committed due to voluntary unemployment or dismissal due to misconduct.

If the contact attempt is successful and the customer wants to reclaim, tell the customer to claim online. Include details from the customer's contact within the Notes tab of the Cease Employment - Change of Circumstance activity.

If the contact attempt is unsuccessful, record this information within the Notes tab of the Cease Employment - Change of Circumstance activity.

For more information on new claims, see:

  • Processing Youth Allowance (YA) (job seeker) claims
  • Processing JobSeeker Payment (JSP) claims

Procedures ends here.

10

Restore Payment + Read more ...

The payment suspension is restorable where the employment has ceased through no fault of the job seeker and evidence has been provided to confirm this. See examples on the Resources page for further information.

If the reason for ceasing employment is accepted and payment can be restored, action the restoration via the Verify Employment Separation guided procedure. The payment cannot be restored manually via the Benefit Action (BA) screen unless an error in recording the YBS indicator has been identified, see Step 4.

The payment can only be restored from the date of notification. There are no backdating provisions.

Any leave or redundancy payments received by the customer on termination from the employer are to be assessed and recorded on the Maintain screen in the guided procedure and applied under the existing Income Maintenance Period (IMP) rules.

Note: the Verify Employment Separation guided procedure may display the IMP leave types in a different order on the Review screen compared to the Income Maintenance Period Summary (IMPS) screen. The overall IMP calculated start and end period in the guided procedure will be calculated correctly and display as per the IMPS screen calculation for the start and end date of the applied IMP.

Once all information has been entered:

  • select 'FINISH' to finalise the guided procedure, and
  • once completed, include comments within the 'Notes' tab of the Cease Employment - Change of Circumstances activity advising:
    • the reason for restoration, and
    • the date the customer first contacted

If the Cease Employment - Change of Circumstance activity has not been completed upon finalisation of the guided procedure, manually change the status on the Change of Circumstances activity to 'Completed'.

Any Centrepay deductions the job seeker had in place prior to commencing the 26 week suspension period and concession card entitlements will recommence.

Continuity dates - Existing continuous period on payment rules will apply to restorations. For example, if a customer is restored after being suspended for more than 13 weeks, continuity dates will be auto-reset as the date payment recommenced.

Note: if the payment does not restore when expected after completing the guided procedure, the record will need to be checked to determine what is causing this. Any incorrect information will need to be updated before the payment can be restored.

Some possible (but not limited to) reasons could include:

  • End date the job seeker's full-time work on the Customer Employment Status Update (EAES) screen, or
  • Key a 'zero' income against the last employer that the job seeker had, or
  • Ensure that there are no outstanding previous reporting periods that have not been completed, or
  • End date the 'other income' on the Other Income Summary (OINS) screen, or
  • End date the job seekers full-time work from the Employment Paid (EAPP) screen

Note: customers who turn 22 during the YBS suspension period may not be able to be restored using the Verify Employment Separation guided procedure. Refer to scenarios in Resources for more information.

Once a customer is restored from a YBWS suspension period, the 26 week YBWS period ends. If they then return to work they must advise, and normal RTW provisions are applied. The customer's payment will not re-suspend unless their employment services provider sent through a new second notification. See Scenario 5 on the Resources page.

Procedure ends here.

11

Job seeker has not provided verification for reason ceasing employment + Read more ...

Payment will be auto-cancelled using reason 'FSD' (Failed to supply documents) from the date the job seeker contacted Services Australia.

There should be no further action needed.

If for any reason the record is not auto-cancelled FSD, the Change of Circumstance activity will be changed to 'For Manual action'. If this occurs the following process will need to be followed:

Select the Cease Employment - Change of Circumstance activity and:

  • include notes within the Notes tab advising:
    • the reason for cancellation, and
    • date the customer first contacted
  • manually change the status on the Change of Circumstances activity to 'Completed'

Manually cancel the job seeker from the Benefit Action (BA) screen using cancellation reason 'FSD'. The customer is to be cancelled from the date they advised the agency they 'ceased employment'. Include the reason for cancelling on the BA screen as 'Failed to supply documents to support end of employment for YBWS job seeker' in the Notes: field and finalise the activity.

A manual DOC will need to be recorded confirming the reason that the YBWS job seeker was cancelled from the date they advised they 'ceased employment' and that if the customer contacts at a later date with the required documentation, their income support payment cannot be restored and they will need to undertake a new claim.

Procedure ends here.

12

Job seeker advises employment hours have reduced or changed to casual or part-time hours + Read more ...

If a customer advises their work hours have changed from full-time to part-time or casual, or they are on leave without pay (LWOP), they should still use the 'Advise End of Employment service' option where possible. The reason for ceasing work should be recorded as 'shortage of work' and proof must be provided.

Check the customers hourly rate of pay and hours per week to decide if customer under the earnings threshold and will be entitled to Youth Allowance or JobSeeker Payment, see Rates and Thresholds. If:

  • Yes - Youth Allowance or JobSeeker Payment (JSP) can be restored via the Benefit Action (BA) screen with restoration code YBS or via the guided procedure with a reason of 'other'. The reason for the restoration must be documented (e.g. customer has a reduced work hours)
    • Once payment is restored:
      - a transaction will be sent to their provider
      - they become active on their provider's case load again and will be subject to whatever the provider decides
      - they will cease to have a YBS indicator on record, and
      - they will be subject to normal cancellation rules in the future unless the provider records another YBS indicator
  • No, leave customer as YBWS suspended. Advise the customer that should their hours reduced further they may be entitled to a payment

Procedure ends here.

13

Customer in YBS suspension period becomes temporarily incapacitated and provides suitable medical evidence + Read more ...

Youth Allowance

A YAL (JSK) customer is eligible to be restored to YAL (JSK) payment regardless of whether they have a job to return to or not. Payment should be restored via BA screen and medical information recorded to allow for exemption from mutual obligation requirements. Check EAES screen to ensure that full-time ongoing employment has been ended.

JobSeeker Payment

The JobSeeker Payment (JSP) is to be restored via the BA screen with restoration code YBS or via the guided procedure with a reason of 'other.' The reason for the restoration must be documented (e.g. customer temporarily incapacitated for job).

Medical information is to be recorded to allow for exemption from mutual obligation requirements. Check EAES screen to ensure that full-time ongoing employment has been ended.

For both YA and JSP, when restoring due to a temporary incapacity the Event Date is the same as the Unfit to work/study/participate from date unless it is a future date. If it is a future date, record the Event Date as today's date (the date the medical certificate is being recorded). Note: this is not the Date of Receipt unless the Date of Receipt is today.

Note: when restoring a YBS suspended job seeker within 13 weeks of the suspension, use the reason code 'YBS' and the restoration date and Date of Receipt (DOR) will need to be the same as the start date of medical certificate. Clearly document the reason for restoration in a DOC.
