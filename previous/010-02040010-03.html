Determining ABSTUDY independence when a customer is currently or previously married,or in a registered relationship

Table 1

Step

Action

1

Independence status + Read more ...

Check if the student or Australian Apprentice was assessed as independent under an ABSTUDY permanent independent criteria for a previous claim.

In Customer First go to the Independent/Homeless/Away from Home Details (NIH) screen.

Have they previously been assessed as independent under an ABSTUDY permanent independent status?

  • Yes: no further action is required to assess independence. However, their relationship status may need updating. See Member of a Couple (MoC) and Separated under one roof (SUOR) assessments. Procedure ends here
  • No: go to Step 2

2

Relationship status + Read more ...

Check if they are currently or previously:

  • legally married
  • married under Aboriginal or Torres Strait Islander Lore
  • in a registered relationship

Does the customer’s relationship status meet any of the above criteria?

  • Yes, go to Step 3
  • No, go to Step 4

3

Supporting documentation + Read more ...

The student or Australian Apprentice may be required to provide evidence to support their claim for independence due to currently or previously married, or in a registered relationship.

To determine if further evidence is required, in Customer First, go to the Marital Status (MS) screen.

Does the student or Australian Apprentice's MS screen have MAR, REG, or DIV recorded in the Marital Status field?

  • Yes, no further evidence will be required. See Step 1 in Table 2
  • No, further evidence will be required to support their independence claim. Evidence must include:
    • an original or certified copy of their marriage certificate
    • legal documentation confirming marriage, or previous marriage
    • a statement confirming the marriage within the community, signed by a recognised Aboriginal or Torres Strait Islander elder responsible for cultural ceremonies, or
    • evidence confirming the relationship between the person and another person is registered under a law of a State or Territory prescribed for the purposes of section 22B of the Acts Interpretation Act 1901 as a kind of relationship prescribed for the purposes of that section and they are not living separately from the other person on a permanent or indefinite basis

4

Independence status not met + Read more ...

The student or Australian Apprentice is not eligible for grant of independent status for ABSTUDY on the grounds of currently or previously married, or in a registered relationship.

Consider:

  • if the student or Australian Apprentice is independent on other grounds. See Independence for ABSTUDY
  • if appropriate, referrals for other assistance, such as a social worker or Indigenous Service Officer

Go to Step 7.

5

Procedure when customer contacts + Read more ...

If the student or Australian Apprentice has contacted through:

  • a service centre, go to Step 6
  • Smart Centre Call go to Step 7
  • the ABSTUDY Smart Centre Processing Services teams, see Step 1 in Table 2

6

Scanning supporting documentation + Read more ...

Ensure the claim/form and all supporting documents are scanned to the student or Australian Apprentice's record to allow for processing by the ABSTUDY Smart Centre Processing Services team.

Go to step 7.

7

Record details of the discussion + Read more ...

Record details of the discussion in a DOC.

Procedure ends here.

Coding ABSTUDY independence when a customer is currently or previously married, or in a registered relationship

Table 2: For ABSTUDY Smart Centre Processing staff only.

Step

Action

1

Assessing eligibility + Read more ...

Check the evidence provided to determine if the student or Australian Apprentice meets eligibility for independence due to them being currently or previously married or in a registered relationship.

If the Marital Status (MS) screen is coded as currently Married (MAR) or Registered Relationship (REG), they are automatically assessed as independent. The Independent/Homeless/Away from Home Details (NIH) screen does not need to be coded with the independence details.

Is the student currently married or in a registered relationship?

  • Yes: Link the student or Australian Apprentice and their partner to ensure the correct ABSTUDY rate is paid. See Change in relationship status from single to partnered. Once linked there is no further action required. The system will grant independence automatically from the MS coding. Procedure ends here.

2

Previous circumstances + Read more ...

Was the student previously married or in a registered relationship?

  • Yes, go to Step 3
  • No, the student or Australian Apprentice is not eligible for independence under the married or registered relationship criteria. Record the details of this decision in a DOC. Procedure ends here

3

Coding independence details + Read more ...

Student or Australian Apprentice meets eligibility for independence due to previously being married, or in a registered relationship.

Complete the following fields on the NIH screen:

  • Action, key 'IND'
  • Start/Reject Reason, key 'PBM'
  • Start Date, key the later of:
    • ABSTUDY start date
    • date the student or Australian Apprentice became independent

Record the details of the decision in a DOC.

Go to Step 4.

4

Determine if a current Child Support assessment exists + Read more ...

To check if an assessment exists for the student or Australian Apprentice, see Maintenance Income Test (MIT) for ABSTUDY and Youth Allowance (YA).

Is there a current Child Support assessment for the student or Australian Apprentice?

  • Yes, go to Step 5
  • No, procedure ends here

5

Terminating event for Child Support send ACTDOC to CSJ + Read more ...

Send an ABSTUDY Child Support Update Fast Note. Select Auto text, use Students, ABSTUDY and Apprentices > Update > ABSTUDY Child Support Update

Complete the details within the Fast Note and select Confirm, ensuring the Fast Note remains open.
