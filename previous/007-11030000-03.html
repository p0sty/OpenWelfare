Information not received from ATO for FTB, CCS and CCB

Step

Action

1

Income details + Read more ...

Actual income details have not been received from the ATO to allow FTB, CCS or CCB reconciliation or FTB lump sum claim to be finalised.

If the customer contacts and:

  • reconciliation or a finalised lump sum claim is pending 'income', go to Step 2
  • an FTB non-lodger debt exists because income details have not been received from the ATO, go to Step 2
  • reconciliation or a lump sum claim payment or debt is 'parked' awaiting a wash up transaction from the ATO, go to Step 11
  • their or their or partner's tax assessment has been amended, go to Step 6

2

Check customer record + Read more ...

FTB

  • Check the FAO Reconciliation and Claim Summary (FRCS) screen for the relevant financial year
  • Code 'L' on the most recent reconciliation or lump sum claim line to go to the FAO Non-Lodger Details (FNLD) screen. FNLD will show if the customer or partner is an outstanding lodger for the relevant financial year, i.e. income details not received from the ATO

CCS

  • Go to Process Direct > CCS Reconciliation Dashboard > Key CRN > Relevant Year
  • Review the most recent reconciliation result display box

3

Check for MIV activity + Read more ...

FTB

Check the Activity List (AL) screen on the customer and partner records for an outstanding activity with the Manual Intervention Team (MIV).

Is there a started MIV activity on AL?

  • Yes, see Manual intervention into family assistance reconciliation. Procedure ends here
  • No:
    • Debt Recovery staff - warm transfer the customer to the Families and Parenting Smart Centre Call queue. They will follow the remaining steps in this procedure
    • All other staff, go to Step 4

CCS

Is there a 'Work item' hyperlink in the CCS Reconciliation Dashboard?

  • Yes, check the customer record to see why they are pending. See Manual Intervention for Child Care Subsidy (CCS) reconciliation. Procedure ends here
  • No, go to Step 4

4

Check link with ATO established + Read more ...

Centrelink will establish a link with the ATO for a family assistance customer (and any partner/s):

  • who have been paid, or
  • who lodge an effective lump sum claim for Family Tax Benefit (FTB), for a financial year

The ATO will:

  • note on their records that the customer is a mutual customer
  • tell Centrelink that a link has been established

If a customer's record is not matched with the ATO, financial year assessment details cannot be shared.

A link transaction will not be sent to the ATO for a lump sum claimant (or their partner) until the lump sum claim has been processed. After the claim has been processed an automatic transaction will be sent to the ATO. This transaction will be used to link a record and request income details for the relevant financial year.

A link may not be established with the ATO for the relevant financial year if a lump sum claim has been:

  • rejected or
  • made not effective

For both FTB and CCS

Check the Mutual Customer Identification (MCI) screen for the person (customer or partner) whose income details have not been received from the ATO. Verify that a link with the ATO has been established for that person.

On the MCI screen of the outstanding lodger, if the Status field is:

  • Blank, check the Tax File Number Summary (TFS) screen. Is the customer able to confirm the TFN?
    • Yes, go to Step 5
    • No, tell the customer to visit the ATO website for more information about obtaining a TFN or confirming their TFN
      Note:       the ATO is unable to provide TFN details directly. The customer must provide TFN details once they have been confirmed by the ATO.
  • Match, the Tax File Number (TFN) provided has been matched with the ATO, go to Step 6
  • Mismatch, no link has been established with the ATO. See Mutual Customer Identification (MCI) results for family assistance customer mismatches
  • ATO pending, a link request remains outstanding. Check the TFN of the outstanding lodger (customer or partner). If the TFN is:
    • different to what is recorded, update the new TFN. See Mutual Customer Identification (MCI) results for family assistance customer mismatches
    • the same as what is recorded, go to Step 10
  • Centrelink pending, there are multiple Customer Reference Numbers (CRN) for the one TFN and Centrelink must do a Manual Follow-up. See Mutual Customer Identification (MCI) for family assistance customers with status of Centrelink Pending (CPN)
  • Confirmed, awaiting a response from the ATO. Go to Step 8

5

Customer can confirm Tax File Number (TFN) + Read more ...

Does the Tax File Number Summary (TFS) screen show the correct details?

  • Yes:
    • Go back to the MCI screen
    • Refresh the record by coding 'RSH' in the Request Action field
    • Press [Enter]
    • Confirm the request by entering 'Y' in the confirm field. This will complete the activity
    • Check the MCI screen to see if the MCI status has changed
      An automatic refresh transaction will be sent to the ATO. It will advise the ATO the person is a family assistance person of interest for the relevant financial year. The ATO will update their records and report available income details. A manual income request is not required
  • No:
    • Update the Tax File Number and the Auth field to 'Y' for the person (customer or partner)
    • Complete the activity
    • Check the MCI screen to see if the MCI status has changed
    • MCI status should now be awaiting. A new link transaction will be automatically sent to the ATO. No further action is required
    • If it is not awaiting, see Mutual Customer Identification (MCI) process for family assistance customers

6

Check link with ATO is open + Read more ...

A link with the ATO will automatically close after three years if:

  • income details have been received for the relevant year and
  • FTB reconciliation completed

If the ATO amends income after the link has closed, the new income details will not transmit.

Check the Mutual Customer Identification MCI screen for the person for whom the income has been amended to ensure the link with the ATO is still open for the relevant financial year.

Is the ATO link still open?

  • Yes, go to Step 7
  • No, go to Step 10

7

Link with ATO still open + Read more ...

Ensure the ATO issued the Notice of Assessment (NOA) for the relevant financial year has been received. The customer may have been advised the tax return has been accepted by the ATO, however, the assessment may undergo ATO checking before the assessment is finalised.

If the:

  • customer has received their NOA, ask for the:
    • Notice of Assessment date of issue
    • Notice of Assessment reference number
    • Go to Step 8
  • customer/partner have not received their NOA, tell them the ATO will transmit their income details when they finalise the income assessment. Procedure ends here

8

Request Actual Income + Read more ...

Use the Request Actual Income (FRAI) screen to manually request income details for a mutual customer for any financial year/s for which income details are required.

Requests are made on the record of the person for whom the income details are required. Note: this will not always be the record of the family assistance customer. It may be their partner.

Responses to Income requests generated by the quarterly bulk income request process will also display.

If the MCI screen is reading 'Awaiting' then FAO Request Actual Income (FRAI) screen cannot be coded until the MCI screen is matched. This can take up to 4 working days to occur.

  • Set up a review for 4 working days to recheck the MCI screen for a match. See How to code and action a manual review
  • If income is still required after the match has occurred, code the FRAI screen for the relevant year and person

If the ATO link for the financial year remains open and there is no outstanding income request on the FRAI screen, a manual income request should be recorded on the relevant person's record for the relevant financial year:

  • Navigate to the relevant personâ€™s record (family assistance customer or their partner for the financial year)
  • On the FRAI screen, select the relevant financial year
  • Code 'CLK' in the Request Income Rsn field and press [Enter]. Confirm the request by entering 'Y' in the confirm field. This will complete the request

Advise the customer that income has been requested from the ATO and this may take up to 10 days to receive. An ACTDOC is not required at this time.If the ATO link for the relevant financial year is no longer open, go to Step 10

Customers can go online to check if reconciliation has completed. Check if the customer wants to register for self service online.

Note: a FRAI request should not be coded on the same day a FTB lump sum claim is processed. The lump sum claim activity will automatically send an income request to the ATO. The FRAI screen can be coded at a later date if income has not been received.

If no response has been received after 10 days, go to Step 9.

9

Income not received following manual income request + Read more ...

If the ATO link for the financial year remains open and a FRAI request did not return income details, income may need to be manually requested from the ATO. The response provided by the ATO will determine the action required. Go to Step 10.

If an income request returns as:

  • 'Income Not Available', it generally means the ATO has no lodgement details to report for the person. Check that the income request was recorded on the correct record (customer or partner) and for the correct financial year.
    • If the person advises they have received a NOA for the relevant year, go to Step 10
    • If the person has not received a NOA for the relevant year they should be advised the ATO will automatically transmit income details once the income assessment has been finalised. Procedure ends here
  • 'Not Found on MCR', 'Outside 3 year limit', or 'Not A FAO customer', go to Step 10
  • 'Pending ATO Assessment' and the customer's NOA issue date is:
    • before the ATO response, go to Step 10
    • after the ATO response, record a new FRAI request. Go to Step 8

10

ATO link closed or details to be followed up + Read more ...

Check if the customer/partner is a DVA recipient using the Department of Veteran Affairs Summary (DVAS) screen in the relevant record.

If they are a DVA recipient, or self-identify as a Veteran, ask if the amended income is a result of the Douglas decision, record this in the Fast Note.

Manually request the income details from the ATO:

  • use the Fast Note - Auto Text > Families > Enquiry > ATO income details required and
  • complete the required fields to send a request to the ATO Liaison Team (TAP) for action
  • the Fast Note will add the keyword ACTTAP

Tell the customer the required information will be obtained from the ATO. The outstanding assessment should be finalised within 10 working days.

Customers can go online to check if reconciliation has completed. Check if the customer wants to register for self service online.

Procedure ends here.

11

Parked payment or debt + Read more ...

Following the receipt of income from the ATO, information about reconciliation entitlements and outstanding family assistance debts may be reported to the ATO.

FTB reconciliation is occurring

If FTB reconciliation is occurring following the receipt of income for the customer or their consenting partner for the relevant financial year, information about arrears or debts for the relevant financial year and outstanding FTB/CCS/CCB debt amounts for prior financial years is reported to the ATO.

The ATO may:

  • offset FTB reconciliation top-ups against outstanding taxation debts, and
  • use tax refunds against outstanding family assistance debts

The reconciliation result will be placed on hold, or parked until the ATO has advised offsetting action. The ATO response transaction is called a wash-up.

FTB reconciliation is not occurring

If reconciliation is not occurring (reconciliation already COMplete, partner lodgement outstanding) as a result of actual income being received, Services Australia will send a transaction to the ATO if the customer has:

  • an outstanding FTB non-lodger,
  • a qualification or reconciliation debt, or
  • CCB reconciliation or CCR debt determined after 1 July 2006

When a FTB reconciliation result or tax offset request is sent to the ATO, a future review is created on the customer's record. The review will appear on the FAL screen and will have a maturity date of one year from the date of registration

FTB payment or FTB reconciliation debt is parked

It may take the ATO up to 28 days to send a wash up transaction after completing:

  • a FTB reconciliation or
  • lump sum claim assessment

They will send the wash up transaction after issuing the NOA.

  • View the date the payment was parked on the FAO Reconciliation and Claim Transaction (FRCT) screen. Key 'T' against the Reco Completed line on the FAO Reconciliation and Claim Summary (FRCS) screen to go to the FRCT screen
  • Check the customer has actually received the NOA. If they did not receive the NOA, tell them that the reconciliation of lump sum claim assessment will not be implemented until:
    • the NOA has been issued and
    • a wash up transaction has been received
  • If the FRCS screen shows that the reconciliation has been cancelled, go to Step 12

Send a request to the ATO Liaison Team (TAP) for action if:

  • it has been more than 28 days since the payment or debt was parked, and
  • the customer has received their NOA, and
  • there is no started Manual Intervention (MIV) activity on the record

Use the Fast Note - Auto Text > Families > Enquiry > Wash up not received. Complete the required fields. The Fast Note will add the keyword ACTTAP.

Tell the customer:

  • the required information will be obtained from the ATO
  • any outstanding assessment will finalise within 10 working days
  • finalisation of a wash-up activity may result in:
    • the payment of parked top-ups,
    • the raising of parked FTB reconciliation debts, or
    • a tax offset being credited to a family assistance debt

Note: this does not apply for CCS.

Procedure ends here.
