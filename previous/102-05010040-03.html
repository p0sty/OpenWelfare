Customer advises they are partnered

Step

Action

1

YA or ABSTUDY customer advises they are now living with a partner + Read more ...

If the customer advises their relationship status will change in future do not code the change. Offer to issue appropriate forms and do a resubmit DOC for 14 days from the expected date of change.

If the customer is:

  • advising they are/were married or in a registered relationship and are:
    • an ABSTUDY customer, see ABSTUDY customer currently or previously married or registered relationship
    • a YA customer, see Assessing independence when a customer is/was married or a member of a couple
  • living with a partner in a de facto relationship, go to Step 2

2

Age of consent + Read more ...

Are the customer and partner above the age of consent? See References for a link to the definition of Defacto Relationships (YA and DSP).

  • Yes, go to Step 4
  • No, go to Step 3

3

Under age of consent + Read more ...

Advise the customer this partnership is not recognised under Social Security Law, see the References page for a link to the relevant legislation. Refer the customer to the Social Worker immediately.

Record details of contact on a DOC.

Procedure ends here.

4

Determine if customer is already independent + Read more ...

Go to the NIH screen to view their independence status.

If the customer is 22 years of age or older they are considered to be independent due to age.

Is the customer already considered independent?

  • Yes, the customer is independent, go to Step 5
  • No, the customer is not independent, go to Step 6

5

Check partner's independence status + Read more ...

If the partner is or has previously been in receipt of a Centrelink payment or benefit, go to the NIH screen to view their independence status. If the partner is not in receipt of a Centrelink payment or benefit, and there is no status of independence on the NIH screen, they cannot be considered independent.

If the partner is 22 years of age or older they are considered to be independent due to age.

Is the partner already considered independent?

  • Yes, go to Step 8
  • No, go to Step 6

6

Determine length of relationship + Read more ...

The customer and/or their partner may be deemed independent based on the length of their relationship. Ask the customer the date their relationship commenced and determine if they may become independent as a result.

For YA see Assessing independence when a customer is/was married or a member of a couple, and return to this step.

For ABSTUDY see ABSTUDY customer in de facto relationship, and return to this step:

  • If the customer and/or their partner would not be considered independent based on the length of their relationship, go to Step 7
  • Where the customer and partner would be considered independent, they must provide evidence of the length of their relationship. If evidence has been provided, go to Step 8
  • If the customer has not provided evidence, they should be asked to do so along with the Partner Details (MOD P) form. Send a Request for Information (RFI) letter and DOC the file with the information provided by the customer, including the date they became partnered. Examples include joint bank account, insurance policy, lease agreements, dependent spouse rebate or a statutory declaration from a third party. Procedure ends here.

Note: if the evidence provided by the customer does not clarify the length of the relationship is sufficient to meet the relevant independence criteria, the Service Officer should only consider the customer as partnered from the date the customer first notified Services Australia that they had become partnered.

7

Customer and/or partner not independent + Read more ...

Advise the customer:

  • The partner's circumstances (i.e., income, etc.) will not be taken into account for either member of the couple while the YA or ABSTUDY customer is dependent
    Note: the customer and partner should not be linked until they are both independent e.g. they qualify as a member of a couple for YA/ABSTUDY or become independent for another reason
    The customer and/or their partner must advise if one or both becomes independent
  • If one customer becomes independent, the personal income test will apply but the partner income test will not apply to the independent customer. The dependent customer will continue to be subject to the personal income test and the parental means tests
  • If both become independent, then the personal and partner income tests will apply to both customers

Create a Display on Access (DOA) DOC on each person's record using the following auto text template:

  • Category 1: Generic
  • Category 2: Update
  • Template: YA/ABY Member of a Couple â€“ Pending Indep
  • Select 'Display on access' then 'confirm'
  • Choose the reason 'MAN'
  • Expiry date: 12 months for YA customers, or 6 months for ABSTUDY customers, from the date of commencement of the relationship or when both the customer and partner will become independent (due to age)
  • Enter the partners details in the Fast Note
  • Set up a Manual Review to review the couple's circumstances for the earlier of:
    • 12 months for YA customers, or 6 months for ABSTUDY customers, from the date of commencement of the relationship, or
    • the estimated date when either partner may achieve independent status for some other reason, e.g. accumulated earnings
  • Record details of decisions/discussion with customers on a DOC

Procedure ends here.

8

Partnered procedure and rate changes + Read more ...

Follow the procedure in the Update relationship status and link customer and partner records table of Change in relationship status from single to partnered.

  • If becoming partnered results in a rate increase, the customer will receive the member of a couple rate from the later of:
    • the date they were considered to be a 'member of a YA or ABSTUDY couple' or the date they became independent on other grounds, if they advise Services Australia within 14 days of the change, or
    • the date they advised the change, if advised more than 14 days after the change occurred
  • If becoming partnered results in a rate decrease, the customer will receive the new rate from the earlier of:
    • the date they were considered to be a 'member of a YA or ABSTUDY couple', or
    • the date they became independent on other grounds
