Type of lump sum + Read more ...

Exempt lump sum

Certain types of lump sums are exempt from income test assessment.

For a lump sum to be exempt it:

  • must be unlikely to be repeated
  • could not reasonably be expected to be received or anticipated, and
  • does not represent receipt of money for services rendered directly or indirectly

See Exempt lump sums.

All other lump sums are one of 2 types:

Remunerative lump sum:

  • a form of wage or amount paid for a service provided
  • generally, assessable if paid after claiming an income support payment. For more information, see Item 3

Non-remunerative lump sum:

  • a 'profit' but is not remuneration and does not match the characteristics of the excluded lump sums. See Item 4

3

Remunerative lump sums + Read more ...

If the remunerative lump sum is an amount of arrears of foreign pension, see Item 6 in Table 2.

All remunerative lump sums are assessable if paid after claiming an income support payment.

  • For employee's commission or royalty types of lump sums, see Income for an independent contractor and commission income
  • For leave payment lump sums where the employee did not take a leave of absence, see Leave and termination payments paid by an employer
  • If the customer or partner has received back pay for a period of employment, see Item 4 in Table 2
  • For bonus type lump sums, such as Aged Care Workforce Bonus Payment, See Item 1 in Table 2
  • For lump sums paid as backpay for self-employed customer or partner, see Item 5 in Table 2

Multiple income amounts paid in the same assessment period

If the customer or partner are paid multiple income amounts in the same assessment period from the same source, see Multiple pays received in a single entitlement period.

4

Non-remunerative lump sums + Read more ...

If the non-remunerative lump sum is from another country, see Item 6 in Table 2.

For lump sums paid from personal insurance schemes or disability benefits, see Item 8 Table 2.

Non-remunerative lump sums are assessed if the customer is claiming or receiving:

  • ABSTUDY and the lump sum was paid on or after the date of claim
  • another income support payment and the lump sum was paid:
    • after the date of claim, or
    • within 52 weeks before the customer's or partner's date of claim

Non-remunerative lump sums:

  • distributions from private trusts and private companies are recorded by a Complex Assessment Officer (CAO) on the TAC system. The information 'ripples' through to the customer's or partner's record
  • all other lump sums, (except foreign pensions) must be recorded on the customer's or partner's Other Income (OIN) screen. See Recording other income on the Other Income OIN screen

Assessing and coding lump sums

Table 2

Item

Description

1

Bonus payments and other lump sums to be treated as employment income + Read more ...

Employment income, including bonus payments received are treated as remunerative lump sums because it is in return for a service provided.

Key the employment income using the Earnings and Reporting workflow.

For remunerative lump sums that represent a period of 52 weeks or less:

  • Record the remunerative lump sum on the Employment Income Paid Details (EAPP) screen using the Employment Type code 'Earnings from Employment’ (EAR)
  • Use the frequency ‘Long Period’ (LOP) to record the income using the date paid as the event date
  • The income is assessed from the EPSD of the period in which the payment was paid, for a number of days equal to the period that the payment represents. This is known as the assessment period
  • See Recording and correcting employment income details for assistance with coding the EAPP screen

For remunerative lump sums that represent a period greater than 52 weeks:

  • Record the remunerative lump sum on the Employment Income Paid Details (EAPP) screen under a separate employer entry. For the new employer entry:
    • Use the Employment Type code 'Earnings from Employment’ (EAR)
    • Enter the employer’s name and add ‘lump sum’ at the end, for example ‘Services Australia lump sum’. If repeating for multiple lump sums, add each lump sum using a new employer entry and add the date the lump sum was received to create a unique employer name
  • Use the frequency code ‘Daily’ (DAY) to record the income using the Entitlement Period Start Date (EPSD) of the period in which the payment was paid as the event date
    • Use a combination of the Payment Calendar and the customer’s Payment Delivery (RAD) and Payment Summary (PS) screens to determine the EPSD of the period if required
  • The income is assessed from the EPSD of the period in which the payment was paid, for a number of days equal to the period that the payment represents, this is known as the assessment period
  • Divide the amount received by the number of days in the period that the payment represents. Record this figure in the Amount field
    • For example, a lump sum of $5,000 for a period of 1,000 days is a daily amount of $5
  • The income must be ceased from the assessment period end date plus 1 day. Use the Perpetual Centrelink Calendar if required to determine the end date of the assessment
    • If this date has not yet passed, record a manual review in Customer First/Customer Record including details of the income received, the assessment period and the date from which the assessment is to be ceased, see How to code and action a manual review. Use review reason ‘Earnings Review’ (INC) and add the keyword OVER52WK
    • If this date has passed at the time the income is being recorded, record and cease the income in the same activity, no review is required
  • To cease the income, record an amount of zero using the assessment period end date plus 1 day as the event date. See Recording and correcting employment income details for assistance with coding the EAPP screen

If a lump sum is received before 7 December 2020, it may be appropriate to record it using a continuous income frequency. The continuous income must be stopped or converted to IOP/LOP within the same activity in which it is recorded, see Resources of Recording and correcting employment income details.

  • Convert the lump sum amount to a fortnightly (2WE) figure, this can be done by dividing the total amount by the number of days the assessment period represents and multiplying the daily amount by 14
  • The date the lump sum was received will be the Date of Event
  • Record details on the EAN screen

2

Keying lump sums for an existing employer group + Read more ...

The Employment Income Summary (EANS) screen can assess multiple income amounts at one time. Each time a new amount is recorded on the Employment Income Paid Details (EAPP) screen, the amounts previously recorded for the same employer (if coded as IOP or LOP) will remain to be assessed for the duration the payment(s) cover, as well as assess the new amount coded.

3

Lump sum incorrectly keyed + Read more ...

If the lump sum is incorrectly keyed on the Employment Income Paid Details (EAPP) screen:

  • update the information to zero ('0') on the EAPP screen manually or via the Earnings and Reporting workflow. See Recording and correcting employment income details

Note: use the same activity and date of event for both updates on EAPP and the Other Income (OIN) screen.

Update the correct information on OINS, see Recording other income on the Other Income OIN screen.

4

Back pay for a period of employment + Read more ...

If the customer or their partner receive back pay of employment income, make sure the following details are available:

  • Date the back pay was received
  • Gross amount received
  • Period which the back pay covers

Assessing the back pay

  • Back pay is assessed:
    • from the Entitlement Period Start Date (EPSD) of the period in which it was paid
    • for the same period of time for which the income was paid
  • If the back pay spans multiple pay periods, which are not consecutive, determine the total number of pay periods where back pay is relevant. For example, when bulk remediation is done and back pay is reviewed for a period of 12 months. If only 2 fortnightly pay periods have back pay, the back pay period is 4 weeks, not 12 months
  • If the back pay includes an interest amount, record it separately as a non-remunerative lump sum. See Table 1, Item 4

Note: From 7 December 2020 when back pay is received for individual pay periods, do not correct the pay periods. If the back pay was received before 7 December 2020, see Resources for an example of how to code.

Coding the back pay

Record back pay on the Employment Income Paid Details (EAPP) screen using the Employment Type code 'Earnings from Employment (EAR). Back pay can be recorded under the same employer as usual wages, however it is important to record it as a separate entry.

For back pay that is for a pay period equal to or less than the number of days in the customer's entitlement period

  • Record the back pay using the frequency code ‘Income for one period’ 'IOP'
  • The income is assessed for each day in the entitlement period in which it’s paid
  • See Recording and correcting employment income details for assistance with coding the EAPP screen

For back pay that is for a pay period greater than the customer's entitlement period but equal to or less than 52 weeks:

  • Record the back pay using the frequency code ‘Long period’ (LOP):
  • The Start Date and End Date fields must include the dates of the period which the back pay covers
  • If the back pay spans multiple pay periods which aren't consecutive:
    • code the End Date as the date the back pay was received
    • count backwards from the End date by the number of relevant pay periods to determine the Start Date. For example, if the end date is 1 May 2022 and the back pay period is 26 weeks, the start date is 1 November 2021
    • clearly record calculations and dates determined on the customer's record
  • The system automatically stops the assessment of income once the applicable time ends
  • See Recording and correcting employment income details for assistance with coding the EAPP screen

For back pay that is for a pay period greater than 52 weeks:

  • Record the remunerative lump sum on the Employment Income Paid Details (EAPP) screen under a separate employer entry. For the new employer entry:
    • use the Employment Type code 'Earnings from Employment’ (EAR)
    • Enter the employer’s name and add ‘lump sum’ at the end, for example ‘Services Australia lump sum’. If repeating for multiple lump sums, add each lump sum using a new employer entry and add the date the lump sum was received to create a unique employer name
  • Use the frequency code ‘Daily’ (DAY) to record the income using the Entitlement Period Start Date (EPSD) of the period in which the payment was paid as the event date
    • Use a combination of the Payment Calendar and the customer’s Payment Delivery (RAD) and Payment Summary (PS) screens to determine the EPSD of the period if required
  • The income is assessed from the EPSD of the period in which the payment was paid, for a number of days equal to the period that the payment represents. This is known as the assessment period
  • Divide the amount received by the number of days in the period that the payment represents. Record this figure in the Amount field
    • For example, a lump sum of $5,000 for a period of 1,000 days is a daily amount of $5
  • The income must be ceased from the assessment period end date plus 1 day. Use the Perpetual Centrelink Calendar if required to determine the end date of the assessment
    • If this date has not yet passed, record a manual review in Customer First/Customer Record including details of the income received, the assessment period and the date from which the assessment is to be ceased, see How to code and action a manual review. Use review reason ‘Earnings Review’ (INC) and add the keyword OVER52WK
    • If this date has passed at the time the income is being recorded, record and cease the income in the same activity, no review is required
  • To cease the income, record an amount of zero using the assessment period end date plus 1 day as the event date, see Recording and correcting employment income details for assistance with coding the EAPP screen

7

Other foreign income lump sum amounts + Read more ...

Some foreign lump sums are exempt lump sums:

  • Examples are, bereavement or funeral grants
  • Refunds of contributions or other withdrawals or commutations are not assessable where the amount consists entirely of a return of the customer's contributions

Note: most overseas compulsory contribution schemes include contributions from the person's employer, making them assessable.

Exempt foreign lump sums are coded on the Foreign Income/Asset Detail (FID) screen, see Exempt lump sums for further information.

Remunerative foreign lump sums and foreign employment income are coded on the foreign Employment Income Paid Details (FEIP) screen, see Foreign income and assets.

If the foreign lump sum is not exempt, and is not a remunerative lump sum it is:

  • treated as a non-remunerative lump sum, and
  • assessed for 52 weeks from the date is was paid

Assessable non-remunerative foreign lump sums are keyed on the Foreign Income/Asset Detail (FID) screen:

  • Record in the source currency
  • Use the exchange rate applicable on the date the lump sum amount was paid
  • Exchange rates are available on the Foreign Exchange Summary (RDFXS) screen
  • If the date the customer received the lump sum is unknown, use:
    • the month of issue of the notice of grant (NOG), or
    • the following month if the notice is issued within 5 days of the end of the month and it is in the customer's favour

Process Direct:

  • Go to the Foreign Income (FIPS) screen
  • On the Foreign Income/Asset Details (FID) table, select Add New Row
  • On Create Foreign Financial, update:
    • Country of Payment: Enter the source country for the payment
    • Currency: Enter the source currency
    • Type: 'LMP' - Non-remunerative Lump Sum
    • Frequency: 'ANN' - Annually
    • Reference/Description: Enter details of the payment received
    • Event Date/Start date: Date the lump sum was received or able to be received
    • Income - Non Pension: Enter the amount of the payment received
  • Select Save
  • Complete the Receipt Date and Channel fields and select Save
  • Select Assess and address any errors/warnings shown in the Message Log on the Errors (SWE) screen
  • Go to the Entitlements (ELD) screen by selecting Assess again
  • Once satisfied that the outcome shown is correct, select Finish
  • Record details of the update in the Note on the Finalise screen

Customer First:

Go to the FID screen and code the following fields:

  • Country of Payment: Enter the source country for the payment
  • Type: 'LMP'
  • Reference/Description: Enter details of the payment received
  • Event Date: Date the lump sum was received or able to be received
  • Currency: Enter the source currency
  • Income Amt: Enter the amount of the income received
  • Frequency: 'ANN' - Annual

Record the details of the update in a DOC.

8

Lump sums paid from personal injury insurance schemes and disability benefits + Read more ...

A lump sum Total and Permanent Disability (TPD) benefit paid from a superannuation fund is not assessed as income, see Assessing withdrawals from superannuation. Any ongoing income or assets generated from a lump sum are assessable. Note: This does not include where the lump sum is arrears of periodic payments.

For assessment of lump sum arrears of periodic payments paid from a personal injury insurance scheme or disability benefit, see Income from personal injury insurance schemes and disability benefits.
