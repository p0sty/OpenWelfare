How to send a payment summary tax statement

This table describes details about the information sent when a request is received for a payment summary tax statement. Note: do not provide a third party with any information unless they have provided evidence of their authority. See Disclosing information to third parties.

Description

Who can the statement be issued to? + Read more ...

Statements may be issued to customer in the following circumstances:

  • Management of tax affairs
  • Customer leaves Australia permanently and needs to finalise their tax affairs
  • Customer is currently residing permanently outside of Australia, and
  • Relationship breakdown

Note: if the information has been requested for a deceased customer, see Requests for information after someone has died

When can the statement be issued? + Read more ...

A part year tax statement can be issued any time throughout the financial year, up until the 9 June. The statement cannot be issued 21 days before the end of a financial year.

If the customer requests a part year tax statement after 9 June, they must wait for 1 July for their Centrelink payment summary to be available.

Can a statement be issued?

  • Yes, see Item 4
  • No, procedure ends here

4

Where is the required information for the statement located? + Read more ...

Navigate to the Tax Year to Date (TXYTD) screen

  • Insert start and end dates for the period
    • The start date will be 1 July of the current year. The end date will be the end date as requested by the customer
  • Insert TXYTD Benefit Group
    • The TXYTD Benefit Group will be 'ALL' for all benefit types
  • Click Continue
  • Copy the web address and paste it into a web browser address field to display the payment details. Taxable and non-taxable payments will show, plus any tax deductions and Remote Area Allowance (RAA) amounts

5

What information is included on the statement? + Read more ...

The Tax Year to Date (TXYTD) screen will display all payments the customer receives for the specified period; only taxable and reportable non-taxable payments are to be listed on the part year tax statement.

If the customer has received more than one primary payment in the period requested by the customer, additional part year tax statements will need to be issued for each primary payment, displaying their individual reportable benefit types and payment periods.

For each primary payment in the period requested by the customer:

  • Identify the date first paid. This is the earliest date in the Delivery Date field
  • Identify the last date paid. This will be the last date in the Delivery Date field
  • Identify all taxable payments received for the date range in the Taxable $ field
  • Identify all non-taxable payments that are reportable for the date range in the Non Taxable $ field
  • Identify all tax deductions for the date range in the Tax Deducted $ field
  • Identify all remote area allowance payments for the date range in the RAA Amt $ field

Note: only the payments received by the customer should be included on the statement. Payments that the customer was entitled to but not received should not be included on the statement.

See Using the Tax Year to Date (TXYTD) screen for more information.

If no reportable payments are identified, procedure ends here.

6

How to issue the statement? + Read more ...

On receipt of the request:

Prepare the QTAX statement for each primary payment received during the period requested

Navigate to the Letter Display (LD) screen.

In the Letter Code: field key 'QTAX' and press [Enter].

In the Service Reason: field key in the benefit type the customer is currently receiving, then press [Enter].

  • Manually input information as required. See Resources for a link to an example of the statement
  • Information must include:
    • Financial year
    • Benefit type
    • Total payment paid
    • Payment amount (Gross Amount - Taxable)
    • Tax withheld amount (Tax Withheld)
    • Non-taxable amount
    • RAA amount
    • Date first paid
    • Date last paid
  • Check that only reportable information is included in the statement and check the information entered is correct
  • Employment Entry Payment ceased on 1 July 2008. This amount will be zero
  • Education Entry Payment (EdEP) if paid is on the One Off Payments (OOP) screen
  • Farm Help has been renamed Farm Household Allowance (FHA)

Issue the letter

  • Key 'FIN'alise in the Command: field and press [Enter]
  • The system will prompt if any required fields are not completed - make any updates and key 'FIN' in the Command: field and press [Enter]

Record a DOC which includes:

  • action taken, and
  • the logon and position of the staff member
