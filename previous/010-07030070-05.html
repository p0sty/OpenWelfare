Scholarships for Indigenous Australians

Table 1

Item

Description

1

Australian Indigenous Education Foundation Scholarship

Australian Indigenous Education Foundation Scholarships are offered to individual students attending a variety of affiliated schools. This scholarship is to cover board and tuition costs. If a student is in receipt of this scholarship, they are auto-qualified to live away from home under section 35.2 of the ABSTUDY Policy Manual. The scholarship is exempt income and does not affect ABSTUDY entitlements.

For social security purposes, this scholarship is exempt under subsection 8(8)(zjb) of the Social Security Act 1991 (tuition fees) and subsection 8(8)(za) of the Social Security Act 1991 (value of free board and lodging).

2

Indigenous Commonwealth Education Costs Scholarship

The Indigenous Commonwealth Education Costs Scholarship provides funding for up to 8 scholarship periods to assist students enrolled in an undergraduate course or a graduate diploma (or equivalent postgraduate course of study).

The Indigenous Enabling Commonwealth Education Costs Scholarship provides an additional year of scholarship assistance to Indigenous students undertaking an eligible enabling course.

For ABSTUDY, this scholarship is not counted as income, as per section 59.5.4.1 in the ABSTUDY Policy Manual.

For social security payments, this scholarship is not treated as income under subsection 8(8)(zja) of the SSA 1991.

Note: receiving a Commonwealth Education Costs Scholarship (CECS) prevents qualification for a Student Start-up Loan (SSL) under Section 1061ZVBB of the Social Security Act 1991 and section 103 in the ABSTUDY Policy Manual.

3

Indigenous Commonwealth Accommodation Scholarship

The Indigenous Commonwealth Accommodation Scholarship provides funding for up to 8 scholarship periods to assist students with accommodation costs who are enrolled in an undergraduate course or a graduate diploma (or equivalent postgraduate course of study).

The Indigenous Enabling Commonwealth Accommodation Scholarship provides an additional year of scholarship assistance to Indigenous students undertaking an eligible enabling course.

For ABSTUDY, this scholarship is not counted as income, as per section 59.5.4.1 in the ABSTUDY Policy Manual.

For social security payments, this scholarship is not treated as income under subsection 8(8)(zja) of the SSA 1991.

Note: receiving a Commonwealth Accommodation Scholarship (CAS) prevents qualification for a Relocation Scholarship (RS) under Section 592J of the Social Security Act 1991 and section 102 in the ABSTUDY Policy Manual. Customers are unable to receive ABSTUDY Residential Costs Option (RCO) payment for the same scholarship period as a CAS unless the offer of the placement at the residential college or hostel was received after the scholarship was paid. See Payment of Residential Costs Option (RCO) for ABSTUDY and ABSTUDY Pensioner Education Supplement (PES).

4

Indigenous Reward Scholarship (previously known as an Indigenous Access Scholarship IAS)

This scholarship scheme provides funding for 1,000 Indigenous Reward Scholarships. Each provider awarding an Indigenous Reward Scholarship may provide the student with an on-going Indigenous Commonwealth Education Costs Scholarship (CECS) and, where eligible, an Indigenous Commonwealth Accommodation Scholarship (CAS).

For ABSTUDY, an Indigenous Reward Scholarship is not counted as income, as per section 59.5.4.1 in the ABSTUDY Policy Manual.

For social security payments, an Indigenous Reward Scholarship is not treated as income under subsection 8(8)(zja) of the Social Security Act 1991 (SSA).

Note: receipt of an Indigenous Reward Scholarship does not prevent qualification for Student Start-up Loan (SSL) or Relocation Scholarship (RS), however, receipt of an Indigenous Reward Scholarship may be an indication that the student may be receiving a CECS or CAS as well.

5

The Kajji Foundation Scholarship

The Kajji Foundation Scholarship is to assist Northern Territory students to relocate and study in Melbourne. The affiliated schools are:

  • St Michael's Grammar, Melbourne
  • Christian Brothers College, St Kilda

The Kajji Foundation Scholarship has been approved to be added to section 35.2 of the ABSTUDY Policy Manual, as meeting the requirements for students to be approved to receive away from home entitlements. The scholarships exempt income and do not affect ABSTUDY entitlements.

6

Cape York Institute (CYI) Academic Leaders - Secondary (ALS)

CYI offers a number of scholarships to Indigenous secondary school students in Queensland.

The ALS Scholarships cover the gap between ABSTUDY and school tuition and boarding fees. All financial benefits are paid directly to the school.

The scholarship is not treated as personal student income for the purposes of ABSTUDY. For social security purposes, this is also exempt. Subsection 8(8)(zjb) of the Social Security Act 1991 provides for exemption for tuition fees and subsection 8(8)(za) of the Social Security Act 1991 exempts board and lodging.

The student's record should be clearly noted on the 'NCL' or 'EoY' DOC (annotate if necessary) that the student is in receipt of a HEP Scholarship for that particular year, and this is not included as personal income in the assessment of entitlement. See Assessing scholarships and mobility provisions for secondary students for ABSTUDY away from home rate.

7

Rosemary Bishop Indigenous Education Scholarships scheme

These scholarships fund, on behalf of the student, part or all of their tuition fees, boarding fees and other course charges. Therefore, the scholarship is not treated as income as per section 59.5.3 in the ABSTUDY Policy Manual. Students in receipt of this scholarship may also approved to receive the away from home entitlements under 35.2.

For social security purposes, this is also exempt. Subsection 8(8)(zjb) of the Social Security Act 1991 provides for exemption for tuition fees and subsection 8(8)(za) of the Social Security Act 1991 exempts board and lodging.

8

Indigenous Cadetship Scholarship (ICS)

The Indigenous Cadetship Scholarship (ICS) is a Commonwealth Government funded program provided by the Department of the Prime Minister and Cabinet.

Students in receipt of the ICS are not eligible for ABSTUDY under Chapter 12.5 of the ABSTUDY Policy Manual (APM) as they are unable to receive Australian Government assistance from two sources for the same course of study.

A to D list of scholarships and application for social security and ABSTUDY payments

Table 2

Item

Description

1

Allowance to cover out of pocket expenses

Allowances received as part of a scholarship, to cover out of pocket expenses (such as photocopying, postage, printing and similar expenses), are not assessed as income. The customer would normally be required to present an itemised claim for reimbursement, along with receipts, in order to receive the allowance.

2

Any other scholarship that is paid directly to a student

These scholarships are treated as income for social security and ABSTUDY payments. The value of the scholarship must be declared. This should be considered as an equity and merit scholarship (EMS) and it attracts the threshold exemption under subsection 8(8)(zjd) of the Social Security Act 1991.

Exception: Commonwealth Trade Learning Scholarships

3

Any other scholarship that is paid to a third party (for example, an education institution) in respect of a student

If the purpose of the scholarship payment is to cover a customer's tuition fees and/or board and lodging costs, it is not assessable as income under section 8.(8)(zj) of the Social Security Act 1991 and ABSTUDY Policy Manual section 59.5.3.

If its purpose is for any other reason, the payment is regarded as 'valuable consideration' and is treated as income. The value of this scholarship and its purpose must be declared.

4

Approved scholarships awarded outside Australia

These scholarships are not assessable as income. Subsection 8.(8)(zj) of the Social Security Act 1991 exempts 'approved scholarships' and Section 24A of the Social Security Act 1991 and ABSTUDY Policy Manual section 59.5.4.6 allows for disallowable instrument to determine what scholarships are exempt under this provision. There are only 3:

  • Rotary Foundation Ambassadorial Scholarship - provided it does not contain any component for living expenses
  • Cambridge Commonwealth Trust Fees Scholarship
  • Scholarships awarded by the Association of Mouth and Foot Painting Artists, Liechtenstein

5

Australian Postgraduate Award

These scholarships are treated as income for social security payments. The value of the scholarship must be declared to Services Australia. This should be considered as an equity and merit scholarship (EMS) and it attracts the threshold exemption under subsection 8(8)(zjd) of the Social Security Act 1991.

Note: on 1 January 2017, all Australian Postgraduate Award (APA) scholarship recipients were transferred to the Research Training Program (RTP) scholarship (found below).

6

Commonwealth Trade Learning Scholarship

The Commonwealth Trade Learning Scholarship is a $500 payment provided to Australian apprentices when they have successfully completed the first and second year of their apprenticeship.

This is exempt from the income test under subsection 8(8)(zia) of the Social Security Act 1991.

This is administered by Department of Education through the Australian Apprenticeship Support Network (Apprenticeship Network) providers.

7

Commonwealth Education Costs Scholarship (CECS)

There are 2 categories of CECS:

  • Indigenous CECS - students enrolled in undergraduate courses or a graduate diploma (or equivalent post graduate course of study)
  • Indigenous Enabling CECS - students enrolled in eligible enabling courses

These scholarships are exempt under subsection 8(8) (zja) of the Social Security Act 1991 and ABSTUDY Policy Manual section 59.5.4.1.

Note: receipt of a CECS prevents qualification for a Student Start-up Loan (SSL) under Section 592F of the Social Security Act 1991 and ABSTUDY Policy Manual 103.6.

8

Commonwealth Accommodation Scholarship (CAS)

There are 2 categories of CAS:

  • Indigenous CAS
  • Indigenous Enabling CAS

Note: CAS is not taken into account when calculating Rent Assistance (RA) entitlement. RA is calculated on the amount of rent paid by the student, without discounting it by the scholarship amount.

Note: receiving a CAS prevents qualification for a 'Relocation Scholarship' under Section 592J of the Social Security Act 1991 and section 102 in the ABSTUDY Policy Manual. Customers are unable to receive ABSTUDY Residential Costs Option (RCO) payment for the same scholarship period as a CAS unless the offer of the placement at the residential college or hostel was received after the scholarship was paid. See Payment of Residential Costs Option (RCO) for ABSTUDY and ABSTUDY Pensioner Education Supplement (PES).

9

Destination Australia Scholarship Program

This program will support both Australian and international students to study in regional Australia. Over 1,000 scholarships of $15,000 will be offered per year to support the study and living expenses associated with studying a Certificate IV to Doctorate level qualification at a regional campus of an eligible tertiary education provider.

This scholarship program delivers on the Government's commitment to attract more students, both domestic and international, to study in regional locations.

This should be considered as an equity and merit scholarship (EMS) and it attracts the threshold exemption under subsection 8(8)(zjd) of the Social Security Act 1991. The scholarship should be keyed as the full scholarship amount. The system auto-reduces the income by the threshold amount.

Table 3

Item

Description

1

Exempt income for Equity or Merit based Scholarship

The exempt income amount of $9,174 is spread out over the calendar year. The exempt income is divided by 26 fortnights.

$9,174 divided by 26 = $352.85 per fortnight.

A customer who has an Equity or Merit based Scholarship coded as fortnightly payments of $450 over 19 fortnights attract an equivalent proportion of the exempt income of $352.85 per fortnight. The income test is then applied to the remaining amount each fortnight of $97.15.

Even though the customer receives a total scholarship amount of $8,550 ($450 x 19 fortnights), they are only entitled to an exempt income reduction of $6,704.15 ($352.85 x 19 fortnights) as they are not receiving the scholarship over the whole calendar year.

2

Futurity (formerly known as Australian Scholarships Group)

Futurity (formerly known as the Australian Scholarships Group (ASG) Friendly Society Secondary Scholarship fund and Scholarship Benefit funds) are products that parents of the student contribute to.

This has specific tax breaks and when paid out to the student must be considered income.

It is not an equity or merit scholarship and therefore does not attract the equity and merit scholarship exemption.

Futurity scholarships should be keyed on the Other Income Summary (OINS) screen as Type, 'OTHER'.

3

Griffith Connect Academic Excellence Bursary

This scholarship is aimed at attracting high achieving students to study at Griffith University. It is a one-off payment of $3,000 paid within 6 weeks of the start of semester 1.

This bursary is awarded as a prize and is therefore an exempt lump sum and not considered ordinary income for social security and ABSTUDY payments.

4

Long Tan Bursary

The Department of Veterans' Affairs (DVA) provides a bursary payable to eligible children of Vietnam Veterans through the Vietnam Veterans Trust.

The bursary has been established to meet the cost of tertiary education and help them obtain formal qualifications and skills needed to pursue their chosen career. Tertiary study includes vocationally oriented TAFE courses, business colleges and arts schools etc. The bursary is valued at up to $9,000 over 3 years and can be used to help cover costs such as enrolment, course fees, and textbooks. Fifty bursaries are awarded across Australia.

This bursary is treated as income of the individual and is assessed in the following manner:

  • The lump sum amount paid at the beginning of the academic year (generally March) is assessed as income over 12 months from when the person was entitled to receive the amount
  • Similar treatment is applied to the receipt of a smaller lump sum paid for the commencement of the second semester (that is it is regarded as a lump sum for assessment purposes)
  • The remainder of the year's money is paid in 10 monthly equal instalments during the academic year (that is March to December inclusive) and are assessed throughout that period as periodic payments

This should be considered as an equity and merit scholarship (EMS) and it attracts the threshold exemption under subsection 8(8)(zjd) of the Social Security Act 1991. The scholarship should be keyed as the full scholarship amount. The system auto-reduces the income by the threshold amount.

5

Non-discretionary scholarship provided by an external provider that pays part or all course fees or charges and board and lodging

These scholarships are provided by external providers such as businesses, charities or government departments and pay, on behalf of the customer part or all of their course fees or charges and board and lodging.

These scholarships are not treated as income for social security payments and ABSTUDY.

From 11 May 2004, all non-discretionary fee-waive or fee-pay scholarships were not be counted as income under the Social Security Act 1991. This means the value of these scholarships was no longer taken into account when applying the income tests for pensions and allowances.

6

Omscholing Dansers Nederland

Payments from Omscholing Dansers Nederland are made up of 2 separate parts:

  • refund of study costs, and
  • monthly ongoing payments

Refund of study costs

The refund of study costs includes tuition fees, travel costs, books and materials, and the cost of a computer. Refunds do not count as income for social security income test purposes, if the customer satisfies Centrelink they incurred the costs. See Additional allowance in the Social Security Guide 4.3.9.40 Income from scholarships.

Monthly ongoing payments

Assess monthly ongoing payments as a merit and equity based scholarship (EMS) under for social security income test purposes. It attracts the threshold exemption under subsection 8(8)(zjd) of the Social Security Act 1991. Key the scholarship as the full scholarship amount. The system auto-reduces the income by the threshold amount.

7

RAMUS (Rural Australian Medical Undergraduate Scholarships)

This is a national scheme to assist with the costs of studying in metropolitan areas.

Where payment of the scholarship is delayed, and a lump sum payment is received, the date from which the continuous earnings is assessed is the date on which the lump sum was received.

This should be considered as an equity and merit scholarship (EMS) and it attracts the threshold exemption under subsection 8(8)(zjd) of the Social Security Act 1991. The scholarship should be keyed as the full scholarship amount. The system auto-reduces the income by the threshold amount.

8

Relocation Scholarship (RS)

RS replaced the Commonwealth Accommodation Scholarship (CAS) in 2010 and assists university students who have to live away from the family home to study. However, universities continue to offer CAS as a limited program for Aboriginal and Torres Strait Islander students only.

RS is paid to eligible Youth Allowance (YA) or ABSTUDY and is not means tested.

RS is paid annually at the beginning of each year for up to 4 years.

RS is exempt from the income test under subsection 8(8AA) of the Social Security Act 1991 and ABSTUDY Policy Manual section 59.5.4.1.

9

Research Training Program (RTP)

These scholarships are treated as income for social security payments. The value of the scholarship must be declared to Services Australia. This should be considered as an equity and merit scholarship (EMS) and it attracts the threshold exemption under subsection 8(8)(zjd) of the Social Security Act 1991.

Note: students receiving a RTP Scholarship are not eligible for ABSTUDY. See ABSTUDY eligibility and requirements.

On 1 January 2017, RTP replaced the Australian Postgraduate Award (APA), International Postgraduate Research Scholarship (IPRS) and Research Training Scheme (RTS).

10

Rotary Foundation Ambassadorial Scholarships

This is an 'approved scholarship' awarded overseas and is exempt under subsection 8(8)(zj) of the Social Security Act 1991 and ABSTUDY Policy 59.5.4.6.

11

Rural Tertiary Hardship Fund

These scholarships are treated as exempt income for social security and ABSTUDY payments.

S to V list of scholarships and application for social security and ABSTUDY payments

Table 4

Item

Description

1

Scholarship from accommodation provider that fully or partially reduces or waives board and lodging charges

These scholarships are not treated as income for social security payments under subsection 8(8)(za) of the Social Security Act 1991 and ABSTUDY Policy Manual section 59.5.3. If other components are paid, for example, a living allowance, the other components are assessable.

2

Scholarship from an educational institution that fully or partially reduces or waives tuition fees or course charge

The part of the scholarship that covers the fees waived or reduced is not regarded as income.

From 1 January 2004, scholarships offered by higher education institutions that:

  • fully waive, or
  • grant a full exemption from fees or contribution amounts, or
  • exempt a student from a Student Contribution charge

are excluded from the Social Security Act 1991 Income Tests under subsection 8(8)(za) of the Social Security Act 1991 and ABSTUDY Policy Manual section 59.5.3.

Note: if other components are paid for example, a living allowance, the other components are assessable and should be considered as an equity and merit scholarship (EMS).

3

Scholarship in the nature of a reward or prize

These scholarships are defined by the following characteristics:

  • the payment of the lump sum is unlikely to be repeated, and
  • the scholarship (reward/prize) could not be reasonably expected to be received or necessarily anticipated, and
  • the payment of the lump sum does not represent receipt of money for services rendered directly or indirectly

These scholarships are not treated as income for social security and ABSTUDY payments.

4

Teacher Training Scholarships

If a customer is undertaking studies, any Teacher Training Scholarships received for themselves or their partner is assessed as income. Any additional allowance in respect of dependent children reduces the additional income free area for dependent children. Any allowance paid to the student child is the child's own income.

This should be considered as an equity and merit scholarship (EMS) and it attracts the threshold exemption under subsection 8(8)(zjd) of the Social Security Act 1991. The scholarship should be keyed as the full scholarship amount. The system auto-reduces the income by the threshold amount.

5

Tertiary Accommodation Grants

Tertiary Accommodation Grants are considered an Equity and Merit based Scholarship (EMS) and attracts the threshold exemption under subsection 8(8)(zjd) of the Social Security Act 1991. Key the full scholarship amount, the system will auto-reduce the income by the threshold amount.

6

Victorian Bushfire Appeal Fund Scholarship

These scholarships are treated as income for social security and ABSTUDY payments. The value of the scholarship must be declared to Services Australia.

Note: they are recognised as Equity or Merit based Scholarships and therefore attract the annual exemption amount.
