Scenario - IM exemption application grace period for customers with dependent children

Table 1:

Scenario

Description

1

Grace period scenario

Sarah is 23 years old and has 2 dependent children. Sarah has been on Parenting Payment Single (PPS) for 13 weeks in the last 26 weeks so has been sent a letter asking to contact Services Australia to talk about Income Management.

  • As Sarah lives in a remote area, Sarah has 56 days to contact before Auto Income Management is applied
  • Sarah contacts the agency after three weeks. At this contact Sarah indicates they would like to apply for an exemption from Income Management
  • After applying for an exemption, Sarah is given 28 days to provide evidence for the exemption
  • At the end of this grace period, if an exemption has not been granted, Sarah will have another 56 days to complete an initial assessment interview before Auto Income Management is applied
  • Sarah can only have one grace period

Scenario - Review of Income Management exemption scenarios

Table 2:

Scenario

Exemption review scenarios

1

Last dependent child leaves care

Bev has two dependent children and was granted an exemption from Income Management on the 7th of May after meeting all relevant criteria under the responsible parenting category.

On the 25th of August, the dependent children leave Bev's care to live with Roy (the other parent). As Bev has no dependent children in care, the exemption can be reviewed and ended. Bev can still apply for an exemption under the participation in education or training category should the criteria be met.

2

Change in principal carer of child

Jenny (receiving Parenting Payment Partnered) and Hugo (receiving JobSeeker Payment (JSP)) are a couple with three dependent children. Jenny currently has an exemption from Income Management under the responsible parenting category. Hugo is currently being income managed.

As Jenny has found some casual work and Hugo will be the principal carer of the children, they have decided Jenny will claim JSP and Hugo Parenting Payment. Once the payments are granted, Hugo claims an exemption from Income Management.

As only one person can claim an exemption under the responsible parenting category where both parents/principal carers are income managed, Hugo can be granted an exemption (subject to relevant criteria being met), and Jenny's exemption would be ended. Jenny can claim an exemption under the participation and training category should the criteria be met.

3

Death of a child

Where a customer has been granted an exemption for a 12 month period, and the sole or last eligible dependent child passes away, the exemption will continue until its expiry date. At that time the parent/principal carer would not be eligible to reapply for another exemption on responsible parenting grounds, should they have no dependent children in their care.

Note: although this is a significant change in circumstance, the Department of Social Services (DSS) has confirmed in these situations the exemption is to remain in place for the duration of the 12 month exemption period.

Scenario - Evidence required for an Income Management exemption

Table 3:

Example

Description

1

Verifying health check and immunisation scenario

Hugh is a four month old baby taken to a health clinic. Hugh's principal carer (Gemma) would need verification of both Hugh's health check and immunisation having occurred at this visit, if these were the relevant two out of three health checks Gemma chooses to submit towards an exemption on responsible parenting grounds.

2

Other scenarios of evidence required

Hugh's older sister, Eloise, has just turned four years old, so Gemma (again, if these are the two selected health activities of the three possible) would need to provide evidence of Eloise receiving each of:

  • The four year old immunisation
  • The three to four year old health check, and
  • The participation in preschool, or two other engagement activities

Gemma had also received a letter advising that because Eloise has turned four they have been selected for the Healthy Start for School initiative. The evidence Gemma provided to prove the three to four year old health check had been completed for Eloise could also be used to update the health check status to satisfy the Healthy Start for School requirements.

Scenario - IM exemption assessments

Table 4:

Example

Description

1

Customer receiving PPP and IM under Disengaged Youth Measure

Wendy is in receipt of Parenting Payment Partnered (PPP) and is being income managed under the Disengaged Youth measure. Wendy has a 3 year old daughter named Skye.

Wendy applies for an exemption from Income Management and the delegate determines Wendy is not financially vulnerable.

Wendy has made sure Skye is up-to-date with the immunisations and health and development checks and this information is recorded in the personal health and immunisation record.

Wendy reports to Services Australia that Skye has been taken to a regular, informal playgroup twice a week that has been set up by a group of young parents who meet in the park for several hours while their children play together. Based on this information Wendy is granted an exemption from Income Management.

2

Customer receiving PPS and IM under the LTWPR measure

Kurt is receiving Parenting Payment Single (PPS) and is being income managed under the Long Term Welfare Payment Recipient measure. Kurt is the carer for 2 children, Troy (aged 15 months) and Barnaby (aged 1 month).

Kurt has applied for an exemption from Income Management and the delegate determines that Kurt is not financially vulnerable.

Barnaby is up-to-date with the immunisations and health and development checks but Troy is not. Troy has received all age appropriate immunisations but has not received all required health and development checks in the previous 12 months. Troy received a 4 month and 12 month health and development check but missed the 6 - 8 month health and development check. In this situation Kurt is not granted an exemption from Income Management.

When Kurt re-applies for an exemption 6 months later, Kurt is able to demonstrate that Troy, who is now 21 months, has received all age appropriate health checks from the previous 12 months. Barnaby is still up-to-date with the health and development checks and immunisations. Based on this information Kurt is granted an exemption.
