Available advance amount

Table 1: this table explains how to identify if a customer has an available advance payment amount through the HEAS.

Step

Action

1

Eligibility criteria + Read more ...

To get an advance payment of a HEAS loan, customers must meet the eligibility criteria required to participate in the HEAS.

See Eligibility for the Home Equity Access Scheme (HEAS) for more details.

If the customer meets the eligibility criteria, they can get an advance payment if:

  • their loan balance is less than their maximum loan amount (MLA), and
  • they have received less than 2 advance payments in the past 26 fortnights, and
  • they have an available advance amount. The most a person can receive as an advance payment in a 26 fortnight period is 50% of the maximum pension rate

To help customers identify if they have an available advance amount, go to Step 2.

2

Home Equity Access Scheme summary + Read more ...

Customers with a current HEAS loan can determine if they can get an advance by viewing their loan information in their myGov Centrelink online account.

To estimate the effect on their fortnightly loan payments, the customer can use the Home Equity Access Scheme calculator.

Tell the customer to open the dropdown Menu in their online account then:

  • select Payments and claims
  • select Manage payments
  • select Home Equity Access Scheme summary

The summary page shows customers:

  • details of prior advance payments issued, and
  • if they have an advance amount available to access

Is the customer able to use their online account to view their available advance amount?

  • Yes,
    • record the contact in a DOC
    • tell the customer to check their available advance online
    • tell the customer that their fortnightly loan payments will reduce if they take an advance payment
    • procedure ends here, unless the customer asks for help on how to apply for an advance request, go to Table 2
  • No,
    • if the customer has a current loan, go to Step 3
    • if the customer does not have a current loan, go to Step 5. Note: see Table 2 for help identifying if a current loan exists

3

Check maximum loan amount (MLA) + Read more ...

The MLA is the most a person can be paid through the HEAS. If a customer's loan balance has reached their MLA, no further loan payments can be issued.

The MLA is recorded on the Home Equity Access Scheme Details (HEAS) screen in the Maximum Loan Amount field. If the customer has nominated to limit their MLA to a lesser amount, this is recorded in the Customer Advised Max Amt field. The lower value is the MLA used in the eligibility assessment.

Check the Current Balance field on the HEAS. Is the balance less than the MLA?

  • Yes, go to Step 4
  • No,
    • the customer cannot get an advance payment as they have reached their MLA
    • if the customer wants to increase their MLA by offering more equity in their Australian real estate, see Security for the Home Equity Access Scheme
    • go to Step 5

4

Home Equity Access Scheme Advance screen + Read more ...

To check for previous advance payments and to see if the customer has an advance amount available, go to the Home Equity Access Scheme Advance (HEASADV) screen in Process Direct.

The screen shows:

  • How many advances the customer has been paid
  • The Maximum Advance Payment Amount

If the Maximum Advance Payment Amount is greater than zero, and the customer has received less than 2 advances in the last 26 fortnights, they can apply to get an advance payment.

Does the customer have an available advance amount?

  • Yes,
    • tell the customer the amount and go to Table 2 if they need help to apply
    • record the details in a DOC
    • procedure ends here
  • No,
    • tell the customer there is currently no available advance amount
    • record the details in a DOC and go to Step 5

5

Loan eligibility and loan estimator calculators + Read more ...

Encourage customers to use the Home Equity Access Scheme loan eligibility calculator to self-identify if they are eligible to:

  • participate in the HEAS, and
  • get an advance payment

The calculator also shows the available advance amount, and any changes to the customer's current fortnightly loan payments.

Customers get the most accurate results if they access the calculator via their myGov Centrelink online account. Tell the customer to open the dropdown Menu in their online account then:

  • select Payments and claims
  • select Manage payments
  • select Home Equity Access Scheme summary

Customers can also access the eligibility calculator via the Services Australia website by searching for 'Home Equity Access Scheme'.

Procedure ends here.

Request an advance via a HEAS variation form (SA497) + Read more ...

If the customer cannot use the Request advance payment online service, they can apply using the Home Equity Access Scheme variation form (SA497).

Tell the customer that their fortnightly loan payments will reduce if they take an advance payment and to use the Home Equity Access Scheme calculator.

Encourage customers to apply online if possible.

Explain the benefits of applying online to the customer, for example:

  • streaming questions and early warnings if they may not be eligible. Note: for more details about why a customer may be ineligible for an advance, see Table 1
  • easy to upload supporting documents, and provide consent online
  • less details for the customer to provide, as details from their Centrelink record pre-populate into the online request
  • accurate estimates via the authenticated calculator of the:
    • maximum advance payment amount available, and
    • impact the advance payment will have on the fortnightly loan payment

If the customer declines the online offer and wants to use the SA497, tell them:

  • to download the form from the Services Australia website
  • details about how to lodge the request are provided in the form

If a SA497 has been lodged and not scanned, Service Officers must make sure:

  • all mandatory questions have been answered, and
  • the supporting documentation is provided before uploading on Day 1

See Attaching electronic documents to a Centrelink customer's record using Document Tools in Customer First for how to scan the form and documents to Document Tools on the customer's record. Go to Step 6

For non Complex Assessment Officer (CAO) processing staff only:

Help customers to request an advance payment online + Read more ...

If a customer requires help with the Request advance payment online service, use Customer Online View to provide support:

  • viewing the details the customer is enquiring about
  • using the Home Equity Access Scheme summary to help inform the customer about their loan and available advance amounts
  • accessing the Home Equity Access Scheme calculators to help the customer with identifying their loan eligibility and rate

Customer Online View does not allow staff to save information they enter when helping a customer, or to start or submit an advance request for the customer.

See Customer Online View for Service Officers for more details. Go to Step 6

6

Advance payment additional questions and documentation + Read more ...

Some customers may need to answer additional questions and provide supporting documentation to establish or re-confirm their eligibility before the advance request outcome is determined.

For example, if it has been more than 13 weeks since the customer last received a fortnightly loan payment or advance payment through the HEAS.

Customers that were asked additional questions in the last 13 weeks will not be asked to answer them again.

Additional questions may relate to:

  • relationship status, to confirm the correct rate of payment (single or partnered) and identify consent requirements
  • real estate, to confirm any changes to the secured property or mortgages

Customers can also request other variations to their loan when submitting their advance request. For example, to stop or start their fortnightly loan payments.

The Resources page has a table on documents required for advance requests.

Go to Step 7, for details about voluntary withdrawal of advance requests if required. Otherwise, the procedure ends here.

7

Withdrawal of an advance request + Read more ...

Customers can withdraw their request for an advance payment at any time before the request is processed and finalised.

Verbal requests for withdrawal can be accepted unless:

  • the customer is a member of a couple and their partner has already provided consent to the advance request, or
  • a nominee submitted the request and the customer and (if applicable) partner have both already provided consent

If all consent has been provided, the withdrawal request must be in writing and signed by the customer and (if applicable) their partner.

Service Officers can request withdrawal action by the Home Equity Access Scheme National team. Use Fast Note, select Auto text, use Complex Assessment > Request for CAO action > CAO Home Equity Access Scheme.

Verbal withdrawal of an advance request must only be actioned if the Home Equity Access Scheme National team is satisfied of the customer's or correspondence nominee's identity.

See Authenticating a Centrelink customer for details on how to establish and confirm the identity of the person verbally requesting the withdrawal.

Home Equity Access Scheme National team only

  • Go to Table 4 for the process to withdraw an advance request via Process Direct
  • If the advance request has already been processed and resulted in a grant outcome, the request cannot be withdrawn
  • The customer has the option of repaying the advance immediately via the normal repayment channels

Note: immediate repayment of the advance will not reinstate the customer's fortnightly loan payments. Once the advance is paid, if the customer was not aware that their fortnightly loan payments would reduce and pays the advance back, this will not reinstate their fortnightly loan payments. The repayment of the advance simply reduces the loan balance.

See Repayment of Home Equity Access Scheme (HEAS) loans.

Check payment history and loan variation requests + Read more ...

The processing of advance requests must be done by a HEAS National Complex Assessment Officer (CAO) if:

  • the advance request is accompanied by a loan variation request, for example: the customer has requested to stop or restart their fortnightly loan payments
  • it has been more than 13 weeks since the customer received fortnightly loan payments, or an advance payment. Check the Payment Summary (PS) screen for payment components LON and LONA

Has the customer requested loan variations, or has it been more than 13 weeks since they received a loan payment?

  • Yes, the variations must be processed with the advance payment request. If the request has been allocated to a non-CAO Home Equity Access Scheme National staff member:
    • Go to Keyword screen in Process Direct, select keyword option HEASTFR which will adjust the work item to the correct skill set
    • Place work item on hold for 1 day
    • Un-assign the work item using reason 'Service Support Referral'. There is no requirement to monitor work item further. When due it will reallocate automatically
    • Procedure ends here for non-CAO staff
    • Go to Step 5 to continue processing the advance payment request

5

Review the Loan Details screen + Read more ...

Staff must review the HEAS Advance (HEASADV) screen in Process Direct as part of the advance payment request. Once reviewed: Keyword ‘HEASADV’ and complete in this specific order:

  • Select ‘Add’
  • Complete the HEAS Advance fields and save
  • Enter Receipt Date and Channel information
  • Select ‘Assess’ and ‘Finalise’

Once the advance is complete, superkey ‘HEAS’ to confirm correct details.

IF the customer is requesting an ‘Advance only’, immediately after coding the advance:

  • Investigate the record before changing the indicator to N, as this may:
    • override a prior eligibility decision
    • start fortnightly loan payments
  • Make amendments:
    • Update the Ceased Loan Indicator = Y with reason ‘Voluntary Withdrawal’
    • Set the Loan Topup Type to ADV
    • Change Percent to 0
    • Change Loan Topup Amt pf to be blank
    • Select ‘Assess’ and ‘Finalise’

Advance payments cannot be paid if the indicator is already set to Y.

If the advance request cannot be completed via Process Direct, updates may be required within customer record under the PLS and PLSS screen, complete in this specific order:

  • The customer has asked to receive an advance payment, but does not want to get fortnightly loan payments. Within the same activity, staff must:
    • ensure the Date of Effect is correct
    • set the Loan Topup Type to ADV
    • change Percent to 0
    • change Loan Topup Amt pf to be blank
    • code Advance under PLSS

Once the advance is complete, confirm correct details on PLS screen.

IF customers is requesting an ‘Advance only’, immediately after coding the advance, in a new activity:

  • Investigate the record before changing the indicator to N, as this may:
    • override a prior eligibility decision
    • start fortnightly loan payments
  • Under PLS screen, amend the Ceased Loan Indicator = Y with reason ‘Voluntary Withdrawal’. Advance payments cannot be paid if the indicator was already set to Y.

If the customer has requested a variation to their loan arrangements at the same time as requesting the advance complete this as a separate activity to address the variation request.

If the customer has not requested fortnightly loan payments, the Loan Topup Type must be set to ADV with Percent as 0 before changing the Cease Loan Indicator from Y to N.

Changing the indicator from Y to N also requires the Reject/Cease reason code to be removed. Go to Step 6.

6

Code the HEAS advance details + Read more ...

  • Go to the Home Equity Access Scheme Advance (HEASADV) screen:
    • the system automatically calculates the maximum advance amount available to the customer. The advance amount entered must be equal to, or less than this amount
    • the advance amount requested by the customer provisionally pre-populates if the request is lodged online
    • for paper form requests, add the advance amount manually
  • Service Officers must phone customers if the customer is not eligible for the amount they have requested. Customers can:
    • withdraw their application, Service Officers can action the withdrawal
    • accept a lower amount. If so, processing continues

To manually add/edit the provisional advance amount, or to reject the advance request, select Edit. Advance requests can be rejected if the customer has:

  • received 2 advance payments in the previous 26 fortnights
  • no available advance
  • reached their maximum loan amount (MLA)
  • withdrawn the request
  • failed to provide consent

Will the advance request be rejected?

  • Yes, go to Step 7
  • No, go to Step 8

7

Contact customer + Read more ...

Before rejecting the request, the Decision Maker must contact the customer to provide an explanation of the decision, and an opportunity for the customer to provide further details. Action as follows:

  • Make genuine attempts to phone the applicant
  • Tell the applicant of the rejection outcome, and explain their review and appeal rights
  • Record the outcome of each contact (including unsuccessful contacts) in the Progress of Claim DOC via the Document List (DL) screen

See Advising verbally of an unfavourable decision, for more details. Go to Step 8

8

Finalise the request + Read more ...

Select Assess:

  • the Entitlements (ELD) screen displays with the provisional outcome. If the outcome is not correct, make relevant coding updates before proceeding to finalise the request
  • select Finalise to complete the request
  • add any relevant details about the assessment to the Note. A dialog box will display and confirm when the Work Item is successfully finalised

The following automated process occurs after the Work Item is finalised (these updates require overnight processing before they can be viewed on the customer's record):

  • A letter automatically generates and sends to the customer to advise of the outcome (grant or rejection). The letter displays on the History Summary (HS) screen, the day after completing the activity
  • For grant outcomes:
    • The advance payment automatically adds to the customer's loan balance on the Home Equity Access Scheme Details (HEAS) screen
    • If the loan is secured against the customer's principal home, the advance payment is recorded on the Direct Investments (SVDI) screen as Investment Type HEA. For more details about making updates to the SVDI coding post-grant, see Table 4

Note: HEAS Service Officers with activities submitted for QMA checking should go to the Keyword screen in Process Direct. Select keyword option <HEASTFR> and adjust the work item for QMA work allocation. This action is not required by CAOs processing HEAS.

Assessment of advance payments as an asset

Table 4: this table explains how to identify and make updates to advance payments that have been recorded as an investment.

Step

Action

1

Advance payments are an assessable asset + Read more ...

If the customer has secured their principal home for their HEAS loan, advance payments paid through the HEAS are an assessable asset.

For the purposes of the income and asset tests for income support payments, the advance payment is:

  • exempt from the assets test assessment for the first 90 days
  • subject to deeming provisions under the income test, and
  • assessed as an asset after 90 days

If the customer (or their partner) receive a rate of pension, their rate may be affected.

2

Check if the advance payment has been granted + Read more ...

Go to the Home Equity Access Scheme Advance (HEASADV) screen in Process Direct.

Has the customer been granted an advance payment under the HEAS?

  • Yes, go to Step 3
  • No, procedure ends here

3

Confirm loan security + Read more ...

Go to screens Home Equity Access Scheme Details (HEAS) and Real Estate/Business Summary (REBS) to confirm which property the customer has secured their loan against.

Has the customer secured the loan against their principal home?

  • Yes, go to Step 4
  • No, the advance payment is not an assessable asset. Procedure ends here

4

Advance payment recorded on SVDI + Read more ...

The system automatically records the advance payment on the Direct Investments (SVDI) screen as a financial investment, after the advance payment is granted. This cannot be coded onto SVDI by staff.

The coding displays the day after the advance payment grant is finalised. The advance payment coding on SVDI includes the following details:

  • Type: HEA
  • Balance: full amount of the advance payment
  • Event Date: equal to the grant date of the advance payments
  • Assets Test Dedn Amt $ field in Customer First and Loan/Encumbrance Amt $ field in Process Direct: full amount of the advance payment

To check if the advance payment has been recorded go to the Savings Summary (SVS) screen. Does the advance payment display as an investment type?

  • Yes, go to Step 6
  • No, go to Step 5

5

Investigate and fix task locking conflicts + Read more ...

  • If an advance payment is not automatically added to the Direct Investments (SVDI) screen, this may have been prevented due to a task locking conflict with another activity
  • To check if this has occurred, go to the Trans/Trigger List (TTL) screen in Customer Record, and look for the HEASAV trigger:
    • If the trigger displays, this confirms the system has made an attempt to record the advance payment on SVDI, but the update was prevented
    • The system will continue to attempt the update for 14 days and then create a Manual Follow Up (MFU) activity with keyword HEASAVC

To investigate and fix the conflict:

  • If the MFU with keyword HEASAVC has already been created, the Home Equity Access Scheme National team must manually update SVDI:
    • see Home Equity Access Scheme (HEAS) reviews for the procedure to process the MFU
  • If the MFU has not been created, and the SVDI screen is locked by a started activity, arrange for the activity to be finalised. This will allow the automated update to proceed overnight

Has the fix resulted in the advance payment being updated on SVDI?

  • Yes, procedure ends here
  • No, escalate to the Home Equity Access Scheme National team for further investigation:
    • Use Fast Note - select Auto text, use Complex Assessment > Request for CAO action > CAO Home Equity Access Scheme
    • Do not close the Fast Note
    • The Home Equity Access Scheme National team will determine if a manual update is required, or if an incident needs to be raised via mySupport

6

Updating the balance of advance payments on SVDI + Read more ...

Customers are required to notify of updates to the balance of the advance payment to ensure correct assessments under the income and assets tests.

Customers can update the balance by:

  • logging into their myGov Centrelink online account
    • accessing the Banking module in the Income and assets summary
    • the advance payment displays to the customer as Home Equity Access Scheme advance payment
  • contacting the agency. If contacted:
    • get the details of the new balance and the date the balance reduced. Note: the balance of the HEAS advance payments should not be increased on Direct Investments (SVDI). If the customer has notified of an increase, escalate to the Home Equity Access Scheme National team
    • Select the HEA advance payment via the SVDI screen
    • code the new balance from the applicable Event Date
    • code the Asset Test Dedn Amt $: in Customer First or the Loan/Encumbrance Amt $: in Process Direct to equal the new balance unless the existing deduction amount is zero. If the existing deduction amount is zero, do not change it
    • DOC the details

Once the balance of the advance payment has reached zero, the customer is not required to make further notifications. Go to Step 7.

7

Reminder letter + Read more ...

  • If the balance of the HEAS advance payment has not reduced to zero, a letter automatically issues to the customer after 69 days
  • This is to remind customers that the asset test exemption period is about to end

8

Removal of asset test exemption + Read more ...

  • Advance payments under the HEAS are only exempt from the assets test for the first 90 days after being granted
  • The advance payment becomes assessable under the assets test on the 91st date
  • The system automatically removes the asset test exemption on the 91st day after the advance payment is granted. This occurs by setting the Asset Test Dedn Amt $: in Customer First or the Loan/Encumbrance Amt $: in Process Direct to zero on the Direct Investments (SVDI) screen
  • If the balance of the advance payment has reduced to zero, there is no impact to the rate of pension the customer, or their partner gets. There may be an impact to their pension rate if they have an asset-tested rate. The customer can continue to notify of changes to the balance of the advance payment at any time

Note: task locking conflicts may occur.
