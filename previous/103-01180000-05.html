Scenarios - Manual and automatic identification for Vulnerable Welfare Payment Recipient (VWPR) Income Management

This table has scenarios of customers who live in a VWPR M area or enhanced IM location who may be referred or automatically identified for the VWPR measure, and when social worker involvement may occur.

Item

Scenario

1

Job seeker has no money for food

Bill is a job seeker aged 23 years on JobSeeker Payment (JSP) and has been on payment for the last 8 weeks.

Bill lives with his grandfather who has a gambling problem.

Bill attends the local Alice Springs Service Centre to apply for an urgent payment as his grandfather has stolen his wallet. Bill tells the Service Officer he has not eaten in 2 days because he does not have any money for food.

A Display on Access DOC indicates Bill has a history of applying for urgent payments due to his wallet being stolen. The Service Officer is concerned Bill's grandfather may be stopping Bill from having enough money to pay for his essential expenses.

The benefits of volunteering for welfare quarantining are explained to Bill but he is not interested. Bill is offered assistance from a social worker.

The social worker meets with Bill and discusses a number of strategies to improve his situation. After considering all options available, the social worker determines welfare quarantining would be the most beneficial for Bill and he is assessed as a Vulnerable Welfare Payment Recipient. Bill is placed on VWPR enhanced IM for 12 months.

Note: as the IM program is closed to new customers since 4 September 2023, if the social worker determines welfare quarantining would be beneficial for Bill, he will commence on enhanced IM..

2

Customer owes rent to Housing Authority

Jodie is receiving the Disability Support Pension (DSP) and rents a unit from the State Housing Authority.

Jodie is 3 months in arrears with her rental payments and the State Housing Authority has sent a referral to Services Australia for Jodie to be assessed under the Vulnerable Welfare Payment Recipient measure.

The social worker meets with Jodie and discusses various options. Jodie was not assessed as a Vulnerable Welfare Payment Recipient by the social worker, but regular Rent Deduction Scheme deductions were set up to pay her arrears and her ongoing rent commitments.

3

Unreasonable to Live at Home (UTLAH) payment granted

Adrian is a 17 year old on Youth Allowance (YA) who has been granted the UTLAH rate of payment.

An automatic trigger will occur to include Adrian on the VWPR Youth measure under the UTLAH category.

Adrian contacts the local Service Centre extremely distressed and says he does not want to be welfare quarantined. He pays rent to friends and is about to become homeless because they do not want to have anything to do with Services Australia.

The Service Officer refers the customer to a social worker because he may be eligible for an exclusion as welfare quarantining may be detrimental to his wellbeing.

4

VWPR social worker assessed - Notice ends

John has been on JobSeeker Payment (JSP) for 14 months. Twelve months ago, a social worker determined John to be a Vulnerable Welfare Payment Recipient and John has been welfare quarantined for the last 12 months.

When the Notice is due to end, the social worker reviews John's circumstances and decides not to extend the Vulnerable notice.

However, as John meets the criteria under the Long Term Welfare Payment Recipient (LTWPR) measure, John will be identified by the system as eligible for welfare quarantining under that measure once the Vulnerable notice ends.

John will be sent a letter advising him he has commenced enhanced IM under the new measure.

5

VWPR Youth (UTLAH) - customer no longer a full time student

Jill receives Youth Allowance (YA) at the UTLAH rate and was automatically identified for VWPR Youth (UTLAH).

Two months after commencing welfare quarantining, Jill became a full time student and her welfare quarantining was ended.

Four months after ending welfare quarantining, Jill dropped out of her studies.

As it has been less than 12 months since Jill first commenced, she will be reinstated for the remainder of the 12 month period. If Jill commenced VWPR Youth (UTLAH) under IM (prior to 4 September 2023) she would be reinstated under IM

Note: this scenario also applies to customers placed on VWPR Youth (UTLAH and SpB) where it has not been 12 months since welfare quarantining first commenced:

  • they stop being an apprentice (only when they commenced the apprenticeship after welfare quarantining commenced)
  • an exclusion is manually or automatically ended before the end of the 12 month period
  • ineligible coding for reasons IIE (Incorrectly identified as eligible) or EPN (Excluded payment nominee) is removed
  • the higher measure to which they were transferred ends

The customer only needs to be receiving an eligible welfare payment and does not need to be living in a declared area for welfare quarantining to be reinstated.

6

VWPR Youth (CrP) - customer becomes a full time student

Jack receives JobSeeker Payment (JSP) and was automatically identified for VWPR Youth (CrP) as he was granted a Crisis Payment (CrP) on his release from prison.

Three months after commencing welfare quarantining, Jack became a full time student and welfare quarantining was ended.

Two months after he ends welfare quarantining, Jack dropped out of his studies.

Although it has been less than 12 months since Jackâ€™s welfare quarantining first commenced, he is not reinstated on VWPR Youth (CrP) as it has been more than 13 weeks since he received the CrP which made him eligible for welfare quarantining.

Note: if the CrP which made them eligible for twelfare quarantining was paid within the last 13 weeks, VWPR Youth (CrP) is reinstated when:

  • the customer is no longer a full time student or apprentice (where they became a full time student/apprentice after welfare quarantining)
  • an exclusion is manually or automatically ended
  • ineligible coding is removed for reasons EPN (Excluded payment nominee)
  • the higher measure to which they were transferred ends

The customer does not need to be living in an IM declared area or enhanced IM location, or under 25 for welfare quarantining to be reinstated.
