2

Review whether an SU1 is required + Read more ...

For most customers advising they, and/or their partner (if applicable), have ceased work in the last 12 months, an SU1 will be required for each employer. However, there are certain circumstances, where an SU1 may not be required. The following circumstances determine when an SU1 may not be required:

Single Touch Payroll (STP)

STP cessation data may pre-fill during statement reporting or within an online claim. If the cessation details are confirmed by the customer, an SU1 is not required for that employer, unless changes have been made to amounts paid or there is any doubt about the number of days for leave or termination payments.

Note: STP data does not prefill the number of days or period covered for these payments. Therefore it is important to review the days provided by the customer is reasonable for the amount paid, example: $3,000 for one day's leave is not reasonable and would need to be verified.

Casual or Part-Time Employees

If a customer is still employed but has had the number of working hours reduced, an SU1 is not required as their employment has not been terminated. If the customer was a casual or part-time employee but is no longer employed an SU1 is required.

Seasonal, Contract or Intermittent Workers

If the customer advises as part of an online claim they are a Seasonal, Contract or Intermittent Worker, the information required will be collected as part of the claim.

If the customer claims via other means (or it is only identified after the online claim has been submitted that the customer was a Seasonal, Contract or Intermittent Worker), an SU496 must be completed. For more information, refer to Seasonal Work Preclusion Period (SWPP).

Self-Employment

If the customer is deemed to be self-employed, a Module F - Business Details (MOD F) is required instead of an SU1.

Customer previously working overseas

There are no provisions in Social Security Law that exempt a customer from providing an SU1 from their previous employers.

Social Security Law authorises Services Australia to request relevant information from persons or entities based outside Australia. Requesting a written or verbal SU1 from an overseas employer may be impractical but overseas employers can use the National Business Gateway to verbally provide an SU1.

Wherever possible, alternative written evidence from the employer (for example, a letter on business letterhead) confirming details of any unused leave and/or redundancy payments paid upon ceasing employment should be provided.

Each case should however be assessed individually to determine the capacity of the customer to obtain the information. If the customer has attempted to obtain the information from the former employer and is unable, a statement should be taken from the customer outlining details of any unused leave and/or redundancy payments paid and the reasons for being unable to provide evidence from the overseas employer.

Employer not contactable/No longer operating

If the customer advises the employer ceased operations, Service Officers should endeavour to verify this information using available online sources - such as Google, Yellow Pages or Business.gov.au. See the Resources page for links.

If all reasonable avenues to find a suitable contact number and address for the employer have been exhausted, a statement should be taken from the customer outlining details of unused leave and redundancy payments paid and the reasons an SU1 could not be provided by the former employer. Request suitable evidence, for example, the last payslip or any correspondence received from the employer about the closure of the business on the business letterhead, if available.

Service Officers must clearly document what efforts have been made to verify the employer has ceased operations and cannot be contacted.

Youth Allowance Students, Austudy students and Australian Apprentices online claims

If the customer (or their partner) ceased work within the last 12 months and has been paid or is due to be paid any unused leave and/or termination payment, they may be subject to an Income Maintenance Period (IMP), an SU1 is required.

Customers who advise they and/or their partner have ceased work in the last 12 months and have not been paid and do not expect to be paid any unused leave and/or redundancy payments from any previous employers do not need to provide an SU1.

3

Check if the SU1 has been provided + Read more ...

If the customer is current on payment and has ceased employment, check that they have been asked to provide an SU1. If not, appropriate notification must be issued to the customer.

Current customers should be encouraged to upload the SU1 via the Upload documents service. Where the customer is not able to use Upload documents, they should lodge the SU1 at their local service centre to be scanned to the customer's record.

When claiming an income support payment, the online claim will identify if an Employment Separation Certificate is required and request this as part of the Next Steps process within the online claim. However, within the online claim questions, if a customer indicates they do not have an Employment Separation Certificate, the online claim will display a message stating 'I understand I need to provide this as soon as possible.'

To determine if there is a requirement to obtain an Employment Separation Certificate, check what information has been requested under the Tasks icon within Process Direct.

Tasks screen displays tasks, for example, documents the customer has been requested to provide. If the document has been returned online, a hyperlink will display in a blue box. Online modules submitted for the claim will also be displayed. Note: scanned documents or those submitted online will display on the Documents screen.

Documents screen displays scanned documents. This includes documents received through the Upload documents online service.

Except for students and apprentices claiming Youth Allowance and Austudy, if an SU1 has not been automatically requested because the customer advised no leave or redundancy payments have been paid or expected, an Employment Separation Certificate is still required. This will establish the reason for ceasing employment and whether any redundancy and/or unused leave payments were made.

Situations where a person is not required to provide an Employment Separation Certificate but verification is still required, is when the person was employed in the last 12 months but:

  • suffered sexual harassment or violence in the workplace, or
  • the previous employer has ceased business

In these situations, we do not ask the person to provide the Employment Separation Certificate. Verification should be obtained by phoning the employer, or sending the Q267 letter requesting an Employment Separation Certificate.

4

Customer contacts to discuss the requirement to provide an SU1 + Read more ...

If a customer contacts to query employment cessation details pre-filled in an online claim or during statement reporting, advise the customer that this information is shared with Services Australia from employers via Single Touch Payroll (STP). Employers are progressively registering to report income and employment cessation details via this service. Customers are to check, confirm or change pre-filled employer reported data. Confirmed STP data that has not had changes made to amounts paid and where there is no doubt about the number of days that leave or termination payments paid covers, does not require verification.

If the customer contacts Services Australia to advise:

  • they do not have an SU1
  • could not provide one, or
  • there is a document that relates to employment at the Participation Interview:
    • check the online or staff assisted claim for any STP employment cessation details, leave and termination payments confirmed by the customer (the source will display as STP). Verification is not required
    • discuss the customer's circumstances and determine if they are able to provide an SU1 or other sufficient documentation
    • check Customer First Third Party Information Summary (TPIS) screen to see if the employer has lodged the SU1 online

If there is no confirmed STP cessation data, or SU1 details on the TPIS screen, and the customer is unable to provide the documentation without assistance:

  • attempt to source a verbal SU1 from the employer(s) on the customer's behalf
  • If the customer has a claim in progress (submitted or in draft) Service Officers are to contact the employer on the customer's behalf to request the SU1. Contact can be by phone or via a Q267
  • DOC the record
    • Go to Step 7

If the customer is able to provide sufficient documentation and the documents:

  • are provided in the contact:
    • run the Request and Manage Customer Tasks guided procedure and set the SU001 - Employment Separation Certificate task to Provided
  • will be provided later:
    • DOC the record with the conversation
    • outline the documentation the customer is expected to provide and the timeframe
    • promote the use of the Express Plus Centrelink mobile app to provide the documents

If the customer has not yet submitted their claim, advise them to complete any outstanding Required tasks, including:

  • booking their jobseeker new claim appointment as identified at Next Steps, and
  • submit their claim in order to progress

Procedure ends here.

5

Employer contacts to discuss the requirement to provide an SU1 + Read more ...

  • If the employer does not wish to register for the Business Hub, the employer can forward the SU1 directly to National Business Gateway (NGB) who will then scan the SU1 to the customer's record. Procedure ends here
  • If the employer is international, obtain a verbal SU1. Go to Step 8

6

Claim submitted without an SU1 or sufficient document + Read more ...

Claims may be submitted without all supporting Required Tasks lodged when the claimant advises they are experiencing certain vulnerabilities. See Circumstance Change Monitor (CCM). Outstanding supporting documents must be provided for a claim to be assessed.

Confirm that an SU1 is required and that the customer has been advised to provide this via the online claim tasks. If not, or the customer has lodged their claim via another channel, issue a request for an SU1 to support the claim.

Review claim documents. If the customer has not contacted to advise they are having difficulty in obtaining an SU1 from their previous employer, or it is determined that the customer has intentionally supplied incorrect information, reject the claim as directed by the payment type.

See Rejecting a Centrelink claim.

7

Contact employer on behalf of the customer + Read more ...

While the customer is responsible for sourcing their SU1(s), an attempt to source a verbal SU1 from the employer(s) on the customer's behalf should be made where the customer advised that they are not able to obtain an SU1.

Ask the customer for details of all employers they ceased work for in the previous 12 months including employer and contact name, phone number (use White Pages online if the customer does not have it - see the Resources page for a link).

Make one genuine attempt to contact each employer using the information provided.

Have you been able to establish contact with the employer?

  • Yes, go to Step 8
  • No, issue a Q267 letter, see Table 2

8

Successful employer contact - verbal SU1 + Read more ...

If successful contact is made ensure:

  • the employer's identity is authenticated
  • the employer has access to and is authorised to provide the required information
  • the employer is willing to provide the evidence verbally

If there is any reason to question the accuracy of the information being provided or the identity of the employer, a verbal SU1 must not be accepted.

Is the employer able and willing to provide a verbal SU1?

  • Yes, record the verbal SU1 using Verbal SU1: Employer Fast Note. See Table 3
  • No, go to Step 9

9

Successful employer contact - unable to source verbal SU1 + Read more ...

If the employer is not able or willing to provide a verbal SU1 during the initial contact, a Q267 letter is to be sent to the employer.

If the employer prefers, they can provide a verbal SU1 at a later time, by calling the National Business Gateway (NBG). For contact details, see National Business Gateway (NBG). Note: NBG has a separate fax number for SU1s being returned.

If the employer refuses to provide an SU1 verbally or in writing, formal request for an SU1 must still be made to an employer.

To avoid placing the job seeker in financial hardship, staff must:

  • DOC the record outlining the conversation with the employer (time, date, phone number, person spoke to and relevant information)
  • Send a Q267, see Table 2

Creating a Q267 letter

Table 2

Step

Action

1

Confirm Q267 required + Read more ...

The Q267 letter is a notice under Social Security Law that provides the employer with 14 days to supply the required information. This letter also includes information on the options available for the employer to provide the SU1.

A Q267 letter should only be sent to the employer where:

  • every reasonable effort has been made by both the customer and Services Australia to source a written or verbal SU1, and
  • the employer is not based overseas, and
  • the customer cannot supply alternate written evidence from the employer that contains the information normally included in the SU1 (for example, a letter on business letterhead)

Is it necessary and appropriate to issue a Q267?

  • Yes, go to Step 2
  • No, go to Step 7

2

Confirm employer contact details + Read more ...

Confirm details of the previous employer, including the business name, ABN and location.

Where details have been provided by the customer, Service Officers should verify this information using available online sources such as Google, Yellow Pages and Business.gov.au to ensure the letter is addressed correctly. See Resources for links.

Create the Q267 letter shell + Read more ...

  • Code the appropriate Service Reason, for example if the customer is claiming JobSeeker Payment, record 'JSP'

Complete the Q267 letter + Read more ...

  • Once options have been selected, in the Command: field type 'DATA' and press [Continue/Enter] to display the letter text
  • Complete relevant details in the body of the letter including the free text fields, making sure the request:
    • is written in plain English
    • will be clear to the employer, and
    • has no grammatical errors

See the Resources page for an example of the free text. Navigate between pages by selecting the Next and Previous buttons

  • The letter text can be viewed/checked by typing 'Text' in the Command: field and pressing [Continue/Enter]. Navigate between pages by selecting the Next and Previous buttons. To return to the draft letter, in the Command: field type 'DATA' and press [Continue] to display the letter text

Finalise the Q267 letter + Read more ...

    Note: if the status is 'GE' (generated), the letter will not be printed. 'S'elect the letter and ensure that all options have been completed and that the printer ID is correct before again typing 'FIN' in the Command: field and pressing [Enter]
  • Return to the Activity List (AL) screen

Explain the letter has been sent however, in the interim the employer can download the SU1 via the Services Australia website or provide the information on business letterhead. The Resources page contains a link to the website.

6

Document and New Claim Actions + Read more ...

If the customer has submitted a new claim, make sure the request is clearly documented on the customer's record, and the new claim activity is held for 15 days after the Q267 was sent.

Invite the customer to provide a written statement including all the information provided on the SU1 in case the employer fails to respond to the Q267 within 14 days, for example:

  • name of company
  • period of employment
  • final payment amount on ceasing employment
  • breakdown of the payments paid
  • date payment paid

If the customer has not submitted their claim due to their outstanding task, advise them to monitor their online claim tasks via online services. When a response is received from the employer, the task will be updated to Provided. It is essential that the customer then:

  • books their jobseeker new claim appointment as identified at Next Steps, and
  • submits their claim in order to progress

7

Alternative evidence + Read more ...

The customer can provide a written statement (letter or Statement form) including all information normally provided on the SU1, if:

  • a Q267 letter has been sent and the employer has not responded within 14 days, or
  • it is not appropriate to send a Q267 (such as employer overseas), and
  • all other avenues including obtaining a verbal SU1 from employers have been exhausted

The customer should be asked to provide any other available documentation for example, termination letter or final payslip.

For a claim, once any employment separation details are provided:

  • run the Request and Manage Customer Tasks guided procedure and set the SU001 - Employment Separation Certificate task to Provided
  • advise the customer to lodge their claim, if they have not already done so

Otherwise, for current customers, assessment of any waiting and/or non-payment penalty periods can proceed.

To assess the information, see Table 3.

Assessing Employment Separation Certificates (SU1s)

Table 3

Step

Action

1

SU1 returned + Read more ...

An SU1 is critical evidence used to establish qualification to income support payments, assessment of applicable waiting or non-payment periods and any compensation payments. Service Officers must ensure all:

  • written evidence (for example, completed SU1s) are scanned to the customer's record and appropriately classified, and
  • other evidence (for example, verbal SU1s) are clearly documented on the customer's record - including details of how this evidence was sourced

Employment cessation details, unused leave and/or redundancy payments reported by employers via Single Touch Payroll will be pre-filled in online claims or Assisted Customer Claims (ACC). Staff can view this information within the Claim Summary in Process Direct. The source will be recorded in the claim as 'Employer Reported'.

Service officers must review all information contained in the SU1 to determine the job seeker's eligibility and payability for the income support payment.

Note: STP data does not prefill the number of days or period covered for these payments. Therefore, it is important to review the days provided by the customer is reasonable for the amount paid, example: $3,000 for one day's leave is not reasonable and would need to be verified.

Waiting periods

The information provided in the SU1 or via STP will determine if the claimant is subject to waiting periods.

The waiting periods for consideration are:

  • Unemployment Non-Payment Period (UNPP) Unemployment failures (UNF)
  • Income Maintenance Period (IMP)
  • Seasonal Work Preclusion Period (SWPP)
  • Compensation Preclusion Period

For more information, the various types of waiting periods a person may need to serve, see Waiting periods for income support payments.

  • If the claimant is subject to:
    • UNPP/UNF go to Step 2
    • IMP go to Step 3
    • SWPP go to Step 4
  • If the claimant has a Compensation Preclusion Period go to Step 5

Note: for Youth Allowance (YA) (Students and Apprentices) and Austudy customers who submit an Employment Separation Certificate (SU1) as part of their claim, data will be extracted from the SU1 and prepopulated into Process Direct. Open the scanned SU001 from the documents button to compare the information from the SU001 with what has been prepopulated into the relevant fields.

2

Unemployed due to a voluntary act or misconduct + Read more ...

When an STP employer reports that an employee ceased work due to reasons of 'voluntary cessation (V)' or 'dismissal (F)', mutual obligation customers are asked to supply more details as to why they are no longer employed. A compliance action is automatically generated for investigation.

Where an Employment Separation Certificate (SU1) has been provided (or information collected verbally by the Service Officer from the employer) and the employer has indicated the reason for separation was employee ceasing work voluntarily, misconduct as an employee, unsatisfactory work performance, or other, the Service Officer must consider whether compliance action should be generated for further investigation.

Where compliance action is generated, the job seeker must be advised that their reason for unemployment will be further investigated and a penalty may be applied.

Where a compliance event is identified at the same time a person is commencing or lodging a claim, the compliance investigation and decision regarding any compliance action must occur before the claim can be finalised as the determination has a direct impact on the job seeker's income support payment start date.

For further information, see Unemployment due to a voluntary act or misconduct.

3

Income Maintenance Period (IMP) + Read more ...

Customers may be subject to an IMP where they have ceased employment and have been paid out unused leave and/or redundancy payments. Where this occurs, the ISP is not payable for the period covered by these payments.

Unused leave and termination payments reported by STP employers are pre-filled in online claims, in Assisted Customer Claims (ACC) and in the Report Employment Income (REI) service for customers to check, confirm or change. Staff using the Earnings and Reporting workflow in Process Direct will have leave and redundancy payments from STP employers pre-filled in the workflow.

Staff must discuss the pre-filled information with the customer. Customers will need to advise the number of days the unused leave or redundancy payment relates to, or the average weekly wage, to assess any Income Maintenance Period. It is important to review the days provided by the customer is reasonable for the amount paid, example: $3,000 for one day's leave is not reasonable and would need to be verified. The responsibility is with the customer to check, confirm or change the details.

Unused leave and redundancy payments confirmed by the customer will be automatically loaded on the Income Maintenance Period Summary (IMPS) screen for customers. Customer confirmed STP cessation details will have a source code of STP on the IMPS screen.

Located at question 7 of the SU1, employers provide information regarding leave and entitlements by asking 'On termination, did you pay or will you pay the person, any unused leave entitlements or final gross redundancy?'

Service Officers code the relevant information provided on the SU1.

For more information regarding IMPs, see Income Maintenance Period (IMP) and Leave and termination payments paid by an employer.

4

Seasonal Work Preclusion Period (SWPP) + Read more ...

Where a job seeker or their partner have been engaged in high income seasonal, intermittent or contract work in the 6 months prior to claiming an allowance they may be subject to a SWPP.

Seasonal work reported by an STP employer will be pre-filled in Online and Assisted Customer Claims and in the Earnings and Reporting workflow in Process Direct.

When determining if a SWPP applies, staff are to review on the SU1:

  • Question 3 - used to determine the period of employment and if an AWOTE may apply
  • Question 4 - used to determine the reason for separation

For more information regarding SWPP, see Seasonal Work Preclusion Period (SWPP) and Leave and termination payments paid by an employer.

5
