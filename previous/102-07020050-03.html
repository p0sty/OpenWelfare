Reassessing eligibility when a student is deferring or discontinuing study

Table 1

Step

Action

1

Student on Austudy or YA has stopped or intends stopping study, or will not continue same course in next study period + Read more ...

If the customer:

  • intended to study and will not, or did not start their course. See Customer will not commence full-time study for Youth Allowance (YA), Austudy and Pension Education Supplement (PES)
  • is currently recorded as being in an 'Intention to Study' period (check coding on the Customer Study Details (EDC) screen in Process Direct or Education Course Details (EDC) screen in Customer First) or they have been Suspended - Failed to Provide Course Details (SUS-PCD). See Processing end of course reviews for Austudy and Youth Allowance (YA)
  • has already started or will not start their course, and their claim is in an ‘Assessed’ status, go to Step 5

For all other customers, go to Step 2.

2

Stopped study due to a medical condition + Read more ...

Has the customer stopped full-time study due to a medical condition?

  • Yes, see the appropriate procedure below:
    • Austudy student or Australian Apprentice becomes incapacitated
    • Youth Allowance (YA) student or Australian Apprentice becomes incapacitated
    • Go to Step 4
  • No, go to Step 3

3

Australian Apprentice + Read more ...

Has the customer started an Australian Apprenticeship?

  • Yes, investigate entitlement to ABSTUDY, Austudy or YA as an Australian Apprentice, see Existing customer starts an Australian Apprenticeship. Procedure ends here
  • No, go to Step 4

4

Part-time study + Read more ...

Is the customer now studying part-time?

  • Yes, see:
    • Changing study load from full-time to part-time or concessional study load for Youth Allowance (YA), Austudy or PES
    • Go to Step 1 in Table 2
  • No, see Step 1 in Table 2

5

Claim is in ‘Assessed’ Status + Read more ...

Update the study details if:

  • a claim is in ‘Assessed’ status, and
  • the customer advises they will no longer be studying full time

See Assessing entitlement to Youth Allowance or Austudy where the customer will not commence full-time study table.

Procedure ends here.

Changing study details to cancel payment when stopping studies

Table 2

Step

Action

1

Changing study details when stopping studies + Read more ...

If a YA or Austudy (Student) stops full-time study they must notify within 14 days (Notification Period) to receive backdating of JobSeeker Payment (JSP) or YA (Job seeker) if they are eligible.

If they fail to notify they have stopped studying full time within 14 days, payment of JSP/YA (Job seeker) may only start from the date the job seeker claim was submitted.

Update the customer record as follows:

  • go to Customer Study Details (EDCH) screen in Process Direct or Education Course History (EDCH) screen in Customer First and correct the Student End Date: field, to the date the student:
    • last studied, or
    • stopped to have an intention to return to study, or
    • is no longer undertaking a sufficient study load to qualify for payment. That is, the day before their study load reduced
  • insert a Newstart Education Status (NES) entry:
    • Event Date: field, code the student end date plus 1 day
    • Student Status: field, code 'NST' (not a full-time student)
    • Education Level Attained: field, update if necessary

Go to Step 2

2

Determining if a manual cancellation is required + Read more ...

If the customer is:

  • under 22 years of age, and
    • no longer wants to continue to receive YA as a jobseeker, go to Step 4
    • wants to continue to receive YA as a jobseeker, go to Step 3
    • failed to complete the Course End Date review, see Student to job seeker transfers (Table 2)
  • 22 years or over and ceased studying:
    • before their 22nd birthday, go to Step 4
    • after their 22nd birthday, manual cancellation is not needed. The payments will cancel automatically from the updates made to the NES and EDCH screens. Go to Step 5

3

YA (Student) Transfer to YA as a jobseeker + Read more ...

Customers under 22 years who advise of stopping study within the 14 day notification period, and are seeking another income support payment do not need to lodge a claim.

If the customer has contacted:

  • within their Notification Period, and they want to receive further financial assistance:
    • for a job seeker payment, see Student to Job seeker transfers
    • for an alternative income support, see Contact in relation to an intended claim (CLK)
  • outside their Notification Period and want to receive further financial assistance, they must lodge a new claim. Go to Step 4

4

Coding a manual cancellation + Read more ...

Manual cancellation of the student payment is needed for customers:

  • 22 years or over who ceased studying before their 22nd birthday, and
  • customers under 22 years of age who do not want to continue to receive YA as a jobseeker

To manually cancel the student payment:

  • go to the Benefit Action (BA) screen
  • Service Reason: field, select the customers payment type
  • Action: field, CAN - Cancel
  • Reason: field 'NST' - not a full-time student
  • Effect Date: field, new study end date plus 1 day

Record the details of the decision to cancel on a DOC.

Go to Step 5.

5

Further financial assistance + Read more ...

If the customer has contacted:

  • within their Notification Period, and they want to receive further financial assistance:
    • for a job seeker payment, see Student to Job seeker transfers
    • for an alternative income support, see Contact in relation to an intended claim (CLK)
  • outside their Notification Period and want to receive further financial assistance, they must lodge a new claim. Go to Step 6

6

Debt incurred for failing to notify + Read more ...

Determine if the customer has incurred a debt for failing to notify of stopping studies, see:

  • Raising debts resulting from a failure to report or notify and
  • Raising debts for Centrelink administered payments for students

Record details of the decision to cancel and any other action taken on a DOC.
