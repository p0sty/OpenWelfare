This page contains information on managing compulsory Quarterly Participation Interviews (QTI) for Partial Capacity to Work (PCW) and Temporary Reduced Work Capacity (TRWC) (0-7 or 8-14 hours per week) job seekers.

Triggering and recording a QTI requirement for a job seeker with PCW or TRWC

Capacity and capability to conduct the interview + Read more ...

Book a Quarterly Participation Interview + Read more ...

Book a Quarterly Participation Interview appointment for the next available time.

  • Appointment Type: select Quarterly Participation Ivw

  • Service Reason: select appropriate ISP

If the rescheduled appointment is in the next 5 business days, tell the customer the time and date because no appointment advice is issued.

Advise the customer their payment will be restored once the interview is conducted.

Procedure ends here.

5

QTI + Read more ...

A QTI is a compulsory requirement for job seekers assessed as having a:

  • Partial Capacity to Work (PCW) of 0-7 or 8-14 hours per week, or
  • Temporary Reduced Capacity to Work (TRWC) of 0-7 or 8-14 hours per week for 12 weeks or more

A job seeker will be identified as requiring a QTI when:

  • an Employment Services Assessment report (ESAT) determines the job seeker has a PCW or TRWC of 0-14 hours per week, including TRWC for less than 12 weeks duration, and
  • the ESAT has been accepted and recorded by the agency

Go to Step 6.

6

Triggering the QTI requirement + Read more ...

  • The ESAt report is accepted, triggering the 12 week count for the QTI. Note: the ESAt report will have a 'submitted' status for 28 days but this does not affect the commencement of QTI requirements. The job seeker's work capacity and QTI requirements are effective from the date the ESAt is accepted
  • If the job seeker has already lodged a medical certificate, follow the process to assess the medical certificate and update the Medical Certificate (MC) screen to either grant or not grant a Temporary Incapacity (INP) exemption

An automatic letter is generated for the job seeker advising them of their:

  • mutual obligation requirements, and
  • requirement to negotiate a Job Plan with the agency

Go to Step 7.

7

An appointment is automatically booked + Read more ...

If the job seeker does not have an exemption from their mutual obligation requirements (for example, INP) with an end date more than 2 weeks in the future, an appointment will be automatically booked using the 'Quarterly Interview' appointment type.

This appointment is to ensure the:

  • QTI is coded on the Activity and Exemption Summary (AEX) screen, and
  • the Job Plan updated

If the job seeker has an exemption for at least 2 weeks, their appointment will be deferred until after the exemption end date. The Job Plan will be updated with the QTI appointment attendance requirement after the exemption ends.

Go to Step 8.

8

Conducting the QTI requirement appointment + Read more ...

If the interview is being conducted:

  • Face to Face, go to Step 9
  • by Phone, send a Phone interview pre-call notification using Desktop Electronic Messaging Capability (DEMC) to job seekers with a phone QTI appointment. See Centrelink letters online and Electronic Messaging
    Does the job seeker attend the appointment?
    • Yes, go to Step 9
    • No, go to Step 16

9

Job seeker attends QTI + Read more ...

Launch the Service Profiling Update Workflow to Conduct the QTI. Launch the workflow:

  • directly from the booked appointment by selecting the 'Execute' button, or
  • via the Workspace page in Customer First

Go to Step 10.

10

Conduct QTI + Read more ...

During the interview discuss the following:

  • The ESAt assessment and whether it is still current and valid, or if the customer's circumstances have changed
  • If the job seeker advises a change in circumstances, re-run the Job Seeker Snapshot and refer for a new ESAt if appropriate. This includes where:
    • there has been a change in circumstances
    • the QTI requirement has expired for a job seeker with a TRWC and the job seeker is likely to be eligible for QTI, as it appears they still have a work capacity of less than 15 hours per week
  • Assistance recommendations from their last ESAt/Job Capacity Assessment (JCA)
  • Social participation options where the job seeker has been assessed as having a TRWC
  • Encourage voluntary job search. Include voluntary participation with a Workforce Australia, Disability Employment Services (DES) or Community Development Program (CDP) provider. Note: voluntary participation with a provider does not end the QTI requirement
  • Any work the job seeker might be doing as paid employment. It could meet the job seeker's mutual obligation requirements and they may no longer be required to attend a QTI
  • Voluntary work and other community assistance options
  • Provide information on the benefits of work (such as Working Credit and Mobility Allowance)
  • Outline the benefits of planning for the future and explain assistance services like Financial Information Services
  • Outline the disadvantages of long term income support dependence, and
  • Explore eligibility for other income support payments
  • If the job seeker has a TRWC, check when this period ends as the job seeker may no longer be required to attend QTIs and will need to re-engage with a Workforce Australia, DES or CDP provider. A referral will be required when the TRWC period ends
  • Check the job seeker knows the date of their next quarterly participation interview and when other reviews or appointments will be

Select the appropriate radio button once a discussion topic is addressed.

Go to Step 11.

11

Negotiate a Job Plan and code the AEX screen + Read more ...

Discuss the new mutual obligation requirements with the job seeker, resulting from the ESAt assessment.

Check the customer's reporting requirement and process if required.

Is the job seeker fully meeting their requirements through paid work or self-employment within their assessed bandwidth hours?

  • Yes, go to Step 12
  • No, go to Step 13

12

Job seeker is fully meeting requirements through paid work or self-employment + Read more ...

For both Centrelink and provider managed job seekers, record paid work as an approved requirement.

In Process Direct:

  • Select Customer Summary from the Process Direct Landing page
  • Enter the customer's CRN, select Go
  • In the customer's record, go to the Activity and Exemption Summary (AEX) screen
  • Code the AEX screen with the appropriate requirement details for:
    • paid work (PTW) or
    • self-employment (SEL)

Advise the job seeker they must notify the agency of any reduction in their working hours or if they cease work. If this occurs, they will be required to attend QTIs with Services Australia for the duration of their PCW or TRWC.

Go to Step 14.

13

Job seeker is not meeting requirements through paid work or self-employment + Read more ...

The job seeker will have QTI requirements for the duration of their PCW or TRWC.

Note: if a job seeker continues to participate in DES, do not update the Job Plan. The DES provider will act on the coding of the requirement on the AEX screen and will contact the job seeker to discuss whether the job seeker will keep participating in DES or not. If, after talking with their DES provider, a job seeker chooses to exit DES (and is eligible to exit), the Job Plan must be updated by Services Australia at that time.

Update the job seeker's record by coding the Activity Type QTI on the AEX screen in Process Direct to auto populate the content of the Job Plan.

In Process Direct:

  • Go to the AEX screen
  • Select Add to view the Create Activity and Exemption Summary (AEX) screen
  • Complete the Activity Type and enter the start and end date of the QTI activity
  • Start date is the date the QTI job plan is negotiated
  • End date is the date equal to the latest end date recorded on the Work Capacity (WC) screen
    • TRWC - the TRWC end date, or
    • PCW job seekers - the ESAT acceptance date plus 2 years > Select Save
  • Complete the Receipt Date: and Channel: fields > Save
    Note: a provisional line will display on the AEX table. For a combined activity, select Add to add the second approved requirement
  • Select Assess and address any errors/warnings
  • Select Assess > Finish. Record details of the update on the Finalise screen
  • Select Finalise to complete the transaction

Go to Step 14.

14

Negotiate a Job Plan + Read more ...

Where the AEX activity coding is subject to a Quality Management Application (QMA) check, the system will not auto populate the Job Plan until the Quality Management Officers (QMO) check is completed. Staff must not commence the negotiation of a new Job Plan until the QMA check is completed.

  • Service Centre staff, see Team Leader for local escalation process to request an urgent QMA
  • Smart Centre staff, see Urgent QMA process for Smart Centres, Labour Hire and Service Delivery Partners

After the AEX screen is updated and confirmed, the system identifies if the Job Plan is to be updated. See Negotiating Centrelink Managed Job Plans.

To update the Job Plan:

  • Select > Participation Summary to view the Key Information screen
  • Select Messages > Action Item to view the Job Plan screen > select Negotiate Plan and negotiate a Job Plan
  • Once the Job Plan is negotiated, the Action Item is removed

Go to Step 15.

15

Complete QTI + Read more ...

After the appointment is completed, finalise the Service Profiling Update Workflow. This will automatically update the status of the Service Component to 'COM' (completed).

Procedure ends here.

16

Manual suspension of payments + Read more ...

As this is the job seeker's only compulsory activity, any outstanding QTI appointments must be completed. If a job seeker fails to attend their QTI, suspend their payment to prompt contact with the agency.

Face to face appointments + Read more ...

Must do's:

  • Make one attempt to contact via their nominated phone number
  • Ensure the appointment is finalised as 'Did not Attend' in the Centrelink Appointment System
  • Do not finalise the Service Profiling Update workflow. The Service Component is to remain 'STA'rted so that once the job seeker attends, the workflow can be run, and Service Component completed

Phone appointments + Read more ...

Must do's:

  • Try to contact the job seeker at the scheduled appointment time
  • If unable to make contact on the first attempt, try again approximately 15 minutes later
  • If unable to make contact after these attempts, finalise the appointment 'Did Not Attend' in the Centrelink Appointment System
  • Do not finalise the Service Profiling Update workflow. The Service Component is to remain 'STA'rted so that once the job seeker attends, the workflow can be run, and Service Component completed

Suspension of payment following Did Not Attend (DNA) + Read more ...

In Customer First

  • Go to Benefit Action (BA) screen
  • Service Reason: payment type
  • Action: Suspend
  • Reason: FAI - Fail to attend Interview
  • Effect Date: enter the date the customer failed to attend appointment
  • Source: select relevant source
  • Receipt Date: date the information was recorded
  • Select Submit Change and go to the Assessment Results (AR) screen and finalise the activity

Create a closed DOC + Read more ...

  • Use Fast Note. Select Auto Text option > Generic > SUS/CAN/RES > Suspend Cancel Reason
  • Once the text has been confirmed, update all the fields in the text. Include the following:
    • Reason for Suspension: Failed to attend appointment on <date>
    • Attempts to contact the customer were made at <time> and <time>
    • Requirements for customer to have their payment restored: Book a Quarterly Participation Interview appointment for customer to attend

Procedure ends here.
