This page contains a summary of how to help customers or their partners obtain a Tax File Number (TFN) from the Australian Taxation Office (ATO), and to temporarily meet the requirements for family assistance payments when experiencing an extreme or urgent circumstance.

On this page:

Assist customers to obtain a TFN

Action an override request

Action a TFN override for Stillborn Baby Payment (SBP)

Assist customers to obtain a TFN

Table 1: This table describes the process for Service Officers to follow when assisting customers or their partners obtain a Tax File Number (TFN) from the Australian Taxation Office (ATO) and to temporarily meet the requirements for family assistance payments when experiencing an extreme or urgent circumstance.

Step

Action

1

TFN requirement + Read more ...

Customers and/or partners must provide or obtain a TFN when they have lodged a family assistance claim or when a Family Tax Benefit current customer becomes partnered, a TFN must be provided for the partner.

Is customer lodging a TFN application or enquiry form for themselves or their partner?

  • Yes, go to Step 6
  • No, go to Step 2

2

Advising of TFN or giving permission for use of TFN + Read more ...

Is the customer advising their or their partner's TFN or giving permission to reuse the TFN they have previously advised?

  • Yes, see Provision of Tax File Number (TFN) for Family Assistance payments. Procedure ends here
  • No, customers who do not have their or their partner's TFN or cannot access their or their partner's TFN can authorise Centrelink to request the TFN details from the ATO. Go to Step 3

3

Newly arrived refugee or TPV holder refugee + Read more ...

Is the customer a newly arrived refugee or Temporary Protection Visa (TPV) holder refugee?

  • Yes, see Obtaining a TFN for newly arrived refugee customer. Procedure ends here
  • No, go to Step 4

4

Online application lodged + Read more ...

Has the customer lodged an online application for a TFN?

  • Yes, go to Step 6
  • No, go to Step 5

5

Issuing forms and providing Identity Confirmation + Read more ...

Issue the appropriate ATO form for the customer/partner to complete, or advise customer to submit the ATO online TFN application.

ATO forms:

  • Tax file number application or enquiry for an individual (NAT1432)
  • Tax file number application or enquiry for an Aboriginal or Torres Strait Islander (NAT1589), or
  • Tax file number application for individuals living outside Australia (NAT2628). This form is applicable for non-residents of Australia for taxation purposes who are in Australia and have become partnered to a current Family Tax Benefit customer

Note: the claimant must tick the box to state that they give permission for the ATO to advise Services Australia of their TFN.

The Resources page contains links to these forms.

Identity confirmation documents for TFNs

Advise the customer that if Identity documentation has not previously been provided, original Identity documents will need to be sighted when the TFN application is lodged. Acceptable Identity documents for TFNs are listed on the relevant forms.

Note: if identity documents are not provided, do not accept the TFN application - manually issue a Q555 to the customer/partner and advise the customer/partner to lodge their TFN application direct with the ATO. See Creating an Online Advice (OLA), including within an existing activity.

Paper applications

Identity documents need to be sighted, and returned to support the paper TFN application. If ATO acceptable identity documentation is not available, check the Alternative Identity Confirmation 'Yes' box, and include copies of all available identity documentation that has been provided with the TFN application form. For further information on checking and completing the identity documentation and Office Use Only section of the NAT1432/NAT1589, see Requesting a Tax File Number (TFN)

If an FTB customer's partner has lodged a NAT2628, it is required to be sent, with their identity documentation, to the ATO

Online applications

When the customer has completed the online ATO TFN application process they are required to take their identity documentation with the Application Summary to the closest participating Australia Post outlet. The Australia Post outlet will confirm their identity documentation and supply the customer with a receipt. The customer must provide the Application Summary and the receipt that has been provided by Australia Post.

Online application: see Requesting a Tax File Number (TFN).

Record issue of form on a DOC.

6

TFN application or enquiry form lodged + Read more ...

If the customer has a TFN that has not been updated on their record, and they do not know what their TFN is, advise the customer to use an Access Centre Front of House (FoH) phone to contact the ATO Personal Tax Enquiries line to obtain their TFN over the phone and provide it in writing to Services Australia.

Has the customer been affected by any of the following extreme or urgent circumstances?

  • Immediate financial needs associated with the birth of a baby (under 3 months of age) or a child coming into care
  • Unanticipated financial needs such as medical costs or costs associated with an emergency
  • A community disaster where the local community is affected by an emergency such as a flood, bushfire or pandemic

Has the customer lodged a TFN application?

  • Yes, and
    • not claiming Stillborn Baby Payment, go to Step 7
    • claiming Stillborn Baby Payment, see Step 1 in Table 3
  • No, normal processing of the TFN application applies. See Helping customers obtain a Tax File Number (TFN) for Family Assistance. Procedure ends here

7

Customer meets criteria for extreme or urgent circumstances + Read more ...

If the customer is claiming as their local community is affected by an emergency such as flood, bushfires or pandemic, check the status of the emergency. See Current emergency management events.

Refer the customer details to the Families and Childcare Level 2 Policy Helpdesk for approval.

Note: if the claimant has lodged the TFN application online, the referring officer must advise in the referral to the Helpdesk that they have sighted the Application Summary and the Australia Post receipt.

If the claimant has lodged a paper TFN application the referring officer must advise in the referral to the Helpdesk that the TFN application has been completed and is ready to be posted or has already been posted to the ATO.

Any referral without this information will be rejected and returned to the Referring Officer.

The Resources Page contains a link to the Level 2 Policy Helpdesk online referral form. The Helpdesk cannot provide approval over the phone. The online enquiry form must be lodged. Wait until referral outcome is complete before advising the customer of the decision.

Has the application been approved by the Helpdesk?

  • Yes, go to Step 8
  • No, normal processing of TFN application applies. See Helping customers obtain a Tax File Number (TFN) for Family Assistance. Procedure ends here

8

Application is approved by the Helpdesk + Read more ...

Level 2 Policy Helpdesk assesses the application and approves override, the Service Officer is to refer the case to a Families Override Officer to code the TFN override.

FCC Override Service Officer codes override and refers claim back to the SO for completion.

When at the end of the TFN override period the TFN has not been received, the FTB will cancel until a TFN is received. A further override cannot be granted or extended for a TFN application.

Action an override request

Table 2: This table describes the process for FCC Override Service Officers to follow when a FYTFNCO override request is received in their mailbox for action.

Step

Action

1

Override request received + Read more ...

Approved FCC Override staff are to update the FYTFNCO screen in the FTB customer/claimant record as follows:

  • Update the Person the override is to apply to - this could be the customer and/or partner
  • Update the Override Type option with code of COM - Compliant
  • Enter Start Date - as approved by the Level 2 Policy Helpdesk
    • Record the date the override is to commence
  • Enter End Date - as approved by the Level 2 Policy Helpdesk
  • Record details on a DOC advising that approval given to grant TFN compliance. Refer the claim back to the Service Officer for completion

Action a TFN override for Stillborn Baby Payment (SBP)

Table 3: This table is for Double Orphan Pension (DOP) processing team only. The table describes the process for DOP staff to apply a TFN override for customers claiming Stillborn Baby Payment

Step

Action

1

Customer claiming SBP meets requirements for extreme or urgent circumstances + Read more ...

Service Officer must seek confirmation from Senior Service Officer (SSO) prior to applying the below coding.

If SSO confirms the TFN override is to be applied, the SSO will record the CRN in the DOP TFN Override Register.

Service Officer can then complete the coding:

  • Within the claim navigate to the CHOC screen
  • On the CHOC screen code an SBP PAY line from DOR of the claim
  • Advise the customer an override has been applied and they are required to notify the Agency of their TFN once they receive it
  • Finalise the claim ensuring that the TFN override action is documented including the reason for the deferral
  • Create a Display on Access (DOA) doc using reason OTH and with expiry date of 42 days. Include text: TFN override has been applied for SBP claim. Customer has been advised they need to apply for TFN and provide us with their TFN once known. If customer contacts, please remind them that TFN needs to be provided.

Procedure ends here.
