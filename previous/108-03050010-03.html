Checking DVA income coding and referring work to the DVA Clearance Team

Table 1

Step

Action

1

DVA payment/benefit notified by the customer or during a new claim + Read more ...

The customer has notified that they received a DVA payment or benefit. This may be notified as part of a new claim.

  • Customer notifies of a DVA grant, a change to existing DVA payment, or they have lodged an application for an Aged Care means assessment and declare a DVA payment, go to step 2
  • Customer advises of a DVA payment as part of a Services Australia new claim, and/or a DVA error presents during new claim processing, go to step 4
  • Customer is advising of a new Family Assistance estimate that includes a change to DVA income, go to step 5
  • For all other DVA payment enquires, go to step 3

2

Is there a DVA payment coded on the customer record? + Read more ...

Check the Department of Veterans' Affairs Summary (DVAS) screen for DVA coding.

Is there a DVA payment/benefit coded on DVAS?

  • Yes,
    • and the information needs to be updated, go to step 6
    • and this information does not need to be updated, however the customer has questions about the impact to their rate of payment, go to step 3
  • No, and the customer needs to declare a new grant or the payment information needs to be coded on DVAS go to step 6

3

Customer has questions about their rate of payment which is being impacted by DVA income + Read more ...

Check the DVAS screen for DVA payment type. The Service Officer should attempt to assist the customer with their enquiry.

If the customer has further questions that the Service Officer cannot answer, refer the enquiry to the DVA Clearance Team.

Go to step 6

4

DVA income or concession is declared in a new claim and/or an edit/error or warning presents during new claim processing + Read more ...

All DVA income declared as part of a new claim needs to be checked and coded by the DVA clearance team. Edits and warnings that may present during new claim processing:

  • E001OG: DVA Reverse Clearance required
  • E002OG: Web Service error
  • SR247: DVA Clearance Required - new customer income
  • SR307: DVA Clearance Required - new partner income
  • SR253: DVA Clearance - review customer payments
  • SR308: DVA Clearance - review partner payments

Before referring work to the DVA Clearance team make sure:

  • all coding has been completed within the new claim,
  • the customer has provided all requested information
  • all applicable specialist referrals have been completed
  • all other errors have been cleared and claim is ready to be finalised

Refer to the clearance team, go to step 6

5

DVA income declared for family assistance purposes + Read more ...

When a customer is updating their income estimate for Family Assistance purposes, Service Officers are to ensure the estimate is correctly recorded. See Background page for further details.

See Helping families provide a reasonable annual income estimate for family assistance payments to help the customer provide an accurate estimate.

Check the Department of Veterans' Affairs Summary (DVAS) screen for DVA payment details.

Note: Incorrect coding of DVA payments may result in the customer receiving the incorrect rate of family assistance payments.

Is the DVA payment information recorded on DVAS correct?

  • Yes: no further action is required. Continue with estimate update. Procedure ends here.
  • No: or the Service Officer is unsure the correct payment information is recorded on DVAS, go to step 6

6

Refer to the DVA Clearance Team + Read more ...

Coding of DVA payments must be completed by the DVA Clearance Team.

Note: Customers in receipt of Veteran Payment from DVA must not have claims rejected due to multiple entitlement exclusion without consultation with DVA. Veteran Payment is a temporary payment and customers may claim at Services Australia while in receipt of this payment.

The following must also be referred:

  • a DVA manual follow-up (MFU) on Activity List (AL) screen is preventing other work from being completed
  • a debt explanation is required, e.g. if there is a residual Services Australia debt amount after the debt has been partially recovered from DVA arrears

In Customer First refer the case to the DVA Clearance Team

Create an open work item using the Fast Note Workflow in Customer First:

Select Auto Text use Generic > Enquiry > DVA Clearance/Advice Request

Text: include details of what the referral is for. Include the payment type, frequency, amounts and dates payments have been received from DVA as relevant.

Select Confirm

If applicable, advise the customer a referral has been made to the DVA Clearance Team for action.

The activity needs to be placed on hold as further work is required after the clearance team have checked or actioned the record.

7

Holding work until DVA Clearance Team actions have been completed + Read more ...

Place claim or activity on hold to user for 5 business days.

DVA Clearance Team will close the Fast Note, code DVA income inside the activity as appropriate, and DOC actions taken and the outcome.

The claim or activity will be taken off Hold in Customer First or changed to In Process for Process Direct.

When the work item is returned, complete the activity.

Procedure ends here.
