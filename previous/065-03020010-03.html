Completing the claim for Age Pension

Table 1

Item

Description

1

Personal details + Read more ...

The customer's personal details must be given to assess eligibility, residence and assist the identification process.

If a customer requires an interpreter, ensure the preferred language is given.

From 1 July 2018:

  • the Customer Contact Details (CCD) screen is read only. See Contact in relation to an intended claim
  • eligible customers will receive payments or concession cards from the date they actually submit a complete claim with all relevant supporting documentation
  • different processes apply for customers in vulnerable circumstances, see Intent to claim and vulnerable customers

Relationship Status: this question must be answered. Note: on page 3 of the SA002 the customer is asked:

  • if they have a partner
  • if their partner is also claiming Age Pension, and if so
  • they are asked to indicate if their partner is using this form or a separate form for a claim or to provide their details

If contact is made and the customer advises:

  • The Claim for Age Pension form has been lodged for both customer and partner, check the scanned images. If the forms are not there, see Digital image not located on customer record.
  • The partner is also claiming Age pension but they wish to provide their details separately, launch the First Contact Service Offer (FCSO) and issue an SA412. If partner is unavailable, record this intent to claim in a DOC on the partners record and issue a Claim for Age Pension and Pension Bonus Scheme (SA002)
  • The customer is the only one claiming, record this information on the customer's record via a DOC and complete the DOC
  • The couple have completed separate claim forms and did not complete the question at the top of page 3 of the form, no action is required

If there is doubt as to the customer's current living arrangements, ask The Living Arrangement question set and if required, issue Relationship Details (SS284) or Relationship Details - Separated under one roof (SS293) to be returned within 14 days.

Question 17 - Permission for partner to enquire: a response to this question is only collected for the customer completing the form, not the partner. It is acceptable to contact the partner by phone and ask this question. Record their response on the Marital Status (MS) screen, or send an 'Authorising a person or organisation to enquire or act on your behalf (SS313)' form for them to complete and return.

2

Contact with Centrelink + Read more ...

Customers who provide a mobile phone number or email address will be automatically subscribed to the Electronic Messaging (EM) service. This means they will receive SMS or email messages about things that are important, such as requests or reminders to provide documents, notification of decisions, and information about payments and services.

The Resources page contains a link to the Terms and conditions of Electronic Messaging on the Services Australia website.

3

Residence in Australia and outside Australia + Read more ...

Residence questions must be answered by both the customer and/or their partner (if applicable) to determine whether the customer is qualified for payment by meeting the residence criteria.

These details are also used to establish if the customer (or their partner) may be eligible for a Comparable Foreign Payment and to further investigate residence requirements.

4

Your payment details + Read more ...

A bank account must be supplied before payment can be made. The account the customer (and their partner, if claiming) nominates must be an account held in their name or jointly with another person. It cannot be in a child's name unless the customer is the signatory or trustee.

The customer (and their partner) can elect to nominate a day they wish to be paid on.

The customer (and their partner) can elect to have tax deducted from their pensions.

If the customer's only income for the financial year is Age Pension, they will not have to pay tax. However, the customer may have to pay tax if they receive any other income during the financial year (such as salary or wages). Customers should be advised to visit the Australian Taxation Office (ATO) website for more information.

5

Tax details + Read more ...

Generally a Tax File Number (TFN) must be provided by the customer and/or partner for payment to be made. Customers who do not have or do not know their TFN can apply for a TFN and authorise the ATO to provide it to Services Australia directly. Some customers may be eligible for a TFN exemption. See Requesting a tax file number for more information about TFN applications and TFN exemptions.

6

Accommodation + Read more ...

Accommodation details are required for many reasons including:

  • to determine if the customer is a homeowner, this will determine the asset limit threshold to use - homeowner or non-homeowner
  • to assess eligibility for Rent Assistance (RA)
  • to see if entry contributions or accommodation payments have been made
  • sharers' rules

Note: some customers who are considered homeowners for Social Security purposes may be entitled to rent assistance. These people are classed as not ineligible homeowners. For more information, see the References page.

Documents supporting the amount of rent a customer pays must be supplied before RA can be paid.

7

Other questions + Read more ...

Customers claiming Age Pension (Blind) are required to lodge a Request for Ophthalmologist/Optometrist Report (SA013) form with their claim.

The Resources page contains a link to this form.

The income and assets tests do not apply to persons who are permanently blind. However, Services Australia needs to know a customer's income and assets if:

  • the customer or their partner is claiming Rent Assistance, or
  • the customer's partner is claiming or in receipt of an income support payment (unless their partner is also receiving payment as a permanently blind person), or
  • the customer or their partner is receiving or claiming compensation, or
  • the customer or their partner is receiving payments under the New Enterprise Incentive Scheme (NEIS), or
  • the customer or their partner is receiving payments under an International Social Security Agreement, or
  • the customer or their partner is receiving a New Zealand Government payment, or
  • the customer or their partner is receiving a payment from the Department of Veterans' Affairs (DVA)

8

Attachment checklist + Read more ...

The checklist is a reminder to customers of what is required to be lodged with their claim.

1

Claim for Pension Bonus Payment (PBP) + Read more ...

The Service Officer must recommend to customers registered in the Pension Bonus Scheme that they contact a Financial Information Service (FIS) Officer, preferably before they lodge their claim for Age Pension and PBP.

If a FIS interview is arranged, the customer may still wish to lodge their claim before the FIS interview - record this information on the customer's record via a DOC but do not complete the DOC. This will alert the processing centre to await the outcome of the FIS interview.

Maximising a customer's entitlement to PBP may depend on various factors. The date a customer finishes work may not be the same as the date their bonus period ends. A customer may disadvantage themselves by lodging a claim too early or too late. A FIS contact will determine the best time for the customer to lodge their PBP claim taking into consideration their individual circumstances.

2

Statement + Read more ...

The claim for PBP must be signed by the customer and their partner (if they have one) before payment can be made.
