Determining if a customer qualifies for a PCC

This table describes the process for determining if a customer qualifies for a PCC.

Step

Action

1

New Zealand Agreement + Read more ...

To see whether a customer is paid under the Agreement with New Zealand, check the Additional Residence Details (ARD) screen.

Any customer who receives a NZ pension that is covered by the Agreement has the NZ pension directly deducted after the income and assets test. To see if a NZ pension is being directly deducted, check the Pensions Rate Calculation (PRC) screen.

If the NZ direct deduction reduces the customer's rate to zero, they remain current and can still receive a PCC. The Benefit Status line will show if the customer is Current Zero Rate (CZR).

Is the customer paid under the Agreement with New Zealand?

  • Yes, go to Step 2
  • No, go to Step 3

2

Paid under NZ Agreement + Read more ...

Customers who are paid under the NZ Agreement are qualified for PCC if they are physically located in Australia, regardless of where they are residing.

If the customer is paid under the New Zealand Agreement via Environment I, a card cannot be issued via the system. Issue an interim voucher for the length of time they will be present in Australia or with a valid to date of up to 3 months after the issue date, whichever occurs earlier.

Customers paid under the Agreement with New Zealand who are residing in Australia may have an additional income test. If they are not entitled under that additional income test, their PCC will be end dated.

To see if a PCC has been end dated under this rule:

  • check the Concession Entitlement Summary (MCCS) screen
  • see if the 'End Rsn' field has a value of 'NZB'

For more information, see the Rate calculation section on the Australian payments tab in New Zealand Agreement and foreign pension information.

Procedure ends here.

3

Check the customer's residence + Read more ...

A data link with the Department of Home Affairs, can, where possible:

  • automatically populate residence details for customers from 1 September 1994 to date,
  • as well as citizenship and travel details for the customer

Once a person identifies as a customer receiving Centrelink services, the Department of Home Affairs will advise:

  • any future changes to their visa subclass, or
  • if they, their partner or their child leaves or returns to Australia

Pension customer subject to stimulus reporting

For customers who are not already in receipt of a PCC, when the pension is granted subject to stimulus reporting:

  • the PCC will be issued when the customer meets the reporting requirements for the first entitlement period and the pension is payable

Note: this only applies to customers who are not entitled to a PCC at the date of grant. If the customer is already in receipt of a payment that attracts the PCC, they retain entitlement to the PCC. For example, transferring from Carer Payment to Age Pension.

Once the reporting requirements are met for the first entitlement period the PCC will be issued from the date of grant of the pension.

Working Credit and employment nil rate period

Customers in receipt of a PCC who are eligible for Working Credit will be entitled to the PCC where the customer is in an employment income nil rate period.

Customers who received some pensions may continue to be qualified for their PCC even after their payment has ceased due to employment.

A person who is no longer eligible for payment because of an increase in the level of their self-employment income also qualifies for an extension concession card.

Due to a current system problem, these self-employed customers may not be issued an extension PCC automatically by the system. Is the customer claiming, receiving or did they receive a social security pension and are they currently an Australian resident?

For residence requirements, see the References page for a link. The customer should provide all relevant documentation including their passport or entry documentation within 14 days of lodging their claim.

  • Yes, go to Step 4
  • No, go to Step 11

4

Customer qualifies for a PCC as long as they remain eligible for the payment in most cases + Read more ...

Customers paid under the Home Equity Access Scheme who do not receive an income support payment under the normal income/asset test are not entitled to a PCC.

These customers may be eligible for a Commonwealth Seniors Health Card (CSHC) or Low Income Health Care Card (LIC). For assistance determining if a customer a PLS-only recipient see Home Equity Access Scheme.

Does the customer receive a Disability Support Pension (DSP) and is currently an Australian resident?

  • Yes, go to Step 5
  • No, go to Step 6

5

Customers in receipt of DSP + Read more ...

Customers who receive DSP qualify for a PCC as long as they remain Australian residents.

Check Concession Entitlement Summary (MCCS) screen for current entitlement details. CCIS screen displays actual cards issued, not current entitlement.

Procedure ends here.

6

Determine customer age and entitlement + Read more ...

Is the customer:

  • aged over 60, and
  • been continuously in receipt of either JobSeeker Payment (JSP), Parenting Payment Partnered (PPP), Sickness Allowance (SA), Special Benefit (SpB) or a social security pension for the preceding 39 weeks and is currently an Australian resident?
    • Yes, go to Step 7
    • No, go to Step 8

7

Customer qualifies for a PCC as long as their payments are continuous + Read more ...

Some customers remain qualified for a PCC for 26 weeks if their payment ceases due to income from employment. See Extended entitlements to Concession Cards.

Check MCCS screen for current entitlement details. CCIS screen displays actual cards issued, not current entitlement.

Note: if the customer reclaims income support, their concession card eligibility will be a Health Care Card (HCC). The reason is that the customer will no longer meet the continuous receipt of a payment requirement.

Procedure ends here.

8

Identify customer entitlement and capacity to work + Read more ...

Is the customer receiving JobSeeker Payment (JSP), Youth Allowance (YA) (Job Seeker (JSK) only) or Parenting Payment (PP) with a partial capacity to work as assessed in an Employment Services Assessment (ESAt)?

  • Yes, customer qualifies for a PCC as long as they remain eligible for JSP, Youth Allowance (YA) (JSK only) or PP, and are an Australian resident.
    • Check MCCS screen for current entitlement details. CCIS screen displays actual cards issued, not current entitlement.
    • Procedure ends here
  • No, go to Step 9

9

Identify customer entitlement and dependents + Read more ...

Is the customer receiving JSP or YA (JSK only) and the single Principal carer of a dependent child?

  • Yes, customer qualifies for a PCC while they remain eligible for JSP or YA (JSK only) as a single principal carer of a dependent child. Procedure ends here
  • No, go to Step 10

10

Determine if extended entitlement to Concession Card should apply + Read more ...

Did the customer receive Parenting Payment Single (PPS) and lost their qualification because their youngest child turned 8 years of age, on or after 1 January 2014?

  • Yes, the customer retains eligibility for a PCC for a period of 12 weeks from the date of cancellation. See Extended entitlement to Concession Cards for more information. Procedure ends here
  • No, go to Step 11

11

Customer does not qualify for a PCC as they do not receive an eligible payment or is not an Australian resident + Read more ...

Customers who receive a loan-only payment under the Home Equity Access Scheme are not eligible for a PCC. The exception to this is for customers who had their pension cancelled (CAN/ASS) on 1 January 2017 as a direct result of changes to the pension assets test.

PLS-only customers:

  • who are grandfathered (GRF) for the grandfathering type 2017 Rebalance Assets Cards (RAC), as per the Grandfathering Summary (GFS) screen,
  • are automatically eligible for a non-income tested CSHC from 1 January 2017 and a non-pensioner PCC from 9 October 2017

For more information, see Non-income tested (NOI) concession cards.
