Lodgement of ACFI forms when the website is unavailable

Table 1

Step

Action

1

Ask caller to email or post a copy of the form + Read more ...

Note: the residential aged care service can submit the ACFI form online via the Aged Care Provider Portal (ACPP).

Ask the residential aged care service to email a copy of the 2 page ACFI form to record a receipt date. See Resources for a link to the ACFI form.

Advise the residential aged care service:

  • This is a one-off emailed or posted ACFI procedure
  • The emailed or posted ACFI will be dated as received and not processed
  • Emailed or posted ACFI will be checked against online events received in following days
  • Emailed or posted ACFI will be matched with the corresponding online event
  • Online event should be processed with received date backdated to email or postal date

Email ACFI form to Aged Care Payments Team - Aged Care and Allied Health Branch.

Encourage the residential aged care service to attempt to submit the ACFI online at a later date. The ACFI will be backdated to the email received date.

Add a care recipient note detailing the support on the decision to backdate the ACFI. See the Using the Aged Care Staff Portal to manage care recipient notes (Care Recipient in Context (CRiC)) table.

Manage an ACFI

Table 3: only Subject Matter Experts (SME) and Team Leaders can view, register, delete, reject or backdate an ACFI.

Step

Action

Manage an ACFI + Read more ...

Note: the Aged Care Programmes Branch or Aged care services can request the deletion of an ACFI.

This process table shows how to manage an ACFI in the Aged Care Staff Portal (ACSP). Service Officers can perform the following task when managing an ACFI:

  • View ACFI events
  • Register ACFI
  • Delete or reject a draft ACFI
  • Backdate, delete, end date or update an accepted ACFI

To manage an ACFI, see the Using the Aged Care Staff Portal to manage Aged Care Funding Instruments (ACFIs) (Service in Context) table.

Once the ACFI has been registered into the Aged Care Staff Portal, add a care recipient note.

2

Care recipient note + Read more ...

Service Officers must add a care recipient note when:

  • registering and ACFI
  • deleting or rejecting a draft ACFI
  • backdating, deleting, end dating or updating an accepted ACFI.

To add a care recipient note, see the Using the Aged Care Staff Portal to manage care recipient notes (Care Recipient in Context (CRiC)) table.
