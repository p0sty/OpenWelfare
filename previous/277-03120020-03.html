Review of change of assessment (COA) application

Effective 16 December 2014, COA applications cannot be lodged on cases where the assessment has ended for the child.

On 1 July 2015 the Social Security Appeals Tribunal (SSAT) merged with the Administrative Appeals Tribunal (AAT) to form the new AAT. Prior to this date SSAT had the jurisdiction to review most child support objection decisions. Reference to previous or pending AAT decisions in this procedure includes previous reviews conducted by SSAT.

Check COA application is eligible and complete + Read more ...

The COA application is eligible if:

  • there is an Australian child support assessment in force (the application is received before the assessment ends for the child/ren on the case)
  • it relates solely to a period more than 18 months prior to the application and a court order grants leave to consider that period
  • it is not seeking a reduction from a minimum assessment, unless the payer has at least regular care, or
  • the assessment is not varied by a court order or agreement that limits the ability to apply for COA
  • it contains no obscene or offensive content, see Open exchange of information for Child Support customers

The COA application is complete if:

  • it is on the COA form
  • a reason is identified
  • a case is identified
  • it is signed everywhere a signature is required
  • it contains any financial information at all in any section of the form

A non-parent carer does not have to provide their financial details.

Electronic signatures

A COA application with an electronic signature can be accepted when:

  • the application has been submitted via their Child Support online account or Webmaster.
    Note: customers who are eligible for a Child Support online account must not be advised to use the Services Australia Webmaster email address. They must link their Child Support online account to myGov and use the document upload function in their online account because Webmaster is not a secure channel
  • the application has been completed with the applicant or respondents name being typed or handwritten in the signature box

If the application may not be eligible, see the Incomplete or ineligible application table.

Suspended cases where the customers have reconciled

The assessment is not considered ‘in force’ and a COA application cannot be made during a period when an assessment is suspended due to reconciliation, However, if the suspension ends before 6 months has passed because the customers separate again, the assessment is again ‘in force’ and a COA application can then be made.

Decisions that span a suspension period can be made. Any departure spanning that period could only affect days in the Child Support period where the customers have been assessed. For example, if a rate was varied, it would only apply to the relevant days before and after the suspension.

Note: immediately escalate any written correspondence containing threats of harm, implied or overt to the designated Team Leader, see Contact with Child Support customers.

5

Supporting documentation + Read more ...

Review the supporting documentation to:

  • identify the COA reason(s) the customer has applied for
  • ensure the application is sufficiently supported. The References page has a link to the Child Support Guide 2.6.2, what are the reasons for a change of assessment
  • determine if applicant may have omitted a reason that they have supplied evidence for or, based on the information, another reason might apply:
    • Different reasons can be introduced at any time before the decision is made
    • DMs must ensure the customer is aware of what will be considered and is given the opportunity to comment and/or provide further evidence
  • identify and discuss sensitive issues with the customer, such as family and domestic violence or mental health, before exchanging information. See Open exchange of Information

Potentially fraudulent evidence

Where there is evidence, or the other parent alleges, that the information or documentation being presented may have been tampered with or is in some way fraudulent:

  • document the identified issues for discussion with the customer
  • provide the customer with the opportunity to supply further evidence, or to correct the previously provided evidence
  • exclude the evidence if it cannot be verified

If the evidence is critical to the decision, it may not be possible to establish a reason to change the assessment and this may result in a refusal.

For more information, see Report Suspected Fraud.

Identified unlawful evidence

Where there is evidence, or the other parent alleges there is, the information or documentation being presented may have been obtained unlawfully, do not use it in the decision making process.

To determine if evidence is unlawful, refer it to Change of Assessment (COA) and Part 6A Programme support Manager (PSM).

If the evidence is identified as having been unlawfully obtained before Open Exchange has occurred:

  • return the evidence to the person who provided it with an explanation that it cannot be used in the process
  • detach the evidence from the Cuba record, as it will not form part of the material used in the decision. It does not need to be provided to the AAT

If the information is identified as having been unlawfully obtained after Open Exchange has occurred or it has been considered by the decision-maker:

  • explain to both parents the information has not been used in the decision
  • keep a record that the information was in the Registrar’s possession, as any records we retain will need to be provided to the AAT in the event of an appeal

Non-parent carers (IDM and CDM Support Officer) + Read more ...

A customer may be both a non-parent carer and a parent. For example, a step parent and a biological parent. This means:

  • on Cuba the non-parent carer has 2 cases, but legally there is only 1
  • both parents and the non-parent carer are parties to the application
  • non-parent carers must apply for child support against both parents unless special circumstances exist
  • the Case Liability Rates screen for each case reflects both liable parents' information, not the non-parent carer
  • system generated letters need to be deleted and unique letters created referring to all involved parties when exchange of information occurs. The Resources page contains links to letters

7

Administrative remedies and agreements + Read more ...

Assess whether an administrative remedy, or partial administrative remedy, may be more appropriate. Administrative remedies must always be the first preference as they provide flexibility. See Step 6 in Change of Assessment enquiries table in Change of assessment enquiries and return to this step.

Document any issues that require further action in the COA Maintenance notepad.

Agreements

Consider whether a child support agreement is a more appropriate option.

If a proposed child support agreement has been identified, or customers are willing to enter into a child support agreement, see Finalising a Change of assessment application.

Registrar initiated COA

Information gathered during the Capacity To Pay process must be carefully considered. Do not make a decision without considering information from the Financial Investigator (FI) who prepared the file because administrative remedies are assessed by the FI as part of the CTP process. Contact the FI for clarification if necessary.

8

The change is just and equitable and otherwise proper + Read more ...

DMs must balance the interests of the customers, the children and the community when making a decision.

In some circumstances, a change to the assessment may not be just and equitable or otherwise proper if it is unfair to the customers, children or to the community even if a reason has been established. For example, where AAT or a Court has already addressed the issues raised in the application and there has been no significant change in the customer's circumstances since that decision (in the event the application had not already been refused on the basis that no reason was established).

If it is not just and equitable or otherwise proper to change the assessment:

  • document the identified issues for later discussion with the customer, and
  • give the customer the opportunity to provide additional relevant information that they may have omitted

The References page contains links to the Child Support Guide 2.6.17, Would a change be just and equitable and Child Support Guide 2.6.18, Would a change be otherwise proper?

S112 application seeking leave of the court is in progress + Read more ...

A customer may seek the leave of a court for consideration of a period greater than 18 months and less than 7 years prior to the date of the COA application. The court can either grant leave for the Registrar to make a determination or for the court to make a departure order under section 118 for that period. A customer is required to provide a copy of their application to the agency.

If there is a Section 112 application in progress and:

  • the children's assessment is due to end in the near future
    • pend the COA application to await the outcome of the court decision, or
    • ask the customer if they want to withdraw the COA application if they are asking the court to make a departure order under Section 118. Explain that if the court declines to make a departure order and instead grants leave for the Registrar to make a determination, they cannot resubmit their COA application once the children's assessment has ended. In most instances, it is better to leave the COA 'pended' until the customer knows if the court is prepared to make a departure
  • the children's assessment is not due to end in the near future
    • establish if the customer wants to withdraw the COA application pending the outcome of their court application and then re-lodge for both the past and future periods at that time

If the customer:

  • wants to withdraw the COA application, go to Step 13 in the Contact applicant table
  • wants to proceed with their application, advise them periods greater than 18 months prior to the COA application cannot be considered until the Section 112 order is obtained
    • Pend the application to await the Section 112 court decision in the COA Maintenance window
    • In the COA Maintenance window, allocate the application to:
      COA Site: INTERNATIONAL COA
      Officer: PENDING OBJ SSAT

Position: A - PENDING OBJ SSAT

Application is for a previously considered period + Read more ...

If the application is not for a previously considered period, go to Step 5.

Where an application was previously refused, check if it was refused because:

  • no reason was established, or
  • it was not just and equitable to make a decision

Compare the previous application and evidence against the new application and evidence. The new COA application can be refused if there is no new information or changes to the customers circumstances. Go to Step 9.

Previous COA decision was made and there was a change in assessment

Where the new application is complete and eligible, it must be reviewed as normal. A new decision is being made based on the information provided and may result in a different outcome to the previous decision.

Note: if the applicant is dissatisfied with a previous decision they may have the option to object which could require an extension of time. Customers do not need to apply for leave of the court under section 112 to dispute a previous decision, even if that decision was made more than 18 months ago, as the objection process is not limited by the 18 month retrospective rules. The References page has a link to the Child Support Guide 4.1, Objections.

If the applicant is dissatisfied with the outcome of an objection, they may appeal to the AAT. The References page has a link to the Child Support Guide 4.2, External review applications to the AAT.

5

Matters raised in COA application are too complex + Read more ...

If the matters raised in a COA application are too complex the application may be refused. Decisions to refuse the COA application due to complexity require the approval of an EL1 DM Team Leader, who must document their authorisation on the COA Maintenance notepad before the decision is processed.

A case may be considered too complex if:

  • there is a history that requires special or sensitive customer management, for example family and domestic violence or frequent AAT applications, or
  • the customers have made numerous and frequent COA applications without a satisfactory outcome, or
  • financial details make it difficult to determine customers' financial circumstances, for example there may be business structures or oversea income, go to Step 6
  • there is contradictory or conflicting information, go to Step 7

Where the applicant objects to the too complex finding (section 98E or 98R refusal decision):

  • prior to 1 July 2015 a customer applied straight to court for a departure from their assessment
  • from 1 July 2015 a customer may apply to the AAT for a first review of the decision. Any decision of the tribunal is final, except where there is an error of law

Note: from 1 July 2015 an application to court for a 118 order on the basis that the matters have been found too complex only applies where another legal matter is pending, and the court determines it is appropriate to deal with the COA application at the same time. It is no longer valid to make an application to the court solely on the basis that the matters have been found too complex.

The References page contains a link to the Child Support Guide 2.6.3, A decision to refuse to change an assessment for more information.

6

Complex financial details + Read more ...

Complex financial arrangements may make it difficult to ascertain financial circumstances. A customer's financial arrangements may be considered complex if they involve:

  • overseas income/assets
    • Where substantial incomes from foreign sources or overseas assets are involved, it may be difficult to adequately determine the financial circumstances of either the applicant or respondent
  • trust or business structures
    • Applicants may need to provide supporting documents about their financial circumstances, for example, explanation of corporate structure or income details. For more information see Calculating financial capacity for business owners
    • Financial business interests may be too complicated to separate individual financial situations sufficiently in order to make a decision

Consider refusing the application if complex financial information obstructs the ability to establish a reason, or determine whether it is just and equitable or otherwise proper to make a change to the assessment. See Step 2 in Finalise the decision table in Finalising a change of assessment application.

7

Contradictory or competing information + Read more ...

If an applicant supplies information which is contradictory or conflicting, for example an ongoing property settlement, document the issues which require clarification.

It may be inappropriate for the application to proceed if:

  • the discrepancy cannot be resolved
  • the issue is relevant to the outcome of the COA
  • the contradiction prevents the making of an accurate decision

8

Grounds to proceed + Read more ...

Read the application and supporting documentation in full to ensure there is sufficient information and evidence to support the COA being progressed. Determine if:

  • the customer has satisfied the basic requirements of each reason they have selected
  • the customer has selected the appropriate reasons, and
  • all relevant information and supporting documents have been supplied

The Resources page has a link to Change of Assessment (COA) reasons and options. The References page has a link to the Child Support Guide 2.6.2, What are the reasons for a change of assessment?

Go to Contact applicant table.

9

Grounds for refusal + Read more ...

A decision to not change an assessment can still be made after a COA application has been started.

An application may be refused if:

  • a previous application has been refused and there is no new information or reasons in the current application
  • a reason is not established
  • it would not be just and equitable to make a change to the assessment
  • it would not be otherwise proper to make a change to the assessment, or
  • the issue is too complex

Note: the lack of financial details from a non-customer carer are not grounds for refusal.

The References page has a link to the Child Support Guide 2.6.3, A decision to refuse to change an assessment.

Contacting the customer + Read more ...

Contact the customer to discuss the COA application and supporting documentation and perform a Proof of Record Ownership (PORO) check, see Authenticating a Child Support customer. Document the call using the Change of Assessment (COA) First Contact macro.

Third party involvement

Child Support legislation prohibits parties from being represented in the COA process, however, in some cases third party involvement may help determine whether a change of assessment should be made. See the References page for a link to Child Support Guide 2.6.5, Change of assessment process - application from payer or payee.

Note: a third party appointed under an administration order or who has Enduring Power of Attorney for either customer is not considered a representative, and can participate in the COA process. The order or Power of Attorney must be recorded in the Customer Representative window.

A third party can be present during the phone conversations in the following circumstances:

  • an interpreter is requested or required:
    • When a customer requests an interpreter, or if a DM thinks the customer is having difficulty understanding, arrange a telephone interpreter immediately, see Interpreter Services. Do not continue with the conversation if there is a risk of misunderstanding due to language or cultural barriers
  • the customer has requested an Indigenous interpreter, or an Indigenous Service Officer (ISO)
  • they are present for the purposes of giving information (in rare occurrences)
  • there are special circumstances, for example, a hearing disability, illiteracy or severe mental illness on the part of either customer. In these cases, a third party may assist the customer to put forward their own case

If it is believed a third party is listening and/or coaching a customer, ask that they stop and, depending on how the customer replies, decide whether or not to continue with the call.

Solicitor to receive correspondence

A customer's solicitor may assist them in preparing their COA application and/or response form. A solicitor cannot represent the customer or be involved in the COA decision-making process but they can receive correspondence on the customer's behalf.

The customer may authorise their solicitor to receive letters or communications from the agency on their behalf. In such cases, ask the customer whether they want all correspondence to be sent to their solicitor, including all information provided by the other customer for exchange with them, or the solicitor to be notified of the final decision only.

13

Withdrawing a COA application + Read more ...

An applicant can withdraw their COA application at any time before a decision is made. They may withdraw for any reason, including:

  • wanting to pursue an administrative remedy
  • child support issues before court
  • a change of mind about seeking a COA

When an applicant decides to withdraw their application, ensure the applicant:

  • understands it is their choice to withdraw the application
  • understands they cannot object to or request a review of a withdrawn COA application as no decision has been made
  • is provided a cooling off period of at least 48 hours before finalising the application as 'withdrawn' in Cuba
  • is aware that they must contact the DM within the 48 hour cooling off period if they decide to change their decision about withdrawing
  • has been advised of any alternative administrative options, where applicable
  • is aware they can re-apply at any time as long as the assessment is in force
  • is aware that where a cross application is in place the COA will progress on the cross application

If the applicant withdraws and:

  • the other party has not been notified of the COA application, do not notify them of the application or the withdrawal, or
  • there is no cross application, but the DM believes the income used in the current assessment does not accurately represent customers' income, property or financial resources, a Capacity to pay referral should be made to ensure the assessment is fair and equitable, or
  • there is no cross application, or it has been withdrawn, process the application as withdrawn in the COA Maintenance window, see Change of Assessment Cuba Process Help. Advise the respondent they can lodge a COA application under any of the 10 COA reasons and document discussion. See Supporting resources on the Resources page for the COA reasons and options information sheet

If the applicant withdraws their application but the respondent does not withdraw their cross-application:

  • advise both customers that the COA process will continue for the cross-application, and
  • process the COA application as 'withdrawn' in the COA Maintenance window, see the Change of Assessment Cuba Process Help, and
  • do not process the cross-application as 'withdrawn'

Note: if the withdrawal is deemed to be insincere, requested under duress, or if there are concerns the applicant may not fully understand the consequences of withdrawing the COA application, ask for the request in writing.

The References page has a link to the Child Support Guide 6.2.1, Information need not be in writing.

Incomplete or ineligible application

Table 6: this table describes the process to follow where COA applications are incomplete or ineligible.

Step

Action

1

Application not complete + Read more ...

If the COA application is not complete, document this as incomplete in the COA Maintenance window with a notepad detailing the reasons.

If a complete application is subsequently received, go to Review of Change of Assessment table.

2

Application ineligible due to offensive content + Read more ...

If a COA application contains obscene or offensive content, DMs must consult Policy Advice through the COA and Part 6A Programme Support Manager (PSM) before making any decision about whether the application should be treated as ineligible. This ensures policy is applied consistently and correctly.

IDMs and CDMs must:

  • email the COA and Part 6A PSM with a subject line of 'COA possible offensive content' detailing the potential offensive content
  • document the decision once the feedback from Policy Advice has been received, citing Policy Advice's feedback in a new notepad in the COA Maintenance window

Do not exchange documents containing offensive content with the other party, see Open exchange of information for Child Support customers.

Go to Step 9.

3

Application ineligible as no assessment in place + Read more ...

A COA application can only be considered if there is a child support assessment in place. An application cannot be considered when:

  • the liability is not an Assessment Act liability, for example a Stage 1 case, spousal maintenance, parentage overpayment order registered under section 17A or adult maintenance order or overseas liability, or
  • an assessment is no longer in force, for example, a section 107 declaration that sets aside an administrative assessment from the start date

Go to Step 9.

4

Application ineligible as it is for a period more than 18 months prior to lodgement of application without a court order to do so + Read more ...

Do not change an assessment for a day in a period more than 18 months prior to the day a COA application was lodged, unless a court has granted leave to do so. A court can grant leave for a period of up to 7 years prior to the date of application. The References page has a link to the Child Support Guide 2.6.1, When can the Registrar consider changing an assessment?

Go to Step 9.

5

Application ineligible due to minimum assessment + Read more ...

Do not reduce an assessment below the minimum assessment rate unless the payer has at least regular care of 1 of the children of the assessment.

Go to Step 9.

6

Application ineligible due to an agreement or court order + Read more ...

A customer cannot apply for a COA if a court order or a child support agreement specifies a periodic rate of child support. The only exception is if a customer is applying for a variation to the provisional notional assessment where the existence of a child support agreement is disregarded, see Notional assessment relating to child support agreements.

A COA application can be considered where a court order or agreement does not specify an annual rate but varies 1 or more of the components of the formula or provides for non-periodic rates. The COA decision can only vary those components not varied by the court order or agreement.

The References page contains a link to 'Can CSP change a child support assessment affected by a child support agreement or court order?' in the Child Support Guide 2.6.4, Kinds of change of assessment decisions.

Go to Step 9.

7

Application ineligible due to deceased customer or child + Read more ...

The Deceased Customer Management (DCM) team manage all cases where a customer or child of the case is deceased, see Deceased Child Support customer management.

A COA application received prior to the customer's death may still be considered if 1 of the customers is deceased and there is an executor, administrator or power of attorney in place. In this instance, seek technical support prior to deciding how to proceed with the application. The References page contains a link to the Child Support Guide 2.6.1, When can the Registrar consider changing an assessment?

An application may be ineligible if the deceased customer did not have the opportunity to provide relevant information and there is no executor or administrator who can do so. Cuba will automatically issue a letter advising the customer or the executor/administrator that the application is ineligible.

Go to Step 9.

8

Application ineligible due to ended assessment + Read more ...

A customer is unable to make a COA application if the child support assessment in relation to the child to whom the application is related has ended. Legislation only allows a customer to make a COA application when a child support assessment is in force.

Where there are multiple children on a case and an assessment ceases to be in force in relation to 1 child, an application cannot be made in relation to that child but can be made for the other child/ren of the case whose assessment/s has not ended.

Where a court has granted the Child Support Registrar leave under Section 112 to make a determination for a specified period, the Registrar is not authorised to make a determination for a particular child under Part 6A if the COA application is received after the assessment for the child has ended. The Registrar can make a determination in respect to any other child/ren for whom the assessment was in force on the day the COA application was made.

A customer should be advised to seek legal advice regarding their options to seek a departure order from a Court in these circumstances.

Terminating events

A child support assessment will remain in force until the agency is notified of a terminating event and takes action to give effect to the terminating event by ending the assessment.

If there is a terminating event which will retrospectively end the assessment for a child from a date which is prior to the lodgement of the COA application, the COA application is valid if it was received on a date when the agency:

  • had not been notified or become aware of the terminating event, or
  • had been notified or became aware of the terminating event but the decision to end the assessment had not yet been made

This includes circumstances where an objection to a previous decision is lodged prior to the COA application, and the objection decision (that results in a terminating event) is made after the COA application was received.

If a decision is made to change the assessment, the assessment can be amended for prior periods, if applicable, up to the date of the terminating event.

If the case is ineligible in CUBA select 'In-No Assess' letter, go to Step 9

9

Contact customer to explain application is ineligible + Read more ...

If the customer is currently being managed by a Personalised Services Service Officer (PSSO), liaise with the PSSO to discuss how the contact will occur, see Step 1 of Review of COA application table.

Contact the customer to explain why the COA application is ineligible.

Discuss their options which could include:

  • seeking legal advice about applying to a court for changes to a court order
  • applying for a low-income non-enforcement (LINE) period, see Stage 1 – low-income non-enforcement (LINE) period
  • applying to have their assessment reduced from minimum annual rate to nil, see Minimum annual rate to nil - Reducing child support assessments
  • an administrative remedy and the advantages of this option over COA, see Step 6 in Change of Assessment enquiries table in Change of assessment enquiries

Offensive content

Where Policy Advice confirm that the application is deemed to be not properly made due to offensive content, inform the customer within 2 working days of receiving that advice that they may reapply by lodging a new COA application without the offensive content.

If the customer wishes to reapply for a COA, issue a unique letter, see Letters for Child Support customers. The letter should state that the application, response or documents are being returned due to offensive content and post this to the customer with a new COA application form.

Note: do not release a copy of the application to the other party. Document the discussion and/or contact attempts on a COA Maintenance notepad.

10

Record application as 'incomplete' or 'ineligible' + Read more ...

Finalise the COA application as 'ineligible', see Change of Assessment Cuba Process Help.

See Finalising a change of assessment application.
