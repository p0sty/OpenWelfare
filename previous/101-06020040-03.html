Partner listed on concession card

Table 1: This table describes the process to check if a customer's partner is listed on the customer's concession card and the referral process where the partner is recorded correctly on the system but is not automatically listed on the cardholder's concession card.

Step

Action

1

Check MCDS screen + Read more ...

Check the Concession Dependant Summary (MCDS) screen.

Is the customer's partner listed as an eligible dependant for concession entitlement?

  • Yes, go to Step 2
  • No, go to Step 5

2

Check card preferences + Read more ...

Check preferences for concession card.

Is suppression of the partner’s name recorded?

  • Yes, remove suppression, if appropriate. Go to Step 3
  • No, go to Step 3

3

Check CCIS screen + Read more ...

Check the Concession Card Issue Summary (CCIS) screen to confirm the current issued concession card includes the customer's partner as an eligible dependant.

Does the current issued concession card include the customer's partner as an eligible dependant?

  • Yes, procedure ends here
  • No, go to Step 4

4

Reissue concession card + Read more ...

Reissue the concession card. Check the CCIS screen to confirm the reissued concession card includes the customer's partner as an eligible dependant.

Does the newly issued concession card include the customer's partner as an eligible dependant?

  • Yes, procedure ends here
  • No, go to Step 7

5

Check MCDD screen + Read more ...

Select the partner on the Concession Dependant Details (MCDD) screen to check the partner's concession entitlement history and current status.

Has the partner's entitlement ended?

  • Yes, go to Step 6
  • No, go to Step 7

6

Check residence qualification + Read more ...

A HCC or PCC will not include the name of the cardholder's partner as a dependant of the cardholder if the partner does not satisfy residence requirements.

Does the customer's partner satisfy residence requirements for HCC and/or PCC?

  • Yes, update residence screens Legal Residence Details (RSLEG), Country of Residence (CRES) and Immigration Enquiry (RSIM), as appropriate. Go to Step 7
  • No, procedure ends here

7

Refresh required + Read more ...

If the partner's entitlement has not been reflected on the MCDS screen or the system has not generated a replacement card automatically (on the CCIS screen) a refresh is required.

In the customer's record, go to the Concession Benefit Action (MCBA) screen and code:

  • Action: RSH
  • Reason: HIC (Health Insurance Commission)
  • Effect date: record the relevant Event Date. For example, if the MCDD screen shows the partner lost concession entitlement due to NIA (not in Australia), record the event date as the date the partner last returned to Australia. If the customer has recently been granted FTB/ISP/LIC, code the DOV as the most recent grant date.

Go to the Assessment Results (AR) screen and finalise.

Did the refresh fix the issue with partner/dependents displaying on Concession record?

  • Yes, procedure ends here
  • No, go to Step 8

8

Refer to Level 2 Policy Help Desk + Read more ...

If an eligible partner has been recorded correctly on the system but is not automatically listed on a cardholder's concession card, refer the customer record to the Level 2 Policy Help Desk for an override to be implemented.

The Resources page contains a link to the Level 2 Policy Helpdesks - Online Query.

Children listed on a parent/guardian concession card

Table 2: This table describes how to determine which children are covered on a parent/guardian's concession card, including Health Care Card (HCC), Pensioner Concession Card (PCC), Low Income Health Care Cards (LIC), Foster Child HCC (FST), Commonwealth Seniors Health Card (CSHC) and Ex-Carer Allowance (child) HCC (EHC).

Step

Action

1

Age of child or young person + Read more ...

If the dependent child or young person is aged:

  • 0 to 18 years, go to Step 2
  • 19 years or over, go to Step 7

2

Dependent child aged 0 to 18 years + Read more ...

If the parent/guardian of the dependent child holds the following concession card:

  • PCC, go to Step 3
  • HCC (other than a Family Tax Benefit (FTB) HCC), go to Step 3
  • FTB HCC, go to Step 4
  • LIC, go to Step 4
  • FST, only the foster child will be listed. See Health Care Cards (HCC) for foster children
  • CSHC, only the cardholder is listed. Procedure ends here
  • EHC, only the cardholder is listed. Procedure ends here

3

PCC or HCC (other than FTB HCC) + Read more ...

The dependent child should be automatically listed on the parent/guardian's concession card.

The child's surname is not displayed on the concession card.

Is the dependent child aged 0 to 18 years listed on the parent/guardian's concession card?

  • Yes, procedure ends here
  • No, go to the Child not automatically listed on PCC or HCC table

4

FTB HCC or LIC + Read more ...

Is the child a Family Tax Benefit (FTB) child or Regular Care Child?

  • Yes, go to Step 5
  • No, go to Step 6

5

FTB child or Regular Care Child + Read more ...

The child is a dependant of the parent/guardian and will be automatically listed as a dependent child on the parent/guardian's FTB HCC or LIC.

The child's surname will not be displayed on the card.

Note: if a child is an FTB child or Regular Care Child who would otherwise be covered by their parent's HCC through entitlement to FTB, except that maximum rate FTB Part A is not paid because of the income test or the parent is not claiming FTB via fortnightly instalments, the child is not qualified for an LIC in their own right.

Is the FTB child or Regular Care Child listed on the parent/guardian's concession card?

  • Yes, procedure ends here
  • No, go to the Child not automatically listed on FTB HCC or LIC table

6

Child 0 to 18 years not FTB child or Regular Care Child + Read more ...

The dependent child:

  • will not be listed as a dependant on the parent/guardian's FTB HCC or LIC
  • can apply for an LIC in their own right

If the dependent child was in receipt of a Carer Allowance (child) (CDA) HCC when they reached 16 years of age and they are currently a full-time student and under 26 years of age, they may be eligible for an EHC in their own right.

Procedure ends here.

7

Young person 19 years and over + Read more ...

The young person:

  • will not be listed as a dependant on the parent/guardian's FTB HCC or LIC
  • can apply for an LIC in their own right

Note: if the young person is under 22 and they are a dependent child, they may be listed on their parent’s income support payment HCC or PCC (unless the young person holds an HCC or PCC in their own right).

Child not automatically listed on PCC or HCC

Table 3: This table describes how to determine why a dependent child is not automatically listed on the parent/guardian's Pensioner Concession Card (PCC) or Health Care Card (HCC) (other than a Family Tax Benefit (FTB) HCC).

Step

Description

1

Check CHOC screen + Read more ...

Note: the Child Override/Claim (CHOC) screen for an eligible child must reflect a LIC/CLM line for the relevant Date of Event to enable the LIC system to include the child in the LIC assessment. Update the child’s CHOC screen via the Child Selection (CHS) screen, if necessary.

Check the CHOC screen to ensure correct child details are recorded. Is there a PPP line for child of a PPP customer, or is there a NSS line for the child of a NSS customer?

  • Yes the CHOC is correct, go to Step 2
  • No the CHOC is not correct, go to Step 5

2

Check MCDS screen + Read more ...

Check the Concession Dependant Summary (MCDS) screen.

Is the dependent child listed as an eligible child for concession entitlement?

  • Yes, go to Step 3
  • No, go to Step 5

3

Check CCIS screen + Read more ...

Check the Concession Card Issue Summary (CCIS) screen to confirm the current issued concession card includes all eligible dependents. For example, check if the system has issued a replacement card that includes the dependent child.

Does the current issued concession card include only eligible dependents?

  • Yes, procedure ends here
  • No, go to Step 4

4

Reissue concession card + Read more ...

Reissue the concession card. Check the CCIS screen to confirm the reissued concession card includes only eligible dependents.

Does the newly issued concession card include the customer's relevant dependants?

  • Yes, procedure ends here
  • No, go to Step 7

5

Check MCDD screen + Read more ...

Select the child on the Concession Dependant Details (MCDD) screen to check the child’s concession entitlement history and current status.

Has the child's entitlement ended?

  • Yes, go to Step 6
  • No, go to Step 7

6

Child's entitlement has ended + Read more ...

If the child's concession entitlement has ended, check the MCDD screen to confirm the reason.

If shared care arrangements apply for the child, check that the level of shared care satisfies the minimum percentage required for the relevant payment on the SSA Shared Care (SSC) screen. The Resources page includes a table listing the minimum percentage of care required.

If the child is aged 16 to 21 years, check that Education Details (on the child information screen in Customer First) reflect full-time student status and update if appropriate. Refer to the Child Education Details workflow

Check the child’s Child Student/Income Circumstances (CHSI) screen. Ensure the Student Code field is populated with an appropriate student status from the relevant Event Date (for example, if the child was undertaking full-time secondary education on the child's sixteenth birthday the date of event will be the child’s sixteenth birthday). See, Child Student/Income Circumstances (CHSI) screen, for more details.

7

Refresh required + Read more ...

If the child’s entitlement has not been reflected on the MCDS screen or the system has not generated a replacement card automatically (on the CCIS screen) a refresh is required.

In the customer’s record, go to the Concession Benefit Action (MCBA) screen and code:

  • Action: RSH
  • Reason: HIC (Health Insurance Commission)
  • Effect date: same as Event Date recorded on CHSI screen

Go to Assessment Results (AR) screen and finalise.

Did the refresh fix the issue with the partner/dependents displaying on Concession record?

  • Yes, procedure ends here
  • No, go to Step 8

8

Refer to Level 2 Policy Help Desk + Read more ...

If an eligible child has been recorded correctly on the system but is not automatically listed on a cardholder's concession card, refer the customer record to the Level 2 Policy Help Desk for an override to be implemented.

The Resources page contains a link to the Level 2 Policy Helpdesks - Online Query.

Child's entitlement has ended + Read more ...

If the child's concession entitlement has ended, check the MCDD screen to confirm the reason.

Check the Child in Customer Care (CHC) and Child Override/Claim (CHOC) screens to identify whether the child is assessed as an eligible FTB child. Update the child’s CHC or CHOC screen via the Child Selection (CHS) screen, if necessary.

The LIC Assessment Summary (LIAS) screen reflects the number of child dependants assessed for LIC HCC purposes. The CHOC screen for an eligible child must reflect a LIC/CLM line for the relevant Date of Event to enable the LIC system to include the child in the LIC assessment. Update the child’s CHOC screen via the Child Selection (CHS) screen, if necessary.

If shared care arrangements apply for the child, check that the level of shared care is at least 14% on the FTB Shared Care (FSC) screen or for LIC check the SSA Shared Care (SSC) screen. The Resources page includes a table listing the minimum percentage of care required.
