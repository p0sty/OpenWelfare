3

Medicare enrolment only + Read more ...

Customers can enrol a newborn for Medicare online.

Customers can lodge a FA081 to enrol the child in Medicare. This includes the Medicare Safety Net and My Health Record. The FA081 must be complete and signed.

The customer can also provide other acceptable proof of birth and lodge a Medicare Enrolment Application (MS004) form. The Resources page contains a link to this form.

If the completed FA081 indicates to only register the newborn for Medicare:

  • Cancel the families scan activity in Customer First. Ensure the Medicare Newborn Manual Enrolment (MAENROL) DOC remains open. If the original MAENROL DOC closes:
    • create this manually in Customer First for processing by Medicare
    • ensure the Date of Receipt (DOR) matches the original MAENROL DOC. See Fast Note: Auto Text > Families > Update > Medicare Enrolment
  • Check if the customer's partner already has a claim for payments. If yes, use the proof of birth to assess the claim. Note: customers claiming PPL/DAP must advise if applied to register the birth with their state, territory or agency

Despite what the customer states on the form, Centrelink and Medicare can use this proof of birth.

Procedure ends here.

4

No claim for child on record + Read more ...

Check the FA081 to see if the customer wants to claim payments for their newborn child. Check their response to the PPL question and the Medicare-only question.

When a FA081 is scanned to the customer's record, a Social Application (SOA) shell Work Item is generated. This SOA activity contains no information apart from:

  • the CRN
  • personal details, and
  • the receipt date

Relevant information will need to be manually updated within the Work Item using information from the scanned FA081 and any associated documentation.

Where a scanned FA081 did not create a SOA activity, manually create a SOA shell via the Process Direct Landing page.

If the customer lodged a FA081 and is:

  • not FTB current and does not have a claim, go to Step 5
  • FTB current (FTB/CUR or FTB/CZR), and
    • wants to claim PPL, go to Step 5
    • does not want to claim PPL (or rejected PPL) and has a complete FA081, reassess FTB in Customer First. If the customer has indicated the birth is not registered, contact the customer and advise them Newborn Supplement cannot be paid with their Family Tax Benefit Part A until they have registered or applied to register the birth. Procedure ends here
    • does not have a pre-birth claim and the FA081 is incomplete or needs more information go to Step 5
    • does not have a pre-birth claim or acceptable Proof of Birth (POB) (e.g. FA081 back page), go to Step 5

If the customer is not FTB current and lodged other acceptable POB, contact to discuss claim options.

  • issue a manual Q777 letter. The Resources page contains the instructions and suggested letter text
  • cancel the scanned image. Record details on a Note/DOC

Procedure ends here.

5

Customer claiming payments + Read more ...

Invite the customer to claim payments for the child.

Staff can inform a customer of contact by phone through sending a desktop message. See Centrelink letters online and Electronic Messaging.

To send a pre-call SMS, in Process Direct:

  • go to the Correspondence (CORRO) screen
  • select the twisty below the Correspondence History table
  • in the Issue Correspondence table, select Pre Call SMS to Customer
  • select Submit
  • A Pre Call Alert SMS notification will be issued to the customer. View this in the Correspondence History table
  • Allow a lead time of 15 minutes in case there is a delay

Before calling the customer, check the customer has registered or applied to register the birth with their state or territory agency. If the customer has not answered, or answered 'no', tell them Newborn Supplement cannot be paid with their Family Tax Benefit Part A until they have registered or applied to register the birth.

Call the customer to discuss claim options.

If contact with the customer is successful:

  • Encourage the customer to lodge an online claim. This is the preferred service offer. Ensure customers have 'active' online account access before asking them to claim online
  • If unreasonable for the customer to claim online, issue a Claim for Parental Leave Pay and Family Assistance (FA100)
  • Extra help is available for remote Indigenous customers making a claim
  • Assisted Customer Claim (ACC) is available for families in crisis

If the customer is not available at the time of contact, check the address details and update if needed. If the customer lodged:

  • an FA081: issue a Q776 letter in Customer First using the FA081 Customer to claim Q776 guided procedure
  • other acceptable POB: create a Q999 letter. See Resources page for letter text

For more information, see Information requests for FTB, PPL and DAP claims and Add Newborn activities.

In Customer First:

Complete the families scan activity. Ensure the MAENROL DOC remains open. If the original MAENROL DOC closes:

  • Create this manually for processing by Medicare
  • Ensure that the Date of Receipt (DOR) matches that of the original MAENROL DOC. See Fast Note: Auto Text > Families > Update > Medicare Enrolment

Check if the customer's partner has a claim for payments. If yes, use the proof of birth for their claim.

Discuss potential entitlement to another Centrelink payment (e.g. Parenting Payment (PP)) with the customer or send a Q999. See Resources page for letter text.

Record details on a DOC in Customer First.

Procedure ends here.

  • Create a Social Application (SOA) shell with benefit type: Add Newborn from the Process Direct landing page.

Go to Step 7.

Customers who have lodged a full FA081 + Read more ...

If the customer has lodged an FA081, check that all questions have been answered in full. If:

  • minimal questions have been missed and the child's name has been provided, collect the missing information over the phone. Assess the Add Newborn using the new information along with the information from the initial claim
  • the customer has the original FA081, ask the customer to complete the missing questions and upload the fully completed FA081

When a FA081 is scanned to the customer's record, a Social Application (SOA) shell Work Item is generated. This activity contains no information apart from:

  • the CRN
  • personal details, and
  • the receipt date

Relevant information will need to be manually updated within the Work Item using information from the scanned FA081 and any associated documentation.

Where a scanned FA081 did not create a SOA, create a SOA with benefit type: Add Newborn via the Process Direct landing page. See Process Direct navigation, common screens and functions for more information. The Add Newborn can be assessed in the newly created activity using the information from the FA081 along with the information from the initial claim.

Contact the customer if there are any concerns that their circumstances have changed since the initial claim was submitted.

Go to Step 8.

Lodged/uploaded FA081 with back page only, or without the back page and without child's name/details or only other acceptable proof of birth provided + Read more ...

Contact the customer, send a pre-call notification SMS through Desktop Messaging before calling, and tell them:

  • to access the 'Add newborn child' service online or the 'Add child' service via the Express Plus Centrelink mobile app and answer all questions displayed, or
  • if the customer has the original FA081, complete it in full and upload all pages of the completed FA081

Place the SOA on hold, reason Pending customer contact. See Information requests for FTB, PPL and DAP claims and Add Newborn activities for further information

Document the record to advise outcome of discussion.

Note: if the customer provides the back page of the FA081 with the Doctor/Midwife section complete, but the customer section at the top of the back page is incomplete, the back page is acceptable proof of birth providing the full serial number is also included.

If the customer is not available:

  • open the Add Newborn SOA activity and select > Request Documents
  • select FCC Add Newborn required from the Available Documents list. See Information requests for FTB, PPL and DAP claims and Add Newborn activities for further information
  • document the record with information requested

If the customer provides the requested information within the required time, go to Step 8.

Where proof of birth is not provided, the claim will automatically NEF-POB 56 days after the nominated start date. The status must be changed to ‘Not Required’ for the SOA created when FA081 lodged and customer has not completed ‘add newborn service’ online or provided FA081 in full. Procedure ends here.

Note: if the requested information not provided is due to a secretary initiated review of decision of the pre-birth PPL claim, change the Add Newborn activity status to 'Not Required'. Reassess and reject the pre-birth claim with the relevant rejection code. See Not effective, rejection or withdrawal of claim for Parental Leave Pay (PPL).

For PPL claims when processing the Add newborn activity + Read more ...

The PPL scheme changed for children born or adopted on or after 1 July 2023. Transition arrangements apply to PPL claims where the expected date of birth or entry in to care is:

  • before 1 July 2023 and the actual date is on or after 1 July 2023
  • on or after 1 July 2023 and the actual date is before 1 July 2023

See Transitional reassessment of claims for Parental Leave Pay pre-birth claims.

The system checks if the PPL nominated start date (NSD) is between:

  • the Expected Date of Birth (EDOB), and
  • Date of Birth (DOB) inclusive

If it is, the system updates the PPL NSD to the DOB. This is the earliest possible start date for that child.

Do not update in a separate activity if the PPL NSD in the pre-birth claim falls between the EDOB and DOB.

Note: some customers change their NSD:

  • after their pre-birth claim has been assessed, and
  • before their Add Newborn assessment has been finalised

This may result in the NSD displaying on Entitlements (ELD) screen to differ from the date recorded on the PPL Claim Details (P1CLM). In these cases, check the Expected Date of Care or Nominated Start Date (P1EDND) screen to ensure PPL is commencing from the correct date.

Go to Step 8.

Action the Add Newborn activity + Read more ...

Prior to completing the necessary updates in the Add Newborn activity, on the Transaction Summary (TS) screen, link the child and complete any partner updates. This action must be completed before selecting Process or the data load from the Add Newborn will not complete correctly on the customer's record. See Table 2 in Process Direct navigation, common screens and functions. Check Transactions tab in Process Direct. If a SOA activity has been created due to customer lodging a FA081 in addition to the Add Newborn the SOA can be marked ‘Not Required’.

Action the Add Newborn activity:

  • Select Process
  • In the customer's record, check/update as appropriate the following screens from the SAP Warnings and Errors (SWE) screen:
    • Child Claim Override (CHOC)
    • Child Miscellaneous Circumstances (CHM)
    • FAO Early Claims Summary (FECD)
    • Medicare Information (MEI)
    • For more information on updating these screens see Processing standalone and combined claims for family assistance and/or Paid Parental Leave scheme payments
  • Note: for a manually created Social Application (SOA) the PPL Claim Details (P1CLM) screen is required to be updated with the original claim data
  • For children born or adopted before 1 July 2023 and a customer advises they have returned to work since becoming the primary carer, the return to work date will need to be updated on the PPL Employment Dates (P1ED) screen
  • In the child's record check/update as appropriate the following screens from CHTS on the SWE screen:
    • Child Registration Details (CHR)
    • Child in Primary Care (CIPC) - only PPL claims for children born before 1 July 2023
    • Child in Care (CHC)
    • Customer Person Details (CPDS)
    • Shared Care Assessment (SCA) - FTB only
    • Care Alignment Details Screen (CADDS) - FTB only
    • Change Child (CCH)
    • Child Relationships Details (CHRE)
    • Newborn Supplement Questionnaire (NBSQ) - FTB only
    • Residency task Selector (RETS)
    • Travel Outside of Australia (TOAS) (view only)
    • Residency Details (RSLEG)
    • Country of Residence (CRES)
    • Immigration Enquiry (RSIMME)
    • For more information on updating these screens see Processing standalone and combined claims for family assistance and/or Paid Parental Leave scheme payments
  • If an FTB current (FTB/CUR or FTB/CZR) customer/partner has claimed PPL only, assess FTB eligibility for the child within the Add Newborn activity, see Processing standalone and combined claims for family assistance and/or Paid Parental Leave scheme payments

As the 'Add Newborn' Service is not a claim, any duplicate 'Add newborn child' service activities completed by the customer for the same child should not be deemed not effective (NEF). Complete all updates within the first initial 'Add newborn child' service. Change the status of any duplicate 'Add Newborn' activities to 'Not Required'. See Process Direct navigation, common screens and functions for more information on changing the status of a work item.

If there is a manual follow up (MFU) on the record that is preventing processing, action via the Activity List (AL) screen in Customer First.

DAP for children born or adopted before 1 July 2023 + Read more ...

Select the appropriate option below:

Customer partnered to birth mother + Read more ...

Partner is claiming FTB/PPL

Customers whose partner is claiming FTB/PPL for the newborn child and a DAP pre-birth claim is assessed 'pending proof of birth' do not need to provide any additional information as their claim will automatically complete when their partner's PPL/FTB claim/s are assessed.

Where:

  • the customer’s pre-birth DAP claim was assessed ‘pending proof of birth’ with the child’s expected date of birth was before 1 July 2023 and the child’s actual date of birth was on or after 1 July 2023
  • the DAP claim will automatically reject and the customer can lodge a claim for PPL

See Eligibility for Parental Leave for children born or entering care on or after 1 July 2023.

Check the Entitlement (ELD) screen to confirm the DAP payment has finalised as part of the PPL/FTB claim processing. If this payment does not appear, see Processing Dad and Partner Pay (DAP) claims.

Note: the Child in Customer Care (CHC) screen for a DAP only customer should not be updated. If the CHC screen was incorrectly updated as part of the claim, delete the 'ICC' (in customer care) line.

Go to Table 2.

Partner is not claiming FTB/PPL

Customers whose partner is not claiming FTB/PPL for the newborn child and the pre-birth DAP claim has been finalised and have:

  • lodged a fully completed FA081 or other acceptable proof of birth, such as a birth certificate, see Processing Dad and Partner Pay (DAP) claims
  • lodged an incomplete FA081 or the back page of the FA081 only, contact the customer to request a fully completed FA081 or alternative Proof of birth (for example, birth certificate) for the newborn. If the customer cannot be contacted or provide these documents, send the customer a Q777 (via the Request Documents Q777 guided procedure) in Customer First asking the relevant questions to complete the customer's DAP claim. The Resources page contains examples of Q777 text. Note: this step is only to be used for DAP customers who have a pre-birth claim DAP/PND-POB

Procedure ends here.

Customer not partnered to birth mother + Read more ...

Customer is the biological father of the child and not partnered to the birth mother

A biological father who is not partnered to the birth mother can claim DAP for their child born or adopted before 1 July 2023 if they meet the eligibility requirements. These customers need to provide proof of birth for the child.

Where the birth mother has not provided proof of birth and the customer cannot provide the back page of the FA081 they will need to provide other acceptable proof of birth to verify the child's birth.

For children born on or after 1 July 2023, biological fathers will need to claim PPL. See Eligibility for Parental Leave for children born or entering care on or after 1 July 2023.

Procedure ends here.

Birth mother claiming PPL/FTB

Where the birth mother has claimed a payment and they have supplied proof of the child's birth, this birth verification can be used for the DAP claim. Alternatively the DAP claimant can provide other acceptable proof of birth to verify the child's birth. See Processing Dad and Partner Pay (DAP) claims.

If:

  • the customer submitted a DAP pre-birth claim for a child whose expected date of birth or adoption was before 1 July 2023 and the child’s actual date of birth or adoption is on or after 1 July 2023
  • the DAP claim will be rejected once proof of birth/entry to care is provided

The rejection letter will invite the customer to claim PPL. See Eligibility for Parental Leave for children born or entering care on or after 1 July 2023.

Procedure ends here.

Birth mother not claiming PPL/FTB

Where the birth mother has not claimed PPL or FTB for the child, the DAP claimant will need to provide the back page of the FA081 (where available). Alternatively the DAP claimant can provide other acceptable proof of birth to verify the child's birth. See Processing Dad and Partner Pay (DAP) claims.

Procedure ends here.
