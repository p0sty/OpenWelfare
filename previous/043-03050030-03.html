2

For customers who are considered to be self-employed + Read more ...

If the customer's contract for services is part of their business, treat the contract income as business income.

If the customer's contract for services is a one-off occurrence and income is received as a lump sum, the Net contract income is treated as a non-remunerative lump sum, see Treatment of lump sums.

If the customer receives commission income, the net commissions are treated as business income.

Commission or contract payments received by self-employed customers may be reduced by the costs of obtaining the income as for businesses. To assess allowable deductions, see Business deductions.

For commission workers considered to be self-employed, go to Step 5.

For contract workers considered to be self-employed, go to Step 6.

3

For customers considered to be employees + Read more ...

If contract income is received by employees periodically, as a wage or retainer, or as lump sum for a period of 52 weeks or less, treat the commission income as employment income:

  • Record the income on the Employment Income Paid Details (EAPP) screen using the Employment Type code 'Earnings from Employment’ (EAR)
  • Use the frequency ‘Income for one period’ (IOP) or ‘Long Period’ (LOP) to record the income using the date paid as the event date
  • The income is assessed from the EPSD of the period in which the payment was paid, for a number of days equal to the period that the payment represents or for the length of the entitlement period (whichever is longer). This is the known as the assessment period
  • See Recording and correcting employment income details for assistance with coding the EAPP screen

If contract income is received by employees as a lump sum for a period greater than 52 weeks:

  • Record the remunerative lump sum on the Employment Income Paid Details (EAPP) screen under a separate employer entry. For the new employer entry:
    • Use the Employment Type code 'Earnings from Employment’ (EAR)
    • Enter the employer’s name and add ‘lump sum’ at the end, for example ‘Services Australia lump sum’. If repeating for multiple lump sums, add each lump sum using a new employer entry and add the date the lump sum was received to create a unique employer name
  • Use the frequency code ‘Daily’ (DAY) to record the income using the Entitlement Period Start Date (EPSD) of the period in which the payment was paid as the event date
    • Use a combination of the Payment Calendar and the customer’s Payment Delivery (RAD) and Payment Summary (PS) screens to determine the EPSD of the period if required
  • The income is assessed from the EPSD of the period in which the payment was paid, for a number of days equal to the period that the payment represents. This is the known as the assessment period
  • Divide the amount received by the number of days in the period that the payment represents. Record this figure in the Amount field
    • For example, a lump sum of $5,000 for a period of 1,000 days is a daily amount of $5
  • The income must be ceased from the assessment period end date plus 1 day. Use the Perpetual Centrelink Calendar if required to determine the end date of the assessment
    • If this date has not yet passed, record a manual review in Customer First/Customer Record including details of the income received, the assessment period and the date from which the assessment is to be ceased. See How to code and action a manual review. Use review reason ‘Earnings Review’ (INC) and add the keyword OVER52WK
    • If this date has passed at the time the income is being recorded, record and cease the income in the same activity, no review is required
  • To cease the income, record an amount of zero using the assessment period end date plus 1 day as the event date. See Recording and correcting employment income details for assistance with coding the EAPP screen

The table on the Resources page explains how to assess commission income for employees after 7 December 2020.

No deductions can be made for expenses.

Note: when an employee, such as a taxi driver, has takings including Goods and Services Tax (GST), the amount of GST is not considered income if the amount is remitted to the Australian Taxation Office (ATO). This is similar to the treatment of business income where GST is forwarded to the ATO.

4

Customers who are commission workers and regarded as employees + Read more ...

For customers who are commission workers and regarded as employees, use the Earnings and Reporting workflow to add new, edit, or update income details.

If the Earnings and Reporting workflow is not available, code manually on the Employment Income Paid Details (EAPP) screen.

If the employee is paid an advance of a future commission, a review of their contract should determine if the advance is a repayable loan - no income is assessed if it is a loan.

If SAC (Sales Commission) employer details are already recorded, multiple instances of new commission income can be recorded against the same employer.

If employer details are not already recorded, add a new employer and code the following fields:

  • Type: key SAC (Sales Commission)

5

Customers who are commission workers or receive royalties and regarded as self-employed + Read more ...

Where the income forms part of the person’s regular business income, assess the income as part of the normal self-employment assessment. See Assessment of income and assets from business structures for Centrelink payments.

Where the income is a one-off occurrence record the income on the Other Income (OIN) screen and assess for a period of 52 weeks.

The date of event (DOV) is the date a change of circumstance or notifiable event actually occurred.

The Other Income Summary (OINS) screen will only assess a single amount of income from each particular employer or other income source. Each new date and amount recorded overrides the previously recorded amount. As lump sums can be apportioned up to 52 weeks, each time an individual lump sum is received, it must be added as a new employer with a unique Employer Name/Description to remain on OINS for the full relevant period.

Check to ensure any old income coded previously on OIN using an ongoing assessment code, for example, MTH, ANN, etc. have been removed at the end of the assessment period.

Code the following fields on the OIN screen:

  • Type: key 'ROC' (Royalty or Commission)
  • Description: key the source of the commission business or royalty income - free text. If the customer receives lump sums from a number of contracts with the same company within a 12 month period, a unique Description needs to be used for each instance of contract income. For example, code as Porter's Homes 12/09/16, Porter's Homes 26/09/16, Porter's Homes 10/10/16, etc., using the date of event of the payment
  • Event Date: key the date the commission was paid
  • Amount $: key the net commission/royalty paid (gross amount received less allowable expenses incurred)
  • Freq: key 'ANN'
  • See Recording other income on the Other Income (OIN) screen for assistance coding the OIN screen

Go to Step 7.

6

Customers who are contract workers and regarded as self-employed + Read more ...

Where the income forms part of the person’s regular business income, assess the income as part of the normal self-employment assessment. See Assessment of income and assets from business structures for Centrelink payments.

Where the income is a one-off occurrence record the income on the Other Income (OIN) screen and assess for a period of 52 weeks.

The date of event (DOV) is the date a change of circumstance or notifiable event actually occurred.

The Other Income Summary (OINS) screen will only assess a single amount of income from each particular employer or other income source. Each new date and amount recorded overrides the previously recorded amount. As lump sums can be apportioned up to 52 weeks, each time an individual lump sum is received, it must be added as a new employer with a unique Employer Name/Description to remain on OINS for the full relevant period.

Code the following fields on the OIN screen:

  • Type: key 'OTH' (other income)
  • Description: key the source of the income. (If the customer receives lump sums from a number of contracts with the same company within a 12 month period, a unique Description needs to be used for each instance of contract income for example code as George's Services 12/09/16, George's Services 2, George's Services 3, etc. using the date of event of the payment)
  • Event Date: key the date the income was entitled to be received
  • Amount $: convert an annual rate of income for the net lump sum amount (income less allowable expenses) of the contractor income. For example if the net income was $3,000 for 3 months of work, an amount of $12,000 would be recorded
  • Freq: key 'ANN'
  • See Recording other income on the Other Income (OIN) screen for assistance coding the OIN screen

Go to Step 7.

7

Set a review to code off income - only for income recorded on OINS + Read more ...

If commission is to be held for a lesser period, a review needs to be coded to remove the commission income from the customer's record.

After coding the commission or contract details on the OIN screen, code a manual review to remove that income assessment. For example if the commission was received on 1 May 2020 and the commission is to be assessed for 6 weeks, the review should be set for 12 June 2020 to zero off the income at the end of the 6 week period.

Check any income coded previously using an ongoing assessment code, for example MTH, ANN, etc. on OIN has been reduced to $0 from the end of its assessment period. To set a manual review, see How to code and action a manual review.

Record a Note or DOC.

If the customer or partner receives Family Tax Benefit (FTB) run the Family Income and Choices workflow to record the customer's new estimate, if required, after completing the income update.

For Child Care Subsidy (CCS) customers, see Changes in circumstances and date of effect for Child Care Subsidy (CCS) and Additional Child Care Subsidy (ACCS).
