Step

Action

Before referring to a provider + Read more ...

Is the job seeker eligible for Disability Employment Service (DES), Australian Disability Enterprises (ADE) or Community Development Program (CDP)?

  • Yes, and the job seeker:
    • is eligible for DES, go to Step 2
    • lives in a Department of Employment and Workplace Relations designated remote area, the job seeker is to be referred to a Community Development Program (CDP) provider. Procedure ends here
    • is eligible for Workforce Australia - Transition to Work (TtW), go to Step 3
  • No, go to Step 4

2

Eligible for DES + Read more ...

Job seekers eligible for DES do not require a referral unless they want to access Workforce Australia. This is voluntary. However, all job seekers must be encouraged to access Workforce Australia and have an initial appointment booked and they will be able to lodge a resume. This will assist them to access a wide range of job vacancies even if they are doing other activities.

Does the job seeker wish to access services from Workforce Australia?

  • Yes, continue with the referral. Go to Step 5
  • No, the job seeker does not require a referral at this time. Procedure ends here

3

Eligible for Workforce Australia - Transition to Work (TtW) + Read more ...

Is the job seeker eligible for TtW?

  • Yes, follow the TtW referral process
  • No, go to Step 4

4

Mutual obligation exemptions + Read more ...

Check if the job seeker has one of the following mutual obligation requirements exemptions lasting more than six weeks in the future:

  • temporary incapacity
  • jury duty
  • volunteering during a state or national emergency

Does the jobseeker have an exemption?

  • Yes, the job seeker is not required to be referred to Workforce Australia. They will be advised by letter toward the end of their exemption to contact Services Australia to arrange for the referral to Workforce Australia. The process is adjusted to coincide with a new expiry date where the exemption period is:
    • reduced
    • extended, or
    • immediately followed by a different allowable mutual obligation requirements exemption
  • No, go to Step 5

5

Further exemptions + Read more ...

Check if the job seeker has one of the following mutual obligation requirements exemptions:

  • refugees (first 6 months)
  • refugees (first 13 weeks)
  • expectant mothers
  • job seekers serving a Community Service Order
  • job seekers with caring responsibilities
  • job seekers experiencing a major personal crisis
  • job seekers experiencing major personal disruption at home

Does the jobseeker have one of the above exemptions?

  • Yes, the customer is not required to be referred to Workforce Australia during the exemption period, however, they can choose to volunteer. If the job seeker:
    • volunteers to participate, continue with the referral, go to Step 6
    • chooses not to participate, procedure ends here
  • No, go to Step 6

6

Referral to other programs + Read more ...

Make a referral for the job seeker to another participation program if appropriate. See:

  • Skills for Education and Employment (SEE)
  • Self-Employment Assistance
  • Reconnect
  • Adult Migrant English Program (AMEP)

Job seekers participating in these programs must still be referred to Workforce Australia.

A voluntary referral to Workforce Australia can also be offered when:

  • a customer is 21 years of age and under, and
    • not on income support, or
    • in receipt of a payment without mutual obligation requirements, or
    • participating in one of the approved activities/programs that fully meet their requirements
  • a principal carer or a customer assessed as having a partial capacity to work is:
    • already fully meeting their requirements by undertaking suitable paid work or self-employment of at least 30 hours per fortnight, or
    • studying full-time

Does the customer need a referral or accept a voluntary referral for Workforce Australia?

  • Yes, go to Step 7
  • No, procedure ends here

7

Is the job seeker registering, already registered or re-registering? + Read more ...

  • If the referral is being done as part of an initial registration process, the List Providers screen will be presented. See Step 1 in Table 2
  • If the job seeker is already registered but has not been referred to an Employment Services Provider (for example, responding to the no provider letter as exemption from mutual obligation requirements is ending):
    • go to the Participation Summary, Job Seeker registration & provider referral information tab
    • select Start job seeker registration button and complete another Job Seeker Snapshot
    • book an appointment through the Online Diary
      Note: the job seeker must receive proper notification and reasonable notice of the appointment. See Step 1 in Table 2
  • If the referral is being attempted and the Service Officer is getting the Job seeker is not eligible for the program error message, see Table 3
  • If the job seeker is re-registering and already has a provider, determine the allowable break in registration. Go to Step 8

8

Job seeker is re-registering + Read more ...

  • If the job seeker is re-registering within their allowable break in registration (13 weeks), there is no change in the registration date and they are reconnected to the same provider, no selection/allocation is required. No appointment is needed. The provider will contact the job seeker. Procedure ends here
  • For job seekers re-registering more than 13 weeks after the date of inactivation, the referral process begins as if the job seeker is registering for the first time. See Step 1 in Table 2

Selection and allocation of a Workforce Australia Employment Services Provider

Table 2: This table describes the process of selecting and allocating a provider. Note: this process does not apply to job seeker selected for Workforce Australia Online for Individuals. These job seekers have the referral completed by the Department of Employment and Workplace Relations (DEWR).

Step

Action

1

The List Providers screen + Read more ...

A list of all providers mapped to the job seeker's postcode appears on this screen. More than one page may be available and there is an ability to scroll through these selections. Providers that are full or not yet open for business will display even though they may not be selected.

This screen provides different options to display Workforce Australia providers that are mapped to the job seeker's postcode. Select either Show providers with Immediate Appointments or Show all providers at the top of the screen. Show Providers with Immediate Appointments will be defaulted on this screen.

  • For RapidConnect customers: when Show Providers with Immediate Appointments is selected, the list will display only those providers that have appointments within the system date plus 2 working days. By selecting the Show All Providers, the list will be amended to display those providers that have available appointments within 14 days
  • For non-RapidConnect customers: when Show Providers with Immediate Appointments is selected, the list will display only those providers that have appointments within the system date plus 2 working days. By selecting Show All Providers, the list will be amended to display those providers that have available appointments within 28 days

Providers who do not have available appointments on the day or within the next 2 working days will have a status of having no immediate appointments sessions within 2 business days. Encourage job seekers to choose a provider with immediate appointments available on the day or within 2 working days.

Ask the job seeker to select a Workforce Australia provider from the list.

  • Only an Available, No sessions within 2 business days, and No immediate sessions within 14 business days provider with available capacity can be selected
  • A job seeker can only have one Workforce Australia provider
  • If the job seeker's preferred Workforce Australia provider is full, they will need to select another provider
  • If the job seeker wants to inform their decision by viewing the Workforce Australia provider performance ratings, see the Resources page - Workforce Australia – Services Provider Performance Ratings

Has the job seeker selected a Workforce Australia provider?

  • Yes, place the mouse on the provider details and select once. Go to Step 7
  • No, go to Step 2

2

Does the job seeker wish to refine the search criteria? + Read more ...

  • Yes, select Refine Search. Go to Step 3
  • No, go to Step 6

3

Refine the search for Workforce Australia provider + Read more ...

The job seeker can refine the search criteria to find a provider more suitable to their needs.

Refine the search by selecting one of the following fields:

  • Provider Name field
  • Provider Postcode field

Enter the additional search criteria and select Search (select once).

4

Ask the job seeker to select a Workforce Australia provider + Read more ...

A list of providers resulting from the refined search appears on the List Providers screen. Please note that this screen may display differently on each web service call.

Ask the job seeker to select a provider.

Only an open provider with available capacity can be selected.

Has the job seeker selected a Workforce Australia provider from the list resulting from the refined search(es)?

  • Yes, place the mouse on the provider details and either select once or press [Enter]. Go to Step 7
  • No, go to Step 5

5

Refusal to satisfy mutual obligation requirements + Read more ...

Has the job seeker refused to register with an Employment Services Provider?

  • Yes, a job seeker must be willing to satisfy their mutual obligation requirements to qualify for payment. A job seeker should have their claim rejected or payment cancelled if they advise they do not intend to meet their requirements, including being registered as a job seeker or referred to an employment services provider. See:
    • Rejecting a claim for JobSeeker Payment (JSP) or Youth Allowance (YA) (job seeker)
    • Cancellation, suspension and rejection reason codes for Newstart System (NSS)
    • Cancellation of payments
  • No, randomly allocate a provider to the job seeker. Go to Step 6

6

Random allocation + Read more ...

Random allocation uses the criteria of the job seeker's postcode to find providers with available capacity.

Select random allocation (select once) to randomly allocate a provider to the job seeker.

Selecting random allocation randomly allocates a provider to the job seeker and navigates to the Available Appointments screen.

If the job seeker is not happy with the provider allocated randomly, up to 4 additional random allocations can be undertaken.

To conduct another random allocation, select back to initial list (select once).

If the job seeker is not happy with any of the maximum 5 random allocations, a Workforce Australia provider will be allocated.

7

Details of the selected Workforce Australia provider are displayed + Read more ...

The Available Appointments screen displays the following information about the selected provider:

  • name
  • address
  • phone number
  • service status
  • session type
  • specialist status (if any)

If the selected Workforce Australia provider has appointments available in the next 2 working days, these appointments will be displayed first. To view available appointments that are outside the next 2 working days, select More Appointments.

Where appropriate, contact the Workforce Australia provider to request more appointments be opened within a certain timeframe (for example, next 2 working days). Once the provider has scheduled more appointments, view the additional appointments by selecting Refresh on the Available Appointments screen. If the resulting Workforce Australia provider has no immediate appointments, the job seeker must provide a reason why they have chosen this provider in preference to a provider with immediate availability. See the Resources page for a list of acceptable reasons. Note: this functionality has been temporarily removed from the Online Diary flow until further notice.

Wherever possible, job seekers should be encouraged to accept referrals to Workforce Australia providers with immediate appointments or their payment start date may be affected.

Open providers will not require a reason code.

8

Make an appointment on the Online Diary + Read more ...

  • Make an appointment on the Online Diary with the selected Workforce Australia provider
  • Print the appointment letter and give or post to the job seeker
  • After making the appointment, record a DOC outlining the details of the appointment and the verbal notification provided to the job seeker. Record the DOC using:
    • the Notes icon within Process Direct, or
    • switch to Customer First and run the Provider Appointment Fast Note - select Auto text, use Jobseekers > Update > Provider Appointment Booked

Note: the job seeker must receive proper notification and reasonable notice of the appointment.

Job seeker not eligible for program message

Table 3: This table helps troubleshoot a solution when a Service Officer encounters the job seeker not eligible for program system message.

Step

Action

1

'Job seeker not eligible for program' system message + Read more ...

This message is not a system fault.

If the message displays when trying to refer a job seeker to an Employment Services Provider, further investigation and action is required before making the referral.

If the message presents:

  • outside of a new claim process - standalone Job Seeker Snapshot, go to Step 2
  • during the job seeker claim appointment (Participation Interview) as part of new claim, go to Step 3

2

For job seeker referrals outside of the new claim process - standalone Jobseeker Snapshot + Read more ...

Navigate to the Employment ESAt referral request history table by selecting:

  • > Participation Summary > Job seeker registration and provider referral information

If a pending ESAt is displayed with a ‘Started’ status in the Type of Action field:

  • a Personal Factors or Special Needs assessment is required to be undertaken by Assessment Services
  • confirm that an ‘ESAt required’ activity exists on the Activities information within the Process Direct Snapshot Icon. Further assessment is required before a referral to an Employment Service can occur
  • procedure ends here.

If a pending ESAt is not displayed with a ‘Started’ status in the Type of Action field, check the:

  • Registration type. If it appears correct, re-run Job Seeker Registration including the Job Seeker Snapshot. See Registering a customer as a job seeker
  • Status of latest Job Seeker Snapshot is Active. Navigate to the Job Seeker Snapshot from the Job seeker registration and provider referral information page. Access the Job Seeker Snapshot details screen after logging into the Workforce Australia system. If:
    • Pending, job seeker needs an ESAt referral or the ESAt recommendation withdrawn before being referred to a provider. See Actioning a pending Job Seeker Snapshot initiated Employment Services Assessment (ESAt) activity
    • Inactive, run the Job Seeker Snapshot to determine the most suitable referral option before making the referral. See Conducting a Job Seeker Snapshot interview
  • ESAt/Job Capacity Assessment (JCA) outcome from the Snapshot history - the recommended outcome and expiry presents. If the assessment:
    • is still current and valid, and the recommended outcome is DES, refer the job seeker to a DES provider. See Referring a job seeker to a Disability Employment Service (DES)
    • was finalised more than 2 years ago or is no longer current and valid, re-run the Job Seeker Snapshot. See Conducting a Job Seeker Snapshot interview
  • Procedure ends here

3

When completing the job seeker claim appointment (Participation Interview) for a new claim + Read more ...

Participation Interview - Job seeker needs an ESAt referral

If a job seeker needs an ESAt referral after completing the Job Seeker Snapshot, DEWR will generate an ESAt request to Services Australia systems. The relevant ESAt referral activities will generate once a Service Officer grants the job seeker's claim.

If Service Officers try to refer a job seeker to an employment service before the Job Seeker Snapshot status becomes Active, the message ‘Job seeker is not eligible for the program’ presents. Check the Job Seeker Snapshot status by selecting:

  • > Participation Interview > Job seeker registration and provider referral information > Job Seeker Snapshot and access the information within the Job Seeker Snapshot history screen after logging into the Workforce Australia system

If the status is Pending, an ESAt referral is needed instead. See Determining when an Employment Services Assessment (ESAt) is required.

Confirm if a RapidConnect determination is present on the Job Seeker RapidConnect (JMRC) screen:

  • Exit Participation Interview
  • Check JMRC screen

If a RapidConnect Status Determined line is present:

  • annotate the Progress of Claim DOC. Advise:
    • the Participation Interview is complete, and
    • claim processing staff must code a manual exemption, using code Exempt Other Reasons (EXO) on the JMRC screen when they finalise the claim. See RapidConnect exemptions

If the RapidConnect Status Determined line is not present:

  • re-run the Participation Interview within the claim transaction
  • check the JMRC screen has a RapidConnect Status Determined line. If so, follow the above workaround

Note: cancel the JobSeeker Registration transaction and change the new claim status from On Hold to In Process.

Procedure ends here.
