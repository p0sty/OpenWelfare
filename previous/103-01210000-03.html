Payment nominee appointment/cancellation enquiry

Table 1:

Portions of this process must only be completed by the Income Management Payment and Contact Team (IMPACT) or Income Management Policy Helpdesk.

Step

Action

1

A principal for Income Management purposes + Read more ...

If a payment nominee's payments are welfare quarantined under the following measures, the customer for whom they are payment nominee (the principal) will also have their payments welfare quarantined:

  • Cape York (CY) enhanced Income Management
  • Child Protection Income Management (CPIM) and enhanced Income Management (CPeIM)
  • Supporting People at Risk (SPaR), or
  • Compulsory Income Management (CIM) and enhanced Income Management (CeIM) - Disengaged Youth (DEY) or Long Term Welfare Payment Recipient (LTWPR)

A principal for Income Management and enhanced Income Management purposes will be welfare quarantined under the same measure as their payment nominee, unless they qualify for a different measure on their own. However, if a principal is no longer eligible for Income Management or enhanced IM on their own, they will start Nominee enhanced IM (NeIM).

Principals who are welfare quarantined under NIM or NeIM are not required to meet the eligibility criteria for the measure applicable to their payment nominee.

2

Payment nominee arrangements + Read more ...

Work Items are created on a welfare quarantined customer's record when an activity is actioned and requires further investigation or a review is required. A nominee related Work Item will be generated to identify a customer who is welfare quarantined and:

  • is a payment nominee or becomes a payment nominee for another person
  • has a payment nominee or has a payment nominee appointed
  • their payment nominee arrangement ends

The following Work Items will appear on the Income Management Summary screen if the welfare quarantined customer:

  • is a payment nominee
    • I045Q1 - Customer on IM and is a pay nominee. Check principal's IM status
  • has a payment nominee whose payments are welfare quarantined
    • I005Q1 - IM customer's nominee arrangement ended. Please review IM
    • I044Q1 - Customer is on IM and has a payment nominee. Check IM status

3

Review Nominee arrangements + Read more ...

The payment nominee arrangement must be reviewed if:

  • the payment nominee is on IM and enhanced IM or referred to any measure of enhance IM
  • the principal is referred to the Cape York enhanced IM, Child Protection enhanced IM, Supporting People at Risk (SPaR) or assessed/automatically identified as a Vulnerable Welfare Payment Recipient (VWPR)
  • at any time there is concern that the nominee arrangements are no longer appropriate
  • there is concern that the appointment of a payment nominee is an attempt to avoid IM or enhanced IM

Record details on a DOC of the review of the payment nominee arrangement. See Reviewing nominee arrangements.

The payment nominee arrangement should be cancelled or revoked if not in the best interests of the principal. For coding, see Cancelling a nominee arrangement.

4

Manual Follow-up (MFU) activities + Read more ...

In addition to a Work Item, an INM/NIM MFU activity that cannot be cancelled will be created when:

  • a payment nominee starts enhanced IM or ceases IM or enhanced IM
  • there is a change in status of the payment nominee's IM or enhanced IM (that is, Auto/Manual)
  • there is a change in the measure under which the payment nominee is welfare quarantined
  • a payment nominee arrangement ends
  • a welfare quarantined customer has or appoints a payment nominee
  • a welfare quarantined customer becomes a payment nominee
  • there is a change to the payment nominee's enhanced IM percentage (Cape York Initiative only)

MFUs will be automatically completed or deleted once the Nominee IM Assessment workflow has been completed, or if it is no longer valid.

Only one INM/NIM MFU will exist at any time. If a new MFU is created when there is an existing MFU, the MFU with the higher priority will be created, and the other deleted.

MFUs should only be actioned by the Income Management Payment & Contact Team (IMPACT). For contact details, see Income Management Payment and Contact team (IMPACT).

Service Officers must investigate the principal's IM or enhanced IM status and update any details on the Nominee IM Assessment screen or issue letters as appropriate.

Selecting the MFU will navigate to the Nominee IM Assessment workflow.

The Resources page contains a list of keywords and circumstances when an MFU will be created and what action is required.

4

Initial Assessment interview + Read more ...

If the payment nominee or principal (if applicable) require an interpreter, only continue with the Initial Assessment interview when an interpreter is present. See Booking an interpreter for an appointment

During the interview the following must be covered.

  • Explain Income Management and delivery of their payments
  • Discuss why Income Management is being put in place
  • Explain to the payment nominee they are responsible for managing the principal's Income Management funds
  • Explain what happens if the principal moves out of an Income Management area
  • Discuss how income managed money is accessed. For more information, see Coding Income Management expenses
  • Explain what income managed money can be used for example, School Meals Program (if applicable), food, rent/housing, utilities, clothing, and medical needs. See Determining a person's priority needs for Income Management
  • Explain how income managed money can assist in budgeting for larger purchases such as car registration, whitegoods, school fees
  • Explain what goods cannot be purchased from income managed funds (that is, prohibited goods and services - alcohol, home brew concentrate, home brew kits, tobacco products, pornographic material or gambling
  • Explain how money can be allocated to BasicsCard (issued to the payment nominee, or the principal at the instruction of the payment nominee) via regular and immediate allocations
  • Discuss priorities for Income Management expenditure. See Determining a person's priority needs for Income Management
  • If applicable, discuss converting existing Centrepay deductions to an income managed expense. Explain the impact of Income Management on any ongoing Centrepay deductions
  • Review and discuss any existing Rent Deduction Scheme payment and whether the customer would like to pay their State Housing Authority (SHA) rent from their income managed funds, their discretionary funds or a combination of both. See Determining a person's priority needs for Income Management for more information
  • Explain how money can be accessed for Income Management customers accessing funds while travelling
  • Explain how payment nominee and principal can contact Services Australia, including self service, in regards to Income Management and BasicsCard. See Income Management and BasicsCard self service options
  • Offer to register the payment nominee for self service
  • Explain how common changes in circumstances may affect Income Management and the importance of notifying Services Australia of any changes in the principal's expenses
  • Advise payment nominee of impact on expense payments if income support payments are reduced due to earnings from employment or other income
  • Explain the hierarchy of expense payments and the priority needs payment order when there are insufficient funds to cover an Income Management expense
  • Explain how lump sum payments such as advances and Family Tax Benefit (FTB) arrears and reconciliation payments are 100% income managed
  • Advise payment nominee and principle (if applicable) when Income Management ends, all Income Management expenses will stop. They will need to make arrangements to continue paying these expenses themselves, or contact the agency to discuss alternative payment options
  • Advise payment nominee and principal (if applicable) of their right to review Income Management and enhanced Income Management decisions

3

Deductions support + Read more ...

IM expenses are set up for regular and one off payments from an IM account with support from Services Australia. Customers cannot access their IM account except to transfer amounts to their BasicsCard.

Customers on enhanced IM can set up payments and pay bills directly from their enhanced IM account using their SmartCard, direct debit and BPAY. The customers payment nominee will manage the enhanced IM account linked to the SmartCard. Funds can be transferred to other enhanced IM accounts. Support for these processes is available through Services Australia or TCU (subject to customers choice on card issuer).

If the customer uses Centrepay, these payments are deducted from the customer's income support payments differently depending on whether the customer is on IM or enhanced IM. See Resources for a deduction comparison document to help explain it to the customer.

4

Balance checks and account services + Read more ...

IM customers have an IM account and must transfer funds to their BasicsCard. This can be done through self-service (web or app), as well as in person or phone with support from Services Australia. The IM account is not a bank account and does not support common banking payment options. Services Australia provides support with payment options when the BasicsCard is not accepted, such as booking and paying for flights and accommodation for travelling customers or paying for goods or services with a credit card.

Customers can check their BasicsCard balances through self-service (myGov, app, phone and big 4 banks, and by attending their local service centre)

Customers on enhanced IM can access the funds in their enhanced IM account through their SmartCard. They do not need to transfer funds between a card and account. The SmartCard will be issued to the customers payment nominee who will manage the customers enhanced IM account. The enhanced IM account is a bank account and supports a range of payment options including BPAY, direct debit, recurring deductions, and online transfers. Balance checks can be completed through the self-service options (online banking, app, by phone, selected ATMs or by SMS) or with support from Services Australia or TCU.

5

Card replacements + Read more ...

IM customers can get new or replacement BasicsCard after contacting the IM line:

  • at all service centres nationally
  • from remote servicing teams, or
  • by mail after contacting the IM line

Enhanced IM customers with a payment nominee are sent a personalised SmartCard to their payment nominee in the mail. An information pack is included containing information on how to activate the SmartCard and available support.

Pending receipt of a personalised SmartCard, Service Officers should tell enhanced IM customers where their payment nominee can obtain a temporary SmartCard.

Temporary SmartCards are available at:

  • selected service centres and participating Agent sites in the Northern Territory (NT),
  • declared welfare quarantined locations, and Cape York and Doomadgee region,
  • selected service centres and participating Agents in voluntary enhanced IM locations

Temporary TCU SmartCards (with PIN mailers) are available at:

  • selected service centres and participating Agent sites in the NT
  • as well as selected service centres in the Cape York and Doomadgee region
  • voluntary enhanced IM locations
  • some Remote Servicing teams.

For information about specific locations, see Enhanced Income Management (IM) - Service centres and Agents stocking temporary SmartCards and Traditional Credit Union (TCU) SmartCards.

Note: remind customers that the personalised and temporary SmartCards are linked directly to the enhanced IM account and are not disposable. To protect themselves, they must keep their SmartCard and PIN to themselves and report any lost, stolen or damaged SmartCards as soon as possible.

6

Access to cash + Read more ...

Under limited circumstances, IM customers can ask to access a part of their IM funds as cash when they cannot use their BasicsCard or when a third party will not accept a payment facilitated by Services Australia on behalf of the customer (Unrestricted Cash Payments or Restricted Cash Payments).

Customers on enhanced IM can use their card at a wider range of businesses. In situations where a customer cannot use their card to make a payment or use their online account, the payment nominee can contact the SmartCard eIM hotline for alternative options to make those payments.

The customer must give suitable supporting information for requests for funds under both IM and enhanced IM.

Note: Unrestricted Cash and Restricted Direct Payments (UCPs and RDPs) are not available under enhanced IM; this includes the 20% Child Protection and 50% Family Tax Benefit Reconciliation payments.

7

Excluded goods and services + Read more ...

IM funds and enhanced IM funds can be used to purchase anything except excluded goods and services.

Excluded goods and services include:

  • alcohol
  • gambling products and services (including lottery tickets and instant scratchies)
  • pornography
  • tobacco and tobacco products, or
  • withdraw funds as cash.

8

Online shopping + Read more ...

IM customers cannot shop online.

Customers on enhanced IM can shop online, except on eBay, PayPal or shops that sell excluded goods or services and/or are on the blocked merchant list.

9

Fees + Read more ...

There are no fees for IM or enhanced IM customers.

10

Tap to Pay and Digital Wallets + Read more ...

IM customers do not have access to digital wallets, payWave or Tap to Pay payments with their BasicsCard.

Customers on enhanced IM do have access to payWave or Tap to Pay using their SmartCard. This feature will need to be activated by the customer before use.

Digital Wallets are not available if a customer chooses TCU as their card issuer.

Note: payWave or Tap to Pay is available on all personalised SmartCards and new temporary SmartCards. Please check the SmartCard for the logo.

11

Interest + Read more ...

IM and enhanced IM customers do not earn interest on funds welfare quarantined accounts.

12

Portability + Read more ...

Nominee customers on IM and enhanced IM who move out of a welfare quarantine location will remain on IM or enhanced IM.

13

Card Choice + Read more ...

IM customers can be issued a BasicsCard that can be used at registered stores, often identified by displaying a sticker. There are over 18,500 terminals available.

Customers payment nominee on enhanced IM in the Northern Territory can choose to have their enhanced IM account managed through Services Australia or Traditional Credit Union (TCU). They are issued with a SmartCard that they can use wherever Visa or EFTPOS is accepted (over 1 million terminals) and online, excluding places that only sell excluded goods or services.

Customers payment nominee on enhanced IM outside the Northern Territory will have their enhanced IM account managed by Services Australia. They are issued with a SmartCard that they can use wherever Visa or EFTPOS is accepted (over 1 million terminals) and online, excluding places that only sell excluded goods or services.
