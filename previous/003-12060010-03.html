Process for assessing Crisis Payment (CrP) due to extreme circumstances family and domestic violence

Step

Action

1

Claim due to extreme circumstances Family and Domestic Violence + Read more ...

For Crisis Payment (CrP) for Other Extreme Circumstance (EXO), see New claim for Crisis Payment Other Extreme Circumstances.

If a Service Officer starts the claim, they will complete the basic eligibility checks before referring it to the social worker.

If the social worker starts the claim they must:

  • run the Assisted Customer Claim (ACC) with the information gathered, and
  • create a social work Crisis Payment referral inside the claim in Process Direct and hold the referral to themselves. See Work Optimiser for staff

Note: for these claims social workers are not required to obtain financial hardship verification from the customer unless directed by Crisis Payment Finalisation Line

2

Social worker - asses the claim + Read more ...

The social worker must:

  • use the Social Work Assessment in Progress Fast Note to add a note to the record
  • if required, review the claim details in Process Direct:
    • use the Inbox tile and enter the customer’s Customer Reference Number (CRN)
    • See Process Direct navigation, common screens and functions
  • check that the customer is now safe, and provide immediate support to the customer
  • start the CrP-EXV assessment. If the customer submits a claim for a CrP-EXV that has previously been rejected for the same circumstance, go to step 17
  • support the customer with other agency interventions, and
  • refer the customer if carer neglect may have occurred. See Reviewing Carer Payment (CP) and/or Carer Allowance (CA) following reports of carer neglect

3

Social worker – contact with the customer + Read more ...

The social worker:

  • must make genuine contact attempts to the customer and third parties over 2 business days. See Genuine attempts to contact by phone
  • must document all contact attempts on the customer’s record
  • can determine if there is sufficient information to make a determination, even if they cannot contact the customer

Was the social worker able to contact the customer:

  • Yes, go to Step 4
  • No, go to Step 6

4

Contact with the customer + Read more ...

Tell the customer about the assessment for Crisis Payment EXV.

Does the customer want to continue with the claim?

  • Yes, go to Step 7
  • No, go to step 5

5

Withdrawal of a claim + Read more ...

A customer can withdraw a claim if the social worker has not made a decision. For how to record a withdrawn claim see Recording Crisis Payment (CrP) assessments - social work.

Does the customer want to withdraw their claim?

  • Yes:
    • the social worker must tell the customer that by withdrawing the claim they:
      - will not have any formal review rights. This includes a right to appeal, as no social work determination was made, and
      - cannot claim for the same FDV event at a later date
    • complete the Crisis payment EXV Claim Withdrawn Fast Note, answering all fields with a Y/N or N/A (do not delete the fields) and make sure the Fast Note is closed
    • complete the social work Crisis Payment activity as this will allocate the claim to a Crisis Payment skill tagged Service Officer for processing
    • tell the customer their request to withdraw the claim has been processed
    • Procedure ends here
  • No, go to Step 7

6

Unable to contact the customer + Read more ...

If the social worker is unable to contact the customer, they must decide if there is enough information to continue with the claim.

Is there enough information to continue with the claim:

  • Yes, go to step 7
  • No, the claim must be rejected:
    • reject the claim due to insufficient information
    • complete the Crisis Payment Grant/Reject Fast Note with the rejection code Could not contact customer (CNC) and place a Display on Access (DOA) DOC on the customer’s record advising the claim was rejected due to CNC
    • complete the Social Work Crisis Payment activity. This will allow the claim to be processed
    • complete a rejection SWIS report (if required). See Recording Crisis Payment (CrP)
    • Procedure ends here

7

What is the customer's situation? + Read more ...

The social worker must determine how to assess the customers claim. Is the customer a:

  • victim of family and domestic violence who is leaving home, and:
    • has an Unreasonable to Live at Home (UTLAH) Income Support Payment (ISP) claim, go to Step 8, or
    • has a CrP claim go to Step 9
  • victim of family and domestic violence who is remaining at home, go to Step 11
  • alleged or actual perpetrator of family and domestic violence who is forced to leave home, go to Step 12

8

Customer lodges a combined Unreasonable to Live at Home (UTLAH)(Under 18) ISP claim and Crisis Payment claim + Read more ...

Does the social worker have the capability and capacity to complete the Crisis Payment assessment and the UTLAH assessment?

  • Yes, the social worker will:
    • assign the Crisis Payment referral to themselves
    • check for UTLAH appointment and proceed with the assessment
    • go to Step 9
  • No, the social worker will:
    • contact the customer to complete a well-being check
    • follow the Business As Usual (BAU) process to request an urgent UTLAH assessment. This should include detail to indicate there is a Crisis Payment claim
    • Procedure ends here

9

Customer is the victim of family and domestic violence and is leaving home + Read more ...

The social worker must discuss the customer's situation and provide interventions and referrals where appropriate.

In this situation, the alleged or actual perpetrator is defined as:

  • partner including husbands, wives, boyfriends or girlfriends
  • ex-partner including ex-boyfriends, ex-girlfriends, ex-husbands or ex-wives
  • carers or paid support workers
  • parent, guardian or other family members
  • child
  • other people the customer lives with

As part of the assessment the social worker must:

  • assess and determine the customer’s eligibility for CrP-EXV under the family and domestic violence criteria
  • determine if the customer left home because of family and domestic violence (extreme circumstance) and claimed within 7 days after the extreme circumstance occurred. Social workers can use their professional discretion if there are exceptional circumstances, which has delayed the customer in lodging a claim. This includes a hospital admission as a result of the violence
  • determine if the customer has found alternative accommodation and intends to establish a new home
  • check if the customer has uploaded third party verification, or if there is a third party who can verify the family and domestic violence circumstance
  • check the referral to determine if the Service Officer requires verification of financial hardship. If it is requested, check if the customer can upload financial verification to their agency record and support them to do so. In limited circumstances, a social worker can take a verbal verification of financial hardship. To complete the financial hardship verification the social worker should consider:
    • funds available for other essential expenses such as utilities cannot be exempt from the assessment. If the customer has funds over the allowable limit, they must be deemed ineligible for CrP
    • joint bank accounts. These must be included in the assessment. However if the account is considered unrealisable these funds may be excluded. See Unrealisable assets under the Assets Test hardship provisions
  • check if the customer needs to have their personal details updated by the Service Officer, for example updating relationship status, current address and accommodation details
  • check if the customer requires an additional social work assessment. Consider if a child support exemption assessment is required or social casework
  • ensure that the CrP-EXV assessment is completed, even if the customer's social security benefit, social security pension or ABSTUDY Living Allowance is not current (suspended or claim pending). Refer the customer for Immediate new claim and non-new claim priority processing or consider if a mutual obligation exemption may be applicable

Note: if a social worker is unable to obtain third party verification, they can use their professional discretion and must document this in the CrP-EXV SWIS report.

If the customers CrP-EXV assessment under the category of Left Home is significantly delayed due to agency processes they may qualify for a point in time assessment. See Step 10.

Does the customer have a fraud investigation DOC on their record?

  • Yes, go to Step 13
  • No go to Step 14

10

Does the customer qualify for a Point in time Assessment + Read more ...

The point in time assessment would apply in very limited circumstances. The customer must meet all of the qualification criteria for CrP-EXV. If the customer’s CrP-EXV assessment has been significantly delayed due to agency processes, the social worker can recommend a CrP-EXV claim is granted under the Left Home category

The customer will qualify if:

  • they were forced to return to the home where the family and domestic violence circumstances occurred due to financial hardship; and
  • at the time of claim it was unreasonable for the customer to be expected to remain in or return to the home; and
  • they have no alternative accommodation options; and
  • they intended to establish a new home; and
  • the customer had an immediate plan to leave the home at the time the customer submitted the claim for Crisis Payment.

The social worker must obtain sufficient verification and clearly document the customer’s circumstances on their record for the Service Officer to determine the customers’ qualification for CrP-EXV.

Go to Step 14

11

Customer is the victim of family and domestic violence and is remaining at home + Read more ...

The social worker must discuss the customer's situation and provide interventions and referrals where appropriate.

In this situation, a family member is defined as:

  • a partner or parent
  • a sibling or child
  • other people treated as the relevant person’s relations (e.g. grandparent or ex-partner)

As part of the assessment the social worker must:

  • assess and determine the customer’s eligibility for CrP under the family and domestic violence criteria
  • determine if the customer has remained in the home after experiencing family and domestic violence (extreme circumstance) and claimed within 7 days after the extreme circumstance occurred. Social workers can use their professional discretion if there are exceptional circumstances which has delayed the customer in lodging a claim, such as a hospital admission as a result of the violence
  • determine the actual or alleged perpetrator was living at the customer’s home at the time of the event
  • determine if the actual or alleged perpetrator (defined as an immediate family member) has left or been legally prevented from returning to the home
  • check if there is evidence that the actual or alleged perpetrator is legally prevented from returning to the home. For example, an Apprehended Violence Order (AVO) with an exclusion provision that prohibits the actual or alleged perpetrator from living in the family home with the claimant (protected person). In some cases the family member may leave without police involvement and verification of the violent relationship will need to be sought from a third party such as:
    • doctors
    • domestic violence services
    • legal services
    • crisis workers
    • other household members
    • community agencies
    • schools
    • child care services
  • check if the Service Officer requires verification of financial hardship. If it is requested, check if the customer can upload financial verification to their agency record and support them to do so. In limited circumstances, a social worker can take a verbal verification of financial hardship. To complete the financial hardship verification the social worker should consider:
    • funds available for other essential expenses such as utilities. These cannot be exempt from the assessment. If the customer has funds over the allowable limit, they must be deemed ineligible for CrP
    • joint bank accounts. These must be included in the assessment. However if the account is considered unrealisable these funds may be excluded. See Unrealisable assets under the Assets Test hardship provisions
  • check if the customer needs to have their personal details updated by the Service Officer, for example updating relationship status, current address and accommodation details
  • check if the customer requires an additional social work assessment. Consider if a child support exemption assessment is required or social casework
  • ensure that the CrP-EXV assessment is completed even if the customer's social security benefit, social security pension or ABSTUDY Living Allowance is not current (suspended or claim pending). Refer the customer for Immediate new claim and non-new claim priority processing or consider if a mutual obligation exemption may be applicable

Does the customer have a fraud investigation DOC on their record?

  • Yes, go to Step 13
  • No go to Step 14

12

Customer is the actual or alleged perpetrator of family and domestic violence and has left the home + Read more ...

The social worker must discuss the customer's situation and provide interventions and referrals where appropriate

In this situation, the other party is defined as:

  • partner including husbands, wives, boyfriends or girlfriends
  • ex-partner including ex-boyfriends, ex-girlfriends, ex-husbands or ex-wives
  • carers or paid support workers
  • parent, guardian or other family members
  • child
  • other people the customer lives with

As part of the assessment the social worker must:

  • assess and determine the customer’s eligibility for CrP under the family and domestic violence criteria
  • determine if the customer is identified as the alleged or actual perpetrator of family and domestic violence (extreme circumstance) and claimed within 7 days after the extreme circumstance occurred
  • check if there is evidence that the actual or alleged perpetrator (claimant) is legally prevented from returning to the family home. For example, an Apprehended Violence Order (AVO) with an exclusion provision that prohibits the customer from living in the family home with the protected person. There may be instances where a legal order is issued for a short time period of 24 to 72 hours. Use this as an indicator that an extreme circumstance has occurred. There may no longer be a legal reason preventing the customer from returning home, but they may decide to establish a new home which would be considered safer for the victim

Note: there is no discretion for social workers to use their professional judgement in cases where there is no evidence of police involvement and no evidence of the customer being legally prevented from returning home.

  • verify that the actual or alleged perpetrator lived with the victim in the family home immediately before the family and domestic violence incident occurred and the legal order was enforced
  • check if the Service Officer requires verification of financial hardship. If it is requested, check if the customer can upload financial verification to their agency record and support them to do so. In limited circumstances, a social worker can take a verbal verification of financial hardship. To complete the financial hardship verification the social worker should consider:
    • funds available for other essential expenses such as utilities cannot be exempt from the assessment. If the customer has funds over the allowable limit, they must be deemed ineligible for CrP
    • joint bank accounts. These must be included in the assessment. However if the account is considered unrealisable these funds may be excluded. See Unrealisable assets under the Assets Test hardship provisions
  • check if the customer needs to have their personal details updated by the Service Officer, for example updating relationship status, current address and accommodation details
  • check if the customer requires an additional social work assessment
  • ensure that the CrP-EXV assessment is completed even if the customer's social security benefit, social security pension or ABSTUDY Living Allowance is not current (suspended or claim pending). Refer the customer for immediate new claim and non-new claim priority processing or consider if a mutual obligation exemption may be applicable.

Does the customer have a fraud investigation DOC on their record?

  • Yes, go to Step 13
  • No go to Step 14

13

Customer has an active fraud investigation Doc on their record + Read more ...

During assessment of a CrP-EXV the social worker must contact Fraud Investigation team if:

  • there is an investigation DOC on the customer’s record, and
  • the DOC on the record states that an investigation is currently in progress, with the investigators name and contact details

Is there an active fraud investigation DOC on the customer’s record?

  • Yes, the social worker should delay making a determination until they speak to the investigator, and the investigator has provided advice. Give the investigator 24 hours to reply before proceeding with the assessment outcome
  • No, proceed with the assessment outcome, go to Step 14

14

Outcome + Read more ...

Determine if the customer qualifies for CrP under the family and domestic violence provisions.

After the claim determination is made:

  • record the determination using the relevant grant, reject Fast Note
  • select Auto text. Use Social Worker > Claims and answer all fields (do not delete the fields) and include any other information relevant for the Service Officer to make a determination. This includes:
    • any updates to the agency record for the Service Officer to complete when finalising the claim. Consider relationship status, address and rent updates, including previous address details, and other updates such as nominee or PPE arrangements. For more information on address updates see the Resource page and New claim for Crisis Payment due to extreme circumstances family and domestic violence
    • any updates to the customers living arrangements. For example, if a customer says they are living in shared accommodation, ask them The Living Arrangements question set and record their answers in the Fast Note
  • record any relationship updates. Create a ‘CrP disclosed relationship - follow up required’ Fast Note, if the customer is recoded as single and advises they are or were partnered during the claim. Select Auto text. Use Social Worker > Claims. Within the Fast Note reference the CrP-EXV SWIS report and add any details regarding the relationship
  • use the CrP Grant/Reject Fast Note to finalise all CrP-EXV assessments as a priority
  • discuss the claim determination with their Social Work Support Manager if they are an undelegated social worker. This should be done prior to completing the CrP Grant/Reject Fast Note. This includes customers who are not ISP current (suspended or claim pending)
  • see Reviewing Carer Payment (CP) and/or Carer Allowance (CA) following reports of carer neglect if carer neglect may have occurred

If the crisis payment is:

  • Granted, go to Step 15
  • Rejected, go to Step 16

15

CrP-EXV- granted + Read more ...

If the claim is granted:

  • contact the ‘Crisis Payment Finalisation SWO USE ONLY’ using the 'Crisis Payment Claim SWO Assessed' Services Australia Workspace transfer option or 178 911
  • tell the Service Officer about the outcome, they will assign the claim to themselves
  • complete the social work referral work item. This will allow the claim to be processed
  • tell the customer of the claim outcome, procedure ends here

If the customer meets the eligibility criteria for CrP-EXV but there is a pending social security benefit, social security pension or ABSTUDY Living Allowance claim, hold the CrP-EXV activity until the claim activity has been determined. Once the customer is current on a social security benefit, social security pension or ABSTUDY Living Allowance, follow the above steps.

16

CrP-EXV rejected + Read more ...

If the claim is rejected:

  • record the rejection code for why the claim is rejected in the fast note
  • complete the social work referral work item. This will allow the claim to be allocated to a skill tagged Service Officer for processing
  • and if the claim needs immediate processing, contact the ‘Crisis Payment Finalisation SWO USE ONLY’ using the 'Crisis Payment Claim SWO Assessed' Services Australia Workspace transfer option or 178 911. Once the Service Officer has answered the call and assigned the claim to themselves, complete the social work referral work item. This will allow the claim to be processed
  • tell the customer of their review and appeal rights. Go to Step 17

17

Review and appeals process + Read more ...

If the customer submits a claim or contacts for the same CrP-EXV circumstance and the:

  • claim has been rejected CNC ‘Could Not Contact’, and
  • customer has contacted within 13 weeks of the claim being rejected

The social worker must:

  • view the CrP-EXV claim in PD, and
  • assess the customer’s circumstances based on the original information provided and make a determination
  • follow steps outlined in step 14 Outcome

If a customer contacts to discuss the outcome of their CrP-EXV claim that has been rejected by a social worker, see the Resource tab

If a customer contacts to discuss the outcome of their CrP-EXV claim that has been rejected by a Service Officer, the social worker must:

  • complete an appeal script, and
  • advise the customer that they will be contacted by a Service Officer to discuss the outcome

Regardless of who made the claim determination, if the customer requests a formal review of the decision:

  • an appeal script must be completed, and
  • the social worker must advise that the formal review will be allocated to an Authorised Review Officer

For more details, see Initial contact about a decision and the review of decision process.

In all instances where a customer contacts wanting to discuss the outcome of their claim, the social worker should:

  • ask the customer if they have new details about the family and domestic violence incident that was assessed, and
  • assist the customer to upload this information to their record

Note: a customer’s claim cannot be granted multiple times for the same family and domestic violence incident.

18

Contingency Arrangements if system issues occur + Read more ...

Social workers should not use contingency arrangements unless directed to do so by the Social Work Services Branch.

Social Work Services Branch will be responsible for providing details on contingency processes to make sure that there is no delay to a customer's claim for CrP-EXV See Resources page for Contingency Fast Notes.
