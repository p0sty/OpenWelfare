This page contains information about the financial penalties that are applied as a result of a mutual obligation failure, work refusal failure or unemployment failure under TCF.

Impact on job seeker as a result of a failure

Item

Description

1

Accepting a financial penalty as part of reporting + Read more ...

Job seekers can choose to accept the financial penalty for certain mutual obligation failures without a compliance investigation.

These failures include a first or second failure in the financial penalty zone for:

  • non-attendance at a:
    • provider appointment
    • third party appointment
    • job interview
    • compulsory activity, or
  • failing to meet other requirements (for example, not meeting points target)

Where the job seeker is reporting online, they are advised of the requirement that they missed and if they have a good reason for not meeting the requirement to contact Services Australia before finalising their report. If the job seeker indicates they:

  • do not want to talk to the agency, completing the report will automatically apply the financial penalty
  • do want to talk to the agency, the failure becomes an active failure and prevents finalisation of the report until the compliance investigation is completed. The job seeker must contact the Participation Solutions Team (PST)

Where a job seeker contacts about an incomplete report due to an outstanding mutual obligation failure, Service Officers must have the PST skill tag or transfer the job seeker to PST for the failure to be investigated prior to finalising the report.

2

Date of effect for mutual obligation failure penalties + Read more ...

Generally, a payment deduction for a first or second mutual obligation failure will affect the participation payment for the period in which the mutual obligation failure occurred.

Where a financial penalty for a mutual obligation failure is accepted by the job seeker as part of finalising their reporting, the associated payment deduction will immediately affect the payment due.

Where Participation Solutions Team (PST) staff conduct a compliance investigation, the financial penalty will immediately affect the payment due.

3

Multiple mutual obligation failures in the same entitlement period + Read more ...

A first and a second financial penalty in the failure group may be applied in the same entitlement period.

Where a second financial penalty has been applied, the job seeker will not receive a participation payment for this entitlement period.

A third mutual obligation failure cannot be applied for the same instalment period in which a 100% payment deduction has been applied.

Note: there is no reduction in the monthly points target, minimum job search or other compulsory requirements where the job seeker has incurred a first or second penalty.

4

Non-compliance cancellations and dates of effect + Read more ...

The date of effect of the payment cancellation resulting from:

  • failure to re-engage within 4 weeks of a mutual obligation failure or work refusal failure is the date the re-engagement requirement was notified
  • a third mutual obligation failure or work refusal failure is generally the start of the instalment period in which the failure occurred. Where reporting for this instalment period has already been completed, the cancellation will apply from the start of the next available instalment period
  • an unemployment failure is the next payment period after the decision is made

The non-compliance cancellation codes and reasons are:

  • CDR - Customer failed to re-engage - a participation suspension has been applied and re-engagement has not occurred within 4 weeks of the reconnection requirement deemed date of notification
  • TCP - Mutual obligation or work refusal penalty
  • TCU - Unemployment failure penalty
  • TCF - Did not meet provider requirement - record has been suspended for more than 13 weeks following failure to meet a provider requirement and a CDR cancellation has not occurred
  • TCW - Provider work refusal failure - record has been suspended for more than 13 weeks following a provider reported work refusal failure and a CDR cancellation has not occurred

Note: legitimate non-compliance cancellations are non-restorable. The job seeker will need to reclaim to restart payment. If a job seeker has met their requirements but their payment is cancelled due to system issues refer to PST for investigation and correction. See Step 7 in Table 1 of Re-engaging job seekers - Targeted Compliance Framework for more information.

5

Duration of post-cancellation non-payment period + Read more ...

The post-cancellation non-payment period commences from the date of effect of the non-compliance cancellation.

The duration of the post-cancellation non-payment period for:

  • a third mutual obligation financial penalty is 4 weeks
  • a work refusal failure is 4 weeks
  • an unemployment failure while in receipt of payment is 4 weeks, or 6 weeks where the failure is related to a relocation job

6

Unemployment preclusion period + Read more ...

When an unemployment failure is identified as part of a new claim, the unemployment preclusion period commences from the date of unemployment, which is generally the day after date last worked.

The duration of the unemployment preclusion period is 4 weeks or 6 weeks if the job seeker left a relocation job within the 6 months immediately prior to the unemployment failure.

Job seekers may have self-served part or all of an unemployment preclusion period prior to claiming payment.

A claimant may also self serve a potential preclusion period if a concurrent waiting period exceeds the length of the unemployment preclusion period.

The amount of Family Tax Benefit (FTB) payable to the job seeker or their partner, including Rent Assistance (RA) paid with FTB, is not affected by the unemployment preclusion period.
