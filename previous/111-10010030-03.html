Scanning Centrelink documents using an MFD 111-10010030

Before starting this process, staff must read the Operational Message.

Expand all


Risks to a customer's privacy have been identified. See Separating Safely - protecting personal details to make sure the customer's personal details are safe before progressing.

Before you scan, use Operational Blueprint to assess the enquiry and process as required.

This page contains information for service centre staff on how and when to scan Centrelink documents using a Multi-Function Device (MFD). Separate procedures apply for Medicare scanning and National Redress Scheme documents.

Action

1

Assess document for immediate updates and processing + Read more ...

To support vulnerable customers, assess the document at the time of lodgement and assess whether immediate processing is needed. Consider all of the following:

  • the vulnerability of the customer
  • how it would affect the customer if information provided is not updated at the earliest opportunity (such as Intervention Orders, Separation Detail (MOD S), Relationship details (SS284) or change of address for customers affected by family and domestic violence and at risk of harm)
  • the steps needed to keep the customer's personal information safe if their relationship or living circumstances have changed. See Separating safely – protecting personal details

Is immediate processing required?

  • Yes:
    • update the record and process as required. For some urgent processing, different processes apply
    • once processing is completed, go to Step 2
  • No, go to Step 2

2

Customer attends a service centre to lodge documents + Read more ...

Promote and help the customer download the Express Plus Centrelink mobile app and upload their documents.

Encourage customers to lodge all their documents electronically if possible. This will help ensure they are quickly available on the customer record.

Use discretion when promoting online services to a vulnerable customer. See Separating safely - protecting personal details.

Is the customer lodging a scannable document?

  • Yes, go to Step 3
  • No:
    • Check if there are any essential tasks to follow up as a result of this enquiry
    • If identity documents were received, see Coding identity documents
    • Procedure ends here

3

Search customer's CRN + Read more ...

Has the customer supplied their Customer Reference Number (CRN)?

  • Yes:
    • If the customer's record needs to be converted into an adult record, see Searching for a customer on the system
    • If the customer already has an adult record, go to Step 4
  • No:
    • Complete a thorough search for the customer
    • If no customer record has been identified after a search, create a new record

4

Prepare the documents + Read more ...

Conduct the initial vetting process. This includes checking that forms are signed if necessary.

Then remove any notes pages and return to the customer. Do not scan notes pages unless the customer has written information on them.

If the CRN is not already recorded on the form, write the Customer Reference Number (CRN) in the top right hand corner of the first page of the customer document. If the customer does not have a CRN, or has a child record, create a new record before scanning.

  • Remove staples and pages that do not need scanning
  • Ensure pages are flat and that folds and crinkles will not stop it feeding through the MFD. If the document is in poor condition, make a photocopy for scanning
  • When photocopying documents, adjust contrast settings for documents of poor quality or high contrast. For example, driver’s licences, coloured backgrounds, photos. If copies are still not clear, consider writing key information or details to help with processing and to prevent unnecessary rescan requests
  • Do not date stamp the documents. Unless it is changed manually at the MFD, the date of receipt is the date of scan
  • Place documents in a logical order as much as possible. For example, payslips in date order

For documents with a barcode, ensure the first page to be scanned displays the barcode. For example, page 4 on FA081. Barcodes are often at the bottom of forms. System generated letters have the barcode at the top. Always scan the claim/renewal form first if scanning multiple forms. See the Resources page for the Scanning Order user guide.

For documents with no barcode, choose the most relevant reclassification page. Place this in front of the unstructured document. See the Resources page for reclassification pages. Print them double sided so they have a Patch T separator on the back.

Include a Received 'as is' coversheet if:

  • it is clear that pages are missing. For example, the customer provided only pages 1 and 2 of a 4 page form, or
  • the quality of the image cannot be improved

This will prevent rework related to rescan requests. See the Resources page for coversheets for printing.

Note: not following the above process will cause delays to the document attaching to the customer's record.

5

Claim forms and supporting documents lodged together + Read more ...

No reclassification page is needed as the claim forms will be barcoded.

Only place a separator page between the documents if separate processing would be required. For example, a Families new claim and a Claim for Carer Allowance received for the same customer, or claims for more than one customer.

  • If scanning onsite, go to Step 6
  • If contingency processing is invoked and the document is being sent to another location for scanning, go to Step 9

6

Scanning onsite + Read more ...

If the documents are:

  • photographic documents and identify information such as driver’s licence and passports, go to Step 7
  • very large or relate to more than one piece of work, go to Step 8
  • a mix of medical and non-medical documents, use separators between the medical and non-medical documents. For example, a claim for Disability Support Pension and supporting medical evidence. See Scanning Centrelink medical/sensitive documents using an MFD. Procedure ends here

7

Photographic documents + Read more ...

Photocopy the document before scanning, select Copy > Original Type option (bottom of the screen) > More option (pop up) > Photo & Text box and Photograph option in the photo type box > OK.

Adjust the darkness to suit by using the Lighten/Darken option if needed. For documents containing lots of blocks of colour it does help to lighten it up to the +3 option for best results. Either the Colour or black and white copy option can be selected when copying. Note: the digital image will not be saved in colour.

Photocopy the document.

8

Very large documents, documents with lots of contrast, or multiple documents + Read more ...

Very large documents (100 pages or more), or documents with photos, graphics or similar content can result in a file too large to open. As a rule, keep image file sizes below 10mb.

Note: Documents larger than 5mb cannot be uploaded via Doc Tools in Customer First. See Attaching electronic documents to a Centrelink customer’s record using Document Tools in Customer First.

Insert a Patch T separator at logical point(s) when scanning documents that may result in a file size too large to open:

  • very large documents. For example, 100 pages or more
  • documents containing photos, contrast (background colour) graphics or other content

See the Resources page for Patch T separator.

The separator will split the document into smaller digital images when attached to the customer's record. If not split, a rescan request may be required if the image cannot be opened. This results in extra work for the service centre and delays for the customer.

If a customer lodges multiple documents related to more than one piece of work, place separators between documents to go to different teams.

  • For example, a Families new claim and a Claim for Carer Allowance for the same customer. Place a separator between these 2 claims. Workload Management will create and allocate the appropriate work items
  • If Module PC - Private Company and Module PT - Private Trust forms are lodged, always scan them separately. They must be attached to a company record for processing in relation to a number of customers

Note: when separating a document, write the CRN on the top of both sections before scanning.

9

Preparing documents to send offsite for contingency processing + Read more ...

If scanning goes offline, notify the Site Manager or appropriate site officer. Follow escalation channels advised by the zone.

The following is only necessary when contingency arrangements have been invoked at a national level and a decision is made to send documents offsite for scanning.

  • Ensure the date of receipt is clearly marked on the documents
  • Ensure the barcode is readable. For example, not covered with a date stamp
  • Record details of the document received on the customer record
  • Multiple customer documents can be sent in one envelope, but clearly separate them
  • Do not send original documents to another location. Copy and certify them before sending. Return originals to the customer at the time of lodgement
  • Do not scan identity documents. Record them using Coding identity documents
  • Send documents for scanning at another location at the required frequency, based on the contingency arrangements at the time
  • Sites must retain documents that cannot be scanned until the Site Manager or appropriate site officer advises the required action

Scanning documents

Item

Description

Scanning documents for child records + Read more ...

When scanning documents relating to a child:

  • scan the document using the carer's Customer Reference Number (CRN)
  • the image attaches to the principal carer's record

2

Scanning documents for Organisation (ORG) records + Read more ...

The image attaches to the ORG Customer Reference Number (CRN).

3

Documents for an online claim the customer is in the process of submitting + Read more ...

If the customer cannot use the Upload documents service and is lodging documents to support a claim they are in the process of submitting online:

  • run the Request and Manage Customer Tasks guided procedure to record receipt of the document. For help, see Circumstance Change Monitor (CCM)
  • select Scan to Store when scanning the documents
  • remind the customer of the importance of completing all required tasks to allow them to submit their claim online

4

Documents for a pending or rejected claim + Read more ...

If the customer indicates they are lodging documents to support a claim already lodged, a claim that has been rejected, or for any other reason:

  • select Scan to Store and scan the documents
  • if all or partial documents have been provided and the claim has been:
    • lodged - follow Progress of claim
    • rejected – follow Request to reassess a rejected claim

5

Documents relating to a review or request for further information or review + Read more ...

Process Direct

Once the customer is in context select the Transaction tile to check if there is an outstanding transaction awaiting the document being provided.

For Online Updates - For Customer Action

  • select the transaction via the work item ID
  • the workflow will navigate to the task, use the function to update the Status to Accepted
  • if all requested documents have been provided, and all relevant actions are taken on the customer's record, close off the work item
  • using the MFD, scan the document/s ensuring that Scan to Store is selected. This will scan to the customer's CRN but not create additional unnecessary work items

For Request for Information - Standalone

  • select the transaction using the work item ID
  • update the Status of the Requested Documents using the Action edit
  • using the MFD, scan the document/s ensuring that Scan to Store is selected

This ensures the customer's payment is not suspended after providing required documents, but before the digital image is available for processing.

Customer First

Check for an outstanding request for documents on the Customer Details screen by selecting the CRN/BP hyperlink once the customer is in context. Outstanding requests for information will appear in the Activities tab.

  • Select the relevant ID hyperlink to view the request details
  • Select Edit in the header to amend the status
  • Use the drop down to change the Activity Status: In Process and Reason: Form Returned - Awaiting Assessment and save
  • Once complete, use the MFD to scan the document/s ensuring that Scan to Store is selected

Check if there is an outstanding activity requesting these documents on the Future Activity List (FAL) screen.

  • Select the manual review by highlighting the desired activity and choosing the To SELECT future activity for the update option from within the Selection Options list and select Continue
  • Scan the documents
  • Select Scan to Store when scanning the document

This ensures the customer's payment is not suspended after providing required documents, but before the digital image is available for processing.

6

Scanning on the MFD + Read more ...

On the Web Applications screen:

  • Touch the Scanning - Centrelink option
  • Touch Open

On the IGA screen, use these fields to input document details:

  • Change Date Received: check the receipt date. If the date is to be changed, enter the new date
  • Enter UserID: touch the field to enter UserID
  • Customer CRN: touch the field to enter the customer's CRN
    • For barcoded documents, the first page scanned must display the barcode
    • For documents with no barcode, the first page scanned must be the reclassification page. The reclassification page has to be double sided, with the patch T separator at the back
    • Reclassification pages and Patch T separators should be printed and not photocopied
  • Scan to Store: touch the field to select (if appropriate)
    This will attach the document to the customer record without creating a work item for action. This should be used if the documents are being processed at first point of contact

Press the Scan button.

Note: access to Web Applications and the IGA screen display may differ slightly, depending on the series of MFD in use.

The Resources page links to the MFD user guide, reclassification and separator pages.

7

Finalising the scan on the MFD + Read more ...

When scanning is finished, press the All Services button to clear the screen.

The digital image can be viewed soon after it has attached to the customer record if automatic classification occurred.

If a document needs manual classification to attribute a valid CRN and/or a form code, there will be a delay attaching the image to the customer record.

See the Resources page for an intranet link to classification timeframes.

For more information, see Viewing Centrelink customers' digital images.

8

Storing scanned documents + Read more ...

If the customer would like the document returned, make a photocopy before returning it to the customer. Store the photocopy with the scan batch.

Documents that are scanned are stored onsite for a set period and then destroyed.

See Storing scanned documents for timeframes and processes.
