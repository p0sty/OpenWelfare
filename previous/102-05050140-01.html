This document outlines how the Child Override/Claim (CHOC) screen is used to insert, correct or delete details for a child to correctly assess a customer's entitlement to payments and services in relation to the child.

Recording information on the CHOC screen

Details may need to be recorded on the CHOC screen for each child to ensure that:

  • the child is included in the assessment of the customer's entitlement for the payment. This can affect:
    • qualification for payment, for example:
      Parenting Payment Partnered (PPP) customers must have CHOC coded with a CLM line for at least one PP child in their care to qualify for payment
      Parenting Payment Single (PPS) customers who are no longer the principal carer of a child need to have the CHOC screen coded to ensure PPS is cancelled or assessed correctly
    • rate of payment, for example: family assistance payments, some social security benefits can be paid at a higher 'with child' rate
  • the customer's principal carer status is assessed correctly. This can affect qualification for Parenting Payment (PP), the rate of payment for JobSeeker Payment (JSP), Youth Allowance (YA) and Special Benefit (SpB), as well as mutual obligation requirements
  • the customer's additional income free area will be correct for single pensioners (excluding single PP customers)
  • the child will appear on the customer's Health Care Card (HCC) or Pensioner Concession Card (PCC)

Recording claim line (CLM) on the CHOC screen will not necessarily mean that the customer will be eligible for the child. This depends on other child circumstances recorded, including shared care, age of child, and student details.

A claim line (CLM) must not be coded for pension payments (for example PPS).

The CHOC screen may also be used to override child eligibility.
