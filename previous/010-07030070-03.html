This page contains general information on scholarship assessment and how amounts received are coded on the system for Centrelink income support payments only, not for Family Tax Benefit (FTB)/Child Care Subsidy (CCS). Approval for the exemption from the income test for social security payments is obtained by the appropriate Services Australia Helpdesk, through the Department of Social Services (DSS) Means Test Policy Section.

Customer declares they have been granted a scholarship + Read more ...

Full details of the scholarship (bursary, stipend or grant) must be supplied by the customer. Details can be accepted verbally if they are available. The Resources page contains additional information that may assist in identifying the type of scholarship.

Full details of scholarship required

The following information is required for coding:

  • official title of the scholarship
  • date of grant of the scholarship
  • amounts and frequency of payments
  • period of scholarship (full year, 6 months etc.), including date of cessation, if applicable

The scholarship information should be available from a document such as a grant letter or a contract.

Note: verification is not required unless the customer is unsure about these details.

If insufficient details are provided by the customer, tell the customer to supply information within 14 days.

Verbally request scholarship details from the customer and record details in an open DOC with a hold date of 14 days to provide the information.

If not having immediate contact with the customer at a review, request the scholarship information. See Requesting information (CLK).

9

Code the EYSB screen + Read more ...

Record the amount a school or scholarship provider contributes towards a scholarship in the Scholarship Contribution Amount field on the EYSB screen. This amount is normally a reduction or waiver in board and/or tuition fees.

The scholarship component waiving, exempting or reducing costs is not regarded as income for ABSTUDY purposes. Record details of scholarship on a Note/DOC.

Boarding school has not specified percentage of fees covered

If a boarding school has not specified what amount of the total scholarship is attributed to a reduction in boarding fees and/or tuition fees, the boarding school must be contacted via phone and the information requested verbally. Where a boarding school advises that they are unable to provide the percentage of fees covered, send an email outlining the situation to the Program Management Students mailbox. The Level 3 helpdesk will liaise with the school to determine coding requirements. Record details on a DOC.

Scholarship covers a portion of all fees

Ensure the amounts keyed on the Annual Boarding School/Hostel Amount field and the Annual Fees/Tuition Amount field on the EYSB screen and/or Net Rent Amount field on the Accommodation History (ACS) screen is the net amount of the school charges after the scholarship has been deducted.

For example, if the school charge is normally $10,000 per annum for board and a scholarship contributes $4,000 per annum to the boarding fees, the correct amount to key on the Annual Boarding School/Hostel Amount field is the net amount of $6,000.

This ensures that the correct amount is paid to the boarding school or hostel, and any excess Living Allowance is paid back to parent/guardian/student.

Scholarship covers 100% of all fees

Where a boarding school pays 100% of a student's boarding and tuition costs, information must still be recorded on the EYSB screen to include the student in the family pool calculation and the payment on advice process.

The EYSB screen will need to be completed with the following information:

  • Annual Fees/Tuition Amount to be recorded as $0.01
  • Boarding School/Hostel Action as 'ASC' (Accepted to board at a boarding school)
  • Annual Boarding School/Hostel Amount as $0.01
  • Scholarship Contribution Amount as the total amount of the scholarship contribution (100% of board and tuition charges) from the school, or from the scholarship provider organisation for Third Party Indigenous Scholarship or a Transition School Scholarship

Event Date and Actual Term Start Date fields are to be completed as per the customer's circumstances.

Record details of the coding of EYSB screen on a Note/DOC.

If there is a component of the scholarship paid for a purpose other than reducing/waving/exempting fees or boarding/lodging, that component is assessable as income. To key the income, see Step 4 in Table 2. Otherwise procedure ends

10

ABSTUDY boarder in receipt of a 'gap' scholarship + Read more ...

There are 8 'gap' scholarship providers:

  • Rosemary Bishop Indigenous Education Scholarship (RBIES) delivered by Yalari organisation
  • Indigenous Youth Leadership Program (IYLP) delivered through the Young Australians organisation
  • Australian Indigenous Education Foundation (AIEF) Scholarship Program
  • Cape York Institute's Academic Leaders - Secondary (ALS) Scholarship
  • Kimberley Education Excellence Program (KEEP) Scholarship
  • Aboriginal Advancement League's First Nations Scholarship (Lady Gladys Nicholls Hostel)
  • Transitional School Scholarship (Melbourne Indigenous Transition School)
  • Woomera Education Scholarship Trust (WEST)

Centrelink has received written confirmation that these scholarships only constitute an amount up to the gap between the school's board/tuition charge and the amount ABSTUDY contributes. For example, if a school charges $10,000 for board and $10,000 for tuition and ABSTUDY contributes $9,000 towards board and $8,500 towards tuition, the scholarship will constitute an amount up to the gap of $1,000 board and $1,500 tuition.

Where the ABSTUDY contribution is at least equal to the school's board and/or tuition charges, the scholarship will constitute non-board/tuition assistance such as enrolment fees, books, excursions, camps etc.

Key the EYSB and ACS screens with the actual full school board and tuition charge in the knowledge that the scholarship will not constitute more than the gap.

Note: students who receive an Aboriginal Advancement League's First Nations Scholarship, reside at the Lady Gladys Nicholls Hostel, and attend Ivanhoe Grammar School are not charged tuition fees. The tuition charge on the EYSB screen should be coded as $0.01.

There is no need to ask for individual scholarship amounts for these scholarships. Do not key the Scholarship Contribution Amount on EYSB screen for any of these scholarships.

Record details clearly on a DOC.

2

Scholarship paid directly to student + Read more ...

Is the scholarship paid directly to the student?

For those students in receipt of ABSTUDY, amounts paid directly to the student are not considered income if they are:

  • reimbursement of specified 'out of pocket' expenses such as photocopying, postage, printing and similar expenses;
  • a scholarship that was awarded outside Australia and is not intended to be used wholly or partly to assist recipients to meet living expenses;
  • scholarships that are paid as one-off sums in the nature of a reward or prize

If money has been paid to an ABSTUDY student to pay for accommodation, this is counted as income, with the exception of money paid through an Indigenous Commonwealth Accommodation Scholarship or equivalent Commonwealth scholarship or grant.

Students and Australian Apprentices are ineligible for ABSTUDY assistance if they get another form of Australian Government income support to study or undertake an Australian Apprenticeship. See Not qualified for ABSTUDY if receiving other assistance to study or as an Australian Apprentice for more information.

  • Yes, the scholarship is treated as income for all Centrelink income support payments and ABSTUDY. Go to Step 4
  • No, go to Step 3

3

Scholarship paid directly to a third party + Read more ...

If the scholarship is paid directly to a third party, it is regarded as 'valuable consideration' and is treated as income for all Centrelink income support payments.

Note: this form of scholarship is not treated as income for ABSTUDY.

For ABSTUDY customers, record details of the scholarship on a DOC. Procedure ends here.

  • Description: Key the name of the scholarship
  • Event Date: Key the first day of the assessable period
  • Amount: Key the amount of the scholarship
  • Freq: Select the relevant frequency of the payments so the system auto-deducts the exempt portion from the total and applies the income test to the remaining amount. For other frequencies, for example, once per trimester, annualise the amount and code as ANN

Note: when an Equity or Merit based Scholarship is coded as a fortnightly or periodic payment, the exempt income amount is spread out over the calendar year. The exempt income is divided by 26 fortnights and this is deducted from the Equity or Merit based Scholarship income coded on the Other Income Summary (OINS) screen. The relevant income test is then applied to any remaining amounts each fortnight or period. See Personal/Partner Income Tests for students, trainees or Australian Apprentices.

For a customer to receive the total benefit of the exempt income amount:

  • they must be on payments for the whole calendar year, and
  • the Equity or Merit based Scholarship income must be affecting for the whole calendar year

If both of the above criteria are not met, the customer is only entitled to the equivalent proportion of the exempt income over the period that the Equity or Merit based Scholarship is coded, or the period that they are in receipt of the student payment. The Resources page contains an exempt income reduction example.

7

Review date for scholarship + Read more ...

If coded in Process Direct:

  • within the workflow, the review is created automatically. This can be checked on the Reviews screen
  • in a new claim, the review will need to be recorded separately

If coded in Customer First, set a review for the appropriate date to cease scholarship assessment.

It is important to code a review to remove recorded scholarship income:

  • Annual payments should be reviewed 12 months after their receipt
  • Periodic payments should be set for review when they are expected to cease

In Customer First, navigate to the Review Registration (RVR) screen and press [Enter] or select the Continue button:

  • Service Reason, key the customer's payment type
  • Review Reason, Review of continuing Income (CIN). Note: for ABSTUDY use reason Manual review (REV)
  • Due Date, update with the maturity date for the review
  • Source, internal
  • Date of Receipt, date scholarship is coded on the record
  • Notes, 'Customer has received a lump sum or non-periodic scholarship. Check with customer if scholarship is continuing, if amount has changed or has ceased. See Operational Blueprint 010-07030070 for instructions'
  • Keywords, SCHEMS
  • Transfer to Region, customer's region

The Review will mature on the date coded in the RVR activity and will allocate via Workload Management for manual action.

Note: customers who claim Youth Allowance (YA), Austudy or ABSTUDY student or apprenticeship payments online and advise they receive an Equity or Merit based Scholarship in their online claim with an annual frequency will automatically have a review set to mature on the appropriate future date. This review will be allocated to a Service Officer to check if the scholarship income coded on the customer file needs to be end dated.

Record all information regarding customer contact and scholarship on a Note/DOC.

To action the matured review, go to Step 8, otherwise procedure ends here.
