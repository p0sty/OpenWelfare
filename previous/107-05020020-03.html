Arranging withholdings to recover a debt

Table 1

Step

Action

1

View the debt + Read more ...

Process Direct

  • In Customer Summary, select the Debt icon and then launch Debt Management
  • Select the Debt Case ID from the Debt List

Customer First and Customer Record

  • Check the (OPKD) screen if unsure of the due date
  • if the customer has multiple debts, the debt ID must be 'S'elected for the relevant debt from the Debt List (OPDL) screen before going to the OPKD screen

Note: check if a temporary write off is in place for the customer's debt(s). If a reassessment, explanation or review is in progress, offer an extension of the temporary pause on recovery when speaking with the customer. See Recovery of debts pending a reassessment, explanation or review of decision.

2

Debt Repayment process + Read more ...

  • Debt staff - use the Debt recovery script when actioning a debt
  • Non Debt staff - use the Debt Repayment guided procedure when actioning a debt

Note: The manual process is only to be used if the script / guided procedure is unavailable, for manual process steps, see Table 3.

Use the guided procedure to remove any warnings for default repayment options. Update the payment option to Withholdings if needed.

Is the customer in receipt of any Centrelink payments?

  • Yes, go to Step 3
  • No:
    • Debt staff, see Arranging cash repayments to recover debts
    • Non-Debt staff, see Step 9 in Table 3

For recovery of Child Care Subsidy (CCS) debts see Recovering Child Care Subsidy (CCS) debts in Process Direct.

3

Check if the payment is an arrears or Pension Bonus payment + Read more ...

Arrears can be withheld if the debt is:

  • over 28 days old, and
  • due and payable

Code the withholdings for up to 100% of arrears of a:

  • social security payment
  • family assistance, or
  • pension bonus payment

The withholding rate should not leave the customer in severe financial hardship.

To code the payment:

  • Complete the coding before the customer's Entitlement Period End Day. The code is found on the Entitlement Delivery (RAD) screen
  • 'S'elect the arrears payment to be withheld, from the Payment Summary (PS) screen
  • The arrears payment to be withheld must be a future payment and display as 'Subject to alteration' on the Payment Delivery (PD) screen

Family Tax Benefit (FTB) and Parental Leave Pay (PPL) arrears payments may be automatically offset against outstanding debts.

Are withholdings to be coded from arrears or pension bonus payment?

  • Yes, see Step 8 in Table 3
  • No, go to Step 4

4

Check for an existing payment arrangement + Read more ...

Go to the Debt List (OPDL) screen.

Is 'no active arrangements' displayed in the top left of the OPDL screen?

  • Yes, go to Step 5
  • No, the customer has an existing arrangement. Go to Step 6

5

Check the status of the debt + Read more ...

Note: Check the Write-off screen (OPWO) to confirm if a DIS write off has been applied. If a DIS write off has been applied, go to Step 11.

If the debt status on the OPDL screen is:

  • D - Determined, there is no current payment arrangement in place. Go to Step 7
  • D - Pend Rcvr Actn, or F - Written Off
    • Non Debt staff, go to Step 9 in Table 3
    • Debt staff, investigate the write off and decide if recovery should continue
    • Otherwise, go to Step 7

6

Existing payment arrangement + Read more ...

Tell the customer the arrangements for repaying their existing debt will continue towards repayment of all debts.

Does the customer agree with this arrangement?

  • Yes,
    • If the customer contacts after an arrangement end date review, the expectation is they will pay a higher rate. Go to Step 9
    • Otherwise, record the details on a DOC. Procedure ends here
  • No, and the customer:
    • wants to pay more, code the withholdings at the rate the customer prefers - see Step 5 in Table 3
    • wants to pay less, go to Step 9

7

Payment in full + Read more ...

If the customer has the capacity to pay the debt in full but asks for withholdings, request payment in full.

Will the customer pay the debt in full?

  • Yes:
    • Non Debt staff, see Step 9 in Table 3
    • Debt staff, see Arranging cash repayments to recover debts
  • No:
    • Non Debt staff, see Step 9 in Table 3
    • Debt staff, go to Step 8

8

Review Income and Investments + Read more ...

Refer debts over $10,000 to the Specialised Recovery Team. Include:

  • the details of the case, and
  • any negotiations with the customer

Check the customer's Centrelink record to see if they have income or investments that could:

  • finalise the debt
  • pay a partial lump sum payment, or
  • pay more than the standard withholding rate

If:

  • Yes, pay debt with partial lump sum or high withholding rate, go to Step 9
  • No, see Step 1 in Table 2

9

Partial lump sum payment + Read more ...

Will the customer pay a partial lump sum payment?

  • Yes:
    • Arrange a cash repayment
    • Negotiate payment of amount left, see Step 1 in Table 2
  • No, Go to Step 10

10

Consider pursuing further recovery action + Read more ...

See:

  • Garnishees
  • Civil procedures to recover Centrelink debts

Before pursuing further action, an APS 5/6 Officer in Compliance and Debt Operations must decide on:

  • garnishee, or
  • other legal recovery

Otherwise, see Step 1 in Table 2.

11

Customer with a DIS (disaster) write-off wants to set up a debt repayment arrangement + Read more ...

Customers may have a DIS write-off applied to their debt in certain circumstances. For example, where they have received a disaster payment.

Explain to the customer they do not need to set up a payment arrangement. Services Australia will contact at a later date to arrange repayment.

Does the customer want to set up a payment arrangement now?

  • Yes, see Step 1 in Table 2
  • No, procedure ends here

Negotiation arrangement

Table 2

Step

Action

1

Withholding start date + Read more ...

Tell the customer the withholding start date. The start date will be:

  • the next available payday, or
  • the first available payday after the due date, whichever is later

Does the customer want a later start date?

  • Yes,
    • Debt Staff - consider a delay of the withholding start date
    • Non Debt Staff - see Step 9 in Table 3
  • No, go to Step 2

2

Discuss Standard withholding rate + Read more ...

Advise the customer of the standard withholding rate that applies.

Standard withholdings automatically start after the due date if:

  • their only debt has not yet reached its due date, or
  • they have not received their first Centrelink payment, and
  • they have not made their first payment

If the customer is satisfied with the standard withholding rate, and:

  • there is no active arrangement in place, standard withholdings apply. See Step 5 in Table 3
  • requests to change an existing rate to standard, see Step 5 in Table 3

If the customer wants to pay:

  • more than the standard withholding rate, discuss how much they wish to pay. See Step 2 in Table 3
  • less than the standard withholding rate, go to Step 3

3

Ask the customer how much they can afford to pay + Read more ...

Check the income and assets on the customer record.

Does the customer's offer meet the minimal acceptable rates? See Withholdings.

  • Yes, accept the offer. See, Step 2 in Table 3
  • No, Go to Step 4

4

Customer's offer is less than the acceptable withholding level + Read more ...

Tell the customer the minimum payment for their debt is the rate '$xxx' (the acceptable withholding level).

Do not advise the customer this is the 'acceptable withholding level'.

See Standard withholdings for debt recovery.

Ask the customer if they can afford this amount.

  • Yes, accept the offer. Tell the customer a review of the arrangement may occur in the future. See Step 1 in Table 3
  • No:
    • Debt staff, go to Step 5
    • Non Debt staff, see Step 9 in Table 3

5

Debt recovery calculator + Read more ...

Use the financial information calculator to assess how much a customer can afford to repay their debt. The Resources page has a link.

If they do not want to give the details over the phone:

  • Send a Financial Information (SS245) form
  • Assess and record the minimum acceptable amount until the customer submits the form
  • This advice should be in the form - 'The lowest amount I can accept until I know your circumstances is $x'

See Step 4 in Table 3.

Manually code and record an arrangement

Table 3

Step

Action

1

Actioning an arrangement + Read more ...

The following provides the steps to manually code and record an arrangement. Use the manual process if the Debt recovery script or Debt Repayment guided procedure us unavailable.

  • Ending a withholding, go to Step 2
  • Coding a withholding arrangement, go to Step 5
  • End dated arrangement, go to Step 6
  • Extend a withholding arrangement, go to Step 7

2

Coding the withholdings + Read more ...

Go to the Withholding Instruction (OPWH) screen.

Is there a current withholding arrangement in place?

  • Yes, key 'T' in the Cease Date: field and press [Enter]. Go to Step 3
  • No, go to Step 4

3

Check of ABSTUDY debts + Read more ...

Does the customer want to vary withholdings on an ABSTUDY debt of more than $1500?

  • Yes:
    • Debt staff, Go to Step 4
    • Non Debt staff, refer to Compliance and Debt Operations
  • No, Go to Step 4

4

DIS Write Off + Read more ...

If a DIS write-off has been applied, to set up a payment arrangement in:

  • Customer Record:
    • Run the Debt recovery script
  • Customer First:
    • Go to the Write-off (OPWO) screen
    • Note the DIS write-off end date and comment
    • Remove the DIS write off
    • Run the Debt repayment guided procedure
    • Re-apply the DIS write-off end date and comment on the OPWO screen
    • Record details of this action on a DOC

Note: when the write-off code is removed as detailed above, re-apply the write-off code on the same day. Where this does not happen, the customer receives a letter warning them that they have an outstanding debt and to enter into a payment arrangement

For Child Care Subsidy (CCS) debts, see Child Care Subsidy (CCS) debts

If a DIS write off has not been applied, go to Step 5.

5

Coding withholding arrangements + Read more ...

See the Resources page for payment types where withholdings can apply.

  • On the Activity List (AL) screen:
    • 'S'elect the DMN/RCV No Arrangement activity. Press [Enter]
    • If there is no DMN/RCV activity, key 'OPAL' in the Nxt: field. Press [Enter]
  • In the Create new arrangement type field, key 'WHS'. Press [Enter]
    • to amend the standard amount, key 'AWH'. Press [Enter]. This triggers automatic withholdings. Record a DOC
  • On the OPWH screen, code these fields:
    • Withholding Type
    • Non Standard Amount $
    • Non Standard % (Complete only if a customer asks for a percentage rate. If a customer requests 100%, the system will pay the customer a minimum of $1)
    • Withholding Debt ID if necessary, for example, for third party withholdings
  • An entitlement letter will issue. This shows the withholding amount
  • Go to the Assessment Finalisation (OPAF) screen
    • Key 'Y' in the Finished? field. Key 'Q' if it needs to be quality checked.
    • Press [Enter]
  • Record the details on a DOC

A non-standard withholding coded after the customer's Entitlement Period end day (see the RAD screen), will commence from the next payment. Select the payment from the Payment Summary (PS) screen. The Payment Delivery (PD) screen will display 'THIS IS THE NEXT PAYMENT AND CANNOT BE ALTERED'.

6

End dated Arrangements + Read more ...

Is there an end date for the non-standard withholding arrangement on the OPWH screen?

  • Yes, the system will review the withholdings 21 days before the end date
    • Tell the customer they will get a letter if their rate changes
    • Debt Staff may extend the end date up to 12 months. Go to Step 7
    • Otherwise, process ends here
  • No:
    • Non Debt staff, go to Step 9
    • Debt staff, this arrangement will not be automatically reviewed. Note: if using the debt recovery script it may ask if a Q246 needs to be sent. Select 'No'
    • Procedure ends here.
  • When recording information in a DOC, include that the customer has been advised:
    • how long the arrangement will last
    • that it may then be reviewed
    • details of any recovery action warnings
  • Adding or amending a withholding rate is not a significant change. Only include the Act reference in the DOC if the customer:
    • is likely to disagree with the rate review, or
    • asked for the rate review

7

Extending a withholding arrangement + Read more ...

Go to the Withholding Instruction (OPWH) screen.

Code these fields:

  • New Arrangement Period - This field defaults at 3 months. APS5 or higher staff can change the value to up to 12 months
  • Comments (if necessary)
  • Press [Enter]
  • New Arrangement End Date will show
  • Confirm Extension Y/N:
    • Key 'Y' to accept extension
    • Key 'N' to keep original end date

The system will review the withholding 21 days before the end date. It will send a letter if the withholding rate changes after the review.

Procedure ends here.

8

Coding withholdings of arrears or pension bonus payments + Read more ...

  • On the Activity List (AL) screen:
    • 'S'elect the DMN/RCV No Arrangement activity. If there is no DMN/RCV activity, key 'OPAL' in the Nxt field
    • press [Enter]
  • In the Create new arrangement type field, key 'WHS' and press [Enter]
  • On the OPWH screen, complete these fields:
    • Withholding Type
    • Non Standard Arrears % (key the percentage to be withheld)
    • Comments (if needed)
  • Go to the Assessment Finalisation (OPAF) screen
  • In the Finished? field, key 'Y'. Also key 'Q' if the activity is to be Quality on line (QOL) checked
  • Press [Enter] to finalise
  • Record the details on a DOC

9

Refer customer to Compliance and Debt Operations (CDO) + Read more ...

Non Debt staff refer the customer to CDO when:

  • The Debt Repayment guided procedure is not working
  • The customer is not in receipt of any Centrelink payments
  • The status of the customer's debt is D - Pend Rcvr Actn, or F - Written Off
  • The customer has the capacity to pay their debt in full (whether they agree to pay in full or not)
  • The customer wants to delay the start date of withholdings
  • The customer requests a refund on their debt payment
  • Do not tell the customer that a refund is possible

If any of the above apply:

  • Record details of the call on a DOC, and cold transfer to the customer to CDO, or
  • If the call cannot be transferred go to Step 11

10

Document the details of the call + Read more ...

Create a DOC and complete these fields:

  • ServRsn: key 'DMN'
  • Enquiry Type: key 'REC'
  • Who: key 'CUS'
  • Source: key 'PHO'
  • Channel Type: key 'PHO'
  • Ext Detail: key 'RECOVERY: CUS PHO RE ACCT' and the date of event (DOV) if any details other than a change in withholding rate were altered
  • Txt: record:
    • Customer Reference Number (CRN) balance
    • any changes to the customer's contact details
    • other relevant call details from the call, see significant decision
  • Key 'Y' in the Complete Act?: field to close the DOC

For more details about DOC standards, see Online Document Recording (ODR).

11

Record callback details + Read more ...

Create a Callback / ACTDOC and complete the following fields:

  • ServRsn: key 'DMN'
  • Enquiry Type: key 'REC'
  • Who: key 'CUS'
  • Source: key 'PHO'
  • Channel Type: key 'PHO'
  • Tfr To: key the Debt Recovery Region code
  • Keywords: key 'CALLDRU'
  • Ext Detail: key 'RECOVERY: CALL CUS RE ACCT'
  • Txt: record:
    • CRN balance
    • changes to the customer's contact details
    • other relevant details
    • that the customer requires a phone call and the time and day arranged to discuss their repayment
  • Press [Enter]
