This document outlines when and how CP can be cancelled when a carer no longer qualifies for CP.

CA auto

When a carer's CP (child) is cancelled, the related CA (auto) payment also cancels.

Check if the carer:

  • is still eligible for CA (child). For example, the carer is still caring:
    • but the CP (child) is cancelled due to income, or
    • for the child care receiver, but is working above 25 hours permanently
  • wants to test their eligibility for CA once their CP (child) and CA (auto) has cancelled. Tell the carer they:
    • need to re-claim CA, and
    • if they apply for CA within 28 days of cancellation, their payment may be backdated to the date CP was cancelled
  • is entitled to any other financial assistance

Cancellation of CP

Cancellation or suspension of CP can only occur under the following reasons in the Social Security Administration Act 1999 (SSAA):

  • section 80 - loss of qualification
  • section 81 - failure to comply with certain notices, or
  • section 82 - failure to take action to obtain a foreign payment

The decision to cancel CP depends on whether the loss of payment will be temporary or not. Document all cancellation decisions with the reason and the section of the Act.

Auto-cancellation of CP

CP can be automatically or manually cancelled when a carer is no longer entitled as a result of:

  • changed circumstances
  • changed circumstances of the caring relationship, or
  • failing to comply with the requirements for payment

If a carer, care receiver, nominee or third party notifies a change in circumstances that may result in the cancellation of CP, do not cancel CP unless it is clear that the carer no longer qualifies for CP. If not, send an ACTDOC to CD1 for further investigation. The Process page explains how to do this.

For example, the carer is not providing adequate daily care as the carer is working full time. Do not suspend or cancel CP unless there is clear evidence supporting the statement. For example, the carer notifies they are working full time.

Follow the Person Permitted to Enquire (PPE) rules when discussing the information provided. Only release information to a PPE.

If the decision is to cancel CP, send a letter to the carer with the cancellation reasons and their review and appeal rights.

If the loss of entitlement to CP is established, code the change in the carer's circumstances on their record to reflect the current circumstances of the carer and the care receiver. This will allow the system to auto-cancel CP when there is no continuing entitlement. Consider whether this is the correct decision and advise the carer whether they qualify for another income support payment.

For example, when a care receiver permanently enters an institution, they leave the care of their carer. Carers continue to receive CP payment for 14 weeks from the first date the carer did not provide constant care. In most cases, it will be the day after the person enters the institution. CP will auto-cancel after the 14-weeks. This does not apply to Carer Payment (XWP).

Circumstances where the system will auto-cancel CP include:

  • death of the carer or death of the care receiver
  • care receiver permanently entering an institution
  • change in medical qualification
  • change in income/assets of the care receiver
  • change in income/assets of the carer
  • change in care or no longer caring
  • care receiver turning 16 years and 3 months of age. CP child will auto-cancel at 16 years 3 months, if the customer fails to submit the CTAT on or before the child turns 16 years, or if an adult assessment has already been finalised and they do not qualify under CP adult provisions
  • care receiver with a terminal illness turning 18 years of age. CP child will auto-cancel at 18 years, if the customer fails to submit the CTAT on or before the child turns 18 years or if an adult assessment has already been finalised and they do not qualify under CP adult provisions
  • failure by carer to report employment income, if required
  • imprisonment or psychiatric confinement of the carer. Note: respite may apply when a care receiver is imprisoned but not when a carer is imprisoned. If the carer is in gaol or psychiatric confinement for:
    • more than 13 weeks due to being charged with an offence, cancel CP
    • 13 weeks or less, suspend CP from the date of confinement. If the carer is still in gaol or psychiatric confinement after 13 weeks, the suspended CP will auto-cancel from the date of confinement

Manual cancellation of CP

Cancel CP if a Service Officer updates a carer and/or care receiverâ€™s record and the carer no longer qualifies for CP. If CP does not auto-cancel, manually cancel the CP. If a care receiver or other person, advises the Services Australia of a change in circumstances which would result in cancellation of CP, do not cancel CP unless the care receiver is able to provide evidence that the carer is no longer entitled to CP. The Service Officer must send an ACTDOC to CD1 for further investigation. See the Process page.

If the decision to cancel CP is made by the agency, the Service Officer must send a letter to the carer with the cancellation reasons and their review and appeal rights.

If a vulnerable and at risk carer remains without income support payment for a minimum of 6 weeks due to an automatic suspension or cancellation, the carer may be eligible for manual intervention.

Note: carers who are overseas permanently are not eligible for this initiative. Cancel any Manual Follow-ups generated for carers in this situation.

Wife Pension transfer to Carer Payment (XWP)

CP (XWP) is not subject to Temporary Cessation of Care (TCC) for Carer Payment, constant care, the 25 hour rule or care receiver assessments. Therefore, some cancellation reason codes cannot be used to cancel CP (XWP). These include 25H, CAB, CAS, CDE, CIH, CNP, CNQ, HBE, MCQ, NMQ, NRQ or RBE.

CP (XWP) is still subject to carer payability rules such as the pension's income and asset tests and portability. CA continues to be subject to all the normal rules. See Transfer from Wife Pension (WP) to Carer Payment.

Effect of other payments on CP

For children born or entering care:

  • on or after 1 October 2016, Paid Parental Leave (PPL) and Dad and Partner Pay (DAP) granted to the carer (and/or their partner) are treated as ordinary income for calculating the rate of payment for CP
  • before 1 October 2016, PPL and DAP are not treated as assessable income for CP

PPL or DAP alone is not enough income to reduce CP to nil rate. The carer would need to have other forms of income for their rate to be reduced to nil (and therefore cancelled). If CP cancels, it can be reinstated after the PPL or DAP period ends.

For Carer Payment (XWP) customers, CP will auto-cancel if their only or last payment level CA is suspended or cancelled for any reason, or changed to CA HCC only or nil rate due to Education, Training and Treatment (ETT). However, CP (XWP) will be auto-restored if payment level CA is restored or reinstated without a gap within 13 weeks. A Manual Follow-up (MFU) and work item will be produced to assess eligibility for CP (XWP) restoration manually, where payment level CA is restored or reinstated without a gap more than 13 weeks later. See Transfer from Wife Pension (WP) to Carer Payment.

Home Equity Access Scheme customers

CP customers who receive loan payments under the Home Equity Access Scheme are eligible to continue receiving their loan payments if CP suspends for certain reasons. In these circumstances, the CP status is set to Current Zero Rate (CZR) for reason PLS to enable the loan payments to continue being paid. During periods of CZR-PLS, the customer is not eligible to receive any other rate components until the CP suspension is lifted.

See Home Equity Access Scheme cancellations and rejections.

Suspension of CP

Before suspending CP, the Service Officer must be certain that the information and/or evidence is sufficient to prove the carer is temporarily not eligible for CP and that there is a legislative basis to suspend the payment, and all appropriate action has been taken.

Generally, only suspend payments if:

  • a determination has been made that the carer is not qualified, or the payment is not payable, temporarily or for a specific period as a result of changes in their circumstances, or
  • the carer has not complied with a request that was reasonable. The request may be to provide information held by the carer, to attend an interview, or to take action (for example, to obtain a foreign payment)

If a decision to suspend CP is determined, the Service Officer must advise the carer of the suspension reason and their review and appeal rights, to avoid placing them in financial hardship.

See Suspension of Carer Payment (CP).
