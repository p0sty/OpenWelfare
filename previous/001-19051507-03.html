A JSP new claim or reindexed claim cannot be granted unless the Participation Interview has been completed and a RapidConnect determination made.

The Participation Interview codes a RapidConnect status on the Job Seeker RapidConnect (JMRC) screen to determine the customer's start date of payments. See RapidConnect.

If error E543NM - 'Participation Workflow has not been completed' appears, the Participation Interview has not been completed successfully, see Table 3 in Participation Interview before continuing with claim processing.

Note: automation - once Service Officers complete the Participation Interview and 'FINISH' is selected, they will be exited from the claim transaction. The claim will immediately be streamed into automation, which creates a lock on the customer's record. Depending on system performance, this can take several minutes. If after 5 minutes, the record is no longer locked with the user ID 'Roxy' the claim will either be 'Done' (no further action required) or SO's can continue to assess the claim.

The claim status must be In Process before it can be processed.

Bank accounts and balances verification exemption + Read more ...

The online claim may request bank verification as a Required task. However, if the customer meets one of the below exemption criteria, it is not required. In these cases, the customer has made a legal declaration that the claim is correct. This is sufficient evidence unless there is doubt about the validity of the information provided in the claim.

The following customers are  exempt  from providing supporting documents confirming bank account balances:

  • been on income support payment in last 13 weeks and did not advise of any new bank accounts
  • have a partner who currently receives a payment with confirmed bank accounts
  • are Indigenous and living in a remote area
  • transitioning from Parenting Payment, Austudy or Youth Allowance (student) to JobSeeker Payment through a transition claim
  • if their combined bank balances from the Date Last Worked or studied plus 1, or the date of claim if they have not previously worked or studied, are:
    • below $2,000 for single people with no dependent children
    • below $5,000 for couples
    • below $5,000 for customers with one or more dependent children
  • self-served the Liquid Assets Waiting Period (LAWP)
  • served any part of a LAWP (including self served) in the last twelve months

Is the customer exempt from providing these documents?

  • Yes: go to Step 11
  • No: go to Step 10

Bank accounts and balances verification + Read more ...

Customers claiming JSP may need to supply proof of all bank accounts they hold, including balances as part of the claim.

If the customer has claimed online, do not request proof of bank accounts. The online claim will show this as a Required task.

Customers may need to provide:

  • evidence of all bank account balances of all accounts held in the customer and their partner's name. The evidence must cover the period from the Date Last Worked (DLW) plus 1 to the claim submission date
  • one of the following:
    • a bank statement for all accounts held in their or their partner's name
    • a copy of the online account information and transactions

If the customer has supplied bank verification that is sufficient to assess their liquid asset position, do not reject claims FSD for failure to provide bank/investment balances. An example is if they have evidence of bank balances provided from Date last worked (or studied) plus 1 and the claim submission date, but the transaction dates are not complete in between these dates.

Note: an ATM slip is not an acceptable supporting document.

If the customer is not subject to a Liquid Assets Waiting Period (LAWP), request new evidence if either of the following apply:

  • the date of the bank account balance is older than 14 business days from claim submission date
  • the date is earlier than their claim commencement date

See the Resources page for examples.

Bank account details may be pre-populated into an online claim. If the customer has not zeroed off the balance or advised the account is closed, Service Officers can accept verbal confirmation that the account is closed. See Documents required for Centrelink new claims.

NDC screen + Read more ...

The Date of Commencement (NDC) screen is pre-filled with assessment information relevant to the calculation of the customer's payment start date.

DLW/DCS/DPQ field

Details prefill for the Date Last Worked (DLW), Date Ceased Study (DCS) or Date Paid Prior to Qualification (DPQ). They may need updating.

Where evidence has been supplied, DLW/DCS/DPQ fields must be coded to reflect that evidence. Specific coding may be required where backdating provisions are to be applied.

LAWP field

A total amount will auto calculate from the SVS, SIS MIS and SVDI screens therefore no updates are required to this field. The only time a manual calculation is required is if the customer has LAWP within a Private Company or Private Trust.

Calculate the start date for payment

The system automatically calculates a start date for eligible claims. It is important to confirm this start date is correct because system calculations are not always correct.

For example, a customer could reclaim a payment after having:

  • a backdated cancellation of the payment, and
  • a debt raised for the period they were not eligible

The system will apply the backdating rule correctly and calculate the date of commencement as Date Paid To (DPT)+1, even if the date of the claim is within 14 days of DPT.

Check the cancellation date and, if it is within 14 days of the claim date, do not apply DPT+1. The start date is the date the customer reclaims payment.

If the system calculates the start date incorrectly and the correct date should be:

  • after the DPT+1, code the correct start date in the Manual Start Date field on NDC to override the system calculated start date
  • before the DPT+1 (which is very rare), check:
    • the customer is not being paid again for a period they were previously receiving payment
    • if the customer was cancelled off their previous payment and if a debt was raised for the period they were not eligible

If the date is being changed to a date before DPT+1, do not code it in the Manual Start Date field on NDC. The start date will overlap with the previous payment dates, which may:

  • prevent the claim from granting properly, or
  • lead to a data corruption on the record

To ensure the customer receives the correct payment:

  • pay the days the customer was entitled to prior to the DPT+1, as a one-off payment
  • code the DPT+1 date in the Manual Start Date field on NDC, and
  • record a DOC detailing the steps taken and the reason for the start date determination

Service Officer discretion is required when changing the start date from the one the system calculated.

Principal carers and shared care + Read more ...

A shared care assessment should be completed in all cases, even if only one person is claiming income support or if each person has another qualifying child solely in their care. The shared care assessment will determine the principal carer status which will determine a customer's entitlement and in some cases, will affect the rate and income test applied for ongoing entitlements for single principal carers.

Customers with shared care arrangements, but are not the principal carer may still be entitled to a 'with child' rate of payment for JSP, YA and Special Benefit (SpB).

If a change of care has occurred or a determination of care is required, see Notification and assessment of shared care arrangements for Family Tax Benefit (FTB).

If there is shared care involved and a determination of care has not been confirmed, the claim must be placed on hold.

A principal carer is a person with primary care of a dependent child under 16 years. A principal carer assessment must be made if one or both customers caring for a child are claiming income support as a principal carer.

A step parent may be a principal carer if they live as a member of a couple with the parent of the child.

Principal Carer status does not automatically end in all cases. If there are no children under the age of 16 in the customer's care check that a Principal Carer status is not current on the Principal Carer Circumstance Details (PCCD) screen.

If member of a couple with a dependent child in care:

  • over the age of 6 years
    • Only one member of a couple can be a principal carer at one time
    • If both claim or get a social security payment based on or affected by principal carer status, they must be advised in writing who the principal carer is
  • under the age of 6 years
    • Only one member of a couple can be a principal carer at one time
    • Continue to process the claim. Once the claim is finalised, see Child Override/Claim (CHOC) screen, Table 3 for coding of the PCCD and CHOC screens

Is the customer a principal carer or shared care assessment completed?

  • Yes, go to Step 13
  • No, go to Step 14

See Principal carer of a dependent child.

Follow up - PLDP + Read more ...

If you have identified the customer was granted PLDP within the last 12 months but their JobSeeker Payment claim does not include reported employment within the past 12 months OR employment income, OR a separation certificate in their claim, ensure you make contact to discuss this with the customer directly. Customers must have lost at least 8 hours or a full day's work to be eligible for PLDP.

Discuss with the customer the mismatch between their PLDP claim and the evidence provided on their JobSeeker Payment claim. You may cover the following points:

  • Have you worked in paid employment in the last 12 months? If so, this must be declared for this claim and you may be required to provide evidence, such as an Employment Separation Certificate
  • Customers must have lost at least 8 hours or a full day's work to be eligible for PLDP. Have you lost at least 8 hours or a full day's work?

If the customer indicates they have not undertaken paid employment in the last 12 months, the JobSeeker Payment claim can proceed, however you may note to the customer that this may identify payment of the PLDP as incorrect. If appropriate, you can initiate a review of the decision to pay PLDP.

  • If the customer indicates they may have claimed PLDP fraudulently, or intentionally misrepresented their circumstances, you can report potential fraud. It is important you do not refer to 'fraud' in your conversation with the customer
  • If the customer indicates they may have been incorrectly paid PLDP, but not through misrepresentation of their circumstances or fraud, you can note this on the customer's record, and flag for potential overpayment. See Table 5 in Processing Pandemic Leave Disaster Payment Claims in Process Direct for instructions

If a customer indicates they have undertaken paid employment in the last 12 months (and therefore PLDP was legitimate), but they have not declared work in their ISP claim, staff should inform the customer, evidence of this employment will be required to appropriately assess their ISP claim, see Requesting information (CLK).

Rejecting the customer's claim or requesting further evidence of employment/separation should only be undertaken once you have discussed the customer's circumstances with them and you have ensured they have correctly declared their information on both PLDP and their income support claim.

JSP Provisional claims + Read more ...

Customers may be eligible for JSP Provisional when they have an outstanding Disability (DSP) claim. Customers who meet this criteria are exempt from mutual obligation requirements for the period during which the DSP claim is being determined (13 weeks).

To determine if the customer is claiming JobSeeker Provisional and exempt from mutual obligations:

  • Select the Keyword icon. If the keyword JSPPROV is displayed the customer is applying for JobSeeker Provisional, or
  • Select the Review of claim. Under the Circumstance details, the circumstances will display Disability Support Pension - JobSeeker Payment confirming the customer is applying for JobSeeker Provisional

Code the DSP exemption:

  • Use Super Key to go to AEX screen
  • Add new row
  • In the Activity Type field, select DSP - Claiming DSP
  • Record the start and end dates of the exemption. The start date being the date the DSP Claim was lodged, noted from the Review of Claim or DSP PoC DOC and end date 13 weeks from that date.
