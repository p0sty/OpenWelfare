New or reindexed claim + Read more ...

A JSP new claim or reindexed claim cannot be assessed unless the Participation Interview has been completed and a RapidConnect determination made.

The Participation Interview codes a RapidConnect status on the Job Seeker RapidConnect (JMRC) screen to determine the customer's start date of payments. See RapidConnect.

To prevent incorrect claim assessment, there may be a need to update the customer's record outside the new claim, for:

  • current education or apprenticeship details displayed on the Customer Study Details (EDC) or Newstart Education Status (NES) screens, the student end date must be updated. Note: the update must be completed in the system of the study payment being received, i.e. ISS - PES. For customers who were receiving:
    • Pensioner Education Supplement (PES), see Cancellation of, or changes to qualifying payment for Pensioner Education Supplement (PES), ABSTUDY PES or Assistance for Isolated Children PES
    • Youth Allowance or Austudy as a student, see end date details for student payment
    • Youth Allowance or Austudy as an apprentice, see end date details for Apprenticeship payment
  • ABSTUDY customers:
    • full time ABSTUDY customers no longer studying or undertaking an Apprenticeship, the payment will need to be cancelled. See Payment of ABSTUDY when a student is deferring or discontinuing study before the end of the course
    • part-time ABSTUDY student continuing in the course, remains in receipt of ABSTUDY and will show as ABY CUR. This will not affect the JSP claim. These students qualify for allowances under the part-time award and do not receive fortnightly payments The ABSTUDY record will need to be checked and the Customer Study Details (EDC) screen may need updating to reflect the part-time course. See Changing study load from full-time to part-time or concessional study load for ABSTUDY or ABSTUDY Pensioner Education Supplement (PES)
  • a student course end date review activity that is started and not completed, a Future Dated Benefit Cancellation activity and JSP lodged claim displays:
    • complete the Future Dated Benefit Cancellation activity in Process Direct. Once completed the JSP claim status will change.
      Note: the Future Dated Benefit Cancellation (ZCOC activity) is identified by keywords STU2JSP and ZYASZJSP and must be completed prior to claim assessment
  • when a customer has a voluntary nominee and in their claim, they answer No to someone acting or receiving payment on their behalf staff must check and update nominee arrangements, see Cancelling a nominee arrangement

Pre-Claim vulnerable circumstance

For customers who have pre-claim vulnerable circumstances recorded within the 8 weeks prior to their intent to claim contact date, the system will automatically calculate the start date.

Pre-claim vulnerable circumstances that are not able to be automatically determined by the system, can be manually added:

  • on the Pre-Claim Vulnerable Circumstance (PRECLM) screen in Process Direct, or
  • via the Create Intent to Claim guided procedure in Customer First

Where the PRECLM displays with a start date that is more than 8 weeks and no end date consider if a pre-claim vulnerable circumstance still exists using the available information.

In these cases assess if the:

  • vulnerable circumstance is no longer active, consider end dating the vulnerable circumstance from the associated circumstance screen and regenerate the claim
  • pre claim circumstances are required to remain current on a customer's record indefinitely. This includes exemptions from seeking child support due to family and domestic violence. In these cases assessment for these circumstances is required:
    • do not end date the exemption
    • manually update the claim start date to the date of submission if required. For more information, see Step 5 in Table 1 of Intent to claim and vulnerable customers

After completing any updates, from the Transaction Summary (TS) screen, select > Regenerate claim.

Streamlined transfer claims + Read more ...

A streamlined claim is offered to some customers when they are losing qualification for their previous payment.

Eligible customers will be offered a 'task' on the home page of their online account, allowing them to advise that they wish to claim JSP. The task is variable according to the payment type. It is available from up to 28 days before qualification for their current payment ends, up until the date they lose qualification.

Streamlined transfer claims can be identified by the DOC on the Document List (DL) screen:

  • For Parenting Payment customers transferring to JSP:
    • DL will display a PP to JSP Transition Claim DOC
    • The DOC will contain the Claim ID
    • Advise of any required supporting documentation that is to be provided
    • This claim was auto-populated from existing verified customer details. This claim can be assessed without the need to review the details in the claim
  • For students transferring to JSP:
    • DL will display a STU to JSP Transition Claim DOC
    • The DOC will contain the Claim ID
    • Advise of any required supporting documentation that is to be provided
    • This claim was auto populated from existing verified customer details. This claim can be assessed without the need to review the details in the claim

As customers transfer from another payment, the Claim Summary will show as 'Claim Empty' as the information contained on their record is deemed current. No updates are required unless the customer indicates any changes in their streamline claim.

For more information, see Transfer to JobSeeker Payment (JSP) from another payment.

9

Qualification for JSP + Read more ...

When assessing qualification for JSP check the customer:

  • is of qualifying age for JSP
  • satisfies the Australian residence requirements
  • is not in receipt of any other social security benefit, social security pension or a Service Pension, Income Support Payment (ISP) or Veteran Payment
  • not receiving ABSTUDY Living Allowance
  • is unemployed, or:
    • unable to look for work due to a medical condition, illness or injury, or
    • employed or studying full time and are unable to undertake these due to a medical condition, illness or injury and have a job or study to return to
  • prepared to negotiate and agree to a Job Plan if required, comply with or renegotiate an existing Job Plan
  • satisfy the requirements of a Job Plan

Is the customer eligible for JSP or will they become eligible within 13 weeks of the date of lodgement?

  • Yes, go to Step 10
  • No, reject the claim. Go to Table 2

Payability for JSP + Read more ...

The customer must meet the payability provisions for JSP (or will meet the provisions within 13 weeks of the date of claim). Base the decision on the following:

  • Income Test for single allowance customers
  • Income Test for partnered allowance customers
  • JobSeeker Payment (JSP) customer going overseas
  • Compensation preclusion
  • Assets Test

Bank accounts and balance verification + Read more ...

Customers claiming JSP supply details of all bank accounts they hold, including balances as part of the claim and make a legal declaration the information is correct. Evidence of bank account balances is not required.

If the customer has claimed online, and the claim was started prior to 5 August 2023, the online claim may have a Required task for proof of bank accounts. This is not required and evidence does not need to be reviewed or requested.

Bank balance information is collected to confirm customer income and asset details and assess waiting periods.

Only request bank balance verification where:

  • there is doubt about the validity of the information provided in the claim and the service officer anticipates a different claim outcome
  • the balances declared in the claim indicate the customer may be experiencing severe financial hardship, and
  • waiver of the Liquid Assets Waiting Period (LAWP) or Ordinary Waiting Period (OWP) can be considered

Bank account details may be pre-populated into an online claim. Verbal confirmation of account closures can be accepted if the customer has not zeroed off the balance or advised in the claim the account is closed. See Documents required for Centrelink new claims.

15

Insufficient supporting evidence + Read more ...

If the evidence supplied by the customer is insufficient to assess the claim additional supporting evidence is required. Where further information is required to grant or reject the claim, make one genuine attempt to contact by phone.

Issue a pre-call notification (SMS only) between 8:00 am and 8:00 pm, using the customer's local time, to advise a Service Officer will call them today from a private number. See Centrelink letters online and Electronic Messaging.

Contact is successful tell the customer:

  • the evidence does not meet the requirements
  • to upload the evidence via the requested task in the online claim on the day of the call. This will allow their claim to be processed as soon as possible. See Upload documents service
  • the claim will be placed on hold for one day
  • the claim will be rejected if they do not provide the evidence
  • place the claim on hold for one day and annotate the Progress of Claim (POC) DOC, with the contact outcome. Procedure end here

Contact is unsuccessful or the customer is unable to supply the extra information or evidence immediately, issue a request for information via > Request Documents. For further information, see Circumstance Change Monitor (CCM). Annotate the Progress of Claim (POC) DOC with details of the request.

Do not request documents that have been requested at Next Step in the claim again, unless further clarification of the customer circumstance is needed to assess the claim.

16

Additional supporting documents required + Read more ...

In some claims, extra evidence may still be required to complete the claim. For example, information about trusts, companies or compensation. Before requesting additional documents, check the record. Do not request documents that have been requested at Next Step in the claim again, unless further clarification of the customer circumstance is needed to assess the claim.

If further essential information is required, make one genuine attempt to contact the customer. Issue a pre-call notification (if the customer has SMS notifications enabled) between 8:00 am and 8:00 pm, using the customer's local time, to advise a Service Officer will call them today from a private number. See Centrelink letters online and Electronic Messaging.

If contact is successful:

  • if appropriate, try to obtain the information verbally
  • record the details of the conversation in the Progress of Claim (POC) DOC
  • encourage them to upload the information via the requested task in the online claim on the day of the call. This will allow their claim to be processed as soon as possible
  • See Upload documents service

If the contact was unsuccessful or the customer is unable to supply the additional information or evidence immediately, issue request for information via > Request Documents. For further information, see Circumstance Change Monitor (CCM). Annotate the Progress of Claim (POC) DOC with details of the request.

Raise an incident via Roxy and issue a RFI, if:

  • the online claim has not made a request to the customer for evidence, and
  • they do not meet any of the claim exceptions

Referrals (if required) + Read more ...

Note: the decision may already be made to streamline reject the claim, however, staff need to ensure any change of circumstances declared in the online claim information is actioned before finalising the claim. For example, a customer has notified change of care for a child, which could result in overpayment of Families payments if not actioned.

If a specialist assessment is required, select > Referral and complete the referral details:

  • Complex Assessment Officer (CAO) if the customer's financial arrangements are considered complex, for example, include a trust or company:
    • the referral ensures income and assets tests are met
    • unless it is clear that the claim must be rejected because basic eligibility criteria has not been met, claims requiring a CAO referral should not be finalised until the assessment has been completed
    • a single referral can be made for multiple assessment reasons
  • Social work services
  • Assessment of Care Arrangements - for staff who are not suitably trained in undertaking care assessments or if care is disputed or disagreed
  • Australian residence assessment, only if immigration data is missing, see Referring inconsistencies in overseas departure and arrival information to Centrelink International Services (CIS)
  • Confirming the need for a Member of a Couple (MoC) assessment
  • Shared care assessment
  • Compensation Clearance request

After a referral is made, the claim remains on hold until the assessment has been completed. Annotate the Progress of Claim (POC) DOC with details of the request.

4

Claim rejection + Read more ...

Before rejecting a customer's claim, check the customer is not vulnerable, at risk, or in crisis.

As the customer has not met all the qualification or payability provisions, the claim will be rejected for the most appropriate reason. Do not request further information unless this will change the decision.

A streamlined rejection can only be coded if:

  • the customer is in receipt of precluding payment (CPP)
  • another payment is more appropriate (COP), or
  • the customer failed to supply documents (FSD)

If the customer has failed to supply their Tax File Number or Proof Of Identity (POI), do not streamline reject FSD.

To reject the claim, go to Rejecting a claim for JobSeeker Payment (JSP) or Youth Allowance (YA) (job seeker). Procedure ends here.

NDC screen + Read more ...

The Date of Commencement (NDC) screen is pre-filled with assessment information relevant to the calculation of the customer's payment start date.

Where evidence has been supplied, DLW/DCS/DPQ fields must be coded to reflect that evidence. Specific coding may be required where backdating provisions are to be applied.

LAWP field

A total amount will auto calculate from the SVS, SIS, MIS and SVDI screens, therefore, no updates are required to this field. The only time a manual calculation is required is if the customer has LAWP within a Private Company or Private Trust. See Liquid Assets Waiting Period (LAWP).

Start date field

The system automatically calculates a start date for eligible claims. These system calculations are not always correct, therefore it is important to confirm the start date.

For example, a customer could reclaim a payment after having:

  • a backdated cancellation of the payment, and
  • a debt raised for the period they were not eligible

To calculate the start date of payment, go to Step 11.

11

Calculate the start date for payment + Read more ...

For a new claim where the customer was not previously on a payment the system will automatically calculate the start date.

Discretion is required when changing the system calculated start date.

The system will apply the backdating rule correctly and calculate the date of commencement as Date Paid To (DPT)+1, even if the date of the claim is within 14 days of DPT. When the cancellation date is outside 14 days of the claim date, do not apply DPT+1. The correct start date for these claims is the date the customer reclaims payment.

To correct the start date where system has applied an incorrect date

Where the correct date should be after the DPT+1, code the correct start date in the Manual Start Date field on the NDC screen.

Where the start should be before the DPT+1 check the following:

  • will the customer be paid for a period in which they were previously receiving a payment
  • was the previous payment cancelled as the customer was not eligible, resulting in a debt being raised

Once checks are completed, apply the following to correct the date:

  • do not code a Manual Start Date field on the NDC screen. This will cause the start date to overlap with the previous payment dates, which may:
    • prevent the claim from granting properly, or
    • lead to a data corruption on the record
  • pay the days the customer was entitled to prior to the DPT+1, as a one-off payment
  • code the DPT+1 date in the Manual Start Date field on the NDC screen, and
  • record a DOC detailing the steps taken and include the reason for the start date determination

Early Claims

For early claims, the date in the DLW/DCS field will be after the date of receipt/submission of the claim.

In some cases, the start date may not be automatically determined due to the DLW being prior to the DOR.

To code the start date in these circumstances:

  • check the automatically calculated start date on NDC
  • consider any waiting periods
  • if required, code the Manual Start Date on the NDC screen

Principal carers and shared care + Read more ...

A shared care assessment should be completed in all cases, even if only one person is claiming income support or if each person has another qualifying child solely in their care.

The shared care assessment will determine:

  • the principal carer status
  • customer's entitlement
  • rate and income test applied in some circumstances, to the ongoing entitlements for single principal carers

Customers with shared care arrangements, who are not the principal carer may still be entitled to a 'with child' rate of payment for JSP, YA and Special Benefit (SpB).

If a change of care has occurred or a determination of care is required, see Notification and assessment of shared care arrangements for Family Tax Benefit (FTB).

For claims with shared care where the determination has not been confirmed, the claim must be placed on hold. A principal carer is a person with primary care of a dependent child under 16 years. A principal carer assessment must be made if one or both customers caring for a child are claiming income support as a principal carer.

A step parent may be a principal carer if they live as a member of a couple with the parent of the child.

The system does not always automatically cease the Principal Carer status on the customer's file. If the file shows there are no children under the age of 16 in the customer's care, check that the Principal Carer status has been ended on the Principal Carer Circumstance Details (PCCD) screen.

Member of a couple with a dependent child in care:

  • over the age of 6 years
    • Only one member of a couple can be a principal carer at one time
    • If both claim or get a social security payment based on or affected by principal carer status, they must be advised in writing who the principal carer is
  • under the age of 6 years
    • Only one member of a couple can be a principal carer at one time
    • Continue to process the claim. Once the claim is finalised, see Step 3 in Child Override/Claim (CHOC) screen for coding of the PCCD and CHOC screens

Is the customer a principal carer or shared care assessment completed?

  • Yes, go to Step 16
  • No, go to Step 17

See Principal carer of a dependent child.
