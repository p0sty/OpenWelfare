Compensation clearance process

Step

Action

1

Are there multiple compensation claims recorded for the same incident date? + Read more ...

Where there are multiple compensation claims for a single incident date, the claims must be linked to each other. This is to correctly assess the compensation payments, preclusion periods or debts.

Is there multiple compensation claims for the same incident date?

  • Yes, see Linking and unlinking compensation claims, once action has been completed, go to Step 3
  • No, go to Step 2

2

Check the Compensation Management Summary (CMS) screens + Read more ...

In Customer First, search the Workspace for the Compensation Management Summary and select the relevant link to show the CMS overview screen.

The CMS overview screen:

  • displays 4 sections:
    • Expand All
    • Action Items
    • Summary
    • Claim Summary
  • has the option to run the Future Preclusion Discussion workflow or New Compensation and Damages Notification (MOD C) workflow.

Select the Claim Summary section followed by relevant Claim ID hyperlink.

Check all details are correct and update if needed:

  • Incident Date
  • Incident Details in the Claim Summary section
  • Claim ID
  • Claim Details
  • Solicitor Details
  • Compensation Payer Details
  • Liable Party Details

Is an Organisational CRN required?

  • Yes, go to Step 3
  • No, go to Step 4

3

Request Compensation Payer Organisational CRN + Read more ...

If the compensation payer is known, however no Organisational CRN exists:

  • Complete the Compensation payer organisational request form
  • The completed form will be reviewed by the Level 2 Compensation Helpdesk who will decide if a new Organisational CRN is to be created
  • Once the Organisational CRN has been created, update the Compensation Payer Details screen

Go to Step 4.

4

Is the customer claiming a Personal Sickness and Accident or Income Protection? + Read more ...

  • Yes, go to Step 5
  • No, go to Step 6

5

Decide if more information is needed from the compensation payer? + Read more ...

Is more information required? See Assessing compensation payments from personal injury schemes.

  • Yes,
    • Send SS485 form to the compensation payer, or ask the compensation payer to complete the online form located on the Service Australia website. See Resources page for form
    • Do not scan blank SS485 to customer record
    • Place the CMM/CLE activity on hold for 22 days
    • Record all action taken in a Fast Note
    • Procedure ends here
  • No, go to Step 6

6

Exemption from compensation provisions + Read more ...

For a list of compensatory payments, see The effect of compensation on Social Security payments.

Is this compensation claim for an exempt compensation claim type?

  • Yes, go to Step 7
  • No, go to Step 8

7

Compensation claim is for an exempt compensation type + Read more ...

  • Change the Clearance Reason using code 'Sufficient information to grant' from the drop down box
  • Change the Finalise Reason using code 'Not affected under the compensation provisions' from the drop down box

No notices are to be sent.

CRT are to:

  • complete the INC PROTECTION CLE – Not Compensation Fast Note
  • record the payments on the relevant screens. It will be included in the assessment of the customer's rate of payment

See Recording other income on the Other Income (OIN) screen and Assessing superannuation.

For Criminal Injuries and Sporting Injury, the Clearance Reason on the Clearance Details screen must match the Claim Type.

  • Select [Finish]
  • Select [Continue] for the claim to auto-finalise
  • Record all action taken in the Clearance Action Fast Note

Once updates are complete, check for any compensation activities created as a result of the assessment and action where appropriate, including activities:

  • CLE ‘clear to pay’ or ‘not clear to pay’
  • CLR ‘Compensation Management System/Clerical Review’
  • REA ‘Review net amount received for assets and investment income’

Cancel activities where not required. Do not refer to a Service Centre, other Zone teams or Smart Centre.

Procedure ends here.

8

More information required from customer + Read more ...

Where a clearance request relates to a new claim or review of an ongoing entitlement for a CAP, failure to provide the information may result in a rejection of the new claim or cancellation of a CAP.

CRT must make genuine attempts to phone the customer to get the information.

If successful and:

  • customer can give information over the phone, update screens, go to Step 10
  • customer cannot give information and is in financial hardship, go to Step 9
  • customer cannot give information over the phone and is not in financial hardship:
    • Send the Q919 letter to the customer
    • Tell them they need to reply to this request within 22 days
    • Tell them if they do not respond, their payment may be suspended
    • Place the CMM/CLE activity on hold for 22 days
    • Record all action taken in a Fast Note
    • Go to Step 14

If not successful:

  • Send the Q919 letter to the customer
  • Place the CMM/CLE activity on hold for 22 days
  • Record all action taken in a Fast Note

Go to Step 10.

9

Urgent clearance request + Read more ...

If the customer cannot give the information over the phone and meets financial hardship criteria, CRT can phone the compensation payer to obtain the:

  • Date last paid
  • Compensation payers reference number
  • Current ongoing rate

If the compensation payer cannot give information over the phone or a debt (periodic or lump sum) has been identified:

  • Send the Q908 letter to the compensation payer requesting details
    • Where possible send via Fax
    • See Faxing letters using Outlook and Customer First
  • Place the CMM/CLE activity on hold for 22 days
  • Record all action taken in a Fast Note

Procedure ends here

10

Has the customer taken reasonable action to claim compensation? + Read more ...

For details, see Reasonable action to claim compensation

  • Yes, go to Step 13
  • No, go to Step 11

11

Customer has not taken reasonable action to claim + Read more ...

  • All determinations must be made by APS 5 level Compensation Recovery staff or above
  • Requests are made by submitting an online form to the Level 2 Compensation Helpdesk
  • Once approval has been given CRT must phone the customer and tell them they must pursue their claim for compensation under s1166 of the Social Security Act 1991

Go to Step 12.

12

Coding no reasonable action + Read more ...

Where an APS5 or above has made a decision that the customer or partner has not taken reasonable action to claim compensation, the CRT must update the:

  • customer's record, and
  • compensation claim in the CMS

The Clearance Details screen shows information from the customer's record.

Review all details on the Clearance Details screen:

  • Select Clearance Reason of No reasonable action to claim from the drop down box
  • Where applicable, select Advice Not Issued for:
    • Issue Rights and Obligations Notice Customer
    • Issue Rights and Obligations Notice Solicitor
    • Issue Preliminary Notice
  • Complete Source and Receipt fields
  • Select Continue to go the Assessment Results (AR) screen
  • Check the result is correct

If the customer or partner is current and receiving a CAP, the CMS will either cancel or suspend payment or reject a CAP new claim for reason 'Fail to claim Compo' - (FCC)'.

  • Send the customer a Q912 Enforcement of compensation rights (s1166) letter
  • Record the decision detailing all action required by the customer in the Clearance s1166/s1167 Fast Note

The compensation claim will remain open for 28 days, if no action is taken in this time, the CMS will finalise the compensation claim and status updated as ‘closed’

The compensation claim will remain in CMS with a status of ‘closed’ until it is reactivated and the clearance reason changed.

Once updates are complete, check for any compensation activities created as a result of the assessment and action where appropriate, including activities:

  • CLE ‘clear to pay’ or ‘not clear to pay’
  • CLR ‘Compensation Management System/Clerical Review’
  • REA ‘Review net amount received for assets and investment income’

Cancel activities where not required. Do not refer to a Service Centre, other Zone teams or Smart Centre.

Procedure ends here.

13

Can the record be cleared? + Read more ...

  • Yes, go to Step 16
  • No, check if information returned, go to Step 14

14

Has Information been returned? + Read more ...

  • Yes, CRT are to update the relevant screens, go to Step 16
  • No, not returned from the:
    • compensation payer, go to Step 15
    • non-customer, go to Step 17
    • current customer, go to Step 18

15

Information not returned from compensation payer + Read more ...

On day 23, the held CMM/CLE activity becomes due.

CRT are to check the customer record to confirm that more information is still required.

If the information is still required, CRT are to phone the compensation payer to confirm the Q908 has been received.

  • If compensation payer advises they have received the form but have not responded:
    • advise if they fail to comply they are in breach of section 197 of the Social Security (Administration) Act 1999 (the Act)
    • under section 197 of the Social Security (Administration) Act 1999 (the Act), failure or refusal to comply with this notice without reasonable excuse is a criminal offence and is punishable by imprisonment for a term not exceeding 12 months
    • section 198 of the Act states that nothing contained in a law of State or Territory operates to prevent a person from giving information or producing documents as required by this notice. This includes privacy laws that may be in force in any State or Territory
  • If compensation payer advises they have not received the request for information:
    • CRT are to fax the Q908, and advise it must be returned within 22 days
    • Place the CMM/CLE activity on hold for 22 days
    • Where information has not been returned after 22 days, do not send another request:

CRT are to phone the customer to advise them of the delay in getting the information and what action has been taken.

Record details of the action taken in a Fast note

Place the CMM/CLE activity on hold for 22 days

Procedure ends here

16

Finish coding the Clearance Details screen + Read more ...

If the customer has ongoing weekly compensation payments, code before finalising the clearance. See Coding and raising debts for periodic compensation payments.

If the CMM/CLE activity is ‘Clear to Pay’, select from the Clearance Reason drop down box option of either:

  • Sufficient information to Grant, or
  • Reasonable Action Taken

Where applicable, from the drop down box, select:

  • Issue Rights and Obligations Notice Customer
  • Issue Rights and Obligations Notice Solicitor
  • Issue Preliminary Notice
  • A preliminary notice is not to be sent:
    • without the correct reference number
    • to the defendant solicitor
    • where periodic payments are ongoing
    • to an overseas liable party
    • when the compensation recipient is not claiming or receiving a CAP, or
    • the compensation recipient is not pursing further compensation

If the non-current customer (compensation recipient) is partnered, and their partner is claiming or receiving an income support payment:

  • Send the Rights and Obligations letter to the non-current customer
  • Do not send a:
    • Rights and Obligations to solicitor
    • Preliminary Notice to the compensation payer

A Manual Follow-up (MFU) review (no preliminary notice issued) is created every 21 days until a preliminary notice is sent. See Compensation Recovery Reviews.

  • Select Yes in the Finished field
  • Select Continue to go to the Assessment Results (AR) screen
  • For non-current customers, cancel the generated CLE 'Clear to Pay' activity and finalise the CMM record
  • Where no new claim activity or referral exists, the CRT must record any updates to the customer’s record and record the clearance details in a Fast Note
  • If the clearance has been requested as part of a new claim activity the CRT will record the compensation clearance in a Fast Note and manually take the new claim off hold. This will result in the new claim being allocated, based on previously established priorities

Once the CMM/CLE activity is completed, code the following payments:

  • Arrears of periodic payments. See Coding and raising debts for periodic compensation payments
  • Lump sum payment. See Calculating a compensation debt for out of court settlements

Check the Activity List (AL) screen for any activities generated as a result. If any CMM activities exist on AL, CRT must complete or cancel the activities.

If extra information is needed to raise a debt for a period in the past, go to Step 20.

Otherwise, go to Step 21.

17

Insufficient information to provide a clearance for a non-current customer + Read more ...

Where the non-current customer has failed to return the Q919 letter, the CMM/CLE activity comes off hold on day 23.

On the Clearance Details screen, select Clearance Reason of 'Insufficient information' from the drop down box

  • A CLE 'Not clear to pay' activity will generate and must be completed or cancelled
  • Create a compensation clearance Fast Note on the customer's record, detailing what action has been taken and what further information is required
  • If the clearance has been requested as part of a new claim activity, CRT must manually take the new claim off hold. This will result in the new claim being allocated, based on previously established priorities.
  • Check the Activity List (AL) screen for any activities generated as a result. If any CMM activities exist on AL, CRT must complete or cancel the activities.

The compensation claim will remain in CMS with a status of active.

Procedure ends here.

18

Insufficient information to provide a clearance for a current customer + Read more ...

Where the current customer has failed to return the Q919 letter, the CMM/CLE activity comes off hold on day 23.

Staff should make genuine attempts to contact customer to:

  • Explain the decision
  • Give them a chance to provide more information, and
  • Advise their review and appeal rights

To suspend the customer, go to Step 19.

19

Suspend payments + Read more ...

Each payment type has its own suspension rules. Before actioning suspension make sure the customer's correct system and environment is selected, then follow the correct suspension process for the payment type being suspended, see Suspension of payments (CLK). A separate Suspension Fast Note must be created on the customer's record.

For partnered customers, where weekly compensation payments have been determined and not declared, and impacts both customer and partner CAP, suspend both.

Where suspension applies to both customer and partner, the process must be done in each customer's record.

  • Cancel the CMM/CLE activity from the Activity List (AL) screen
  • Create a compensation clearance Fast Note on the customer's record detailing what action has been taken and what further information is required to complete the Clearance and restore the customer payments

To suspend the customer's payment in Customer First, go to Benefit Action (BA) screen and code the following fields:

  • Svc Rsn: see Resources for benefit type (service reason) codes
  • Action: SUS (suspend)
  • Reason: FRC (Failed to return correspondence)
  • Press [Enter] and the date of effect will default or enter the date of effect of the suspension in the Effect Date: field. See Suspension of payments (CLK)
  • Complete Source and date of receipt
  • Go to the Assessment Results (AR) screen
  • Finalise the activity
  • Automatic notification of suspension letter will be sent to customer and partner (where applicable)

Document the suspension by recording all relevant details using the Suspend Cancel Reason Fast Note. The Fast Note is located via:

  • Auto Text
  • Category 1: Generic
  • Category 2: SUS
  • Template: Suspend Cancel Reason

The Suspension Fast Note must contain the following:

  • Decision made, payment type and Act/Guide reference upon which decision was based
  • Date of effect of decision
  • Reasons for decision, including why the person was not qualified or why the payment is not payable, for decisions made under section 80
  • Follow up action required before restoration can take place, 'Do not restore customer and/or partner until compensation details are provided. Refer to compensation clearance Fast Note DOC dated xx/xx/xxxx'

20

Request further information + Read more ...

The agency can request information from claimants, current and ex-recipients or their partners where applicable. See Requesting information (CLK).

After completing the clearance, decide if further information is required to raise a debt. Check if the customer has received compensation payments during previous CAP periods. Contact can be made with the customer to clarify this.

If further information is needed to raise a debt, contact the customer to advise:

  • What information is required
  • What the consequences are of failing to comply with the request (eg. suspension/cancellation)
  • The timeframe allowed to respond

After contact with the customer or if contact with the customer is unsuccessful:

  • Send a Q919 letter requesting the information
  • In Customer First, create manual review activity on the Review Registration (RVR) screen:
    • Key CMM in Service Reason field
    • Key FOR in Review Reason field
    • Key the Due Date of 22 days from the date of the request
    • Complete Source and Receipt fields
    • Transfer to region code CTN
    • Record all action taken in the clearance Fast Note

Ex-recipients

An ex-recipient of a social security income support payment or concession card is not obligated to answer questions about events that occurred more than 13 weeks before the date of the request unless the notice is issued under Part 5 section 196 of the Social Security (Administration) Act 1999, where no time limits apply.

When sending a request to an ex-recipient for information about events more than 13 weeks ago:

  • Issue a request for information under Social Security (Administration) Act 1999 - Section 192. See Requesting information (CLK)
  • Available compensation documents are:
    • MOD C - Compensation and Damages
    • SS445 - Compensation Advice of Periodic Payments
    • SS446 - Compensation Advice of Lump Sum Payments
  • The ex-recipient will need to complete the forms and supply supporting documents to the best of their ability
  • Record all action taken in the clearance Fast Note

When the activity falls due, see Step 10 in the Request information from a claimant/recipient table on the Customer First tab

Go to Step 21.

21

Create a Clearance Action Fast Note outlining the clearance details + Read more ...

Complete the DOC with relevant information. Delete unwanted text for the Clearance Action decision:

  • Scanned/Receipt date
  • Date of Incident
  • Compensation Payer/Insurer
  • Contact person
  • Phone number
  • Details discussed
  • Attempt 1/Attempt 2/Discussion
  • Act Ref: Part 3.14 of Social Security Act 1991
  • Weekly compensation paid
  • Period
  • Reason compensation ceased
  • Incident after date of grant
  • CAP Qualification Indicator
  • Compensation assessed as Direct Deduction/Income
  • Type of claim
  • Claim Open/Closed
  • Common law claim proceeding
  • Lump Sums Paid
  • Impairment amount and date paid

Go to Step 22.

22

Reactivate the CMS record + Read more ...

Once the information is returned and the customer's CAP is restored, the claim will remain in CMS with a status of finalised until:

  • it is reactivated, and
  • the clearance reason is changed

See Reactivating a compensation claim.

23

ZIAM_CLE_GEN - Compensation (Clear to pay/Not Clear to pay) + Read more ...

'Clear to Pay' or ‘Not Clear to Pay’ work items are created after a compensation clearance assessment has been completed. Generally, the work item is intended to advise the referring area of the compensation clearance decision.

The work item type allocated for manual completion is ZIAM_CLE_GEN.

The work item may be duplicated, therefore it is important to check for and complete both the:

  • Integrated System (ISIS) activity (viewable via AL screen), and
  • any associated Customer First (CF) SAP Interaction Record/s or Process Direct - Workload Management System

To complete the activity:

  • Check the Document List (DL) screen for a DOC advising of the Compensation Clearance decision
  • On the AL screen, select the CMM/CLE activity
  • Finalise the activity via the AR screen or cancel the activity and annotate the Compensation Clearance decision DOC

Procedure ends here.
