For the Income Management Exemption and Assessment Team (IMEAT) only

This page contains processing information relating to determining financial vulnerability as part of an assessment for an exemption from Income Management for customers with dependent children.

Vulnerability test for an exemption from Income Management

Table 1: This table outlines how to determine financial vulnerability as part of an assessment for an exemption from Income Management for customers with dependent children.

Step

Action

1

Customer seeking exemption of Income Management + Read more ...

Customer is transferred to the Income Management Exemption and Assessment Team (IMEAT)

Check if the customer requires an interpreter. If required, see Booking an interpreter for an appointment.

Confirm that the customer has dependent children for Income Management exemption purposes.

Is the customer on auto Income Management?

  • Yes, manually place the customer on manual Income Management to ensure that their payment does not become SUSpended (SUS-INM) while they are awaiting the outcome of their exemption request.
    Go to Step 2
  • No, normal processing applies. Go to Step 2

2

Run the Compulsory IM exemption workflow + Read more ...

Record the exemption category as 'customer with dependent children' and the date on the application.

Note: the exemption type defaults to the most appropriate option, however, this can be changed.

Go to Step 3.

3

Interpreter Requirements screen + Read more ...

This screen will present if the customer does not have their interpreter language recorded as English on the Other Contact Details (OCD) screen.

If there is no language recorded on the OCD screen Service Officers must exit the workflow and update the OCD screen before the exemption workflow can proceed.

The Financial Vulnerability Assessment Interpreter Requirements screen will present each time the assessor re-visits the assessment until the exemption activity has been finalised.

Record interpreter details:

Is the Customer Present? Select Yes or No as appropriate. It is possible some work is completed without the customer's presence, but the interview must never be carried out without the customer being present.

Customer's Spoken Language: This will default to the interpreter language recorded on the OCD screen.

Interpreter Required: This will default to Yes.

Is an interpreter being used to conduct the Financial Vulnerability Assessment interview with the customer? Select Yes, No, Not Required or Not Available as appropriate from the drop down menu.

  • If Yes is selected, no other information is required, select 'Continue'. Go to Step 4
  • If No is selected, select the applicable reason from the drop down menu:
    • Appointment not booked
    • Conflict of interest
    • Customer declined an interpreter
    • Customer wishes to use agent or family member
    • Deaf or hearing impaired
    • Services Australia does not have an interpreter who speaks the customer's language
  • If Not available is selected, select the applicable reason from the drop down menu:
    • Interpreter did not attend interview
    • Interpreter service for that language not available
    • Same gender interpreter not available
  • If Not required is selected, select the applicable reason from the drop down menu:
    • No contact required
    • Speaks and understands English

More Information: If an interpreter is not available, not being used or not required, additional information can be recorded in addition to the standard reasons selected above. For example, where 'Customer wishes to use agent or family member' is selected, the agent or family member's details can be recorded in this field.

Select Continue.

4

Financial exploitation + Read more ...

The Financial exploitation page displays if the customer's record is Restricted Access.

  • If the record is Restricted Access check the Document List (DL) screen for DOCs showing whether there has been denied contact from third parties

See Step 1 in the Customer information required for Vulnerability assessment table

Customer information required for Vulnerability assessment

Table 2: This table outlines how to determine financial vulnerability as part of an assessment for an exemption from Income Management for customers with dependent children.

Step

Action

1

Discussion with customer + Read more ...

Financial exploitation is where a person is subject to undue pressure, harassment, violence, abuse, deception or exploitation for resources by another person and/or people, including other family members and members of a community.

Care needs to be taken as customers experiencing family and domestic violence can be subject to financial exploitation.

Discuss the following in relation to the last 12 months:

  • If the customer has a password protected or Restricted Access record, ask them for the reason why, and if the issue has been resolved
  • Does the customer find it difficult to say no to some family members or others when asked for money?
  • Does the customer need to hide money or have secret money?
  • Is the customer having trouble meeting their priority needs or making their money last from one pay to the next because they are paying things for other people or giving other people their money?
  • If third parties have been contacting the agency, why have they been contacting, and has this been at the request of the customer. Is the customer aware that third parties had been contacting?

2

Demonstrated Budgeting and Savings + Read more ...

Discuss with the customer the following:

  • Does the customer have a budget?
  • Does the customer have any financial goals and a plan for achieving them?
  • Has the customer been evicted for not paying rent in the last 12 months? If so, how did this come about?
  • Has the customer had a service or utility disconnected in the last 12 months? If so, how did this come about?
  • Has the customer used lay-buy to purchase a large ticket item (for example, white goods) in the last 12 months?
  • Does the customer have any regular payments set up outside of Centrepay or income management (IM), such as direct credit instalment payment of utilities etc.?
  • How does the customer plan to pay for large bills like car registration or winter gas/electricity bills?
  • Does the customer have a good idea about what all their expenses are and how much income they have each week/fortnight?
  • Does the customer have unpaid fines or significant debts?

3

Centrepay deductions + Read more ...

A summary of Centrepay deductions for the customer in the previous 12 months is displayed.

Has the customer had any Centrepay deductions in the previous 12 months?

  • Yes, check if Centrepay arrangements have been started and then stopped. If so, discuss the reason for this. Go back to Step 2
  • No, discuss the following:
    • Was the customer aware of Centrepay options?
    • Did the customer choose not to use Centrepay because they are able to pay their bills on time without it?
    • Has the customer previously enquired about Centrepay but found that the Third Party Organisation (TPO) was not registered?
      Go to Step 4

4

Income Management allocations + Read more ...

Periods the customer was on Income Management in the previous 12 months (including the category of Income Management) and a history of Income Management expenses for the previous 12 months are displayed.

If the customer has been income managed in the previous 12 months, discuss the following:

  • Has the customer understood what Income Management is and how it works?
  • If expenses were regularly changed, what was the reason for this?
  • If the expenses were never changed, why not?
  • Was the customer meeting priority needs with their discretionary income? If so, why?
  • Will the customer be able to manage their money to meet their priority needs without Income Management? If so, how?
  • Does the customer intend to continue to pay for things that were being paid for by Income Management, or do they intend to stop paying for these things? If so, why?

5

BasicsCards + Read more ...

The number of replacement BasicsCards issued to the customer in the previous 12 months is displayed.

Frequent replacement BasicsCards may be an indicator of financial vulnerability.

If the customer has had a replacement BasicsCard in the previous 12 months, discuss the reasons why.

For all customers who have been issued with a BasicsCard in the previous 12 months discuss the following:

  • Has the customer given their PIN to anyone else and had money taken from their account without their permission?
  • Does the customer use book up (obtaining goods on credit) and leave their card at the shop?
  • Do people take and use their card without their permission?

6

Declined BasicsCard transactions + Read more ...

The number of declined BasicsCard transactions in the previous 12 months is displayed.

A high number of declined transactions incurred by the customer may be an indicator of financial vulnerability.

If the customer has had a high number of declined BasicsCard transactions in the previous 12 months, discuss the following:

  • Why has the customer had a high number of declined transactions?
  • How does the customer typically plan to pay for groceries each week/fortnight? Is money set aside or does the customer spend what is left on their card?
  • When the customer goes shopping, do they have a good idea about what they need and how much it is going to cost?
  • When the customer has insufficient funds to purchase all the things they want, how do they decide what to buy and what to put back (are essential food and goods being covered)?

7

Entitlement Period End Date (EPED) changes + Read more ...

The number of EPED changes in the previous 12 months is displayed.

If the customer has had an EPED change in the previous 12 months, discuss the reasons for this.

8

Urgent Payment applications + Read more ...

Information on any urgent payment applications made (granted or rejected) in the previous 12 months is displayed.

If the customer has had an urgent payment application granted or rejected in the previous 12 months, discuss the following:

  • Is there a pattern suggesting that the customer is applying for urgent payments for the same things each time such as late rent, utility bills, and public transport costs?
  • Has the customer applied for an urgent payment for things that they could have foreseen and planned for?
  • For any urgent payments that have been rejected, what were the reasons?

9

Courses to learn or update financial skills + Read more ...

Indication that the customer has been granted a Matched Savings Scheme Payment (MSSP) in the last 12 months is displayed.

If so, view the referral summary.

If the customer has completed any courses to learn or update financial skills (including a Money Management course), discuss the following:

  • What was the course the customer completed and who conducted the course?
  • Did the customer develop a personal budget as a result of attending this course?
  • Did they stick to the budget or achieve any financial goals (purchase of large ticket items, for example, white goods, saving money for a specific purpose etc.) as a result of attending the course?

See Step 1 in the Processing Vulnerability assessment for IM exemption table.

Processing Vulnerability assessment for IM exemption

Table 3: This table outlines how to determine financial vulnerability as part of an assessment for an exemption from Income Management for customers with dependent children.

Step

Action

1

Financial Vulnerability Assessment Interview screen + Read more ...

Service Officers are to ask customers a series of questions based on 'factors' in order to assess vulnerability:

  • Select a financial vulnerability factor radio button to expand the question set and complete the Current factor notes field
  • If a previous assessment has been completed for a factor the factor notes can be viewed, or copied into the current assessment's factor notes by selecting the Copy history checkbox
  • If a factor does not apply to the customer (for example, urgent payments) the system will insert 'Not Applicable' into the Current factor notes and the factor will be deemed as answered
  • Any factors not assessed are marked with a red cross
  • Factors which have been assessed are marked by a green tick
  • All factors must be assessed before continuing to the Financial Vulnerability Assessment Decision screen

Note: if the Financial Vulnerability Assessment interview is not completed, select Save and Exit and the interview can be completed at a later date. Procedure ends here.

If all factors have been addressed and the interview completed, select Continue.

2

IM Financial Vulnerability Assessment Decision screen + Read more ...

A Financial Vulnerability Assessment decision must be recorded using the following criteria:

  • Meeting priority needs - The customer has been applying appropriate resources to meet the priority needs of themselves and their family
  • Financial exploitation - The customer has control over their money and is not subject to financial exploitation
  • Budgeting and payment patterns - The customer did not regularly require urgent funds to pay for foreseeable costs; did not regularly change their EPED and had valid reasons for applying for urgent payments
  • Financial stability - The customer has a stable pattern of payments and budgeting practices to meet their priority needs from their income support payment and family assistance payment

Financial Vulnerability Assessment decision overview

A decision cannot be recorded until each criteria is addressed as indicated by a green tick (indicating a 'Yes') or a red cross (indicating a 'No') under the 'Decision'.

Financial Vulnerability Decision details

Has the customer been applying appropriate resources to meet priority needs? Select Yes or No as appropriate.

The reasons for the decision must be recorded in the Free text: field.

Financial Vulnerability Assessment result

Based on the results recorded for each interview factor and decision, select the Overall outcome from the dropdown menu:

  • Vulnerable in the last 12 months
    • In the Financial Vulnerable at: field insert date of the last vulnerability indicator, or date customer applied for exemption where no specific vulnerability date is identified
  • Not vulnerable in the last 12 months

Note: if the Financial Vulnerability Assessment cannot be completed and a decision made, select Save and Exit to complete the Financial Vulnerability Assessment at a later date. Procedure ends here.

Select Continue.

3

Outcome of IM Financial Vulnerability Assessment screen + Read more ...

The IM Financial Vulnerability Assessment Outcome screen displays the outcome of the completed Financial Vulnerability Assessment.

This assessment outcome is displayed in a printer-friendly format allowing Service Officers to print as required (for example, discuss any factors with Team Leader).

While the exemption application is still incomplete, state (that is, has not been granted or rejected). Service Officers can edit the assessment content by selecting the Edit button.

Select Continue.

4

Financial vulnerability + Read more ...

Has the customer shown signs of financial vulnerability in the previous 12 months?

  • Yes, go to Step 5
  • No, go to Step 6

5

Reject exemption application as customer assessed as financially vulnerable + Read more ...

Continue the Compulsory IM exemption workflow and complete the Exemption Determination screen.

  • Exemption Decision: Status: select Reject
  • Decision date: This will default to today's date
  • Reason: Select 'Not financially stable'
  • Finalise the activity on the Income Management Summary page.
  • Advise customers of their right to request Reviews of Income Management decisions
  • Advise customers where to find more information to help with managing their finances. Customers can visit servicesaustralia.gov.au/managingmoney for useful information and resources
  • Procedure ends here

6

Customer assessed as not financially vulnerable + Read more ...

Continue the Compulsory IM exemption workflow. To finalise an exemption, see Providing evidence and finalising the assessment for an exemption from Income Management for customers with dependent children.
