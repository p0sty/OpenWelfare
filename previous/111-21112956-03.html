Lodging dispute

Table 1

Step

Action

1

Staff research + Read more ...

Before lodging a dispute, staff must research Operational Blueprint to make sure their dispute is supported by a business process or policy.

This evidence will need to be submitted when lodging the dispute.

Staff can seek support through their Tier 1 Technical Support to understand a QOL or QMA result and feedback.

Note: disputes can only be lodged if overall QOL/QMA accuracy is affected, that is:

  • proficiency impacting errors
  • the overall QOL/QMA decision, not individual errors within the check.

2

Informal resolution + Read more ...

Is the original Quality Checker located in the same site?

  • Yes,
    can the dispute be resolved informally through discussion/explanation of decision with the Quality Checker?
    • Yes, go to Step 3
    • No, go to Step 4
  • No, go to Step 4

3

Dispute resolved informally + Read more ...

Has the Quality Checker reversed their original decision?

Yes, Original Quality Checker to email SSW Quality Team – general mailbox with the following details:

  • Email subject: Reversal of original quality check decision
  • Email body:
    • Service Officers logon
    • Quality check type – QOL or QMA
    • Date check completed
    • Activity (AMR) or work item ID
    • Reason decision has been reversed
  • SSW Quality will lodge and finalise a QOL/QMA dispute to ensure the reversed decision is reflected in SmartTools
  • A notification email is sent to all parties when the dispute is finalised

No, procedure ends here.

4

Lodge dispute + Read more ...

Complete the F2F QOL and QMA Disputes form and include supporting evidence. See Resources for a link to the form.

Do not include any customer identifying data, such as Customer Reference Number (CRN) or names in the form.

  • Open the F2F QOL and QMA Disputes form
  • Select Submit a dispute and complete all fields
  • Read and acknowledge the privacy statement
  • Fill in all fields: (Mandatory fields are marked with an asterisk (*)
    • Enter QOL, AMR or QMA Work Item ID and the date activity was submitted exactly as they appear in QOLCheck tool or Process Direct QMA Feedback tile
    • For QOL - open QOLCheck tool, select Replay Work and complete search criteria to locate activity details
    • For QMA - select the QMA Feedback tile in Process Direct to view results and comments from Quality Management Officers
  • Select Submit

Note: disputes will be returned to the staff member to resubmit or withdraw when:

  • insufficient evidence supports the dispute, or
  • activity cannot be located

5

Dispute Progress and outcome + Read more ...

Staff will be able to view the progress of their dispute on the F2F QOL and QMA Disputes page. See Resources for a link to the page.

A notification email is sent to all parties when the dispute is finalised.

Note: Once the QOL/QMA dispute decision has been finalised, no further escalation steps are available. Team Leaders can email SSW Quality If there are extenuating circumstances.

6

Withdrawing QOL/QMA disputes + Read more ...

Staff can withdraw a QOL/QMA dispute after it has been lodged.

Is the status of the dispute New?

  • Yes,
    • select the Edit icon to open the F2F QOL and QMA dispute form
    • read and acknowledge the privacy statement
    • scroll to the bottom of the form and select Withdraw
  • No,
    • select the Edit icon to open the F2F QOL and QMA dispute form
    • contact the independent reviewer to withdraw the dispute
    • if the independent reviewer is not available email SSW Quality

Independent review

Table 2

Step

Action

1

Independent Review - review and decision + Read more ...

The Independent Reviewer locates the dispute form in F2F QOL and QMA Disputes inbox and makes an assessment.

  • Select the Edit icon to open the F2F QOL and QMA dispute form
  • Read and acknowledge the Privacy statement
  • Review the information entered by the staff member
  • Note: email SSW Quality if a duplicate dispute is identified

2

Has sufficient information been provided to complete the dispute? + Read more ...

  • Yes, go to Step 3
  • No, return the dispute to the staff member, requesting more details. Select the appropriate option:
    • Insufficient evidence to support the dispute, or
    • The activity cannot be located
    • Procedure ends here

3

Undertake review + Read more ...

To undertake the review:

  • Independent Reviewer field: enter the staff logon to allocate the dispute
  • Independent Reviewer comments field: review the dispute and add comments supporting the decision
  • If the Independent Reviewer does not have the technical expertise to review the dispute, consulting with a technical specialist is appropriate. Seek technical expert advice from the:
    • SSO network
    • Service Delivery Support team, or
    • SSO Program Support Manager co-ordinator, who will escalate within SSW Quality to locate an appropriate business or policy team

If the Independent Reviewer recommendation is:

  • Upheld or Upheld for another reason, go to Step 4
  • Overturned, go to Step 5

Note:

  • SSOs should ensure they have access to the QMA Dashboard for SSO/QDO
    • Access to the QMA Dashboard is limited to 6 months, SSOs should reapply for access before it expires (no expiry reminder is sent)
  • if the Independent Reviewer identifies errors, they must update the customer record as soon as possible. Do not wait for the outcome of the right of reply process (if implemented)
  • if the QMA Replay function is not available, uphold the dispute if the customer outcome is correct

4

Decision is Upheld or Upheld for a different reason + Read more ...

In the Independent Reviewer determination field, select either:

  • Upheld (agree with the original check result)
  • Upheld for a different reason (original checking decision is correct but for a different reason. For example, different error reason)

If further action is needed by the staff member:

  • complete the Is further action required field, detailing action to be taken
  • select Submit

An automatic email is sent to the staff member, their Team Leader and the original Quality Checker explaining the outcome.

Go to Step 6.

5

Original decision to be overturned + Read more ...

Locate the dispute in the F2F QOL and QMA Disputes page and select:

  • Overturned (Original decision overturned. That is, agree with dispute)

If further action is needed by the staff member:

  • complete the Is further action required field detailing action to be taken
  • select Submit

Arrange access for the original Quality Checker to the F2F QOL and QMA disputes page.

An email is automatically sent to the original Quality Checker with:

  • the independent reviewer's recommendation, and
  • instructions for submitting a right of reply

6

Providing feedback to the Quality Checker + Read more ...

Does feedback (not covered by the QOL/QMA process) need to be provided to the Quality Checker? For example, feedback about the quality of the QOL/QMA feedback.

  • Yes, go to Step 8
  • No, procedure ends here

7

Feedback to Quality Checker via the Staff Feedback Tool + Read more ...

Provide feedback via the Staff Feedback Tool, see Resources for a link to the tool and feedback templates:

  • under Programme & Business Assurance select Quality Management then the relevant tool and feedback type:
    • QMA
    • QOL
  • update the F2F QOL and QMA Dispute form - National Feedback provided to Quality Checker and select the appropriate reason/s:
    • Missing or incorrect resources supporting the decision such as Operational Blueprint not provided
    • Explanation of action to be taken to correct the error not included
    • Description of the error and why it was incorrect not provided
    • Feedback not clear
    • Feedback not constructive

Right of reply

Table 3

Step

Action

1

Right of reply + Read more ...

If the Independent Reviewer's recommendation is to change the original decision, the original Quality Checker will be given a right of reply.

  • Original Checker, go to Step 2
  • Independent Reviewer, go to Step 3

2

Original Quality Checker - review of overturned recommendation + Read more ...

Response to be completed within 5 business days.

Note: if unable to respond within 5 days and disagree with the dispute recommendation, send an email to SSW Quality.

The original Quality Checker reviews the overturned recommendation - right of reply email.

If they:

  • Agree with/accept the overturned recommendation:
    • select Agree with/accept decision and enter supporting comments (optional) within the F2F QOL and QMA Disputes form
    • once completed, select Submit
    • procedure ends here
  • Disagree with/don’t accept the overturned recommendation:
    • select Disagree with decision, enter supporting comments within the F2F QOL and QMA Disputes form
    • once complete, select Submit

The F2F QOL and QMA Disputes form is automatically returned to the Independent Reviewer, who will review the details provided.

Procedure ends here.

3

Independent Reviewer – Right of reply + Read more ...

Was the Right of Reply response received from original Quality Checker within 5 business days?

  • Yes, go to Step 4
  • No,
    • finalise dispute decision Overturned
    • procedure ends here

4

Independent Reviewer – Right of reply received within 3 business days + Read more ...

Read the response from the original Quality Checker. If the Independent Reviewer:

  • agrees with/accepts the original Quality Checker's decision:
    • update the comments within the dispute form
    • finalise dispute decision Upheld
    • procedure ends here
  • disagrees with/doesn't accept the original Quality Checker's decision and a mutual decision:
    • can be reached with the Quality Checker, finalise dispute in line with the mutual decision. Procedure ends here
    • cannot be reached, go to Step 5

5

Independent Reviewer – Right of Reply referral to Independent Party (another SSO) + Read more ...

  • Contact another SSO via the MS Teams team chat
  • Discuss the decision and response from the original checker with the independent SSO
  • If both SSOs agree, the Independent Reviewer updates the comments within the dispute form, noting
    • the dispute was referred to another independent party (an SSO)
    • the mutual decision reached between the two SSOs (Independent Reviewer and independent party)

If no agreement can be reached, go to Step 6

6

Independent Reviewer – Right of reply referral to Independent Party (relevant program or policy team) + Read more ...

Within 5 business days, ask for a third party review by the relevant program, policy team or Level 2 Helpdesk to obtain a resolution. If unsure which team should complete this review, contact SSW Quality with the details.

7

Independent Reviewer – response received + Read more ...

Response from relevant program, policy team or Level 2 Helpdesk received:

  • Independent Reviewer to review the response
  • Add response from relevant program, policy team or Level 2 Helpdesk to the QOL/QMA Dispute form

Is recommendation to Overturn the decision supported?

  • Yes, finalise the dispute - decision Overturned
  • No, finalise the dispute - decision Upheld

An email is automatically sent to the staff member, their Team Leader and the Quality Checker advising the outcome.
