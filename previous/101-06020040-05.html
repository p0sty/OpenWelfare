Shared care - level of care required

Customers who have shared care arrangements are required to have a certain percentage of care to enable the shared care dependents to be listed on the parent/guardian's concession card, including Health Care Card (HCC), Pensioner Concession Card (PCC), Low Income Health Care Cards (LIC), Foster Child HCC (FST), Commonwealth Seniors Health Card (CSHC) and Ex-Carer Allowance (child) HCC (EHC).

Percentage of care required for concession cardholders with shared care arrangements

Table 1: This table describes the percentage of care required before a cardholder’s shared care dependents are listed on the cardholder's concession card, including Health Care Card (HCC), Pensioner Concession Card (PCC), Low Income Health Care Cards (LIC), Foster Child HCC (FST), Commonwealth Seniors Health Card (CSHC) and Ex-Carer Allowance (child) HCC (EHC).

Parenting Payment (PP)

Minimum of 50% care. However, care is considered 'equal' when the difference in care is less than 10%. In cases of 'equal' care i.e. (46/54) the principal carer with less than 50% care will be entitled to have the child listed on their concession card.

Generally, the person who has the majority of care of a PP child is entitled to have the child listed on their HCC.

PP customers who have a Regular Care Child will be eligible to have the child listed on their HCC.
