Determining Trust/Company distribution amounts + Read more ...

Distribution amounts can be determined by examining the private trust's distribution or private company's dividend statement.

Distribution amounts paid to an attributable stakeholder can be disregarded include:

  • amounts already been included as attribution income
  • amounts from prior year profits

The gifting provisions in relation to distributions paid to a non-attributable stakeholder will be disregarded if:

  • the customer can show they were not in receipt of an income support payment and could not have reasonably known they would require income support at the time the distribution or dividend was paid, or
  • the non-attributable stakeholder has been assessed as a Genuine Investor and the distribution paid to the Genuine Investor is commensurate with their investment in the entity, or
  • the distribution has been paid in lieu of wages

For the non-controller the distribution will be assessed as income for 52 weeks, unless it is determined the distribution was either paid periodically or in lieu of wages.

Where the customer is advising the distribution has been paid periodically, refer these cases to the Level 2 Policy Helpdesk.

Where the distribution has not been paid periodically and will be assessed as income for 52 weeks, set a review to update the Trust/Company Distribution (TRD) screen 52 weeks after the distribution. See Resources for an example.

Where a controller ceases involvement with the entity, any distribution (excluding final distribution) received prior to severing ties, is assessed for 52 weeks. Set up a review to update the TRD screen 52 weeks after the distribution. See Resources for an example.

Family Domestic Violence (FDV)

There are special provisions for customers affected by Family Domestic Violence (FDV) and where previous trust or company distributions affect, or will affect, their rate of income support payment (ISP) or entitlement to Low Income Card (LIC).

If the customer disclosed, or there is evidence the customer may be, or has been, experiencing FDV (including FDV in child/parent relationships) and a previous trust or company distribution will/is affecting the rate of their ISP payment or entitlement to LIC?

Is there evidence or information provided by the customer that indicates they:

  • are a non-attributable stakeholder or a private trust or private company, and
  • a previous private trust or company distribution will/is affecting their rate of ISP payment or entitlement to LIC, and
  • do not have access to, or benefit in any way, from any distributed funds due to FDV, and
  • has separated or contact has ended with the perpetrator of violence, such as the family members are no longer living together or a child was affected by FDV. (The customer is not required to be previously partnered to the perpetrator of the FDV to seek an exemption)

Yes, see Table 2

No, go to Item 2

2

Recording Trust/Company distribution amounts + Read more ...

Go to the Trust/Company Distribution (TRD) screen and code the following fields:

  • Event Date: record the date of the resolution to distribute
    • if the resolution to distribute states a percentage and not a specific amount, the beneficiary's legal entitlement to a distribution only commences from the date the amount is known. This is usually when the tax return is completed. The date the amount is known is the date of event
    • if the resolution declares to distribute a specific amount for a beneficiary but the trust tax return has not been lodged at the time of the resolution, the date of event is the date the resolution was made, not the date the tax return was lodged
  • Amount: record the amount of the distribution in whole dollars
    Note: if distributions have not ceased, but no distribution has been paid for a particular financial year, $0 should be recorded in order to ensure a review is triggered correctly for the following financial year's returns and financial statements
  • CRN: Code the Customer Reference Number (CRN). The distribution amount will pass through to that customer's record and display on the Trust/Company Income/ Assets Details (TRCIAS) screen in the customers record. If the customer is also a controller of the trust/company only the distribution amount in excess of their attributed income will be treated as distribution income
    Note: where the distribution is made to a child and that child has a CRN because their parent may be receiving family assistance, do not record the child's CRN
  • Gen INV: or Deprivation:
    • If the distribution was made to a genuine investor, code 'GNI'
    • If the distribution was made during the 1999/2000 year, code 'CON'
    • If either 'GNI' or 'CON' have been recorded in the Gen INV: or Deprivation: field this amount will not be treated as being deprivation/gift of the controllers
  • Distrb Ceased: If the distribution amount is set to zero and the customer has received the final distribution, enter 'Y'. This will help when deciding which trust/company records can be archived and which should be selected for a program review. It is essential to leave this field blank if the customer may receive a distribution in the future
  • Multiple Distributions: If a Trustee resolves to make multiple distributions to a customer within a financial year, each distribution will need to be assessed as income for a period of 52 weeks from the date of the resolution to distribute. For example, if the Trustee resolves to make a distribution of $5,000 on 1 August 2015, a TRD screen will be recorded with a date of event of 1 August 2015 and a distribution amount of $5,000. If the Trustee resolves to make a further distribution of $2,000 on 1 March 2016, a new TRD screen will need to be recorded with a date of event of 1 March 2016 and a distribution amount of $7,000 ($5,000 plus $2,000). If no further distributions are made to the customer, the TRD screen needs to be recorded with a distribution of $2,000 ($7,000 - $5,000) from 30 July 2016 (for example 52 weeks after the resolution to distribute) and a TRD screen with a nil distribution and a date of event of 28 February 2017. A review should be set to follow up any further distributions and update the TRD screen as required, 52 weeks after each distribution

Code a review on the Review Registration (RVR) screen in the trust record:

  • Serv Rsn: TRF
  • Rev Rsn: TDI
  • Due Date: this will be 52 weeks from the date of each distribution
  • Text: include details of updates that need to be made to the trust record, such as follow up any further distributions

This process will normally form part of coding of all an entity's income, assets and attribution details. As such, ensure distribution details are included when completing a DOC outlining the actions taken.

The Trust/Company Distribution Summary Screen (TRDS) will display:

  • the event date
  • the total distributions to non-genuine investors or non-concession amounts
  • the total distributions to genuine investors or concession amounts

4

Work Bonus income percentage to be updated + Read more ...

An update to the Work Bonus income percentage is needed when:

  • the trust or company is assessed for the first time
  • a percentage has not been previous recorded
  • a change in income earned from the customer's personal exertion has been identified. For example, the trust or company now has an employee who is undertaking work previously done by the customer

Is an update required?

  • Yes:
    • navigate to the Trust and Companies Income and Assets Summary (TRCIAS) screen
    • select the entity by entering 'S' in the field next to the entity name and press enter
    • enter the customer and partner’s (if applicable) Work Bonus Income % as advised by the customer
    • enter the Work Bonus Event Date – this is the date the Work Bonus income % applies from. The date cannot be before the first instance of income from the trust or company for the customer
    • enter the source and date of receipt
    • Note: the TRCIAS screen is able to be updated outside of any other outstanding activities on the customer’s record (for example new claims)
  • No, go to Item 5

5

Trust has been vested and a beneficiary only received a distribution within the last twelve months + Read more ...

Any income distributions to customers, who are only beneficiaries of the trust, will need to be maintained as income for a period of 52 weeks from the date the trustee resolved to make the distribution. This may mean the trust cannot be coded as being vested until the distributions cease to be assessable.

The trust income, assets, liabilities and attribution should all be set to nil but any distributions to beneficiaries only will be coded/remain on the TRD screen until they cease to be assessable.

Code a review on the RVR in the trust record:

  • ServRsn: TRF
  • RevRsn: TDI
  • Due Date: this will be 52 weeks from the date of the resolution to distribute
  • Text: include details of updates that need to be made to the trust record, such as reducing distributions to nil and coding the ‘Wound Up’ indicator on TRMD

Family Domestic Violence (FDV) + Read more ...

There are special provisions for customers experiencing Family Domestic Violence (FDV) where previous trust or company distributions affect their rate of income support payment (ISP) or entitlement to Low Income Card (LIC).

In certain circumstances, Department of Social Services (DSS) may grant an exemption for distribution/divided income. Complex Assessment Officers (CAO's) refer applications for an exemption to the Means Test Helpdesk for assessment.

  • the exemption can only apply where the customer is not an attributable stakeholder of the entity.
    If the CAO has assessed the customer as an attributable stakeholder, procedure ends here
  • in cases where the FDV involves a partner, the customer and partner must be separated for the exemption to apply.
    If the customer remains partnered, procedure ends here

The following information is required:

  • consent from the customer to be referred to a social worker to obtain FDV verification
  • explicit authority from the customer for DSS to access their record, go to Step 2
  • confirmation the customer is affected by FDV, go to Step 3
  • details of distribution/dividend income assessed per Table 1

When the required information is gathered, go to Step 4

2

Explicit authority from the customer to provide information to DSS, and for DSS to access their record + Read more ...

To apply for the exemption, the customer needs to provide written authority to allow Services Australia to provide information, and allow DSS to access their record to assess an exemption. If for any reason written authority cannot be obtained, get verbal consent.

Suggested wording: 'I <customers name>, consent to Services Australia providing information to the Department of Social Services (DSS), and consent to DSS accessing my Centrelink record for the purposes of making an assessment regarding my trust/company income distribution/dividend'

Social workers identifying a customer may be eligible for exemption must request written authority when referring to a CAO. Go to Step 3.

Where a CAO identifies the customer may be eligible for the exemption, they must discuss the requirement with the customer and advise they will be referred to a social worker. Go to Step 3

3

Confirmation that customer is affected by FDV + Read more ...

Complex Assessment Officers referral to social worker:

A social worker assessment is needed to assess the customer circumstances to determine if they are affected by FDV.

To refer the assessment, use Fast Note - select Auto text > Complex Assessment > Request for CAO action > CAO seeking FDV assessment.

Hold the referral pending social worker response. The social worker will assess the circumstances and advise of the outcome via email, or contact the CAO and their Team Leader.

Has FDV been confirmed?

  • Yes, continue with assessment
  • No, procedure ends here

Referral received from a Service Officer or social worker identifies customer impacted by distribution/dividend income:

Where a current CAO assessment exists and the customer is impacted by distribution or dividend income and a social worker confirms the customer is impacted by FDV, the social worker will run the Fast Note workflow - select Auto text > Complex Assessment > Request for CAO action > Social Worker FDV assessment for CAO

4

Referral to Means Test + Read more ...

Department of Social Services (DSS) make the decision to disregard trust or company distributions.

CAO's email the referral to the Income Support Means Test team to request an exemption from DSS. The email is to include the following information:

  • customer details including name and CRN
  • trust/company details, including the name of the trust/company
  • the amount and timing (date) of the distribution(s) or dividend(s) currently being/to be assessed
  • confirmation the customer is impacted by FDV and is separated (if applicable)
  • confirmation explicit authority for DSS has been provided. If this could not be in writing, advise why

CAO to DOC the record to confirm:

  • the outcome of the social worker assessment
  • referral made to DSS through the income and assets means test for a decision on disregarding trust or company distribution

CAO assessment:

  • for non-new claim assessment, hold CAO referral pending DSS outcome
  • for new claims, continue with the CAO assessment, unless
    • the distributions will REJ INC, or
    • the income precludes payment, hold referral pending DSS outcome

When the assessment has been returned by DSS and the Means Test team, go to Step 5

5

DSS exemption decision + Read more ...

Has DSS granted an exemption?

  • Yes, document the decision. Go to Step 6
  • No, document the decision and tell the customer of the outcome. See Table 1 to assess the distributions

6

Exemption granted + Read more ...

For assessments where a distribution is recorded:

  • zero the dividend/distribution income
  • use the earliest date of event DSS advised the income can be disregarded
  • check any arrears are paid as appropriate

For assessments where the distribution is not yet recorded, record details of the exemption in the CAO report.

In both instances, the CAO is required to contact the customer to advise:

  • the exemption has been granted
  • the dates the exemption is applicable
  • requirements to notify of change in circumstances. For example, the exemption will cease if the customer reconciles with their partner or can access the funds from the entity
