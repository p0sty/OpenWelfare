Different eligibility criteria apply depending on a customer’s period of self-isolation, quarantine or caring. Confirm the period the customer is claiming before assessing their claim.

Customers can claim online again from 8am Wednesday 20 July 2022. If a customer calls, tell them to visit https://www.servicesaustralia.gov.au/pandemic-leave-disaster-payment for how to claim online. If a customer does not have access to online services, they can claim over the phone using Assisted Customer Claim (ACC) from 9am on the same date.

There are different claiming periods depending on the self-isolation, quarantine or caring period claimed. See the Resources page.

If a customer has lodged a claim online or using ACC that has not yet been finalised, they cannot lodge another one. If a customer calls to ask why they cannot lodge more than 1 claim, tell them:
‘You can lodge your next claim once you have received confirmation from Services Australia on the claim outcome of your previous claim.’

Reminder: more than one Flexible Support Payment (including Pandemic Leave Disaster Payment) cannot be paid on the same day. See Processing Pandemic Leave Disaster Payment claims in Process Direct.

  • they were unable to work from home
  • the 7 day period, starts on or after 18 January 2022

  • they are not getting on any day of the 7 day period:

2

Eligibility for a second payment after the end of the first 7 day period

Customers who have tested positive with COVID-19 or caring for a person who has tested positive with COVID-19 + Read more ...

Customers:

  • can only make one claim for each 7 day claim period
  • may be eligible to get a second payment after their first 7 day period of self-isolation, quarantine or caring ends

For the first 7 day claim period, customers must meet all eligibility criteria for at least one day in that 7 day period.

If a customer continues to self-isolate, quarantine or care for someone for more than the first 7 day claim period, they may be eligible to get another Pandemic Leave Disaster Payment. This period:

  • must directly follow the first 7 day claim period, and
  • does not need to be for a full 7 days

To be eligible for a second or subsequent Pandemic Leave Disaster Payment for a period of self-isolation or quarantine that goes past the first 7 day claim period, the customer must:

  • lodge a new claim, and
  • meet all eligibility criteria for the new claim period, including having new evidence, if requested, to support their claim

If the customer is claiming under the same impact reason of testing positive or is caring for the same person who tested positive, they cannot use the same evidence from the previous claim. They must have new evidence of a medical certificate or letter from an appropriate health professional that outlines the customer or the person they are caring for is still needs to isolate in the current period claimed because of the effects of COVID-19.

Customers:

  • must self-declare they have this evidence, and
  • do not need to provide this evidence, unless:
    • there are concerns their claim may not be valid or genuine, or
    • they are lodging a fifth or subsequent claim and 4 or more claims have already been paid

Note: statutory declarations will not be accepted as evidence to support a claim.

Customers can also be eligible for additional periods of self-isolation or quarantine or caring for a period that starts at a later time than the first period.

If a customer needs to self-isolate, quarantine or care for another person for more than 7 days and this period has:

  • already ended, and the customer meets all other eligibility criteria:
    • more than one 7 day payment can be granted
    • customers can only be granted one payment on any day, as 2 or more payments cannot be released on the same day for any customer
    • staff must complete the assessment of the second payment, but cannot finalise the claim with a grant outcome until the next day
  • not yet ended, and the customer meets all other eligibility criteria:
    • one 7 day payment can be granted
    • to test their eligibility for another payment, the customer will need to lodge another claim on the first day (or later) of the next period of their self-isolation, quarantine or caring period

See the:

  • multiple claim scenarios in Scenarios from 18 January 2022
  • Scenarios – policy to apply

Customers who are a close contact or caring for a person who was a close contact + Read more ...

Customers can make 1 x 7 day claim even if the self-isolation, quarantine or caring period is greater than 7 days.

The customer can lodge a new claim for another period of 7 days if they can claim under a new impact reason. That is they:

  • or the person in their care tests positive with COVID-19,
  • start caring for a different person (who is a close contact of a person who has tested positive with COVID-19 or who tests positive with COVID-19), or
  • or the person in their care becomes a close contact of a different person who tests positive with COVID-19

Customers will need to self-declare:

  • the new claim reason, and
  • they have new evidence to support the new claim period

Customers do not need to provide this evidence, unless:

  • there are concerns their claim may not be valid or genuine, or
  • they are lodging a fifth or subsequent claim and 4 or more claims have already been paid

Note: statutory declarations will not be accepted as evidence to support a claim.

For claims lodged on or after 27 April 2022 and 8 May 2022, the definition of close contact and selections in the online claim have been updated. See Close contact

See the state or territory details for what is a ‘required authority’.

Informed means a required authority has contacted a customer directly by:

  • phone
  • SMS
  • letter, or
  • email

Customers caring for someone who has tested positive with COVID-19 do not need to confirm a required authority has informed them to self-isolate or quarantine.

If a customer receives a generic letter from a required authority that does not include their residential address, that letter must be followed by a personal instruction to that customer.

3

Working from home + Read more ...

A customer’s home may be considered their workplace for the purposes of meeting the ‘unable to attend work, and therefore unable to earn an income’ criteria. This may be the case if, for example, a customer:

  • has tested positive with COVID-19,
  • usually works from home, and
  • is too unwell to work (earn an income)

If a customer is not unwell from the effects of COVID-19, staff must use careful questioning to decide why a customer is unable to earn an income.

4

Close contact

Self-isolation, quarantine or caring period starts on or after 18 January 2022 + Read more ...

The following applies for self-isolation, quarantine or caring periods that start on or after 18 January 2022, if claiming under one of the following criteria:

  • unable to attend work, and are therefore unable to earn an income, as they:
    • are required or recommended to self-isolate as they are a close contact of a person who tested positive with COVID-19
    • are caring for a child (16 years or under) who is required or recommended to self-isolate as they are a close contact of a person who tested positive with COVID-19
    • are caring for a person who is required or recommended to self-isolate as they are a close contact of a person who has tested positive with COVID-19, and the person the customer is caring for has a disability or severe medical condition

To meet the definition of ‘close contact’ a person must:

  • usually live in the same household as a positive COVID-19 case during their infectious period, or
  • have stayed in the same household for more than 4 hours as a positive COVID-19 case during their infectious period

An infectious period may be up to 3 days before a person experiences symptoms of COVID-19.

This definition has been set by the Australian government.

For claims lodged on or after 27 April 2022

Customers can make a self-declaration that they meet either:

  • the Australian Government definition of close contact, or
  • the close contact definition in their state or territory

Staff do not need to

  • check which definition is met
  • refer to state or territory close contact definitions, or
  • find out if a customer meets a state or territory close contact definition.

For claims lodged on or after 8 May 2022

The online claim has been updated to direct customers to check their state or territory government requirements regarding self-isolation and quarantine rules. This is due to recent changes some states and territories have made to close contact rules.

Options to select in the online claim around the need to self-isolate for close contact impact reasons have been updated to include

  • a person’s state or territory government requires them to isolate, or
  • a person’s employer has directed them not to attend their workplace even though their state or territory government does not require them to self-isolate.

Staff do not need to

  • check state or territory government rules in relation to requirements to self-isolate or quarantine
  • find out if a customer should or should not self-isolate or quarantine
    • under their state or territory government rules, or
    • due to being directed by their employer not to attend work

Customers do not need to have received a direct instruction from a health official to self-isolate or quarantine. While this is one form of evidence a customer may have, and can provide if requested, it is not a requirement to meet the eligibility criteria.

If the customer has received a direct instruction from a health official to self-isolate or quarantine, this would need to outline that the customer meets the definition of close contact (above) set by the Australian government. A state or territory's definition of ‘close contact’ may not align with this definition. The customer will not be eligible unless they meet the Australian Government’s definition of ‘close contact’.

If assessing a claim where a customer has been paid for a 7 day claiming period immediately before for the same impact reason, see Eligibility for a second payment after the end of the first 7 day period.

Customers:

  • must have evidence, if requested, of a positive test result and the address of the COVID-19 positive case for whom they or the person they are caring for are a close contact
    • Positive test result means, the person who caused the close contact status has:
      - a positive test result for COVID-19 from a testing centre (this includes pathology centres) or health professional (that is, evidence of a positive Polymerase Chain Reaction (PCR) test), or

Assessing insufficient appropriate leave entitlements + Read more ...

A customer is considered to have insufficient appropriate leave entitlements, if they:

  • have accessed all appropriate leave (from all employers) and have no appropriate leave remaining
  • do not have enough days of appropriate leave to cover the self-isolation, quarantine or caring period they are claiming for, or
  • have appropriate leave, but the employer cannot pay appropriate leave entitlements due to financial hardship

Appropriate leave includes:

  • sick leave (if the customer can access this from their employer due to testing positive with COVID-19)
  • leave for the purposes of caring for another person, that is, personal leave or carer's leave (if the customer is caring for another person who has tested positive with COVID-19 or is caring for another person who has to self-isolate or quarantine)
  • pandemic leave

Any other leave type is not appropriate leave. For example, annual leave. Customers do not need to use up annual leave to get the Pandemic Leave Disaster Payment.

This means a customer will not meet the eligibility criteria if they have appropriate leave entitlements that cover the full period they are claiming.

To receive the Pandemic Leave Disaster Payment a customer must have been likely to have worked for at least 8 hours or one full day during the period of self-isolation, quarantine or caring they are claiming.

One full day of work does not need to be at least 8 hours.

For example: a customer normally works a 3 hour shift and cannot attend that shift because they are self-isolating or quarantining, or caring for another person.

The customer meets this eligibility criteria as they have lost one full day of work in the period they are claiming.

If a customer does not lose one full day of work, they must lose at least 8 hours of work in the period they are claiming.

For example:

  • A customer normally works 1 x 10 hour shift. They work 5 hours of that shift. The customer does not meet this criteria as they have not lost at least 8 hours of work or one full day of work in the period they are claiming
  • A customer normally works 2 x 10 hours shifts. They work 5 hours each day of those 2 shifts. The customer does meet this criteria as they have lost 10 hours of work in the period they are claiming
