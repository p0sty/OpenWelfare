Energy Bill Relief Fund enquiry

Step

Action

1

Customer contacts in relation to the Energy Bill Relief Fund + Read more ...

Has the customer contacted in relation to:

  • Letter received from Services Australia inviting them to log on to their Centrelink online account via myGov to provide consent. Go to Step 2
  • Not receiving the rebate after completing the Energy Bill Relief Consent workflow. Go to Step 6
  • Editing consent to the energy bill relief. Go to Step 7
  • Withdrawing consent to the energy bill relief. Go to Step 8
  • Eligibility for the rebate and has not received a letter from Services Australia, or is a DVA customer only:
    • Tell the customer they will need to contact their electricity retailer to discuss their individual circumstances
    • For general information on eligibility direct the customer to energy.gov.au > Energy Bill Relief Fund
    • Procedure ends here

2

Customer received a letter from Services Australia + Read more ...

In September 2023, Services Australia wrote to Centrelink customers inviting them to provide consent by completing the Energy Bill Relief Consent workflow.

Is the customer able to complete the Energy Bill Relief Consent workflow via self service?

  • Yes:
    • Confirm the customer is the account holder or named on the electricity account. The customer will also need their electricity bill (digital or printed) details to complete the workflow
    • Tell the customer to complete the Energy Bill Relief Consent workflow via Menu > My details > Personal and contact details > Energy Bill Relief Consent workflow
    • The workflow will provide a link to energy.gov.au > Energy Bill Relief Fund for additional information
    • Procedure ends here
  • No, customer can choose to provide consent:
    • with the assistance of a Service Officer, go to Step 3
    • directly to their electricity retailer. Procedure ends here

3

State/territory energy concession recipients + Read more ...

Does the customer receive an existing state/territory energy concession or rebate?

  • Yes, customer does not need to complete the Energy Bill Relief Consent workflow, even if they received a letter. Their electricity retailer will automatically apply the rebate. Procedure ends here
  • No, and the customer advises they:
    • meet the eligibility criteria for their state/territory energy concession. Tell the customer to contact their electricity retailer, who will automatically apply the Energy Bill Relief Fund if eligible. Tell the customer to go to energy.gov.au for further information. Procedure ends here
    • do not meet the eligibility criteria for their state/territory energy concession. Go to Step 4

4

Before starting the Energy Bill Relief Consent workflow + Read more ...

Queensland and Western Australia residents

Customers residing in Western Australia or Queensland do not need to complete the Energy Bill Relief Consent workflow. Electricity retailers in Western Australia and Queensland will automatically apply rebates to eligible households.

Check customer's address

The workflow will pre-populate the customer’s legal name and home address. If the customer's address needs updating, use the Change in Contact Details workflow before starting the workflow. See Updating address details.

Make sure the customer:

  • is the account holder or named on the account
  • has their electricity bill with them
  • consents to share their details electronically

Does the customer meet the above criteria?

  • Yes, go to Step 5
  • No, the customer:
    • is not the electricity account holder or named on the account. Tell the customer to go to energy.gov.au for further information. Procedure ends here
    • does not have their electricity bill (digital or printed) with them. Tell the customer this is required to provide consent. Procedure ends here
    • does not consent to share their details electronically. Tell the customer to contact their electricity retailer to ask about the rebate. Procedure ends here

5

Energy Bill Relief Consent workflow + Read more ...

In Process Direct:

  • Key EBRF into the Super Key
  • The Energy Bill Relief Consent page will display
  • Select Provide Consent. The Centrelink Confirmation eService (CCeS) message will then be presented. This must be read as is to the customer
  • Does the customer consent to sharing details with their electricity retailer after hearing the CCeS message?
    • Yes, select Confirm
    • No, select Cancel. Tell the customer they will need to contact their electricity retailer directly. Procedure ends here
  • Use the customer's electricity bill to input the electricity retailer's ABN, select Search ABN
  • Is there an ABN match with the customer's electricity retailer?
    • Yes, select the customer's Electricity Retailer Name from the dropdown menu
    • No, if an ABN is entered that is not recognised, an error message will display ‘ABN entered is either invalid or not registered with us.’ The consent workflow cannot be completed. The electricity retailer may not be registered for Centrelink Confirmation eServices (CCeS). See the Resources page for a list of registered ABNs. Alternatively, the customer may be in an embedded network. Direct the customer to energy.gov.au for more information. Procedure ends here.
      Note: embedded networks are private electricity networks that serve multiple customer premises (or lots) within a building or self-contained site (such as a caravan park, apartment building or retirement home or village).
      An error message will appear in the ABN field if an ABN is entered incorrectly and Save is selected. Enter the correct ABN, select the electricity retailer name from the dropdown menu and select Save. The error icon will remain on the ABN field but the electricity retailer details and consent will save successfully
  • Use the customer's electricity bill to input the customer's account number (if known). Confirm the electricity retailer details are correct and select Save:
    • A message will present showing ‘Consent Details saved successfully’. A Note will be automatically generated detailing the electricity retailer's details and the customer’s consent
    • Tell the customer their consent and details will be sent to their electricity retailer
    • Direct the customer to energy.gov.au, if they have any questions about rebate amounts or when to expect the rebate. Customers should be encouraged to wait until they receive their next electricity bill to see if their retailer has applied the rebate
  • Procedure ends here

6

Energy Bill Relief Fund rebate not received + Read more ...

If a customer contacts in relation to not receiving the Energy Bill Relief Fund rebate after completing the Energy Bill Relief Consent workflow, check the electricity retailer's details are correct.

Are the details in the Energy Bill Relief Consent Note correct?

  • Yes:
    • Tell the customer to go to energy.gov.au > Energy Bill Relief Fund for households to check the timeframes for their state or territory. Customers should be encouraged to wait until they receive their next electricity bill to see if their retailer has applied the rebate
    • Procedure ends here
  • No:
    • Edit consent, go to Step 7
    • Withdraw consent, go to Step 8

7

Edit consent + Read more ...

Customer contacts and wants to change their electricity retailer's details, or they previously provided incorrect details.

In Process Direct:

  • Key EBRF into the Super Key
  • Select the Energy Bill Relief Consent workflow from the dropdown menu

Is the Edit details option available and the customer wants to change their electricity retailer’s details?

  • Yes, the customer details can be edited:
    • Select Edit details and update the customer’s electricity retailer details. Select Save
    • A message will present showing ‘Consent Details saved successfully'. A Note will be automatically generated detailing the electricity retailer’s details and the customer’s consent
    • Tell the customer their updated consent and details will be sent to their electricity retailer
    • If the customer has any questions about the rebate, direct them to energy.gov.au
    • Procedure ends here
  • No:
    • The Energy Bill Relief Consent workflow will need to be re-run to withdraw consent
    • Complete the Energy Bill Relief Consent workflow again to provide new consent

8

Withdraw consent + Read more ...

Customers can withdraw their consent at any time.

Is the customer able to complete the Energy Bill Relief Consent workflow via self service?

  • Yes:
    • Tell the customer to complete the Energy Bill Relief Consent workflow via Menu > My details > Personal and contact details > Energy Bill Relief Consent workflow
    • Select Manage consent to begin the withdraw consent workflow
    • Procedure ends here
  • No, customer can choose to withdraw consent:
    • with the assistance of a Service Officer via Process Direct. Go to Step 9
    • directly with their electricity retailer. Procedure ends here

9

Process Direct - withdrawing consent + Read more ...

In Process Direct:

  • Key EBRF into the Super Key
  • Select the Energy Bill Relief Consent workflow from the dropdown menu

Does the customer agree to withdrawing consent?

  • Yes:
    • In Consent History, confirm the electricity retailer name and select Withdraw Consent
    • The Consent Message will then be presented. This must be read as is to the customer
    • Select Confirm. A Note will be automatically generated detailing the electricity retailer’s details and the customer’s consent
  • No:
    • Select Cancel
    • Tell the customer they will need to contact their electricity retailer directly to withdraw their consent
    • Annotate the Note that the customer contacted but did not proceed with withdrawing consent
    • Procedure ends here

Note: if the customer wishes to provide updated consent details, re-run the Energy Bill Relief Consent workflow again to record new consent. See Step 4.
