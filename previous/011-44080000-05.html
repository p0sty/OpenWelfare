Medicare entitlement types and eligibility for LHC letter

This table outlines Medicare entitlement types, eligibility to obtain an LHC letter and date applicable. An applicant can request a lifetime Health cover letter if they are registered for full Medicare benefits

Medicare entitlement

Migrants (permanent resident visa)
Previously enrolled in Medicare

Yes

Initial date of registration (processing date)

Note: if a migrant has been absent from Australia, their initial registration date (processing date) in Medicare remains their LHC date. This cannot be changed.

For those who were enrolled in Medicare before 1 April 2007, the Medicare entitlement start date is to be used.

They will need to obtain an International Movement record (IMR) from the Department of Home Affairs. An IMR provides a person’s entry and exits from Australia. They will need to give the IMR to their private health fund to have their LHC loading reassessed.

Previously enrolled in Medicare

Yes

Where an Australian citizen has been absent from Australia, their initial registration (processing date) in Medicare remains their LHC date. This cannot be changed.

Note: for those who are enrolled in Medicare before 1 April 2007, the Medicare entitlement date is to be used.

Prior to the Consumer Directory Maintenance System (CDMS), when a newborn was added to a family Medicare card their entitlement date would be the same as the parent. This date could be prior to their date of birth. In these instances the Medicare registration date will be their date of birth.

They will need to obtain an International Movement record (IMR) from the Department of Home Affairs. An IMR provides a person’s entry and exits from Australia. Once an IMR is received they will need to provide to their private health fund.

See:

Australian citizens

Previously enrolled in Medicare and have not been absent from Australia

No

The LHC letter is not relevant to Australian citizens who have been previously enrolled and have not been absent from Australia.

Norfolk Island residents

Yes

On 1 July 2016, residents of Norfolk Island became eligible for Medicare benefits and therefore became able to purchase Australian private health insurance policies.

Norfolk Island residents who were aged over 31 at 1 July 2016 had until 30 June 2017 to purchase insurance without incurring an LHC loading. If they purchased from or after 1 July 2017, a LHC loading applies.

Younger residents will have until their normal LHC base day of 1 July following their 31st birthday to purchase insurance without incurring a LHC loading.
