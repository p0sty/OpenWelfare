Assessing a permanent blindness claim

Step

Action

1

Is the customer getting Disability Support Pension (DSP) or Age Pension? + Read more ...

  • Yes, if a Request for Ophthalmologist/Optometrist Report (SA013) or equivalent information has not been received, issue the form using the Request for Information workflow. See Resources for a link to the form. Go to Step 3
  • No, go to Step 2

2

Has the customer lodged a claim for DSP or Age? + Read more ...

  • Yes, go to Step 3
  • No,
    • Advise the customer no further action can be undertaken until a claim has been lodged
      Claiming DSP
      Claiming and re-claiming Age Pension
    • Discuss the importance of providing an SA013 or similar from their ophthalmologist.

Also see:

  • Eligibility for DSP
  • Qualification for Age Pension (Blind)

Procedure ends here.

3

Customer provides a completed SA013 or other acceptable report + Read more ...

An SA013 is preferred. However, any report which provides the information requested by the SA013 is acceptable.

An optometrist can complete the report but it must clearly state it is supported by a report from the treating ophthalmologist and provides details about that ophthalmologist report.

4

Assess permanent blindness + Read more ...

To be assessed as permanently blind, the customer must have one of the following:

  • Visual acuity as measured on the Snellen Scale after correction by suitable lenses of less than 6/60 in both eyes. See the Resources page for information on whether other ratings on the Snellen Scale qualify a customer as permanently blind
  • A field of vision constricted to 10 degrees or less of arc around central fixation in the better eye, irrespective of corrected visual acuity (equivalent to 1/100 white test object), or
  • A combination of visual defects resulting in the same degree of visual impairment as described in the two points above

Does the customer clearly meet the necessary criteria for permanent blindness?

  • Yes, permanent blindness is coded on the Pensions Disability Information (PDI) screen. DSP Processing Smart Centre staff assess the SA013:
    • Granting Disability Support Pension (DSP) (manifest), or
    • Transferring to Disability Support Pension (DSP) (Blind), or
    • Assessing Age Pension (Blind) claims, or
    • Age Pension (Blind) transfer provisions

Procedure ends here

  • No, go to Step 5

5

Customer does not meet criteria for permanent blindness + Read more ...

Does the SA013/report indicate the customer clearly does not meet the criteria for permanent blindness?

  • If the customer clearly does not meet the criteria, go to Step 6
  • If it is possible, on further assessment, the customer may meet medical criteria for DSP, go to Step 7

6

Customer is not considered permanently blind + Read more ...

Is the customer current on DSP or Age?

  • Yes, a Q999 must be issued advising the customer they do not meet the criteria for blind pension and their payment will continue as is. Include the Appeal paragraph in the letter. Create a DOC detailing the outcome and the reason for the decision (see Resources for DOC text). Procedure ends here
  • No, go to Step 7

7

Customer is under Age Pension age and may be eligible for DSP + Read more ...

If the customer has lodged a DSP claim, it is assigned to the Medical Assessment Team (MAT). See Medical Assessment Team (MAT).

The Assessor advises one of the following:

  • manifest medical eligibility criteria is met, including permanent blindness. See Disability Support Pension (DSP) manifest eligibility
  • the customer is manifestly medically ineligible
  • insufficient medical evidence prevents assessment of the customer's medical condition/s and functional impacts
  • there are doubts about the medical evidence authenticity. Note: MAT or the Health Professional Advisory Unit (HPAU) must investigate further by contacting health professionals listed in the evidence to confirm the diagnosis and prognosis
  • a current and valid assessment can be used for the claim determination, or a Job Capacity Assessment (JCA) referral is required to determine medical eligibility
