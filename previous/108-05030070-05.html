Example of PPL claim spanning 2 financial years

Table 2

Item

Description

1

PPL claim spanning across 2 years

Jane lodges a Parental Leave Pay (PPL) pre-birth claim on 1 May 2022 for a child with an expected date of birth of 28 June 2022. In this claim, Jane provides previous year income details (2020-21 financial year). Jane's claim is assessed PPL/ASS-PCB, pending proof of the child's birth.

Jane delivers a healthy baby on 4 July 2022.

On 22 July 2022, Jane completes the Add Newborn mobile application service to provide proof of the child's birth and finalise the claim for PPL.

Although Jane delivered the child in the 2022/23 financial year, there is no requirement to provide additional income details for 2021/22.

Note: any incidences of the system requesting previous financial year income in scenarios mirroring the above circumstances must be reported to ICT for further investigation.

Determining correct financial year for DAP income test for a child born or adopted before 1 July 2023

Table 3

Example

Description

1

Examples of when DAP is rejected due to income

If the claim was rejected due to income the customer may request a change to their nominated start date.

  • if the new nominated start date is before the date of claim and in a new financial year, then the Income Test is assessed on the financial year based on the new nominated start date
  • if the new nominated start date is after the date of claim and in the same financial year as the date of claim, then the Income Test is assessed on the financial year based on the date of claim

Note: income assessment is based on the financial year before the date of claim or nominated start date (whichever is earlier).

2

Marcus

Marcus' child was born on 1 June 2022. Marcus lodged a DAP claim on 30 July 2022 and advised a nominated start date of 15 July 2022.

The claim has been rejected as Marcus' income in the 2011/2022 financial year is $162,000.

Marcus decides to change the nominated start date to 15 June 2022. The DAP claim is now assessed on adjusted taxable income in the 2020/21 financial year as the new nominated start date is before the date of claim and is now assessed on the financial year based on the new nominated start date.

3

Antonio

Antonio has lodged a claim for DAP on 23 October 2021 for a child born on 2 January 2021. Antonio has nominated the DAP period to start on 18 August 2021. Antonio's adjusted taxable income for 2020/21 financial year is $158,000.

As Antonio's income is too high to be eligible for DAP, a new nominated start date of 10 January 2021 is advised. The income for the DAP claim is now assessed on the 2019/20 financial year.

4

Robert

Robert's child came into care on 4 March 2022. A DAP claim was lodged on 3 July 2022 with a nominated start date of 16 May 2022.

Robert's income for 2020/2021 is over the income limit to be eligible and so the nominated start date is changed to 21 December 2022.

The income for the DAP claim is now assessed on the 2021/2022 financial year.

5

Giovanni

Giovanni's child is born on 6 September 2021 and a claim for DAP is lodged on the same day. The nominated start date is 30 September 2021. Giovanni's income is $157,000 for the 2020/21 financial year.

Giovanni will be working and/or receiving paid leave for the next 12 months and therefore is unable to change the nominated start date.

Giovanni is not eligible for DAP. The claim is rejected.

6

Marly

Marly lodged a claim for DAP on 2 July 2022 with a nominated start date of 21 December 2022 for a child who came into care on 2 July 2022. Marly's adjusted taxable income is $178,000; the claim will reject due to income.

Marly decides to change the nominated start date to 14 May 2023 when more unpaid leave will be taken.

As Marly's nominated start date is still in the same financial year as the original nominated start date and not before the date of claim, the income is still based on 2021/22 financial year and the claim will be rejected.
