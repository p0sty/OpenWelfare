Description

Customer advises excess income when applying for advance

Franklin is on JobSeeker Payment (JSP) and contacts by phone to request an advance payment of $500. Franklin is on weekly payments due to a history of requesting urgent payments for food, medication or accommodation.

Franklin's weekly net rate of payment is $248.50 as there are no deductions. Franklin advises that at the end of each fortnight there is $380.00 left.

The Service Officer should ask Franklin:

  • how much money is spent on accommodation
  • how much money is spent on food
  • how much money is spent on medication

FAQ - Applications for advance payments

Table 2

Item

Description

1

Question: How can customers apply for an advance payment?

Answer: Generally, eligible customers are able to apply for an advance payment via any self service channel, over the telephone or by visiting their local service centre. There are some restrictions on which customers can use self service.

Customers who apply for an advance payment in the service centre or via self service channels will be issued with either a receipt number only or an immediate printed receipt number and information about the decision that has been made at the time of contact. Smart Centre Service Officers are able to issue a verbal receipt number to the customer.

2

Question: Can a partner apply for an advance payment on behalf of a customer?

Answer: No, unless they are the customer's nominee. A partner, although they may have permission to enquire, is not allowed to claim an advance payment on behalf of the customer.

3

Question: How can 'at risk' customers and customers with a nominee arrangement in place apply for an advance payment?

Answer: For Social Security entitlement advances, 'at risk' customers are customers who are independent Youth Allowance (YA) or independent ABSTUDY recipients and are aged under 18 years.

'At risk' customers applying for a Social Security entitlement advance must have a face-to-face interview when applying (except if rural and/or remote). A thorough assessment of the customer's individual circumstances is needed before the advance is assessed.

Customers must be made fully aware of the impacts of taking the offer an advance payment.

Rurally isolated customers are those who live in rural or remote areas who live more than 90 minutes from a service centre and have no access to a service centre.

Customers with a nominee are not able to use self service channels to apply for an advance payment and must contact a Service Officer at either a service centre or Smart Centre.

4

Question: How can weekly payment customers apply for an advance payment?

Answer: Weekly payment customers can apply for an advance payment via either Smart Centre Call or face to face. Weekly payment customers cannot use self service channels.

Note: the information currently displayed in the advance payment workflow about weekly payment customers needing face to face contact to request an advance payment is incorrect.

These customers also need a thorough investigation of their circumstances and ability to repay without placing themselves in financial hardship.

5

Question: Can income managed customers apply for an advance payment?

Answer: Yes, customers whose payments are income managed can apply for an advance payment. However, 100% of the funds will be income managed so will not be paid into the customer's nominated bank account. The funds will be available the next day in the customer's Income Management account. They can be accessed by the customer, through their BasicsCard or Third Party Organisation allocations.

Note: there is no one day or 2 day delivery based on the payment type the customer is receiving. Funds are credited with the nightly payment strip and available in the Income Management account the next day. This means customers who are granted an advance on a Friday can access these funds on the weekend using self service options to transfer to their BasicsCard.

FAQ - Tax status, payment options and eligibility

Table 3

Item

Description

1

Question: What is the tax status of an advance payment?

Answer: For tax purposes, advance payments are treated as a 'loan of money'. This applies irrespective of when the advance payment was paid during the financial year. Advance payments do not attract any additional tax liability, as they are not an extra payment but a lump sum payment of the customer's ongoing entitlement.

2

Question: Can the advance be paid as a lump sum or instalments?

Answer: All advances can be paid as a lump sum into the customer's normal bank account.

Social Security entitlement advance payments can also be paid in 2 instalments.

The customer must nominate the instalment amount and the date they need the second instalment to be paid. Staff must code this date in line with current payment processing times.

For pension customers, the instalment date is coded as 2 working days before the date the customer has requested the funds be paid. For non-pension customers, the instalment date is coded as one working day before the date the funds are needed by the customer.

The second instalment must be paid within 6 months of grant.

When a customer elects to have instalments, the recovery rate is calculated on the full amount to be advanced.

3

Question: If an advance has been processed in 2 instalments in error, can this be undone?

Answer: No, it cannot be undone, but the date of delivery of the second instalment can be changed to still deliver the advance in 2 instalments, but over consecutive days. Within the Manage Advances action in Process Direct, select the current advance where the date needs adjusting, and then select Adjust Instalment date from the Action menu to alter the delivery date of the second instalment.

4

Question: If an advance has been processed in error, can this be undone?

Answer: If the advance has been processed due to a fraudulent claim or has been granted on an incorrect record, the advance can be removed by ICT. See, Deletion of advance granted on incorrect record or fraudulently claimed.

5

Question: If a customer calls to request an advance payment (and it has been processed), can the customer contact the agency later the same day to advise they no longer wish to receive the advance and request it be cancelled?

Answer: No. Once the advance has been processed, the decision has been made under legislation to meet the customer's request. The customer should be advised the advance has been processed and payment has been made.

If the advance has been paid in instalments, the delivery date of the second instalment can be changed to deliver the advance in 2 instalments but on consecutive days. Use the modify function within the advance payment workflow to alter the delivery date of the second instalment.

6

Customer has been granted an advance payment on a multiple record

If a multiple record has been identified, see Centrelink customer has multiple Customer Reference Numbers (CRN) and Initial remediation on a multiple or intertwined Centrelink Customer Reference Number (CRN) for details on the action to take to have the record corrected.

If the customer has been granted an advance payment on the incorrect record and the balance is still outstanding, a mySupport request needs to be raised to request the advance payment be moved to the correct CRN. If the advance cannot be moved, then a debt will need to be raised for the outstanding balance.

If the advance is fully repaid, the advance information cannot be moved to the correct record.

FAQ - Debts and repayments of advance payments

Table 4

Item

Description

1

Question: Is a customer entitled to an advance payment if they have a temporarily written off overpayment?

Answer: A temporarily written off overpayments is still a recoverable debt owing to the Commonwealth Government and therefore the customer is not entitled to an advance payment.

The exception to this is for FTB advances, if the debt has been temporarily written off because of an ex-partner non-lodger debt or bankruptcy debt, these debts will not affect their eligibility for an advance payment.

2

Question: Is the customer entitled to an advance payment if they have a permanently written off overpayment?

Answer: If the overpayment has been permanently written off, while the customer still owes the amount to the Commonwealth Government, it is no longer recoverable by means of deductions. This means the customer is eligible for an advance payment.

3

Question: Should a Service Officer grant a customer an advance when they apply with an outstanding debt balance of less than 5 cents?

Answer: Yes. When a customer pays an outstanding balance of a debt but a rounding amount remains outstanding, any advance payment application will automatically reject. Service Officers are allowed in these instances to override the rejection using the advance payment workflow where the customer has an outstanding debt balance of less than 5 cents. This is so the administrative process, which occurs, as the remaining rounding amount will not clear until after the customer’s next pay day, does not disadvantage the customer.

Example: Customer has a debt for $234.67, they attend a Post Office or service centre to pay the full amount but due to rounding can only pay $234.65 (the customer cannot pay more than the amount due). The 0.02 cents outstanding will not clear until the customer's next payday. A Service Officer can manually override the application rejection and process the next advance if the customer meets all the other qualification criteria.

4

Question: If the customer has a Compensation Insurer debt that has not been repaid, can they receive an advance payment?

Answer: This type of debt does not meet the qualification requirements. It should not stop the customer from receiving an advance. The debt is owed to the Commonwealth by the insurer, not the customer.

As the system is unable to identify this type of debt from other debts, the workflow will automatically reject the application. In these cases, the rejection should be overridden.

5

Question: How is an advance payment for Parenting Payment single calculated?

Answer: PPS customers would receive the smallest of the following amounts:

  • The amount requested, OR
  • 6% of the person's annual payment rate, OR
  • $500.00 (legislated maximum amount)

Note: the minimum amount that can be paid is $250. If the lowest amount is less than $250, customer is not eligible for an advance payment.

The annual payment rate is the rate of PPS payable on the last payment delivery day before the person applies for an advance. The rate includes Rent Assistance, Energy Supplement, Pension supplement or Pharmaceutical Allowance and is the rate after the income test has been applied. The rate does not include Remote Area Allowance.

Customer has requested $700 advance payment

Rate of payment is (customer is under Age Pension age and has income):

  • Base rate of payment - $855.00
  • Energy Supplement - $12.00
  • Pension Supplement - $25.20

Total fortnightly payment = $ 892.20 or $23197.20 per annum

Advance payable is the lesser of

  • Amount requested $700,
  • 6% of $23197.20 = $1391.83
  • Legislated maximum amount $500

Customer would be eligible for a maximum advance of $500

Rates as at 1 July 2022.

6

Question: How is an advance payment for JSP, PPP, YA or Austudy calculated?

Answer: JSP, PPP. YA or Austudy customers would receive the lowest of the following amounts

  • The amount requested, or
  • 7% of the persons 'last full fortnightly benefit rate' x 26, or
  • $500.00 (maximum legislated amount)

Note: the minimum amount that can be paid is $250. If the lowest amount is less than $250, customer is not eligible for an advance payment.

The fortnightly benefit rate is the rate of payment payable on the last full payment delivery day before the person applies for an advance. The rate includes Rent Assistance, Energy Supplement, pension supplement or Pharmaceutical Allowance and is the rate after the income test and parental means test has been applied. The rate does not include Remote Area Allowance.

A JSP customer applies for an advance of $350.

The last fortnightly rate of JSP (single customer over 22 with no children) was

  • Base rate of payment - $642.70
  • Rent Assistance - $145.80

Total fortnightly payment $788.50

Advance payable is the lesser of

  • Amount requested $350
  • 7% of $788.50 x 26 ($55.20 x 26) = $1435.20
  • $500 (maximum legislated amount)

Customer would be eligible for an advance of the requested amount $350.

Rates as at 1 July 2022.

FAQ - Repayment of advance payments

Table 5

Item

Description

1

Question: Can a customer repay an outstanding debt to qualify for an advance payment?

Answer: Customers can make a debt repayment via their Centrelink online account using the Money You Owe service. If the repayment is made via Centrelink online account using their debit or credit card, the repayment will be recognised immediately as 'paid' and the customer may apply for a new advance.

Customers may also use Australia Post Billpay, Direct Debit and BPAY® to repay their debt. The customer will not be able to apply for a new advance until Services Australia has received payments made by these methods. It may take from 3 - 7 days to receive these payments.

2

Question: How are repayments calculated?

Answer: The system will generate a repayment amount spread over the next 13 fortnight period.

If the amount of repayment requested by the customer is equal to or greater than the system generated amount, this amount is accepted and updated on the customer's record when the advance payment is being granted.

If the amount of repayment requested by the customer is less than the system generated amount, the customer's advance payment will be rejected, as the customer is unable to afford the repayment.

For customers who may have more than one advance payment in place at a time, each advance payment is recovered separately. If the customer is granted a second or third advance payment while still repaying an earlier one, the amount deducted from their fortnightly payment will increase.

3

Question: Can a customer make extra advance repayments outside of their regular fortnightly repayments?

Answer: A customer may elect to repay an amount/s of an outstanding advance payment at any time. Customers can repay their advance amount using their debit or credit card via their Centrelink online account, or with staff help over the phone.

Customers will also be able to print from their online account an Australia Post Billpay barcode to make repayments at any Australia Post outlet. The barcode will also be available via the Express Plus Centrelink mobile app, but this will need to be saved to the mobile device. Each advance will have its own unique identifier number and a separate barcode will be needed for each advance.

If the repayment is made via Centrelink online account or Express Plus Centrelink mobile app, the repayment will be recognised immediately as 'paid' and the customer may apply for a new advance as long as all other eligibility criteria is met.

For payments made at an Australia Post outlet, if the repayment is made by any payment method other than cheque, the repayment will be immediately recognised as 'paid'. If payment is made by cheque, it can take up to 7 working days for the payment to be finalised.

If the customer is repaying the outstanding amount/s in order to immediately qualify (pending all other criteria) for another advance payment, see Lump sum repayment of advance payment.

Note: customers cannot repay an amount that is greater than the outstanding balance.

4

Question: Customer has transferred to another payment. Why have their repayments stopped?

Answer: If a customer transfers Social Security entitlement payments, for example from Carer Payment to Youth Allowance, a Manual Follow-up (MFU) will trigger the recovery of their outstanding advance payment after 3 weeks. The repayment rate cannot be modified before the review maturing.

If the person is no longer entitled to a Social Security entitlement payment, for example transfers from Carer Payment to Special Benefit and there is an outstanding advance balance, the balance becomes a debt to the Commonwealth.

5

Question: Can a customer have their rate of repayment increased?

Answer: Yes. Customers may request their rate of repayment to be increased. This request will be approved only if the customer will not suffer severe financial hardship. There is no restriction on the amount a staff member can increase the repayment amount to, as long as the customer has the capacity to meet the repayment amount without being placed in hardship.

For further information, see Changes to the repayment rate of an advance payment.

6

Question: How much can a customer increase their rate of repayment to when using online services or Express Plus app?

Answer: The recipient is limited to increasing their repayment amount to double the minimum repayment amount.

Example: If minimum repayment amount is $38.50, the maximum repayment amount can be $77.00.

For further information, see Advance payment options online.

7

Question: Can the customer have their rate of repayment decreased?

Answer: Decreasing the rate of repayment or stopping the repayment should only be considered if:

  • the customer has special and unusual circumstances, and
  • they would suffer severe financial hardship if the repayment rate was not reduced

Note: future applications for an FTB Advance Payment will reject if an outstanding advance of any type has a reduced recovery arrangement in place.

Repayments are not to be decreased on the same day as the advance is granted as the customer must have capacity to make repayments at the standard rate for the advance to be granted.

For further information, see Changes to the repayment rate of an advance payment.

8

Question: Can the customer use online service or Express Plus app to decrease their repayments?

Answer: If a customer has previously requested to increase their rate of repayment above the standard rate, they may reduce their repayment amount to the standard rate. Customers will be able to do this via the digital channels, over the phone, or at a service centre.

For further information, see Advance payment options online.
