Customer requests an exemption from Income Management

Step

Action

1

Check which Income Management measure the customer is under + Read more ...

To check, go to the Income Management Summary screen:

  • Qualification Details block for current Income Management customers, or
  • Customer is in Service Profiles block for customers identified for a measure of Income Management

If under:

  • the DEY or LTWPR measure, go to Step 2
  • the Vulnerable Welfare Payment Recipients (VWPR) measure due to an automatic trigger (this will display as 'Vulnerable - Special Benefit', 'Vulnerable - UTLAH' or 'Vulnerable - Crisis', the customer may be eligible for an exclusion. See Exclusions for automatically triggered VWPR Youth Income Management for Service Officers. Procedure ends here
  • the VWPR measure due to a notice being applied by a social worker, advise the customer they are not eligible for an exclusion. Record details on a DOC. Advise the customer they can request a review of Income Management, see Reviewing, reconsidering and ending VWPR Notices assessed by social workers. Procedure ends here
  • any other measure, advise the customer an exemption or exclusion from Income Management is not available for the measure under which their payments are income managed. Record details on a DOC. Remind customers they can request reviews of Income Management decisions. Procedure ends here

2

Discuss eligibility and process for exemption + Read more ...

Confirm the customer has dependent children.

A dependent child is a child of:

  • compulsory school age for the purposes of Income Management, or
  • under compulsory school age, who is either fully or partly in their care

Only the 'primary carer' as per the Services Australia records can apply for an exemption 'with dependent children'.

The other carer will need to seek 'without dependent children' exemption option.

Customers may also be identified for an exemption service offer via Work Item I551Q1 â€“ Customer is eligible for CIM Exemption Service Offer. This will appear on the Income Management Summary screen.

To discuss eligibility with customers who:

  • have dependent children, go to Step 3
  • have no dependent children, go to Step 4
  • are in receipt of SpB, go to Step 5

3

Customers with dependent children + Read more ...

The customer must pass an assessment of financial vulnerability, and:

  • for each school aged child, the customer must provide evidence their child has been attending school regularly for the past 2 terms or semester
  • for each child under school age, the customer must provide evidence demonstrating participation in early childhood services such as age related immunisation, childcare and general health check-ups

If the school aged child is attending school regularly when in the care of one parent, but not with the other, the customer applying for the exemption will need to demonstrate they are taking reasonable steps to ensure the child attends school and have taken steps to stop truancy, for example, addressing bullying at school.

Advise the customer:

  • If they wish to test their eligibility, they can be put in contact with a Service Officer in the Income Management Exemption and Assessment Team (IMEAT) to have an interview over the phone
  • The types of evidence to be supplied may depend on a person's family situation and will be discussed in more detail during the interview

For more information see Requirements for an exemption from Income Management for customers with dependent children.

Go to Step 6.

4

Customers without dependent children + Read more ...

Explain exemption requirements.

The customer may qualify automatically or seek an exemption if they:

  • are a full-time student or an Australian Apprentice in receipt of Youth Allowance, or
  • have received less than 25% of the maximum base rate of payment for 4 out of the last 6 fortnights

An automatic exemption will be granted to customers who have received less than 25% of their basic rate of payment for 4 out of the last 6 fortnights.

An automatic exemption is not granted when the customer:

  • starts full-time study or an Australian Apprenticeship while on Income Management, or
  • previously had an automatic exemption ended for reason 'customer request' (CLR)

If an automatic exemption has not occurred and a customer wishes to test their eligibility for an exemption under one of the above criteria, IMEAT will need to complete a manual assessment.

Go to Step 6.

5

Customers receiving SpB + Read more ...

The customer may be exempt if both the following are met:

  • they are not a Principal Carer of a child under 6 years of age, and
  • they are aged 16 years or older

Advise the customer if they wish to test their eligibility, they can be put in contact with a Service Officer in the Income Management Exemption and Assessment Team (IMEAT) to have an interview over the phone.

6

Exemptions from Income Management + Read more ...

Does the customer want to apply for an exemption from Income Management?

  • Yes, go to Step 7
  • No, for customers:
    • without dependent children, record details on a DOC. Procedure ends here
    • with dependent children, see Recording Exemption Service Offer for customers with dependent children. Procedure ends here

7

Auto or Manual Income Management + Read more ...

Is the customer on Auto or Manual Income Management status?

  • Auto, complete the Initial Assessment interview to change the customer to Manual IM before referring the customer to IMEAT. This ensures their payment does not suspend while the exemption is assessed. See Initial Assessment interview for Disengaged Youth and Long Term Welfare Payment Recipients. Go to Step 8
  • Manual, go to Step 8

8

Hand-off to Processing Team + Read more ...

Customers without dependent children

Send an ACTDOC with the keyword IMEAT to Region Code EAT (Income Management Exemption and Assessment Team (IMEAT). requesting the customer be assessed for an exemption from Income Management

Include in the ACTDOC the reason for the assessment, for example, started full-time study, receiving less than 25% of their rate of payment.

Advise the customer to contact after two working days to query the status of their request.

Procedure ends here.

IMEAT, go to Step 9

Customers with dependent children

For more information, see Recording Exemption Service Offer for customers with dependent children,

Warm transfer call to IMEAT. (only if Step 7 has been actioned). For a link to IMEAT contact details in Office Locator, see the Resources page.

Direct the customer to the phones to contact the Income management line to be transferred to IMEAT. Scan any evidence provided. For a link to IMEAT contact details in Office Locator, see the Resources page.

If the transfer to IMEAT is not successful, or the customer requests IMEAT contact them, send a CALLDOC with the keyword IMEAT to Region Code EAT with the relevant details. Ensure the customer's contact details are correct. Procedure ends here.

IMEAT, go to Step 9

9

Confirm category for exemption + Read more ...

For Income Management Exemption and Assessment Team only.

Confirm the correct category to consider an exemption.

A customer has a dependent child for exemption purposes if the child is of compulsory school age for the purposes of Income Management or younger and is either wholly or partly in the customer's care. If a child is in shared care, the child can only be considered the dependent child for exemption purposes for one person. Proceed with the assessment based on the category type:

  • Customers with dependent children: conduct a financial vulnerability test
  • Customers without dependent children involved in study or apprenticeship, or receiving less than 25% of their maximum basic rate of payment
  • Customers receiving Special Benefit
