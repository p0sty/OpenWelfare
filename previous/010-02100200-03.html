Determining eligibility for Fares Allowance

Table 1

Step

Action

1

Request travel assistance + Read more ...

A student/applicant requests travel assistance for Orientation or Special Purpose Visit travel.

Fares Allowance (FAA) can be approved for travel between the:

  • home and term address, or
  • term address and home community for journey’s relating to serious problems of adjustment or similar circumstances. All other options to resolve the issue at the term address must have been exhausted

FAA can be used by either the student or

approved non-student travellers.

Note: an Australian Apprentice is not entitled to Fares Allowance (FAA) for the Australian Apprenticeship.

Is the student eligible for Fares Allowance (FAA)?

  • Yes, and they are requesting:
    • Special Purpose Visit travel, see Table 2
    • Orientation travel, see Table 3
  • No, go to Step 2

2

Not entitled + Read more ...

Student and/or non-student traveller are not entitled to travel for Orientation or a Special Purpose Visit:

  • Students may be entitled to FAA for other travel types
  • Family or community members may be entitled to non-student travel

If the travel request has been received by:

  • ABSTUDY Smart Centre Telephony:
    • record details of discussion on a DOC
    • explain their review and appeal rights
    • procedure ends here
  • ABSTUDY Smart Centre Travel Team:
    • finalise the travel Booking Status as Rejected in the Travel Management Application (TMA)
    • record details of the decision in the Notes field. See Coding pre-booked FAA entitlements for ABSTUDY and ABSTUDY Pensioner Education Supplement (PES)
    • procedure ends here

Special Purpose Visit (SPV) travel

Table 2

Step

Action

1

Variations to school vacation travel + Read more ...

Does the student have school vacation travel booked within 2 weeks of the requested SPV travel date and the student will not be returning to home before the start of the school term?

  • Yes, the request should be treated as a variation to the original school vacation travel. See Fares Allowance (FAA) authorised in advance of travel for ABSTUDY
  • No, go to Step 2

2

Special Purpose Visit (SPV) travel + Read more ...

SPV travel for student/s or approved non-student travellers for:

  • secondary student participating in:
    • NAIDOC week activities,
    • Graduation

Go to Step 3

  • secondary student participating in a school event, including but not limited to:
    • school play
    • award ceremonies
    • parent/teacher interviews
    • sporting events

Go to Step 4

  • Secondary student currently in a boarding school or hostel experiencing adjustment problems or similar circumstances not resulting in suspension, go to Step 5
  • None of the above, go to Step 8

3

NAIDOC Week or Graduation events + Read more ...

Travel may be approved for a reasonable number of approved travellers to travel from the student’s home address to the term address for NAIDOC Week or Graduation events.

Graduation events

SPV travel for a Year 12 formal that can been completed within the same journey as the Year 12 Graduation event, is considered as part of the graduation event. This does not contribute to the SPV entitlement of 3 events per calendar year.

Where the student:

  • attends boarding school, the requirements for the student or non-student traveller to attend must be outlined in the travel request form
  • is in a hostel or private boarding arrangement, a statement from the school must be provided outlining the requirements for the student or non-student traveller to attend
  • has completed secondary studies, for which they received ABSTUDY Away from Home assistance, SPV travel to attend Graduation events for the completed studies can be accessed

Is the SPV travel request for a confirmed NAIDOC Week or Graduation event?

  • Yes, go to Step 7
  • No, go to Step 8

4

School related events + Read more ...

Travel may be approved for a reasonable number of approved travellers to travel from the student’s home address to the term address for School events.

A maximum of 3 events per calendar year can be approved for Special Purpose visit travel.

Where the student:

  • attends boarding school, the requirements for the student or non-student traveller to attend must be outlined in the travel request form
  • is in a hostel or private boarding arrangement, a statement from the school must be provided outlining the requirements for the student or non-student traveller to attend

Is the SPV travel request for a confirmed school event?

  • Yes, and the student has:
    • reached the limits for the calendar year go to Step 6
    • not reached the limits for the calendar year go to Step 7
  • No, go to Step 8

5

Serious problems of adjustment or similar circumstances + Read more ...

A student may be considered to have serious problems of adjustment at their boarding or term location if they have:

  • prolonged homesickness
  • poor attendance at classes
  • behavioural problems which affect the student’s academic performance and/or, are in serious breach of the school’s or boarding education institution’s standards of behaviour

Note: A return trip from the term address to the home address should only be approved as a last resort. This would need to increase the likeliness of the student remaining in education.

Is the student experiencing confirmed serious problems of adjustment or similar circumstances where a visit is expected to stabilise the situation?

  • Yes, go to Step 7
  • No, go to Step 8

6

Additional Assistance in lieu of Fares Allowance + Read more ...

In some circumstances the student or approved traveller may be eligible for ABSTUDY Additional Assistance in lieu of ABSTUDY Fares Allowance. This is payable at the discretion of the Department for Social Services (DSS).

Escalate these enquiries to the Level 2 Policy Helpdesk via Local Peer Support (LPS) and include all of the following details:

  • Reason for travel:
  • Travel dates:
  • From destination:
  • To destination:
  • ABSTUDY Award of student:
  • Cost:
  • Is the traveller seeking accommodation and meal costs: Yes/No:
  • Reason for request:

For escalating assistance, see Technical Support Model – boutique work

Procedure ends here

7

Authorisation and reimbursement + Read more ...

The student and/or non-student traveller is eligible for Special Purpose Visit travel

Where a family or community member is travelling with the student, they will be considered a non-student traveller.

Travel is approved to undertake a return journey between the home and term address. In some circumstances travel to a destination other than the permanent home or usual study location may be available. See the Resources page for Assessment scenarios

Does the student require Fares Allowance (FAA) to be:

  • authorised and booked in advance of travel, or
  • reimbursed after the travel has been undertaken

Procedure ends here

8

Not entitled to Special Purpose Visit travel + Read more ...

Student and/or non-student traveller are not entitled to travel for Special Purpose Visit:

  • Students may be entitled to FAA for other travel types
  • Family or community members may be entitled to non-student travel

If the travel request has been received by:

  • ABSTUDY Smart Centre Telephony:
    • record details of discussion on a DOC
    • explain their review and appeal rights
    • procedure ends here
  • ABSTUDY Smart Centre Travel Team:
    • finalise the travel Booking Status as Rejected in the Travel Management Application (TMA)
    • record details of the decision in the Notes field. See Coding pre-booked Fares Allowance (FAA) entitlements for ABSTUDY and ABSTUDY Pensioner Education Supplement (PES)
    • procedure ends here

Orientation travel

Table 3

Step

Action

1

Orientation travel + Read more ...

For Orientation travel requests the following must be included with the travel request form:

  • students attending boarding school, the requirements for the student or non-student traveller to attend must be outlined in the travel request form
  • students attending hostels or with private boarding arrangements, a statement from the school outlining the requirements for the student or non-student traveller to attend

Orientation travel is required for student/s or approved non-student travellers when:

  • secondary school or boarding facility requires prospective students to attend an interview or selection process prior to acceptance, go to Step 2
  • student is intending to study in an approved tertiary course of at least one year's duration, go to Step 3
  • secondary student is from a remote Aboriginal community or it is likely they will experience serious problems of adjustment, go to Step 4
  • boarding provider or school is seeking approval for a transition (or like) program for students from remote Aboriginal communities, go to Step 5
  • none of the above, go to Step 7

2

Prospective student/s attending an interview or selection process + Read more ...

Students can travel to attend orientation activities, enrolment interviews or other selection processes prior to:

  • commencing secondary boarding
  • moving between secondary boarding institutions

Some students may have a requirement to attend an interview or selection process in the year prior to commencing at secondary school.

For these students a new ABSTUDY claim is required if they are not already current on ABSTUDY. This is to confirm the student will meet the eligibility requirements once they commence at the new school. See Early claims.

In some cases, there may be a number of students from the same community that are required to complete an interview or selection process. It may be more cost effective for a representative from the education institution to travel to the community to conduct the interviews. See Non-student travellers for ABSTUDY for more information.

Is the student and/or non-student traveller attending a confirmed interview or selection process?

  • Yes, go to Step 6
  • No, go to Step 7

3

Tertiary study + Read more ...

For Orientation travel for tertiary study the student must meet all of the following:

  • intending to study in an approved tertiary course of at least one year duration and
  • they are attending a confirmed interview or selection process relating to the tertiary course

Does the student meet all of the above?

  • Yes, go to Step 6
  • No, go to Step 7

4

Intending secondary boarder + Read more ...

Students may access Orientation travel if they are intending to:

  • board away from home to attend secondary school and
  • attend a confirmed Orientation activity

Students must be from a:

  • remote Aboriginal community, or
  • town in a remote location where, the student is likely to experience serious problems of adjustment

Does the student meet the above?

  • Yes, go to Step 6
  • No, go to Step 7

5

Boarding provider or school transition program + Read more ...

The boarding provider or school can seek approval for a transition (or like) program to assist with Orientation travel for students from remote Aboriginal communities. The boarding provider or school must provide details outlining the program.

Can the transition program be approved for Fares Allowance for participating students?

  • Yes,
    • place a Display on Access (DOA) DOC on the boarding providers or schools ORG record confirming the program approval
    • go to step 6
  • No, where approval is undetermined:
    • escalate the enquiry to the Level 2 Policy Helpdesk, via Local Peer Support (LPS), with a limit of one enquiry per program, and include the details outlining the transition program
    • procedure ends here

6

Authorisation and reimbursement + Read more ...

The student and/or non-student traveller is eligible for Orientation travel.

Where a family or community member is travelling with the student, they will be considered to be a non-student traveller. Students and non-student travellers may be eligible to claim accommodation costs for their stay. See Additional costs associated with travel for ABSTUDY for details.

Travel is approved to undertake a return journey between the home address and a term address. Travel to a destination other than the permanent home or usual study location may be available in certain circumstances. See the Resources page for scenarios.

Determine if the student requires Fares Allowance (FAA) to be:

  • authorised and booked in advance of travel, or
  • costs reimbursed after the travel has been undertaken

Procedure ends here

7

Not entitled to Orientation Travel + Read more ...

Student and/or non-student traveller are not entitled to travel for Orientation:

  • Students may be entitled to FAA for other travel types
  • Family or community members may be entitled to non-student travel

If the travel request has been received by

  • ABSTUDY Smart Centre Telephony:
    • record details of discussion on a DOC
    • explain their review and appeal rights
    • procedure ends here
  • Smart Centre Travel Team:
    • finalise the travel Booking Status as Rejected in the Travel Management Application (TMA)
    • record details of the decision in the Notes field. See Coding pre-booked FAA entitlements for ABSTUDY and ABSTUDY Pensioner Education Supplement (PES)
    • procedure ends here
