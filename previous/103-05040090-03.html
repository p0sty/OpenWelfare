Processing using the Advance Payments workflow for recovery and re-issue

Assisted processing instructions for investigation, recovery and re-issue of an advance payment that may have been claimed fraudulently. Portions of this process can be completed by the Intelligence and Investigations Branch only.

This process does not apply to advance payments for Home Equity Access Scheme loans. Refer these enquiries to the Home Equity Access Scheme National for investigation.

Step

Action

1

Investigating the customer's record + Read more ...

Customer advises that an advance payment appears to have been fraudulently applied for under their name and directed to an account to which they do not have legal access.

Investigate the customer's record.

  • Discuss with the customer the details in relation to the advance payment, that is, when it was granted, the channel through which the application was made, any recent bank account updates etc. to ensure the customer did not contact and make the application.
  • If the customer has a correspondence nominee discuss with the customer the possibility that the nominee made the application on behalf of the customer. It is appropriate to contact the correspondence nominee to verify whether or not they made the application for the advance payment.
  • Verify that the customer did not have legal access to bank account in which the advance payment was deposited. To verify if the customer had legal access to the account, contact must be made with the financial institution. If the customer has access to this account, the payment cannot be reissued.
  • Correct payment destination. For more information, see Replacing direct credit, New Payments Platform (NPP) or Real Time Gross Settlement (RTGS) at a customer's request.

2

Correct the customer's payment destination + Read more ...

Correct the customer's payment destination details, ensuring the customer has legal access, either solely or jointly, to the account.

For further details, see Changing payment destination.

3

Recall the advance payment + Read more ...

  • A request for refund must be actioned for the advance payment
  • This must be actioned before any recovered amounts can be reissued
  • The customer must be warned that if the funds have not been returned, Services Australia may raise a debt against them to recover the original funds where a case of fraud is not identified

Code request for refund of payment

  • While in the customer's record go to the Payment Summary (PS) screen by typing 'PS' in the Nxt: field
  • Identify the payment to be refunded
  • Select the payment to be refunded by typing 'F' next to the payment line. This is the Request Refund of Payment (RFR) option. This will display the Request for Refund (RFR) screen. The reason for refund field will be defaulted to '3rd'
  • Enter the source of the information in the Source: field
  • Enter the date of request in the DOR: field
  • Enter Yes/No/Qol in the Finished field
  • Press [Enter] to complete this activity
  • Ensure the Request for Refund/Recall (RFR) activity has been automatically generated on the Future Activity List (FAL) screen to check in 14 days' time and again in 28 days that the original payment has been returned. These activities will normally not show until daily driver (DDR) has run on the record

If the activity has been selected by Quality Online (QOL) the future activity may not generate immediately. In these cases:

  • note the CRN, and
  • check the FAL screen the next day

For more information, see Replacing direct credit, New Payments Platform (NPP) or Real Time Gross Settlement (RTGS) at a customer's request.

4

Suspend recovery of the advance payment + Read more ...

Run the Manage Advances action in Process Direct to modify the advance repayment.

In Process Direct:

  • Select the Customer Summary tile
  • Search for the customer
  • On the Snapshot screen, select > Manage Advances

From the Manage Advances page:

  • Select the current advance payment from the dropdown list
  • From the Action dropdown list, select Adjust Repayment

On the Adjust Advance Repayment screen, click on the twisty next to Adjust Repayments and then complete:

  • Contact method: field, select from dropdown list
  • How much can you afford to repay each fortnight?: field, code the rate of repayment as $0 to suspend recovery
  • Thinking about your current fortnightly income and your expensesâ€¦ field, code the amount as zero
  • As you are requesting to reduce your repayment, are you in severe financial hardship due to a special and unusual change in your circumstances
    • If No, reject request as customer is not in hardship
  • When will you be able to increase your repayment to the minimum repayment amount?
    • Enter date
    • Note: repayments can be reduced for no more than 3 months
  • A review will automatically be created

If Process direct is unavailable, see Changes to the repayment rate of an advance payment for instructions on using Customer First.

5

Reissue any recovered amounts of the advance payment + Read more ...

If the customer requires recovered advance payment amounts to be reissued immediately due to financial hardship the customer's request should be actioned. Go to Step 6.

If the customer will not be placed in financial hardship, the reissue of recovered advance payment amounts will be undertaken by the Intelligence and Investigations Branch as part of the fraud investigation processing.

6

Reissue payments + Read more ...

Access to the Payment Adjustment (PYAJ) screen has been limited. See Using the Payment Adjustment (PYAJ) screen for the list of staff with access.

On the PYAJ screen, the authorised staff member must code the following fields (using field help ('?') if required:

  • Reason Code: AVR
  • Period Date (from & to)
  • Component: Basic
  • Amount: $
  • Reason for Payment:

The Net Amount: $ field will be automatically calculated by the system.

  • Press [Enter] to continue. The Payment Adjustment Confirm (PYAJC) screen will display
  • On the PYAJC screen, confirm that the details of the payment are correct, then in the Submit? (Y/N/Q): field, enter 'Y', 'N' or 'Q' and press [Enter]
  • The Assessment Results (AR) screen should be carefully checked before finalising any activity

Record details of payment made on a DOC.

7

Update the advance balance if recovered amounts are reissued + Read more ...

See the 'Adjusting the balance of an advance payment' table in Lump Sum repayment of advance payment.

8

Report the suspected fraud + Read more ...

Refer the case to an Intelligence and Investigations Branch by recording the suspected fraud on the Report Suspected Fraud application.

The Resources page contains a link to the Report Suspected Fraud application.

9

Immediate application for an advance payment + Read more ...

Does the customer wish to apply immediately for an advance payment?

  • Yes, refer to Deletion of advance granted on incorrect record or fraudulently claimed. Procedure ends here.
  • No, corrective action will be undertaken as part of the fraud investigations. Specialist intervention to remove the advance payment from the customer's record will be undertaken when the fraud investigation is concluded and a case of fraud is identified or if the customer wishes to apply for an advance payment prior to the fraud investigation being finalised. See Deletion of advance granted on incorrect record or fraudulently claimed for referral details.
