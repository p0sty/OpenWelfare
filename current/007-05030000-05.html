Claim for Dad and Partner Pay form (FA080)

FAQs on work, income and residence

Table 1

Item

Description

1

Question: If the customer has met the work test, but has ceased working can they still be paid Dad and Partner Pay?

Answer: There is no obligation for the customer to notify of a termination of employment as it has no impact on the customer's entitlement for DAP.

2

Question: If the customer does not qualify as they do not meet the work/income and/or the residency test, can they elect someone else to claim instead?

Answer: No, DAP is not transferrable to another person.

3

Question: In the case of a self-employed customer who is not working in the DAP period but still receives income via their business (due to the business continuing to operate), can they remain entitled to DAP?

Answer: Yes, the income received from the business will not affect entitlement to DAP.

4

Question: Is confirmed income from the Australian Taxation Office for Family Tax Benefit reconciliation acceptable proof of income for DAP?

Answer: Yes.

5

Question: Is DAP paid while the customer is overseas?

Answer: Yes, if the customer still meets the residence requirements and the child is in the customer's care while overseas.

6

Question: Is there an online estimator for DAP?

Answer: No, there is no online estimator available for DAP.

7

Question: Why has the rate of income support payment reduced during the DAP period?

Answer: DAP is counted as ordinary income for calculating the rate of income support payments (ISP).

This means if the customer (and/or their partners) receive an ISP, the ISP rate will reduce during the DAP entitlement period.

If the ISP reduces to a zero rate due to DAP, the ISP will cancel. The customer must lodge a new claim if they wish to re test their eligibility for an ISP.

If the DAP nominated start date is backdated to a date that falls within a period for which an ISP has already been paid to the customer (and/or their partner), an ISP overpayment will be raised.

Foster carer and adoption eligibility for DAP

Table 2

Item

Description

1

Question: Are there any circumstances in which a foster carer would be eligible for DAP?

Answer: No, DAP is not payable for foster carers. However, a customer may be eligible for DAP where a child becomes entrusted to the customer's care as part of an adoption process and all other eligibility criteria are met.

2

Question: Would DAP be payable where a long term foster carer adopts the child or the child is adopted by marriage?

Answer: No, known adoptions are not eligible for DAP.

Information on new claims

Table 3

Item

Description

1

Question: Does the DAP customer need to provide confirmation of their identity with their claim?

Answer: No, there are no identity confirmation requirements for DAP customers.

2

Question: If a customer lodges a DAP claim 51 weeks after the child is born and nominates a DAP period in the past, can the full amount (14 days) still be paid?

Answer: A DAP claim must be lodged within 52 weeks from the date of birth or entry into care. If a customer's nominated start date is in the past they may be payable for the 2 weeks DAP payment providing they meet other eligibility requirements.

3

Question: Can the biological father who is not partnered to or living with the birth mother still claim DAP?

Answer: Yes, the biological father does not need to be living with the birth mother to receive DAP. If the customer has care of the child for each day in DAP period they may be eligible for the payment.

4

Question: If a public holiday falls during a customer’s DAP period and they are paid for that day, do they remain entitled to DAP?

Answer: No, the customer would not be entitled to DAP from that day. Customers must be on unpaid leave and not working to receive DAP. If a customer advises in their DAP claim they will be paid for public holidays during their nominated period, they should be contacted to discuss whether they are able to change their DAP period to receive the full amount of DAP.

5

Question: Are there any keeping in touch days for DAP?

Answer: There are no keeping in touch days for DAP.

The oversight provisions under the Paid Parental Leave scheme for self-employed customers will apply to DAP.

6

Question: What is the key performance indicator for DAP claims?

Answer: 21 days.

7

Question: Can a DAP customer access Flexible PPL days?

For children that are born on or after 1 July 2020, Parental Leave Pay (PPL) includes 30 Flexible PPL days. Primary claimants can give permission for another person (such as their partner or the child’s other parent) to claim some or all of their Flexible PPL days.

DAP customers can access Flexible PPL days, provided:

  • they meet the eligibility requirements for PPL, and
  • lodge an effective claim

They need to be not working, meet residency requirements and have primary care of the child on any days they want to access Flexible PPL.

If permission has been given for a secondary claimant to claim Flexible PPL, once the secondary claimant reaches the maximum 18 week limit of combined DAP and PPL, any Flexible PPL days they claim will be rejected (reason code: ‘DXP’).

To return these Flexible PPL days to primary claimant, the primary claimant must to remove the permission for another person to claim. These days will be returned to the primary claimant’s unclaimed Flexible PPL balance. Any days that are not claimed before the child’s second birthday/anniversary of adoption will be forfeited.

DAP care scenarios

Table 4

Item

Description

1

Unexpected emergency

Darren has claimed and has been paid DAP and had a nominated start date of 1 February. On 11 February, Darren contacts to advise of the requirement to travel interstate on 9 February as a relative was hospitalised.

Darren's wife and new baby did not travel with Darren and Darren is still interstate. Darren queries whether eligibility will continue for DAP and whether there will be a requirement to pay back some of the payment received.

Outcome: If a DAP customer does not have care of the DAP child for 1 or more days during the DAP period as a result of an unexpected emergency, the DAP customer will remain eligible for DAP. As Darren has had to travel interstate due to an unexpected emergency, Darren must be advised eligibility will continue for DAP and Darren will not be required to repay any of the payment received.

2

Circumstances beyond control

Ian has been paid DAP for the 2 week period starting 10 April 2019 for Ian's child Isaac, who was born on the 30 March 2019. Ian is separated from Helen, the birth mother.

Ian planned on visiting Isaac on each day during the DAP period in order to bond with Isaac and provide support to Helen. Helen and Isaac travelled to visit Helen's parents for a weekend during Ian's DAP period and subsequently Ian was not able to visit Isaac over that weekend as planned. Ian contacts to query what will happen to the DAP payment.

Outcome: If a DAP customer does not have care of the DAP child for one or more days during the DAP period as a result of circumstances beyond their control, the DAP customer will remain eligible for DAP. Ian must be advised eligibility will continue for DAP and Ian will not have to repay any of the DAP payment received.

3

Unwell and unable to care for child

Dara and Lisa recently adopted baby Grace. Dara is the primary carer for Grace as Lisa is working full time. Lisa has arranged to take 2 weeks unpaid leave commencing 15 January 2019 and has claimed DAP for this period.

Due to Lisa becoming unwell during the DAP period, Lisa is unable to care for Grace for a period of 3 days. Lisa is concerned that not providing care for Grace, there may be no eligibility for DAP for this period.

Outcome: Lisa must be advised that although not physically caring for baby Grace, eligibility continues for DAP for this period, as Lisa is residing with Grace and would maintain a level of care, control and oversight even whilst unwell.

4

Child hospitalised

Jason lodged a pre claim for DAP and nominated the expected date of birth of the child as the DAP period start date.

Jason's wife Alison gave birth to baby Piper 6 weeks early and as a result Piper remained in hospital in the special care nursery for the first 3 weeks after birth. Jason contacts as unsure whether there is still eligibility for DAP while Piper remains in hospital.

Outcome: Jason must be advised that in the circumstance where a child is hospitalised, the DAP customer is:

  • still deemed to be caring for the child, and
  • will remain entitled to DAP

Requirements for an effective DAP claim

Table 5

Item

Description

1

Individual adjusted taxable income

To meet the DAP income test the customer must have an individual adjusted taxable income (ATI) below the relevant financial year income limit. Evidence is required if the customer's adjusted taxable income is $135,000 and the relevant financial year income limit.

The income limit is indexed annually in line with the Consumer Price Index.

Note: if the customer has previously met the income test for a Parental Leave Pay (PPL) claim and is now claiming DAP, the customer will automatically meet the income test for DAP.

If the customer claims DAP for the same child as the previous PPL claim, the income test will only be assessed based on the eligibility of the PPL if the customer has failed the income test based on the DAP claim.

2

Evidence to support the DAP income test

Evidence for the DAP is required if the customer's adjusted taxable income is between $135,000 and the relevant financial year income limit.

The following are examples of suitable documents to verify:

  • taxable income:
    • Notice of Assessment (NOA) from the Australian Taxation Office (ATO)
    • actual adjusted taxable income details transferred electronically from the ATO to Centrelink
    • payment summaries
    • payslips with year to date amounts if their income tax assessment is not available due to timing
    • a letter from their accountant
    • advice from their employer with sufficient documentary evidence supplied
    • bank statements showing interest earned
  • other income:
    • evidence of rental property income and expenditure
    • evidence of foreign income or tax exempt foreign income
    • a letter from employer in relation to salary sacrifice amounts and reportable fringe benefits

Original documents are not required. The customer can fax or post copies of their evidence to family assistance.

3

Care of a child for DAP purposes

To be eligible for DAP, the customer must be caring for the child on each day of the DAP period, although more than 1 person may be caring for the same child on a particular day.

The customer may be the primary carer of the child or providing care for the child at the same time as another person (for example, the child's birth mother) who is providing primary care for the child.

To be considered as having 'care of the child' for DAP, the customer does not need to:

  • be living with the child (although generally would be)
  • have any particular percentage of care, or
  • have legal responsibility for the child

If the customer does not have care of the child on their nominated DAP period start date they will not be eligible for DAP. If they do not have care of the child on another day during their DAP period they will not be eligible from that day onwards.

In the case of child bereavement, the customer may remain eligible for DAP despite not having care of the child on 1 or more days in the DAP period.

If a customer has circumstances that have arisen beyond their control which impact on their care arrangement, they should not be considered ineligible for DAP, for example:

  • the customer becomes sick in the DAP period
  • the customer has to leave their residence to visit someone due to an unexpected emergency, for example, a family member/friend being hospitalised
  • their baby is born prematurely and remains in hospital for special care or has to go back into hospital for care, or
  • the DAP customer is refused access to the baby/child by the other carer

4

Effective DAP claim

Requirements for an effective claim:

  • the claim has been lodged within the DAP allowable claim period
  • the claim is submitted online or lodged via the Claim for Dad and Partner Pay (FA080)
  • information and evidence (if required) on whether the customer meets the Australian residence requirements
  • Tax File Number (TFN)
  • bank account details
  • expected date of birth or adoption date (for an early pre- birth claim)
  • child's actual date of birth or date of adoption for a post birth claim)
  • period nominated start date
  • information and evidence (if required) about how the customer meets the DAP work test, and
  • information and evidence (if required) about how the customer meets the DAP income test
