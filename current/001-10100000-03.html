Identifying and generating compliance action

Table 1: This table describes how to identify a job seeker's refusal, or failure to accept or commence a job offer and generate compliance action.

Item

Action

1

Provider generated compliance action + Read more ...

Where a provider identifies that a job seeker refused, or failed to accept or commence work, providers are responsible for assessing whether the work was suitable. The non-compliance event is not reported if the provider establishes that the job offered was not suitable work, or the job seeker had a valid reason for refusing the offer.

Under the Targeted Compliance Framework, providers use payment suspension to establish contact with the job seeker following a work refusal failure. Payment is not payable until the job seeker has contacted the provider to discuss the job they have refused and their reasons for the refusal.

When the provider decides to report a work refusal failure to Services Australia, both a Department of Education, Skills and Employment validity check and a Services Australia validity check are completed before a compliance investigation is generated.

ParentsNext participants are not required to accept offers of work, ParentsNext providers are unable to report work refusal failures.

The Participation Solutions Team (PST) is responsible for undertaking the compliance investigation.

2

Employer advises Services Australia directly + Read more ...

Service Officers generate compliance action where an employer contacts Services Australia directly to advise that a job offer not arranged by a provider has been refused, or a job seeker failed to accept or commence a job.

The following information should be gathered from the employer:

  • business name, contact name and details
  • nature of the job offered, for example, job title, status (full-time/part-time/casual), hours of employment
  • the date the job seeker refused, failed to commence the job offered
  • any reason offered by the job seeker for refusing or failing to commence the job offered

Where the information from the employer is received via the National Business Gateway, the service centre will generate the compliance action.

The Participation Solutions Team (PST) are responsible for the compliance investigation and determining if a failure has been committed.

3

Generating a compliance investigation in Customer First + Read more ...

Select the Participation Compliance workflow and choose the Generate Compliance Action workflow.

Within the workflow, clearly document:

  • a failure date based on the date the job seeker refused, or failed to accept or commence the job offer
  • the participation payment the job seeker was receiving at the time the refusal occurred
  • in the What is the nature of the job seeker's failure?: field Select the Employment related option
  • select the reason for the compliance action
  • employer details including business name, and contact name and details
  • the details of the information received from the employer including the nature of the job offered

Scan or fax any relevant supporting document evidence, for example a written statement received from the employer using the most appropriate reclassification page based on the type of evidence received.

Transfer the compliance action to the Participation Solutions Team (PST) who will commence outbound contact attempts once this is received.

Investigating a work refusal, or failure to accept or commence a job offer

Table 2: This table provides information for Participation Solutions Team (PST)-skilled Service Officers who are responsibility for investigating a work refusal and determining if a failure has been committed.

Item

Action

1

Primary focus of the compliance investigation + Read more ...

The compliance investigation focuses on establishing the facts that are relevant to the compliance event under consideration to enable an evidence based decision that is in line with the legislative framework and policy intent.

When investigating a job seeker's refusal, or failure to accept or commence a job offer, the relevant matters that must be considered include

  • the circumstances that led the job seeker to refuse the offer or failure to commence the job
  • whether the employment was suitable work, and
  • whether there were any factors that made it reasonable for the job seeker to refuse the job offer

2

Starting point for the investigation + Read more ...

At the beginning of the investigation, the Service Officer should explain that a failure has been reported for refusal or failure to accept or commence a job offer, as well as the purpose of the investigation.

During the course of the conversation the Service Officer must keep conversations focused to ensure all relevant facts and evidence required to make a good decision are collected.

The information recorded when the compliance investigation was generated should be used to progress the discussion. This may include:

  • information recorded by the provider
  • information recorded by the Service Officer in the workflow when generating the compliance action

3

Job seeker's explanation of reasons for refusing the job offer or failing to commence suitable work + Read more ...

During the investigation, the job seeker must be given opportunity to respond to any adverse claim that has been made against them and disclose any further information they would like considered.

As a result of this discussion the Service Officer should record:

  • employer details including company name and contact details (where they are not already recorded in the Participation Compliance workflow)
  • the nature of the employment, how was the offer made (in person, phone, email etc.), if they signed an employment contract
  • the job seeker's explanation for refusing the job offer or failing to commence
  • any efforts made by the job seeker to commence the employment
  • any other relevant information provided by the job seeker

For further information see Participation Compliance workflow

4

Suitability of work + Read more ...

A failure has only been committed if the work was suitable for the job seeker based on their individual circumstances at the time that they were offered the job. Consideration must be given to the job seeker's individual circumstances such as their skills, experience, qualifications, medical history and availability of appropriate child care to check if the work was suitable or not.

Additional considerations are required to assess suitability of work for job seekers who have a partial work capacity, are principal carers or ParentsNext participants.

Where the job seeker's refusal or failure to commence was reported by a provider, the suitability of the work is considered by the provider prior to reporting this to Services Australia. Service Officers assume that work was suitable for the job seeker, based on the report from the provider, unless they identify further information during the compliance investigation that would indicate the work was unsuitable

Consultation with a PST Service Support Officer (SSO), social worker, other specialist officer or Indigenous Cultural Consultant maybe appropriate to establish the impact of job seeker circumstances on suitability of work. See Social work role in the Participation Solutions Team (PST) for information about assistance available from PST-skilled social workers.

For further information on suitability of work, see Suitable/unsuitable work.

5

Considerations when assessing if job seeker has a reasonable excuse for refusing, failing to accept or commence suitable work + Read more ...

Where work was considered suitable and the job seeker refuses, fails to accept or commence suitable work, consideration must be given to their reasons for doing so. Where a reasonable excuse is established, a failure has not been committed.

All factors that significantly and directly impacted the decision to refuse or not commence the work are relevant matters for consideration. These include, but are not limited to:

  • age
  • mobility
  • qualifications
  • language proficiency
  • stability of accommodation
  • personal issues
  • behavioural issues
  • work history
  • geographical location
  • family and caring responsibilities

Refusing suitable work is a serious consideration. It is generally not reasonable for a job seeker to refuse work for factors within their control.

For further information, see Assessing reasonable excuse for non-compliance with mutual obligation requirements.

6

Determining if the job seeker refused, or failed to accept or commence suitable work without a reasonable excuse + Read more ...

Where the work is assessed as unsuitable work for the job seeker or the job seeker establishes a reasonable excuse for refusing, or failing to accept or commence suitable work, the compliance action is rejected.

Where the job seeker has refused, or failed to accept or commence suitable work and a reasonable excuse is not established, the penalty imposed depends on the compliance system they will be managed under.

For further information about finalising the compliance action, go to:

  • Targeted Compliance Framework, see Unemployment failures
  • Job Seeker Compliance Model, see Applying an Unemployment Non-Payment Period (UNPP) and determining hardship provisions

For job seekers claiming or in receipt of Special Benefit (SpB) the treatment of a work refusal differs depending on which mutual obligation requirements apply to the job seeker:

  • Under Mutual obligation requirements for Special Benefit (SpB), job seekers compliance action may be applied for refusing, or failing to accept or commence suitable work (as per JobSeeker Payment (JSP))
  • For all other job seekers receiving SpB, refusing, or failing to accept or commence suitable work will mean that the job seeker is not qualified for SpB
