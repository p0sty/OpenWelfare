2

Check the need for a Complex Assessment Officer (CAO) referral + Read more ...

The following cases are considered complex. They must be referred to a CAO for action, regardless of the expertise of the Compliance Officer:

  • Private Trusts and Companies
  • Assets Hardship
  • Home Equity Access Scheme
  • Asset Test exempt lifetime and life expectancy income streams from Self-Managed Superannuation Funds (SMSFs)

Note: all undisclosed trust, company and investment properties (if the investment property is part of a trust or company) assessments must be referred to a CAO

See Step 5 in the Complex Assessment Officer referrals table.

Is a referral to a CAO required?

  • Yes, go to Step 3
  • No, go to Step 4

3

Refer to CAO + Read more ...

See Step 10 in the Complex Assessment Officer referrals table.

The CAO Referral Guided Procedure will add a keyword BICAO to the referral.

When making a referral to a CAO for customers who are involved in a Private Trust or Company, the review is to be placed on HLD for 30 days.

On IRCS screen, code the following:

  • Method type: key 'OUT'
  • Date Contacted: key 'T' for today
  • Press [Enter], when the Confirm Y/N box appears, key 'Y'
  • Press [Enter] and the IRCD screen will be displayed

On IRCD screen code the following:

  • Expected response date: 30 days in the future
  • Person Contacted: CAO
  • Press [Enter], [Enter] to return to the IRRS screen
  • 'T' the intervention (this will display the Manage Reviews (IRMR) screen)
  • Add the keyword: CAOREF
  • Press [Enter]

See the CAO referral DOC template in the Resources page for documenting requirements.

  • The CAO will forward an ACTDOC to the referring CO once they complete the assessment
  • Undertake the required actions outlined by the CAO

Once actions are undertaken, go to Step 8.

4

For FDC Cases only - JETCCFA + Read more ...

Assess the customer's eligibility for JETCCFA.

For any periods when the customer is not entitled to an Income Support Payment, they are also not entitled to the JETCCFA payments they may have received.

To help the Quality Development Officer (QDO) work out the amounts of JETCCFA paid to the customer:

  • send an email to the QDO mailbox
  • include JETCCFA in the subject line
  • include the following in the body of the email
    'Can you please provide the details of JETCCFA paid to (CRN), (customer's name) and (Date of Birth) from the (Date) to (Date).'

JETCCFA debts are raised and recovered under common law, under the Public Governance, Performance and Accountability Act 2013 (PGPA Act).

Does the customer have a Foreign Pension Data Exchange or Consumer Price Index referral?

  • Yes, go to Step 5
  • No, go to Step 8

5

Foreign Pension Indexation (FET/FPA) Data Exchange (DEX) or Consumer Price Index (CPI) referrals + Read more ...

The customer will need to provide information on increases to their foreign pension income since the scope start date. If they cannot provide pension notices they will be need to provide pension amounts from bank statements.

Determine the frequency and foreign pension amounts in foreign currency.

There may be a need to convert from AU$ to foreign currency if the customer cannot provide the amounts in foreign currency. To do this:

  • go to the Foreign Exchange Summary (RDFXS) screen
  • select the relevant country
  • find the date that corresponds to the first increase since the last foreign pension update to the customer's record
  • multiply the AUD$ amount by the Exchange Rate shown on Foreign Exchange Details (RDFXD) screen

This will give the foreign pension amount in foreign currency.

Article 10 documents

Some customers have had Article 10 documents returned. These can be used as part of the update of their foreign pension. Conduct the intervention conversation with customer as per the process. However, address any information already provided in Article 10. To find Article 10 documents search scanned documents.

DEX or CPI updates

These updates are automated and cannot be changed. Refer any errors to a QDO.

Agreement and Non-Agreement countries

See the Resources page for a list of agreement countries.

Is the update for an Agreement country?

  • Yes, the agreement is for an Agreement country, go to Step 6
  • No, the update is for a Non Agreement country, go to Step 7

6

Agreement Country + Read more ...

Use the foreign pension rate calculator for the following countries: Austria, Belgium, Canada, Croatia, Cyprus, Finland, France, Germany, Greece, Italy, North Macedonia, Poland, Serbia, Slovenia, Switzerland, United Kingdom (UK) and United States of America (USA). For information, see International Social Security Agreements.

For all other countries, use the non-rate calculator process. Go to Step 7.

See the International Community Calculators and Rates link on the Calculators and Tools page for a link to the Foreign Pension Rate Calculators and Historical Rate Information.

Update the Payment Country/Type from the drop down menu:

  • Find the year that corresponds to the first increase since the last foreign pension update to the customer's record
  • Enter the known foreign pension rate at that time
  • This will provide the next change point/s and amounts to be updated

Note: read the notes at the bottom of the calculator for information about that pension type.

Go to Step 8.

7

Non Agreement Country - non-rate calculator process + Read more ...

Check when any pension increases occurred. If the date of any pension increases are not known, use 1 January of each relevant year.

If the customer cannot provide increases in foreign pension amounts in foreign currency, there may be a need to convert from AU$ to foreign currency. To do this:

  • go to the Foreign Exchange Summary (RDFXS) screen
  • select the relevant country
  • find the date that corresponds to each increase, or 1 January each year if not known
  • multiply the AUD$ amount by the Exchange Rate shown on Foreign Exchange Details (RDFXD) screen

8

Selecting the IRS intervention + Read more ...

Before any updates on the customer's record:

  • make sure the IRS intervention has a status of started (STA), and
  • be in the Review Image

Check if the customer had a partner getting income support payments during the scope of the intervention.

If so, make sure the partner intervention has been loaded and initiated before coding and assessing the information on the customer's record.

9

Coding income and asset + Read more ...

Code all information on the customer's record and record it in the intervention DOC. This will leave the customer's record correct and up-to-date.

For help with coding, see Earnings Apportionment Tool (EAT) or the following on the Resources page:

  • Recording Real Estate and Requesting a Valuation (Current and/or Historical)
  • Additional Risks
  • UII Trigger and Discussion Resource

Use the Telephony Intervention Processing script (TAPS) to update income and assets information, except for Family Day Care (FDC):

  • The Pensions Task selector (PTS) screen displays
  • Confirm dialogue box displays:
    'The script has paused to allow you to update the customer's income details. When you have made any necessary updates, make sure that you are still in the activity and click on OK to continue.'
  • Press OK on this dialogue box
  • A Record Free Text dialogue box presents, for recording the details of the income and asset updates. Move this to the second screen to record details while making the updates
  • Manually update the relevant income and assets by selecting the appropriate information from the PTS screen
  • After all updates, while still in the activity, select OK in the script to continue
  • The only information in the intervention DOC about the income and assets updates will be the information added to the free text boxes

Income/Assets Update and Reviews Script

  • TAPS can only be run on the customer's record during the initial phone conversation. If not previously run, all updates to income and assets will then need to be done via the Income/Assets Update and Reviews Script and not TAPS

Foreign Pension script

If the customer has a Foreign Pension that needs updating, this needs to be done via the Foreign Pension script before running TAPS:

  • Run the Foreign Pension script to update foreign investments
  • Do not finalise the activity started by the Foreign Pension script. Leave the activity started
  • Once the Foreign Pension script is finished run TAPS
  • Before making more updates to the customer's income and assets, pick up the activity started by the Foreign Pension script
  • This will makes sure all updates are made within one activity

Information relating to the following compliance triggers may be updated verbally or from documented verification.

Verbal updates can be coded as per the actual event dates and/or at the start and end dates of investment as provided by the customer. If employment income and self-employment from Tip-offs information relates to the following, these are to be updated via documented verification only:

  • ATO Company (ASR/ACP) and ATO Trust (ASR/TST)
  • ATO Investment Property (ASR/INP)
  • Investment Property (all) - Customer in Aged Care
  • Family Day Care (UII/FDC)

Check the intervention DOC (and customer's record) for any evidence that would support a decision not to apply a Recovery Fee.

Note: when the customer contacts to discuss, any Compliance Officer can talk to the customer and advise whether the Recovery Fee will apply.

Is the Recovery Fee to be applied?

Determine debt and calculate + Read more ...

All debts that can be calculated via the Centrelink system and explained via the use of ADEX should be calculated in this manner. Only use MultiCal where the Centrelink system calculation is not possible or the outcome is not correct.

Check the Assessment Result (AR) screen to make sure the system has correctly calculated any potential overpayment, for example:

  • Debt schedule - do the debt dates/date of event (DOV) match the period?
  • Have the updates/coding been entered correctly?
  • Check the following to make sure the updates are as expected:

    • Income Bank (NIB)
    • Employment Income Assessment (EIA)
  • Can a reassessment be made using the Benefit Action (BA) screen?

For MultiCal debt calculation, get all information from the customer over the phone where possible to enable a debt calculation.

The CO is responsible for progressing the MultiCal activity. If they cannot action, their site is responsible for progressing the activity. The Team Leader will need to reassign the activity to another CO with the required skill set.

2

Contact customer and partner + Read more ...

If the customer is not on the phone, make three genuine attempts to contact the customer (and the partner where it has been determined the partner has incurred a debt).

  • Follow the phone contact requirements
  • To create and manage the phone contact attempt/s, See Step 1 in Table 2 in Coding the Contact screen in IRS
  • Record all attempts to contact the customer and partner on the intervention DOC and update the Review Contact Details (IRCD) screen. See the Attempted Customer Contact (Outbound call attempts) DOC template in the Resources page for the documenting requirements

Was contact successful?

  • Yes, go to Step 3
  • No, after the third attempt, go to Step 5

3

Advise outcome + Read more ...

If the customer and/or partner is responding to the Pre-Adverse Decision letter, record the customer and/or partner contact on the intervention DOC and update the Review Contact Details (IRCD) screen. See Details of Debt Calculated DOC template in the Resources page for the documenting requirements.

Where the customer and/or partner contacts, make sure the MAI contact is closed off on the IRCD screen.

Prior to discussing any information with the partner, make sure they have permission to enquire. See Person Permitted to Enquire (PPE) Authority for more information.

Discuss the outcome of the intervention with the customer, nominee and/or partner.

Clearly explain to the customer what information was used to calculate the outcome, this could include a combination of verbal or verified information obtained from the customer and/or a third party.

Tell the customer:

  • Debt: 'Based on the information you have advised/ we have obtained from third party regarding your financial circumstances, this has resulted in an overpayment of your Centrelink payment of $xxxxx for the period (date to date)
  • Coronavirus Supplement debt (if applicable): This has also resulted in an overpayment of the Coronavirus supplement of $xxxxx for the period (date to date)
  • their review and appeal rights
  • of their obligation to notify the Agency, generally within 14 days, of any changes in their circumstances. For example, change of address, relationship status, income from any source

Tell the partner (if applicable):

'Your partner has been selected for a compliance intervention. Their financial details were not taken into account in the payments made to you, therefore you have incurred an overpayment of $xxxxx for the period from (date) to (date)'

Ask the customer if they have more information they wish to provide before the outcome is finalised. Where the customer wants to give more information and documented verification is not required, a satisfactory verbal explanation for the discrepancy may be provided. Update the customer record and recalculate as required.

If the customer is not satisfied with the decision and they have no more information they can tell the customer:

  • the decision will be made using the information available
  • they should provide any extra information/evidence that may be relevant to the decision, as soon as practical. Where more information is received, a reassessment will occur

Is the customer giving more information?

  • Yes, go to Step 4
  • No, go to Step 6

4

Customer has more information + Read more ...

If the customer has more information they want to give but cannot provide it over the phone or if the information required needs to be documented verification, tell the customer:

  • they have 7 days to provide the information
  • that the overpayment will be raised and the intervention finalised with the information we have used if the requested information is not provided within the expected response time
  • Document the contact and conversation, see the Customer to call back DOC template in the Resources page for the documenting requirements

Place the intervention on hold for 14 days, see Step 5 in Table 2 in Coding the Contact screen in IRS.

Add a keyword to the IRS intervention. In Customer Record:

  • Go to Review Summary (IRRS) screen
  • 'T' the intervention (this will display the Manage Reviews (IRMR) screen)
  • In the Workgroup: field enter relevant workgroup
  • In the Position: field enter CO logon
  • Add the keyword 'DBTHLD'
  • Press [Enter]

Make sure the details of the conversation with the customer are recorded in the intervention DOC.

If a work item has been allocated, CO must assign it to themselves via Hold to User process in Table 5 of Work Optimiser for staff

When customer provides the information and a reassessment is undertaken, see Step 3 in this table.

If the customer fails to respond when the review comes DUE, go to Step 7.

5

Issue Pre-Adverse Decision Letter + Read more ...

Where there have been 3 unsuccessful call attempts made to the customer and/or partner to tell them of the outcome, a manual Q888 Pre-Adverse Decision letter must be issued to the customer and/or partner.

See the Resources page for the manual Q888 letter template. Make sure the correct letter template is used, based on the customer's circumstances.

Add a keyword to the IRS intervention. In Customer Record:

  • Go to Review Summary (IRRS) screen
  • 'T' the intervention (this will display the Manage Reviews (IRMR) screen)
  • In the Workgroup: field enter relevant workgroup
  • In the Position: field enter CO logon
  • Add the keyword 'Q888'
  • Press [Enter]
  • Allocate the IRS intervention to your workgroup and position

Assign the work item in Work Optimiser and DOC the record. The Resources page contains a link to the documenting requirements template.

Place the intervention on HLD for 28 days pending contact from the customer. To place the intervention on hold, navigate to the IRCS screen and code the following:

  • Method type: key 'MAI'
  • Date Contacted: key 'T' for today
  • Press [Enter], when the Confirm Y/N box appears, key 'Y'
  • Press [Enter] and the IRCD screen will be displayed

On IRCD screen code the following:

  • Expected response date: 28 days in the future
  • Person contacted: enter the name of the person who will receive the letter
  • Letter code: ADVERS
  • Address: enter the address where the letter will be sent
  • Text: Q888 Pre-Adverse Decision letter issued

If customer fails to respond when the review comes DUE, go to Step 7

6

Advise the customer + Read more ...

Advise the customer of the:

  • debt period
  • debt amount
  • reason for the debt, and
  • 'You will receive a letter with this information and instructions outlining the next steps.'

Record all information in the customer's intervention DOC to clearly explain the:

  • customer's circumstances
  • information relating to the compliance trigger
  • the scope of intervention
  • outcomes of the intervention

All DOCs on a customer's record are to provide a clear and concise history of the customer's interactions and must also adhere to the:

  • Online Documentation Recording (ODR) Standards and
  • Getting it Right (GIR) DOC Minimum Standards

The Resources page contains a link to the documenting requirements.

7

Raise debt in accordance with guidelines + Read more ...

Use the Debt Action script whenever possible.

Raise the debts (including partner) in accordance with current guidelines.

Note: Coronavirus Supplement debts are not to be bundled with other debts, they must be raised separately. Waiver provisions can be applied to Coronavirus Supplement debts.

Debts less than $50 can be waived.

Where a decision has been made to waive the debt due to a vulnerability concern identified on the customer and/or partner record, the debt must be raised then waived.

Make sure the recovery fee question is answered.

Note:

  • the date entered into the date contact made with customer question should be the date the Debt Action Script is run
  • if the debt amount generated via ADEX differs from the final debt amount being raised, fully document the reasons for this in the stand alone debt DOC
  • Coronavirus Supplement debts are raised under S1223(1) of the Social Security Act (S1223(1) for the script)
  • the reason code for Coronavirus Supplement debts should be - NEP - Not eligible for payment
  • the date for Coronavirus Supplement debts should be the 'payment date' â€“ the date the Coronavirus Supplement was paid in the system, not the date the customer received it

See Sending Account Payable letters for a table of debt reason codes, default text for the Account Payable (OPAOD) screen, and the rules for entering free text on the account payable letter.

Refer to DOC templates for UII in the Resources page for the documenting requirements.

2

Finalise the intervention + Read more ...

Check screens

First make sure all actions are complete on the customer (including any linked reviews) and (ex) partner records:

  • Check all activities on the Activity List (AL) screen are completed
  • Check all entries on the Review Contact Details (IRCD) screen are completed
    Response?: field: 'Y' or 'N'

Use the Telephony Intervention Processing script (TAPS) to finalise the intervention (except for FDC). If TAPS is unable to finalise the customer's or (ex) partner's intervention, use the Finalise Review script.

Prior to finalising the review, check to see that all updates made during the intervention process have been correctly attributed to the review within IRS. For example, all income or asset updates and suspension action have been attributed to the IRS review.

The TAPS or Finalise Review script will pause at the Attribute Result (IRAR) screen in IRS. The IRAR screen will include all activities on the customer record that have occurred over the life of the IRS review.

CO will need to select the activities that relate to the UII process. For example suspension, cancellation or a debt which resulted from updating the customer's financial circumstances. Make sure that a debt raised as part of UII is attributed to the review.

For more information on activities that need to be attributed to the review, see the following:

  • Attribute/unattributed activities as per current guidelines. See Finalising and attributing actions to a Service Update or Review in the Integrated Review System (IRS)
  • Choose an appropriate Result Reason as per current guidelines. See Calculating a Review Result and Restoration Processing in the Integrated Review System (IRS)

If the customer has a linked review or (ex) partner who also has an intervention, it is important to finalise all interventions at the same time.

If required, seek assistance from the QDO to confirm all updates have been correctly attributed prior to finalisation.

3

Issuing a Q765 advice + Read more ...

Send a Q765 unless:

  • the intervention outcome has been given over the phone
  • debt advice has been issued

A Q765 can be issued if requested by the customer.

Referral to the Intelligence and Investigations Branch

After completing an intervention, the case can be referred to the Intelligence and Investigations Branch if identified non-compliance is outside the scope of the intervention.
