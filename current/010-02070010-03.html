2

Institution must lodge an SY034 for assessment + Read more ...

Where an activity is run for a group of students, the institution must lodge an ABSTUDY Submission for away from base activity funding form (SY034).

Lodgement of an SY034 is not required from a student arranging their own activity with the institution. However, they must provide written confirmation from the institution of the date and period of the activity.

Away from Base claims must:

  • be lodged by the ABSTUDY closing date. See Closing date for ABSTUDY claims, and
  • provide all necessary information for approval to occur

The submission form must be referred to when assessing approval for an Away from Base activity.

Has the submission or statement from the institution for a student, been lodged for the activity by the closing date?

  • Yes, go to Step 4
  • No, the activity is not approved. See Step 8 in Table 4

3

Request to vary an approved submission + Read more ...

Educational institution lodges a request to make changes after the original Away From Base submission. All requests must be made in writing.

Students that require a variation to an approved Away From Base activity must provide written confirmation from the education institution detailing the changes.

Variations can be approved if they meet the Away from Base activity approval criteria.

Has a written request or statement from the institution been lodged for the variation?

  • Yes, reassess the activity based on the variation. Go to Step 4
  • No, advise the education institution or student via a Q999 letter that the variation is not approved. Individual course approval criteria

Table 2

Step

Action

1

Indigenous Youth Mobility Programme (IYMP) + Read more ...

Is the activity to determine suitability for a placement in the IYMP?

  • Yes, go to Step 4
  • No, go to Step 2

2

Ensure the activity is an approved course for ABSTUDY purposes + Read more ...

Check the National Course Approvals sub-site to determine if the course is approved for ABSTUDY.

Is the course for which the testing and assessment activity is being held, an approved course for ABSTUDY purposes?

  • Yes, go to Step 3
  • No, the activity is not approved. See Step 8 in Table 4

3

Course requirements + Read more ...

The institution handbook will detail the entry requirements for individual courses. If not, a letter of confirmation is needed from the institution that a testing and assessment activity is an alternative entry option.

The course of study that the testing and assessment activity relates to must be:

  • a secondary level enabling course at a university where it is:
    • an alternative entry to a mainstream higher education course, and
    • essential a preliminary assessment is done before being admitted
  • an approved tertiary course where:
    • a selection test, interview or audition is a compulsory requirement for entry to the course, or
    • it is essential to determine the general suitability to do the course, or
  • an approved tertiary course must conduct a selection test/interview because the student's academic ability cannot be assessed from previous study. See ABSTUDY eligibility criteria for Testing and Assessment Award for more information

Does the course meet 1 of the above requirements?

  • Yes, go to Step 4
  • No, the activity is not approved. See Step 8 in Table 4

4

Ensure testing and assessment activity has not been done for this course + Read more ...

A testing and assessment activity should only need to happen once for a course in a year.

An activity will not be approved where:

  • an institution has been unsuccessful in obtaining sufficient student numbers from a previous ABSTUDY funded testing, and
  • assessment activity is for the same course intake in a year

Has a testing and assessment activity already been done for this course in the same calendar year?

  • Yes, activity is not approved. See Step 8 in Table 4
  • No, see Step 2 in Table 4

5

Tertiary level (including Masters or Doctorate level) course + Read more ...

Is the course a tertiary level (including Masters or Doctorate level) course?

  • Yes, see Step 1 in Table 3
  • No, the activity is not approved. See Step 8 in Table 4

Activity location

Table 3

Step

Action

1

Location of activity + Read more ...

Restrictions apply about the location of proposed activities.

If the activity is a:

  • placement:
    • at the study location, see Step 1 in Table 4, or
    • not at the study location, go to Step 2
  • field trip, go to Step 4
  • residential school, go to Step 5

2

Placement + Read more ...

It is expected all placement activities be done at the study location. Only in exceptional circumstances can a placement be approved at a location other than the place of study. Such circumstances could include:

  • no local practitioners operate in the field of study
  • all available local placements have been filled, or
  • the placement is in a specialist field (e.g. teaching remote students)

Will the placement be in the student's home state/territory due to any of the above circumstances?

  • Yes, see Step 1 in Table 4
  • No, go to Step 3

3

Interstate placement + Read more ...

A placement may be approved for Away from Base assistance if 1 of the following applies:

  • travel between New South Wales (NSW) and the Australian Capital Territory (ACT)
  • interstate travel because the students attend an education institution near a state/territory border, or
  • interstate travel because experience or training essential for completion of the course requirements is unavailable in the home state/territory

Does the placement meet 1 of the above requirements?

  • Yes, see Step 1 in Table 4
  • No, the activity is not approved. See Step 8 in Table 4

4

Field trip + Read more ...

It is expected all learning experiences would normally be done at the study location. If mandatory learning experiences are unavailable at the study location, the activity must be at the closest alternate location to minimise the activity cost.

A field trip can only be held at an interstate location in the following circumstances:

  • travel between NSW and ACT
  • interstate travel because the institution is near a state/territory border, or
  • interstate travel because experience or training essential for completion of the course requirements is unavailable in the home state/territory

Is the activity held at the closest location that can offer the necessary learning experiences?

  • Yes, see Step 1 in Table 4
  • No, the activity is not approved. See Step 8 in Table 4

5

Residential school + Read more ...

A residential school may be approved for Away from Base assistance if it is being done at:

  • the education institution location, or
  • another location in the same state/territory (where the face-to-face tuition components cannot be done at the education institution or at another closer location)

Does the residential school activity meet 1 of the above conditions?

  • Yes, see Step 1 in Table 4
  • No, the activity is not approved. See Step 8 in Table 4

Conditions for activity approval

Table 4

Step

Action

1

Activity an important part of the course + Read more ...

Will the academic work completed during the activity contribute to the course assessment?

  • Yes, the institution must confirm this in writing. The education institution must also provide course and subject outlines for verification. Go to Step 2
  • No, the activity is not approved, go to Step 8

2

Away from Base activities must be of reasonable duration + Read more ...

Some limits on Away from Base entitlements apply.

Testing and assessment activities must not exceed 5 days. Any submission requesting funding for an activity more than 5 days, must seek approval by the Department of Social Services (DSS).

The maximum Away from Base assistance available for a field trip is 7 days, regardless of the length of the trip.

Residential schools usually involve a period of 1 or 2 weeks, unless the institution provides a valid reason.

See examples on the Resources page.

Is the length of the activity justified?

  • Yes, and the activity is a:
    • testing and assessment activity, go to Step 4
    • placement, field trip or residential school, go to Step 3
  • No, the activity is not approved, go to Step 8

3

Assistance under the Indigenous Advancement Strategy (IAS) + Read more ...

Are the costs of student participation in the activity approved for assistance under the IAS?

  • Yes, the activity is not approved, go to Step 8
  • No, go to Step 4

4

ABSTUDY can provide Away from Base assistance for Indigenous students + Read more ...

Assistance is only available where non-Indigenous students in the same course, are expected to cover their own costs for the activity.

If there are non-Indigenous students in the course, are they expected to cover their own costs for the same activity?

  • Yes, go to Step 5
  • No, the activity is not approved, go to Step 8

5

Activity meets reasonable costs + Read more ...

The activity may not be approved unless it meets reasonable costs.

Is the activity of reasonable cost?

  • Yes, go to Step 6
  • No, the activity is not approved, go to Step 8

6

Checking previous advances for Away from Base funding have been acquitted + Read more ...

Funding advances for single activities must be acquitted by the student or education institution within 1 calendar month of completing the activity. No future activities can be funded until the previous activity has been acquitted.

To check for the acquittal of payments made in advance to an education institution or student:

  • run the ABSTUDY Operational Information (OI) reimbursement report
  • if the report returns a result of any payments with an Assessment Outcome of 'Paid in Advance', the payment has not been acquitted

Has the previous advance for funding of an Away from Base activity been acquitted?

  • Yes, the activity is approved, go to Step 7
  • No, the activity is not approved, go to Step 8

7

Activity is approved + Read more ...

Funding is payable in advance or reimbursed after completion of the activity.

Funding can only be made for students assessed as eligible to attend an ABSTUDY Away from Base activity.

If an education institution or student has requested funding to be paid in advance:

  • code a manual review for 1 months' time for follow-up of lodgement of acquittal details
  • on maturity of the review, see Acquittal of funding for ABSTUDY Away from Base allowances
  • record details on a DOC on both the student's and the education institution's records

See Approval of ABSTUDY Away from Base assistance to calculate the authorised allowances for the approved Away from Base activity.

Procedure ends here.

8

Activity is not approved + Read more ...

The Away from Base activity is not approved. Finalise the assessment outcome in the Travel Management Application (TMA). See Coding Fares Allowance (FAA) reimbursements and Away from Base entitlements for ABSTUDY and ABSTUDY Pensioner Education Supplement (PES).

Procedure ends here.
