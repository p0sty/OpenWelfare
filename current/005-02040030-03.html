Determining financial assistance for an unaccompanied humanitarian minor (UHM) 005-02040030

This page contains the process to follow when an unaccompanied humanitarian minor (UHM) contacts Centrelink and requests financial assistance. Parts of this procedure can be completed by a social worker or the Special Benefits Processing team only.

UHM contacts to request financial assistance

Step

Action

1

A customer contacts for assistance and identifies as being a unaccompanied humanitarian minor (UHM) + Read more ...

Is the customer under the age of 16, or aged 16 - 19 and in full time secondary study?

    • Does the customer have a Customer Reference Number (CRN)?
    • Who does the customer live with?
    • Does a parent/guardian or potential Approved Care Organisation (ACO) support the customer?
    • Is the customer supported by the Department of Home Affairs UHM Program Support Allowance (UPSA), (previously known as 'Maintenance Allowance for Refugees)? The Other Government Payment (OGPS) screen in ISIS will still display as MAR (Maintenance Allowance for Refugees)
    • Is the customer engaged in full time study/training? (Only if customer is over the age of 16)?
  • Immediately refer the customer to a social worker. Procedure ends here
  • Social workers, go to Step 2
  • Special Benefits Processing team, go to Step 7
  • No, go to Step 9

2

Authorised party + Read more ...

For Services Australia social workers only

Check if there is an authorised party who can receive Family Tax Benefit (FTB) for the customer. This could be:

  • an Approved Care Organisation, or
  • a foster carer, or
  • a parent/guardian

See, Eligibility for Family Tax Benefit (FTB) for Approved Care Organisations (ACO), or Eligibility for Family Tax Benefit (FTB) for a child in foster care

If the authorised party can receive Family Tax Benefit (FTB) for the customer, they may claim Double Orphan Pension (DOP), see Eligibility and coding claims for Double Orphan Pension (DOP).

Is there an authorised party who can receive FTB for the customer?

  • Yes, go to Step 3
  • No, go to Step 4

3

Approved Care Organisation (ACO) or foster carer to claim Family Tax Benefit (FTB) and Double Orphan Pension (DOP) + Read more ...

Tell the customer the authorised party or foster carer must claim FTB and DOP for the customer.

Note: an organisation must be approved as an ACO before claiming FTB.

If already approved, an Approved Care Organisation must lodge the provided 'ACO admission, transfer or discharge spreadsheet' when a child is admitted, transferred or discharged from the organisation. For more details see the Resources page.

For foster carers, see Claiming Family Tax Benefit (FTB) instalments for children in care other than a newborn or adopted child.

Customers must claim FTB before their DOP claim can be assessed. If the child is not a FTB child, they will not qualify as a DOP child.

Procedure ends here.

4

Is the customer in the guardianship of a state or territory government? + Read more ...

  • Yes, go to Step 5
  • No, they may qualify for Special Benefit - Under 16 years of age. Go to Step 6

5

State/territory support + Read more ...

If the customer is in state or territory care they may be eligible for certain Income Support Payments. If the customer is:

  • aged 16 - 19, they may be eligible for Youth Allowance (YA), go to Step 9
  • under 16 years of age, they may be eligible for Special Benefit (SpB) if the state/territory's support is insufficient to cover their everyday living expenses.
    UHMs in state care are assessed as independent and therefore exempt from the parental means test

Social Workers must determine if the support provided is sufficient by looking at income and expenses (for example, if the customer receives $200 a fortnight and rent is $240 a fortnight, this support may not be sufficient). See examples in the Resources page.

Is the state/territory's support sufficient for the customer?

  • Yes, Special Benefit is not payable. Customer may lodge a claim for SpB if they wish to access a right to review. Procedure ends here
  • No, the customer may qualify for Special Benefit if they are:
    • under 16 years of age and a permanent visa holder, or
    • under 18 and a Temporary Visa Holder (785 or 790)
  • Go to Step 6

6

Claim for Special Benefit (SpB) + Read more ...

Assist the customer to claim SpB. See Eligibility and new claim procedures for Special Benefit (SpB). Procedure ends here for social workers.

7

Referral to helpdesk to verify level of support + Read more ...

For Special Benefit Processing team only

Service Officers must forward the customer's details to the Special Benefit Helpdesk using the webform. The Special Benefit Helpdesk will check with Department of Home Affairs about the level of support provided. See the Resources page for the webform link.

Insert the following information into the Enquiry Description section of the webform:

Customer has identified as an unaccompanied humanitarian minor (UHM), please obtain the following information from Department of Home Affairs:

  • Is the person a state ward under the guardianship of the Minister and an UAM?
  • Is the person receiving any financial support?
    How much is this support and for how long will the support be paid by Department of Home Affairs or any of its service providers?
  • Is the person receiving in kind support, for example, is he/she receiving free board and lodgings?
  • Is the person being placed in the care of someone, and would this someone be able to claim Family Tax Benefit for the child?

When a response from the Special Benefit Helpdesk is received, go to Step 8

8

Action Special Benefit (SpB) claim when email received from helpdesk + Read more ...

The Special Benefit Helpdesk will reply to the email with the details of the level of support for determining eligibility for SpB.

  • If the level of support is not sufficient, assess the SpB claim, see Eligibility and new claim procedures for Special Benefit (SpB)
  • If sufficient level of support provided, reject the SpB claim

Procedure ends here.

9

Customer is over 16 years of age, normal Youth Allowance eligibility criteria applies + Read more ...

  • If the customer is not a full time student, see Claiming Youth Allowance (YA) (Job Seeker)
  • If the customer is a full time student, and FTB is not appropriate, see Initial contact with students or Australian Apprentices claiming Youth Allowance (YA)
