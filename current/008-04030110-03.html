DES referrals + Read more ...

If the job seeker:

2

Referral request - check eligibility for DES + Read more ...

In some circumstances where a job seeker has previously exited from DES and is now requesting to be re-referred, a new Employment Services Assessment (ESAt) may be required before they can recommence a DES placement, even if they already have a current ESAt or Job Capacity Assessment (JCA) that recommends referral to DES.

Check if the job seeker has previously been exited from DES in Process Direct:

  • key 'RRSUM' in the Super Key field, press [Enter] or
  • select > Participation Summary > Activity type/Non-applied exemptions > Referral summary

Has the job seeker previously exited from DES?

  • Yes, go to Step 3
  • No, go to Step 4

3

Job seeker has previously exited from DES + Read more ...

Check if the reason for the exit was one of the following:

  • the job seeker achieved a 26 week outcome
  • the program review did not recommend extended employment assistance (past 18 months)
  • the participant completed extended employment assistance but has not achieved an outcome (that is, exit following 24 months in employment assistance)
  • the exit occurred while the participant was receiving ongoing support

Did the job seeker previously exit from DES for one of the above reasons?

  • Yes, the job seeker cannot be re-referred to DES unless recommended by a current and valid assessment of the job seeker's work capacity completed after the exit from DES
    • if a new assessment is required, see Job Capacity Assessment (JCA) Referral or Employment Services Assessment (ESAt) Referral
    • otherwise, go to Step 4
  • No, go to Step 4

4

Check the following before coding referral + Read more ...

  • A current and valid assessment of the job seeker's work capacity with a DES - Disability Management Service or DES - Employment Support Service referral recommendation. Note: to view an existing ESAt report go to the Referral Summary (RRSUM) screen in Process Direct and select the relevant ESAt/JCA report
  • If there is no current and valid assessment, advise the job seeker an ESAt is required to determine if DES is the most appropriate employment assistance. Wherever possible, the Assessor will then make the referral through Online Diary based on the job seekers' choice of provider
  • A job seeker with a baseline work capacity of 0-7 hours per week can be referred to DES if they have a work capacity (with intervention) of greater than 0-7 hours per week or if it is clearly noted in the report they could achieve a higher work capacity with ongoing intervention (8+ hours per week)
  • A job seeker with an assessed current or future work capacity of 30+ will not be eligible for a DES referral after 1 July 2021 even if a current ESAt or JCA indicates a DES recommendation
  • Active Job Seeker Registration. This can be for Volunteers not in receipt of payment or Fully Eligible Participants receiving a payment that has no mutual obligation requirements
  • Ensure job seeker has not been working at or above their Employment Benchmark hours for the last 6 fortnights. Job seekers working at or above their Employment Benchmark may be eligible for Workforce Australia if they are looking to increase their work hours

Is the job seeker a full-time student who needs to be registered as a job seeker?

  • Yes, go to Step 5
  • No, go to Step 6

5

Youth Allowance or ABSTUDY full-time student or Australian Apprentice requires a DES referral + Read more ...

Manual referral through the Employment Systems Helpdesk is required as the job seeker registration cannot be completed by Services Australia.

If the job seeker contacts about:

  • a new claim, advise the job seeker Services Australia will arrange an ESAt referral as part of processing the claim. Procedure ends here
  • a change in circumstances, go to Step 6.

6

Create new referral to DES + Read more ...

If the job seeker is currently receiving assistance from another Employment Services Provider contact the current provider and advise them about the proposed new referral action being taken.

In Process Direct

  • select Customer Summary
  • enter the customer's CRN, select Go
  • in the customer's record select > > Participation Summary > Online diary > Create new referral > DES â€“ Disability employment Service
  • An eligibility check will be done automatically as part of this process:
    • If the job seeker's eligibility for DES is not confirmed by Online Diary, an error message will be displayed. This will usually be because the job seeker does not have a current JCA or ESAt recommending referral to DES, or no longer meets the eligibility criteria
    • The Online Diary prevents job seekers being inappropriately referred where the job seeker has a temporary reduced work capacity of 0-7 hours per week. Once the period of temporary reduced work capacity of 0-7 hours per week has expired and the job seeker meets all other eligibility criteria, the Online Diary should allow the job seeker to be referred
    • If the job seeker has been and is still working at or above their Employment Benchmark for the last 6 fortnights, the DES referral should be deferred

Note: job seekers with mutual obligation requirements assessed as having a partial capacity to work of 8 - 14 hours or a temporary reduced work capacity of 8 - 14 hours can voluntarily participate DES.

A list of providers is displayed on the List Providers screen based on the proximity to the job seekers address. This screen displays the available providers in the job seeker's area.

To make the displayed list of DES providers more specific select the Refine Advanced search type button to search by the following parameters:

  • provider name
  • provider postcode
  • suburb

This can be used when the job seeker wants to be referred to a DES provider outside their local area. Advise the job seeker:

  • one provider can be excluded
  • a provider can be randomly selected if they are unable to make a choice

To update preferences/exclusions, select the relevant check box for a provider and then select the Refresh provider list button to confirm selection.

Referring job seekers to DES and recording outcomes

Table 2: This table provides a summary of the process for making referrals to appropriate providers and recording outcomes of the referral to DES.

Step

Action

1

Determine suitability of providers + Read more ...

The following information about the provider may be considered:

  • type of assistance provided
  • work options
  • specialisation in particular types of disabilities
  • distance from their home
  • transport availability

It is possible that not all services in the local area would be discussed with the job seeker. For example, it would not be appropriate to refer a job seeker who is a quadriplegic to a service specialising in assisting job seekers with a hearing impairment.

To locate the contact details of providers, go to the Workforce Australia website.

2

Job seeker selects preferred provider + Read more ...

Service Officers are to provide job seekers with information to assist them choose a provider however, they must not choose a provider for them. Where a job seeker does not want to choose a provider the random allocation process must be utilised.

Job seekers can change their choice of DES provider up to 5 times over a 2 year period by calling the Department of Employment and Workplace Relations at any time without needing to provide a reason.

Has the job seeker selected their preferred provider?

  • Yes, select the preferred provider from the list. The Provider Details screen displays. Go to Step 3
  • No and the job seeker
    • does not have mutual obligation requirements, give the job seeker information about suitable providers. Procedure ends here
    • has mutual obligation requirements, press the 'random allocation' button (select once) to randomly allocate a DES provider for the job seeker. Go to Step 3

3

Check for appointment availability + Read more ...

Are there any appointments for the provider listed on the Available Appointments screen?

  • Yes, go to Step 4
  • No, if the status on the Provider List Screen displays:
    • No available timeslots - staff are able to call the DES provider to open more appointments. Where this status is displayed, providers have no right of refusal and should make times available
    • Full - at maximum caseload - staff are able to call the provider to see if they are interested in increasing their maximum caseload for the purposes of accepting a referral However, the provider is not obligated to comply with this request, and if they do not choose to increase their caseload the customer will need to choose another provider
    • Not Available - these providers cannot accept referrals, and staff should not contact these providers.
  • If the provider opens further appointments, re-open online diary to view all available appointments.

To locate the contact details of providers, go to the Workforce Australia website.

4

Available appointments + Read more ...

The Online Appointment System (OAS) displays appointments available with the DES service provider within the next 28 days

The appointments are listed from soonest to latest.

Has a suitable appointment been chosen?

  • Yes, go to Step 5
  • No, retry referral later,
    • If the job seeker has mutual obligation requirements, Services Australia should make reasonable attempts to find a suitable appointment time, provided this does not delay the job seeker's connection with DES. Procedure ends here

5

Book appointment + Read more ...

  • Select the appointment date to book the appointment
  • The Appointment Details screen is displayed
  • A different appointment can still be booked by selecting the Appointment Selection button. This will reopen the Available Appointments screen
  • Select job seeker's preferred date and time on the Appointment Details screen
  • Select Book Appointment
  • Advise job seeker of appointment details
  • If referring a job seeker with mutual obligation requirements to DES:
    • Notify the job seeker about the requirement to attend any interview with the recommended provider, and the possible consequences if they fail to attend. Notification about requirement to attend does not apply if a job seeker receiving a payment with mutual obligation requirements has a partial capacity to work (baseline and with intervention work capacity) of less than 15 hours per week. In this case the job seeker is not required to participate in DES. They may voluntarily exit from DES at any time, and are not subject to any penalty if they choose not to participate in DES
    • Record the notification on a DOC
    • The DES provider will renegotiate the job seeker's Job Plan when they commence the program. This applies to all job seekers, regardless of whether they have mutual obligation requirements. Staff do not add participation in DES into a job seeker's Job Plan

Note: Services Australia can reschedule this initial appointment only when the appointment time is still in the future (this does not apply if the DES provider has recorded the referral themselves).

6

Placement and outcomes of the referral + Read more ...

  • Referral, placement, non-acceptance, and finalisation of the referral are automatically updated in the Referrals system, and can be viewed under the Referrals Summary in the job seeker's record. Provider appointments can be viewed using the Online Diary workflow
  • If the job seeker does not attend their initial appointment, the service provider is responsible for rescheduling the appointment
  • All DES referral events are updated automatically on the job seeker's record, including cessation of DES placement

Has the job seeker been placed by a DES provider but the correct DES status is not displaying?

  • Yes, go to Step 7
  • No, procedure ends here

7

DES placement not displaying correctly + Read more ...

Confirm the job seeker's placement with the DES provider. If confirmed:

  • the details of the placement and/or finalisation recorded by the DES Provider in the Employment Services system have not been sent to the system correctly
  • contact the Department of Employment and Workplace Relations
  • advise the Employment Systems Helpdesk operator that a TT155 advice is required as the DES data is not displaying in the system
  • Employment Systems Helpdesk will then resend the TT155 transaction and this will update the system

8

DES referral did not proceed + Read more ...

An automatic issue is created when a job seeker with mutual obligation requirements is referred to DES, and the referral does not proceed - meaning that the job seeker's DES referral status went from 'referred' to 'finalised', and did not result in a placement.

The details of the Issue are:

  • System code: NSS
  • Issue code: 005030
  • Issue text: Job seeker has not commenced in their approved activity of DES as expected - referral action required

If the assessor has since made a new referral recommendation or referred to a new provider, there is no requirement for any further Services Australia action on this activity.

If the job seeker still has a current and valid ESAt which recommends referral to DES and the referral has not been successful, investigate the reason why the referral did not proceed.
