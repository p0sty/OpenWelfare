2

Type of assets owned by the customer + Read more ...

Customers may own other assets, which are not real estate. These can include:

  • the portion of a principal home used to operate a business. This is included in the assets of the business
  • assets that are recorded on Other Assets (OAS) screen. For example:
    • marina berths
    • boats
    • licences (taxi or fishing)
    • race animals (horse or greyhound)
    • art, coin or stamp collections

Notes:

  • Assets such as airplane hangars or car park spaces must be recorded as real estate type OTH
  • Self-contained flats and other dwellings on the same title as the principal home which are currently rented (other than to a near relative), must be recorded as real estate type SCF:
    • Real estate assets coded as SCF are not in the risk profiling and auto-indexation cycle. The nature of this arrangement is not suited to indexation
    • For more information about Dual Occupancy, see the Background page of Assessing house and curtilage

Does the customer own assets that are not real estate?

  • Yes, go to Step 3
  • No, only real estate, go to Step 4

3

Valuation of non - real estate assets + Read more ...

Non-real estate assets include, but are not limited to:

  • art, coin or stamp collections
  • fishing licences or other licences
  • taxi licences
  • race animals, for example, horse or greyhound
  • vintage or classic car collections
  • other livestock
  • marina berths
  • plant and equipment
  • rent roll

Any evidence the customer can supply to help determine the value of the other asset must be provided. For example, documents about recent sales or private professional appraisals. This helps establish if a valuation is required. Only ask the customer to provide documents if they already have them. Do not ask the customer to pay for new valuation assessments or reports.

A valuation is only required where:

  • the customer is likely to be impacted by the assets test and/or
  • the customer's estimate of the asset value appears unreasonable

If a valuation is required for non-real estate assets, complete and submit a SA470 to the Valuation Helpdesk. See the Resources page for a link.

When the value of a non-real estate asset has been determined, see Coding income and assets for Centrelink payments and services for coding information.

Taxi licences

With the introduction of ridesharing services, such as Uber and Ola over the past several years, most Australian states have undergone major taxi licencing reforms. This means that some taxi licences no longer hold any value.

Licences in these states have no assessable value from the following dates:

  • NSW – 1 February 2023
  • VIC – 1 July 2018
  • SA – 1 July 2016
  • NT – 1 January 1999

Refer to Resources for all other states and territories.

If a valuation is still required, email a copy of the taxi licence if available, and a completed SA470 form to the Valuation Helpdesk. Ensure the following details about the taxi licence are included in Question 16 of the form:

  • Type of licence (metropolitan, regional)
  • The location for the licence to operate (Mandurah, Hobart etc.)

Procedure ends here

4

Risk based model + Read more ...

A risk based model for assessment of real estate values automatically assesses a customer’s individual circumstances to determine the appropriate ongoing method of assessment for their property. This may be:

  • acceptance of the customer's estimated value
  • a check of the customer's estimated value

The risk based model will be applied to most properties, such as houses, units and townhouses, and the system will automatically initiate the process to determine if a customer’s estimate is acceptable or a valuation is required.

Other properties are also included such as:

  • RET: retail premises
  • CMP: commercial premises
  • IDP: industrial premises
  • FRM: farm
  • MGD: market garden
  • HBF: hobby farm
  • BHB: bush block
  • HMB: home block on greater than 2 hectares
  • VAC: vacant blocks

Not included in the risk based model

Some property is not included in the risk based model and will require a valuation request to ensure the customers assets are assessed correctly. These can include:

  • overseas properties
  • property that has been gifted
  • a person's home when they enter residential care
  • property considered as Other (OTH) such as car parks and marina berths
  • properties that have never been advised and historical valuations are required
  • self-contained properties (SCF)

Go to step 5

6

Previously recorded property + Read more ...

If the property has previously been recorded and the customer or partner has provided a new estimated value as part of a new claim, the existing values may remain unchanged.

If the Asset Valuation Source: is IDX is less than 12 months ago go to Step 8

Do not update the Current Market Value and Asset Valuation Source:

  • if Asset Valuation Source: VAL or ONS and the Actual Valuation Date: is less than 12 months ago, no updates are required
  • unless the customer has advised of a significant change to the property value, see Maintaining the value of real estate assets on customer records. This is for the Current Market Value, Asset Valuation Source or Current House & Curtilage fields (if applicable)

Update the Current Market Value and Asset Valuation Source if the:

  • new estimate is higher than the recorded value, or
  • Asset Valuation Source: IDX, VAL or ONS and the Actual Valuation Date: is greater than 12 months ago, or
  • Asset Valuation Source is any other code, then:
    • insert a new entry on the RE screen (must be ‘Insert’ not ‘Correct’ – do not change the existing valuation details)
    • add the Event Date and Actual valuation date as the date of the claim
    • update the Current Market Value field with the customer's estimate enter the Asset Valuation Source field as CLI

Procedure ends here.

7

Property not previously recorded + Read more ...

Complete all mandatory fields in RE, REVI and REBI screens. For more details, Assessing and coding real estate details

Record any new real estate details where a valuation is not required using the customer's estimated value and the valuation source code of CLI (client estimate).

The Other Circumstances and Directions to the property field on the REVI screen allows free text. Use this field to record any additional information, for example, 'water rights to be included in valuation'. Providing all available information will enable a successful and accurate valuation.

A property valuation may need to be requested if:

  • the value of the customer's total assets is within $10,000 of the assets limit
  • the customer's estimate of real estate value seems too low or unreasonable and a higher valuation will affect payment
  • the customer has a partially constructed home which is to be assessed as an asset

See Resources for examples of Unreasonable estimated property values

Note: do not request a valuation, until qualification has been determined and the claim can be assessed. For example, do not request a valuation until it has been determined the claimant qualifies for a payment such as, an impairment and a continuing inability to work at a level that qualifies for DSP and all required documentation has been provided.

  • No, Procedure ends here

Customer claiming Pension Bonus + Read more ...

A valuation must be requested if the customer is claiming:

  • Age Pension and registered for Pension Bonus Scheme (PBS), or
  • Pension Bonus Bereavement Payment (PBBP)

These claims cannot be finalised until the valuation is complete.

Go to Step 10

10

Request a valuation + Read more ...

Check the property has been correctly coded on the RE and REBI screens. Make sure the address is correct and a Lands Title Reference has been provided to locate the property.

If the valuation is for a:

  • new property advised by the customer, continue to Request a valuation within Customer First
  • property within a new claim
    • in Process Direct, apply handover function
    • in Customer First continue below

Request a valuation within Customer First

  • Go to the Real Estate Business Details (REBS) screen
  • Select the relevant property
  • On the Real Estate Valuation Information (REVI) screen update the following:
    • Event Date: to today’s date Note: do not use any dates other than ‘today’
    • Code all relevant details
    • Other Circumstances – delete any text that is not relevant to the current valuation request and add the words 'High Priority - Desktop'
    • Authority to Inspect - Contact Name: and Phone: update if these have been provided by the customer
  • Finalise the activity
  • DOC the customer record with the reason for the valuation request
  • Refer to an APS5 or above to submit the valuation request, following local protocols or via Direct Referral to SSO - see the Resources page for a link to the webform
  • If an E607CQ error occurs, complete the required work around
  • Place the claim on hold for 14 days or code a manual review to check if the valuation has been returned

Submitting request for valuation (for APS5 or above only)

Valuations are completed at a cost to the agency, check the referral is valid and all necessary information has been updated correctly for the property.

On the REVI screen:

  • In the Request Valuation/Profile field: record 'VAL’
  • [Enter] to submit request
  • Check the Future Activity List (FAL) screen to confirm the valuation request has been sent. A successful request for a valuation will show the property ID number matching the ID on the REBS screen.
  • There will not be an activity generated on the AL screen.

Procedure ends here until valuation returned.

11

Submission of a valuation request SA470 form + Read more ...

Some real estate asset valuations require a submission using a Services Australia Valuation Request (SA470) form. These submissions are required for real estate:

  • not included in the risk based model
  • requiring historical valuation dates

See Resources for a link to the Services Australia Valuation Request (SA470) form.

Staff should refer to an APS5 or above to determine if a valuation request is for a valid reason. This includes determining if it is approved when the circumstances are not listed below.

If a valuation request is required complete the SA470 in an editable format, see Resources for instructions:

  • Include the reason for the valuation request in the SA470t.
  • DOC the customer record with the reason for the valuation request
  • Include Urgent in the summary line and reason in the body of the email, if the request is urgent
  • Email the SA470 to the Valuation Helpdesk

Circumstances where a valuation may be required

Overseas real estate

A valuation is required when:

  • the property is in United Kingdom, United States of America or New Zealand
  • the customer's estimate appears to be unreasonable and does not fall within $20,000 of the asset impact level, or
  • the customer is (or is likely to be) impacted by the assets test

In all other cases, accept the customer's estimate

Gifting

  • A valuation is required for all gifting assessments that involve real estate
  • Ensure the gifting date and any relevant details are included at Question 16 of the SA470

Self-Contained Flats/Other real estate

  • Self-Contained Flats (SCF) and Other (OTH) real estate, such as car parks will not be included in the risk based model
  • A valuation can be requested where the:
    • customer is likely to be impacted by the assets test and/or
    • estimated asset value appears unreasonable

See Resources for examples of unreasonable estimated property values.

Undisclosed properties

Complete an SA470 for relevant historical valuations for undisclosed properties. Include full details of the:

  • valuations required including historical dates, and
  • permission to inspect, if it has been obtained

If the SA470 has been completed in regard to any suspected fraud or corruption:

  • add to the subject line of the email that the request is for a fraud or corruption investigation
  • confirm if the valuation and/or witness statements are required

Note: if the request includes a witness statement, attach the Witness Statement template relevant to the jurisdiction and the SA470 to the email request.

Other real estate assets not included in the risk based model

The following properties are not suitable for inclusion in the risk based model:

  • Real estate type OTH (other real estate assets for example car parks, marina berths)
  • Real estate linked to a business
  • Exempt real estate assets

Procedure ends here

12

Automatic risk profiling when claim finalised or new real estate added + Read more ...

The Asset Valuation Source on the Real Estate (RE) screen will be automatically updated to NVE - Valuation/Estimate check is not required. The real estate value will be automatically profiled.

Estimate verification required

If an estimate verification is required:

  • the system will automatically submit the customer's estimated value for verification
  • a future activity will be on FAL screen with the property ID number(s). For example, Estimation: 2
  • When the estimation has been returned and it is:
    • acceptable, the Valuation Source code on the RE screen will update as EST - Estimate Verified
    • not acceptable the property will be automatically submitted for valuation

Valuation required

If a valuation is required:

  • the property will be automatically submitted for valuation
  • a future activity will be on the FAL screen with the property ID number(s). For example, Valuation 1
  • when the valuation is returned it will update on the RE screen with a Valuation Source code of VAL

Procedure ends here.

  • is a claim still to be finalised, go to Step 2

Claim not finalised + Read more ...

When the assessed value is returned, the claim activity will be updated:

  • on the RE screen:
    • the Current Market Value, Valuers Job Number and Valuer Property Number fields will be coded

Valuation resulted in rate change + Read more ...

If a valuation has resulted in a change to the customer's rate of payment they will automatically be sent a rate change letter. The letter will say there has been a change to the value of real estate assets.

If the customer does not agree with the property valuation, see Maintaining the value of real estate assets on customer records.

4

Follow up required- error keywords + Read more ...

If follow up is required in:

  • Process Direct a work item will be assigned with a relevant keyword
  • Customer First an MFU will show on the AL screen with a relevant keyword

To action:

  • check the details of each Keyword below
  • follow the instructions provided
  • check the customers record for a doc from the Valuation Helpdesk with details of the action requirements

Keyword REBEXP- Valuation or estimation data not received

  • The valuation or estimation data has not been received within the required timeframe
  • If the data is received automatically after creation of the MFU, the system will automatically upload the valuation amount and finalise the MFU
  • If the valuation has not been returned contact the Valuation Helpdesk who will contact the valuation provider to confirm the expected date of receipt

Keyword ERRSLD - Property sold follow up required

  • The valuation provider has advised the property has been sold
  • Contact the customer and update the customer's record as required

Keyword - REJISI - Insufficient information follow up required

  • There has not been enough information provided to enable the valuation provider to complete this valuation
  • Check the customers record for any scanned information that may have been provided eg. MOD R
  • The customer may have to be contacted to request/confirm required details
  • The REVI screen on the customer’s record must be updated with the required information
  • Once the record has been updated, a manual VAL request must be re-submitted by an APS 5 or above

Keyword REJPMM - Property mismatch follow up required - returned by provider

  • The information provided was not sufficient to correctly identify the property to complete the valuation
  • The customer may have to be contacted to request/confirm required details
  • Contact the Valuation Helpdesk for more information

Keyword REJNAP - Property error

  • There has not been enough information provided to successfully complete the valuation
  • Contact the Valuation Helpdesk for more information

Keyword REBIMM - Internal mismatch

  • A property mismatch can occur when the Pensions system tries to apply the returned estimation or valuation data
  • Contact the Valuation Helpdesk for more information

Keyword REBUVR - Unsolicited VAL, ONS or EST response

  • A valuation response has been received with no request outstanding
  • If a customer's relationship status changes while a valuation is in progress, only the customer's record will be updated
  • Complete the required work around on the customer and/or former/surviving partner record if required
  • Contact the Valuation Helpdesk for details of the property valuation, to update the real estate details
