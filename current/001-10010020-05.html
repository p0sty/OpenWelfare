Key messages for the Targeted Compliance Framework

Table 1: This table describes key Targeted Compliance Framework messages that Service Officers deliver at various participation related contacts.

Item

Description

1

Meeting your requirements + Read more ...

Service Officer: 'Be aware and know what your requirements are. It's important for you to meet your requirements and record or report your attendance where asked to do so. This is so your payments are not affected and you don't get a financial penalty.'

2

Missing your requirements + Read more ...

Service Officer: 'If you are in the green or warning zone and you miss a requirement you will receive a message from the Department of Education, Skills and Employment. The message will tell you what you need to do to avoid your payment being stopped. You must contact your provider within two business days of the missed requirement.'

3

Payment suspension + Read more ...

Service Officer: 'Every time you miss a requirement, such as an appointment or an activity, your payments may be stopped until you re-engage with your provider. You must contact your provider beforehand if you can't meet your requirements.'

4

Demerits + Read more ...

Service Officer: 'If you do not have a valid reason for missing your requirements you will accrue a demerit. Each demerit will last for 6 months before it expires. Keep track of your demerits so you can avoid a financial penalty. If you accrue 3 demerits in 6 months, you may need to attend a Capability Interview with your provider.'

5

Capability Assessments + Read more ...

Service Officer: 'As you have 5 demerits in 6 months, your provider has told you to attend an assessment with Services Australia. This is called a Capability Assessment and is to make sure you can meet the requirements in your Job Plan/Participation Plan. Your payment will be stopped until you participate in this assessment.'

6

Financial Penalty Zone + Read more ...

Service Officer: 'You are currently in the financial penalty zone. If you do not meet your requirements and do not have a reasonable excuse, you will be at risk of getting a financial penalty. You may lose part or all of your payment.'

7

Financial penalties + Read more ...

Service Officer: 'If you get:

  • one financial penalty you will lose 50% of your fortnightly payment
  • two financial penalties you will lose all of your fortnightly payment
  • three financial penalties your payment will be cancelled for 4 weeks

If your payment is cancelled, you will have to reapply to get paid again.'

8

Accepting and commencing suitable work + Read more ...

Service Officer: 'Your mutual obligation requirements include accepting all offers of suitable work and starting any jobs you have accepted.

If you do not meet these requirements and do not have a reasonable excuse, your payments will be cancelled for 4 weeks. If your payment is cancelled, you will have to reapply to get paid again.'

Note: does not apply to ParentsNext participants.

9

Leaving work voluntarily or being dismissed due to misconduct + Read more ...

Service Officer: 'If you become voluntarily unemployed without a good reason, or are dismissed from your job because of misconduct, your payments will be cancelled for 4 weeks. If your payment is cancelled, you will have to reapply to get paid again.

You may have to wait 6 weeks before being paid if you received relocation assistance through your provider.'

Note: does not apply to ParentsNext participants.

Key messages for the Job Seeker Compliance Model

Table 2: This table describes key Job Seeker Compliance Model messages that Service Officers deliver at various participation related contacts.

Item

Description

1

Entering into a Job Plan with your CDP provider + Read more ...

Service Officer: 'Your mutual obligation requirements include entering into and complying with a Job Plan with your CDP provider when asked to do so.

If you do not enter into a Job Plan when asked to do so, we will talk with you about your reasons for this. You may incur a connection failure and will be notified of a requirement to enter into a Job Plan at a new appointment we make for you.

If you do not attend this new appointment or enter into a Job Plan, your payment may stop. You may incur a reconnection failure which means you will not get back payment and a penalty may be applied. At this point your payment may also be cancelled.

You may not be paid if you refuse to enter into a Job Plan when asked to do so.'

2

Accepting suitable work + Read more ...

Service Officer: 'Your mutual obligation requirements include accepting all offers of suitable work.

You may incur a serious failure if you refuse a suitable job offer or fail to commence suitable work and do not have a valid reason.

If you incur a serious failure, your payment will stop for up to 8 weeks.'

3

Leaving work voluntarily or being dismissed due to misconduct + Read more ...

Service Officer: 'If you become voluntarily unemployed without a good reason, or are dismissed from your job because of misconduct, you may have to wait 8 weeks before you receive payment.

You may have to wait 12 weeks before being paid if you received relocation assistance through your provider.'

4

Attending compulsory appointments with the provider + Read more ...

Service Officer: 'Your mutual obligation requirements include attending compulsory appointments with your provider.

It is important you attend all appointments arranged for you. If you are unable to attend, you must contact the provider to explain why you cannot attend before the time of the appointment.

If you do not contact beforehand, your payment may be stopped and you may incur a non-attendance failure, and a penalty may also be applied. You must arrange a new appointment.'

5

Investigation of Non-Attendance Failure + Read more ...

Service Officer: 'Your payment has been stopped because you have missed a compulsory appointment with your provider. We need to investigate the reasons why you have not attended the appointment in order to determine if a financial penalty will be applied.

If the non-attendance failure is applied, the penalty will start from the date you were notified by your provider that your payments had stopped due to missing an appointment. A reconnection appointment will be booked for you to attend.

If you do not attend the reconnection appointment, a further penalty may be applied from the date of the appointment until you do attend. Your payment may also be cancelled.

If you are unable to attend the reconnection appointment, you must contact your provider as soon as possible to explain why you are unable to attend. A penalty may be applied if you have not contacted beforehand, even if you have a good reason for not attending.'

6

Investigating of a reconnection failure + Read more ...

Service Officer: 'Your payment has been stopped because you have missed your reconnection appointment with your provider. We need to investigate the reasons why you have not attended the appointment in order to determine if a penalty will be applied.

If the penalty is applied, a reconnection penalty will apply from the date of the non-attendance until you attend the further reconnection appointment booked by your provider.

If you do not attend the further reconnection appointment, the penalty will continue until you do attend. Your payment may also be cancelled.

If you are unable to attend the further reconnection appointment, you must contact your provider as soon as possible to explain why you are unable to attend. A penalty may be applied if you have not contacted beforehand, even if you have a good reason for attending.'

7

Comprehensive Compliance Assessments and persistent non-compliance + Read more ...

Service Officer: 'During a Comprehensive Compliance Assessment we will talk with you about your reasons for not meeting your requirements. We will also discuss if you need further or different assistance to help you meet your requirements.

You may incur a serious failure if you are found to have been persistently not meeting your requirements. Your payment will stop for 8 weeks.'
