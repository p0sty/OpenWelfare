3

Circumstances identified during a ParentsNext appointment or for a customer already referred to a provider or where a work item with the keyword PNXEXP has been allocated + Read more ...

The majority of mutual obligation requirements exemptions are also applicable for ParentsNext participants however, there are some circumstances where the exemption type and coding differ for ParentsNext participants. There are two ParentsNext specific exemptions; Temporary Period of Confinement exemption and Bereavement exemption.

Both the agency and ParentsNext providers can grant exemptions. A list of all the exemptions that can be applied by the agency and by ParentsNext providers, including the maximum duration for each exemption is available on the Resources page.

Is the Service Officer skilled to action the ParentsNext exemption request?

No, go to Step 4

Yes, and the customer advised:

  • they are pregnant, see Step 1 in Table 2
  • a new child under 9 months old has entered their care, see Step 2 in Table 2
  • they have short term caring responsibilities, see Step 3 in Table 2
  • their partner or an immediate family member has passed away, see Step 7 in Table 2
  • they are experiencing family and domestic violence or relationship breakdown, see Exempting a job seeker from mutual obligation requirements due to special circumstances
  • they have a temporary incapacity, see Assessment of a medical certificate to determine exemption from mutual obligation requirements for a temporary incapacity
  • they or their child have a serious illness, see Exempting a job seeker from mutual obligation requirements for serious illness
  • they have long term caring responsibilities (foster carer, non-parent carer, home schooling, distance education, child not eligible for Carer Payment etc.), see Exempting a job seeker from mutual obligation requirements due to special circumstances
  • they are caring for a large family with 4 or more children who are either 0-15 years old, or who are 16-18 years old inclusive but still completing secondary study, see Exempting a job seeker from mutual obligation requirements due to special circumstances
  • they are experiencing other exceptional circumstances, including homelessness, jury duty, community service order, natural disaster, major disruptions to their home or major personal crisis see Exempting a job seeker from mutual obligation requirements due to special circumstances

2

Temporary Period of Confinement exemption + Read more ...

Customer's that are within 6 weeks of their expected date of confinement or have a new child under 9 months of age enter their care are eligible for a ParentsNext Temporary Period of Confinement (PTC) exemption.

Evidence of the expected date of confinement or the new child entering care must be provided before the PTC exemption can be recorded. Evidence of the expected date of delivery can include a letter from a doctor or registered midwife. It does not need to be a Services Australia medical certificate.

The exemption applies until the child turns 9 months (39 weeks) of age.

Is the customer able to provide evidence of their expected date of confinement or the new child entering care at the time of the contact?

  • Yes, go to Step 8
  • No, go to Step 4

3

Short term caring responsibilities + Read more ...

Where a ParentsNext participant has short term caring responsibilities, they may be eligible for a Special Family Circumstance exemption which can be applied for up to 16 weeks.

Note: the specific Caring exemption is not available for ParentsNext participants as the maximum duration is limited to 14 weeks (applicable to job seekers).

If the customer has long term caring responsibilities (foster carer, non-parent carer, home schooling etc.), see Exempting a job seeker from mutual obligation requirements due to special circumstances.

The customer must provide evidence of the short term caring responsibilities in the form of a medical certificate or a letter from a medical practitioner confirming the caring requirements.

Is the customer able to provide evidence of their caring responsibilities at the time of the appointment or contact?

  • Yes, go to Step 8
  • No, and the contact is:
    • During a ParentsNext appointment, go to Step 5
    • Outside of a ParentsNext appointment, go to Step 4

4

Customer not able to provide evidence of their circumstances at contact outside of an appointment + Read more ...

As the customer is not able to provide evidence of their expected date of confinement, a new child under 9 months of age entering their care or short term caring responsibilities, an exemption cannot yet be recorded.

If the customer:

  • is not yet connected to a ParentsNext provider, advise them to provide evidence of their expected date of confinement, new child entering care or short term caring responsibilities before or at their next Services Australia ParentsNext appointment, procedure ends here
  • is connected to a provider, go to Step 6

5

Customer not able to provide evidence of short term caring during a ParentsNext appointment + Read more ...

As the customer has not yet been referred to a ParentsNext provider, a future ParentsNext appointment must be booked to ensure that evidence of their short term caring responsibilities is received and recorded where appropriate.

  • Advise the customer:
    • they must provide evidence of their short term caring responsibilities before or at their next appointment (they can use the Centrelink Express Plus mobile app to do this)
    • once they provide evidence they will be exempt for the duration provided on the evidence
  • Book a ParentsNext appointment for at least 21 days after the date of the current appointment. For more information on booking appointments, see Booking appointments in the Centrelink Appointment System
  • Advise the customer of the appointment details and that if they do not provide the required evidence before or at the next appointment they will be referred to a ParentsNext provider
  • Record a Fast Note on the customer's record:
    • In the Search field, key 'ParentsNext'
    • Under the Template field, select ParentsNext Review - Other
    • Delete the irrelevant options
    • record that the customer was advised they must provide evidence of their short term caring responsibilities at or before their next ParentsNext appointment or they will be referred to a provider
    • Complete the DOC

Procedure ends here.

6

Customer already referred to a provider not able to provide evidence of their circumstances + Read more ...

As the customer has already been referred to a ParentsNext provider, a future ParentsNext appointment must not be booked.

Advise the customer:

  • they must provide evidence of their expected date of confinement (for example, a letter from a doctor or registered midwife) or the new child entering care or the short term caring responsibilities to either their provider or Services Australia before an exemption can be recorded (they can use the Centrelink Express Plus mobile app to do this)
  • once evidence has been received and recorded they will be granted an exemption until the new child is 9 months of age
  • if evidence is not provided they will remain as a compulsory ParentsNext participant and must continue to meet their ParentsNext participation requirements
  • DOC the record to detail the customer was advised they must provide evidence of their circumstances before an exemption can be recorded and that if they do not provide it they must continue to meet their ParentsNext participation requirements

Procedure ends here.

7

Death of a partner or immediate family member + Read more ...

Compulsory ParentsNext participants are eligible for an exemption from participation requirements for up to 16 weeks from the date of their partner's or immediate family member's death. The Bereavement exemption must be coded to ensure the customer is able to be exempt for up to 16 weeks.

Note: this exemption differs from the Bereavement mutual obligation exemption which is limited to 14 weeks.

Death of a partner

Where the Miscellaneous Update Details (NMIS) screen is coded in relation to the partner's death, a mutual obligation exemption will be automatically granted for a maximum of 14 weeks or if the customer is pregnant, to the end of the pregnancy (whichever is longer) where evidence is provided. A manual Bereavement exemption must still be coded to ensure the customer can access the maximum 16 weeks available.

Where the customer is still a compulsory ParentsNext participant when the expected date of confinement is reached, the system will automatically apply a ParentsNext temporary period of confinement exemption until the child is 9 months of age.

If this is the initial contact after the death of a partner, see Initial contact after someone has died for more information about available services and referrals.

Go to Step 8.

8

Coding the appropriate exemption + Read more ...

Note: Service Officers must not send a referral to the social work team to code an exemption.

To code the appropriate exemption:

  • Navigate to the Activity and Exemption Summary (AEX) screen
  • Select Add button under 'Manual Activities and Exemptions' section
  • Select the appropriate ParentsNext exemption type:
    • PTC - ParentsNext Temporary Confinement
    • SCI - Special Family Circumstances - for short term caring
    • BVP - Bereavement exemption - for death of partner or immediate family member
  • Start date: date the exemption is being recorded
  • End date: the end date should be based on the type of exemption being applied and the information relevant to the customer's circumstances
  • Select Continue to finalise the update
  • Create a DOC to detail the reasons for the exemption
  • If the customer is present at the time the exemption is being applied advise them:
    • they will be required to participate once the exemption has ended, (unless they exit the program beforehand) including attending any scheduled appointments with the agency or their provider
    • they must still report fortnightly to Services Australia during the period of their exemption to ensure they receive their Parenting Payment (PP)
  • Where the customer:
    • has not yet been referred to a ParentsNext provider, the system will automatically book a new ParentsNext appointment at the end of the exemption where they are still eligible so the customer can be referred to a ParentsNext provider
    • has already been referred to a ParentsNext provider, it will be the responsibility of the ParentsNext provider to re-engage the customer at the end of the exemption period where they are still eligible. The agency is not required to undertake any follow up at the end of the exemption in this situation

Procedure ends here.

3

Customer in receipt of Parenting Payment Single (PPS) + Read more ...

A qualifying child for a customer in receipt of PPS for the purposes of ParentsNext is determined by what is recorded on the Child in Customer Care (CHC) screen and the Pensions Child Summary (PNKDS) screen.

  • Key PNKDS in the NEXT field and press [Enter]
  • Is the child/ren correctly showing as a dependent:
    • Yes, lodge a request for Technical Peer Support Officer (TPS) assistance (phone appointment) or the Access Support Line (face to face appointment) who will assist in determining why the child is not being correctly assessed
    • No, lodge a request for Technical Peer Support Officer (TPS) assistance (phone appointment) or the Access Support Line (face to face appointment) to request assistance in updating the PNKDS screen
  • Once the record is updated, go to Step 5

Note: if there is a delay in the update correctly reassessing eligibility for ParentsNext, run the daily driver.

4

Customer in receipt of Parenting Payment Partnered (PPP) + Read more ...

A qualifying child in care for a customer in receipt of PPP for the purposes of ParentsNext is determined by what is recorded on the Child Override/Claim (CHOC) screen. Therefore, if a child does not have a PPP entry correctly recorded on the CHOC screen, that child will not be considered in the eligibility determination for ParentsNext.

Update the CHOC screen:

  • Navigate to the Child Selection (CHS) screen and 'S'elect the relevant child
  • Key CHOC in the NEXT field and press [Enter]
  • Key the following fields on the CHOC screen:
    • Date child's date of birth or date entered care
    • Svc Rsn customer's payment type (PPP)
    • Reason claim
  • Finalise the activity by typing AR in the Next field and press [Enter], if the activity will be sent for QOL include notes to detail the update was made to reassess eligibility for ParentsNext
  • Complete a DOC including the details of why the updates were made

Note: if there is a delay in the update correctly reassessing eligibility for ParentsNext, run the daily driver.

8

Current study meets minimum ParentsNext study requirements + Read more ...

As the customer has provided evidence of meeting the minimum ParentsNext study requirements, the study can now be recorded.

  • Advise the customer:
    • they will be exited from the program due to their current study but may be re-selected, if they are eligible, once their study ends
    • if they are reselected in the future, another appointment will be booked at that time

Is the study for greater than 12 months?

  • Yes, go to Step 9
  • No, see Step 1 in Table 5

9

Study for greater than 12 months + Read more ...

The study must be reviewed in 12 months to ensure the customer is still studying and that the study continues to meet the minimum ParentsNext study requirements.

  • Create a manual review:
    • Key 'RVR' in the Next: field
    • Press [Enter] or select Continue
  • On the RVR screen, code fields:
    • Service Reason: the customer's payment type (e.g. PPP or PPS)
    • Review Reason: NEV - Possible Notifiable Event
    • Due Date: 12 months from the system date
    • Text: Record that the customer must be contacted in 12 months to confirm they are still studying and that the study continues to meet the minimum ParentsNext study requirements
    • Keywords: PNXSTU
    • Source: and Date of Receipt:
    • Press [Enter] or select Continue to accept the review.
    • The review has now been set. It will appear on the customer's FAL screen with the status of REV.
  • Record a closed DOC on the record using the PNX - Current Study Fast Note:
    • Launch Fast Note
    • In the search field type 'ParentsNext'
    • Under the template field, select PNX - Current Study

Offer customer option to volunteer in ParentsNext + Read more ...

Advise the customer of:

  • the outcome of the appointment:
    • That due to their current circumstances they are no longer a compulsory ParentsNext participant at this time
    • If their circumstances change in the future they may again become eligible for ParentsNext as a compulsory participant and be required to attend another appointment
    • Record the appointment outcome as 'completed'
  • the option to participate voluntarily:
    • If they would like to receive the support and services that ParentsNext offers they can volunteer for the program
    • The services they will receive are the same for everyone regardless of compulsory or voluntary status

Does the customer wish to volunteer?

  • Yes, go to Step 3

3

Customer elected to participate voluntarily + Read more ...

Note: if there is a delay in the update correctly reassessing eligibility for ParentsNext, run the daily driver.

6

Refer a voluntary participant to a ParentsNext provider + Read more ...

Note: E009JW is a generic error code may be encountered within the Looking for Work workflow or the Online Diary.

Navigate to the Online Diary via Workspace > Participation Summary

  • Within the ParentsNext provider section, select Make Referral
  • Navigate to the List Providers page and select the book appointment button:
    • A list of all ParentsNext providers who have appointments available within the next 2 working days will appear on the List Providers page
    • Select the ParentsNext provider. Where multiple providers are available, ask the customer their preferred provider. If a customer is indecisive, service officers must not provide a recommendation
  • From the Available Appointments page, select a suitable appointment (no earlier than 7 days in the future if an interpreter is required)
    • If there are no appointments available showing, select the 'more appointments' button to display more appointments (the date from will need to be updated to the relevant date the search is to be conducted from)
    • In the Reason for choosing more appointments, select the relevant reason. Note: not all reasons are appropriate for ParentsNext
  • When an appointment has been selected, the 'Appointment Detail' page will display:
    • Confirm details of the appointment with the customer
    • Additional Requirements: field, enter information the provider may need to be aware of, for example, if an interpreter is required
    • Appointment Notification: field, select Face to Face (where the Services Australia appointment was conducted at a service centre) or Phone (where the Services Australia appointment was conducted via phone)
  • Select the 'Book Appointment' button to finalise the appointment booking process
  • The Appointment Outcome page will display:
    • The details of the appointment
    • Was this appointment created in error?: field, this question should be left blank.
    • Select the Finish button
  • From the Provider Appointment page, read the customer the information contained within the message displayed
  • Select Continue to finalise the procedure

Advise the customer no further confirmation from Services Australia will be provided (that is, they will not receive a letter or electronic message confirming the appointment details) however they will receive a reminder about their appointment from their provider 1 day before the appointment. Based on their preferences this will either be an SMS, email or a reminder via their JobSearch inbox

Procedure ends here.
