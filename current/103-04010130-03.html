Processing of a Group schedule and payments to organisations

Step

Action

1

Request for payments to be delivered to an organisation + Read more ...

Note: this procedure does not apply in circumstances where a change is required to an organisation's record, including:

  • Change of name or ownership for an organisation
  • Change of group payment bank account details or other bank account details where a nominee is linked to more than one recipient
  • Change to an organisation's contact details

Only officers with the appropriate ISP access have the ability to update organisation details. Refer to Nominee organisations for more information.

A customer's existing group payment arrangement may be changed or ceased by a Group Payment Administrator or financial institution representative. The customer also has the ability to change their payment destination unless the Group Payment arrangement is made under an involuntary arrangement, for example via a court order.

Enquiry from a customer

If the customer:

  • enquires about arranging to have their payments delivered to an organisation by group payment, go to Step 2
  • is lodging or has lodged, a completed Please send my payments to an institution - Group Payment (SS270) form or an Authorising a person or organisation to enquire or act on your behalf (SS313) form, go to Step 3

Enquiry from a group payment organisation

If an organisation:

  • advises that they have not received their group payment schedule:
    • via Organisation Online Mail (OOM) or Letters in Business Hub, go to Step 8
  • contacts about the information contained on a group schedule or would like to register to receive their group payment schedule online ,go to Step 9
  • is the customer's payment nominee and requests that the payment be made on group schedule, issue SS270 and go to Step 2

2

Issue the SS270 or SS313 + Read more ...

Send or issue an SS270 or SS313 form to be completed and returned.

  • Certain organisations, such as public trustees and some nursing homes, are nominee organisations that receive customers' payments through the Group Payment Scheme. In these cases an SS313 should be issued
  • Where the organisation is only a Group Payment organisation, and not also a nominee, only an SS270 should be issued

If it is not known whether the organisation is a nominee or group payment organisation conduct a search on the Organisation Name (ON) screen by entering their ABN in the Organisation ID: field and keying ATO in the Organisation Code: field and ABN in the ID Type: field. Nominee organisations will have a stakeholder code of NOM, group payment organisations will have a stakeholder code of TPP.

The Resources page contains a link to the forms.

3

SS270 or SS313 received + Read more ...

If processing an SS313 form, see Adding or rejecting a nominee request.

If processing an SS270 form, go to Step 4.

4

Check weekly payments + Read more ...

If the customer has weekly payment arrangements in place they must be ceased before changing the payment destination to group code.

See Assessing, coding and reviewing weekly payments.

Once any required updates have been completed, go to Step 5.

5

Check if record/s are in the same system environment + Read more ...

Is the customer (and linked record/s if applicable) in the same system environment as the organisation is physically located?

  • Yes, go to Step 6
  • No, transfer customer (and linked records if applicable) to the same environment as the Group Payment Schedule organisation. See Inter-environment change of address (ICoA) transfer of a customer record, then go to step 6

6

Change payment destination + Read more ...

Update the payment destination using the:

  • Change Payment Destination workflow in Process Direct, or
  • Change Payment Destination workflow in Customer First

To code manually in Customer First:

  • Key 'PA' in Nxt: field. The Payment Destination Summary (PAS) screen will display if customer has received, at any time, different payments (for example JobSeeker Payment (JSP), Disability Support Pension (DSP), etc.) from Centrelink. If customer has only ever received one payment the appropriate payment destination screen will display. 'S'elect from the PAS screen the current payment to be changed and the appropriate payment destination screen will display on the Payment Destination Details (PAD) screen. The following fields must be blank:
    • Direct Credit...........FI Code:
    • Account Number:
    • Account Title:
    • Direct Credit Exemption Reason:
  • Complete the Schedule........Group Home Code: field with the group schedule code
  • Ensure that the partner's payment destination is not updated, unless the partner has also requested to have their payment destination changed
  • Complete the Source: and DOR: fields, and press [Enter]

A warning message will display 'W021-I - Check that payment delivery day code is D on the RAD screen'. Updating the customer's payment destination to a group home code will usually change the customer's EPED to B and delivery day code (DDC) to D. The Entitlement Delivery (RAD) screen must be checked to make sure these have changed. If not changed, update the EPED to B. For more information see Changing Entitlement Period End Dates (EPED).

7

Record details on a DOC + Read more ...

Code a Display on Access (DOA) DOC to ensure all Service Officers are aware of the arrangement in place (i.e. a group schedule arrangement) and include a warning not to move the record (and linked records if applicable) from this environment due to the Group Payment Schedule arrangement.

Procedure ends here.

8

Group schedule not received + Read more ...

Check that the organisation is approved for this service:

  • The Resources page contains a link to a listing of organisations approved for this service. Use the organisation's three digit Group Payment code to locate the organisation name and Customer Reference Number (CRN) on the listing
    • If the organisation is not listed, go to Step 10
    • If the organisation requires a copy of the Group payment schedule, the schedules can be reprinted from the History Summary (HS) screen up to 62 days after the date of payment
  • Enter the organisation's Third Party Payment (TPP) CRN

In the organisation's record:

  • ensure that the record has a stakeholder code and service reason 'TPP'
  • reissue the letter from the HS screen
  • record details on a DOC
  • if the organisation has enquires about their group payment schedule, go to Step 10

Procedure ends here.

9

Organisations NOT receiving group schedules via OOM or Business Hub + Read more ...

If an organisation has not received their group schedule, the Service Officer who received the call or email is to report the issue to mySupport. The Resources page contains a link to mySupport. The request must contain:

  • the brief description 'Group payment schedule not received'
  • the organisation's name, address and group schedule code (this is a three digit number)

In urgent cases/emergencies, an organisation can contact the National Business Gateway or Service Officers can contact the ICT Service Desk Urgent Escalation line. See Resources for a link to the contact details using mySupport.

The issue will be investigated and the organisation will be contacted.

If the organisation is interested in receiving their payment schedule online, go to Step 10.

Otherwise, procedure ends here.

10

Organisation enquires about payment schedule + Read more ...

Where an organisation:

  • has questions about information contained in their group schedule, the Service Officer should attempt to answer the query
  • has an issue with their online account or viewing their schedule, obtain as much information as possible about the enquiry, the organisation's TPP CRN and three digit Group Payment code together with contact details for the organisation. The Service Officer should send the information via email to the National Business Gateway for further action. The issue will be investigated and the Organisation will be contacted
  • would like to register to receive group payment schedules online, send an email to the Seniors, Carers, Nominees and Bereavement Programme. The email must contain the full name and reference number of the organisation. For contact details, see Bereavement
