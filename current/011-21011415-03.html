Handle enquiries about COVID-19 vaccines

Table 1

Item

Description

1

Enquiries from individuals + Read more ...

Service Officers can tell individuals:

  • that COVID-19 vaccinations and boosters are:
    • not mandatory
    • free for all individuals regardless of their Medicare eligibility
  • how to access proof of vaccinations
  • to visit health.gov.au for the latest news and information (COVID-19 vaccines > Find out more about COVID-19 vaccines)

Note: a security check must be completed if personal details need to be checked or updated. See Table 2 for links to relevant processes.

2

Enquiries from vaccination providers + Read more ...

Service Officers can tell vaccination providers:

  • that they can let their patients know that COVID-19 vaccinations and boosters:
    • are not mandatory
    • more information is available at health.gov.au
  • to visit health.gov.au for the latest information and resources (COVID-19 vaccines > Find out more about COVID-19 vaccines)

Note: a security check must be completed if personal details need to be checked or updated. See Table 2 for links to relevant processes.

3

Enquiries from general practitioners (GP) about AIR registration + Read more ...

Service Officers:

  • must complete a security check before releasing any information
  • access the Provider Directory System (PDS) to check:
    • the provider has a number for the location from where they will be administering COVID-19 vaccinations
    • the address for that location is correct

Providers who don't have a number for the location can manage their provider numbers in HPOS.

See:

  • Authentication and release of information for Australian Immunisation Register (AIR) - Verify the identity of recognised vaccination providers for AIR enquiries table
  • Action a vaccination provider's enquiry on the AIR

4

Enquiries from GP clinics and respiratory clinics, ACCHOs and pharmacies about AIR registration + Read more ...

Service Officers:

  • must complete a security check before releasing any information
  • access the Provider Directory System (PDS) to get the:
    • start date for the AIR program registration
    • AIR provider number

See:

  • Authentication and release of information for Australian Immunisation Register (AIR) - Verify the identity of recognised vaccination providers for AIR enquiries table
  • Action a vaccination provider's enquiry on the AIR

5

Enquiries about how to record COVID-19 vaccines on the AIR + Read more ...

COVID-19 vaccinations and boosters can be recorded on the AIR using:

  • practice management software integrated with the AIR system
  • the AIR site through HPOS
  • manual forms (only to be used if digital channels are unavailable)

Direct health professionals who use practice management software to their software provider to check what they need to do to record COVID-19 vaccines.

6

Enquiries from practices about the Practice Incentives Program (PIP) – COVID-19 Vaccine Incentive + Read more ...

Services Australia does not have any specific information about the incentive. See the MBS Online - FAQ - Bulk Billed MBS patient COVID-19 Vaccine Suitability Assessment Service.

Practices should:

  • be referred to the FAQs. In particular the heading ‘Will the COVID-19 vaccine suitability assessment service attract incentive payments under the Practice Incentive Program?’
  • contact their Primary Health Networks or RACGP (if they are a member) for further information about the planned rollout

Help individuals get ready for vaccination

Table 3

Item

Description

1

Do not give health or clinical advice + Read more ...

If an individual asks if they should get vaccinated, or when and where they can get vaccinated:

  • do not provide advice about vaccines
  • tell them they can go to the online COVID-19 Vaccine Clinic Finder
  • direct them to the National coronavirus and COVID-19 helpline or australia.gov.au for the latest information

To help individuals get ready for COVID-19 vaccination:

  • individuals enrolled in Medicare, go to Step 2
  • individuals who are not eligible for Medicare, go to Step 3

2

Individuals who are enrolled in Medicare + Read more ...

Individuals who are eligible for Medicare can do 3 things to get ready:

  • get a myGov account
  • link Medicare and update their contact details

Trained Service Officers can help individuals by:

  • telling them how to get a myGov account. See How users create a myGov account and link services
  • giving them a linking code to link Medicare to myGov. See Using the myGov Staff Linking Application
  • telling them How to get proof of your COVID-19 vaccination in Medicare online account or the Express Plus Medicare mobile app
  • checking their contact details for Medicare are correct. See Amend address in the Consumer Directory Maintenance System (CDMS) for Medicare

3

Individuals who are not eligible for Medicare + Read more ...

Tell individuals who are not eligible for Medicare that they can get proof of their vaccinations online by:

  • getting an Individual Healthcare Identifier (IHI). They can request an IHI online by accessing the IHI service in myGov or completing the Request an Individual Healthcare Identifier form (MS003)
  • using the IHI service or registering for a My Health Record using their IHI. The Australian Digital Health Agency is responsible for My Health Record. See Register for a My Health Record for how to register

Trained Service Officers can help individuals by:

  • checking the individual's details in the AIR are correct. To update, see Amend individual registration on the Australian Immunisation Register
  • reading the IHI general enquiries- frequently asked questions
