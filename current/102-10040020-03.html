Common examples of scenarios + Read more ...

As a general principle, ABSTUDY (including ABSTUDY Schooling A and ABSTUDY Pensioner Education Supplement payments) may be restored by Smart Centre Call staff if all the following are met:

  • the action can be resolved at the point of first contact
  • the action does not need a complex assessment (e.g. restoration of an ABSTUDY secondary boarder record is complex), see ABSTUDY cancellation, suspension and rejection codes for further details
  • The customer:
    • does not need to give any written documents, or
    • has given the required written documents, but it has not yet been actioned by the ABSTUDY Smart Centre Smart Centre Processing team
  • There is no information stating that ABSTUDY restoration cannot occur

Common scenarios where it is suitable for Smart Centre Call to restore ABSTUDY payments are:

  • The payment has been suspended whereabouts unknown (SUS-WUK) and the customer has contacted to advise the new address
  • The payment is cancelled or suspended pending provision of proof of enrolment (POE). The customer has given the correct documents but because of a high volume of work they have not been actioned by the ABSTUDY Smart Centre Processing team.   • The payment has been cancelled 'Not entitled to STARTUP-HELP' (SUH) and the customer has provided POE for their Startup Year Course and evidence they have been selected for a STARTUP-HELP loan. See Startup Year Courses
  • The payment is SUS-IMP go to Step 4

Note: the same principles apply to restoration of ABSTUDY Australian Apprentice records.
In practice, this means that Smart Centre Call staff can restore ABSTUDY Australian Apprentice records in scenarios where the action can be resolved at the point of first contact. For example, SUS WUK, suspended/cancelled for failing to report.
Smart Centre Call staff should refer the action to the ABSTUDY Smart Centre Processing team in cases where further follow-up is needed before restoration can occur. For example, the Commonwealth registration number needs to be obtained from the Department of Social Services (DSS).

Customer been suspended imprisoned (IMP) + Read more ...

Before restoring ABSTUDY payment for a customer released from prison, the Service Officer must first confirm the date the customer was released from prison (if not already recorded on the customers record) by either:

2

Process if customer is cancelled for a period of more than 15 weeks + Read more ...

If the customer is cancelled due to being incarcerated specific coding is needed, go to Step 4.

Smart Centre Call Service Officers cannot restore ABSTUDY records, if a new claim activity (re-grant) is needed to reinstate payments. For example, payment cancelled 15 weeks or more.

The Service Officer should send an ABSTUDY action request Fast Note to the ABSTUDY Smart Centre Processing team, including relevant details.

Select: Auto Text >Students, ABSTUDY & Apprentices>Update>ABSTUDY action request>Confirm.

If a customer is cancelled for a period of 15 weeks or more, it is not possible to action a restoration activity on the system. Where continuous entitlement is established, restoration is achieved using a new claim activity (re-grant).

The customer may need to lodge a new claim for this restoration to occur. See Claiming ABSTUDY.

The date of cancellation is not always the same as the date paid to plus one (DPT+1). When restoring payments through either a new claim or restoration activity, care must be taken. Make sure any arrears are correct and that determined or undetermined related debt activities are taken into account.

Note: if an arrears payment has been created and there is an undetermined debt for the same payment type and period, the undetermined debt may be finalised as 'no debt' (FND). Use the Debt Action script.

Manually adjust any arrears generated as a result of the debt in the new claim activity. If the debt has already been determined, then a manual adjustment cannot occur.

When restoring continuous entitlement via a new claim activity, Income Bank balance will reset to 'nil' from the date of re-grant.

As the customer has a continuous entitlement to ABSTUDY, their Income Bank balance may need to be manually reset via the Rate Component Override (EPRC) screen.

Check if the customer is owed any arrears and that they appear on the Assessment Results (AR) screen.

Record details of contact and restoration on a DOC.

Procedure ends here.

3

Schooling A Award student cancelled ANQ + Read more ...

Due to system restrictions, a student's payment may cancel as 'Applicant not qualified' incorrectly. This may occur where:

  • there has been a gap in the applicant's entitlement to a Health Care Card (HCC) or Low Income Health Care Card (LIC)
  • the applicant is the holder of an auto issue Health Care Card (HCC)
    • on the basis of maximum rate FTB A or a long-term customer of JobSeeker Payment (JSP), and
    • a reassessment has resulted in the required exemption on the Household Income and Assets (NHI) screen dropping off
  • the applicant has transferred from one Income support payment to another (for example PPP to PPS) and there is a gap between entitlements

Note: check the Concession Entitlement Summary (MCCS) screen in the applicant's record. The MCCS screen displays the dates of entitlement for a HCC or a LIC. For more details, see Extended entitlement to Concession Cards.

Is the schooling student still qualified for the Schooling A Award?

  • Yes and the applicant:
    • is the holder of a current HCC or LIC but there has been a gap in the applicant's entitlement to the Health Care Card (HCC) or Low Income Health Care Card (LIC), see Step 2 in Table 4
    • is the holder of a current HCC on the basis of maximum rate FTB A or a long-term customer of JSP ,see Step 1 in Table 4
    • has transferred from one Income support payment to another with a gap in entitlement between payments, see Step 2 in Table 4
    • is an eligible applicant, but the Schooling A Award has been cancelled more than 15 weeks, see Step 2 in Table 4
  • No, tell the customer they no longer qualify for ABSTUDY payments. Record details of decision in a DOC. Procedure ends here

4

Customers incarcerated for more than 15 weeks + Read more ...

ABSTUDY records for customers incarcerated for 15 weeks or more cannot be restored.

Smart Centre Call Service Officers should send an ABSTUDY action request Fast Note to the ABSTUDY Smart Centre Processing team, including relevant details. Select: Auto Text >Students, ABSTUDY & Apprentices>Update>ABSTUDY action request>Confirm.

If a customer is incarcerated for 15 weeks or more it is not possible to action a restoration activity on the system. A new claim activity (re-grant) is needed to reinstate payments.

Customers are not entitled to ABSTUDY payments while incarcerated. Restoration is achieved using a new claim activity.

The customer may need to lodge a new claim for the restoration to occur. See Claiming ABSTUDY.

When re-granting, the DOR input must be the date the customer was released for incarceration. Education Payments Start Date (EPDC) screen may also need a manual start date to be input to match the DOR (customers release date). Customers in receipt of ABSTUDY are not entitled to payments whilst incarcerated.

Record details of contact and restoration on a DOC.

    • Service Reason field - ABT
    • Activity field - NCL
    • Complete the Source and Date of Receipt fields. As the payments are only being re-indexed due to system limitations that prevent restoring, the DOR can be coded as the date the customer contacts. If the payment is to be re-granted from a date in the previous year, the DOR has to be the date to be granted from to prevent payments commencing from 1 January
  • Enter through the new claim screen flow. Make sure the details already recorded are still correct. Generally there will be no need to change any details. For further details, see Assessing the claim for ABSTUDY Schooling A (SY018)

  • Make sure the Office Code (OC) screen is updated to the correct customer's local Service Centre or Remote Servicing Team
  • Check the NHI screen displays the PIT exemption. If not present and one should be:
    • Code a 'PITRSH' activity on the applicant record, by typing 'PITRSH' in the Next field of the applicant record
    • The system will generate an activity on this record, select and finalise this activity. PIT data will be refreshed between the applicant and student
  • If the applicant has transferred from one income support payment to another, and there is a gap in entitlement, check whether the applicant was in receipt of a Health Care Card (HCC) on the basis of maximum rate FTB A or a long-term customer of JobSeeker Payment (JSP) covering the gap in entitlement:
    • If yes, a manual PIT exemption will need to be coded on the system
    • If no, or there has been a gap in the applicants entitlement to a Health Care Card (HCC), ABSTUDY can only be re-granted from the date the PIT exemption recommences
  • Finalise the activity on the Assessment Results (AR) screen. Check there are no incorrect arrears payments or debts raised
  • Record reason for decision and action on a DOC

Check applicant's record for any undetermined debts on the Activity List (AL) screen. A debt may generate when the student's payments cancel due to the system limitations. It may be necessary to:

  • finalise the debt as 'no debt' ('FND') via the Debt Action script, and
  • manually adjust any arrears generated because of the debt in the new claim activity, to zero

Restore ABSTUDY + Read more ...

If the customer is under income management (INM on the benefit status line) and they have an arrears or lump sum payment entitlement, do not make any changes to the customer's record to allow this payment to be made. Contact the Income Management Team to allow income management to be turned off for the arrears payment before it is made.

If the customer's Family Tax Benefit (FTB) is cancelled as a result of the income support payment being cancelled, restore FTB first.

Note: if a customer is cancelled for a period exceeding 15 weeks, it is not possible to restore using the BA screen. Restoration can be achieved using a new claim activity.

  • In the Nxt field, type 'SVABY' and in the System field, type 'ISS' and press [Enter]
  • Go to the Benefit Action (BA) screen and code the following:
    • Svc Rsn field - 'ABY' (for ABSTUDY); or 'ABT' (for Schooling A students)
    • Action field - 'RES' (for 'restore')
    • Source and DOR fields
    • press [Enter]
    • Effect Date should default to date of suspension or cancellation
    • press [Enter], to go to the Assessment Results (AR) screen
  • If the customer is income managed under the Voluntary Income Management measure, and the agreement was in place for more than 13 weeks, the Voluntary Income Management Continuation Assessment page displays. It can then be recorded whether the customer wishes to continue or terminate their VIM agreement. Refer to Effect on Income Management when payment is suspended, on zero rate or cancelled, or Unemployment Non-Payment Period applied. Check the Assessment Results (AR) screen. Is there a negative adjustment displaying, e.g. the system has determined that the customer has been overpaid an allowance under ABSTUDY?

Check entitlements before proceeding with payment of arrears to avoid any overpayments.

Is an excess payment (negative adjustment) identified?

  • Yes, go to Step 7
  • No, record details of contact and restoration on a DOC. Procedure ends here

7

Determine any excess payment + Read more ...

When a customer's ABSTUDY record has been restored, some changes that are made to the customer's record in the restoration activity can cause the system to determine an excess payment.

If arrears are also payable in the same activity, the excess payment will be fully or partially recovered from those arrears.

Where the excess payment (negative adjustment) is:

  • equal to the arrears amount, no arrears will be paid to the customer and no debt shell will be created
  • more than the arrears amount, no arrears will be paid to the customer, and a debt shell will be created (as there is residual excess payment)
  • less than the arrears amount, the remaining arrears (after the excess payment has been recovered) will be paid to the customer, no debt shell will be created

The Resources page contains an example of where an excess payment is offset by arrears where no debt shell is created.

Note: the excess payment may not display as a debt shell. It will be hard to track where the excess payment was recovered from. DOC the assessment outcome and include details of the excess payment. This is extremely important if the customer requests a review of decision. Refer to External review of ABSTUDY decisions for more details.
