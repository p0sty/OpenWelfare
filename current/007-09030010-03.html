PPL details for customers with children born or entering care both before and on or after 1 July 2023.

This page contains information for Service Officers when determining the correct code to use when cancelling, rejecting or making a claim for Parental Leave Pay (PPL) not effective.

The tables below show the pre and post claim codes used for assessing a PPL claim as NEF (not effective), REJ (rejected) or CAN (cancellation).

On this Page:

Legend - Explanation of reason code fields

Explanation of reason code fields used on this page

Table 1: This table describes information on the correct code to use when cancelling, rejecting or making a claim for PPL not effective.

Item

Description

1

Valid action codes

Manual cancellation of PPL should not be undertaken when a customer advises of a change in circumstances. The change in circumstances for PPL must be updated on the customer record.

Customer First

The PPL Claim Summary (P1CS) screen allows the user to select a PPL claim and manually update the status to NEF, REJ or CAN. This is completed on the PPL Claim Action (P1CA) screen by coding 'A' beside the relevant claim line on the P1CS screen.

Process Direct

The PPL Claim Action (P1CA) screen allows the user to manually update the status to NEF, REJ or CAN.

2

Rejection/Not effective

Once PPL status is made NEF or REJ it cannot be reassessed. A new claim activity needs to be processed to assess the customer's PPL entitlement.

3

Restoration

A PPL restoration can only be actioned if the PPL claim is cancelled, except where it is cancelled 'EOP - End of Payment' (where the customer has already received their full PPL entitlement).

4

Level

Claim - status applies to PPL claim

Period - status applies:

  • for a PPL claim for a child born or adopted before 1 July 2023, the PPL period (and any connected Flexible PPL days)
  • for a PPL claim for a child born or adopted on or after 1 July 2023, a block of consecutive PPL days

Day - individual PPL day level status

Note: for claims for children born or adopted on or after 1 July 2023, the manual coding of levels is not required on the P1CA screen. Coding will be automatic and will not display.

5

Legend

Manual - reasons that can be manually input via the PPL Claim Action (P1CA) screen

Auto - automatically occurring reasons (cannot be keyed manually)

Open (OPN) - further updates can be made to the claim, including changes to the customer’s claimed and unclaimed Flexible PPL days

Closed (CLO) - claim is closed as the customer is no longer eligible for PPL. Further updates can only be made for an acceptable reason. For example, PPL may be restored when cancelled with a restorable reason such as CRQ or OTH.

Assessed and current zero rate codes for PPL

This table contains a list of the PPL assessed (ASS) and current zero rate (CZR) codes. The overall claim status for PPL-ASS and PPL-CZR claims is always open.

Term

ASS or CZR

Description

ADN

ASS

Awaiting nomination of a PPL day

ADW

ASS

All PPL days have been withdrawn

ASD

ASS

Preliminary eligibility has been determined, and claim is awaiting:

  • For children born or adopted before 1 July 2023:
    • start date of PPL period
    • date of transfer of primary care, in cases of full transfer to secondary claimant)
  • For children born or adopted on or after 1 July 2023, first PPL day

AWP

ASS

Awaiting Permission Days. Claim lodged by an Approval Seeker and the Approval Giver has not yet provided permission to share PPL days to the Approval Seeker.

EIA

ASS

Preliminary eligibility has been determined, and claim is awaiting PPL employer acceptance of role

EIR

ASS

Preliminary eligibility has been determined, and claim is awaiting PPL employer registration

FLX

CZR

For PPL claims for a child born or adopted on or after 1 July 2023, eligibility has been determined, the customer has been paid one PPL day and has future payable PPL days remaining.

Note: on days when PPL is being paid, the claim status will be current (CUR)

For PPL claims for a child born or adopted before 1 July 2023, eligibility has been determined and there are still remaining Flexible PPL days to be paid for the child (primary claimant) or for the customer (secondary claimant).

Note: on days when Flexible PPL is being paid, the claim status will be current (CUR).

Only available for claims for children born or adopted on or after 1 July 2020

PCB

ASS

Eligibility has been determined and the claim is pending proof of birth/entry into care. This status only relates to pre-birth claims for PPL
