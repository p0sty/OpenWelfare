Advice of death of a customer + Read more ...

When a person contacts to advise of the death of a customer, staff must offer:

  • the notifier the services of a social worker in the first instance or at any time the notifier becomes or shows signs of distress during the contact
  • the pamphlet Needing help after someone has died? (HD001) and if accepted, issue to the person if at a service centre, otherwise refer them to 'Death of a loved one' on the Service Australia website

The following cultural issues must be respected and considered by Service Officers when being advised of the death of an Indigenous customer:

  • In some cases, immediate family members cannot mention the name of the deceased person. This means immediate family members may have to ask other relatives/friends to call on their behalf or attend the interview with the notifier
  • In this situation, it may also be offensive to the person for the Service Officer to say the name of the deceased customer. If unsure, sensitively ask the person if it is all right for them to use the deceased customer's name. If the person is not comfortable with this, avoid using the deceased customer's name wherever possible. See Cultural considerations when someone dies

3

Gather all information possible + Read more ...

Take action immediately following notification of a death, including via:

  • an Advice of death form (SA116)
  • information provided in a claim by the partner of a deceased person

Note: get as much information as possible during the initial contact to reduce the need for future contact with the deceased person's partner or family during this stressful time.

To confirm the death, the notifier must at least give the full name and date of death of the deceased adult plus at least 2 of the following:

  • deceased adult's last address in full
  • date of birth
  • Customer Reference Number (CRN), or
  • partner's name

The notifier must also give their name, address, phone number and relationship to the deceased.

Check the correct customer's record has been selected before taking steps to record the death. For example, do not record the death on the deceased customer's partner or parent's record. Performing a death action on the wrong record causes unnecessary grief and stress to the deceased person's family.

Is there enough information to confirm the death of this customer?

  • Yes, death can be confirmed, go to Step 5
  • No, need more checks to confirm death, go to Step 6

4

Notification of death is an aged care MFU: I012 DSS have advised that aged care resident died on (date) + Read more ...

An MFU is created when an aged care service has coded that a customer has left care for reason - deceased.

If notification is received from an aged care service, get further confirmation before actioning the notification of death guided procedure.

Check Customer First for any record of contact regarding a customer’s death:

  • Check Document Tools for an SA116 – Advice of Death or letter
  • Check Document List (DL) screen for other notification received,

If no other evidence is on record, investigate further to confirm death:

  • Website search – for example, use customer’s name, state and year of death with the word death or funeral
  • Refer to Resources for website links
  • confirm that service that has coded departure from care by checking the RCA Institution Summary (RIS) screen
  • Contact the aged care service to confirm the details that have been coded
    • Search the internet to get the service phone number
    • Give details for care recipient
    • Verify departure reason and date of death with service
    • Note the person’s name that gave information for inclusion in notes

For all care types, if date of death is confirmed and is different to the date that is in ACSP:

  • document reason for different date being used in Notification of Death procedure
  • Aged Care Programmes will investigate departure details
  • escalate with relevant details to Aged Care Programmes - Operations team using the escalation form, the Resources page has a link to the form

Is there enough information to confirm the death of this customer?

  • Yes,
    • death can be confirmed,
    • if confirmed date is different to reported date, escalate to Aged Care Programmes – Operations team,
    • if there are activities blocking completion, refer to Step 5 for assistance in getting them cleared,
    • When ready to action, see Completing Notification of Death guided procedure using data match for steps on completing the guided procedure using aged care service details, including confirmation source
    • procedure ends here
  • No,
    • If the death cannot be confirmed or the service cannot give information, suspend the customer’s payment, go to Step 6
    • If confirmation is received that the customer has not died, see Aged care MFUs after departure from care

5

Confirm the death of the deceased + Read more ...

Staff can access and run the Notification of death guided procedure in the deceased customer’s record. This will help determine if the death can be confirmed.

Confirm if the person notifying of the death is the executor of the estate. If the informant is not the executor, but knows the executor details, get the:

  • name and address of the executor, and
  • name of the funeral director or hospital

Nominee arrangements and Power of Attorney (POA) stop at the time of death. Send any correspondence, including the payment summary and possible debt recovery action from a deceased estate to the:

  • executor, or
  • administrator of an estate

Services Australia's ability to finalise any outstanding matters will be impacted if executor or administrator details are:

  • incorrectly recorded, or
  • not recorded when details have been given

If the executor details are not known at the time of death notification, leave the executor details fields in the Notification of Death guided procedure blank.

Note: executor details can be recorded manually on the Death/Executor (DEA) screen if details become known later.

Go to step 7

6

Death cannot be confirmed + Read more ...

If the death cannot be confirmed:

  • use the Notification of death guided procedure to obtain and record all relevant information from the informant, including their own name, address, contact number and relationship to the deceased
  • obtain executor details, if known
  • obtain the name of the funeral director or hospital where the deceased passed away, if known

The guided procedure will not code the death, but will create a DOC, which is an open work item to be allocated to a Service Officer with the required skill to complete.

Note: if multiple notifications are received about the death of the same person, do not re-run the guided procedure. Instead, check the deceased's record and annotate the original DOC to state details are correct and who advised.

If the guided procedure is not available, create an open work item. Use Fast Note - select Auto text, use Generic > Update > Death Notification (Adult) - ACTION REQUIRED.

If there is doubt as to the deceased's identity:

  • contact the funeral director or the hospital where the deceased passed away
  • if this is not an option and contact with relatives is proposed, social worker involvement may be necessary

If death cannot be confirmed and the customer's whereabouts cannot be established, suspend payment until the death can be confirmed:

  • select the suspension option in the Notification of Death guided procedure, it will use the reason 'other' (OTH). The suspension date of effect is the unconfirmed date of death or date paid to plus 1 (DPT+1), whichever is the later
  • if the guided procedure is not available or the suspension option cannot be selected, code the suspension manually
  • record a DOC on the customer's record, ensuring that it meets Online Document Recording (ODR) standards for significant decisions and including what action is needed to confirm the death
  • do not issue a suspension letter. Cancel the advice activity on the AL screen triggered by the 'OTH' suspension

Note: it is imperative that the death is confirmed and actioned as a matter of urgency.

When the death is confirmed, go to Step 7.

7

Check for started activities + Read more ...

A death action cannot be processed with activities on the Activity List (AL) screen. The Notification of death guided procedure will give a prompt if there is an activity on the AL screen.

Note: There may be outstanding activities not shown on AL. Check Process Direct Transactions button to ensure all outstanding activities are processed.

If a deceased customer's record was suspended while the death was being confirmed and their death has now been confirmed, manually check for started activities on the AL screen.

Make sure all activities are completed on the customer's and partner's records, including digital image work items, and run the daily driver (DDR).

If there is an 'STA' debt activity on the AL screen, create an open work item advising of the death and requesting the debt activity be completed. The work item will automatically map to the relevant area. Use Fast Note – select Auto text, use Debts > Debt Raising > Debt Raising ACTion DOC Request

If there is an RCA/NCL or RCA/HOM activity on the AL screen, add the keyword RCAPRI. The RCA team will action the activity and complete the death action.

Process any other outstanding activities, (for example, claims or reassessments):

  • Service Officers who do not have the skills to process the outstanding activities should use existing escalation channel, refer to Immediate new claim and non-new claim priority processing
  • Do not delete or cancel activities in order to finalise a death action. Claims/activities cannot be actioned/reindexed on deceased records

Have all started activities been completed?

  • Yes, go to Step 9
  • No, go to Step 8

8

When it is not possible to complete an activity + Read more ...

If there is an outstanding activity that the Service Officer is unable to complete, suspend the deceased customer's payment (if not already suspended).

The suspension option is not available in the Notification of death guided procedure if there is an activity stopping the death action:

  • manually suspend using the reason 'OTH' (other) pending completion of the outstanding activity
  • the suspension date of effect is the date of death or date paid to plus 1 (DPT + 1), whichever is later
  • record a DOC on the customer's record, ensuring that it meets Online Document Recording (ODR) minimum standards for significant decisions and including what action must occur so the death can be actioned
  • as the customer's death has been confirmed, do not send a letter. It is not appropriate to send a letter to a deceased customer. Cancel the advice activity on the AL screen triggered by the 'OTH' suspension

If an open DOC/work item about the death notification has not already been created, either

  • run the Notification of death guided procedure to generate an open work item for the relevant staff to complete the death action once the outstanding activities have been completed, or
  • if the guided procedure is not available, create an open work item.
    Use Fast Note - select Auto text, use Generic > Update > Death Notification (Adult) - ACTION REQUIRED and enter all the relevant information about the Death notification to allow another person to run the workflow on another day including, but not limited to:
    • identification of the person notifying of the death
    • the information obtained to confirm the identity of the deceased person
    • the name and address of the executor or Administrator, and
    • the name of the funeral director or hospital

If a DOC/work item about the death notification is assigned to a Service Officer, but there are still outstanding activities on the record they can't complete:

  • place the work item on hold for a day (or 2 or 3 days if the activity may take a while to complete)
  • the Service Officer must check the record each day if the 'hold to user' option is selected

When activity is completed, go to Step 9.

14

Process the death action + Read more ...

Run the Notification of death guided procedure to code the death action.

If the guided procedure is not available, manually code the death action.

When recording a death the Notification Handler (NOHL) is incorrectly using the date of notification as the date to increase the survivor's rate to the single rate. The workaround for this is to record the date of receipt (DOR) as the later of either the date of death (DOD) or date paid to + 1 (DPT + 1).

Note: the workaround auto-applies if the Notification of death guided procedure is used.

Before finalising the death action activity, check the auto-generated Lump Sum Bereavement Payment (LBP) amount on the Assessment Results (AR) screen. If it appears to be correct, a manual calculation is not needed. Only if a manual calculation is needed, see Calculation of a Lump Sum Bereavement Payment (LBP).

The deceased person's pension is cancelled from the later of the date of death (DOD) or date paid to + 1 day (DPT + 1).

If the surviving partner was a joint owner of any assets with their late partner, full ownership of the assets legally transfers to the surviving partner following the death of their partner. However, for social security purposes, if assets are owned in joint names with the deceased partner, the surviving partner is only assessed as owning 50%. The system will continue to assess that the surviving partner owns 50% of the asset, until the surviving partner advises of the transfer of assets.

During the death action, if the system displays the warning 'WC - Change prior to Limiting Date - Update WC balance':

  • check the Working Credit Balance Summary (WCBS) screen, and
  • if needed, correct the balance on the W/C Manual override (WCRC) screen

Note: if someone other than the partner (a third party carer) is getting Carer Payment for the deceased, see Death of an adult or child care receiver and the effect on Carer Payment.

Surviving partner is a homeowner and either an aged care resident or in a care situation + Read more ...

If the surviving partner has a status of RCA/CUR-DSS, they have started Aged Care. See RCA Circumstance (RCIRC) or RCA Institution Summary (RIS) screens to confirm that surviving partner is in Care Type: Residential and not Care Type: Home.

Their former home is an exempt asset, if the:

  • surviving partner is either an aged care resident or in a care situation, and
  • customer was residing in the principal home

When the customer at home dies, a 2 year exemption period starts for the partner. Other exemptions may also be applicable for aged care residents. See Vacation of principal home due to illness.

If a member of a couple dies after their partner vacated their home to enter a care situation, the start date of the 2 year exemption period is set from the later of the date:

  • the partner entered a care situation
  • the deceased customer entered a care situation (if applicable)
  • of the customer’s death, if they resided in the principal home

5

Switching to the partner's record + Read more ...

After the death action has been recorded, the system will auto-switch to the Assessment Results (AR) screen of the surviving partner's record and show:

  • the surviving partner's rate that will auto-update to the single rate, or maintained at the single rate for a surviving partner who was separated due to ill health. The rate is based on the surviving partner's income and assets. The rate is varied from date paid to + 1 day (DPT + 1) or date of death (DOD), whichever is the later
  • an auto-calculated Lump Sum Bereavement Payment (LBP) if the partner is eligible. For example, the single rate is less than the combined member of a couple rate

If the surviving partner starts work, or is currently working, working credits may enable them to keep some of their income support payment while they are working.

If their income reduces their fortnightly rate to nil and some of that income is employment income, they may remain current at nil rate for up to 6 fortnights and retain certain benefits.
Note: if the person's deceased partner was working and had any working credits, these credits cannot be transferred to them.

If the surviving partner is granted a reversionary income stream, assess from the date it is first entitled to be received. A lump sum arrears payment received by the reversionary beneficiary, accrued between the death of the primary beneficiary and when the reversionary beneficiary starts to get regular payments, is assessed as income from the income stream. This means the income is assessed over the period to which it relates. Effective from 9 May 2018, if bereavement provisions apply and the assessment of lump sum arrears result in a debt during the bereavement period for the surviving spouse, it can be waived under the legislative instrument ‘Social Security (Waiver of Debts - Bereavement Period) Specification 2017’ made under subsection 1237AB(1) of the Social Security Act 1991.
Note: any debt incurred after the Act's 14 week bereavement period has ended would not be waived under the provisions of this legislative instrument. The legislative instrument is not retrospective. This means that any of these debts that occur before 9 May 2018 cannot be waived under class of debt waiver provisions

Is the surviving partner receiving Carer Payment (CP) or Carer Allowance (CA)?

Note: if someone other than the partner (a third party carer) gets CP/CA for the deceased, see Death of a care receiver and the effect on Carer Payment, or Death of an adult care receiver and the effect on Carer Allowance.

  • No, go to Step 7

6

Surviving partner gets Carer Payment (CP) or Carer Allowance (CA) + Read more ...

If the surviving partner gets CP and:

  • the deceased partner was the care receiver, CP can continue to the partner for 7 full payments after the death of the care receiver. A Lump Sum Bereavement Payment (LBP) may also be payable. Note: CP (XWP) continues during the bereavement period if the care receiver who died was the only or last CA care receiver. A bereavement period does not apply if CP (XWP) continues because the customer gets payment level CA for a different care receiver. See Transfer from Wife Pension (WP) to Carer Payment (CP)
  • the deceased partner was not the care receiver:
    • CP can continue whilst the customer is still providing care. A LBP may be payable
    • tell the customer of allowable absences from providing care
  • the surviving partner is getting CA and an income support payment (other than Carer Payment) that does not qualify them for a bereavement payment and:
    • the deceased partner was the care receiver, a CA Bereavement Payment may be payable
    • the deceased partner was not the care receiver, CA can continue while the customer is still providing care

8

Lump sum bereavement payment for DVA customers + Read more ...

DVA may be responsible for paying the lump sum bereavement payment.

Transfer the BRV activity to the DVA Clearance Team (SCS) if the deceased partner was receiving any of the following payments from DVA:

  • Social Security Age Pension (paid by DVA)
  • Age Service Pension
  • Partner Service Pension
  • Invalidity Service Pension
  • Carer Service Pension
  • Veteran Payment
  • Income Support Supplement (ISS)

Transfer the BRV activity to the DVA Clearance Team (SCS) for assessment of the LBP if:

  • both the surviving partner and deceased partner were receiving an income support payment paid by Services Australia, and
  • either of them are receiving a Defence Force Income Support Allowance (DFISA) payment

If the deceased partner was getting a DVA Disability Pension, DVA auto-pays the LBP to the surviving partner.

Note: if the surviving partner is a social security pensioner, they can receive a LBP from DVA as well as from Services Australia.

SCS will assess entitlement for the LBP and calculate the amount.

Will SCS finalise the bereavement activity?

  • Yes, go to Step 10
  • No, go to Step 9

9

Manual calculation and issue of LBP + Read more ...

The Service Officer should perform a lump sum bereavement payment calculation manually. Select the BRV/OOP activity and on the One Off Payments (OOP) screen:

  • in the Effect date field, key the date of death
  • in the Payment Type field, key 'BLS' (Lump Sum Bereavement Payment)
  • press [Enter] to view the Assessment Results (AR) screen
  • key 'O' next to the BRV/OOP line to display the OOP screen
  • in the Amount column on the Bereavement Payment line, key the LBP amount
  • press [Enter] twice to return to the AR screen. The activity can now be completed
  • record details in a DOC

10

Action to take after death action is completed + Read more ...

After completing the death action:

  • offer referral to a social worker if appropriate
  • if the deceased person was receiving Family Tax Benefit, see Death of a family assistance payment or Parental Leave Pay (PPL) customer or child
  • if the surviving partner is getting Carer Payment and will not have an ongoing entitlement, tell the surviving partner to claim an alternative payment before Bereavement Period End Date (BPED) + 1 is reached. An auto-review (reason either BRV or BRS) is set for the BPED + 1
  • reissue a concession card to the partner if it does not auto-reissue
  • identify any service providers to be advised of the death
  • update executor details after the death has been recorded
  • surviving partners getting a pension are issued a Centrelink Bereavement Statement 8 weeks after their partner's date of death. A bereavement income and asset review may also be completed
  • If the death action resulted in a potential overpayment for the deceased person, see Requesting refunds and raising debts for deceased customer.

These actions may auto-cancel the surviving partner's payment. If a letter is not auto-created, send a Q486 letter to tell them about the cancellation and available bereavement assistance.
