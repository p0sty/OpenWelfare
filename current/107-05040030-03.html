  • Update the comments line on the Garnishee Details (OPGD) screen. An example of wording may be â€“ Garnishee set for $50 per week, payments being sent monthly for $200

4

Reasonable effort to contact + Read more ...

Make a reasonable effort to contact the employer or third party. Contact the person/section responsible for implementing the garnishee. In the case of a new garnishee confirm if the garnishee notice has been received. For existing or older garnishees ascertain the reason for payments ceasing.

Is it possible to speak directly to the relevant employer or third party by phone?

  • Yes:
    • for new garnishees, go to Step 7
    • for existing garnishees, go to Step 8
  • No, go to Step 5

5

Leave a message for the relevant employer or third party + Read more ...

If the person responsible for implementing the garnishee is unavailable, leave a message. Avoid giving the employee's name or the reason for the call. Providing the customers CRN is acceptable.

Leave a message such as 'This is a message for (name) or (payroll). Please call Services Australia on (phone number) by 5pm on dd/mm/yy, quoting reference number 111 111 111x.'

Is it possible to leave a message for the employer or third party?

  • Yes:
    • leave a message
    • record the time and reason of the call in a DOC
    • if there is no response, make a second attempt 2 days later
    • go to Step 6
  • No:
    • make a second attempt on the next business day
    • if unable to speak with a third party or leave a message on the second attempt, record the time and reason of the call in a DOC
    • see Step 2 in Table 2

6

Has the employer or third party responded to message/s left? + Read more ...

  • Yes:
    • for new garnishees, go to Step 7
    • for older or existing garnishees, go to Step 8
  • No, see Step 2 in Table 2

Has the garnishee notice been received? + Read more ...

  • Yes, go to Step 8
  • No, see Step 3 in Table 2

Can garnishee be implemented or continued? + Read more ...

Is the customer still employed?

  • Yes, go to Step 9
  • No, see Varying or ceasing a garnishee notice to recover debts

9

Resolving difficulties or concerns + Read more ...

Find out why the employer or third party has not started or continued the garnishee. Try to resolve any difficulties or concerns.

If the employer or third party does not want to apply the garnishee, explain that they are required to do so by law.

Will the employer or third party now implement or recommence the garnishee?

  • Yes, go to Step 1 in Table 2
  • No, go to Step 2 in Table 2

Implementation or non-compliance

Table 2

Step

Action

1

Implement the garnishee notice + Read more ...

Confirm when the first payment will occur.

Confirm the employer's intended method and frequency of remittance.

Update the Pay Start Date & Frequency: field on the Garnishee Details (OPGD) screen. This should be:

  • the date the garnishee payment is expected, or
  • two weeks from today's date (whichever is the later)

Go to Step 4.

2

Non-compliance + Read more ...

Advise the employer or third party in writing they did not comply with a garnishee notice.

  • Calculate the amount recoverable to date
  • Send a 'Garnishee-warning re non-compliance-employer & FI' (Q276) letter
  • Code the Review Registration (RVR) screen, completing the following fields:
    • ServRsn: key 'DMN'
    • RevRsn: key 'REV'
    • Due Date: key the date 14 days from the date the Q276 letter was sent
    • Text: key 'follow up wage garnishee non-compliance'
      Coding the review for 14 days in the future gives the employer or third party time to respond to the Q276

If the employer or third party does not respond to the Q276 letter after 14 days, make a final attempt to contact employer or third party by phone.

If the employer or third party still refuses to comply with the notice, or cannot be contacted:

  • cease the garnishee. See Varying or ceasing a garnishee notice to recover debts
  • record details on a DOC, and
  • pursue other recovery or non-recovery action

Process ends here.

3

Garnishee notice has not been received + Read more ...

  • Reprint the garnishee notice
  • Discuss with the employer the best way to get notice to them (for example, mailing address, fax, Registered Mail, PGP secure webmail)
  • Update the Pay Start Date: field on the Garnishee Details (OPGD) screen. This should be to the date garnishee payment is expected or 2 weeks from today's date (whichever is later)

Go to Step 4.

4

Record all details on a DOC using Fast Note located in Customer First + Read more ...

On the summary line, key 'Recovery: Rang Empl re Garnishee'.

In the Txt: field, record all details of the conversation with the employer or third party.
