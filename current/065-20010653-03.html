Manual adjustment calculations - preliminary checks

Action

1

Determine if a manual adjustment calculation is appropriate + Read more ...

Confirm an adjustment will not occur automatically at a future date. See Service Support Officer (SSO), Technical Peer Support (TPS) or ICT referrals for assistance.

A manual adjustment calculation is not appropriate where:

  • an ad-hoc review will process a review of fees and charges including calculation of any refund due
  • a quarterly review (QR) will automatically complete a review of fees and charges
  • a departure review will automatically complete a review of fees and charges
    • after changes to income and assets are coded, including calculation of any refund due
  • a referral to systems support assistance to remediate record is able to adjust fees and charges and pay any refunds owed
  • a system issue preventing a correct refund being paid has not been escalated to ICT

Is a manual adjustment calculation appropriate for this care recipient?

  • Yes, manual adjustment calculation required, go to Step 2
  • No,

6

Care recipient entered care prior to 25 November 2018 + Read more ...

Previous home care data did not transfer into SPARC. Where required, manually add previous home care fees paid into calculations.

Due to system limitations, the anniversary date used for any calculations remains the same as the residential care date of entry. A care recipient should not pay more than the annual cap amount within a 12 month period.

When a care recipient changes care type, that becomes a transition year:

  • The start date is the anniversary date of the initial care type, and the annual cap is calculated from that date
  • Once the annual cap is calculated, the anniversary date becomes the date of entry of the new care type
    • This means it may be more than 12 months before a fee is reset after an annual cap is reached

10

Determine whether CSR to zero applies + Read more ...

Care subsidy reduction (CSR) to zero only applies to any means tested care fees (MTCF) a care recipient is liable to pay. It applies if:

  • a care recipient is not issued a fee advice within 6 months of entry into care
  • a care recipient leaves a residential care service (without entering another residential care service), or dies, before the service provider has been informed of the care recipient's CSR, or
  • a fee advice was not issued more than 7 days prior to departure

Confirm if the care recipient was issued with a fee advice letter by checking the following:

  • For residential care letters issued after 22 July 2022 check ACSP:
    • in care recipient record check Correspondence > Letters summary
  • For residential care letters before 22 July 2022 check ACMPS Communication History for home care and residential care recipients
    • For residential care recipients, RC39 or 11 series letters sent from ACMPS prior to 14 November 2015 qualify as fee notification as they specify the MTCF
    • RC39 letters sent after this date do not advise the MTCF, therefore the care recipient has not been correctly notified of their fees

If a fee notification not sent within 6 months of entry to care, or more than 7 days prior to departure, also check:

  • Customer First - Document Tools and Document List to confirm manual letters issued

If a fee notification was not sent within 6 months of entry into care, include CSR to zero in the manual adjustment calculations for MTCF. Take into account the date of initial fee advice.

See Aged Care fees and charges - care subsidy reduction to zero for more information.

Review financial hardship and manual suspensions + Read more ...

If financial hardship assistance is granted, the reduced means tested care fee (MTCF) rate must be taken into account when calculating the total value of MTCF paid since the start date. Note: a backdated hardship assessment can refund fees in a future QR.

To determine if financial hardship assistance is granted:

  • Home Care:
    • In ACMPS, check the Hardship & Income Suspension and Notes tabs
  • Residential Care:
    • In ACSP, check Hardship and income suspension > Hardship summary
    • For suspension of fees, check Means Testing > Remediation of fees
  • Both - check Customer First Document List and Document Tools for evidence of financial hardship assistance

8

Multiple providers while in residential care + Read more ...

When a care recipient has multiple residential care providers, assess the periods of care separately.

Complete the Manual Adjustment Calculator with a separate tab for each provider and an overall accrual calculation of all fees paid to all providers.

For each previous provider, compare the correct income and assets based fees to the previous fees charged. If the calculator shows a result that is:

  • Positive
    • Issue a refund to the previous provider
    • Use correct fees based on income and assets in the overall calculation
  • Negative due to error in manual refunds paid (over-refunding)
    • Use correct fees based on income and assets in the overall calculation
  • Negative due to previously charged fees lower than correct fees
    • Use the previously charged fees in the overall calculation as no fees are now able to be recovered and will not be offset between providers
  • Neither positive or negative (nil), use the correct fees based on income and assets in the overall calculation

For the current provider, always compare the correct income and assets based fees to the previous fees charged in the calculation.

Create manual letter + Read more ...
