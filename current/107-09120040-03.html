The payment details are adjusted by the system.

Procedure ends here.

5

Excess payment reason + Read more ...

When updating the YA customer record with the verified (actual) parental income, check if a legally recoverable debt exists. See Finalised 'no debt' (FND) or 'zeroing' debts that are not legally recoverable.

See the Resources page for an example of a legally recoverable debt.

If a YA customer's rate of payment is based on an estimate of parental income, a letter requesting verification is sent. Check the Household Income and Assets (NHI) screen to confirm the date of issue. No debt can be raised before the 21 day period after the letter is sent.

Did the YA customer's excess payment arise because verified (actual) parental income is higher than the estimate?

  • Yes, go to Step 6
  • No, go to Step 8

6

Actual income timeframe + Read more ...

Did the YA customer provide the verified (actual) parental income within the 21 day timeframe given in the verification letter?

  • Yes, go to Step 7
  • No, go to Step 8

7

Verified income higher than estimate + Read more ...

If the debt occurred solely because the verified (actual) parental income is higher than the estimate, the excess payment is not legally recoverable. Go to Step 9.

8

Examine the cause of the debt + Read more ...

A legally recoverable debt exists if:

  • the YA customer did not respond within 21 days to the agency's notice of request to verify their parental income. Raise the debt from day 22 after this notice was sent. See Step 9 in Table 2
  • parental income is unable to be verified within 21 days, and the YA customer did not advise of verified parental income by the nominated date (between 30 September to 31 December). Raise the debt based on the estimate for the whole period (starting from 30 September)
  • the YA customer lodges a Tax Notice of Assessment (TNA). See Finalised 'no debt' (FND) or 'zeroing' debts that are not legally recoverable

No legally recoverable debt exists if:

  • the DMN/NDB activity generated even though the YA customer responded to the agency's notice of request within 21 days, and there is an increase to the verified (actual) parental income
  • the agency did not send the YA customer a notice of request to verify (actual) parental income. The customer cannot be expected to know that they have an obligation to provide this information. See Finalised 'no debt' (FND) or 'zeroing' debts that are not legally recoverable

Go to Step 9.

Finalising a PIT debt activity

Table 2

Step

Action

1

Finalise activity on the ASC + Read more ...

Finalise the activity on the Assessment Consequences (ASC) screen.

Is the debt amount over $50?

  • Yes, go to Step 2
  • No, no further action is required because the system automatically waives debts of less than $50 resulting from a reassessment. Procedure ends here

2

Is the debt correct? + Read more ...

Check the debt amount to make sure it is correct.

For ABSTUDY customers, the debt will be generated from 1 October, regardless of whether notification of actual income was received within 14 days or not.

3

Debt waiver + Read more ...

Is it appropriate to waive the debt?

  • Yes, see Waiving Centrelink debts. Procedure ends here
  • No, for recovery of the debt go to Step 4

4

ABSTUDY customer debt + Read more ...

Is the debt for an ABSTUDY customer?

  • Yes, go to Step 5
  • No, go to Step 8

5

ABSTUDY debt payee/debtor + Read more ...

Determine who is the payee/debtor for the ABSTUDY debt.

Check the Payment Summary (PS) screen for details of the relevant payments the student was not qualified to receive. Select the relevant payment(s).

The system navigates to the Payment Details (PD) screen. The name and address of the payee is in the To: field. The payee could be the:

  • student
  • parent
  • guardian
  • applicant, or
  • the institution where the student resides or attends

Raise the debt against the payee's record.

6

Check the AL screen + Read more ...

Check the Activity List (AL) screen for a 'DMN/NDB' activity.

Is the ABSTUDY debt shell correctly created in the payee's record?

  • Yes, go to Step 8
  • No, go to Step 7

7

Finalise debt shell + Read more ...

Finalise the debt shell that is not required - 'S'elect the 'DMN/NDB' activity from the AL screen. The OPMA screen displays. A number of fields will have already defaulted to this screen. Continue to code the screen:

  • Calculated Amount $: field, enter '0.00'
  • FND Reason: field, enter 'TPD' for third party debt
  • Addntl Reference No: field, enter the CRN of the student/parent/guardian
  • press [Enter]
  • finalise the OPAF screen and record details of the decision in a DOC in that record. For further information, see Online Document Recording (ODR)

Send an Account Payable letter. See Safeguarding customer privacy when raising and recovering debts, before issuing the account payable letter. Take the appropriate actions to protect the customer's privacy.

Go to Step 10.

8

Debt Action script + Read more ...

The Debt Action script may be used to raise the debt.

Note: adjust the text of the Account Payable letter to suit the student's circumstances.

Is the script available and working?

  • Yes, follow the prompts. Go to Step 10
  • No, follow the manual procedure

Raise the debt. See actioning an undetermined debt on the Debt Management and Information System (DMIS).

Send an Account Payable letter. See Safeguarding customer privacy when raising and recovering debts, before issuing the account payable letter. Take the appropriate actions to protect the customer's privacy.

Finalise the activity and note the Debt ID. Record details of the decision on a DOC, including the Debt ID number in the summary line.

Go to Step 10.

9

Calculate the debt + Read more ...

Calculate the debt from:

  • the 'Date of Issue' of the TNA if this is after 1 January, or
  • the first day of the period that includes 1 January, if the 'Date of Issue' is before 1 January

Where the debt shell created by the system has been backdated to a date including 1 October:

  • finalise 'no debt', and
  • use FND reason code excess payment EXP - no legal debt

To raise the debt manually, see Create a new debt record manually on the Debt Management and Information System (DMIS).

10

Ensure debt is correct + Read more ...

Make sure details of the debt are available.

Scan any paper documents used as evidence to support the debt to the customer's file. See Calculating Centrelink entitlements and gathering information when investigating debts.
