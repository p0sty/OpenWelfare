Claim lodgement + Read more ...

A claim work item is created for allocation and processing in Process Direct where a customer submits:

  • an online claim
  • an Assisted Customer Claim (ACC), or
  • a Claim for Youth Allowance (SY001) or Claim for Austudy (SY002) paper claim

Where a paper claim is scanned onto a customer's record, the system creates a Social Online Application (SOA) Shell.

Where a SOA Shell claim is allocated to a Student Processing skilled Service Officer and the claim is for a job seeker or Australian Apprentice, it must be re-categorised appropriately:

  • Australian Apprentice claim (ZCLM_ZASA)

Check course is approved + Read more ...

5

Check customer meets requirements for being a student + Read more ...

A customer applying for YA or Austudy as a student must be studying full-time, or undertaking an approved concessional study load. For more information, see:

  • Satisfying the activity test for Youth Allowance (YA) by undertaking full-time study
  • Satisfying the activity test for Austudy

YA students, who are temporarily incapacitated, may also be considered a student during that time. See Youth Allowance (YA) student or Australian Apprentice becomes incapacitated.

Is the customer:

  • studying full-time
  • undertaking an approved concessional study load, or
  • applying for YA and is unable to study full-time due to a temporary incapacity?
    • Yes, go to Step 6
    • No, customer is not qualified for YA or Austudy as a student. Reject the claim, see Table 7

6

Identifying the most beneficial payment + Read more ...

The following resources may assist in identifying the most beneficial payment. This must be determined before assessing the student claim.

  • JobSeeker Payment (JSP), customers returning to study may be able to remain on JSP or test their eligibility for YA/Austudy, see JobSeeker Payment (JSP) job seekers returning to full-time study
  • Youth Allowance (job seeker), the claim does not need assessment. Manually change the status of the claim to Not Required and follow the process in Youth Allowance (YA) job seeker or Australian Apprentice returning to full-time study. Procedure ends here
  • Parenting Payment Partnered (PPP), customers undertaking a course of any length may remain on PPP or transfer to YA/Austudy. See Part-time or full-time study as an approved activity for Parenting Payment (PP)
  • Partner Allowance (PA), Farm Household Allowance (FHA), customers may remain on their current payment or choose to transfer to YA/Austudy, whichever is more beneficial
    PA customers who choose to transfer to YA/Austudy will not be able to reclaim PA in the future
  • Bereavement Allowance (BVA), customers can choose to transfer to YA/Austudy at the end of the bereavement period
  • Pensioner Education Supplement (PES) qualifying payment, in most cases PES would be the most advantageous payment for the student and it is more beneficial for the customer to continue to receive their current payment

Is YA/Austudy the most beneficial payment?

  • Yes, and the customer is transferring from:
    • JobSeeker Payment (JSP), go to Step 8
    • All other payments, go to Step 7
  • No, reject the claim using reason COP 'Another Payment is More Appropriate'. See Table 7

7

Customers transferring from payments other than JSP + Read more ...

To be eligible for a payment transfer, a customer must lodge a completed claim within 14 days from the day:

  • they ceased to qualify for their original payment, or
  • the original payment will cease because of the transfer

Customers receiving income support payments other than JobSeeker (JSP) may choose to transfer to a student payment at any time during their studies. When assessing a transfer for these customers, the student start date only needs to be considered if the customer is making an early claim or more than 14 days have passed since their original payment cancelled.

When was the claim lodged?

  • Within 14 days of the customer's student start date or within 14 days of the date the original payment will cancel because of the transfer, see Table 2
  • Up to 13 weeks before the customer's student start date, the claim may be assessed as an early claim, see Table 2
  • More than 13 weeks before the customer's student start date, the claim cannot be accepted as an early claim, see Table 7
  • Within 13 weeks of the day their original payment stopped (or will stop because of the transfer), go to Step 9
  • More than 13 weeks after the day their original payment stopped (or will stop because of the transfer), the claim cannot be assessed as a transfer. See Assessing and coding student and Australian Apprentice claims for Youth Allowance (YA) or Assessing and coding student and Australian Apprentice claims for Austudy. Procedure ends here.

8

Customers transferring from JobSeeker payment (JSP) + Read more ...

When was the claim lodged?

  • Within 14 days of the customer's student start date, see Table 2
  • Up to 13 weeks before the customer's student start date, the claim may be assessed as an early claim, see Table 2
  • More than 13 weeks before the customer's student start date, the claim cannot be accepted as an early claim. Reject the claim, see Table 7
  • More than 14 days after the customer's student start date. Is study in any course included in their current Job Plan?
    • Yes, if a customer has study recorded in their current Job Plan their claim can be assessed as a transfer regardless of whether study or an exemption existed on their student start date. This is because a determination as to whether the customer was meeting their activity test requirements would have been made at the time the current Job Plan was negotiated, therefore there is no need to make a retrospective assessment of this. See Table 2
    • No, if the customer's mutual obligations have been paused or there is an existing exemption from the activity test, treat these customers as though study is included in the Job Plan. Continue assessing the claim as a transfer, see Table 2. Otherwise, go to Step 9

9

Check the reason claim lodgement is delayed + Read more ...

Has the customer lodged the student claim within 13 weeks of starting study or the day their original payment stopped (or will stop because of the transfer) and suffered a medical condition that was the sole or principal cause of the delay in lodging the claim?

  • Yes, the start date is their transfer day, see Table 2
  • No, the claim cannot be assessed as a transfer, go to Step 10

10

Cancelling the current payment + Read more ...

Is the customer's current payment cancelled?

  • Yes, assess the YA/Austudy claim see Assessing and coding student and Australian Apprentice claims for Youth Allowance (YA) or Assessing and coding student and Australian Apprentice claims for Austudy. Procedure ends here
  • No, action the cancellation if required. JSP customers are not considered to have met their activity test requirements for JSP while studying, cancel the JSP from the student start date as per JobSeeker Payment (JSP) job seekers returning to full-time study
    • Do a SAP refresh in Process Direct before assessing the student claim
    • Assess the YA/Austudy claim
    • Go to: Assessing and coding student and Australian Apprentice claims for Youth Allowance (YA) or Assessing and coding student and Australian Apprentice claims for Austudy

Academic Transcript + Read more ...

Before requesting a transcript, the following must be considered:

  • For Austudy, was previous study undertaken by the student at the same level as the current course?
  • For Youth Allowance, was previous study undertaken by the student in the same course?
  • Was the previous study undertaken within the 10 years before the current course start date?
  • Based on the information provided, is it clear the customer:
    • can be granted now, and a transcript requested post-grant?
    • does not have any remaining allowable time, and must be rejected?

Where the customer indicates they will continue to study beyond their allowable time end date:

  • grant the payment coding an Allowable Time End Date on the Education Course Details (EDC) screen, and
  • where appropriate, request a transcript after finalising the claim

If considering requesting a transcript:

  • attempt to call the customer. If the customer can provide enough verbal information to make a reasonable assessment for allowable time/aggregation of study, go to Step 5

4

Transfer from JSP: YA/Austudy claim submitted within 14 days of the student start date + Read more ...

Grant the YA/Austudy payment and cancel the Jobseeker Payment from the customer's Student Start Date. There is no gap in entitlement.

This applies whether or not the customer had study in their Job Plan.

In Process Direct, update the following fields on the Miscellaneous Circumstance Details (NMIS) screen:

  • Current Circumstances: field key either FTS if no Education Entry Payment (EdEP) is to be paid or FTE if EdEP is to be paid. See Step 2 in Table 6 for further information on assessing entitlement to EdEP
  • Event Date: field key the date the customer started study

Go to the Date of Commencement (NDC) screen:

  • Code a Manual Start Date matching the customer's Student Start Date
  • In the Comments field, add: 'Claim submitted within 14 days of commencing study'

Continue to process the claim. Make sure the following are the same as the Manual Start Date:

  • Participation Status Event Date (PSED) on the Education Details (EDC) screen, and
  • Start Date on the Newstart Education Status (NES) screen

When finalising the claim, the customer's JSP cancels (JSP/CAN-STU-CUS COMMENCE/RESUME STUDIES *AUTO) from the Student Start Date.

If the claim is delayed because of processing timeliness, cancelling JSP may lead to a debt. This may be offset by arrears for the YAL/Austudy payment.

See Step 1 in Table 6.

5

Transfer from JSP: YA/Austudy claim submitted more than 14 days after student start date + Read more ...

If study in any course is included in the customer's current Job Plan, the customer is meeting their activity test requirements for JSP while studying.

A customer must have study included in their current Job Plan. Where the customer's student start date is in the past and no study was included in their Job Plan or any exemption in place on their student start date but they have since negotiated a new Job Plan with study included, they are meeting their activity test. This is because a determination as to whether the customers meeting their activity test requirements would have been made at the time the current Job Plan was negotiated, therefore there is no need to make a retrospective assessment of this.

Is study in any course included in the customer's current Job Plan or is there a current exemption from the activity test? See Mutual obligation requirements exemptions.

  • No, go to Step 6
  • Yes, grant the YA/Austudy payment and cancel the Jobseeker Payment from the date the claim was submitted (DOR). There is no gap in entitlement

In Process Direct, update the following fields on the Miscellaneous Circumstance Details (NMIS) screen:

  • Current Circumstances: field key either FTS if no Education Entry Payment (EdEP) is to be paid or FTE if EdEP is to be paid. See Step 2 in Table 6 for further information on assessing entitlement to EdEP
  • Event Date: field key the date the claim was submitted (DOR)

Go to the Date of Commencement (NDC) screen:

  • Manual Start Date: field - key the date the claim was submitted (DOR)
  • Comments: field - add 'Eligible to transfer due to study in Job Plan or exemption'

Continue to process the claim. Make sure the following are the same as the Manual Start Date:

  • Participation Status Event Date (PSED) on the Education Details (EDC) screen, and
  • Start Date on the Newstart Education Status (NES) screen

When finalising the claim, the customer's JSP will cancel (JSP/CAN-STU-CUS COMMENCE/RESUME STUDIES *AUTO) from the claim DOR.

The JSP cancellation may lead to a debt, which may be offset by arrears for the YAL/Austudy payment.

See Step 1 in Table 6.

Table 6

Step

Action

1

Assess entitlement to the Long-Term Income Support (LTIS) rate + Read more ...

Customers who transfer from an income support payment to YA or Austudy may be entitled to the LTIS rate for YA or Austudy.

To determine eligibility for this rate and the process to code a rate override (if required), see:

  • Assessing entitlement to the Long Term Income Support rate of Austudy for students or migrants studying English language courses, or
  • Assessing entitlement to Youth Allowance (YA) special rate for Long Term Income Support Student or migrants studying English language courses

Note: there are 2 known circumstances where the system may not correctly assess LTIS entitlement. Coding of the Rate Component Override (RCO) screen is required to ensure payment correctness.

  • Customer is returning to a course started/studied in the past. The first time the customer attempted the course they were receiving an eligible payment for 26 out of 39 weeks immediately before the student start date and were therefore entitled to the LTIS rate. If they have not been receiving eligible payments for 26 out for 39 weeks immediately before the return to study date, the system will not automatically assess entitlement to the LTIS rate
  • Customer is returning to a course they started in the past. The first time the customer attempted the course they were not receiving an eligible payment for 26 out of 39 weeks immediately before the student start date and were therefore not entitled to the LTIS rate. If the customer has now been receiving eligible payments for 26 out for 39 weeks immediately before their return to study date, the system will incorrectly grant them an entitlement to LTIS rate

Once LTIS is assessed, go to Step 2.

2

Assess entitlement to the Education Entry Payment (EdEP) + Read more ...

Determine if the customer is entitled to EdEP.

EdEP has a similar rule to the LTIS rate. The 52 weeks is immediately before the customer 'first' commences the course. If the customer:

  • has not yet started their studies, go to Step 8
  • is transferring from a payment other than JobSeeker Payment (JSP), select Assess after all the relevant screens have been updated and view the results on the Entitlements (ELD) screen to see if the system will auto pay EdEP. If not, finalise the claim. Go to Step 8
  • has been receiving JSP, and their study has already started, update the Miscellaneous Circumstance Details (NMIS) screen with:
    • Current Circumstances: as 'Full-time Student (plus EdEP)' (FTE) within claim
    • Event Date: as the date the customer ceased to qualify for JSP. Go to Step 3

  • the Payment Type: as Education Entry Payment (EDP)

Outstanding compliance action + Read more ...

Customers receiving the following participation payments are not granted any exemption from their mutual obligation requirements while their claim for YA or Austudy is being assessed:

  • JobSeeker Payment (JSP)
  • Youth Allowance (YA (Job seeker))
  • Parenting Payment (PP) with mutual obligation requirements, or
  • Special Benefit (SpB) with mutual obligation requirements

If the customer has failed to comply with any mutual obligation requirements before the YA/Austudy claim is finalised, compliance action may have been generated or applied in the period since the new claim was submitted.

Outstanding compliance action, such a participation suspension, should not prevent finalisation of the YA/Austudy claim.

After the YA/Austudy claim has been finalised and payment granted, background processing will finalise any outstanding compliance action that is no longer required.

Where payment suspension for a participation reason is before the start date of the YA/Austudy claim, the customer may need to contact either their provider or the Participation Solution Team (PST) as previously notified. Further action by staff in Student Processing is not required. For more information, see Services Australia's responsibilities for managing compliance with compulsory requirements.

Go to Step 4.
