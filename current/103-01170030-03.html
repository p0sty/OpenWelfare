Preparing for Initial Assessment interview

1

Is the customer eligible for DEY or LTWPR Income Management? + Read more ...

Customers may be identified for Disengaged Youth or Long Term Welfare Payment Recipients Income Management because:

  • Disengaged Youth: customer has been in receipt of a category E welfare payment for at least 13 weeks during the past 26 week period
  • Long Term Welfare Payment Recipient: customer has been in receipt of a category E welfare payment for at least 52 weeks during the past 104 week period

For more information, see Compulsory Income Management (CIM) for Disengaged Youth (DEY) and Long Term Welfare Payment Recipients (LTWPR) measures.

When a customer is identified for Income Management:

  • they are advised in writing to contact Services Australia to arrange an Initial Assessment interview within the Income Management engagement period, and
  • Work Item 'I053Q1 Income Management Assessment is required' will be displayed on the Income Management Summary screen and Issues page

Service Officers not trained in Income Management must cold transfer customer to the Income Management line, Indigenous Services line (if appropriate), or Multilingual Call (if appropriate). See the Resources page for a link to the National Transfer numbers.

Service Officers not trained in Income Management + Read more ...

Initial Assessment interviews and associated processes must only be conducted by Service Officers trained in Income Management.

Is the Service Officer Income Management trained?

  • Yes, go to Step 3
  • No, follow this process, and if needed:
    • seek help from a specialised Service Officer (if available) or a Service Support Officer, or

3

Has an Invitation to contact letter been issued? + Read more

When a customer has a payment nominee arrangement, the system does not automatically issue an invitation to contact letter. The Income Management Policy helpdesk staff have the appropriate ISP resource to manually generate the letter.

On the Income Management Summary screen, confirm the Customer is in Service Profiles: block displays and the customer is profiled for 'Long Term Welfare Payment Recipient Income Management' or 'Disengaged Youth Income Management'.

  • Check the Nominee Link Summary (NOLS) screen to identify whether the customer has a current payment nominee arrangement
  • Check the Letter History Summary (HS) screen to identify whether an INMIMIV1 letter been issued in the last 56 days

Does the customer have a payment nominee and an invitation to contact letter has not been issued?

  • Yes, and the customer requests to attend their interview at a later date:
    • Send the Customer Reference Number (CRN) via email to the Income Management Policy helpdesk requesting an invitation to contact letter be issued to the customer
    • Book an Income Management Initial Assessment interview for and tell the customer of the date, time and location of their appointment
    • Tell the customer if they do not attend their interview, their payments will be income managed
    • Record details in a DOC
    • Procedure ends here
  • Yes, and the customer wants to complete their Initial Assessment interview now:
    • Contact the Income Management Policy helpdesk and request an invitation to contact letter be generated for the customer
    • Exit the customer's record to allow the Income Management helpdesk completing appropriate coding
    • Go to Step 4
  • No, go to Step 4

The Income Management Policy helpdesk will:

  • Launch the Compulsory IM Assessment workflow
  • On the IM Decision screen, select the check box to Send customer initial Income Management letter?

Procedure ends here.

Step

Action

1

Check customer's nominee status + Read more ...

Go to the Nominee Link Summary (NOLS) screen to see if the customer has, or is, a payment nominee (NOP).

Note: correspondence nominee (NOC) arrangements do not exclude a customer from Income Management.

Is there a payment nominee arrangement in place?

  • Yes, go to Step 2
  • No, see Step 1 in Table 3

Review Nominee arrangements + Read more ...

Customers identified for Income Management must have their payment nominee relationship reviewed. For more information, see Nominee arrangements under Income Management.

Service Officers are to review nominee arrangements to confirm the arrangement is appropriate and in the customer's best interests.

Note: having a correspondence nominee (NOC) only does not exclude a customer from Income Management.

Is the payment nominee arrangement to continue?

Note: if the customer is a payment nominee for another person, tell the customer the person for whom they are a payment nominee may now be eligible for Income Management. See Nominee arrangements under Income Management.

  • Yes, go to Step 3
  • No, cancel the nominee arrangement. See Step 1 in Table 3

3

Does the customer have an excluded payment nominee? + Read more

  • Yes, the customer is not eligible for Income Management if they have an excluded payment nominee for Income Management purposes. Go to Step 4
  • No, tell the customer their payment nominee must be present for the Initial Assessment interview as they are responsible for managing the customer's funds. Go to Step 5

4

Customer has an excluded payment nominee + Read more ...

  • Tell the customer their payments will not be income managed due to their payment nominee arrangement. However, if the arrangement changes, they may become eligible for Income Management
  • Create a DOC on the customer's record with the following information:
    • 'Customer has an excluded payment nominee and is not eligible for Income Management'
  • Send the Customer Reference Number (CRN) via email to the Income Management Policy Helpdesk advising the customer is ineligible for Income Management as they have an excluded payment nominee

The Income Management helpdesk will record the customer as ineligible for Income Management.

  • Select Yes for the question, Is the customer ineligible for Compulsory Income Management?
  • Select Excluded Payment Nominee from the dropdown menu
  • Continue workflow
  • On the Expense Management Summary screen, tick off the Initial Assessment Work Item
  • Select Continue then Finalise

Procedure ends here.

5

Is the payment nominee present? + Read more

  • Yes, see Step 1 in Table 3
  • No, book another appointment at a suitable time for the payment nominee. It is mandatory for the payment nominee to attend an Initial Assessment interview. Although it is not compulsory for the customer to attend, it is preferred to make sure they are aware of how Income Management will affect them. Procedure ends here

Conducting the Initial Assessment interview

Table 3: This table has steps to conduct the Initial Assessment interview.

Step

Action

1

Conduct Initial Assessment interview + Read more ...

During the interview, the following must be covered:

  • Explain Income Management and how delivery of their payments will change
  • discuss why Income Management is being put in place:
    • Disengaged Youth: customer has been in receipt of a category E welfare payment for at least 13 weeks during the past 26 week period
    • Long Term Welfare Payment Recipient: customer has been in receipt of a category E welfare payment for at least 52 weeks during the past 104 week period
  • Explain how long Income Management will be applied to their payment
  • Explain what happens if the customer moves out of an Income Management area
  • Discuss how income managed money is accessed. For more information see Coding Income Management expenses
  • Explain what income managed money can be used for, that is, School Meals Program (if applicable), food, rent/housing, utilities, clothing, medical needs. Discuss the priorities applicable to the customer. See Determining a person's priority needs for Income Management
  • Explain what goods cannot be purchased from income managed funds that is, prohibited goods and services - alcohol, home brew concentrate, home brew kits, tobacco products, pornographic material or gambling
  • Explain how money can be allocated to a BasicsCard:
    • Residual Payment method (default), or
    • they choose the Partial Payment method, and
    • immediate transfers (if Income Management funds are available)
  • If applicable, discuss changing existing Centrepay deductions to an income managed expense. Explain the impact of Income Management on any ongoing Centrepay deductions
  • Review and discuss any existing Rent Deduction Scheme payment and whether the customer would like to pay their State Housing Authority (SHA) rent from:
    • their income managed funds
    • their discretionary funds, or
    • a combination of both
  • Explain how money can be accessed for Income Management customers accessing funds while travelling
  • Explain the process for contacting the agency, including self-service, in regards to Income Management and BasicsCard. See Income Management and BasicsCard self service options. Offer to register the customer for self service
  • Explain how common changes in circumstances may affect Income Management and the importance of notifying the agency of any changes in their expenses
  • Advise the impact on expense payments if income support payments are reduced due to earnings from employment or a payment penalty applied, and the priority needs payment order when there are insufficient funds to cover an expense
  • Explain how lump sum payments such as Advances, arrears and Family Tax Benefit (FTB) reconciliation payments are 100% income managed
  • Tell the customer when Income Management ends, all Income Management expenses will stop. They must make arrangements to continue paying these expenses themselves, or contact the agency to discuss alternative payment options (for example, Centrepay)
  • Tell the customer of their right to review Income Management decisions

2

Income Management to Cashless Debit Card transition + Read more ...

Customers must be advised they have the option to permanently transition to the Cashless Debit Card instead of starting IM, if they live in the Northern Territory.

There are key differences between IM and the CDC and how the programs operate.

To explain the differences see the Key comparison document - Income Management including BasicsCard and Cashless Debit Card document in Resources.

Does the customer want to permanently transition to the Cashless Debit Card?

  • Yes, transfer the customer to the CDC Hotline
    • Procedure ends here
  • No, continue with the IM initial interview at Step 3

3

Discuss with customer how Priority Needs will be met + Read more ...

Regular expense allocations will be established to meet their priority needs and those of their family.

Discuss with the customer what ongoing expenses they have (for example, rent, food, utility bills). If the expense meets a priority need, this can be paid from their income managed funds.

For more information, see Determining a person's priority needs for Income Management.

4

Launch the Compulsory IM Assessment workflow + Read more ...

Does the Decision screen display I165Q1 - INMCDI access required to update customer record?

  • Yes:
    • Email the Income Management Helpdesk with the Customer Reference Number (CRN) requesting customer be placed on Manual Income Management
    • The Income Management helpdesk will complete the Compulsory IM Assessment workflow to switch the customer on Income Management, using information recorded from the interview
    • Proceed with Initial Assessment interview, complete the priority needs workflow and set up any expenses, recording all relevant information in a DOC. This will allow the Income Management helpdesk to code any details which could not be coded when the interview was conducted
    • Do not tick off the Initial Assessment Work Item
    • Go to Step 7
  • No, complete the Decision screen:
    • Select Manual for Place customer on Manual or Auto Income Management?
    • If the customer is on auto Income Management, select Yes for Switch customer to Manual Income Management?
    • Select Continue to go to the Income Management Initial Interview screen
    • Go to Step 5

5

Continue workflow: Initial Interview and Interpreter requirements + Read more ...

Interview Details

Select the appropriate channel in the Channel: field. The following fields have default values:

  • Logon ID interviewer: if this is not the Service Officer conducting the interview, change to the correct logon ID
  • Date: displays as today's date. This should only be changed if the Initial Assessment interview was held on a previous date. Note: although the interview may be coded for a previous date, the date of effect will be the EPED following an assessment being completed
  • Channel: field, defaults to CSC if interview conducted in Service Centre or will not display if interview conducted by Smart Centre
  • Location: field will default to CSC if interview conducted in a service centre

Interpreter Requirements

Details of interpreter requirements must be recorded for interview purposes.

If no information is recorded on the Other Contact Details (OCD) screen, the Service Officer must exit the assessment workflow and update the OCD screen before proceeding with the interview. See Booking an interpreter for an appointment.

Customer's spoken Language: This will default to the interpreter language recorded on the OCD screen.

  • Interpreter Required: This will default to:
    • Yes if the OCD screen indicates an interpreter is needed
    • No if the interpreter language is recorded as English, and cannot be changed. If an interpreter is needed for the interview, the Service Officer must exit the workflow and update the OCD screen as appropriate

Is an interpreter being used?

This will only present if the customer's language is not English. Select Yes, No, Not Available or Not Required from the dropdown menu as appropriate.

Reason: if an interpreter is Not available, Not being used or Not required, the reason the interview continued is to be selected from the applicable drop down menu:

  • Yes: no other information is needed, select Continue and go to Step 6
  • No: select the applicable reason from the dropdown menu:
    • Appointment not booked
    • Conflict of interest
    • Customer declined an interpreter
    • Customer wishes to use agent or family member
    • Deaf or hearing impaired
    • Services Australia does not have an interpreter who speaks the customer's language
  • Not Available: select the applicable reason from the dropdown menu:
    • Interpreter did not attend interview
    • Interpreter service for that language no available
    • Same sex interpreter not available
  • Not Required: select the applicable reason from the dropdown menu:
    • No contact needed
    • Speaks and understands English
  • More information: If an interpreter is Not available, Not being used or Not required, additional information can be recorded in addition to the standard reasons selected above. For example, if the 'Customer wishes to use agent or family member' is selected, the agent or family member's details can be recorded in this field

It is not recommended the interview proceed without an interpreter.

Confirm the customer has been read the Privacy Notice and the Third Party Disclosure Information by selecting the Yes radio button. This must be read and understood by the customer before proceeding.

Select Continue.

6

Continue workflow: Expense Management Summary screen + Read more ...

This page is a summary of the customer's payments and deductions and used to:

  • start new Income Management expenses (regular or one-off)
  • vary or stop existing Income Management expenses
  • vary or stop existing Centrepay (voluntary) deductions
  • access the Priority Needs Expenses screen

7

Centrepay and Rent Deduction Scheme (RDS) + Read more ...

Does the customer have any Centrepay deductions or deductions to a State Housing Authority (SHA)?

Check the Voluntary Deduction for All Benefits on the Expense Management Summary screen to display deductions being paid from the customer's gross discretionary funds.

  • Yes, go to Step 8
  • No, go to Step 9

8

Changing Centrepay / Rent Deduction Scheme payments to Income Management expenses + Read more ...

Centrepay:

Discuss with the customer whether current Centrepay deductions should be:

  • converted to an Income Management expense
  • maintained as they are
  • varied,
  • or stopped

Search the Income Management Third Party Organisation (TPO) using Office Locator to determine the preferred payment methods for the TPO. Check for any payment notes that might impact converting the deduction from Centrepay to Income Management.

To change a Centrepay deduction to be paid in full or partially from income managed funds:

  • obtain any additional information, such as account details, from the Centrepay deduction to be cancelled or varied
  • action Centrepay deduction as appropriate:
    • stop the deduction if it will be paid in full from income managed funds, or
    • vary the deduction as discussed with the customer. For more information see Starting, changing and cancelling a Centrepay deduction at the request of a customer
  • code new Income Management expense

Rent Deduction Scheme:

Discuss with the customer if they would like their rent to continue being paid from discretionary funds, changed to an Income Management expense or a combination of both. For more see Updating an existing Rent Deduction Scheme (RDS) deduction for a customer information who has commenced Income Management.

9

Continue workflow: Code expenses + Read more ...

As per the priority needs discussion with the customer during the interview, set up the regular expenses to be paid from Income Management funds.

If there are offset payments note the Entitlement Period End Date (EPED), as shown on Expense Management Summary screen. Offset payments affect how expenses are coded. The next EPED date will be the start date for each expense allocation.

For new expenses:

  • select Add regular expenses from the Please select the expense you wish to add: dropdown list
  • for details on how to code expenses, see Coding Income Management expenses
  • for details on how to code School Meals Program expenses, see Payment of School Meals Program (SMP) expenses from Income Management funds or Centrepay.

For customers who wish to pay their expenses via BasicsCard and chose Partial Payment method, regular expenses must be set up once the assessment is finalised.

Note: if the customer is being changed from 'Auto' to 'Manual' Income Management, any existing Income Management expenses set up at the time Income Management started can be viewed under the 'Regular Expenses' section. Expenses should be reviewed and updated as appropriate. For details on how to code changes to existing expenses, see Coding Income Management expenses.

When all expenses have been added, select the Priority Needs link.

See Step 1 in Table 4.

Finalising the Initial Assessment interview

Table 4: This table explains the steps for finalising an Initial Assessment interview.

Step

Action

1

Continue workflow: Priority Needs discussion result + Read more

The results of the priority needs discussion must be recorded on the Priority Needs Expenses screen.

If this is the first discussion for a customer starting Income Management, an answer Yes or No must be given for each of the Priority Needs. If priority needs have been recorded previously, two questions will display:

  • 'Is a discussion being held?' Select Yes as a discussion must be recorded as part of an Initial Assessment interview
  • 'Are there changes?' Select Yes or No as appropriate. If the answer is Yes then a change must be made to either the priority expense/s need/s and/or how the expense/s is/are being met

Priority Needs Expense Category

Customer Response: This field records how the customer will or will not meet the priority need. The field appears only after Yes or No is selected against the priority expense type, and appears against each expense type field when selected. When the field appears, a payment method type must be selected.

  • Yes: select the appropriate value
  • No: select the appropriate value:
    • Not applicable to customer
    • Not met

If this is not the first discussion, Service Officers can view historical discussions under the Priority Needs Discussion History block. Past discussions can be selected and viewed, however the details are protected.

Select Continue to be directed back to the Expense Management Summary screen.

2

Finalise workflow: Income Management Summary screen + Read more

If there are Work Items to be actioned, these will be displayed on the Income Management Summary screen in the Existing Work Items section.

If Work Items cannot be actioned within the workflow, investigate and action while the customer is still present, once the workflow has been finalised.

If the customer is not present, the work items should be left for a Service Officer to action at the next customer contact.

For more information on work items, see Disengaged Youth (DEY) and Long Term Welfare Payment Recipients (LTWPR) Income Management Work Items.

Finalise the activity on the Income Management Summary page.

Review the Income Management Summary screen to confirm:

  • Income Management has been correctly applied ('Qualification Details' block), and
  • payments and expenses are correctly reflected in the 'Fortnightly (14 days) balance details' block

Finalise the activity on the Income Management Summary page.

The workflow is completed. Manually record a DOC with the following information:

  • Customer has been advised and understands:
    • the Privacy Notice and Third Party Disclosure
    • they can apply for an exemption from Income Management and the exemption criteria
    • how to request reviews of Income Management decisions
    • if applicable, why they have been transferred to this measure of Income Management and the impacts of transferring between measures
  • Any other information as needed under the guidelines for adding a DOC to a customer's record
  • Any other relevant information, for example, what communication products were given to the customer

After finalising the assessment workflow, Service Officers must update the Office Code (OC) screen to align Service Reason PQN with the customer's correct Office Code. If the Office Code is not updated, all automatically generated Work Items and IM reviews will be assigned to the recorded Smart Centre and display as outstanding activities.

3

Does the customer need a BasicsCard? + Read more

  • Yes, go to Step 4
  • No, go to Step 5

4

Issuing the BasicsCard + Read more

Issue the BasicsCard. See BasicsCard offer and issue of initial BasicsCard.

  • Explain how funds will be transferred to the BasicsCard:
    • the Residual Payment method (default), or
    • they choose the Partial Payment method. Set up any regular expenses to the BasicsCard as needed (see Coding Income Management expenses)

Tell the customer to attend their local Service Centre to be issued with a BasicsCard. If the customer lives in a remote area, check if the customer lives in an area serviced by the Remote Servicing Teams (RST).

If they live in an area serviced by the RST:

  • check Office Locator see the Resources page for a link
  • tell the customer the date and location of the next visit if available

5

Does the customer need any specialist referrals, for example, social worker, Multicultural Service Officer? + Read more ...

  • Yes, refer the customer to appropriate specialist as per current business process. For more information, see Specialist officers and other internal supports for customers. Go to Step 6
  • No, go to Step 6

6

Finalise interview + Read more ...

Give the customer copies of expense allocations and any other relevant information about Income Management.

Tell the customer they should contact the agency if they have any problems with allocations or wish to change them.

If the customer has a suspended Income Management status, the interviewing Service Officer must restore the payment. For more information on restoring payments, see Restoration of payments (CLK).

Service Officers not trained in restoration of suspended payments for Income management must warm transfer the customer to the income Management line, Indigenous Services line or Multicultural Call (if appropriate). See the Resources page for a link to the National Transfer numbers.
