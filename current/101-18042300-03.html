Pre-claim processing

Note: for LIC claims more than 10 months old where error E0031 presents, navigate to the Customer First Tab, Table 5 for processing steps needed. After the claim has been finalised in Customer First/Customer Record, the claim activity in Process Direct will need to be set to ’not effective’.

Step

Action

1

Locate claim + Read more ...

Locate the work item in Process Direct

Select the Work item to go to the Customer and Claim Information table on the Transaction Summary (TS) screen to review and process the customer's claim.

Paper claims

When a Claim for a Health Care card (SS050) is scanned to the customer's record, a Social Application (SOA) shell work item is generated on the customer's record. If the customer has indicated on the paper claim that their partner will require a Health Care Card (HCC) of their own, create a SOA shell on the record by scanning the paper claim to both records. This has no claim information apart from:

  • the Customer Reference Number (CRN)
  • personal detail, and
  • receipt date

Claim information will need to be manually added into the work item using details from the scanned claim and documents.

    • less than the time allowed to respond since the claim lodgement, hold the claim for the appropriate timeframe from the Claim Submission Date on the Status screen. On the Notes screen add or update the Claim Progress note for action taken. Procedure ends here
    • more than the time allowed to respond since claim lodgement, REJ the claim FSD. See Rejecting a Low Income Health Care Card (LIC) claim

Request more documents or information + Read more ...

If more details or clarification of claim or submitted documents are needed to determine the customer's eligibility make genuine attempts to contact the customer.

If the contact was successful record the details of the conversation in the Progress of Claim (POC) Note:

  • if all information to assess the claim has now been provided, go to Step 7
  • if information is still needed and/or it is not appropriate for the customer to provide information or evidence verbally issue a request for information via > Request Documents. This will place the claim on hold. Procedure ends here

Complex Assessment Officer (CAO) referral + Read more ...

Unless it is clear the claim must be rejected because basic eligibility criteria has not been met, claims needing a CAO referral should not be finalised until the assessment has been completed.

If the customer’s financial circumstances are complex, for example, they include a trust or company, a CAO referral is needed to make sure the income and assets tests are met.

  • Select from the bottom right hand corner of the screen
  • Select Referral and complete the referral details

After a referral is made, the claim remains on hold until the assessment has been completed.

5

Determining a new LIC start date (backdating) + Read more ...

To cover medical, pharmaceutical or other related expenses already incurred, the start date of a LIC may be backdated to a particular date of medical service or treatment.

The customer must provide evidence of the medical service or treatment to support the request for backdating the start date with the new claim.

Customers will need to keep records of expenses to claim reimbursement from Medicare after the LIC is granted. The customer can take the receipt/s and concession card to Medicare to request a refund of medical expenses. See Resources for details.

Note: to backdate the start date for a LIC, the Date of Receipt (DOR) for the claim activity must equal the date of medical service or treatment and it must not be more than 40 weeks earlier than today's date.

The start date field on the LIC Start Date (LSD) screen defaults to the DOR. This date may be changed during a new claim activity and can be no earlier than 40 weeks before today's date.

If backdating to an earlier date, refer to the Level 2 Policy Help Desk - Concessions via the Online query form to request a manual override when all evidence has been gathered.

When completing the online query form, make sure the query is for 'Additional Products and Services' and the category is 'Concessions'.

Once the correct start date is known, go to Step 6.

6

  • 'Complete' all related scanned images on the customer's record, see Viewing Centrelink customers' digital images

If the claim was rejected automatically or needs to be rejected manually, see Rejecting a Low Income Health Care Card (LIC) claim.

Income affecting LIC assessment is summarised on the LIC Assessment Summary (LIAS) screen.

Note: when granting a new LIC, check that the EPED for any existing payments has not been altered.

The LIC is automatically issued on the card carrier (SS357). No separate grant letter is sent.
