5

Determining a new LIC start date (backdating) + Read more ...

To cover medical, pharmaceutical or other related expenses already incurred, the start date of a LIC may be backdated to a particular date of medical service or treatment.

The customer must provide evidence of the medical service or treatment to support the request for backdating the start date with the new claim.

Customers will need to keep records of expenses to claim reimbursement from Medicare after the LIC is granted. The customer can take the receipt/s and concession card to Medicare to request a refund of medical expenses. See Resources for details.

Note: to backdate the start date for a LIC, the Date of Receipt (DOR) for the claim activity must equal the date of medical service or treatment and it must not be more than 40 weeks earlier than today's date.

The start date field on the LIC Start Date (LSD) screen defaults to the DOR. This date may be changed during a new claim activity and can be no earlier than 40 weeks before today's date.

If backdating to an earlier date, refer to the Level 2 Policy Help Desk - Concessions via the Online query form to request a manual override when all evidence has been gathered.

When completing the online query form, make sure the query is for 'Additional Products and Services' and the category is 'Concessions'.

Once the correct start date is known, go to Step 6.

  • go to Step 2

2

Back date grant of LIC NCL more than 40 weeks before DOR used for indexing + Read more ...

  • Identify the correct LIC start date by checking the DOR
  • Within the LIC NCL activity complete all necessary coding including the update of all relevant income information provided with the claim
  • Make sure the customer meets eligibility and qualification criteria and the income test for 8 weeks before the intended start date of the LIC claim
  • Record on a DOC:
    • the correct LIC start date
    • the reason/s for backdating, and
    • that eligibility has been confirmed
  • Place the LIC NCL activity on hold and send an online query to Level 2 Policy Helpdesk - Concessions requesting that LIC start date backdating be applied by a manual override
  • Once Level 2 Policy advises the LIC start date backdating via override is in place, continue the LIC claim processing in Customer First
  • Finalise the LIC grant DOC
  • Once the claim has been finalised in Customer First/Customer Record, the claim activity in Process Direct may need to be set to 'not effective'
