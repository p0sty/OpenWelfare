Examples

Example of objection lodged outside the 28/90 day timeframe

  • M and F are assessed for the costs of the child A, who is in the shared care of M and F
  • M notified Child Support on 13 August that the care of A had changed on 1 August. Child Support made a care percentage decision on 26 August, deciding that a change had occurred, that M now had 40% care and F had 60% care
  • F objected to that decision on 11 December, explaining that they were not able to lodge the objection earlier. Child Support considered the objection and decided on 6 February to allow the objection, as M’s care percentage is 36% and F’s care percentage is 64%
  • Child Support then considered if special circumstances prevented F from lodging the objection within 28 days. Child Support is satisfied that special circumstances did exist. Child Support then considered if the discretion to extend the period should be exercised. Child Support is satisfied that F did not rest on their rights and that M would not be prejudiced by a decision to extend the period in which to lodge the objection. Child Support makes a determination under s87AA(2) that the objection was lodged within time
  • The assessment is amended from 1 August to use the objection decision percentages (M 36% and F 64%) to replace the original care percentage decision

If special circumstances exist, but it is not appropriate to extend the lodgement period to the date that the objection was lodged, do not exercise the discretion to extend the lodgement period. Process the decision as if there are no special circumstances. Go to Step 4.

Advise both customers they have appeal rights to the AAT on the objection decision and on the determination regarding the date of effect under s87AA(2).

The customer may lodge an application to have the objection decision, the date of effect or both components of the objection reviewed by AAT.

If further assistance is required to determine whether special circumstances exist, contact a Service Support Officer (SSO).

Update the special circumstances in the Objection Maintenance window and record the outcome.

Note: if the applicant has applied for special circumstances and the date of effect needs to be determined for the objection decision, consideration of special circumstances must be finalised in time for the objection to be processed within the legislated timeframes.

Care suspension periods and objections examples

Table 2: This table provides examples of objections lodged outside of 28 days (without special circumstances) when the assessment was suspended.

Item

Examples

1

Original care determination suspended the assessment - objection lodged outside of 28 days without special circumstances

Fred and Sue have 1 child called Sam who is in Fred’s 100% care. Sue advises Child Support on 20 October 2018 that Sam is now in Sue’s 100% care from 10 September 2018.

A care decision is made, to reflect Sam is in Sue’s 100% care from 20 September 2018, the assessment is suspended from date of event to date of notification.

Fred lodges an objection to the care decision on 1 January 2019 to the care decision. The original care decision is reviewed and an objection decision is made. The decision is that from 10 September 2018 Sue’s care is 0% and Fred’s care is 100%.

The new care determination applies from 1 January 2019, the date the objection was received.

Care percentage used in the assessment:

  • Before the care change, Fred has 100% care and Sue has 0% care
  • From 10 September, Fred has 0% care and Sue has 0% care
  • From 20 October, Fred has 0% and Sue has 100% care
  • From 1 January, Fred has 100% care and Sue has 0% care

2

Original care determination did not suspend the assessment - objection lodged outside of 28 days without special circumstances

Tim and Sally have 1 child called Jack who is in Tim's 100% care. On 20 October 2018, Sally advises the gaining of 100% care of Jack from 10 September. Child Support investigate the care and determine no change in care has occurred. On 1 January 2019 Sally lodges an objection. The original care decision is reviewed and an objection decision is made. The decision is that from 10 September 2018, Sally has 100% care of Jack. The new care determination applies from 1 January 2019, the date the objection was received. A suspension will not be applied as there is no period of time when all parents had less than 35% care in the assessment

Care percentage used in the assessment:

  • Before care change, Tim has 100% care and Sally has 0% care
  • 20 October (no change determination), Tim has 100% and Sally has 0% care
  • 1 January (date of objection), Tim has 0% care and Sally has 100% care

Care terminating events and objections example

Table 3: This table describes examples of care terminating events when an objection is lodged.

Item

Examples

1

Original care determination terminated the assessment - objection lodged outside of 28 days without special circumstances

Kevin and Jodie have 1 child called Justin who is in Jodie's 100% care. On 1 August 2018, a care change occurs and Kevin now has 100% care of Justin. Services Australia is not notified of the change in care until 8 March 2019 (more than 26 weeks after the care change occurred).

As Jodie's care reduces below 35% care from 1 August 2018 (date of event) and Kevin's increase percentage of care cannot be used in the assessment until 8 March 2019 (date of notification), a terminating event occurs on 1 August 2018. Neither parent has a care percentage of 35% or more. When Services Australia was advised of the change in care on 8 March 2019, a new application for child support is required if either parent wants to restart the case.

Jodie lodges an objection on 1 May 2019. The original care decision is reviewed and an objection decision is made. The decision is that from 1 August 2018 Jodie has 100% care of Justin. The new care determination applies from 1 May 2019, the date the objection was received. As the assessment was terminated, Jodie will need to make a new application for child support.

Customer contact

Opportunities for customer contact and information to be discussed

Table 4: This table describes different situations where customer contact may occur and the information to be discussed and recorded at each instance.

<Category title>

<Description>

Personalised Services

If the customer is managed by Personalised Services, contact the Personalised Services Service Officer (PSSO) to consult prior to taking action.

The PSSO will advise of any service restrictions, customer issues or other matters that could impact on communicating with the customer. See Personalised Services Mandate

The PSSO advice must be considered in progressing the objection, but it does not replace the authority or delegation of the Objection Officer.

Initial contact

Make at least 1 outbound contact attempt on all available numbers. Send a pre-call SMS to help contact the customer.

If the 1st contact attempt in unsuccessful, make 2 additional attempts on all numbers, within 2 business days of either receiving the objection or speaking to the applicant at different times of the day, before issuing letters

Document the initial contact using the Objection First Contact macro, Discussions in initial contact may include:

  • asking about their concerns about the original decision and what they hope to achieve by lodging an objection
  • if the discussion gives no reason to believe the original decision may be wrong, advise the customer their objection is likely to be disallowed
  • clarification of information previously discussed with the decision maker or other Service Officer
  • confirmation of their intention to lodge an objection
  • the objections process, including Child Support open exchange of information policy. See Open exchange of information for Child Support customers
  • administrative options available to resolve their concerns
  • advising that they may withdraw their objection at any time before the decision has been finalised
  • they must lodge a separate objection for each case upon which they are objecting to a decision
  • there may also be more than one respondent, for example in a case involving a non-parent carer.
  • a payer may object to a decision to accept an application for assessment on grounds other than disputed parentage. In this case they may apply for Child Support to suspend payments pending the objection decision. See Suspending disbursements of child support and the References page for links to the Child Support Guide

Care objections

Encourage the customer (including non-mutual customers) to lodge their objection with the agency that made the original decision.

Note: if the customer is objecting to the revocation date of the care decision, Child Support must make the objection decision. This includes when Centrelink made the original care percentage decision.

If Centrelink made the original care percentage decision, explain how the decision affects the customer’s child support assessment. For further explanation, refer them to Centrelink after resolving all Child Support related matters.

See Care objections.

Potential FDV content

If there is potential FDV content concerns, encourage the customer to withdraw the objection and resubmit it without the offending content.

See Make decision and finalise Child Support objection.

Offensive content

Seek policy advice through local PSM before deciding whether to treat the objection as improperly made.

If CS Policy deem there is any offensive content:

  • no call attempts are required
  • send a letter to the applicant advising Child Support cannot accept the objection because it contains offensive content

See Make decision and finalise Child Support objection.

Invalid objections

No phone contact is required with the applicant or respondent.

Send a letter to the applicant telling them the objection is invalid and why. This includes where the customer has not responded to an invitation for an EOT.

See Make decision and finalise Child Support objection

Possible withdrawal

Make one outbound contact attempt on all numbers if it is possible the applicant will withdraw their objection. For example, if the applicant indicates they do not want information exchanged.

If contact is not successful, send a Please contact letter.

If the applicant withdraws their objection, explain:

  • it is their choice to withdraw the objection
  • they cannot request an Administrative Appeals Tribunal (AAT) review of a withdrawn objection as no decision has been made
  • the other party may lodge their own objection. If the other parent has already lodged their own objection and does not want to withdraw, the objection will continue
  • there is a cooling off period of 48 hours before finalising the application as 'withdrawn' in Cuba
  • they must contact the Objections Service Officer within 48 hours to change their decision about withdrawing their objection
  • they can object at any time. However, they must apply for an extension of time if they lodge the objection outside the relevant timeframe.
  • the applicant can object to a care percentage decision (including date of event of a care change) at any time. However, if the respondent objects outside the relevant timeframe, changes will only apply from the date they lodged the objection. This can be waived if there were special circumstances that impacted on the respondent's ability to lodge an objection earlier

  • See Make decision and finalise Child Support objection.

Subsequent customer contact

Document any further incoming or outgoing customer contact, including:

  • if there is no reasonable basis to conclude that the original decision may be wrong:
    • manage the customer’s expectations about the objection decision.
      Note: advise the customer that all relevant information will be considered before making the decision
  • the Objections Officer can request additional information to help determine whether an objection can be made, including:
    • reasons the objection was lodged out of time to make a decision on an extension of time or
    • clarification of what decision is being objected against
  • evidence requested to support their objection, including a clear timeframe to provide it
  • possible outcomes
  • the customer must continue to pay the ongoing liability while the objection is in progress
  • minimum collection activity if a debt exists, using the Collection and debt macro

  • Note: make sure the customer is aware that information gathered may not be exchanged with the other party during the objections process. However all relevant information may be exchanged if the matter progresses to the AAT. See Open Exchange of Information for child support customers for further information.

Part 6A objections

For Part 6A objections, use the financial circumstances worksheet to record the financial information. If the applicant cannot provide the information during the initial call, arrange a time to call back.

Note: do not ask for financial information if the customer has recently provided it.

For the applicant

Use the Objection letters macro to generate the following texts:

  • 'acknowledgement of objection' (no extension of time, or
  • 'acknowledgement of objection' (with extension of time)

For the respondent

Use the Objection letters macro to generate the following texts:

  • 'acknowledgement of objection' (no extension of time), or
  • 'acknowledgement of objection' (with extension of time) text:

Paste the 'acknowledgement of objection' respondent text into an MX01 - Unique letter. Attach to the letter:

  • a copy of the objection letter in accordance with open exchange of information
  • any additional relevant information provided with the objection
  • a summary of additional claims or reasons discussed with the applicant, if applicable
  • update the Objection Maintenance window notepad to reflect the letters sent

For all letters sent requesting evidence, specify:

  • what type of evidence is to be provided
  • options for how the evidence can be provided, including online services, and
  • the date the evidence is required

Document any additional information requested and timeframes for returning evidence in a notepad in the Objection Maintenance window with the subject headings:

  • 'Information required from applicant' or
  • 'Information required from respondent'

See Documenting Child Support information in Cuba.

Note: Use Child Support online where possible to ensure communication is received as quickly as possible. See CSAOnline secure message Cuba Process Help.

  • See Gather evidence relating to Child Support objection.

No response or evidence received

If the customer does not provide the requested evidence or response, and the due date has passed, a follow up call is not required.

If the objection is not to proceed, go to Make decision and finalise Child Support objection

Multiple cases

An objection decision can have varying impacts where there are multiple cases. For example, if an objection is about an estimate, revoking an estimate on one case would impact other cases using the same estimate.

Ensure you review any associated cases for potential impacts when actioning an objection.

Further evidence

Outbound phone contact to request further evidence from the applicant or respondent is discretionary.
