This page contains information on how to help Parents who want to claim Parenting Payment (PP).

Select the relevant tab:

  • Self service tab explains how to help a person claim online
  • Assisted tab explains how to check a parents circumstances to ensure a new claim is required, completing an assisted customer claim with a customer/nominee, and lodgement of paper forms

Self service

Claiming PP online

Table 1

Step

Description

1

Check access to online account + Read more ...

Check the customer is registered with an active Centrelink online account.

If they do not have an active account, they must confirm their identity at their local service centre to register for a Centrelink online account.

Advise customers how they can create a myGov account and link it to their Centrelink services. This will also enable them to receive Centrelink mail online through the myGov inbox.

Service centre staff can help customers with their online claim through self-service terminals.

Customers can use the Payment and Service Finder on the Services Australia website to check which payment is more appropriate for them, and to start their claim. The Resources page contains a link.

From 1 July 2018, intent to claim provisions only applies to customers in vulnerable circumstances.

2

Starting the online claim + Read more ...

Customers can access the Payment and Service Finder on the Services Australia website to check if Parenting Payment (PP) is the most appropriate service offer.

Customers must be registered with an active Centrelink online account to start an online claim.

Once registered, to start an online claim for PP, customers must:

  • sign into myGov and access their linked Centrelink online account
  • select Payments and Claims > Claims >Make a Claim menu option
  • on the Make a claim page, select the Get started button under the Families category
  • on the What would you like to do? window, select Apply for Parenting Payment
  • Eligibility check for Parenting Payment question set will commence. The customer must answer the streaming questions to check they are claiming the most appropriate payment based on their circumstances. This question set is also used to determine whether the customer is eligible for a reduced question set claim
  • after selecting Continue, the customer will navigate to the Claim navigation page to commence their claim
  • The Claim navigation page will display sections of the claim required to be completed:
    • Your personal details
    • Your circumstances
    • Your financial details
    • Review and confirm
    • Next Steps

3

Electronic messaging + Read more ...

Customers who provide a mobile phone number or email address:

  • are advised they are automatically subscribed to Electronic Messaging (EM) services, unless they have previously declined EM
  • are asked to nominate a preferred contact method (SMS or email) for electronic messages
  • are automatically subscribed to receive their Centrelink mail online via their myGov Inbox
  • can opt out of these services at any time

4

To view or update a started claim online + Read more ...

To access the online claim, customers must:

  • sign into myGov and access their linked Centrelink online account
  • select Menu (top left corner of screen) > Payments and Claims > Claims > My online claims. A list of all online claims started by the customer will display

If the status of the claim is incomplete, the customer can choose to either:

  • Continue claim to display the next question set for completion, or
  • Cancel claim. Page will display to confirm customer wants to do this. Once cancelled, the online claim cannot be viewed or continued. No claim information is recorded. The customer can start a new online claim at any time

5

Claim progress + Read more ...

The Claim navigation page displays the sections of the claim that must be completed. The sections must be completed in order and the next section cannot be started until the previous section is complete.

The sections are accessed by selecting the Get started button.

Each section contains modules with relevant questions. On each question page, the customer can select Previous, to go back to the previous question. When each module is completed and the end of the section is reached, the customer is returned to the Claim navigation page to select the next section.

Customers can click on the face of Digital Assistant Oliver for help with the questions on the current page. Help will open in a separate window and may be printed.

As a section is completed the customer has the option to return to a previously completed section to change their answers by selecting the Edit option. They can then navigate directly to the module they wish to update by selecting the relevant tab.

Customers can navigate between pages in the online claim. If a question is answered incorrectly, a red cross will appear in the dots on the right side of the page. Customers can select the red cross and it will take them directly to the page containing the error.

The claim does not have to be completed in 1 session. Customers can leave the Claim navigation page at any time and responses will be saved. The claim can be continued later.

6

Your personal details + Read more ...

Question sets include:

  • Relationship details
  • Contact Requirements - Aboriginal, Torres Strait Islander, Australian South Sea Islander, Interpreter, Permit to Enquire, Nominee
  • Australian Residence
  • Accommodation
  • Child details

7

Your circumstances + Read more ...

Question sets will depend on the customer's circumstances and the payment they are claiming. For example:

  • Circumstances
  • Your previous employer details
  • Your Leave and Redundancy payments
  • Additional circumstances

8

Your financial details + Read more ...

This section allows the claimant to supply or confirm information about their financial income and assets before submitting their claim. The module includes questions about:

  • payment instructions
  • savings
  • investments
  • business, trust and companies
  • other assets
  • income
  • additional circumstances

Provide details later

If the customer cannot provide all their income and asset information, they can provide this information on a paper form. This is advised at Next Steps.

9

Review and Confirm claim + Read more ...

The Confirm Details page contains a summary of all information provided by the customer so they can check what they have entered is correct. The information displays in sections, for example, About your situation.

A summary of My profile information also displays. Customers must ensure all information is correct.

To correct online claim or My profile information, the customer can select the Update button relevant to the answers requiring change. This will take them back to My profile or the relevant section of the claim to review/update the particular claim questions and responses.

Declaration

Once all information is correct, the customer must accept the 'Declaration' and the Acknowledgement by ticking the box. This means a signed claim form is not required.

10

Uploading documents for online claims + Read more ...

Customers can access their started online claim using their Centrelink online account or the Centrelink Express Plus mobile app to upload documents required to submit their claim.

On the Next Steps section, in the Claim Navigation page, click the Upload documents button.

If necessary, Service Officers can scan the documents and submit the claim for the customer:

  • scan the documents
  • run the Request & Manage Customer Tasks guided procedure to submit the claim

If the customer uploaded or provided documents before starting the online claim or Assisted Customer Claim (ACC), the document displays as Required on the Next Steps section. The customer can either:

  • re-upload the document on the Next Steps section from the Documents and Forms section, or
  • contact by phone or in person to have the Request & Manage Customer Tasks guided procedure run to change the document status from Required to Provided

11

To submit claim + Read more ...

Customers must:

  • answer all mandatory questions
  • provide all their required documents
  • verify their identify (when no Identity Confirmation Indicator (ICI) recorded)

Claims not submitted within 13 weeks will expire.

The claim cannot be submitted if the Required tasks are not complete. Once the relevant tasks are completed, the button will change to Submit so the customer can submit the claim.

Exceptions will be made for some vulnerable customers.

Answer eligibility check questions + Read more ...

Read the Privacy message in full to the customer. The customer must agree to the privacy agreement prior to commencing the claim.

In the streaming questions:

  • Indicate the customer's relationship status
  • Indicate if customer is a principal carer
  • Indicate if the customer is in crisis (also to be used when customer transferring from DSP following a DSP Medical Risk Based Review). This will allow the claim to be submitted prior to providing any required documents, see Circumstance Change Monitor (CCM)

Your circumstances + Read more ...

Questions will request the customer to provide information on their previous circumstances prior to submitting the claim for payment. Circumstances the customers may select include:

  • wage/salary earner (full time, part time or casual)
  • seasonal, contract or intermittent wage/salary earner
  • self employed
  • self-employed and wage/salary earner
  • student - full time education
  • student - part time education
  • student receiving ABSTUDY
  • apprentice or trainee
  • Community Development Employment Project
  • caring
  • in psychiatric confinement (14 days or more)
  • in gaol (14 days or more)
  • unemployed
  • other

Based on the selection, further questions are on the following pages:

  • Previous Employer details
  • Leave and Redundancy Payments
  • Seasonal Contract or Intermittent work details

If customer selects any of the work related circumstances:

  • date last worked must be provided
  • if still employed, and
  • details of the former employment such as the employer's name and contact details

Action to transfer following a Disability Support Pension (DSP) Medical Risk Based Review

Table 3

Step

Action

1

Action required after DSP cancellation decision + Read more ...

  • Future Activity List (FAL) activity matures and is allocated via WLM. See MRBR Decision - DSP SOI DOC for DSP cancellation details

Has the customer applied for a formal review of the DSP cancellation?

  • Yes - the customer is not to be contacted when the FAL activity matures:
    • complete the PP Review (PP/REV) activity. The Service Officer must annotate the MRBR Decision - DSP SOI DOC with DSP-CUR/PPR Alt payment unable to be assessed until the formal review of decision outcome is known
    • Procedure ends here
  • No - the Service Officer must make 2 genuine attempts to contact the customer. Clearly document any contact attempts on the customer's record

If contact with the customer is unsuccessful, go to Step 2.

If contact with the customer is successful, go to Step 3.

2

Contact with customer is unsuccessful + Read more ...

  • Complete the PP/REV activity
  • Annotate the MRBR Decision - DSP SOI DOC with contact attempts. No further action is required in relation to the staff-assisted claim
  • Procedure ends here

3

Contact with customer is successful + Read more ...

If the customer applies for a formal review of decision at this contact, do not complete the steps below, go to step 4.

If the customer does not apply for a formal review of the decision, the Service Officer is to complete the following:

  • Run Assisted Customer Claim (ACC) as outlined in the Assisted Customer Claim (ACC) table. Note: the PP new claim must be submitted using the In Crisis exemption to bypass the Circumstance Change Monitor (CCM) (where required)
  • Check the customer record to determine whether documents listed at Next Steps have previously been provided or are not genuinely required:
    • If document/s previously provided or not required, ensure the Provided checkbox is selected
    • Notify the customer of any outstanding tasks before submitting the claim
  • Where outbound contact has been made:
    • The claim is to be held until end of DSP POG (day 43). Note: where further documents are required for the PP new claim, advise the customer to provide these documents as soon as possible to prevent claim rejection and/or any delay in payments
    • Add the keyword DRBRTRF to the PP new claim activity
    • Complete the PP/REV activity
    • Procedure ends here. When Service Officers are allocated the PP new claim work item post DSP cancellation, go to Step 5
  • Where the customer has contacted inbound to request ACC:
    • Where no additional documents are required, hold the claim until the end of the POG (day 43)
    • Where additional documents are required, the customer must be provided with 14 days to return the supporting documents. Hold the claim for 15 days or to the expiration of the POG, whichever is greater. Note: where further documents are required for the PP new claim advise the customer to provide these documents as soon as possible to prevent claim rejection and/or any delay in payments
    • Add the keyword DRBRTRF to the PP new claim activity
    • Procedure ends here. When Service Officers are allocated the PP new claim work item post DSP cancellation, go to Step 5

4

If the customer applies for a formal review of the DSP cancellation decision at this contact + Read more ...

  • ACC must not be run. The Service Officer must:
    • Run the Internal review/Explanation script, see Step 7
    • Complete the PP/REV activity
    • Annotate the MRBR Decision - DSP SOI DOC on the customer's record with: DSP-CUR/PPR Alt payment unable to be assessed until the formal review of decision outcome is known
    • Tell the customer DSP will be paid until the outcome of the review is known and that an Authorised Review Officer (ARO) will contact them about the decision
  • Procedure ends here

5

Action required post DSP cancellation + Read more ...

  • Service Officer will be allocated the PP new claim work item via WLM. See MRBR Decision - DSP SOI DOC for DSP cancellation details
  • Make 1 attempt to contact the customer to conduct the PP claim SOI (before finalising the new claim) to advise customer:
    • Proposed outcome of claim (grant/reject/reject 'FSD' Failed to supply documents)
    • New payment details, for example, reporting requirements, next/future payments and mutual obligations requirements (where applicable). Note: advise the customer the claim may be subject to a quality check (QMA) and this may delay outcome letter
    • If customer applies for a formal review of the decision at this contact, go to Step 6
  • Finalise claim
  • Procedure ends here

6

Customer applies for a formal review of the decision at post DSP cancellation contact + Read more ...

Run the Internal review/Explanation script, see Step 7:

  • PP new claim is to be rejected 'CLR' (Client request - claim withdrawn/voluntary surrender) and annotate PP rejection DOC with DSP-CUR/PPR Alt payment unable to be assessed until the formal review of decision outcome is known
  • Tell the customer DSP will be restored until the outcome of the review is known and that an ARO will contact them about the decision
  • Procedure ends here

7

Application for a formal review of the DSP cancellation decision + Read more ...

If the customer applies for a formal review of decision during alternate income support payment contact, the Service Officer must run Internal review/Explanation script making sure they select the Special Case Identifier of the DSP Medical Risk Based Review.

Note: updating the PPR: field in the APL system is a mandatory step. When running the script ensure the PPR: field is always updated.

To apply Payment Pending Review (PPR) for these cases, the PPR: field in the APL system must be updated with the code MED for 'Medical Eligibility'. This field is also available within the Appeal (APL) system on the following screens:

  • ODM reconsideration (ODM)
  • Modify reconsideration (MR)
  • Add ARO Case (ARO)
  • Add AAT1 Case (ASC)
  • Add AAT2 Case (AAC)

The Internal Review/Explanation script must be used to record this information.

When the PPR: field has been coded in the APL system, a PEN/PPR activity will be created. This activity will be sent to Medical Risk Based Review processing Services Officers to complete PPR coding.
