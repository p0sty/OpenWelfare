Assessing ABSTUDY independence through workforce participation

Table 1.

Step

Action

1

Full-time employment + Read more ...

Has the student or Australian Apprentice been employed full-time (an average of 30 hours per week) for at least 18 months (including the COVID-19 concession period where applicable) during any 2 year period?

  • Yes, see Assessing independence when a customer is self-supporting through paid employment
  • No, note any periods they were employed full-time in the last 4 years. For what can be included as full-time employment, see Item 1 on the Resources page. Go to Step 2

2

Australian Apprentice + Read more ...

Is the customer an Australian Apprentice?

  • Yes, go to Step 4
  • No, go to Step 3

3

Self-supporting through paid employment from a regional or remote area + Read more ...

A student may qualify for independence if since leaving secondary school they have:

  • been employed, and, within a 14 month period (including the COVID-19 concession period) have total earnings of at least 75% of Wage Level A of the National Training Wage Schedule included in a modern award, or
  • had part-time employment for at least 15 hours per week for at least 2 years (including the COVID-19 concession period where applicable)

Does the student meet one of the above?

  • Yes, the student may qualify for independence under the regional or remote area self-supporting criterion
  • No, go to Step 4

4

Other Government income/registration as a job seeker + Read more ...

Check if the student or Australian Apprentice was:

  • receiving income from a government authority (other than a Prescribed Education Payment), and/or
  • registered as a full-time job seeker with Services Australia for at least 3 of the last 4 years (156 of the last 208 weeks)

For what defines receiving income from a government authority, see Item 2 on the Resources page.

Has the student or Australian Apprentice met one or both of the above?

  • Yes, go to Step 10
  • No, note any periods they were receiving income from a government authority and/or as a full-time jobseeker in the last 4 years. Go to Step 5

5

Illness + Read more ...

Check if the student or Australian Apprentice has been unable to work due to illness for at least 3 of the last 4 years (156 of the last 208 weeks). For what defines unable to work due to illness, see Item 3 on the Resources page.

Have they been unable to work due to illness for at least 3 of the last 4 years (156 of the last 208 weeks)?

  • Yes, go to Step 10
  • No, note any periods they were unable to work due to illness in the last 4 years. Go to Step 6

6

Lawful custody + Read more ...

Check if the student or Australian Apprentice has been in lawful custody for at least 3 of the last 4 years (156 of the last 208 weeks). For information on what defines lawful custody, see Item 4 on the Resources page.

Has the student or Australian Apprentice been in lawful custody for at least 3 of the last 4 years (156 of the last 208 weeks)?

  • Yes, go to Step 10
  • No, note any periods they were in lawful custody in the last 4 years, go to Step 7

7

Religious order + Read more ...

Check if the student or Australian Apprentice has been supported by a religious order for at least 3 of the last 4 years (156 of the last 208 weeks). For what defines being supported by a religious order, see Item 5 on the Resources page.

Have they been supported by a religious order for at least 3 of the last 4 years (156 of the last 208 weeks)?

  • Yes, go to Step 10
  • No, note any periods they were supported by a religious order in the last 4 years. Go to Step 8

8

Total accumulated weeks + Read more ...

Add the number of weeks the student or Australian Apprentice has accumulated in each of the categories in Step 1 and Steps 4 to 7.

Does this add up to a total 156 out of the last 208 weeks (including the COVID-19 concession period where applicable)?

  • Yes, go to Step 10
  • No, go to Step 9

9

Independence status not met + Read more ...

The student or Australian Apprentice is not eligible for grant of independence status for ABSTUDY under the workforce participation category.

  • Consider whether they are independent on other grounds. See Independence for ABSTUDY customers
  • If appropriate, refer them for other assistance, e.g. social worker or Indigenous Service Officer

Go to Step 11.

10

Supporting documentation + Read more ...

The student or Australian Apprentice must provide evidence to support their claim for independence where there is no evidence on their record.

Evidence may include:

  • a statement from an employer certifying periods of full-time employment
  • statements of earnings showing the periods of employment and indicating whether the employment was on a full-time basis
  • a statement from an accountant or taxation agent indicating periods in full-time self-employment, or as a sporting professional
  • a statement from a religious order detailing the periods they were supported by the order
  • a statement from correctional authorities detailing periods spent in lawful custody
  • medical evidence supporting illness or incapacity preventing employment

Note: customers do not require evidence to have the COVID-19 concession period applied to their claim. Periods outside the COVID-19 concession period still require sufficient evidence to confirm the customer meets independence and retain the same evidence requirements.

11

Procedure when customer contacts + Read more ...

If the student or Australian Apprentice has contacted through:

  • a service centre, go to Step 12
  • Smart Centre Call, go to Step 13
  • the ABSTUDY Smart Centre Processing Services teams, see Step 1 in Table 2

12

Scanning supporting documentation + Read more ...

Scan the claim/form and all supporting documents to the student or Australian Apprentice's record to allow for processing by the ABSTUDY Smart Centre Processing Services team.

Procedure ends here.

13

Record details of the discussion + Read more ...

Record details of the discussion in a DOC.

Procedure ends here.

Coding ABSTUDY independence through workforce participation

Table 2

Step

Action

1

Assessing eligibility + Read more ...

Check the evidence provided to determine if the student or Australian Apprentice meets eligibility for independence through workforce participation.

Is there enough evidence to support granting independence through workforce participation?

  • Yes, if processed in:
    • Customer First, go to Step 6
    • Process Direct, go to Step 2
  • No, if processed in:
    • Customer First, go to Step 7
    • Process Direct, go to Step 5

2

Coding independence details in Process Direct + Read more ...

The independence data will pre-fill the Independent/Homeless/Away from Home Details (NIH) screen from information in the claim.

New Claims in Process Direct:

  • In the Super Key: field, Independent/Homeless/Away From Home Details (NIH)
  • Action field, code 'IND'
  • Start Date: field, code the commencement date for the independent status
  • Start/Reject Reason: code one of the grant reasons as follows:
    • PFA - be a ABSTUDY student/Australian Apprentice who has participated in the workforce for 3 out of 4 years
    • PFC - be a ABSTUDY student/Australian Apprentice who has participated in the workforce for 3 out of 4 years including the COVID-19 concession period
  • Start Date: code the later of:
    • ABSTUDY start date
    • the date the student or Australian Apprentice became independent

3

Australian Apprentice + Read more ...

Is the customer an Australian Apprentice?

  • Yes, go to Step 9
  • No, go to Step 4

4

Relocation scholarship eligibility + Read more ...

For qualifying students, the Regional Location Details (RLD) screen prepopulates information provided in the online claim. Check the information and dates on the RLD screen are correct.

For information on coding the RLD screen, see Relocation Scholarship (RS) payment and coding.

Go to Step 9.

5

Correcting independence details in Process Direct + Read more ...

If the student or Australian Apprentice does not meet eligibility for independence through workforce participation, correct the pre-filled information on the Independent/Homeless/Away from Home Details (NIH) screen.

Go to NIH screen via Super Key or by selecting My Circs icon, correct these fields:

  • Start/Reject Reason: code:
    • RFT - be a ABSTUDY student/Australian Apprentice who has participated in the workforce for 3 out of 4 years (ABY only) - not supported
    • RFC - be a ABSTUDY student/Australian Apprentice who has participated in the workforce for 3 out of 4 years including the COVID-19 concession period - not supported
  • Start Date: code the later of:
    • ABSTUDY rejection date
    • the date they did not meet independence

Go to Step 9.

6

Coding independence details in Customer First + Read more ...

Student or Australian Apprentice meets eligibility for independence through workforce participation.

In Customer First

  • Complete the following fields on the Independent/Homeless/Away from Home Details (NIH) screen via Next: field
  • Action: code 'IND'
  • Start/Reject Reason: code:
    • PFA - be a ABSTUDY student/Australian Apprentice who has participated in the workforce for 3 out of 4 years
    • PFC - be a ABSTUDY student/Australian Apprentice who has participated in the workforce for 3 out of 4 years including the COVID-19 concession period'
  • Start Date: code the later of:
    • ABSTUDY start date
    • date the student or Australian Apprentice became independent
  • Complete the Source and DOR field details as appropriate
  • Press [Enter] to update NIH details
  • Complete the assessment on Assessment Results (AR):

If the student is studying an approved scholarship course, code 'Y' in the To update regional classification details, go to RLD: field.

For more information on coding the Regional Location Details (RLD) screen, see Relocation Scholarship (RS) payment and coding.

Go to Step 8.

7

Coding rejection of independence in Customer First + Read more ...

Student or Australian Apprentice does not meet independence through workforce participation.

Complete these fields on the Independent/Homeless/Away from Home Details (NIH) screen:

  • Action: code 'IND'
  • Start/Reject Reason: code:
    • RFT - be a ABSTUDY student/Australian Apprentice who has participated in the workforce for 3 out of 4 years (ABY only) - not supported
    • RFC - be a ABSTUDY student/Australian Apprentice who has participated in the workforce for 3 out of 4 years including the COVID-19 concession period - not supported
  • Start Date: code the later of:
    • ABSTUDY rejection date
    • date they did not meet independence

8

Record decision in Customer First + Read more ...

Record details of the decision in a DOC. The information must indicate the grounds the claim for independence has been granted or rejected, and if rejected, the reasons for the rejection.

Create an IND Granted/Rejected DOC in Customer First.

Use Fast Note - select Auto Text, use Students, ABSTUDY & Apprentices > Update > IND Granted/Rejected > Select Confirm. Complete the DOC outlining the reasons for Grant/REJ.

Has the claim for independence been granted?

  • Yes, go to Step 10
  • No, procedure ends here

9

Record notes of the decision in Process Direct + Read more ...

Record details of the decision in Notes or Document annotations. The information must indicate the grounds the claim for independence has been granted or rejected, and if rejected, the reasons for the rejection.

Has the claim for independence been granted?

  • Yes, go to Step 10
  • No, procedure ends here

10

Determine if a current Child Support assessment exists + Read more ...

To check if a current Child Support assessment exists for the student or Australian Apprentice, see Maintenance Income Test (MIT) for ABSTUDY and Youth Allowance (YA).

Is there a current Child Support assessment for the student or Australian Apprentice?

  • Yes, go to Step 11
  • No, procedure ends here

11

Terminating event for Child Support send ACTDOC to CSJ + Read more ...

Send an ABSTUDY Child Support Update Fast Note. Select Auto text, use Students, ABSTUDY and Apprentices > Update > ABSTUDY Child Support Update.

Complete the details within the Fast Note and select Confirm, ensuring the Fast Note remains open.
