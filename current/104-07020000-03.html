Emergency responses to customer aggression

Table 2: examples of customer behaviour and suitable staff and site leadership responses for each stage of response.

Description of customer behaviours, staff and site leadership response

Customer attends in breach of MSP + Read more ...

Refer to Managed Service Plan (MSP) - Customer not complying, go to Step 2. Partial face-to-face services, go to Step 3.

De-escalate - face-to-face environment + Read more ...

Examples of customer behaviours include:

  • Raised voices between customers
  • Loud swearing
  • Agitated movements

Staff response

  • Staff who are directly involved should use their skills to calm the customer and defuse the situation

  • Staff who are not directly involved should monitor the incident and be ready for a possible escalation

Disengage - face-to-face environment + Read more ...

Examples of customer behaviours include:

  • Escalating behaviour
  • Intimidation through invasion of personal space
  • Violent gestures or threats of violence

Staff response

  • Get support from site leaders or ART members, either verbally or on Microsoft Teams, if safe to do so
  • Staff not involved in the incident should:
    • monitor the incident
    • tell leadership or ART members, if they not already aware of it
    • be ready for a possible escalation and emergency response
  • Do not continue to serve the customer. Direct them to leave if it is safe to do so. See the Resources page for a link to the Public Order (Protection of Persons and Property) Act 1971 (PoPPA)
  • If the person refuses to leave, tell them that police have been called
  • Move away from the person. Staff should not turn their back on the person, and should try to keep furniture between the person and themselves

See Disengage.

When there is a security guard on site, the security guard’s first response must be to immediately attend the scene of any obvious disturbance.

The guard must stand by, assess the situation and remain ready until one of the following applies:

  • They are asked to intervene by site leaders,
  • The situation clearly requires immediate intervention
  • Site leaders indicate the security guard is no longer needed

4

Duress - face-to-face environment + Read more ...

Examples of customer behaviours include:

  • Sustained loud swearing, verbal abuse, or yelling
  • Intentional aggressive movements
  • Hostile or argumentative situation with staff or customers
  • Refusing to leave the site
  • Self-harming

Staff response

  • Activate the duress alarm and call the police on 000
  • Do not continue to serve the customer
  • Staff and other customers should move away from the incident and be ready to shelter in place or evacuate, depending on the ART’s instructions

ART response

  • Make sure police have been called – call 000. Clearly state the site address, what is happening, and ask for an immediate police presence.
  • Tell the customer to leave under PoPPA. Resources has a link
  • If they leave, consider locking the main entrance to prevent them from re-entering. See Locking the customer entrance in response to a threat or incident
  • If the customer does not leave, consider initiating a Code Grey

5

Code Grey - face-to-face environment + Read more ...

Code Grey is the response to a severe threat where staff and other people are involved in a direct physical threat that could result in injury. These include:

  • When a person has a knife, or weapon (other than a firearm, or flammable substance). This includes if they claim to have one, or are suspected to have one. If it is a firearm or flammable substance, see Code Black
  • A customer is threatening to use a weapon, including an improvised weapon
  • Physical assault such as pushing, fighting, spitting and shoving between customers and staff
  • Violence between customers where the safety of others is compromised
  • Police use of force at the site, including pepper sprays or batons

Staff response

  • Activate the duress alarm if it is safe to do so
  • Staff should move away from the person without turning their back on them. Staff should try to keep furniture between the person and themselves
  • Move to the site's shelter in place location, and tell others to do the same
  • Staff and others should not move from the shelter in place location unless their safety is threatened

ART response

  • Announce a Code Grey
  • Do not gather around or near the customer. Move to the shelter in place location
  • Make sure police have been called. Call 000 and ask for police and other emergency services if required. Wait for them to arrive
  • Make sure all staff are accounted for. Consider contacting staff who are offsite and tell them about the Code Grey
  • Observe the person from a safe location, ideally from the secure back of house (BOH) on CCTV
  • Tell customers, staff and others to stay in the shelter in place until advised
  • If customers refuse to move, staff should not remain with them but must move to the shelter in place location

6

Code Grey - non-customer facing environment + Read more ...

In a non-customer facing environment, a Code Grey is a building or floor lockdown in response to:

  • A violent threat
  • Danger, or
  • An armed person near the building that poses a threat to people inside

Staff response

  • Stay in the work area and wait for further instructions from the ART
  • Do not go near windows or doors, into any lift foyers, or near the entrance
  • Do not leave the building or floor unless told to do so by the ART

ART response

  • Upon identifying or being notified of the incident assess the risk and confirm what response is required
  • Once it is assessed that a Code Grey response is required, make sure that everyone on the site receives the Code Grey instruction. For multi-tenanted sites, contact the Chief Warden who will tell other tenants as necessary
  • Tell staff to shelter in place and stay away from the windows and doors
  • Arrange for the police to be called if this has not already happened
  • Advise Agency Security as soon as it is safe and practicable to do so
  • Update staff (as appropriate) on the status of the incident
  • Continually reassess the risk to ensure that Code Grey remains the appropriate response. For multi-tenanted sites keep in contact with the Chief Warden.

Once the threat has been removed, issue an ‘all clear’ and resume normal site access.

7

Code Black - face-to-face environment + Read more ...

  • Code Black is the response to an extreme threat level that could result in serious injury or death to customers and staff. These can include:
    • hostage scenarios
    • customer with a firearm
    • customer brandishing flammable substance within the site
    • a gas is released within the site

Staff response

  • Activate the duress alarm if it is safe to do so
  • Tell customers to evacuate through the safest exit
  • If it is safe to do so, move directly to the Code Black assembly area
  • If it is not safe to evacuate, find a place to hide out of the aggressor's sight
  • Whenever possible, avoid restricting the option to evacuate
  • If staff choose to hide, they may also need to consider options to disrupt or disable the customer if they are located. Any action should only be taken as a last resort, and to protect the life of the individual or others in the area
  • If staff are unable to evacuate or find a place to hide, comply with the aggressor's instructions. Do only as told and nothing more

ART response

  • Announce a Code Black
  • Once outside, remain vigilant as the aggressor could move to your location. Be aware of the exit points and watch for any threat coming from inside the site
  • Make sure police have been called and wait for them to arrive
  • Make sure all staff and customers are accounted for. Consider contacting staff who are offsite and to tell them about the Code Black
  • Liaise with the police once they arrive
  • Encourage staff and customers to stay in the Code Black assembly area
  • If other customers on the site refuse to move, staff should not remain with them and must move to the Code Black location

For a very serious firearm or active shooter incident, the ART may instruct staff to disperse from the site. If this happens, staff and site leaders should try to contact one another to confirm their location and safety.

Refer to the Resources page for a link to the Emergency Response Procedures – Aggression (ERPAs) including Code Grey and Code Black for a link to the Code Grey and Code contact card.

8

Code Black - non-customer facing environment + Read more ...

In a non-customer facing environment, a Code Black (evacuation) is a response to an extreme threat in the building which could result in serious injury or death, such as a lethal weapon.

Staff response

  • Have an evacuation route in mind. Move away from any sounds of violence and evacuate regardless of whether others agree to follow
  • Look for any threat affecting the nearest evacuation route
  • If it is safe to do so, move away from the building towards the Code Black assembly point.
  • Do not assemble at the fire evacuation assembly points
  • If it is not safe to evacuate (for example, if the offender is close by) find a place to hide that:
    • remains out of the offender's line of sight
    • does not restrict your potential escape route
    • provides cover
  • Note: turn mobile phones to silent and turn off the vibration
  • As a last resort, staff should consider options for arming themselves with improvised weapons for defence in the event they are located by the offender
  • If staff are still in the building when the police arrive, staff should:
    • follow police instructions
    • avoid quick movements or shouting and keep their hands in clear view. This is to help the police distinguish staff from the offender

ART response

  • Upon identifying or being notified of the incident assess the risk and confirm what response is required
  • Once it is assessed that a Code Black response is required, make sure that all people on site receive the Code Black instruction. For multi-tenanted sites, contact the Chief Warden/Emergency Control Organisation as soon as possible so that they can tell other tenants in the building
  • Direct staff to:
    • evacuate the building immediately if it is safe to do so, look for any threat that might affect the nearest evacuation route, and move to the Code Black assembly point
    • if the incident is an active shooter incident, staff should be directed to disperse, not move to the Code Black location
  • ART may need to tell staff to disperse from the Code Black assembly point, such as if an offender follows staff to the Code Black assembly point
  • Make sure that the police have been called
  • Advise Physical Security as soon as it is safe and practicable to do so

Implement a malicious call trace (MCH) + Read more ...

A MCH can be activated in response to:

  • phone threats to harm staff or others (including any other organisation or person outside of the agency)
  • customers talking about suicide or self-harm
  • threats to property owned by Services Australia

To help complete the trace (including if Services Australia Workspace is not available), gather the following information:

  • time and date of call
  • the number the customer dialled
  • the number the customer dialled from, i.e. their home or mobile number

If Services Australia Workspace is available, and it is an:

3

Clarify the customer's statement and threat + Read more ...

Clarify the customer's statement and threat. If the customer withdraws the threat, continue with normal procedures.

If the customer does not withdraw the threat, confirm details including:

  • who the customer is threatening
  • where the customer is calling from
  • who the threat maker is
  • why the customer is making the threat
  • what the customer is intending to do and is there a weapon involved
  • how the customer is intending to carry out the threat
  • when the customer is intending to carry out the threat (date, time)
  • where the customer is intending to carry out the threat

Use the Phone bomb threat checklist in the instance of a bomb threat. A link to the checklist is available on the Emergency Response Procedures - Aggression (ERPAs) including Code Grey and Code Black intranet page. Resources has a link.

5

Contact the customer + Read more ...

Imminent threat with a known target

If the person making the threat is close to the threatened site and it’s likely that they could arrive at the site prior to being contacted by the supervisor, another member of the leadership team should contact the Manager or Team Leader of the threatened site immediately.

The supervisor should immediately contact the customer to clarify the threat and seek a withdrawal.

Imminent threat with an unknown target

Clarify the person’s whereabouts in order to determine how soon the threat may be carried out, and the target of the threat.

If the situation is urgent, actions can be shared amongst the leadership team.

  • If the customer cannot be contacted or refuses to withdraw the threat, go to Step 6
  • If the customer withdraws the threat, go to Step 7

Note: access call recordings if appropriate to verify or clarify the threat. See Call and screen recording - information and access.

6

Call 000 + Read more ...

Call the police immediately on 000 in the following circumstances:

  • contacting the customer is unsuccessful
  • the customer's identity is unknown
  • the customer refuses to withdraw the threat
  • it is decided a call trace is needed

Tell the police the:

  • details of the threat
  • details of the person that made the threat
  • contact details for the manager at the threatened site

The staff member who first received the threat should be available to give police additional details if required.

Make sure the police reference number or details are recorded. Any additional information received from police should be given to the threatened site.

Contact the threatened site to give them the details of the threat and tell them that police have been contacted.

Malicious call trace request and police follow-up

Ask the Police to contact their State Communications Centre to have a request for trace faxed to Telstra's Trace Control Centre

See Resources for the Services Australia Quick Reference Guide.

Agency Security and the team leader or manager should consider:

  • the nature of the threat and location of the threat maker
  • if the customer is known to the site and their circumstances

  • closing the site and/or evacuating the site
  • the appropriateness of deploying a temporary security guard

Making a decision to lock the entrance to a site in response to a threat or incident

Table 5: reasons and considerations for managers and team leaders when making a decision to lock the entrance to a site in response to a threat or incident.

Step

Action

1

Reasons for locking the entrance doors + Read more ...

Reasons for locking the entrance to a site include (but are not limited to):

  • stopping an aggressive customer from entering the site
  • serious incident has occurred and the customer has left the site
  • advanced warning of threat received
  • aggressive customer is loitering outside the site

2

Considerations before taking action to lock the entrance to a site + Read more ...

If the risk to the site or staff is considered high or imminent, the decision to lock down should not be delayed.

When making the decision to lock the entrance to a site:

  • consider if the customer is known to the site and/or has a history of violence
  • consider the immediate location of the customer (if they are close by and are likely to attend)
  • seek advice from a Regional Security Adviser
  • consider if the police know the customer; does the customer own firearms or do the police think the threat is imminent

A site entrance should not be locked if the customer is inside.

In customer facing sites, signage should be displayed at the main entrance advising customers that the site is closed. Service Centre should contact their Operations Management Team for the approved signage.

If a site is closed due to a safety or security incident, normal business continuity and escalation procedures should be followed, in line with the Escalation Policy and using the escalation template on the Escalation Coordination team page. Resources has a link.

Threat to attend a site + Read more ...

A threat to attend a site in breach of isolation requirements should be treated as credible.

Incident - Disengage

Disengage - customer facing environment + Read more ...

Example behaviours. Customer:

  • Escalates their behaviour after refusing to answer screening questions
  • Escalates their behaviour after refusing to check in using the QR code (where required)
  • Deliberately breaches 1.5 metre distancing
  • Escalating behaviour after refusing to wear a mask in a mandated area (with no lawful exemption)
  • Becomes abusive when support with self-service is unavailable

Response:

  • Staff directly involved should seek support from site leadership or ART members, if safe to do so
  • Do not continue to serve the customer
  • Ask the customer to leave
  • Tell the customer if they do not leave, the police will be called
  • ART/Leadership should direct the customer to leave (does not apply if customer outside) using under the Public Order Protection and Property Act 1971 (PoPPA)
  • Uninvolved staff should monitor the incident and be ready for a possible escalation and emergency response

Follow-up actions required:

  • Record an incident using relevant incident details. For example, Abuse - Actual - Verbal. All incidents relating to COVID-19 must include the word 'COVID' within the incident description. See Step 6 in Table 1 of Reporting, recording and escalating incidents of customer aggression
  • An incident can also be recorded if the customer is unknown. See Step 3 in Table 6 of Reporting, recording and escalating incidents of customer aggression
  • Issue a warning letter/SMS. See General correspondence - Customer aggression letters and Centrelink letters online and Electronic Messaging
  • Record an ESSentials incident, if impacted (OH&S - Incident or Injury)

Incident - Code Grey

Code Grey - customer facing environment + Read more ...

There is a direct physical threat that could result in injury.

Example behaviours:

  • A person is spitting at staff, threatening to spit at staff, or infect others with COVID-19
  • A person is in possession of, claims to be (or is suspected to be) in possession of a knife or weapon - not a firearm or flammable substance
  • A person is threatening to use an improvised weapon
  • Physical assault such as pushing, fighting spitting and shoving, including between customers
  • Police use of force on site, including use of pepper spray, baton and other use of force

Staff response

  • Activate the duress alarm, if it is safe to do so
  • Move to the site's shelter in place location, directing others to do the same
  • Staff and others should not move from the shelter in place location, unless their safety is threatened

ART response

  • Initiate a Code Grey through verbal instruction
  • Do not gather around or near the person - move to the shelter in place location
  • ART should assess whether it is safer to evacuate customers who are sheltering in the Code Grey area, rather than remaining in close proximity in the Code Grey area
  • Call police and other emergency services, if required. Wait for emergency services to arrive
  • Make sure all staff are accounted for - consider contacting staff who are offsite and advising them of the Code Grey
  • Observe the person from a safe vantage point - ideally from the secure back of house (BOH) location, via CCTV
  • Upon confirming that the threat is over, the ART should advise the site to stand down. For example, announce 'Code Grey stand down.'
  • If customers refuse to move, staff should not remain with them but must move to the shelter in place location

Follow-up actions required

  • Record an incident using relevant incident details. For example, Abuse - Actual - Verbal. All incidents relating to COVID-19 must include the word 'COVID' within the incident description. See Step 6 in Table 1 of Reporting, recording and escalating incidents of customer aggression
  • For actual or threats of spitting at staff or others:
    • record the incident using behaviour type Assault-No weapon - Actual/Threat - Bodily fluids. Add additional behaviours as required

  • An incident can also be recorded if the customer is unknown. See Step 3 in Table 6 of Reporting, recording and escalating incidents of customer aggression
  • Consider implementing a Managed Service Plan
  • Record an ESSentials incident, if impacted (OH&S - Incident or Injury)
  • Consider contacting the Employment Law Team about the suitability of legal action and legal support. The Resources page has a link

Incident response - COVID-19 incidents in queues outside of the office/controlled entry

COVID-19 incidents outside - customer facing environment + Read more ...

Service Officers or Security Guards should not assist customers outside of the office. Where staff become aware of aggression in the queue, entry/exit to the site must cease.

Actions required:

  • Close doors to service centre. Customers may be exited through another door if safe to do so
  • Police to be contacted - dial 000
  • Entry to site will be restricted while aggression continues
  • Record an incident using relevant incident details. For example, Abuse - Actual - Verbal. All incidents relating to COVID-19 must include the word 'COVID' within the incident description. See Step 6 in Table 1 of Reporting, recording and escalating incidents of customer aggression

Person deliberately coughs or spits towards staff

Person deliberately coughs or spits towards staff + Read more ...

An incident involving a person deliberately coughing or spitting towards a Services Australia worker (including a security guard) is considered to be an assault and may be prosecuted under relevant State, Territory or Commonwealth laws.

Actions required:

  • Coughing towards staff - Duress
  • Spitting, threatening to spit, or infect others with COVID-19 - Code Grey (if customer on premises) or consider locking the entrance to prevent the person entering/re-entering
  • Call Police and/or emergency services if required - dial 000
  • Direct the person to leave (does not apply if customer outside), using Public Order Protection and Property Act 1971 (PoPPA)
  • Follow Workplace, Health and Safety directions. The Resources page has a link to WH&S information sheet: Spitting and coughing during COVID-19 pandemic
  • Contact the Employment Law Team about the suitability of legal action and legal support. The Resources page has a link
