  • For guidance, see Booking appointments in the Centrelink Appointment System. Procedure ends here

If:

    • due to imprisonment (IMP) go to Step 8

  • the customer is currently overseas, go to Step 5

3

PP is suspended + Read more ...

Investigate the reason for the suspension to determine if the suspension can be resolved and the appointment can still be conducted.

See Cancellation, suspension and rejection codes for Parenting Payment (PP) for a list of suspension codes and whether PP can be restored.

Attempt to contact the customer. Has contact with the customer been successful?

  • Yes, and the suspension:
    • can be resolved, go to Step 4
    • cannot be resolved, go to Step 6
  • No, go to Step 7

4

Current suspension can be resolved + Read more ...

Make any required updates about the suspension and restore payment:

  • Go to the Benefit Action (BA) screen
  • Svc Rsn field, PPP or PPS
  • Action field, Restore
  • Reason field, leave blank
  • Effect Date field, select [Enter] for the date of effect to default to the date of suspension
  • Source field, select relevant source
  • DOR field, date the information was recorded
  • Go to the Assessment Results (AR) screen and finalise the activity
  • Use Fast Note - select Auto Text use Parents > SUS/CAN/RES > PP restored. Record all relevant details, include any manual adjustments made to arrears/overpayment

See Step 1 in Table 5.

5

Customer overseas at time of ParentsNext appointment + Read more ...

If it is identified the customer is overseas at the time of their scheduled appointment, their expected date of return to Australia must be determined.

Go to the Immigration Advised Movements (RSIM) and/or Customer Advised Travel Details (RSCD) screen.

Has the return to Australia date been recorded?

  • Yes:
    • Book a ParentsNext appointment for 7 days after the expected date of return to Australia. For guidance, see Booking appointments in the Centrelink Appointment System
    • Record the ParentsNext appointment outcome as 'CANCELLED - Did Not attend (DNA)'
  • No:
    • Record the ParentsNext appointment outcome as 'CANCELLED - Did Not attend (DNA)'
    • Document all information using the Fast Note - select Auto Text, use Parents > Update > ParentsNext Travel Overseas
    • See Step 8 in Table 4

6

Current suspension cannot be resolved + Read more ...

Tell the customer of the suspension reason and action needed for payment to be restored:

  • Record the ParentsNext appointment outcome as 'completed'
  • Book a ParentsNext appointment for 14 days' time, by which point the suspension should be resolved. For guidance, see Booking appointments in the Centrelink Appointment System. Procedure ends here

7

Payment currently suspended - contact unsuccessful + Read more ...

Record the ParentsNext appointment outcome as 'Did Not Attend - DNA':

  • Record a DOC to detail an outbound contact attempt about the scheduled ParentsNext appointment was unsuccessful
  • There is an auto-review that will check the customer's eligibility 4 weeks after they were selected. If the customer is still eligible at that time a new ParentsNext appointment will be auto-booked or a review created to manually follow up the status

8

Customer imprisoned at time of scheduled ParentsNext appointment + Read more ...

If a customer's PP is suspended due to imprisonment (IMP) at the time of the scheduled ParentsNext appointment, the record must be investigated to determine if a release date is recorded. See Confirming prison admission and release dates.

Note: Service Officers do not need to make any contact with either the customer's Prison Liaison Officer or the prison/institute directly. Only interrogation of the record is needed.

Is a release date recorded on the customer's record?

  • Yes:
    • Book a ParentsNext appointment for 14 days after the release date. For guidance, see Booking appointments in the Centrelink Appointment System
    • Record the current ParentsNext appointment outcome as 'completed'
    • DOC the record to detail the customer was imprisoned at the time of the appointment and a new appointment has been booked, procedure ends here
  • No:
    • Do not book a future ParentsNext appointment. There is an auto-review that will check the customer's eligibility 4 weeks after they were selected. If the customer is still eligible at that time a new ParentsNext appointment will be automatically booked or a review created to manually follow up the status
    • Record the appointment outcome as 'completed'
    • DOC the record to detail the customer was imprisoned at the time of the appointment but a new appointment was not booked as there is no release date recorded, procedure ends here

Action required where customer is no longer eligible for ParentsNext

Table 3

Step

Action

1

Customer is displaying as ineligible for ParentsNext + Read more ...

If the customer's participation status is displaying as ineligible at the time of their scheduled appointment they must be offered the option to participate in ParentsNext as a volunteer unless they have been exited for the following reasons:

  • Parenting Payment is currently cancelled
  • They no longer have a child under 6 in their care
  • they relocated to a CDP location or Norfolk Island

If the appointment was a phone appointment and the customer is not already present, contact must still be attempted as a courtesy, as they will have received notification of the appointment.

Was contact with the customer successful?

  • Yes, go to Step 2
  • No:
    • Record the appointment outcome as 'completed'
    • DOC the record to detail the customer is no longer eligible for ParentsNext at this time (and if it was a phone appointment, that an outbound contact was attempted). Procedure ends here

2

Offer customer option to volunteer in ParentsNext + Read more ...

Tell the customer:

  • Today we will be talking about the ParentsNext program
  • Due to your current circumstances they can choose to take part in ParentsNext as a voluntary participant
  • This means that you do not need to participate in the program. However, you can still choose to volunteer
  • ParentsNext is available to anyone who receives Parenting Payment with a child under six
  • You don't have to look for work as part of the ParentsNext program. It's a program to help you prepare for getting a job when your youngest child goes to school - or sooner if that is what you want. Your ParentsNext provider can help you to think about your future and what you hope to achieve
  • Your ParentsNext provider can help you set your future goals and work with you to reach them
  • ParentsNext supports parents and carers in lots of different ways. Some examples of support available are:
    • help finding a training course or work if you are ready
    • putting you in touch with other parents and carers
    • connecting you with local services such as counselling, financial advice, health, housing and domestic and family violence support
    • financial support to help with the cost of starting work or study, such as paying for a course or laptop, or buying clothes for work
  • Although you do not need to look for work, your provider will help with work readiness skills - like writing resumes and job interview practice
  • Support you to identify and work towards your education and employment goals
  • Have access to financial help through the Participation Fund, a flexible pool of funds that can be used to cover costs associated with work or study, such as the cost of training courses, child care, transport, IT equipment and job appropriate clothing

Tell the customer that they can find more information about the program and read stories from participants at the ParentsNext website.

Does the customer want to volunteer?

  • Yes, go to Step 3
  • No, tell the customer if they change their mind about being a ParentsNext volunteer, they can either:
    • contact Services Australia, or
    • talk to a ParentsNext provider to check if they are eligible to participate in the program
      To find a ParentsNext provider, go to Workforce Australia's website. Record the appointment outcome as 'completed'. Procedure ends here

3

Customer elected to participate voluntarily + Read more ...

As the customer has elected to participate voluntarily, they need to first be registered via the Volunteer Guided Procedure (GP).

Note: the Volunteer GP will only be selectable if the customer meets the eligibility criteria for voluntary participation.

Register the customer as a voluntary participant:

  • Go to the ParentsNext Summary screen via the Workspace > Social Services Benefit Management menu
  • Launch the Volunteer GP from the Volunteer button under the Volunteer section of the page and ask the customer the eligibility questions
  • Select the relevant radio button based on the customer's responses to each question
  • Does the customer wish to volunteer? This will automatically default to 'No' if the customer's responses result in them being ineligible, otherwise select 'Yes'
  • Comments field, record relevant notes based on whether the customer was eligible to volunteer or not
  • Go to the Review screen and finalise the GP

Note: if there is a delay in the update correctly reassessing eligibility for ParentsNext, run the daily driver.

See Step 2 of Table 6.

For ParentsNext processing Service Officers only.

Conducting a ParentsNext appointment + Read more ...

What type of ParentsNext appointment has been booked?

  • Phone appointment, go to Step 2
  • Face to face appointment, go to Step 5

2

ParentsNext Phone Appointment + Read more ...

Phone appointments will allocate as a work item via Workload Manager. Service Officers with the appropriate skill tag will be allocated the work item once they select ready for work.

The customer's appointment notification advises them the appointment may be within an hour of their scheduled appointment time. An attempt to contact can be made any time up to one hour after the appointment.

Do not attempt contact with a customer before 9 am their local time.

If the work item is for the:

  • first outbound attempt and has presented:
    • within one hour after the scheduled appointment time, go to Step 4
    • later than one hour after the scheduled appointment time, go to Step 3
  • second outbound attempt, go to Step 4

3

ParentsNext appointment work item has presented later than one hour after the scheduled time + Read more ...

If an appointment work item presents late (that is, later than one hour after the scheduled appointment time), still attempt to contact the customer.

If contact is:

Successful, apologise for the delay. If the customer:

  • is able to undertake the appointment immediately, see Step 1 in Table 5
  • is not able to undertake the appointment immediately
    • book a ParentsNext appointment for a future date that suits the customer
    • Record this appointment outcome as 'completed'
    • DOC the record to detail that ParentsNext appointments were running behind, an outbound contact to apologise was made and a new appointment booked. Procedure ends here

Unsuccessful,

  • Do not suspend the payment
  • Book a ParentsNext appointment for a future date
  • Record the appointment outcome as 'completed'
  • DOC the record to detail that ParentsNext appointments were running behind, an outbound contact to apologise was attempted and that a new future appointment was booked

Procedure ends here.

4

Attempt to contact the customer + Read more ...

Use all available phone numbers when attempting to contact the customer.

Note: the phone number on the appointment work item can differ from the phone numbers on the Customer Summary screen or Telephone Details screen. See Calling a customer or returning a customer's call.

Note: if the customer's listed contact number/s are disconnected, no longer belongs to them, or the customer has incoming call restrictions, they are considered to have failed to attend their appointment.

Did the customer attend the phone appointment?

  • Yes, see Step 1 in Table 5
  • No, go to Step 6

5

Face to face appointment + Read more ...

Face to Face appointments will be booked into the customer's local service centre where the customer has no current contact numbers recorded.

Has the customer attended their face to face appointment?

  • Yes, see Step 1 in Table 5
  • No, update the appointment status to 'CANCELLED - Did Not Attend (DNA)'
    • If this is the first or second DNA appointment, book a new appointment
    • If this is the third DNA appointment they have missed code a CCE exemption, go to Step 8

6

Customer has failed to attend their ParentsNext phone appointment + Read more ...

Go to the Document List (DL) screen to determine if a previous outbound contact has been attempted.

If the customer's listed contact number/s are disconnected, no longer belongs to them, or has incoming call restrictions:

  • a second outbound contact attempt is not needed
  • the work item must not be reallocated

If it is the:

  • First outbound attempt, including where the call was auto-blocked or rejected, go to Step 7
  • First outbound attempt - contact number/s disconnected or no longer belongs to the customer:
    • Update the appointment status to 'CANCELLED - Did Not Attend (DNA)'
    • If the customer's contact number/s are disconnected or incorrect it should be updated (removed) from the customer's record. See Updating telephone details and/or paying Telephone Allowance (TAL)
    • Go to Step 8
  • Second outbound attempt:
    • Update the appointment status to 'CANCELLED - Did Not Attend (DNA)'
    • If the customer's contact number/s are disconnected or incorrect it should be updated (removed) from the customer's record. See Updating telephone details and/or paying Telephone Allowance (TAL)
    • Go to Step 8

7

Customer has failed to attend their ParentsNext phone appointment at first outbound attempt + Read more ...

  • Document (DOC) the customer's record with the ParentsNext Apt 1st Attempt Fast Note. As the customer's time zone may be different to staff, make sure when detailing the time the outbound call attempt was made, it is clear it has been recorded as the customer's local time
  • Go to the Electronic Messaging and online letters (EMOL) screen
    • Determine if the customer is registered for Electronic Messaging via SMS
    • If the customer is registered, a Desktop message is to be sent to notify of the impending contact
    • Select Desktop Messaging from the Customer Communication menu in Workspace
    • Select the document description of 'appointment SMS'
  • Go back to the Inbox
  • Select Unassign from the Result List
  • Select the appointment Work Item Id using the Reason 'Second Contact Attempt Required'. This will remove the work item from the user's inbox and reallocate the work item back to the pool to be reallocated at a later time for a second outbound attempt to occur

Procedure ends here.

Has the customer not attended 3 appointments:

  • Yes, see Step 1 in Table 6
  • No, procedure ends here

Discussion with customer + Read more ...

Service Officers are to discuss the customers circumstances and book all customers into an appointment with a ParentsNext provider. The customer can choose to opt out of the program after their initial appointment with a provider.

If during the appointment the customer advises they:

  • are experiencing exceptional circumstances including homelessness, a medical incapacity, or a major personal crisis exist which is impacting on their ability to participate in ParentsNext in the short term, an exemption may be needed. See Table 1 Step 3 in ParentsNext exemptions and exits
  • do not want to be referred - apply a CCE exemption and do not make the referral. See Table 1 Step 3 in ParentsNext exemptions and exits

Job seeker status + Read more ...

4

ParentsNext provider appointment + Read more ...

Tell the customer:

  • I will now book you an appointment with a ParentsNext provider
  • At your appointment, your provider will give you more information about the program and available support
  • While you are being referred to a provider, you do not need to take part in the program if it is not right for you and your Parenting Payment won't be affected if you choose not to take part. You can tell your provider if you choose not to participate
  • If you cannot attend an appointment with their provider, or have concerns about attending an appointment in person, it is important that they contact them in advance to let them know

If customer requests not to be referred to a provider, apply a CCE exemption and do not make the referral.

Do not:

  • use the Additional Requirements field to request a phone/video conferencing appointment when booking an appointment, or
  • give customers any expectation of whether the provider will grant their reasons for wanting a phone or video conferencing appointment

If a ParentsNext provider considers that attending the appointment in person will not benefit the customer, they may use their discretion to conduct the initial appointment via phone or video conferencing.

5

Referral to a ParentsNext provider + Read more ...

E009JW is a generic error code, which may be encountered within the job seeker registration or the Online Diary.

If an interpreter is needed at the ParentsNext provider appointment the appointment must be booked for no earlier than 7 days in the future to allow for the provider to arrange an interpreter.

In Process Direct:

  • Go to the Online Diary via Participation Summary
  • Select Create new referral > SPP-Parents next provider > Create Referral. The workflow will go to the List Providers screen
  • A list of all ParentsNext providers available within the customer's employment region will appear on the List Providers screen

Note: if a customer has a temporary address recorded providers may display within the online diary for a location they are not currently residing in. The referral to a ParentsNext provider must still be made for an initial appointment based on the providers returned in the search. The customer can contact the provider to discuss their circumstances and options for ParentsNext servicing.

Results generally will be as shown below.

If there is more than one provider listed, Service Officer's must read out all available providers and/or sites to the customer.

Providers with available appointments displayed, the Status field relating to appointments will be one of the following:

  • Available - indicates there are future appointments available, go to Step 7
  • No immediate sessions within 14 days - indicates there are future appointments:
    • select a ParentsNext provider which will display a list of available appointments
    • Future Appointment Date allows Service Officers to manually select a date to search for more appointments
    • select Refresh
    • available appointment, go to Step 7
    • no suitable available appointment, go to Step 6

No providers displayed or no available appointments, or provider full, for providers that are displayed, go to Step 6.

6

No provider or provider appointments available, or provider full + Read more ...

There may be instances where there are either no ParentsNext providers at all or those that are returned have no available sessions.

If this occurs, the Status field for providers on the List providers screen may display as one of the following:

  • No available sessions, go to Step 7
  • Full - indicates the provider has reached capacity of their market share, this provider cannot be offered to the customer as an option, if:
    • there are other providers listed an alternate provider must be offered, go to Step 8
    • it is the only provider listed, go to Step 11
  • No ParentsNext providers found - indicates there are no providers within a 100km radius of the customer's location, go to Step 11

7

No available sessions + Read more ...

No available sessions indicates that the provider does not currently have any future appointments available.

If:

  • multiple providers are displayed and some have available sessions:
    • Offer the customer a referral to only the providers that have available sessions. Do not detail those that do not have available sessions
    • If a customer requests to be referred to a specific provider that has no available sessions (even though that provider was not offered as an option), tell the customer that provider is not currently available and an alternate provider should be selected
    • Tell the customer they can discuss the option of a transfer to another provider with the provider they are referred to
    • A referral to a provider with no available sessions should only be considered under exceptional circumstances and where the customer refuses to be referred to an alternate provider. Exceptional circumstances do not include a preference to a provider in the local area, unless the available providers are not within a reasonable distance to the customer or they are unable to access the provider via public transport
  • the customer insists on a referral to a provider with no available sessions, these cases should only be escalated if all of the following apply:
    • The customer has initiated the request to a specific provider
    • They refuse to be referred to a provider that has available sessions
    • They have provided an exceptional reason as to why they are unable to be referred to a provider with available sessions. Transport issues are not considered an exceptional circumstance, unless the available providers are not within a reasonable distance or there is no public transport available
    • Attempt one outbound contact with the ParentsNext provider to request additional appointment sessions be made available
    • If contact is unsuccessful with that provider and the customer still refuses to be referred to an alternate provider with available sessions, go to Step 12
  • only one provider is displayed or multiple providers displayed but all are showing as No available sessions, attempt one outbound contact to the provider to request additional appointment sessions be made available. Provider details can be found using the Find a Provider tool on the Workforce Australia website. See the Resources page for a link to the tool. If contact is:
    • successful, exit and re-enter the online diary to refresh the available appointments, go to Step 8
    • unsuccessful, go to Step 11

8

Select a ParentsNext provider + Read more ...

Customers must be referred to one of the available providers considered to be within a reasonable distance:

  • Select a ParentsNext provider (where there are multiple providers with available sessions ask the customer their preferred provider; Service Officers must not suggest, recommend or select a provider for the customer)
  • From the Available Appointments screen, select an appointment date within 1 to 5 business days after the customers next EPED:
    • If there are no suitable appointments in this period due to the customer's previous commitments, or no availability, select an appointment date 1 to 5 business days after the customer's next following EPED.
      Note: if there are still no provider appointments available, go to Step 11
  • When an appointment has been selected, the Appointment Details screen will display, go to Step 9

9

Confirm the appointment details + Read more ...

  • From the Appointment Details screen:
    • Confirm details of the appointment with the customer. Tell them no further confirmation will be provided (that is, they will not receive a letter or electronic message confirming the provider appointment details)
    • Additional Requirements field, key information the provider may need to be aware of, (for example, if an interpreter is needed). Do not use this field to request a phone/video conferencing appointment. Appointments with a provider must be booked as Face to Face)
    • Appointment Notification field, select Face to Face (if the Service Australia appointment was conducted at a service centre) or Phone (if the Service Australia appointment was conducted via phone)
  • Select Book Appointment to finalise the appointment booking process
  • The Appointment Outcome screen will display:
    • The details of the appointment
    • Was this appointment created in error? field, this question should be left blank
    • Select Finish
  • From the Provider Appointment screen, read the customer the information within the message displayed
  • Select Continue to finalise the procedure
  • Tell the customer they will receive a reminder about their appointment from their provider one day before their appointment. Based on their preferences this will either be an SMS, email or a reminder via their Workforce Australia inbox

Note: tell the customer to contact their provider about alternate servicing arrangements, i.e. venue, phone, or video conferencing, when they are having issues about:

  • accessing their provider's location, or
  • it is not within a reasonable distance, or
  • they have concerns about attending in person

Do not give customers any expectation that the provider will accept their reasons for requesting a phone or video conferencing appointment.

If a ParentsNext provider considers that attending the appointment in person will not benefit the customer, they may use their discretion to conduct the initial appointment via phone or video conferencing.

Remind customer of requirements of all income support recipients to report to Centrelink if they or their partner earn an income and how to do this online.
