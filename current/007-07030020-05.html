Reason

Scenarios for children born or adopted before 1 July 2023

Table 2: this table describes scenarios to illustrate how to apply the correct PPL start date.

Scenario

Description

1

Customer makes an effective post birth claim and provides proof of child's birth/placement within 28 days of birth/placement

If the customer made an effective claim for PPL for the child and verified the child's birth within 28 days from the day of the child's birth or placement as part of the adoption process, the PPL period start day is the later of:

  • the day the child was born, or
  • the primary claimant's nominated start date

2

Customer makes an effective post birth claim within 28 days of the child's birth/placement but only provides proof of birth/placement after 28 days

If the customer made an effective claim for PPL for the child within 28 days from the day of the child's birth or placement as part of the adoption process, but did not provide proof of birth until after the 28 days, the start day is the later of:

  • the day the primary claimant provides the proof of birth, or
  • the primary claimant's nominated start date

3

Customer makes an effective PPL claim after 28 days from the child's birth/placement

If the customer made an effective PPL claim, which included proof of birth/placement after 28 days from the child's birth or placement as part of the adoption process, the start date is the later of:

  • the day the claim is made, or
  • the primary claimant's nominated start date

4

PPL start date - Post birth claim lodged more than 28 days after birth

16 September: Milly gives birth to a healthy baby.

30 September: Milly lodges the Newborn Child Declaration (FA081) (received at the hospital) at a local office.

4 October: Milly is sent a request to claim letter asking Milly to lodge an online claim or FA100.

17 October:

  • Milly lodges an effective paper claim with all remaining documentation required for the PPL claim
  • Milly nominates to receive PPL from the baby's date of birth

As Milly lodged the FA081 only on 30 September and the online claim was submitted more than 28 days after the birth of the child, the earliest start date for PPL will be 17 October, date of claim.

If Milly had lodged an online claim, Milly would have only been given the option to claim form the date of claim or a date in the future.

5

PPL start date - Post birth claim lodged within 28 days of birth

3 March: Tilly gives birth to a healthy baby.

15 March: Tilly lodges a PPL claim via the FA100 paper claim.

26 March: Tilly is sent a request for information letter asking Tilly to lodge proof of birth, otherwise the claim will not be effective.

5 April: More than 28 days after birth:

  • Tilly uploads the fully completed Newborn Child Declaration (FA081) received at the hospital
  • Tilly has now lodged all documentation and information to make the claim (lodged 15 March) an effective claim

As the date of receipt for Tilly's effective claim was lodged within 28 days of the birth, Tilly's PPL can commence from the date of birth of the child.

Scenario for children born or adopted on or after 1 July 2023

Table 3

Item

Description

1

Customer makes an effective post birth claim and provides proof of child's birth/placement

For the customer to make an effective claim for PPL, the child’s birth must be verified.

PPL days can be backdated up to 100 days from the date of receipt, with the earliest PPL date being from the child’s birth/placement. A PPL date cannot be nominated before the child’s birth/placement.

Claimants have to nominate at least one PPL day to be payable.

An arrears payment can be paid for a claimant up to 100 days in the past and may result in arrears and the customer may incur a debt for Income Support Payment and/or Family Tax Benefit Part B. Debt offsetting may occur in these cases.
