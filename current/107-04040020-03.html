4

Code the embargo + Read more ...

Policy on exchange rates:

Amounts paid by foreign authorities are converted to Australian dollars at a different rate that the official exchange rate used in system calculations. This means that the amount received does not match the calculated debt amount.

Policy advice is that, where the delegate is satisfied that the variation between the debt amount and the amount received is solely due to exchange rates, any debt is considered to be fully repaid, regardless of any shortfall or excess. Any bank fees deducted in transactions are also ignored.

In these cases, the coding of the amount received must always be adjusted to match the debt amount and recorded in the DOC.

Coding:

For each record on the schedule, on the Foreign Claim Summary (FGS) screen, enter 'E' against the relevant foreign payment to navigate to the Foreign Indirect Embargo (FGIE) or Foreign Direct Embargo (FGDE) screen.

For indirect embargo:

On the FGIE screen, code:

  • Amt Received $A:

Notes:

  • Adjusted where relevant for exchange rates, as above
  • Code 0.00 for failed embargo
  • Date Received:
  • Receipt Num:

For direct embargo:

On the FGDE screen, code:

  • Date Grant Notice Received:
  • Date Arrs Amt Received: as advised by the Finance Team
  • Amt Foreign Arrs Sent: in source currency
  • Exchange Rate: as advised by the Finance Team
  • Receipt Num:
  • Amt Received $A:

Notes:

  • Adjusted where relevant for exchange rates, as above, including for recovery of partner debts
  • Code 0.00 for failed embargo
  • List Num:
  • Embargo Start Date:
  • Embargo End Date:
  • Arrears Start Date:
  • Arrears End Date:
  • Amt to be Recovered $A:

Note: where partner's debt has also been recovered from the arrears, the amount coded is the total amount for both debts.

Note: in all cases, unless otherwise recovered, any debt for the partner must be raised at this point per normal procedures ensuring the reason for the debt is 'CFP', see General debt raising information.

The Balance Outstanding $A: field is automatically populated.

Is there a valid positive or negative balance outstanding?

  • Yes, for:
    • negative adjustments, go to Step 5
    • positive adjustments, go to Step 6
  • No, finalise the activity and go to Step 7

5

Negative adjustments + Read more ...

Any valid shortfall after the embargo is coded must be raised as a debt.

  • Code the Raise debt: field as 'Y' and finalise the embargo activity
  • Use the created debt activity on the Activity List (AL) screen to raise the debt per normal procedures, see General debt raising information

Go to Step 6

6

Positive adjustments + Read more ...

Information on agreed arrangements with the Finance Team and using Essentials is available for staff in CIS through the International Services homepage. See the Resources page for a link to the International Services homepage.

Any valid excess amount after the embargo is coded must be paid to the customer through Essentials. A vendor must be created before payments can be issued.

The Finance Team in Zone Tasmania create vendors within 48 hours upon request by CIS.

  • Prepare and send the vendor request to the Finance Team and set a review for 48 hours
  • Where vendors have not been created in the time frame, contact the Finance Team to resolve
  • Access Essentials and issue the payment to the customer

Go to Step 7

7

Embargo lists + Read more ...

Information on agreed arrangements with the Finance Team and access to tools to assist in recording embargoes is available for staff in CIS through the International Services homepage. See the Resources page for a link to the International Services homepage.

  • Access the Embargo List
  • Record all details of the embargo for each record
  • Record all details of the embargo in a DOC
  • Send the Embargo List to the Finance Team

The Finance Team are responsible for accounting for the monies received in accordance with organisational requirements.
