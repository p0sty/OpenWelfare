Determine if handoff relates to undisclosed products + Read more ...

The handoff can be created:

  • by coding discrepancies, such as an incorrectly recorded purchase date or price, or
  • where a service officer needs to match provider data to any other existing income stream product on record

Compare the source of the funds used to purchase the new income stream between the provider data and customer record.

Note: the customer may partially or fully commute their old income stream and purchase a new product using commuted funds and funds from another source.

If the handoff is due to:

  • a coding error for an existing product, see:
    • a defined benefit income stream, or
    • checking for differences for account-based and market-linked products
  • an undisclosed income stream, and the product was a result of a commutation from an existing income stream, see:
    • a defined benefit income stream, or
    • checking for differences for account-based and market-linked products

Investigate the record (ISRNMF, ISRNEWAML) + Read more ...

The handoff was created:

  • as the income stream product is not coded on the record, or
  • no match could be made with an existing product

Investigate if the product already exists on the record with outdated provider information as a result of a Successor Funds Transfer (SFT). New defined benefit income stream (ISRNMF, ISRNEWDEF) + Read more ...

The handoff was created because:

  • a defined benefit income stream product is not coded on the record, or
  • no match could be made with an existing product

If the customer has been current on an income support payment and over age pension age before the start of the income stream, the source of funds used to start the product will need to be determined and additional updates may be required.

Notes:

  • Product ownership must be confirmed either verbally, using schedules or in writing. Coding should not occur prior to this.
  • investigating the source of the funds is not necessary if the customer is under age pension age, as defined benefit products will be started from superannuation funds, which are not assessable for this cohort

If the customer is under age pension age make sure all historical information required is available. Go to Step 7.

Conduct the following investigations:

  • Search the customer's record for any supporting documentation advising the source of the funds, including in uploaded scans or on Document List (DL)
    • This can include an income stream schedule or commencement documents from the fund advising the rate of payment at commencement, including any applicable tax-free component
  • Check if all required financial year CPI updates are available including payment details at commencement
  • If the handoff is due to a coding error for an existing product, see a defined benefit income stream

Has the source of the income stream been advised and any supporting documentation been supplied?

  • Yes, go to Step 4
  • No:
    • Ask the customer for the missing details. Make 2 genuine attempts to confirm the managed investment details on the customer's record. DOC the record for each phone contact attempt. See the Resources page for DOC template
    • Request additional documents using RFI or QSS32. See the Resources page for DOC template
    • For products not coded within the timeframe for information requests, lodge an investigation request for details that cannot be requested from the customer
    • Note: historical details are not needed for Commonwealth Seniors Health Care Card (CSHC) and Carer Allowance (CA) only customers, unless they have received an income support payment since the date of purchase
    • Procedure ends here

Investigate managed investments + Read more ...

Check if any managed investment/s need updating. View the scans on the Documents tab. The scans on the customer's (and/or partner's) record may contain details about investments listed or declared and not yet coded.

If documents are found showing the customer has advised a Managed Investment has been closed to purchase an Income Stream, see:

  • Adding or updating an account-based income stream
  • Adding or updating a market-linked income stream
  • Adding or updating a defined benefit income stream

The managed investment may relate to the income stream in several ways, or not at all:

  • multiple managed investment products can be converted into a single income stream
  • only part of the managed investment may have been used to purchase an income stream
  • the managed investment may be a lump sum payment not converted into a defined benefit
  • the managed investment a separate investment and not directly related to the income stream

From 20 September 2007, a managed investment product cannot be converted to a market-linked income (MLI) stream. For example, the customer cannot purchase a new MLI product using money from their superannuation.

Review the EANS screen to determine if the customer's employment accounts for additional superannuation. If customers are currently working they may be still be accumulating superannuation.

Is there enough information on the record to identify if any of the managed investment/s were used to fund the entire income stream?

  • Yes, go to Step 6
  • No, go to Step 5

Update the MIS entry + Read more ...

The handoff was created because a product is not coded and superannuation investments are coded on the MIS screen.

If the bulk update for 20 March or 20 September has been actioned the event date will show as a future date. Follow the workaround for MIS/SIS updates before 20 March or 20 September to complete the update.

Go to MIS:

  • Find the relevant product under the Managed Investments (MIP) or Unregistered Managed Investments (MIUS) table
  • Expand the entry to show historical data under the Managed Investments History or Unregistered Managed Investment History table
  • Update the investment as needed by selecting the Add new line icon on the right of the table
  • Code the Event date field:
    • For market-linked or account-based income streams, code today's date
    • For defined benefit income streams, code the CPI date in the provider data
  • If the whole of the investment was used to purchase the income stream, zero off the investment:
    • For account-based investments, zero off the Account amount field
    • For unit based investments, zero off the Number of units field
  • If the managed investment has entries after the Event date, delete or update each Date of Event
  • Save the changes
  • Update any other investments on MIS or MIUS as needed

ISRDBT + Read more ...

Key SUPS in Super Key to go to the Income Stream (SUPS, SUPI, SUPV) table. Delete any provisional data applied by the system.

Take note of the data applied in the update. This will need to be re-applied at the correct Date of Event (DOV).

Delete the provisional data in CoC if the activity is processed in Customer First or Process Direct.

The handoff occurs when the auto-update applied by the provider data results in a big change to the customer's rate.

    • place the activity on hold. Procedure ends here.
  • Yes:
    • and the product is already coded, refer to the processes for account-based and market-linked products or

  • No,

    • Request additional documents using RFI or QSS32. See the Resources page for DOC template
