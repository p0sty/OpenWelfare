This page contains details about fixing an incorrect date of birth (DOB) recorded for a customer or their child.

On this page:

Customer disputes DOB record

Table 1

Step

Action

1

Customer disputes official date of birth (DOB) recorded + Read more ...

A customer's official DOB for Centrelink purposes is the date indicated by:

  • State or Territory Registrar of Births, Deaths and Marriages (BDM) (if born in Australia), or
  • Department of Home Affairs (if born outside of Australia)

Only the dates identified on documents issued by BDM or Department of Home Affairs can be accepted as the official DOB.

Note: the exception is where it has been established the birth is not registered. If a customer is unable to provide a document from BDM or Department of Home Affairs, see Verifying the date of birth or age of a customer or child.

Is the customer at a service centre?

  • Yes, go to Step 3
  • No, go to Step 2

2

Customer advises via Centrelink smart centre call + Read more ...

DOB has been incorrectly recorded

Check the customer’s record for evidence, see:

  • DOCs on the record
  • Scanned documents
  • Identity Summary. Has DOB been verified as part of the identity confirmation process? See Identity Confirmation

Is there evidence of correct DOB?

  • Yes, update DOB on the record See Step 1 in Table 2
  • No, advise the customer to provide evidence of DOB, for example:
    • newly issued birth certificate from Births Deaths and Marriages, or
    • letter from Department of Home Affairs

See also:

  • Confirming a customer's identity or
  • For a child, Documents required for Centrelink new claims

Record details on a Display on Access DOC. Procedure ends here.

3

Customer advises at the service centre + Read more ...

Sight official date of birth identity documentation to confirm if the DOB is incorrectly recorded on the customer's record. If the DOB has been changed by Births, Deaths and Marriages or Department of Home Affairs, record details of the document sighted, including relevant serial number on a DOC.

Note: if the customer does not have confirmation of their own or their child's date of birth when they advise the recorded date of birth is incorrect, check their record and existing DOCs for previously supplied proof. If valid, use that as confirmation.

Is the date of birth recorded on the system correct?

  • Yes, tell the customer their details are correct. Procedure ends here
  • No, see Step 1 in Table 2

Correcting a DOB record

Table 2

Step

Action

1

Change the date of birth + Read more ...

If the date of birth correction for a:

  • customer record, go to Step 4
  • child record that is Family Tax Benefit (FTB) current and the child entered the customer's care:
    • from the child's DOB, go to Step 2
    • after the child's DOB, go to Step 3
  • child record that is not FTB current, go to Step 3

2

Change date of birth for FTB current child who entered the customer's care from DOB + Read more ...

Use the correct Date of Receipt (DOR) for the correction. The DOR must be the date the correct proof of birth was provided.

To correct an FTB child's official date of birth, select the child from the Child Selection (CHS) screen and:

  • on the Change Child (CCH) screen, amend the Birth Date field
  • on the Child in Customer Care (CHC) screen, amend the Event Date field
  • on the FTB Shared Care (FSC) screen, amend the Start Date field
  • on the SSA Shared Care (SSC) screen, amend the Start Date field
  • enter details in the Source field
  • enter details in the DOR field using the later of the date FTB was originally claimed for the child or the date the correct proof of birth was provided
  • finalise the activity, and
  • record on a DOC why the DOB has changed and the document provided to support the change

If the activity results in:

  • an overpayment or an arrears payment, go to Step 5
  • no overpayment or arrears payment, and either was expected, contact Technical Peer Support (TPS) or Access Support Centre. Procedure ends here
  • no overpayment or arrears payment, and neither was expected, procedure ends here

3

Change date of birth for non-current FTB child or FTB current child who entered customer's care after DOB + Read more ...

Use the correct Date of Receipt (DOR) for the correction. The DOR must be the date the correct proof of birth was provided.

Select the child from the Child Selection (CHS) screen:

  • on the Change Child (CCH) screen, amend the Birth Date field
  • enter details in the Source field
  • enter details in the DOR field. Note: there is no need to enter details in the Action field
  • press [Enter] and finalise the activity
  • record on a DOC why the DOB was changed and the document provided to support the change

If the activity results in:

  • an overpayment or an arrears payment, go to Step 5
  • no overpayment or arrears payment, and either was expected, contact Technical Peer Support (TPS) or Access Support Centre. Procedure ends here
  • no overpayment or arrears payment, and neither was expected, procedure ends here

4

Change customer's date of birth + Read more ...

There should only be one date of birth recorded for a customer. Action the coding below for all current Name Types on the customer’s record.

Use the correct Date of Receipt (DOR) for the correction. The DOR must be the date the correct proof of birth was provided.

To change a customer’s date of birth:

  • go to the Customer Person Detail Summary (CPDS) screen
    • select the first Name Type
    • at the Date of Birth field on the Customer Person Details (CPD) screen, enter the correct date of birth
    • enter detail in the Source field
    • enter details in the DOR field
    • select continue
    • repeat for all current Name Types on the CPDS screen
  • update the Start Date on the Country of Residence (CRES) screen to the correct date of birth
  • update the Date of Event of the Country of Citizenship on the (RSLEG) screen to the correct date of birth
  • open the Marital Status (MS) screen
  • select the earliest Marital Status event
  • add the correct date of birth in the Event Date field
  • select Correct in the Action field
  • finalise the activity, and
  • record on a DOC to explain why the DOB was changed and the document provided to support the change

If the activity results in:

  • an overpayment or an arrears payment, go to Step 5
  • no overpayment or arrears payment, and either was expected, contact Technical Peer Support (TPS) or Access Support Centre. Procedure ends here
  • no overpayment or arrears payment, and neither was expected, procedure ends here

5

Customer or child born in Australia + Read more ...

Was the customer or child born in Australia or has their birth been registered with BDM?

  • Yes, go to Step 6
  • No, go to Step 7

6

Australian customers and children + Read more ...

The date BDM changed the DOB is the date of event which determines whether arrears are payable or a debt must be investigated.

For help, see:

  • Payment of arrears after reassessment
  • General debt raising information
  • Debt investigation

Complete activity via the Assessment Results (AR) screen.

Record details on a DOC including:

  • the details of the document sighted, and
  • relevant serial number

Procedure ends here.

7

Overpayment or arrears when customer or child was born overseas + Read more ...

The Department of Home Affairs is the only agency authorised to change an official DOB for customers born overseas.

Note: the exception is if the customer was born overseas but their birth has been registered in Australia.

The most common reason Department of Home Affairs change a DOB is for refugee migrants whose original DOB may have been assumed when official records were unavailable.

The date Department of Home Affairs changed the DOB, is the date of event which determines whether arrears are payable, or, an overpayment must be investigated.

For help, see:

  • Payment of arrears after reassessment
  • General debt raising information
  • Debt investigation

Complete activity via the Assessment Results (AR) screen.

Record details on a DOC including:

  • the details of the document sighted, and
  • relevant serial number
