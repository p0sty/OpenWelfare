This document outlines information about Department of Veterans’ Affairs (DVA) and the Veterans' Affairs Processing (VAP) speech pathology business rules used by Services Australia Service Officers.

Speech pathology services

The DVA provides funding to eligible DVA health card holders (the veteran community) that covers Speech Pathology services provided to entitled persons by registered Speech Pathologists.

Speech Pathologists assist people who have swallowing and communication difficulties, through the provision of treatment and counselling. Speech pathologists assess, diagnose and manage disorders of speech and language. Speech Pathology services must be claimed in accordance with the patient’s assessed clinical need.

Services Australia provides claim processing and payment services to facilitate this program.

Eligibility to receive speech pathology services

Members of the veteran community are eligible to receive DVA funded Speech Pathology services based on the type of DVA health care card they hold:

DVA Gold Card and Totally Permanently Incapacitated (TPI) Gold Card holders

DVA funds most Speech Pathology services necessary to meet a clinical need.

DVA White Card holders

DVA funds Speech Pathology services related to the card holder's accepted disabilities only, and treatment may require prior financial authorisation before it is commenced.

Patient Eligibility for VAP contains the steps to follow to verify a DVA health card holder’s eligibility before paying or rejecting Speech Pathology services when the system returns 2-CHK-ELIG message.

DVA health card holders should check that their Speech Pathology service provider accepts the DVA Gold Card and White Card before starting treatment.

Eligibility to provide DVA speech pathology services

Only a Speech Pathology service contracted with DVA as a participating DVA provider that is registered with Services Australia (has a Medicare provider number) at the time of the service is eligible to provide services to eligible DVA health card holders.

DVA service providers agree to provide services to DVA health card holders through a payment arrangement, which means the card holder does not pay for those services.

The Resources page contains a link to ‘Notes for Allied Health Providers – Speech Pathologists’ on the DVA website. This contains detailed eligibility requirements and also information for Speech Pathology services.

DVA fee schedule (item numbers) for speech pathology services

The DVA Speech Pathologist Schedule of Fees contains a range of consultation items available to eligible DVA health card holders for both professional attendances in rooms, or at a place other than rooms (called domiciliary visits) such as patient’s home, residential aged care facility or institution.

Items in this fee schedule are in the range SH01- SH99.

Prior financial authorisation requirements

Speech Pathology service providers may be required to seek prior financial authorisation from DVA before providing certain Speech Pathology services. The fee schedules contain information on which items require prior financial authorisation.

To request financial approval, Speech Pathology service providers must contact DVA before administering services. To be able to claim payment service providers must complete a Treatment Prior Financial Approval Request form. The Resources page has a link to the DVA website.

DVA Speech Pathology services business rules

These business rules are issued by DVA and are used by VAP staff when processing claims:

  • All speech pathology items have time-based fees
  • Speech Pathology fees are based on an hourly rate and payment is made to the nearest quarter of an hour
  • Services rendered away from the provider's rooms attract a loading in addition to the normal time-based fee. This loading is equal to one quarter of the normal hourly rate
  • Treatment in residential aged care facilities (RACF) which requires a greater level of care and treatment in public hospitals requires prior approval. The Resources page contains a link to the DVA Speech Pathologist Services Fee Schedule. See also Prior Financial Authorisation (PARS Approval) for VAP
  • Treatment for White card holders must be related to an accepted disability
  • Providers are to comply with billing arrangements as outlined in the Notes for Dental and Allied Health Providers available on the DVA website

Claim lodgement information

DVA Speech Pathology services claims can be lodged in 3 ways. The Resources page contains a link to how to claim for health professionals on the Services Australia website.

Online claiming

Speech Pathology service providers can submit electronic claims for processing without the need to send any paperwork to Services Australia.

Paper copies of forms do not need to be retained if claiming online. However, a copy of the voucher should be provided to the DVA health card holder. Speech Pathology service providers should be sure that they can, from other means of record keeping, satisfy any request from Services Australia or DVA for evidence of service and details of treatment.

The DVA health card holder should be provided with a record of the treatment provided.

DVA Webclaim

This is a real-time web based electronic claiming channel that allows health care providers to submit electronic claims via the internet, without the need to send any paperwork. It is accessed via the Health Professionals Online Services (HPOS) portal.

Paper copies of forms do not need to be retained if claiming via DVA Webclaim. However, a copy of the voucher should be provided to the DVA health card holder. Service providers should be sure that they can, from other means of record keeping, satisfy any request from Services Australia or DVA for evidence of service and details of treatment.

Manual (paper based) claim

DVA Speech Pathology providers are required to lodge ‘Health Practitioner Service Voucher’ and ‘Allied Health Time-based Voucher’ forms. These forms can be printed from the DVA website. Service providers can use their own invoice forms for submitting accounts if they can provide the same level of information as required by the DVA forms. After completion, service providers should post the forms to Services Australia for processing as per the instructions on the form.

The Resources page contains a link to how to claim information on the DVA website.

Enquiries escalation

The VAP National Enquiry Line (Tier 1) is used as the ‘front door’ for all VAP Speech Pathology services enquiries. VAP trained Service Officers will help with the enquiry or escalate to the appropriate department for resolution.
