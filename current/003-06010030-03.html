Identifying customer vulnerability through file assessment

This table provides information to help identify circumstances on a customer’s record that indicate they may be experiencing or at risk of experiencing vulnerability.

Item

Description

1

Is the customer affected by family and domestic violence, or is there a history of family and domestic violence? + Read more ...

  • AEX - alerts to significant events that have impacted the customer including:
    • MPC (personal crisis)
    • MPD (personal disruption)
  • CUSE/OOP - crisis payment grants for family and domestic violence or other extreme circumstances
  • MNGS - exemption for maintenance collection
    • VIO (family and domestic violence)
    • IMP (imposition e.g. sexual assault)
  • Circumstances Impacting Compliance - in Process Direct, view the Circumstances impacting compliance screen by keying BIJC or Circumstances Impacting Compliance into the Super Key, followed by enter. This screen provides information on family relationship/domestic violence

Check the Document List (DL) screen for social work contact. More information may be available in the Social Work Information System (SWIS), but a social worker must check this.

2

Are there any issues regarding mental or physical health that need to be considered? + Read more ...

  • MC - Medical conditions
  • JCA - This information can be located in Customer First
    • Workflow - under 'referral' drop down box select 'JCA referral information' then select 'view report'
    • Manual coding - access JCA screen and select hyperlink
  • DL - for social work contact. More information may be available in SWIS, but this must be checked by a social worker
  • Circumstances Impacting Compliance - in Process Direct, view the Circumstances Impacting Compliance screen by keying BIJC or Circumstances Impacting Compliance into the Super Key, followed by enter. This screen records information on physical and psychological factors
  • NOLS - nominee. To find this information, select the arrangement and view the reason:
    • CRT - Court Appointed
    • PAI - Power of Attorney - incapacitated, or
    • POA - Power of Attorney (POA) and/or nominee is a Public Trustee or Guardian

3

Is the customer at risk, or experiencing homelessness or unstable accommodation? + Read more ...

  • Homelessness Indicator - at risk, temporary accommodation or ongoing homelessness
  • Homeless Indicator Details:
  • In Process Direct - key 'HID' (Homeless details) in the Super Key field
  • Customer First - go to the Homeless Indicator Details workflow
  • ADH/ADS/ACS - Check frequent changes of address including boarding homes/ caravan parks (more than three times in one year indicates at risk of homelessness)
  • ADH - C/ - Care of the post office/Services Australia/external agency indicates customer has no alternate mailing address and has arrangements in place
  • Circumstances Impacting Compliance - in Process Direct, view the Circumstances Impacting Compliance screen from the Participation menu which records homelessness information
  • NIH - Independent status codes HDH, HNH, HSR, HOE and PPG indicate a customer has Unreasonable to Live at Home (UTLAH) status or is otherwise unable to live with a parent. Codes PSF or PSU indicate a state ward status (risk and child protection issues)
  • DL - exploring documents including/ from:
    • Whereabouts unknown (WUK)
    • SU19 being collected
    • Community Engagement Officer contact
    • Social worker contacts (more information may be available in SWIS; but this must be checked by a social worker)
  • AEX - alerts to significant events that have impacted the customer including:
    • MPC (personal crisis)
    • MPD (personal disruption)
  • 'At Risk' - indicates young people at risk
  • NOLS - nominee. To find this information, select the arrangement and view the reason:
    • CRT - Court Appointed
    • SNI - Security Notice Issued, and/or
    • nominee is a Public Trustee or Guardian

4

Has there been any previous or current court or legal action that may indicate the customer is at risk? + Read more ...

  • Circumstances Impacting Compliance - in Process Direct, view the Circumstances Impacting Compliance screen by keying BIJC or Circumstances Impacting Compliance into the Super Key, followed by enter. This screen records recent prison release
  • PINS - Fines being deducted from payments
  • CUSE - Crisis payment grants
  • DL - Community Engagement Officers or prison liaison team contacts
  • NOLS - nominee. To find this information, select the arrangement and view the reason:
    • CRT - Court Appointed
    • SNI - Security Notice Issued, and/or
    • nominee is a Public Trustee or Guardian

5

Are there any personal or caring issues which may be impacting the customer? + Read more ...

  • DL - exploring documents including from:
    • Contact with service centres
    • Smart Centres
    • Recent claims indicating significant life events for example, crisis payment, parenting claim, carer, disability support pension
    • Customer Relation Unit (CRU) regarding issues other than mainstream services (such as urgent payments, welfare referrals, personal issues)
    • Social workers (more information may be available in SWIS; but this must be checked by a social worker)
  • MS - recent separation. If the customer is separating from a relationship, there are key steps they should consider to keep their personal information safe. The sequence of these key steps is important and all updates need to be made separately for Centrelink, Medicare and Child Support. See Separating Safely – Protecting personal details
  • CRS - view care providing details by selecting the care receiver then ADMD for an adult or CDMD for a child
  • AEX - alerts to significant events that have impacted the customer including:
    • MPC (personal crisis)
    • MPD (personal disruption)
    • Carers exemption (CAR)
  • 'At Risk' - indicates young people at risk
  • DOA - display on access. This can provide current issues impacting customers
  • Circumstances Impacting Compliance - in Process Direct, view the Circumstances impacting compliance screen by keying BIJC or Circumstances Impacting Compliance into the Super Key, followed by enter. This screen records significant caring issues
  • NOLS - nominee. To find this information select the arrangement and view the reason:
    • VOL - a voluntary nominee arrangement with a family member as the nominee
    • CRT - a court appointed nominee
    • PAI - Power of Attorney – incapacitated, or
    • POA - Power of Attorney and/or nominee is a Public Trustee or Guardian

6

Are there any other indicators of social exclusion that may be impacting the customer? + Read more ...

  • Job Plan - the customer may have counselling or contact with other agencies to address non-vocational barriers in their Job Plan, this can be found in the participation drop down box
  • Participation Summary - will advise of the 'stream' the customer is allocated in (Stream C indicates a higher level of vulnerabilities) this information can be found in the participation drop down box
  • NOLS - nominee. To find this information, select the arrangement and view the reason:
    • VOL - a voluntary nominee arrangement with a family member as the nominee
    • CRT - Court Appointed
    • PAI - Power of Attorney - incapacitated
    • POA - Power of Attorney and/or nominee is a Public Trustee or Guardian
  • DOA - display on access can provide current issues impacting customers
  • BIIS - Outlines Participation Solutions Team (PST) investigation
  • Circumstances Impacting Compliance - in Process Direct, view the Circumstances Impacting Compliance screen by keying BIJC or Circumstances Impacting Compliance into the Super Key, followed by enter. This screen records significant caring issues
  • AEX - alerts to significant events that have impacted the customer including:
    • MPC (personal crisis)
    • MPD (personal disruption)
  • Financial hardship
    • Overpayments (OPDL)
  • Financial hardship indications from multiple requests for:
    • urgent payments
    • weekly payments
    • crisis payments
    • welfare referrals
    • change of payday (EPED change)
    • high Centrepay deductions
    • cancellation of Centrepay deductions

7

Are there any indicators of literacy and numeracy, language or cultural issues to be considered? + Read more ...

  • EDCH - education achievements
  • OCD - identifies cultural or linguistic diversity
  • CRES - will identify residency and potential language barriers, may alert to past experience of torture and trauma, arrival on refugee/ Humanitarian visa
  • DOA - display on access can provide current issues impacting customers including the need for an interpreter
  • RRSM - Referral summary screen providing information for literacy and numeracy referrals
  • Circumstances Impacting Compliance - in Process Direct, view the Circumstances Impacting Compliance screen by keying BIJC or Circumstances Impacting Compliance into the Super Key, followed by enter. This screen records literacy/numeracy/English indicators
  • NOLS - nominee. To find this information, select the arrangement and view the reason:
    • VOL - a voluntary nominee arrangement with a family member as the nominee
    • CRT - a court appointed nominee
    • PAI - Power of Attorney – incapacitated, or
    • POA - Power of Attorney and/or nominee is a Public Trustee or Guardian

8

Does the customer live in an area impacted by a natural disaster? + Read more ...

Check for any relevant details about areas impacted by disasters on the Operational Blueprint Emergency Events page.

9

Are there any indicators of the customer accessing Place-Based Initiatives? + Read more ...

Check for any relevant information, including DL, for connection to Place Based Initiatives, for example Income Management (IM) and enhanced IM.
