For ABSTUDY Smart Centre Processing staff only.

Student circumstances

Step

Action

1

Age + Read more ...

Is the student aged 22 years or older?

  • Yes, student meets independence. No updates required, see ABSTUDY customer is independent through age
  • No, go to Step 2

2

Relationship status + Read more ...

Is the student currently or previously married or in a registered relationship?

  • Yes, see ABSTUDY customer currently or previously married or in a registered relationship
  • No, go to Step 3

3

Dependent child + Read more ...

Does the student have, or have had, a dependent child in their care?

  • Yes, and the child is:
    • their own, see ABSTUDY customer has, or has had, a dependent child
    • another person’s child, see ABSTUDY customer who has the care of another person's child
  • No, go to Step 4

4

Workforce criteria + Read more ...

The student may qualify as independent if they met one of the following:

  • in the workforce for at least 3 of the last 4 years (156 of the last 208 weeks)
  • self-supporting through full-time paid employment
  • full-time or concessional study load students from regional and remote areas under self-supporting through employment

Does the student meet one of the above?

  • Yes, see ABSTUDY customer is independent through workforce participation for independence assessment
  • No, go to Step 5

5

Lawful custody + Read more ...

Has the student or Australian Apprentice been imprisoned for a total of at least 6 months or more at a corrective institution, remand centre or youth training centre?

  • Yes, see ABSTUDY customer is independent due to previous lawful custody
  • No, go to Step 6

6

Orphan + Read more ...

A student or Australian Apprentice is considered to be an orphan where their parent/s are either:

  • deceased
  • missing and presumed deceased

Does the student meet the above definition of an orphan?

  • Yes, see ABSTUDY customer is an orphan
  • No, go to Step 7

7

Special Adult Status + Read more ...

Does the student have Special Adult Status in a traditional community?

  • Yes, see ABSTUDY customer who has Special Adult Status in a traditional community
  • No, go to Step 8

8

Parental responsibilities + Read more ...

Parent/s who cannot exercise their parental responsibilities include parent/s who are:

  • in gaol serving a prison sentence or remanded in custody for at least 14 days
  • mentally incapacitated for an indefinite period
  • living in a nursing home or similar institution for an indefinite period
  • missing and all reasonable attempts to locate or contact them have failed

Are the student’s parents are unable to exercise parental responsibilities?

  • Yes, see ABSTUDY customer's parents cannot exercise their parental responsibilities
  • No, go to Step 9

9

Unreasonable to live at home (UTLAH) + Read more ...

A student or Australian Apprentice may be unable to live at the home of both their natural, adoptive or relationship parents (or if they have only one parent, that parent) because of:

  • extreme family breakdown or similar exceptional circumstances, or
  • no parental concern from one or both parents, or
  • serious risk to their physical or mental wellbeing due to family and domestic violence, sexual abuse or similar unreasonable circumstance, or
  • their parents being unable to provide a suitable home due to a lack of stable accommodation, and
  • they are not receiving continuous financial or other support directly or indirectly from either a parent or guardian on a long-term basis

Is the student or Australian Apprentice unable to live at the home of either their natural, adoptive or relationship parents (or if they have only one parent, that parent) due to one of the above reasons?

  • Yes, see ABSTUDY unreasonable to live at home (UTLAH)
  • No, go to Step 10

10

Returned to live in an Indigenous Australian community + Read more ...

Has the student or Australian Apprentice returned to live in an Indigenous Australian community after being adopted or fostered by a non-Indigenous family for more than 2 years?

  • Yes, see ABSTUDY customer who has returned to live in an Indigenous Australian community
  • No, customer is not deemed independent
