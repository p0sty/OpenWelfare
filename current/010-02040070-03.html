Student or Australian Apprentice's age + Read more ...

Is the student or Australian Apprentice 15 years of age or older?

  • Yes, go to Step 2

2

Parental circumstance + Read more ...

Check if the student or Australian Apprentice's parent(s) cannot exercise their parental responsibilities.

They are not considered independent under this category if the below conditions do not apply to one parent. They may qualify for independence if they are, or have been dependent on someone other than their natural, adoptive or relationship parents.

Identify if each of their natural, adoptive or relationship parents, or their parent if they only have one parent, is:

  • in gaol serving a prison sentence or remanded in custody for at least 14 days
  • mentally incapacitated for an indefinite period
  • living in a nursing home or similar institution for an indefinite period, or
  • missing and all reasonable attempts to locate or contact them have failed

Do any of the above conditions apply?

  • Yes, go to Step 3
  • No, go to Step 4

3

Supporting documentation + Read more ...

The student or Australian Apprentice must provide evidence to support their claim for independence due to their parents being unable to exercise parental responsibilities.

Using personal information from a parent's record to assess their child's independence is acceptable.

Evidence required if the parent is:

  • serving a prison sentence or remanded in custody for at least 14 days:
    • a statement from Corrective Services giving details of the period(s) of their imprisonment, or
    • details of the periods and name(s) of corrective institution(s) for verification with Corrective Services, see Confirming prison admission and release dates
  • in a nursing home, hospital or similar institution:
    • verification from the nursing home, hospital or similar institution confirming the duration of the parent(s) stay
  • mentally incapacitated for an indefinite period of time:
    • medical evidence from the treating doctor or specialist, stating the nature of the illness, how it affects their ability to care for their children and the period the parent is unable to exercise their parental responsibilities
  • unable to be located
    • official documentation confirming the parent(s) is missing or whereabouts are unknown

The student or Australian Apprentice may apply under the unreasonable to live at home category where the parent(s) are incapacitated temporarily and cannot provide a home. See ABSTUDY unreasonable to live at home.

Go to Step 5.

4

Independence status not met + Read more ...

The student or Australian Apprentice is not eligible for grant of independent status for ABSTUDY under the category their parents are unable to exercise parental responsibilities.

  • If they are under 16 years of age and not living in the parental home, refer the unsupported person now to a Services Australia social worker
  • Consider whether they are independent on other grounds. See Independence for ABSTUDY customers
  • If appropriate, refer them for other assistance, e.g. social worker or Indigenous Service Officer

Go to Step 7.

5

Procedure when customer contacts + Read more ...

If the student or Australian Apprentice has contacted through:

  • a service centre, go to Step 6
  • Smart Centre Call, go to Step 7
  • the ABSTUDY Smart Centre Processing Services team, see Step 1 in Table 2

6

Scanning supporting documentation + Read more ...

Ensure the claim/form and all supporting documents are scanned to the student or Australian Apprentice's record to allow for processing by the ABSTUDY Smart Centre Processing Services team.

Procedure ends here.

7

Record details of the discussion + Read more ...

Record details of the discussion in a DOC.

Procedure ends here.

Step

Action

1

Independent category + Read more ...

Is the student or Australian Apprentice claiming independence as their parent(s) are missing and all reasonable attempts to locate or contact them have failed?

  • Yes, go to Step 2
  • No, see Step 1 in Table 3

2

Student or Australian Apprentice's attempts to contact or locate parent(s) have failed + Read more ...

If the parent(s) are legally missing and presumed dead, the student or Australian Apprentice may be independent as an orphan, see ABSTUDY customer is an orphan.

Can the student or Australian Apprentice provide evidence their parent(s) are missing and all reasonable attempts to locate or contact them have failed?

  • Yes, see Step 1 in Table 3
  • No, go to Step 3

3

Cannot provide evidence + Read more ...

If the student or Australian Apprentice cannot provide evidence:

  • Advise them that Services Australia will attempt to locate their parent(s)
  • Obtain information from them to help with identification searches of their parent(s). For example, date of birth, other names, last known address or last employer
  • Explain the privacy rules. See Accepting and disclosing information.

4

Locating parent(s) + Read more ...

Try to locate the parent(s) by:

  • Contacting relatives who may know where the parent(s) are
  • Writing to the last known address
  • Checking the internet, for example the electoral roll, or phone book
  • Attempt to locate the parent's record in Customer First. This is permitted under the privacy rules as the record is being accessed for the purpose of assessing a claim from a third party

Have the parent(s) been located?

  • Yes, go to Step 5
  • No, go to Step 7

5

Services Australia located parent(s) + Read more ...

Is/are the parent(s) deceased?

  • Yes, go to Step 8
  • No, go to Step 6

6

Located parent(s) circumstances + Read more ...

The student or Australian Apprentice's natural, adoptive or relationship parent(s), or if they have only one parent, that parent has been located.

The student or Australian Apprentice may be eligible for independence if their parent(s) are:

  • in gaol serving a prison sentence or remanded in custody for at least 14 days
  • mentally incapacitated for an indefinite period
  • living in a nursing home or similar institution for an indefinite period

Do any of the above apply?

  • Yes, see Step 1 in Table 3
  • No, go to Step 9

7

Parent(s) cannot be located + Read more ...

If the parent(s) cannot be located, the student or Australian Apprentice is eligible for independence due to their parent(s) being unable to exercise parental responsibilities.

If the parent(s) have a history of going missing, consider the period they have been uncontactable and the likelihood of return.

See Step 1 in Table 3.

8

Parent(s) deceased + Read more ...

The student or Australian Apprentice is not eligible for independence under the category their parents cannot exercise parental responsibilities. However, they may be independent as an orphan.

Consult with a social worker before discussing the death of a parent(s) with a student or Australian Apprentice.

If processed in:

  • Customer First, record details of decision in a DOC. The information must indicate the grounds the claim for independence has been rejected, and the reasons for the rejection. Procedure ends here
  • Process Direct, see Step 5 in Table 3

9

Parent(s) located - claim for UTLAH + Read more ...

The student or Australian Apprentice is not eligible for independence under this category. The claim for independence automatically becomes a claim for ABSTUDY unreasonable to live at home.

Ask them if Services Australia can contact a parent to discuss a UTLAH assessment.

Request a signed statement declaring it is unreasonable for them to live with their parent(s) along with permission for their contact details to be disclosed.

If processed in:

  • Customer First, record details of decision in a DOC. The information must indicate the grounds the claim for independence has been rejected, and the reasons for the rejection. Procedure ends here
  • Process Direct, see Step 5 in Table 3

Table 3: For ABSTUDY Smart Centre processing staff only.

Step

Action

1

Assessing eligibility + Read more ...

Check the evidence provided to determine if the student or Australian Apprentice meets eligibility for independence due to their parents being unable to exercise parental responsibilities.

Is there enough evidence to support granting independence due to their parents being unable to exercise parental responsibilities?

  • Yes, if processed in:
    • Customer First, go to Step 6
    • Process Direct, go to Step 2
  • No, if processed in:
    • Customer First, record details of decision in a DOC. The information must indicate the grounds the claim for independence has been rejected, and the reasons for the rejection. Procedure ends here
    • Process Direct, go to Step 5

2

Coding independence details in Process Direct + Read more ...

The independence data will prepopulate the Independent/Homeless/Away from Home Details (NIH) screen from information provided in the online claim.

Check the information on the NIH screen.

Key NIH in the Super Key or select the My Circs icon.

If the information has not pre-filled or needs changing, code manually. For example, an incorrect start date has been recorded.

If manual coding is required complete these fields:

  • Action: code 'IND'
  • Start/Reject Reason: code 'PPG'
  • Start Date: code the later of:
    • ABSTUDY start date
    • date the student or Australian Apprentice became independent

The student or Australian Apprentice and their partner must be linked to ensure the correct ABSTUDY rate is paid. See Change in relationship status from single to partnered.

3

Australian Apprentice + Read more ...

Is the customer an Australian Apprentice?

  • Yes, go to Step 8
  • No, go to Step 4

4

Relocation scholarship eligibility + Read more ...

For qualifying students, the Regional Location Details (RLD) screen will prepopulate information provided in the online claim. Check the information and dates on the RLD screen are correct.

For information on coding the RLD screen, see Relocation Scholarship (RS) payment and coding.

Go to Step 8.

5

Deleting independence details in Process Direct + Read more ...

If the student or Australian Apprentice does not meet the eligibility for independence due to their parents being unable to exercise parental responsibilities, delete the pre-filled details from the Independent/Homeless/Away from Home Details (NIH) screen.

To delete an entry:

  • Key NIH in the Super Key or select the My Circs icon
  • select the Delete icon to the left of the line where start/reject reason PPG is coded
  • select Yes to confirm deletion

Record details of the decision in Notes or Document annotations. Indicate the grounds for rejecting the claim for independence.

Procedure ends here.

6

Coding independence details in Customer First + Read more ...

Student or Australian Apprentice meets eligibility for independence due to their parents being unable to exercise parental responsibilities.

Complete the following fields on the Independent/Homeless/Away from Home Details (NIH) screen:

  • Action: code 'IND'
  • Start/Reject Reason: code 'PPG'
  • Start Date: code the later of:
    • ABSTUDY start date
    • date the student or Australian Apprentice became independent

If the student is studying an approved scholarship course, in the To update regional classification details, go to RLD: field, code Y.

For more information on coding the RLD screen, see Relocation Scholarship (RS) payment and coding.

7

Record decision in Customer First + Read more ...

Record details of decision in a DOC. The information must indicate the grounds the claim for independence has been granted.

If granted independence under these criteria and the period of their eligibility can be foreseen, set a manual review. For example, if there is the likelihood the parent(s) will be released from prison or discharged from a hospital or nursing home. See How to code and action a manual review.

Go to Step 9.

8

Record decision in Process Direct + Read more ...

Record details of the decision in Notes or Document annotations. Indicate the grounds for granting the claim for independence.

If independence under these criteria is likely to end, code a manual review. For example, if it is likely the parent(s) will be released from prison or discharged from a hospital or nursing home.

9

Determine if a current Child Support assessment exists + Read more ...

To check if a current Child Support assessment exists for the student or Australian Apprentice, see Maintenance Income Test (MIT) for ABSTUDY and Youth Allowance (YA).

Is there a current Child Support assessment for the student or Australian Apprentice?

  • Yes, go to Step 10
  • No, procedure ends here

10

Terminating event for Child Support send ACTDOC to CSJ + Read more ...

Send an ABSTUDY Child Support Update Fast Note. Select Auto text, use Students, ABSTUDY and Apprentices > Update > ABSTUDY Child Support Update.

Complete the details within the Fast Note and select Confirm, ensuring the Fast Note remains open.
