Action

1

A principal for Income Management purposes + Read more ...

If a payment nominee's payments are income managed under the following measures, the customer for whom they are payment nominee (the principal) will also have their payments income managed:

  • Cape York (CY) Initiative
  • Child Protection Income Management (CPIM)
  • Supporting People at Risk (SPaR), or
  • Compulsory Income Management (CIM) - Disengaged Youth (DEY) or Long Term Welfare Payment Recipient (LTWPR)

A principal for Income Management purposes will be income managed under the same measure as their payment nominee, unless they qualify for a different Income Management measure on their own. However, if a principal is no longer eligible for Income Management on their own, they will automatically transfer to Nominee Income Management (NIM).

Principals who are income managed under NIM are not required to meet the eligibility criteria for the measure applicable to their payment nominee.

2

Payment nominee arrangements + Read more ...

Work Items are created on an income managed customer's record when an activity is actioned and requires further investigation or a review is required. A nominee related Work Item will be generated to identify a customer who is income managed and:

  • is a payment nominee or becomes a payment nominee for another person
  • has a payment nominee or has a payment nominee appointed
  • their payment nominee arrangement ends

The following Work Items will appear on the Income Management Summary screen if the income managed customer:

  • is a payment nominee
    • I045Q1 - Customer on IM and is a pay nominee. Check principal's IM status
  • has a payment nominee whose payments are income managed
    • I005Q1 - IM customer's nominee arrangement ended. Please review IM
    • I044Q1 - Customer is on IM and has a payment nominee. Check IM status

3

Review Nominee arrangements + Read more ...

The payment nominee arrangement must be reviewed if:

  • the payment nominee is referred to any measure of Income Management
  • the principal is referred to the Cape York (CY) Initiative, Child Protection Income Management (CPIM), Supporting People at Risk (SPaR) or assessed/automatically identified as a Vulnerable Welfare Payment Recipient (VWPR)
  • at any time there is concern that the nominee arrangements are no longer appropriate
  • there is concern that the appointment of a payment nominee is an attempt to avoid Income Management

Record details on a DOC of the review of the payment nominee arrangement. See Reviewing nominee arrangements.

The payment nominee arrangement should be cancelled or revoked if not in the best interests of the principal. For coding, see Cancelling a nominee arrangement.

4

Manual Follow-up (MFU) activities + Read more ...

In addition to a Work Item, an INM/NIM MFU activity that cannot be cancelled will be created when:

  • a payment nominee starts or ceases Income Management
  • there is a change in status of the payment nominee's Income Management (that is, Auto/Manual)
  • there is a change in the measure under which the payment nominee is income managed
  • a payment nominee arrangement ends
  • an income managed customer has or appoints a payment nominee
  • an income managed customer becomes a payment nominee
  • there is a change to the payment nominee's IM percentage (Cape York Initiative only)

MFUs will be automatically completed or deleted once the Nominee IM Assessment workflow has been completed, or if it is no longer valid.

Only one INM/NIM MFU will exist at any time. If a new MFU is created when there is an existing MFU, the MFU with the higher priority will be created, and the other deleted.

MFUs should only be actioned by the Income Management Payment & Contact Team (IMPACT). For contact details, see Income Management Payment and Contact team (IMPACT).

Service Officers must investigate the principal's Income Management status and update any details on the Nominee IM Assessment screen or issue letters as appropriate.

Selecting the MFU will navigate to the Nominee IM Assessment workflow.

The Resources page contains a list of keywords and circumstances when an MFU will be created and what action is required.

6

Initial Assessment interview + Read more ...

If the payment nominee or principal (if applicable) require an interpreter, only continue with the Initial Assessment interview when an interpreter is present. See Booking an interpreter for an appointment

During the interview the following must be covered.

  • Explain Income Management and delivery of their payments
  • Discuss why Income Management is being put in place
  • Explain to the payment nominee they are responsible for managing the principal's Income Management funds
  • Explain what happens if the principal moves out of an Income Management area
  • Discuss how income managed money is accessed. For more information, see Coding Income Management expenses
  • Explain what income managed money can be used for example, School Meals Program (if applicable), food, rent/housing, utilities, clothing, and medical needs. See Determining a person's priority needs for Income Management
  • Explain how income managed money can assist in budgeting for larger purchases such as car registration, whitegoods, school fees
  • Explain what goods cannot be purchased from income managed funds (that is, prohibited goods and services - alcohol, home brew concentrate, home brew kits, tobacco products, pornographic material or gambling
  • Explain how money can be allocated to BasicsCard (issued to the payment nominee, or the principal at the instruction of the payment nominee) via regular and immediate allocations
  • Discuss priorities for Income Management expenditure. See Determining a person's priority needs for Income Management
  • If applicable, discuss converting existing Centrepay deductions to an income managed expense. Explain the impact of Income Management on any ongoing Centrepay deductions
  • Review and discuss any existing Rent Deduction Scheme payment and whether the customer would like to pay their State Housing Authority (SHA) rent from their income managed funds, their discretionary funds or a combination of both. See Determining a person's priority needs for Income Management for more information
  • Explain how money can be accessed for Income Management customers accessing funds while travelling
  • Explain how payment nominee and principal can contact Services Australia, including self service, in regards to Income Management and BasicsCard. See Income Management and BasicsCard self service options
  • Offer to register the payment nominee for self service
  • Explain how common changes in circumstances may affect Income Management and the importance of notifying Services Australia of any changes in the principal's expenses
  • Advise payment nominee of impact on expense payments if income support payments are reduced due to earnings from employment or other income
  • Explain the hierarchy of expense payments and the priority needs payment order when there are insufficient funds to cover an Income Management expense
  • Explain how lump sum payments such as advances and Family Tax Benefit (FTB) arrears and reconciliation payments are 100% income managed
  • Advise payment nominee and principle (if applicable) when Income Management ends, all Income Management expenses will stop. They will need to make arrangements to continue paying these expenses themselves, or contact the agency to discuss alternative payment options
  • Advise payment nominee and principal (if applicable) of their right to review Income Management decisions

Does the payment nominee require a BasicsCard to be issued? + Read more ...

2

Issue the BasicsCard + Read more ...

Issue the BasicsCard to the payment nominee (or the principal, if authorised by the payment nominee). See BasicsCard offer and issue of initial BasicsCard.

Check if the payment nominee (or principal if applicable) lives in an area serviced by a Remote Servicing Team (RST).

  • If they live in an area serviced by an RST:
    • check Office Locator (see the Resources page for a link)
    • advise the payment nominee the date and location of the next visit if available
  • If the payment nominee (or principal if applicable) does not live in an area serviced by an RST, advise them to attend their local Centrelink Service Centre to be issued with a BasicsCard

3

Issue letters + Read more ...

Letters are not automatically issued to principals and their payment nominees for Nominee Income Management (NIM) and must be issued manually.

All NIM letters must be issued by Income Management Payment and Contact Team (IMPACT) staff only.

When Service Officers complete the Nominee IM Assessment workflow and NIM is commend or ended, send an ACTDOC with the keyword ACTIMT to Region Code IMT, with the summary line 'NIM ltr required'.

IMPACT Service Officers will issue the applicable OLA letters to the principal and the payment nominee (where applicable) within standard timeliness standards. Nominee Income Management letters are:

  • Q745 - Manual On advice - Payment nominee
  • Q746 - Invitation to contact - Payment nominee
  • Q748 - Invitation to contact - Principal
  • Q749 - Manual On advice - Principal
  • Q793 - End advice - Principal and Correspondence Nominee
  • Q794 - End advice - Payment nominee
  • Q795 - Pre-exit advice - Principal
  • Q796 - Pre-exit advice - Payment nominee
  • Q797 - Change of Nominee Income Management percentage - Payment nominee
