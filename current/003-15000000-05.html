Information about exemptions for Child Support staff

Customers can apply for a full or partial exemption from taking reasonable maintenance action if pursuing child support places them and/or their family at risk of family and domestic violence.

Exemptions are granted by social workers and/or Indigenous Services Officers (ISO) and can be granted before the customer applies for child support or if an active child support case exists.

Circumstances where an exemption may be appropriate include:

  • a history of violence occurring between parties of a child support case
  • a paying parent has threatened the receiving parent and/or their immediate family (or another party to the child support case) with family and domestic violence if maintenance action for child support is pursued, or
  • if a customer fears that taking action for child support will result in another party to the child support case reacting with harm or violence towards them or their family

Note: customers who have been granted a full exemption and want to end their case must make an election to end the child support case.

If a partial exemption is granted for family and domestic violence reasons:

  • the customer is assessed (for FTB purposes) on the amount they are actually receiving instead of the amount assessed by Child Support
  • the customer is considered to be taking maintenance action
  • the case is placed into private collection, and
  • both customers continue to receive assessment notices

Common scenarios when identifying customers affected by family and domestic violence

Table 1

Item

Scenario

1

Customer applies for a payment/service and mentions recent family and domestic violence event

If the customer who applied for a payment/service mentions that a recent family and domestic violence event triggered them to leave the relationship, follow the Risk Identification and Referral (RIR) Model.

Acknowledgements may include the following:

  • 'It sounds like you were in a very tough situation, are you safe now?'
  • 'I'm glad that you were able to tell us about this today so we could discuss all your payment options'
  • 'It sounds like you have been through a lot, I have links to other services if you would like some additional support'

2

Customer applies for a payment/service and mentions that they have recently separated

If the customer advises that they "have never been allowed to deal with this sort of stuff", asking clarifying questions and the risk identification question will enable staff to appropriately acknowledge and assess the situation.

When family and domestic violence is a concern, referrals can be provided as per the Risk Identification and Referral (RIR) Model.

3

Customer queries why the agency is asking the risk identification question

If the customer asks 'Has someone said something?', a suggested response may include:

  • 'This question is asked to all our customers to make sure we understand their needs and provide the support they need.'

Or, if the customer asks 'Are you asking if they hit me or something?' a suggested response may include:

  • 'One of the behaviours that would put you at risk is physical violence, but we are also asking about emotional, financial, and other forms of abuse'

Note: it is important that staff use other descriptors to talk about family and domestic violence rather than the term itself. This is because many people affected by family and domestic violence do not identify with this term.

4

Customer mentions family and domestic violence during Interactive Voice Response (IVR) questioning

If the Customer Overview screen shows the banner Family violence indicated - offer referral options before the end of this call, make sure the customer's concerns are confirmed and acknowledged. A confirmation response may be:

  • 'I can see you have let us know that you have some family and domestic violence concerns, can I just check that you are safe?', or
  • 'When you called us today you let us know that that you have some family and domestic violence concerns, is that right? Would you like to discuss the payment and service options that are available?'

Common scenarios to help identified customers with family and domestic violence issues

Table 2

Item

Scenario

1

Customer, or someone else, has had to change accommodation due to violence

Customers may be eligible for a Crisis payment given their extreme circumstances when they request urgent financial help as:

  • they have had to move home due to family and domestic violence, or
  • an alleged or actual user of violence has been removed from the family home due to family and domestic violence

See Eligibility for Crisis Payment (CrP) and New claim for Crisis Payment under extreme circumstances - family and domestic violence.

2

Jobseeker is unable to search for work due to experiencing violence

Jobseekers who advise that they are unable to comply with their mutual obligation requirements as they are affected by family and domestic violence, or other similar extreme circumstances, may be able to apply for a temporary period of exemption.

See Exempting a job seeker from mutual obligation requirements due to special circumstances.

A social worker will decide on the grant and duration of the period using Service Profiling, see Principal Carer Social Worker Exemption Service Update for review of Domestic Violence or Relationship Breakdowns exemptions.

3

Customer is under 22 years and unable to live at home due to violence

Customers who are under 22 years who claim Youth Allowance (YA), Disability Support Pension (DSP) or Special Benefit (SpB) may be eligible for financial help if they are affected by family and domestic violence, or other similar extreme circumstances.

See Unreasonable to live at home (UTLAH) initial contact for Youth Allowance (YA), Disability Support Pension (DSP), Special Benefit (SpB) or Tertiary Access Payment (TAP).

4

Customer has recently moved to Australia and is unable to be supported by others due to violence

Customers, who have arrived as recent refugees or humanitarian entrants and are affected by family and domestic violence, face difficult and unique circumstances. Customers in this situation may be eligible for specialised and intensive case management support from Specialised and Intensive Services (SIS).

See Specialised and Intensive Services (SIS).

5

Customer is applying for or collecting child support and this poses a safety risk to themselves or their family

Customers wanting to apply, end, or who currently receive child support services and are affected by family and domestic violence may be eligible for an exemption from seeking child support.

See Exemptions from seeking child support for Family Tax Benefit (FTB) customers.

6

Customer wants to update a Medicare card

Customers who advise that their circumstances have changed as they are affected by family and domestic violence may need to update their Medicare card and/or any My Health Record nominated and/or authorised representative's details.

See Copy or transfer a person or child to another Medicare card to update any Medicare card details and for any changes to a customerâ€™s My Health Record they need to contact the My Health Record enquiry line.

7

Customer advises of a change of care as a result of a family and domestic violence situation

Customer advises of a change of care because of a family and domestic violence situation and there is concern that the customer or children will be placed at risk if the losing carer is contacted, evidence for change of care supplied by the gaining carer can be used to confirm the change of care. However, the delegate must be satisfied based on the available evidence that a change of care has occurred.

See Change of care for Family Tax Benefit (FTB) and Reassessment of Family Tax Benefit (FTB) for a current customer where an additional child enters care in the current or previous financial year.
