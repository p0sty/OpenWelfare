Making a referral to SEE

Table 1

Step

Action

1

Service Officer has been allocated a SEE related work item + Read more ...

The following work items are created when a job seeker is identified as potentially benefiting from a referral to Skills for Education and Employment (SEE) when the Job Seeker Snapshot is run, and the Service Officer confirms that the referral is required:

  • ZIAM_JSK_SEE_REF
  • ZCOC_JSK_SEE_REF

Note: make sure the job seeker meets the eligibility criteria before referring them.

Some work items (ZIAM) are allocated to Service Officers within Customer First, however, actioned in Process Direct. Service Officers should consider the information provided within the relevant Work Item on the Activity List (AL) screen in Customer First.

When the activity is actioned in Process Direct, Service Officers must return to Customer First and check the AL screen to make sure the Manual Follow Up (MFU) activity is completed.

See Customer First navigation, common screens and functions.

Where a job seeker requires an Interpreter to undertake the phone contact, see Contacting an on-demand telephone interpreter (Interpreter Connect).

If a JSR 1st attempt to contact Fast Note has already been recorded on the Document List, go to Step 2.

Service Officers allocated one of the above work items must contact the job seeker to discuss the referral. 2 genuine attempts to contact must be made. If the contact number recorded on the job seeker's record is a mobile number, and they have registered for SMS messaging, create a pre-call notification (SMS) only using Desktop Electronic Messaging Capability (DEMC). After sending the SMS, wait 1 or 2 minutes before making the outbound call attempt.

If the job seeker’s telephone details are not recorded, incorrect or outdated:

  • manually cancel the MFU activity from the AL screen in Process Direct, or
  • complete the MFU in Customer First

Was contact with the job seeker successful?

  • Yes, go to Step 3
  • No, hold the work item to the next calendar day, and:
    • record details about the outbound contact attempt using the JSR 1st attempt to contact Fast Note in Customer First so the next Service Officer can action the second contact attempt and finalise the activity
    • select the ‘Display on access’ box
    • check ‘OTH-Urgent free text message exists’ displays in the Reason field
    • the Expiry Date field should default to the maximum date allowed (60 days from today)
    • procedure ends here

2

Second outbound attempt to contact job seeker + Read more ...

Make a second genuine attempt to contact the job seeker if the first attempt was unsuccessful.

If the contact number recorded on the job seeker’s record is a mobile number, and they have registered for SMS messaging create a pre-call notification (SMS) only using Desktop Electronic Messaging Capability (DEMC). Following creation of the SMS, wait 1 or 2 minutes before making the outbound attempt.

Was the contact successful?

  • Yes, go to Step 3
  • No:
    • Close the JSR 1st attempt to contact Display on Access (DOA) DOC
    • Annotate the JSR 1st Attempt to contact DOC, stating that the 2nd attempt to contact was unsuccessful
    • As SEE is a voluntary program prior to the job seeker accepting a referral, manually cancel the MFU activity from the AL screen in Process Direct, or complete the MFU in Customer First
    • Procedure ends here

3

Determine if job seeker is Centrelink or provider managed + Read more ...

The Customer Information field on the Activity and Exemption Summary (AEX) screen shows if a customer is Centrelink or provider managed. Note: volunteers must be registered as volunteer job seekers before a referral can be made.

In Process Direct:

  • select Customer Summary from the Process Direct Landing page
  • key the customer's CRN, select Go
  • in the customer's record key 'AEX' in the Super Key, select Enter
  • the Activity and Exemptions Summary (AEX) will display

For:

  • Centrelink managed job seekers, go to Step 4
  • Provider managed job seekers, go to Step 5

4

Centrelink managed job seekers + Read more ...

Centrelink managed job seekers may also participate in SEE. An update to their Job Plan is not required as the job seeker will already be fully meeting requirements via another approved activity. SEE participation alone is not enough to meet the criteria to be Centrelink managed.

Explain the SEE programme to the job seeker, including potential entitlement to the:

  • Language Literacy and Numeracy Supplement (LLNS)
  • Pensioner Education Supplement (PES)

See Skills for Education and Employment (SEE).

The Service Officer must check the following screens in Process Direct before starting the referral process. This assists with determining eligibility:

Citizenship and Visa sub class:

  • key 'RSLEG' in the Super Key, select Enter
  • the Legal Residency Details screen will display

Permanent visa details:

  • key 'RSRD' in the Super Key, select Enter
  • the Residence Results Display screen will display

End date of the Newly Arrived Resident's Waiting Period (NARWP):

  • key 'RSPAP' in the Super Key, select Enter
  • the Period of Australian Presence screen will display

If the job seeker has mutual obligation requirements:

  • advise the job seeker of their obligation to continue to meet their mutual obligations as set out in their Job Plan while they participate in SEE, if assessed as suitable at the Pre-Training Assessment

Does the job seeker agree to a referral to SEE?

  • Yes:
    • Use the Create SEE/AMEP Referral workflow in Process Direct to refer them to SEE, and
    • Arrange a Pre-Training Assessment appointment with the SEE provider while the job seeker is present
    • See Table 2
  • No:
    • Manually cancel any SEE related MFU activities from the AL screen in Process Direct, or
    • Return to Customer First and check the AL screen to make sure the activity is completed
    • See Customer First navigation, common screens and functions
    • Procedure ends here

5

Provider managed job seekers + Read more ...

Customer is eligible for SEE.

If they are managed by a Workforce Australia Employment Services Provider:

  • tell the job seeker they are eligible to be referred to SEE. If the job seeker agrees to the referral, contact the job seeker's Employment Services Provider
  • discuss any impact on the requirements already in the Job Plan, if a referral is made to SEE. This discussion can be with any staff member of the provider organisation, not just the job seeker's Case Manager
  • if the provider has reasonable grounds to oppose a referral, tell the job seeker to contact their Case Manager. Do not proceed with the referral

If the provider agrees, continue with the referral:

  • obtain the jobseeker's consent to pass on their personal details to the SEE provider
  • use the Create SEE/AMEP Referral workflow in Process Direct to refer the job seeker to SEE

See Table 2.

If they are managed by Workforce Australia Online, tell them to contact the Digital Services Contact Centre and ask for a SEE Referral.

Procedure ends here.

3

Job seeker is not eligible for SEE + Read more ...

A job seeker is not eligible when they:

  • are in Self-Employment Assistance
  • are in Adult Migrant Education Program (AMEP)
  • have recently participated in SEE and the last program exit date is within 12 weeks of the next referral date:
    • for a new provider, the workflow will complete and create a DOC advising the date the job seeker can be referred back to SEE
    • for a previous provider, job seekers can contact the provider themselves to re-start SEE
    • for the same provider, the following error will display 'E409RR - Not Eligible, can be referred to SEE after <Due Date>'. A new referral cannot be completed before the specified date
  • have recently successfully completed SEE. This is shown by the latest SEE status reason of Lang/Lit/Num Successful on the Referral Summary (RRSUM) screen. Customers who have completed the program will be eligible again after a 12 week break
  • for other reasons, the workflow will complete and create a DOC with the text Customer is not eligible for SEE

Job seeker has changed address:

The job seeker can:

  • contact their previous provider to advise they have relocated and request their record be updated in the provider's system with Transfer to new provider. Once updated, a referral to the new provider is possible
  • wait until 12 weeks from their last exit date, then seek a new referral, or
  • resume with the previous SEE provider by contacting the provider directly

Job seeker wants to change SEE providers:

  • advise them to wait until 12 weeks from their last exit date, then seek a new referral

Finalise the transaction:

  • Select Next
  • The Referral Summary (RRSUM) screen will display a provisional line and list the referral ineligibility reason under the Status reason column
  • Select Next
  • The SAP Warnings and Errors (SWE) screen will display
  • Finalise the transaction by selecting Assess again, then select Finish. Record details of the update on the Finalise screen
  • Select Finalise to complete the transaction
  • ZIAM work items:
    • manually cancel any SEE related MFU activities from the AL screen in Process direct, or
    • return to Customer First and check the AL screen and make sure the activity is marked as completed
    • see Customer First navigation, common screens and functions

Procedure ends here.

4

No suitable providers are available + Read more ...

Ask the job seeker if they can attend a SEE provider at a nearby location. A job seeker may choose to undertake the program at another location due to:

  • transportation difficulties
  • a disability
  • caring responsibilities
  • cultural constraints
  • no accessible SEE provider

To search for other SEE providers in nearby locations, select Yes in Include nearby regions. The list expands to include SEE providers in surrounding regions.

If the job seeker wishes to choose a provider outside their surrounding region, select Provider Search to manually search by region code, postcode or town/suburb.

Is a referral to another suitable SEE provider an option for the job seeker?

  • Yes, go to Step 5
  • No, when there are no suitable service providers available for the job seeker, a service gap is to be recorded:
    • From the Record service gap field, select Yes
    • Select a service gap reason from the drop down list options. When this option is selected, the list of service providers is disabled
    • Select Next to continue the workflow
    • Select Yes to Are you sure you want to record the service gap?
    • Select Next and address any errors/warnings
    • Finalise the transaction by selecting Assess, then select Finish. Record details of the update on the Finalise screen
    • Select Finalise to complete the transaction
    • ZIAM work items:
      manually cancel any SEE related MFU activities from the AL screen in Process direct, or
      return to Customer First and check the AL screen and make sure the activity is marked as completed
      see Customer First navigation, common screens and functions

Procedure ends here.

5

Suitable provider is available + Read more ...

Ask the job seeker if they are interested in undertaking SEE by distance education, if the suitable provider list includes distance education providers. If not, only select local providers.

To search for a provider:

  • complete any of the Search criteria fields and select Search Providers
  • select the appropriate Service provider in the Service Providers table

Is a referral to a distance education provider being made?

  • Yes, go to Step 6
  • No, go to Step 7

6

Referral to distance education provider + Read more ...

Job seekers may choose to undertake SEE by distance learning.

For all SEE courses through a distance education provider, job seekers require:

  • a referral from Services Australia or an Employment Services Provider, and
  • Pre-Training Assessment appointment with the distance education provider to assess language, literacy and numeracy needs

Services Australia initiated referral to a distance education provider:

  • Select TAFE SA (Distance) provider from the Service Provider table
  • Make sure the job seeker consents to the disclosure of their relevant personal details
  • Advise the job seeker that the provider will contact them to arrange a Pre-Training Assessment appointment either by online, telephone interview and/or mailed response

Job seeker initiated referral to a distance education provider

If the job seeker has already contacted TAFE SA (Distance) and requires a referral from Services Australia:

  • confirm the job seeker is eligible for SEE
  • contact the distance education provider using the contact details located in the Referral Summary after the referral has been completed

For further details about the SEE distance education provider, see the Resources page.

Go to Step 7.

7

Make referral to SEE + Read more ...

Select the job seeker's preferred provider from the Service Provider table.

The Create referral screen will display.

Complete the following fields using the drop down menu:

  • Channel Code - the method the job seeker used to contact Services Australia
  • Requested by - the person/organisation requesting the referral (for example, Services Australia, Employment Services Provider)
  • Reason for referral - the reason why the job seeker has been referred to this Service Type
  • Due Date - this automatically populates

The Referral Status table shows:

  • Status - Provisional is displayed, as the initial referral status until referral is completed
  • Action date of effect - the date the referral was created
  • Status type code - the reasons for a provisional referral status will be displayed
  • Elig check for the eligibility check
  • Referred for the pending referral
  • Status reason:
    • Awaiting contact, this is for the service provider to contact the customer
    • Eligibility check for skills for education and employment displays until the service provider has completed assessment of eligibility for the SEE course
  • If a referral is being made to a SEE provider outside the job seeker's region, use Edit under Action and choose the appropriate Status reason from the following options:
    • Client Preference
    • Family Reasons
    • No Service Available
    • Significant Disability
    • Travel Distance
  • Only the service provider can be changed by selecting Previous and selecting a new provider before confirming the referral. Status type code and Status reasons are only able to be altered by staff with specific access
  • Select Next. The workflow returns to the Referral Summary showing the provisional record just created
  • Select Next and address any issues on the SWE screen
  • Finalise the transaction by selecting Assess, then select Finish. Record details of the update on the Finalise screen
  • Select Finalise to complete the transaction

8

Arrange a Pre-Training Assessment appointment with the SEE Provider + Read more ...

For all referrals to a local provider, arrange an appointment with the SEE provider for the Pre-Training Assessment. This is mandatory for all non-distance education SEE referrals.

Participation in the SEE Pre-training assessment and training is compulsory if assessed as suitable for the program. Make sure the job seeker understands the compliance consequences if they do not attend their Pre-Training Assessment appointment.

Service Officers should not discuss the job seeker's circumstances with the provider. Only disclose enough information to book the appointment.

  • Check if the job seeker has a silent phone number. If they do, seek the job seeker’s consent to give the number to the provider over the phone, as the referral system will exclude it from the online referral
  • Phone the provider and make a Pre-Training Assessment appointment using the contact details located in the Referral Summary after the referral has been completed
  • Tell the job seeker of the:
    • appointment date
    • appointment time
    • appointment location, and
    • consequences of not attending or to contact the SEE provider to reschedule their appointment if they are unable to attend
