Assessment of asset value of Home Equity Conversion loans and Reverse Mortgages 108-04100020

This page contains information on the assessment of asset value of Home Equity Conversion (HEC) loans and reverse mortgages.

Information on HEC

Identifying when to apply the HEC provisions

Table 1: This table describes the steps to follow when a customer is thinking about borrowing money against the equity in their or partner's principal home and how to identify when to apply the HEC loans.

Step

Action

1

Borrowing money against equity in their or their partner's principal home? + Read more ...

Is the customer thinking about borrowing money against the equity in their or their partner's principal home?

  • Yes, refer the customer to the Financial Information Service (FIS) Officer. Procedure ends here
  • No, go to Step 2

2

Purchase or investment property? + Read more ...

Has the customer borrowed money against, or for the purchase of an investment property?

  • Yes, HEC provisions do not apply. See Loans and liabilities against assets. Procedure ends here.
  • No, go to Step 3

3

Money borrowed against Principal home + Read more ...

Has the customer borrowed money using their principal home as security or secured a mortgage against their or their partner's principal home?

  • Yes, go to Step 4
  • No, HEC provisions do not apply. Clarify what the customer has done and action accordingly. Procedure ends here

4

What was the mortgage obtained for? + Read more ...

Was the mortgage obtained to purchase the customer's or their partner's principal home?

  • Yes, HEC provisions do not apply. Clarify what the customer has done and action accordingly. Procedure ends here
  • No, go to Step 5

5

What type of loan was obtained? + Read more ...

Confirm with the customer that the loan is a Reverse mortgage or an actual HEC Loan agreement, and not a home equity loan, by requesting or sighting documents that show:

  • the date the mortgage was secured
  • the amount secured
  • how and when the loan will be disposed of

Has the customer provided the required documents?

  • Yes, go to Step 6
  • No, tell the customer what documents are required and make a request for information

Note: usually a HEC loan or Reverse mortgage is not repayable until the homeowner moves out of their home or dies. If in doubt, contact a FIS officer for assistance.

6

What is the loan agreement? + Read more ...

Is the loan agreement a HEC Loan agreement or Reverse mortgage?

  • Yes, see Step 1 in Information on HEC or Reverse Mortgage table
  • No, HEC provisions do not apply, clarify what the customer has done and action accordingly. Procedure ends here

System coding

Information on HEC or Reverse mortgage

Table 2: This table describes the process if the customer has spent or dispensed the HEC or Reverse mortgage money. It also contains the details required in the specific fields on the relevant screens.

Step

Action

1

Has the loan been spent or dispensed? + Read more ...

Has the customer spent or dispensed with any of the HEC loan or Reverse mortgage money?

  • Yes, go to Step 2
  • No, go to Step 3

2

Obtain details if the loan money has been spent + Read more ...

Where the customer has spent any of the loan money, details of how the customer has spent the money is required. For example, if the customer has gifted any money, code the details of the gift on the Gifted/Deprived Asset (GIFT) screen. If the customer purchased a motor vehicle, code the details of this purchase on the Other Assets (OAS) screen.

  • Code asset(s) on the relevant screen(s)
  • Record details on a DOC

Does the customer have any HEC loan money that has not been spent or used yet?

  • Yes, go to Step 3
  • No, record details on a DOC. Procedure ends here

3

The loan amount over $40,000 and has not been spent? + Read more ...

Is the amount of the loan that has not been spent yet (excluding charges and interest) more than $40,000?

  • Yes, go to Step 5
  • No, go to Step 4

4

The loan amount is less than $40,000 and has not been spent? + Read more ...

The loan amount that has not been spent yet, is less than $40,000.

The first $40,000 of a home equity conversion loan or Reverse mortgage agreement that has not been spent, is an exempt asset for 90 days only but is deemed during this time.

  • Advise the customer to notify when they have spent any of the loan
  • If after 90 days the customer has not spent the loan, the amount becomes an assessable asset

Code a review with reason of 'HEC' for 90 days to code the loan as an asset if the customer has not spent the money before this.

Code the loan amount on the Direct Investments (SVDI) screen. Go to Step 6.

5

The loan amount that has not been spent is more than $40,000 + Read more ...

The amount of the loan that has not been spent, that is, in excess of the first $40,000, is an assessable asset.

The full amount is coded on the Direct Investments (SVDI) screen with the first $40,000 coded as an asset value exempt amount for 90 days only. The whole amount is deemed during this time.

Tell the customer to notify when they have spent any of the HEC loan.

If after 90 days a customer has not spent the loan, the full amount becomes an assessable asset.

6

Coding the Direct Investments (SVDI) screen + Read more ...

  • Code the name of the organisation and the name of the investment in the Institution Name: field
  • Code the account number for the investment in the Account Number: field
  • Code the type of investment 'LON' in the Type: field
  • Code the full balance drawn down, minus any amount already spent, of the HEC loan or Reverse mortgage in the Balance: field
  • If the savings account is owned in conjunction with the customer's partner, code the percent owned by the customer and their partner in the %Owned Client/Partner: field
  • Code the date of event in the Event Date: field
  • Code the date the direct investment was made in the Investment Date: field
  • Code the amount (up to $40,000) that is an exempt asset in the Assets Test Deduction Amt $: field
  • Code the source of the information in the Source: field
  • Code the date of receipt of the information in the DOR: field
  • Press [Enter]

The Direct Investment Summary (SVIS) screen displays showing the updated savings.

Complete the activity on the Assessment Results (AR) screen.

Note: as the loan is spent, the balance must be updated.

7

Coding a review + Read more ...

Code a review with a review reason of 'HEC' for 90 days to code the loan as an asset if the customer has not spent the money before this.

This is to code the loan as an asset by deleting any amount in the Assets Test Deduction Amt: field on SVDI if the customer has not spent the money prior.

Record details on a DOC.

8

Coding the spent loan + Read more ...

As the loan is spent, code the amount spent, off the SVDI screen and code any assets purchased or gifts on the relevant screen.

9

Coding if the money has not been spent after 90 days + Read more ...

After 90 days update any money not yet spent on the SVDI screen by zeroing the amount in the Assets Test Deduction Amt: field and the full amount being displayed as an asset.

Record details on a DOC.
