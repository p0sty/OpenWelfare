This page contains information on the Low Income Health Care Card (LIC) and the eligibility criteria for this concession card.

Eligibility for LIC

This table describes how to check if a customer was in receipt of an income support payments when the last Health Care Card/Pensioner Concession Card was issued and whether the customer is entitled to a Health Care Card.

Step

Action

1

Current income support customer? + Read more ...

Is the customer is in receipt of an income support payment?

  • Yes, go to Step 2
  • No, go to Step 3

2

Customer should receive a Health Care Card (HCC) or Pensioner Concession Card (PCC) with current payment + Read more ...

Access customer's record, go to the Concession Card Issue Summary (CCIS) screen and check when last HCC/PCC was issued. Advise the customer of the outcome. The customer is entitled to request issue of non-priority concession cards, e.g. LIC or Family Tax Benefit HCC.

If a replacement card is required, customers can request one by using the Request a document option online. If customers are unable to request online, issue a replacement card if required. For example, the card was not issued in the last 14 days. See How to reissue concession cards.

If no replacement is requested, go to Step 6.

3

Carer Allowance (CA) customer + Read more ...

Was the customer a CA customer the day before their 16th birthday?

  • Yes, see Ex-Carer Allowance (child) Health Care Card (EHC). Procedure ends here
  • No, go to Step 4

4

FTB child + Read more ...

Is the customer a secondary student under the age of 19 years who is a Family Tax Benefit child or Regular Care Child?

  • Yes, the customer is not qualified for a LIC in their own right, but may be included on their parent's card
  • No, go to Step 5

5

Customer in receipt of Veterans' Children Education Scheme or Postgraduate Awards Scheme + Read more ...

Is customer receiving a prescribed education payment that is Veterans' Children Education Scheme (VCES) or Postgraduate Awards Scheme?

  • Yes, see Step 3 of the Assessing and coding LIC claim table on the Customer First tab of Processing Low Income Health Care Card (LIC) claims
  • No, go to Step 6

6

Advise customer of LIC qualifications + Read more ...

Note: this also includes customers getting or claiming a Disaster Income Recovery Subsidy (DIRS).

Qualifications include that the customer must:

  • Be in Australia in order to lodge a valid claim
  • Meet the residence requirements for LIC
  • Meet the LIC Income Test under section 1071A of the Social Security Act 1991. See the Resources page for further information regarding income limits

Dependants must meet certain age qualifications to be included on their parent's LIC. See Dependents listed on a customerâ€™s concession card.

Does the customer have an automatic issued Concession card?

  • Yes, do they want to continue the claim for a LIC?
    • Yes, go to Step 7
    • No, reject the claim - Client withdraws claim. Procedure ends here
      If customer cannot be contacted to confirm withdrawal, go to Step 7
  • No, go to Step 7

7

Claim process + Read more ...

Advise customer they may:

  • claim LIC online, or
  • run the First Contact Concessions, Carers and Seniors (FCCCS) workflow. This will issue a customised Claim for a Low Income Health Care Card (SS487)

If the customer prefers, they may claim LIC using the form Application for a Health Care Card (SS050).

For more information on the claim process, documents required and coding, see Claiming LIC and Processing LIC claims.
