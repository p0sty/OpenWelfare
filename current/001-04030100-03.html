Qualification for YA + Read more ...

To qualify for payment a YA (job seeker) must:

  • be of qualifying age for YA (job seeker)
  • meet Australian residence requirements for YA
  • be unemployed, or:
    • unable to look for work due to a medical condition, illness or injury, or
    • employed and are unable to undertake these due to a medical condition, illness or injury and have a job or study to return to
  • be prepared to negotiate and enter into a Job Plan if required to do so, or comply with, or renegotiate an existing Job Plan
  • not be receiving ABSTUDY Living Allowance
  • satisfy the requirements of their Job Plan

Customers under 22 years of age and who have not completed Year 12 (or equivalent) or a Certificate III or higher, are identified as Early School Leavers. They are required to undertake approved activities and/or job search in order to qualify for payment of YA.

Is the customer eligible for YA or will they become eligible within 13 weeks of the date of lodgement?

  • Yes, go to Step 10
  • No, reject the claim. Go to Table 2

10

Determine if YA is payable + Read more ...

The customer must meet the payability provisions for YA (or meet the provisions within 13 weeks of the date of claim). Base the decision on the following:

  • Personal/Partner Income Test. For income limits, see Rates and Thresholds
  • Parental Income Test (PIT). If PIT reduces the rate to nil from the date of claim, check if YA would be payable from 1 January using base tax year income. If so, contact the customer to confirm they wish to be assessed from 1 January in the following calendar year
  • Away from home and accommodation circumstances
  • Independence, including unreasonable to live at home (UTLAH)
  • Portability provisions
  • Compensation preclusion

Is the customer payable now, or will be within 13 weeks of the date of claim?

  • Yes, go to Step 11
  • No, reject the claim. Go to Table 2

Note: if a UTLAH assessment is in progress, do not reject the claim FSD (failed to supply documents). Place the claim on hold pending the outcome of the UTLAH assessment.

Bank accounts and balance verification + Read more ...

Customers claiming YA supply details of all bank accounts they hold, including balances, as part of the claim and make a legal declaration the information is correct. Evidence of bank account balances is not required.

If the customer has claimed online and the claim was started prior to 5 August 2023, the online claim may have a Required task for proof of bank accounts. This is not required and evidence does not need to be reviewed or requested.

Bank balance information is collected to confirm income and asset details and assess any waiting periods.

Only request bank balance verification where:

  • there is doubt about the validity of the information provided in the claim and the service officer anticipates a different claim outcome
  • the balances declared in the claim indicate the customer may be experiencing severe financial hardship, and
  • waiver to a Liquid Assets Waiting Period (LAWP) or Ordinary Waiting Period (OWP) can be considered

Bank account details may be pre-populated into an online claim. Verbal confirmation of bank account balances is acceptable if the Service Officer has no doubt of the validity of the information provided.

Insufficient supporting evidence + Read more ...

If the evidence supplied by the customer is insufficient to assess the claim additional supporting evidence is required. Where further information is required to grant or reject the claim, make one genuine attempt to contact by phone.

Issue a pre-call notification (SMS only) between the hours of 8:00 am and 8:00 pm customer's local time, to advise a Service Officer will call them today from a private number. See Centrelink letters online and Electronic Messaging.

Contact is successful tell the customer:

  • the evidence does not meet the requirements
  • to upload the evidence via the requested task in the online claim on the day of the call. This will allow their claim to be processed as soon as possible. See Upload documents service
  • the claim will be placed on hold for one day
  • the claim will be rejected if they do not provide the evidence

Place the claim on hold for one day and annotate the Progress Of Claim (POC) DOC, with the contact outcome. Procedure end here.

Contact is unsuccessful or the customer is unable to supply the extra information or evidence immediately, issue request for information via > Request Documents. For further information, see Circumstance Change Monitor (CCM).

Do not request documents that have already been requested at Next Step in the claim, unless further clarification of the customer circumstance is needed for the claim assessment.

Claim rejection + Read more ...

Before rejecting a customer's claim, check the customer is not vulnerable, at risk, or in crisis.

Calculate the start date for payment + Read more ...

For a new claim where the customer was not previously on a payment the system will automatically calculate the start date.

Discretion is required when changing the system calculated start date.

The system will apply the backdating rule and calculate the date of commencement as Date Paid To (DPT)+1, even if the date of the claim is within 14 days of DPT.

When the cancellation date is outside 14 days of the claim date, do not apply DPT+1. The correct start date for these claims is the date the customer reclaims payment.

To correct the start date where system has applied an incorrect date

Where the correct date should be after the DPT+1, code the correct start date in the Manual Start Date field on the NDC screen

Where the start should be before the DPT+1 check the following:

  • will the customer be paid for a period in which they were previously receiving a payment
  • was the previous payment cancelled as the customer was not eligible, resulting in a debt being raised

Once checks are completed, apply the following to correct the date:

  • do not code a Manual Start Date field on the NDC screen. This will cause the start date to overlap with the previous payment dates, which may:
    • prevent the claim from granting properly, or
    • lead to a data corruption on the record
  • pay the days the customer was entitled to prior to the DPT+1, as a one-off payment
  • code the DPT+1 date in the Manual Start Date field on the NDC screen, and
  • record a DOC detailing the steps taken and include the reason for the start date determination

Early Claims

For early claims, the date in the DLW/DCS field will be after the date of receipt/submission of the claim.

In some cases, the start date may not be automatically determined due to the DLW being prior to the DOR.

To code the start date in these circumstances:

  • check the automatically calculated start date on NDC
  • consider any waiting periods
  • if required, code the Manual Start Date on the NDC screen

Principal carers and shared care + Read more ...

A shared care assessment should be completed in all cases, even if only one person is claiming income support or if each person has another qualifying child solely in their care.

The shared care assessment will determine:

  • the principal carer status
  • customer's entitlement
  • rate and income test applied in some circumstances, to the ongoing entitlements for single principal carers

Customers with shared care arrangements who are not the principal carer may still be entitled to a 'with child' rate of payment for JSP, YA and Special Benefit (SpB).

If a change of care has occurred or a determination of care is required, see Notification and assessment of shared care arrangements for Family Tax Benefit (FTB).

For claims with shared care where the determination has not been confirmed, the claim must be placed on hold.

A principal carer is a person with primary care of a dependent child under 16 years. A principal carer assessment must be made if one or both customers caring for a child are claiming income support as a principal carer.

A step parent may be a principal carer if they live as a member of a couple with the parent of the child.

The system does not always automatically cease the Principal Carer status on the customer's file. If the file shows there are no children under the age of 16 in the customer's care, check that the Principal Carer status has been ended on the Principal Carer Circumstance Details (PCCD) screen.

Member of a couple with a dependent child in care:

  • over the age of 6 years
    • Only one member of a couple can be a principal carer at one time
    • If both claim or get a social security payment based on or affected by principal carer status, they must be advised in writing who the principal carer is
  • under the age of 6 years
    • Only one member of a couple can be a principal carer at one time
    • Continue to process the claim. Once the claim is finalised, see Child Override/Claim (CHOC) screen, Step 3 for coding of the PCCD and CHOC screens

Is the customer a principal carer or shared care assessment completed?

  • Yes, go to Step 18
  • No, go to Step 19

See Principal carer of a dependent child.
