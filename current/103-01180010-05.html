Scenarios - Manual and automatic identification for Vulnerable Welfare Payment Recipient (VWPR) Income Management

This table has scenarios of customers who live in a VWPR Income Management area who may be referred or automatically identified for the VWPR measure, and when social worker involvement may occur.

Item

Scenario

1

Job seeker has no money for food + Read more ...

Bill is a job seeker aged 23 years on JobSeeker Payment (JSP) and has been on payment for the last 8 weeks.

Bill lives with his grandfather who has a gambling problem.

Bill attends the local Alice Springs Service Centre to apply for an urgent payment as his grandfather has stolen his wallet. Bill tells the Service Officer he has not eaten in 2 days because he does not have any money for food.

A Display on Access DOC indicates Bill has a history of applying for urgent payments due to his wallet being stolen. The Service Officer is concerned Bill's grandfather may be stopping Bill from having enough money to pay for his essential expenses.

The benefits of volunteering for Income Management are explained to Bill but he is not interested. Bill is offered assistance from a social worker.

The social worker meets with Bill and discusses a number of strategies to improve his situation. After considering all options available, the social worker determines Income Management would be the most beneficial for Bill and he is assessed as a Vulnerable Welfare Payment Recipient. Bill is placed on VWPR Income Management for 12 months.

2

Customer owes rent to Housing Authority + Read more ...

Jodie is receiving the Disability Support Pension (DSP) and rents a unit from the State Housing Authority.

Jodie is 3 months in arrears with her rental payments and the State Housing Authority has sent a referral to Services Australia for Jodie to be assessed under the Vulnerable Welfare Payment Recipient measure.

The social worker meets with Jodie and discusses various options. Jodie was not assessed as a Vulnerable Welfare Payment Recipient by the social worker, but regular Centrepay deductions were set up to pay her arrears and her ongoing rent commitments.

3

Unreasonable to Live at Home (UTLAH) payment granted + Read more ...

Adrian is a 17 year old on Youth Allowance (YA) who has been granted the UTLAH rate of payment.

An automatic trigger will occur to include Adrian on the VWPR Youth measure under the UTLAH category.

Adrian contacts the local Service Centre extremely distressed and says he does not want to be on Income Management. He pays rent to friends and is about to become homeless because they do not want to have anything to do with Services Australia.

The Service Officer refers the customer to a social worker because he may be eligible for an exclusion as Income Management may be detrimental to his wellbeing.

4

Crisis Payment on prison release + Read more ...

One week ago, 22 year old Janine was released from prison. Janine attended her local Service Centre to finalise her JobSeeker Payment (JSP) claim and was granted a Crisis Payment due to her prison release.

An automatic trigger will occur to include Janine on the VWPR Youth measure under the CrP category.

5

16 year old granted Special Benefit + Read more ...

Alannah is a 15 year old who has been granted Special Benefit (SpB) under 16 years old (SpB - USY).

An automatic trigger will occur to include Alannah on the VWPR Youth measure under the SpB category.

6

VWPR social worker assessed - Notice ends + Read more ...

John has been on JobSeeker Payment (JSP) for 14 months. Twelve months ago, a social worker determined John to be a Vulnerable Welfare Payment Recipient and John has been income managed for the last 12 months.

When the Notice is due to end, the social worker reviews John's circumstances and decides not to extend the Vulnerable notice.

However, as John meets the criteria under the Long Term Welfare Payment Recipient (LTWPR) measure, John will be identified by the system as eligible for Income Management under that measure once the Vulnerable notice ends.

John will be sent a letter requesting him to contact Services Australia to arrange an Initial Assessment interview under the new measure.

7

VWPR Youth (UTLAH) - customer no longer a full time student + Read more ...

Jill receives Youth Allowance (YA) at the UTLAH rate and was automatically identified for VWPR Youth (UTLAH) Income Management.

Two months after commencing Income Management, Jill became a full time student and her Income Management ended.

Four months after Income Management ended, Jill dropped out of her studies.

As it has been less than 12 months since Jill first commenced Income Management, it will be reinstated for the remainder of the 12 month period.

Note: this scenario also applies to customers placed on VWPR Youth (UTLAH and SpB) Income Management where it has not been 12 months since Income Management first commenced:

  • they stop being an apprentice (only when they commenced the apprenticeship after Income Management commenced)
  • an exclusion is manually or automatically ended before the end of the 12 month period
  • ineligible coding for reasons IIE (Incorrectly identified as eligible) or EPN (Excluded payment nominee) is removed
  • the higher measure to which they were transferred ends

The customer only needs to be receiving a Category H welfare payment, and does not need to be living in a declared area, for Income Management to be reinstated.

8

VWPR Youth (CrP) - customer becomes a full time student + Read more ...

Jack receives JobSeeker Payment (JSP) and was automatically identified for VWPR Youth (CrP) Income Management as he was granted a Crisis Payment (CrP) on his release from prison.

Three months after commencing Income Management, Jack became a full time student and Income Management was ended.

Two months after Income Management ended, Jack dropped out of his studies.

Although it has been less than 12 months since Jackâ€™s Income Management first commenced, Income Management is not reinstated as it has been more than 13 weeks since he received the CrP which made him eligible for Income Management.

Note: if the CrP which made them eligible for the Income Management period was paid within the last 13 weeks, VWPR Youth (CrP) Income Management is reinstated when:

  • the customer is no longer a full time student or apprentice (where they became a full time student/apprentice after commencing Income Management)
  • an exclusion is manually or automatically ended
  • ineligible coding is removed for reasons EPN (Excluded payment nominee)
  • the higher measure to which they were transferred ends

The customer does not need to be living in a declared area or under 25 for Income Management to be reinstated.
