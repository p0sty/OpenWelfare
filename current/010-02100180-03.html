Non-student travel process

FAA eligibility + Read more ...

To assess a travel request for a non-student traveller, eligibility to FAA must be established for the student. See Assessing eligibility for Fares Allowance (FAA) for ABSTUDY.

Has eligibility for FAA been determined?

  • No, see Step 8 in Table 2

2

What type of non-student travel is being considered? + Read more ...

ABSTUDY Fares Allowance (FAA) may be paid for a person other than the student.

When the travel request is for:

  • a supervisor, go to Step 3
  • dependants of a student not undertaking an Away from Base activity, go to Step 5
  • dependant/s of an Away from Base (AFB) student, go to Step 7
  • dependants of an approved supervisor, go to Step 8
  • family (including dependents of parents/guardians) or community members of the student, see Step 1 in Table 2
  • travelling companion (including a service animal) for ill, injured or disabled student, see Step 4 in Table 2
  • education institution representatives, see Step 5 in Table 2
  • a response received from the Level 2 Policy Helpdesk go to Step 10

3

Student/group suitable to be supervised + Read more ...

Is the student/group of students studying at a secondary school, and/or under 18 years of age?

  • Yes, go to Step 4
  • No, see Step 8 in Table 2

4

Supervisor travel approval + Read more ...

The supervisor must be a:

  • parent/guardian
  • family member
  • community representative, or
  • representative of the student's school or hostel

All travel types are available for a supervisor.

The supervisor is eligible for one return journey between their permanent home address and the student's study location for each approved occasion. A supervisor may also elect to travel one way and terminate their journey at the destination.

For group travel, the ratio of supervisors to students is assessed separately for each journey. Consideration is given to the student's circumstances and the circumstances of the journey. In general, the rule is one supervisor is allowed for every 6 students.

As the approved traveller, the nominated supervisor is the only person entitled to undertake the journey. If another person undertakes part or all of the journey, the nominated supervisor will be marked as a No Show for that travel. Penalties and overpayments may be applied to the nominated supervisor. See Step 6 in Table 2

5

Conditions for approving student’s dependants' travel + Read more ...

Dependants' travel may only be approved if:

  • the student is eligible for Parenting Payment Single (PPS),
  • the student's partner is eligible for Parenting Payment Partnered (PPP),
  • the student or student's partner:
    • holds a Health Care Card (HCC), a Low Income Health Care Card (LIC) or Pensioner Concession Card (PCC) and
    • has a dependent child

Is one (or more) of the above conditions met?

  • Yes, go to Step 6
  • No, go to Step 9

6

The student's dependants' entitlements + Read more ...

The types of travel entitlement available for dependant travellers are:

  • travel at the commencement and end of study
  • orientation and special purpose visit travel
  • school vacation travel
  • travel on compassionate grounds

Other types of specified travel may be approved for dependent travellers in exceptional circumstances. This is assessed on a case by case basis.

Dependants are normally expected to travel with the student but may use FAA to travel separately (for example to travel to the place of study after the student has found accommodation).

Note: the student is not eligible for mid-year travel if the dependants have travelled to the place of study.

Is the dependent an approved traveller for one of the above travel types?

  • Yes, see Step 6 in Table 2
  • No, and:
    • assessment for different travel type is required, escalate to Level 2 Policy Helpdesk, go to Step 9
    • dependent is not considered an approved traveller, See Step 8 in Table 2.

7

Travel for dependent/s of an Away from Base (AFB) student + Read more ...

Dependents of an approved student traveller, travelling for an approved Away from Base activity can access ABSTUDY Additional Assistance in lieu of Fares Allowance to have their travel approved in certain circumstances.

Additional Assistance for an AFB student’s dependent can be approved for up to a maximum of 2 dependants per student. Additional Assistance include fares, meals and reasonable additional accommodation costs (for example, cot hire or additional bed fee). Other costs, including childcare, are the responsibility of the student. In exceptional circumstances, where a student requires additional support for their dependant, escalate these instances to DSS.

For the dependent to be approved, the student must meet the following circumstances:

  • there is no appropriate alternative care for AFB student’s dependant and
  • there are alternative caring arrangements for the dependant while the student participates in the AFB activity, and:
    • the AFB student’s dependant is under school age, or
    • the AFB student’s dependant is vulnerable, or
    • the AFB student is receiving Parenting Payment Single (PPS), and
  • the travel of the dependant would support the student to attend the AFB activity

Are the above conditions met?

  • Yes, the dependent is approved, see Step 6 in Table 2
  • No, go to Step 9

8

Travel for dependent/s of a supervisor + Read more ...

If a child less than 2 years of age is required to travel with a parent/guardian, the child is considered an approved dependant traveller. The Airline does not charge a seat ticket for the child, the child is added onto the itinerary and travels on the ticket of the parent/guardian.

Additional Assistance for a supervisor’s dependent can be approved for up to a maximum of 2 dependants per supervisor.

To approve travel for a dependant over 2 years of age for an approved supervisor all of the following must be met:

  • the supervisor is the student's:
    • parent/guardian or community representative (including a family member), or
    • private board provider
  • there is no alternative supervisor for the student
  • there is no other care for the supervisor’s dependent/s
  • the supervisor’s dependent/s are under school age
  • it would be unreasonable to use the student's entitlements to Special Purpose Visit Travel

This does not apply if the dependent traveller is included in the reasonable amount of travellers for the event and the travel reason is one of the following:

  • NAIDOC week events
  • graduation events
  • the student is experiencing serious problems with adjustment or similar circumstances

Are the above conditions met?

  • Yes, the dependent is approved, see Step 6 in Table 2
  • No, go to Step 9

9

Conditions not met for approval of dependents' travel + Read more ...

Where the dependent did not meet the outlined conditions, they are not considered an approved dependent traveller of the student or non-student traveller. Travel or Away From Base Service Officers can request approval for these dependent travellers by escalating the request to the Level 2 Policy Helpdesk via LPS for assessment.

Ensure the escalation includes the following template and any supporting documents provided by the approved traveller:

We are seeking approval for dependent child (NAME) to travel with their parent guardian.

Summary of case

  • Travel Date:
  • Approved Travellers:
  • Travel type:
  • Travel destinations:
  • Number of nights:
  • Additional Costs for ACM/MEA: Yes/No
  • ABSTUDY Award of student:
  • Permanent Home:
  • Study Location:
  • Unapproved Traveller/s and age:

For dependents of AFB students also include the following details in the summary of case:

  • Dependent is:
    • Under school leaving age
    • Vulnerable
    • None of the above
  • AFB student is:
    • in receipt of PPS
    • not in receipt of a PPS

Reason for request

Note: include team leader as an additional contact in the escalation in case of unplanned leave.

Once this is completed record details of the travel request and escalation on the approved travellers Customer Reference Number (CRN).

Procedure ends here.

10

Level 2 Policy Helpdesk response + Read more ...

The Level 2 Policy Helpdesk will advise the assessing Travel Officer if travel for a dependent has been approved. Has travel for the dependent been approved?

  • Yes, see Step 2 in Table 2
  • No, see Step 8 in Table 2

Non-student travel process

Table 2:

Step

Description

1

Visiting student at the term address + Read more ...

Family or a community members may be approved as a non-student traveller for FAA to visit the student’s term address. The non-student traveller may be required to travel under one of the student's entitlement trips.

To assess if FAA entitlement is payable see:

  • Compassionate Travel for ABSTUDY
  • Orientation and Special Purpose Visit travel for ABSTUDY

Has FAA entitlement been determined for a non-student traveller to travel to the term address?

  • Yes, go to Step 2
  • No, family or community member travel does not apply. Consideration may be given to FAA for a travelling companion. Go to Step 4

2

Limits of approval for travel + Read more ...

To assess entitlement to travel under FAA for a non-student traveller, the student cannot have exceeded the entitlement limit for the calendar year. For further information see:

  • Compassionate Travel for ABSTUDY
  • Orientation and Special Purpose Visit travel for ABSTUDY

Has the student reached the entitlement limit for approval of the applicable travel type?

  • Yes, and the:
    • response from Level 2 Policy Helpdesk provides approval to pay ABSTUDY Additional Assistance in lieu of FAA, go to Step 3
    • travel is not approved go to Step 8
  • No, go to Step 3

3

Family or community member(s) travel approved + Read more ...

Return travel costs to the student’s term address may be approved for a parent/guardian, partner, other nominated family members or community members between the approved traveller's home address and the student's term address.

If the student has a terminal illness, approval may be granted for both parents/guardians to visit.

If FAA is for Orientation of Special Purpose Visit travel, approval may be given for a reasonable number of approved travellers.

See Resources for examples.

Go to Step 6.

4

Undesirable for the ill, injured or disabled student to travel alone + Read more ...

It would be considered undesirable to travel alone in various circumstances; such as:

  • the health of the student is likely to deteriorate and/or results in the student being unable to adequately travel alone
  • an injured student is unable to travel alone and negotiate the necessary requirements (for example, stairs, baggage etc.)
  • a disabled student cannot undertake travel without assistance (for example, the student is blind and needs to travel with a service animal (dog))

See Resources for examples.

Is it undesirable for the student travel alone?

  • Yes, a travelling companion may be approved for FAA. All types of travel are available for a travelling companion. Go to Step 6
  • No, go to Step 8

5

Education institution representatives travel + Read more ...

Where student/s are qualified for FAA, representatives of an education institution or hostel may be approved to travel to the student/s home community in the following circumstances:

  • the student/s are eligible for orientation/special purpose visit travel, and
  • the proposed travellers have direct contact with ABSTUDY students at the boarding location, and
  • the purpose of the trip is to engage with the family, where a family or community member is unable to visit the boarding location for a school event such as parent-teacher interviews

or

  • the student/s are eligible for orientation/special purpose visit travel, and
  • the proposed travellers have direct contact with ABSTUDY students at the boarding location, and
  • there are at least 6 ABSTUDY students at the school/hostel who come from the communities to be visited and there is likely to be a continued boarding connection between the education institution and the community

or

  • the student is eligible for Away From Base travel, and
  • the student will be participating in either a testing and assessment activity or a residential school, and
  • the education institution can demonstrate that it is cost-effective for its representatives to travel to a community or communities instead of the students/parents travelling to the education institution location

Have any of the above conditions been met?

  • Yes, travel may be approved for up to 2 education institution representatives. Go to Step 6
  • No, go to Step 8

6

Additional costs + Read more ...

An approved non-student traveller is also eligible for additional costs associated with travel for such things as meals and accommodation when an overnight stay in required as part of the journey.

An approved non-student traveller may also be responsible for any penalties or other costs incurred as part of the journey such as a No Show penalty, lost tickets or additional costs charged by an accommodation provider (for example, mini-bar, damage to room, lost keys etc.).

Go to Step 7.

7

Advising the non-student traveller of approval + Read more ...

Determine if FAA is to be:

  • authorised and booked in advance of travel or
  • costs reimbursed after the travel has been undertaken. The traveller will be required to complete a Fares Allowance Claim (SY032). The Resources page contains a link to the SY032

Procedure ends here.

8

FAA not approved + Read more ...

Advise the non-student traveller they are not eligible for FAA.

If the travel request has been received by ABSTUDY Smart Centre telephony, record details of discussion on a DOC. Explain their review and appeal rights. See Initial contact about a decision and the review of decision process.

If the travel request has been received by the ABSTUDY Smart Centre Travel Team, finalise the travel Booking Status as 'Rejected' in the Travel Management Application (TMA). Record details of the decision in the Notes field. See Coding pre-booked Fares Allowance (FAA) entitlements for ABSTUDY and ABSTUDY Pensioner Education Supplement (PES).

Procedure ends here.
