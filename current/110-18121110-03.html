This page contains the process a Compliance Officer (CO) must follow when assessing and finalising a BIBI intervention within the Compliance Assurance and Debt Operations Division.

How to assess and locate information provided for BIBI interventions

Table 1

Step

Action

1

Check if a manual partner intervention is required + Read more ...

Determine if the updates will affect the customer's partner payments. Make sure a partner intervention has been loaded and linked before raising a debt.

If the partner intervention has not been loaded or linked, it is imperative that a partner intervention is created.

Do not load or initiate a manual partner intervention if:

  • the partner was not receiving a Centrelink payment during the period of the intervention, or
  • Family and Domestic violence has been identified

Is a manual partner intervention required?

  • Yes, go to Step 2
  • No, go to Step 6

2

Create the manual partner intervention + Read more ...

On the New Review (IRNR) screen, complete the following fields to add the manual partner intervention:

  • Review Type: UII (Same type as the customer)
  • Review Reason: MPR
  • Cycle number: Match the one on the customer record
  • Service Reason: e.g. JSP, PPS, AGE
  • Review Method: same as customer
  • Review Origin: same as primary intervention on Customer record

Once loaded, the partner intervention will show a status of REG (Registered).

3

Link the customer record and partner record + Read more ...

In the Integrated Review System (IRS), go to the Link Summary (IRLS) screen and complete the following fields to link the records:

  • CRN column: code the other customer's Customer Reference Number (CRN)
  • Rev ID column: code the review ID of the customer's review that is to be linked
  • Lnk Rsn column: code a valid link reason (e.g. PTR- Customers are partnered)

For help, see Linking reviews in the IRS.

4

Notify the partner + Read more ...

Speak to the partner during the initial call, or attempt an outbound call to the partner.

Is the partner available?

  • Yes, advise the partner of the intervention. Go to Step 5
  • No,
    • Issue a manual Q766 Notice of compliance review letter to the partner, including the Income Confirmation phone number
    • Create an online advice
    • Go to Step 5

5

Start the partner intervention + Read more ...

To start the MPR intervention, record contact details in IRS.

The CO must leave the intervention as STA and allocate the intervention to their workgroup and position.

6

Customer and third party responded to request for information + Read more ...

Has information been provided by the customer or third party?

  • Yes, go to Step 7
  • No, use suitable information from the customer record check to assess the bank interest and business income relevant to the intervention. Documents can be used if they are deemed sufficient to enable an update. Go to Step 8

7

Locate income and assets information + Read more ...

Use the information obtained during the intervention to update the customer's and/or partner's record to determine:

  • historical qualification and entitlement
  • whether any overpayment has occurred, and
  • current qualification and ongoing entitlement

For help, see the Assessing and Coding Income and Assets guide on the Resources page.

8

Selecting the IRS intervention + Read more ...

Before any updates to the customer's record:

  • make sure the IRS intervention has a status of STA (started), and
  • be in the Review Image

9

Coding income and assets + Read more ...

Record all information on the customer's record and in the Note. This will ensure the customer's record is correct and as up-to-date as possible.

Use the Income/Assets Update and Reviews script to update all income and assets details.

Bank interest information may be updated from either verbal or documented verification. Verbal updates can be coded as per the actual event dates and/or at the start and end dates of investment as provided by the customer

Business income information may only be updated from documented verification

For help, see

  • Assessing and Coding Income and Assets guide on the Resources page
  • business income coding examples and screen shots on the Resources page:
  • Assessment of income and assets from business structures for Centrelink payments
  • Raising social security debts due to self-employment or business income

After updating income details on the customer record, the CO will be able to determine if there is a debt or adverse decision.

Is there a debt or adverse decision?

  • Yes, see Table 2
  • No, see the Table 3

How to determine, calculate and raise overpayments

Table 2

Step

Action

1

Determine whether a Recovery Fee is to be applied + Read more ...

If the intervention includes verification of earned income (include income from self-employment), determine whether the Recovery Fee should be applied.

The recovery fee will only be applied if the customer has not met their obligations. For example, they failed to provide information relating to their income confirmation.

The recovery fee will not be applied if the customer has fulfilled their obligations. For example, they provided information relating to their income confirmation.

Note: the system will raise earnings debts with a period of 14 days or less upon completion of the earnings activity. The Recovery Fee is not applied to auto raised debts. The customer will receive the standard debt advice as part of the automatic process.

2

Determine debt and calculate + Read more ...

All debts that can be calculated via the Centrelink system and explained using ADEX should be calculated that way. Only use MultiCal if the Centrelink system calculation is not possible or the outcome is not correct.

Check the Assessment Result (AR) screen to make sure the system has correctly calculated any potential overpayment, for example:

  • Debt schedule - do the debt dates/date of event (DOV) match the period?
  • Have the updates/coding been entered correctly?
  • Check the following to make sure the updates are as expected:
    • Working Credit Balance Summary (WCBS)
    • Income Bank (NIB)
    • Employment Income Assessment (EIA)
  • Can a reassessment be made using the Benefit Action (BA) screen?
    • Use REA in relevant host system, or
    • RCR in PEN/PGA (for cancelled PEN and PGA records) and leave Reason blank

Note: check if Review of Assessment (ROA) coding is needed to generate allowable arrears. Do this when corrected income is less than declared, but the system did not generate arrears for those periods/fortnights.

If an ADEX debt calculation:

  • can be undertaken, the overpayment must be calculated with the customer over the phone where possible
  • is not possible a MultiCal debt calculation may be required

For MultiCal debt calculation, get all information from the customer over the phone where possible to enable a debt calculation.

The CO is responsible for progressing the MultiCal activity. If they cannot action, their site is responsible for progressing the activity. The Team Leader will need to reassign the activity to another CO with the required skill set.

For more information, see:

  • Debt tools
  • Debt investigation
  • Actioning an undetermined debt on the Debt Management and Information System (DMIS)
  • Using ADEX to explain a system generated debt
  • Using the correct date of effect when raising debts
  • Calculating Centrelink entitlements and gathering information when investigating debts
  • Waiving Centrelink debts

3

Contact customer and partner + Read more ...

Make 3 genuine attempts to contact the customer (and their partner if the partner has incurred a debt).

Follow the phone contact requirements.

Record all attempts to contact the customer in the Note.

Record contact details in IRS.

Was contact successful?

  • Yes, go to Step 4
  • No, after 3 attempts, go to Step 6

4

Advise outcome + Read more ...

If the customer and/or partner is responding to the Pre Adverse Decision letter:

  • record contact details in IRS
  • record details on the intervention DOC. See the Resources page for the DOC template

Discuss the outcome of the intervention with the customer and/or partner.

Tell the customer what information was used to calculate the outcome. This could include a combination of verification from the customer or a third party (employer).

Tell the customer:

Debt: 'Based on the information you have advised/ we have obtained from third party regarding your income from (income source), this has resulted in an overpayment of your Centrelink payment of $xxxxx for the period (date to date). If you do not believe this outcome is correct, we can update your details now if you have further information available.'

Tell the partner:

Partner: 'Your partner has been selected for a compliance intervention. The income from (income source) was not taken into account in the payments made to you, therefore you have incurred an overpayment of $xxxxx for the period from (date) to (date).'

Ask the customer if they have more information they want to provide before the outcome is finalised. They may be able to verbally explain the discrepancy. Update the customer record and recalculate as required.

If the customer is not satisfied with the decision and they have no more information they can provide, tell them:

  • the decision will be made using the information available
  • they should provide any extra information or evidence that may be relevant to the decision as soon as they can. If more information is received, there will be a reassessment.
  • their review and appeal rights. See the Reviews and appeals of a Centrelink decision page on the Services Australia website.

Is the customer providing more information?

  • Yes, go to Step 5
  • No, go to Step 6

5

Customer is providing more information + Read more ...

If the customer wants to provide more information but cannot provide it over the phone, tell them:

  • they have 7 days to provide the information
  • the intervention will be finalised using the information available if the requested information is not provided within the expected response time

Record a contact in IRS and put the intervention on hold.

COs must self-assign the work item via Work Optimiser and ensure the IRS review is in their Work Group and Position.

Record details of the conversation with the customer in the intervention DOC.

When the customer provides information or activities come DUE

  • If the customer provides new information, raise the debt and finalise the intervention
  • If the intervention comes DUE and the customer has not given new information, raise the debt and finalise the intervention based on previous information.

6

Issue Pre-adverse decision letter + Read more ...

After 3 unsuccessful call attempts to the customer and/or partner to advise the outcome, issue a manual Q888 Pre Adverse Decision letter to them.

This letter tells them what evidence has been used to calculate a debt and gives an estimated debt amount. It gives them a further right of reply before finalising the outcome and lets them know what will happen next.

See the Resources page for the Q888 template. Use the correct letter template based on the customer's circumstances.

In IRS, record a contact and put the intervention on hold (HLD) for 28 days, pending contact from the customer:

  • On IRCD screen, key:
    • Expected response date: 28 days in future
    • Letter code: 'ADVERS'
    • Text: 'Q888 Pre Adverse Decision letter issued'
  • On IRMR screen, self-assign the intervention and add keyword:
    • Workgroup: relevant workgroup
    • Position: COs logon
    • Keyword: 'Q888'

COs must also self-assign the work item via Work Optimiser.

DOC the record. See the Resources page for DOC requirements.

When the customer calls or activities come DUE

  • If the customer calls in response to the Q888, update their record based on any new information they give, remove the Q888 keyword on the IRMR screen, raise the debt and finalise the intervention
  • If the intervention comes DUE and the customer has not called or given new information, raise the debt and finalise the intervention based on information previously

Procedure ends here.

7

Raise debt in accordance with guidelines + Read more ...

Use the Debt Action script if possible.

Suggested text for Accounts Payable:

  • Customer suggested text
    'The correct amount of your income from (income source) was not taken into account in the payments made to you for the period from (date) to (date). This means you have been overpaid $(amount). We are, therefore, required to recover this amount.'
  • Partner suggested text
    'The correct amount of your partner's income from (income source) was not taken into account in the payments made to you for the period from (date) to (date). This means you have been overpaid $(amount). We are, therefore, required to recover this amount.'

Raise debts as per current guidelines. See General debt raising information.

Debts less than $50 can be waived under the current guidelines. See Small debt waivers.

Make sure the recovery fee question is answered.

Notes:

  • In the 'date contact made with customer' question, enter the date the Debt Action Script is run
  • if the debt amount generated via ADEX differs from the final debt amount raised, must fully document the reasons for this in the stand alone debt DOC

Annotate the debt DOC with the following text:

'Intervention finalised as a result of the Business Income and Bank Interest interventions. Please transfer to 1800 064 586 if the customer can provide new information to support a reassessment of the initial overpayment, or if the explanation relating to the overpayment was unsuccessful.'

Deceased customer

If the customer is deceased and a debt is likely, make sure before sending a debt advice that a social worker decides the best way to contact the executor. See:

How to finalise BIBI interventions

Table 3

Step

Action

1

Document the intervention + Read more ...

It is important to record all information in the Note to clearly explain:

  • the customer's circumstances
  • information relating to the compliance trigger
  • the scope of the intervention
  • the customer's action
  • outcomes of the intervention

All Notes on a customer's record are to provide a clear and concise history of the customer's interactions. They must meet Online Document Recording (ODR) standards and Getting it Right (GIR) Minimum Standards.

See the Resources page for DOC templates.

All compulsory scripts must be run, to ensure that:

  • the DOC is appropriately updated, including the Extra Details line
  • all actions are recorded in the IRS

2

Finalising the intervention + Read more ...

Before finalising the intervention, ensure all actions required by the Compliance Officer (CO) are completed.

Use the Finalise Review Script to finalise the intervention.

Check the following in IRS as part of the script flow:

  • Attribute/unattributed activities as per current guidelines. See Finalising and attributing actions to a Service Update or Review in IRS
  • Choose an appropriate Result Reason as per current guidelines. See Calculating a Review Result and Restoration Processing in IRS
  • Ensure all finalisation information has been appropriately recorded under the Note tab

If the customer has a linked review or partner who also has an intervention, finalise all interventions at the same time.

3

Issuing a Q765 advice + Read more ...

A Q765 must be sent unless the intervention outcome:

  • has been advised over the phone
  • is related to a member of couple trigger and a decision letter has been issued
  • resulted in an adverse decision, for example:
    • payment/card remains suspended/cancelled
    • payment was varied
  • debt advice has been issued

A Q765 can be issued if requested by the customer.

4

Further investigation required + Read more ...

After completing an intervention, if the CO believes there is a need for further investigation of declared income due to non-compliance outside the scope, they can refer the case to the Intelligence and Investigations Branch.
