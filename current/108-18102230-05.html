Overpayments of Rent Assistance (RA) for aged care

Table 2: This table describes some information on overpayments of Rent Assistance for care recipients at an aged care service.

Item

Description

1

Care recipients in care at 1 October 1997, when RA ceased to be payable, but payment continued

Some care recipients received further instalments of RA after 30 September 1997. In these cases there was no change in these care recipients' circumstances and no requirement for notification under Section 68(1) of the Social Security (Administration) Act 1999.

These instalments are not recoverable because they are not debts under sub-section 1223(1) of the Act (as the persons were qualified for the payments at the time they were granted and the RA continued to be payable under a valid determination).

For care recipients who were in care at 1 October 1997 and received further instalments of RA, their records should be amended to show that these RA payments were not debts.

2

New care recipients to residential care on or after 1 October 1997, who continued to receive RA for their principal home

Section 68 of the Social Security (Administration) Act 1999 requires care recipients to notify a change of address within 14 days of their entry to an aged care service. They can be paid RA during the notification period (with any further payment being a recoverable debt).

In the case of a care recipient who fails to notify, any payment of RA from the 15th day after admission is a recoverable debt.

It should be noted that this is a highly sensitive issue for vulnerable care recipients in a period of confusion and transition in their lives. Recovery of debts must therefore be handled in a sensitive manner.

As these cases come to notice, contact with the aged care service should provide an indication of the care recipient's ability to understand the need to advise of a change of accommodation.

A care recipient moving to high level residential care may have little perception of their obligations, but it would probably be reasonable for a person entering low level care to advise Services Australia within the prescribed period.

Consideration should be given to waiving the debt if special circumstances exist.
