This page contains the process of using an Employment Services Assessment (ESAt) report to determine payment type.

Assessing the ESAt report

Assessing the ESAt report

Table 1

Step

Action

1

ESAt report is received + Read more ...

An Employment Services Assessment (ESAt) report is received at the new claim stage or when a review is conducted to determine the most appropriate payment type for the customer.

The ESAt report should be considered in conjunction with all other available information, such as DOCs, the customer's file and the electronic Medical/Sensitive Information File Envelope (eMIFE).

In most circumstances an ESAt will not be used to determine appropriate payments. Exceptions to this include:

  • where the ESAt has been upgraded to a JCA because there is sufficient evidence to assess the customer's eligibility for DSP
  • for a JobSeeker Payment claim, an ESAt has been requested to assess whether the customer is temporarily incapacitated for their usual work
  • the report indicates the customer is unable to use public transport without substantial assistance

Check the report to ensure it is complete, accurate and the information can be used to make a decision.

Go to Step 2.

2

Public transport + Read more ...

The Medical Conditions section will indicate whether the Assessor has indicated the customer's medical condition prevents them from using public transport without substantial assistance.

If substantial assistance is:

  • required, go to Step 3
  • not required, go to Step 5

3

Is the customer receiving Mobility Allowance (MOB)? + Read more ...

  • Yes, go to Step 4
  • No, this question is only a trigger to consider possible eligibility for MOB and invite a claim - it does not mean the customer is medically eligible
    Check the medical condition is permanent or likely to require substantial assistance for an extended period of time (one year or more) and the customer has a qualifying activity. Consider issuing a claim after determining possible eligibility for MOB. Go to Step 5

4

Reviewing eligibility for MOB + Read more ...

This question is not intended to generate a review if the assessor indicates the medical condition does not prevent the customer from using public transport without substantial assistance.

The decision regarding whether a review is required must consider the medical qualification for MOB and be supported by the medical evidence.

See Assessing medical evidence for Mobility Allowance (MOB) and Eligibility for Mobility Allowance (MOB).

If the customer's inability to use public transport without substantial assistance is due to their permanent medical condition, a medical review is not conducted.

If they are temporarily unable to use public transport without substantial assistance due to their medical condition, set up a review to determine:

  • when it is likely they would be able to use public transport without substantial assistance, or
  • 12 months

whichever comes first.

Go to Step 5.

5

Functional impacts are temporary or permanent + Read more ...

The ESAt report should indicate if the functional impact of a condition (illness, injury or disability) is permanent or temporarily impacts on work capacity.

The ESAt provides an assessment of the customer's current capacity to work as a number of hour per week, and their future work capacity within or after 24 months.

Is the functional impact of the condition temporary or permanent?

  • temporary, go to Step 6
  • permanent, go to Table 1 Step 1

6

Does the customer have a job or study to return to after they recover from their temporary incapacity? + Read more ...

  • Yes, go to Step 7
  • No, go to Step 9

7

Customer expects to return to work or study + Read more ...

For the customer to successfully return to work or study, their incapacity should be assessed as either temporary (less than 2 years) or their incapacity is not expected to severely impact on their ability to continue their job or studies. The report should indicate a realistic timeframe for the customer's recovery.

Any follow up action required must be done where an appropriate intervention is recommended. Go to Step 8.

8

Suitable payment type - temporary incapacity + Read more ...

Unless there is contrary information in the report, the following payment types would be appropriate in these circumstances:

  • If the customer is employed, advise them they may be eligible for JobSeeker Payment (JSP)/Youth Allowance (YA) (Incapacitated)
  • If the customer is a student or apprentice and currently in receipt of ABSTUDY, see Payment of ABSTUDY when a student or Australian Apprentice becomes incapacitated
  • If the customer is a student or apprentice and:
    • aged 24 or under and currently in receipt of Youth Allowance (YA), see Youth Allowance (YA) student or Australian Apprentice becomes incapacitated
    • aged 25 or over and currently in receipt of Austudy, see Austudy student or Australian Apprentice becomes incapacitated

Procedure ends here.

9

Customer is not in employment or study - temporary reduced capacity or incapacity + Read more ...

Customer may be eligible for JSP if they are 22 or over, or YA if they are 21 years of age or under.

Assess mutual obligation requirements based on the report.

Customers unable to work for eight or more hours per week and who are unable to undertake another appropriate activity, may be exempt from the mutual obligation requirements.

Customers presenting with a temporary medical condition that is likely to improve within 2 years are to be classified as job seekers where the report indicates the:

  • customer is able to work for eight or more hours per week, or
  • illness, injury or disability is expected to improve or resolve within 2 years

A customer can satisfy their mutual obligation requirements by looking for eight or more hours of suitable work per week. An unemployed person who has a capacity to work for eight or more hours per week should be encouraged to remain in the labour market through modified mutual obligation requirements and referrals to appropriate programs of assistance.

Procedure ends here.

Making a decision about payment based on the ESAt report

Table 2

Step

Action

1

Is the referral reason to determine eligibility for Youth Disability Supplement (YDS)? + Read more ...

  • Yes, YDS is payable if the report indicates baseline and with intervention work capacity is less than 30 hours per week within the next 2 years, regardless of whether conditions are FDTS
    • when the report is accepted, YDS will be automatically calculated as part of the YA or ABSTUDY rate. Check if the payment needs to be backdated. Issue manual advice. See Youth Disability Supplement (YDS). Procedure ends here
  • No, go to Step 2

2

Customer can currently work at least 15 hours per week or is expected to work at least 15 hours per week within the next 2 years + Read more ...

Does the report indicate the customer can, or is expected to, work more than 15 hours but less than 30 hours per week in the next 2 years?

Check baseline work capacity, and work capacity with intervention are not recorded as 30+ hours.

  • Yes, the customer is identified as having a partial capacity to work. They are not eligible for DSP. They can claim JSP/YA if no other payment is suitable. Mutual obligation requirements are based on the work capacity bandwidth hours. See Job seekers with a partial capacity to work or a temporary reduced work capacity. Procedure ends here
  • No, go to Step 3

3

Does the ESAt report indicate eligibility for DSP? + Read more ...

  • Yes, go to Step 4
  • No, the customer may have reduced mutual obligation requirements for a temporary period. Assess mutual obligation requirements based on the ESAt/JCA report. Procedure ends here

4

ESAt report indicates eligibility for DSP + Read more ...

Where the Assessor has sufficient medical evidence to assign an Impairment Rating an ESAt can become a JCA.

Check the report meets all the following requirements to invite a claim for DSP:

  • the report indicates the customer attracts an Impairment Rating of 20 points or more, for a condition(s) that is permanent and FDTS
  • the report indicates the customer has actively participated in a Program of Support (POS), or, has a severe impairment and is exempt from the active participation in a program of support requirement
  • the report indicates a future work capacity of less than 15 hours per week with intervention in the next 2 years, and
  • the customer does not have a current DSP claim

Note: if the active participation in a program of support (POS) question is answered N, the assessor will not be able to assess the work capacity fields for this customer.

Have all the above requirements been met to invite a claim for DSP?

  • Yes, go to Step 5
  • No, the customer is identified as having a partial capacity to work and can claim JSP/YA. Assess mutual obligation requirements based on the ESAt report

Procedure ends here

5

Contact customer to invite claim for DSP + Read more ...

Service Officers are responsible for making 2 attempts to contact the customer to invite a claim for DSP.

Make the first attempt to contact the customer.

Note: if the manifest criteria is Terminal Illness (T), staff must not issue the Claim for Disability Support Pension for a Terminal Illness (SA494) to a customer who is unaware of the terminal prognosis.

Was the first contact attempt successful?

  • Yes, go to Step 7
  • No:
    • run the DSP Invite to claim guided procedure in Customer First which will record the unsuccessful contact attempt. Note: the invitation to claim DSP letter (Q055) is only issued by the guided procedure after 2 unsuccessful contacts have been made
    • ESAt activity to be held to user for the second contact attempt

6

Second contact attempt with customer + Read more ...

Was the second contact attempt successful?

  • Yes, go to Step 7
  • No:
    • do not run the First Contact Service Offer (FCSO) workflow
    • run the DSP Invite to Claim guided procedure in Customer First which will send an Invitation to claim DSP letter (Q055), advising the customer Services Australia has current medical information for them and they do not need to provide any additional medical information for their claim at this time. The letter also says they can upload the invitation to claim letter instead of medical evidence if they claim DSP online
    • the script will create a DOC record indicating further medical evidence is not required
    • send the Claim for Disability Support Pension (SA466). Make sure the front cover of the SA466 clearly indicates no further evidence is required with this claim
    • when the customer responds to the Q055, run the FCSO workflow to record response to letter
    • manually accept the ESAt report, go to Step 8

7

Contact with customer is successful

Customer accepts invitation to claim DSP: + Read more ...

See Claiming Disability Support Pension (DSP).

  • offer an online claim first. Explain the benefits of claiming online
  • if they do not want to claim online, offer to run Assisted Customer Claim (ACC) with them
  • otherwise, run the DSP Invite to Claim Guided Procedure in Customer First which will send an Invitation to claim DSP letter (Q055). Advise the customer Services Australia has current medical information for them and they do not need to provide any additional medical information for their claim at this time. The letter also says they can upload the invitation to claim letter instead of medical evidence if they claim DSP online
  • send the Claim for Disability Support Pension (SA466). Make sure the front cover of the SA466 clearly indicates no further evidence is required with this claim
    Note: if the manifest criteria is Terminal Illness (T), staff must not issue the Claim for Disability Support Pension for a Terminal Illness (SA494) to a customer who is unaware of the terminal prognosis
  • if applicable, tell the customer their eligibility may need to be confirmed by a Government Contracted Doctor - this does not apply if the claim can be manifestly granted. See Disability Medical Assessment (DMA) referrals
  • the customer may qualify for JSP or YA (Provisional) while the DSP claim is being assessed
  • manually accept the ESAt report, go to Step 8

Customer declines invitation to claim DSP: + Read more ...

  • if the customer declines an invitation to claim DSP and the Service Officer believes the customer may lack insight into their condition, the Service Officer should consult with senior staff, for example, an Assessor or Social Worker, to explore possible alternative approaches where appropriate
  • the customer may choose to claim/receive JSP/YA with a partial capacity to work. Customers with a work capacity of 0-7 or 8-14 hours per week are not required to look for work or participate with an Employment Services Provider, but may volunteer to connect with a provider
  • manually accept the ESAt report, go to Step 8

8

Manually accept the ESAt report

Youth Disability Supplement (YDS) for students under 22 years of age + Read more ...

YDS is payable if the report indicates baseline and with intervention work capacity is less than 30 hours per week within the next 2 years, regardless of whether conditions are fully diagnosed, treated and stabilised (FDTS).

When the report is accepted, YDS will be automatically calculated as part of the Youth Allowance (YA) or ABSTUDY rate, check if the payment needs to be backdated. Issue manual advice.

See Youth Disability Supplement (YDS).

Independence for Youth Allowance (Job seeker) due to partial capacity to work + Read more ...

A Youth Allowance (YA) (job seeker) may be considered independent when they are assessed as having a partial capacity to work (PCW) as determined by an ESAt. The customer will be eligible for independent status for the period they are assessed as having a PCW.

Note: this assessment of independence does not apply to YA (students/Australian Apprentices) or Disability Support Pension (DSP) customers.

The commencement date for the independent status is taken to be 1 of the following:

  • New claims - the PCW status should be the new claim start date (if the customer's disability/illness/incapacity was identified and the ESAt or Job Capacity Assessment (JCA) referral was required as part of the new claim processing. Where this is the case the event date on the Medical Conditions Details (MC) screen should be corrected to match the start date on the Pensions Assessment (PNA) screen/Start Date Calculator (NDC) screen), or
  • Payment current - the PCW status start date should be the ESAt or JCA referral date (which is the date the disability/illness/injury was identified)

See Assessing independence for customers with a partial capacity to work.

Job seekers aged over 22 + Read more ...

Select the Accept report button to accept the report.

Once an ESAt report has been accepted by Services Australia, it will be automatically attached to the job seeker's electronic Medical Information File Envelope (MIFE).

The Medical Conditions Details (MC) screen and the Work Capacity (WC) screen are displayed with information updated from the report.

For a Job Seeker Snapshot initiated ESAt, any ESAt flag raised is finalised automatically when the report is submitted.

The report remains submitted for 28 days. It then becomes finalised from day 29. If required, staff with relevant access can request early finalisation of the ESAt report, so that DSP new claims are not delayed if a new referral needs to be made immediately after the previous report is submitted. Once the report is selected for finalisation, it will be finalised overnight.

Once the ESAt report is accepted, it will automatically generate a DOC which will include details where:

  • a Disability Employment Services (DES) referral was recommended and actioned
  • a DES referral was recommended and not actioned/requires action
  • a DES referral was recommended and deferred as the job seeker has a temporary reduced work capacity (TRWC)
  • the date a TRWC is expected to end
