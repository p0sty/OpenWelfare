This page contains procedures on how to help with My Health Record National Provider Portal (NPP) access issues. These processes are to help with enquiries from:

  • Responsible Officers (ROs)
  • Organisation Maintenance Officers (OMOs), and
  • health care providers

On this page:

Check the access for ROs/OMOs to HPOS

Check the access for healthcare providers to HPOS

Accessing the National Provider Portal (NPP)

Check the access for ROs/OMOs to HPOS

Table 1: This table outlines how to check the Health Professional Online System (HPOS) access for ROs and OMOs only. If the individual is a healthcare provider, see Table 2.

Step

Action

1

Check if the RO/OMO is registered in the Healthcare Identifier (HI) Service + Read more ...

Perform a security check with the caller.

Are they registered in the eHealth Program (EHP)?

  • Yes, go to Step 3
  • No, go to Step 2

2

There is no HI Service registration + Read more ...

Tell the caller that they do not have a record in the HI Service.

Ask if they are calling about:

  • the HI Service, or
  • another program

If they are asking about:

  • another program, get the name of the program and:
    • warm transfer them to the program area number, or
    • give them the contact number to call from the Health Professionals contact page
    • procedure ends here
  • the HI Service, direct them to Healthcare Identifiers Service for health professionals. This will give them:
    • details about eligibility, and
    • how to register
    • procedure ends here

3

Check if the RO or OMO is linked to an organisation + Read more ...

Is the RO or OMO linked?

  • Yes, go to Step 4
  • No, tell the caller they will need to either:
    • register a new organisation, or
    • link to an existing the organisation already registered in the HI

For details about registering tell the caller to:

  • read the Healthcare Identifiers Service for health professionals information on the Services Australia Website, and
  • use the online form

Procedure ends here.

4

Check the caller has a PRODA account + Read more ...

If they have a PRODA account, go to Step 5.

If not, tell the caller they must register for one. They need this to access the HI Service in HPOS.

They need to make sure they link their PRODA account to their HI Service record. For details about how they do this they can read the PRODA information. This is on the Services Australia Website.

Procedure ends here.

5

Check if the PRODA account is linked to the HI Service record + Read more ...

Perform a security check for their PRODA account.

In their EHP record, check the Certificates tab.

If they have linked their PRODA account, the distinguished name will show prodaaccounts.

Is the caller’s PRODA account linked?

  • Yes, go to Step 10
  • No, go to Step 6

6

Check the name details + Read more ...

Are the name details the same?

  • Yes, go to Step 7
  • No, tell the caller their PRODA name will be added as an additional name to the EHP record
    • add their name
    • do not amend the core details
    • go to Step 7

7

Check the date of birth + Read more ...

Check if the date of birth the same. If it is, go to Step 8.

If the date of birth is different, check if the date of birth in EHP is a data entry error.

If it is an error, fix the date of birth to match the PRODA account. Go to Step 8

If it is not a data entry error, the caller must:

  • lodge a HI Services Application to replace a RO or add/remove an OMO for an organisation form (HW040), and
  • include documents as evidence of change

This will update their record. When the update is complete they will get a letter in the mail.

Tell the caller they can link their PRODA account to their HI Service record after they get the letter.

For help to do this they can:

  • use the guidelines on the Services Australia Website, or
  • call back

Procedure ends here.

8

Check the gender details + Read more ...

Check if the gender details are the same.

If they are, go to Step 9

If they are not tell the caller that the details on EHP differ to the details in their PRODA account.

Do not ask if they have had sex reassignment surgery. This is a sensitive topic for some people.

If the caller says there is an error, fox the details in EHP to match their PRODA account. Go to Step 9

If the caller says they have changed their sex and/or gender details they must:

  • lodge a HI Services Application to replace a RO or add/remove an OMO for an organisation form (HW040), and
  • include documents as evidence of change

This will update their record. When the update is complete they will get a letter in the mail.

Tell the caller they can link their PRODA account to their HI Service record after they get the letter.

For help to do this they can:

  • use the guidelines on the Services Australia Website, or
  • call back

Procedure ends here.

See also:

Gender Guidelines

9

Help the caller with linking their PRODA account + Read more ...

Guide the caller through the Manage/link identifiers process in HPOS.

10

Help the caller to access HPOS + Read more ...

Check if the caller needs help:

  • accessing the Health Processional Online Services (HPOS), or
  • creating authorisation links for healthcare providers to access the NPP. If so, go to Table 3

Guide the caller through the Manage/link identifiers process in HPOS.

Do they need more help?

  • Yes, go to Step 10
  • No, procedure ends here

10

Check if the caller needs help to access HPOS or the National Provider Portal (NPP) + Read more ...

Do they want help accessing:

  • the Health Professional Online System (HPOS), go to Step 11
  • the National Provider Portal (NPP), go to Step 12

11

Check access to HPOS + Read more ...

Ask the caller to try logging on to Health Professional Online Services (HPOS).

If they can log on procedure ends here.

If they could not log on, and they are set up correctly:

  • send an email to PRODA who will investigate the issue. Include the below:
    • the identity details for both names, and
    • state that their details match, and
    • the caller’s contact details, and
    • the problem the caller is having with HPOS, and
    • a request for PRODA to contact the caller after their investigation is complete
  • tell the caller that PRODA will
    • investigate the issue, and
    • contact them with further details

Procedure ends here.

12

Check access to the National Provider Portal (NPP) + Read more ...

Ask the caller to try logging onto the NPP.

If they can log on, procedure ends here.

If they could not and their PRODA and HI Service details are correct tell the caller that they need to be linked to the organisation.

To check if a link has been created, they must contact the organisation’s RO or OMO.

If the caller:

  • is the RO or OMO, or a sole trader, see Step 4 in Table 3.
  • is not the RO or OMO, procedure ends here

Accessing the National Provider Portal (NPP)

Table 3: This table details how to check if a healthcare provider has the right authorisation link to access the NPP.

Note: escalate the caller to Provider HI Service and My Health Record Subject Matter Expert (SME) if they have already been through this process, and it is still not working.

Step

Action

1

Caller details + Read more ...

Make sure the caller is either an RO or OMO.

Only an RO or OMO can check an organisation's set up.

Has the call just started?

  • Yes, go to Step 2
  • No, go to Step 4

2

Perform a security check + Read more ...

Perform a security check with the caller.

Are they registered as an RO, and OMO or sole trader for an active organisation?

  • Yes, go to Step 4
  • No, go to Step 3

3

Organisation is not registered or active + Read more ...

To access the NPP, they need to be active or registered in the HI Service.

Tell the caller they need to:

  • reactivate the organisation in Health Professional Online Services (HPOS) to access the HI Service or My Health Record, or
  • register a new organisation if the other one is no longer in business. For details about registering, see Healthcare Identifiers Service for health professionals
  • be linked to an organisation that is already registered in the HI Service

Procedure ends here.

4

Check the My Health Record registration + Read more ...

Access the Provider Participation Register (PPR).

Search for the Healthcare Provider Identifier - Organisation (HPI-O).

Is the HPI-O registered in the My Health Record?

  • Yes, go to Step 6
  • No, go to Step 5

5

Set up the My Health Record + Read more ...

Help the RO or OMO register. Follow the How to register steps.

When complete, they need to set up authorisation links.

When the registration is complete, go to Step 8.

6

Check if the healthcare provider is linked + Read more ...

Select the Authorisation Links button.

Under the Current Authorisation Links, is the healthcare provider listed?

  • Yes, go to Step 8
  • No, go to Step 7

7

Set up the authorisation link + Read more ...

Help the RO or OMO set up an authorisation link. Follow the steps in Link a healthcare provider to a healthcare organisation in the Provider Participation Register (PPR) .

When complete, go to Step 8.

Note: If the RO or OMO does not have the correct HPI-I or name, ask if the healthcare provider can confirm it over the phone.

8

Process is complete + Read more ...

Check that all the details are correct. Tell the RO or OMO that the healthcare provider can now access the NPP.

If there are still access issues, escalate the call to Provider HI Service and My Health Record SME.
