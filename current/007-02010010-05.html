Surrogacy scenarios

Item

Birth mother claiming PPL and is giving child up as part of an adoption or surrogacy arrangement, and…

1

Claims 20 week (100 PPL days) continuous payment - child born on or after 1 July 2023

Jane is single and gives birth to child Michael on 1 January 2024. Jane has lodged an effective early claim for PPL claim prior to Michael's birth and indicated Michael is being adopted out. Jane:

  • provides proof of birth for Michael shortly after the birth
  • confirms that Michaels birth has been registered with Births. Deaths and Marriages
  • confirms that care has been relinquished to Michael's adoptive parents
  • nominates the date of birth as the first PPL day, and
  • chooses to receive all 100 PPL days in a single block

Jane is eligible for PPL from 1 January 2024 to 17 May 2024.

Jane would not be eligible for PPL for any period on or after 20 May 2024, as this is more than 20 weeks after the birth.

Jane is eligible to receive 100 PPL days as one continuous period.

If applicable, an employer determination will be made and PPL will be delivered via Jane’s employer

2

Claims 20 week continuous payment - child born on or after 1 July 2023

Kate is partnered and gives birth to child Luke on 1 February 2024 and relinquishes care to the commissioning parents on that day. Kate lodges an effective PPL claim on 30 March 2024.

Kate’s PPL can start from the date of birth of the child. PPL claims for children born 1 July 2023 or later can be backdated up to 100 days and starts from the date the effective claim and supporting documents are lodged.

Kate cannot be paid past 9 April 2024 under the relinquishing care rules as this is more than 20 weeks after the birth.

Kate elects to claim 100 PPL days as a continuous payment.

Kate is paid 100 all PPL days regardless of being partnered at the time of claim due to being assessed under the relinquishing care provisions.

Kate is unable to share PPL days with her partner.

3

Claims 20 week continuous payment - child born on or after 1 July 2023

Maeve gives birth to child Benny on 1 February 2024 and relinquishes care to the adoptive parents the following day.

Maeve lodged an effective claim with all required documents and chose to receive PPL in a continuous block for 20 weeks. 5 PPL days per week Mon-Fri.

On 10 March, Maeve contacts to notify a day of upcoming work on Monday 11 March 2024. As a PPL day cannot be paid for a day where more than one hour of work is performed, Maeve nominates to change the PPL for 11 March 2024 to the Saturday 16 March instead.

From 11 March Maeve is no longer receiving PPL in a continuous block (5 day week) as this block has been broken by the work day.

Maeve will need to nominate the dates for the remaining PPL days. This can be done by using the PPL online service.

Maeve can still claim the full 100 days by accessing some PPL days on the weekend. However, PPL days cannot be paid past 9 April 2024 under the relinquishing care rules, as this is more than 20 weeks after the birth.

4

Partnered Birth mother sharing PPL - child born on or after 1 July 2023

Sarah and Sam are partnered. Sarah is a surrogate birth mother for a family member.

Sarah gives birth to child Max on 1 February 2024.

As part of the surrogacy agreement Sarah and Sam are caring for the child for 14 days before they relinquish care of the child to the commissioning parents.

Sarah contacts to discuss whether they can claim and share PPL days during this period as both Sarah and her partner Sam will be taking time off work to care for the child.

As Sarah and Sam had not relinquished care of the child during the 14 day period and would both be meeting the care requirements for the child, they may be able to claim and share PPL if they meet all the eligibility requirements. However, once care of the child is relinquished, only Sarah as the birth mother would be able to continue to receive any further PPL days, and only in the 20-week period immediately following the birth.

5

Claims 18 week continuous payment - child born before 1 July 2023

Jamie gave birth to Marcus on 1 February 2023 and lodges an effective PPL claim within 28 days of giving birth. Jamie:

  • nominates the date of birth as the PPL period start date, and
  • connects the 30 Flexible days to the end of the PPL period (18 weeks continuous payment)

Jamie is eligible for PPL from 1 February 2023 to 6 June 2023. Jamie ceases to be eligible for PPL on 6 June, which is 18 weeks following the birth.

  • PPL period 1 February 2023 to 25 April 2023
  • Connected period 26 April 2023 to 6 June 2023

Jamie is eligible to receive 18 weeks PPL as one continuous period.

6

Claims 18 week continuous payment - child born before 1 July 2023

Kayla gives birth to Kevin on 1 February 2023 and lodges an effective PPL claim on 7 March 2023. As this is more than 28 days after Kevin’s birth, Kayla’s PPL period starts from the date the effective claim and supporting documents are lodged. Kayla cannot be paid past the 6 June 2023 as this is when baby Kevin is 18 weeks old.

Kayla elects to be paid the PPL as a continuous payment.

  • PPL period 7 March 2023 to 29 May 2023
  • Connected period 30 May 2023 to 6 June 2023 (5 payable week days)

Kayla is paid 12 weeks PPL period and 5 connected Flexible PPL days.

7

Claims 12 week PPL Period and Flexible PPL days - child born before 1 July 2023

Lucy gives birth to Amy on 1 February 2023 and lodges an effective PPL claim on 7 March 2023. As this is more than 28 days after Amy’s birth, Lucy’s PPL period starts from the date the effective claim and supporting documents were lodged. Lucy cannot be paid past the 6 June 2023 which is 18 weeks following the birth.

Lucy elects to be paid the PPL period and nominates consecutive Flexible PPL days to start after her PPL period.

  • PPL period 7 March 2023 to 29 May 2023
  • Flexible PPL days 30 May 2023 to 6 June 2023 (7 days)

Lucy is paid 12 weeks PPL period and 7 Flexible PPL days.

8

Claims 18 week continuous and returns to work within PPL period - child born before 1 July 2023

Rachelle gives birth to Emily on 1 February 2023 and lodges an effective PPL claim within 28 days of giving birth. Rachelle:

  • nominates the date of birth as the PPL period start date, and
  • connects the 30 Flexible PPL days to the end of the PPL period (18 weeks continuous payment)

Rachelle is eligible for PPL from 1 February 2023 to 6 June 2023. Rachelle ceases to be eligible for PPL on 6 June 2023, which is 18 weeks following the birth.

  • PPL period 1 February 2023 to 25 April 2023
  • Connected period 26 April 2023 to 6 June 2023

Rachelle contacts Services Australia to advise of returning to work on 18 April 2023. As this is within the PPL period, Rachelle’s PPL period will cease on 18 April 2023.

Rachelle is paid 11 weeks of the PPL period. Rachelle is still able to claim Flexible PPL days on days they are not working between 26 April 2023 (after the PPL period) and 6 June 2023 (18 weeks following the birth).

9

Claims 18 week continuous PPL payment and returns to work in future date - child born before 1 July 2023

Monica gives birth to Demi on 1 February 2023 and lodges an effective PPL claim within 28 days of giving birth. Monica:

  • nominates the date of birth as the PPL period start date, and
  • connects the 30 Flexible PPL days to the end of the PPL period (18 weeks continuous payment)

Monica is eligible for PPL from 1 February 2023 to 6 June 2023. Monica ceases to be eligible for PPL on 6 June 2023, which is 18 weeks following the birth.

  • PPL period 1 February 2023 to 25 April 2023
  • Connected period 26 April 2023 to 6 June 2023

Monica contacts the agency on 26 April 2023 to advise Monica is planning to return to work on 25 May 2023. The Service Officer tells Monica that PPL will be cancelled on 25 May 2023 due to returning to work

The Service Officer discusses Flexible PPL days. Monica:

  • decides to withdraw the existing connected Flexible days, then
  • nominates new Flexible PPL days (on days Monica is not working) from 26 April 2023 to 25 May 2023

This includes weekends, as Monica does not work Saturdays or Sundays.

Monica is paid 12 weeks of PPL period and 30 Flexible PPL days.
