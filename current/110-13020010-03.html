A request to contact letter is issued when an UII has been initiated.

The letter requires the customer to contact within 21 days on the phone number provided to discuss their income and asset circumstances.

Introduction, Identity Confirmation and purpose + Read more ...

'Welcome to the Services Australia Customer Compliance Line. This call may be recorded or listened to for quality and coaching purposes.'

The Compliance Officer is to introduce themselves. and then:

  • Locate the customer's record and authenticate the customer

Contact customer + Read more ...

If the customer is current and has not responded in the required time, determine if manual suspension action is needed. Make 2 genuine attempts to contact the customer. Assign the work item to themselves via Hold to User process in Table 5 of Work Optimiser for staff.

4

Manual suspension + Read more ...

This is required if the customer has not responded to the request to contact letter.

If the customer is vulnerable or at risk after investigating the customer record, see Identifying customer vulnerability and risk issues. The customer may benefit from a referral to a social worker in Integrity and Information

Date of Effect (DOE)

If the customer fails to provide requested information when responding to a section 63 or section 68 notice, the date of effect of this adverse determination is generally the day following the expiration of the period allowed.

Where the customer has already been paid for this date the date of effect for the suspension will be Date Paid to (DPT) plus 1. See the Resources page in Suspension of payments (CLK) for examples.

To manually suspend the payment, in the customer's record:

  • Select the review in IRS (this will make sure the Compliance Officer is within the IRS review, before making any updates to the customer record. This is also known as being within the 'review image').
  • Go to the Benefit Action (BA) screen. Code the following:
    • Key (payment type, for example, JSP) as the type of benefit in the Svc Rsn: field
    • Key 'SUS' in the Action: field
    • Key 'FRC' in the Reason: field
    • Press [Enter] and the date of effect will default or enter the date of effect of the suspension in the Effect Date: field
    • Complete footer details, Source: field and date of receipt in the DOR: field
    • Go to the Assessment Results (AR) screen
    • Finalise the activity
  • Update the letter contact on IRCD screen to 'N' the customer did not contact
  • Record a new contact on the IRCS/IRCD screen to place the intervention on hold (HLD) for 28 days, pending contact from the customer

To create a new contact to place the intervention on hold pending contact, go to the IRCS screen and code the following:

  • Method type: key 'OUT'
  • Date Contacted: key 'T' for today
  • Press [Enter], when the Confirm Y/N box appears, key 'Y'
  • Press [Enter] and the IRCD screen will be displayed

On IRCD screen, code the following:

  • Person Contacted: key 'customer'
  • Expected response date: 28 days in the future
  • Text: 'customer suspended (FRC) - auto advice issued. Resubmit to follow up'
    • Add a keyword to the IRS intervention. In Customer Record:
    • Go to Review Summary (IRRS) screen
    • 'T' the intervention (this will display the Manage Reviews (IRMR) screen)
    • Add the keyword 'SUSHLD'
    • Press [Enter]
  • Record the suspension action in the intervention DOC. See the Customer suspended (FRC) - Fail to contact (Manual suspension only) DOC template in the Resources page for the documenting requirements

Generate a Display on Access (DOA) DOC on the customer record to make sure that if/when the customer contacts or comes back onto payment, that the customer is referred to the Compliance Risk Branch for discussion prior to any restoration. See Table 4 under the Customer First Tab in Creating, reviewing and deleting documents (Fast Note, Callbacks, ACTDOCs and DOA DOCs) for the process to add a DOA DOC.

Note: if customer responds to the suspension letter, the DOA DOC must be end dated.

Partner records

  • If customer is partnered, a manual partner review needs to be loaded and started
  • Before any suspension action on the partner, allow the full 22 days before applying an unfavourable decision

If the customer:

  • contacts after suspension, go to Step 5
  • does not contact after 28 days, go to Step 6

5

Customer contacts about suspension action + Read more ...

When talking to the customer, be aware of the procedures to refer a customer to a social worker in Payments and Integrity and for identifying customer vulnerability and risk issues.

If the customer contacts about the suspension, follow the agency's phone contact requirements - introduction, Identity Confirmation and purpose.

Payment cannot be restored unless the customer remains eligible for the whole period covered by the restoration. The customer's record must be updated for any changes of circumstance, for example, unearned income or employment income, so incorrect arrears are not paid.

Restore and code any relevant income/assets for any entitlement periods covered by the restoration period:

  • Restore the customer's payments
  • Select the review in IRS (this will make sure the Compliance Officer is within the IRS review, before making any updates to the customer record. This is also known as being within the 'review image').
  • Go to the Benefit Action (BA) screen:
    • in the Svc Rsn: field, key the type of benefit (e.g. JSP/PPS)
    • in the Action: field, key 'RES'
    • leave the Reason: field blank
    • press [Enter] and in most cases, the Effect Date: will default to the date of suspension, if it does not, key the date the customer was suspended from
    • Code Source: and DOR: fields
  • update any unearned income (for example, bank balances) if the customer has the information available at the time of contact
  • check result on the Assessment Results (AR) screen before finalising activity. If customer is entitled to arrears, check the correct amount is to be paid
  • Annotate the existing intervention DOC, with the conversation and action taken. See the Customer payment restored DOC template in the Resources page for the documenting requirements
  • Record the contact in IRS

Once the restoration action has been completed, Compliance Officer will need to determine what stage the intervention is at and continue with the intervention.

If the customer contacts:

  • before third party verification has been requested, see Table 2
  • after third party verification has been requested, tell the customer third party information has been requested. Continue the intervention. See Table 2

For Foreign pension compliance triggers there will be no third party verification. See Requesting verification for UII.

3

Anomaly or mismatch + Read more ...

This can be identified if there is a discrepancy between the customer's details in the match data and what is provided by the customer, e.g. the match data may relate to another person. This is an 'Identification mismatch'.

Additional checks

Use all information available to determine if an anomaly or mismatch exists between the customer to determine if there in an Identification mismatch the following action is required:

  • Check for alternative spelling or misspelling of a customer's name does not automatically mean a mismatch
  • Look for a link between the identity details, e.g. parent/child, or other family relation. This may include same names, different date of birth, where an individual could have picked up another person's Tax File Number (TFN) and started using it unknowingly
  • Take extra care in the case of twins or intertwined records

Check the following screens:

  • Customer Personal Details Summary (CPDS) screen for alternative names
  • Link Summary (LS) screen and Child Selection (CHS) screens for possible parents/children
  • Document List (DL) screen and Display on Access (DOA) for any DOCs which record the use of alternative names for employment and/or other purposes
  • Address History (ADH) screen and the Online Search (OLS) facility to determine if the customer lives at that address or in that zone

If at least 2 commonalities can be identified (e.g. name and date of birth matches, or name and address matches) between the customer and the match data, it can be determined that the match data is not a mismatch and should be continued.

Can an ID anomaly or mismatch be identified from the customer record?

  • Yes, go to Step 4
  • No, continue with the intervention, go to Step 6

Compliance Officer action for Anomaly or mismatch identified + Read more ...

If a discrepancy is identified between the customer's details and the match data, referral to the CADO Service Desk is required. This will allow more checks to be undertaken.

Tell the customer that if the Anomaly/Mismatch is confirmed, the intervention will be finalised. If it is not confirmed, more information may be needed from them or the third party.

The intervention will need to be placed on HLD, go to the IRCS screen and code the following:

  • Method type: key 'OUT'
  • Date Contacted: key 'T' for today
  • Press [Enter], when the Confirm Y/N box appears, key 'Y'
  • Press [Enter] and the IRCD screen will be displayed

On IRCD screen code the following:

  • Expected response date: 35 days in the future
  • Person contacted: Service Desk
  • Text: Referred to Service Desk for ID anomaly/mismatch assessment

Further Actions:

  • Make sure the intervention DOC reflects validation of the anomaly/mismatch
  • Add the keywords 'UIIAMM' and 'UIISD' to the (IRMR) screen
  • Forward the following details via the CADO Service Desk Online Form:
    • CSAQuery: Compliance Helpdesk
    • CSAEnquiry: Compliance Helpdesk
    • Customer Reference Number (CRN)
    • Main Payment type: UII
    • Reference check: Mismatch UII review escalation

Fully document the reason/s in the referral to the CADO Service Desk Online Form.

Record the referral in the intervention DOC. See the Anomaly or mismatch DOC template in the Resources page for the documenting requirements.

Make sure to self-assign the:

  • IRS intervention into their Workgroup and Position
  • intervention DOC and any UII activities created as part of the intervention in Work Optimiser

No more action is needed until the referral has been returned from the Service Desk.

Service Desk actions if the Anomaly/Mismatch is supported:

  • Record the decision on the intervention DOC
  • Close the contact on the IRCD screen by:
    • end dating the Anomaly / Mismatch referral on the IRCD screen using 'T' for today's date (this will return the intervention back to STA)
  • Remove the hold date from the intervention DOC on the AL screen and complete the DOC
  • Remove the UIIAMM and UIISD keywords on IRMR screen
  • Cancel the review, Reason NVM
  • Send the Service Desk response to the Compliance Officer advising of actions taken

Compliance Officer action:

Once the referral has been returned from the CADO Service Desk and the decision is:

  • supported:
    • make 1 phone attempt to contact the customer and advise the outcome. If unsuccessful, no more action is needed
  • not supported, go to Step 5

Customer agrees with the match data + Read more ...

In some cases, the customer may be able to explain the discrepancy.

Some examples of reasonable explanations:

  • LTO match: the customer owns property on corner block, LTO match stating address is on one street, but the customer advised the address is on the other street
  • Investment match: the customer advised they had moved out of principle home, property is coded on Real Estate and Business (REBS) screen. Customer then advised they moved back to the principle home and the property is still on REBS. The Address History (ADH) screen shows the customer living at the principal home

If the explanation for the discrepancy is acceptable, fully document all actions and decisions on the customer's record, to finalise the intervention, see Table 2 in Requesting verification for UII.

Manual partner intervention + Read more ...

If the customer has a partner and no partner intervention is loaded, a manual partner review will need to be created:

  • If during the intervention process, it is identified that the intervention outcome could have an impact on a partner's (or ex-partner's) entitlement (current or past) a manual partner intervention must be created on the partner's record
  • This will make sure all updates made to the customer's and partner's records are attributed to the interventions
  • A manual partner intervention must not be loaded if the partner was not getting a Centrelink payment during the period of the intervention

Is a manual partner intervention required?

  • Yes, add a Manual Partner Intervention
  • No, go to Step 8

8

Scope of intervention and authority to change scope + Read more ...

Consider the following questions as a whole to assess scope.

Is verification required?

Can the customer:

  • reasonably explain the compliance trigger(s)
  • provide the information over the phone

Information to be verified

  • Unearned income and/or asset details listed in the compliance trigger on the Match Data (IRMD) screen
  • Income and Assets
  • Previously undisclosed unearned income (divulged during the conversation)

Period of verification start date

What is the start date?

The compliance trigger determines the scope start date. See the Resources page for the UII Trigger and Discussion Resource.

If the compliance trigger extends before the scope start date, information can be requested for up to 12 months before the scope start date.

If income to be verified is assessed via tax returns, the scope start date is the first day of the financial year the match/allegation date falls in.

If there are multiple compliance triggers, use the earliest Scope Start Date.

Period of verification end date

The scope end date will be the customer's income support payment Date Paid To (DPT) when deciding the scope period.

Requests may be refined if:

  • information has been previously verified
  • start/end dates of income and assets are known (and are with in scope)
  • customer/partner/ex-partner were not in receipt of income support

Scope assessment may occur at the following points:

  • initial phone conversation
  • actioning a manual suspension

Updates/verification to be requested for:

  • the determined scope period only
  • customer/partner and/or relevant ex-partners
  • where a customer and partner both have interventions they are to be assessed under the same scope

Note: a combination of documented verification and verbal updates can be used to finalise an intervention where appropriate.

  • Verbal updates: the customer provides the information over the phone
  • Documented verification: the customer or third party is required to provide documents

The Scope of the Intervention must be recorded in the Intervention DOC. See the Scope of intervention DOC template in the Resources page for the documenting requirements.

If extra information is received outside the requested scope of intervention, it must be coded by CRB.

Can the customer explain and provide all information over the phone?

  • Yes, go to Step 9
  • No, go to Step 10

9

Verbal updates + Read more ...

What to consider when taking verbal updates.

Note: verbal updates can be coded as per the actual event dates and/or at the start and end dates of investment as provided by the customer.

If using a combination of 'Documented Verification' and 'Verbal Updates':

  • Staff taking the verbal information must thoroughly document the intervention DOC to allow the information to be coded at a later date
  • Once all the information is received, staff will need to code both the verbal information and the documented verification to finalise the intervention
  • Where the Compliance Officer has determined that a rate reduction and/or overpayment is likely, they must update the customer's record with a date of event of DPT+1 to make sure the customer's ongoing payment is correct. This will avoid having debt shells on the customer's record

Family Day Care

If the customer cannot answer over the phone the initial questions listed in the UII Trigger and Discussion Resource, issue a QSS32 with the relevant questions.

Mandatory evidence

In addition to all verbal updates obtained, policy dictates that mandatory documented evidence is also required for the following triggers:

  • FDC cases as income needs to be verified
  • Undisclosed companies as Modules and supporting documents are required
  • Undisclosed investment properties as a Module R and supporting documents are required. If the property is sold, there is no need for a Mod R and verbal updates can be accepted. See the UII Trigger and Discussion Resource for more information

10

Discuss Recovery Fee + Read more ...

If the intervention includes earned income, (including self-employment) the discussing at this point in the intervention process is an education message to explain:

  • what the 10% Recovery Fee is, and
  • why the agency can impose a 10% Recovery Fee to the debt

Suggested dialogue:

  • 'Are you aware if you incorrectly declare your income from employment or self-employment, Services Australia may impose a 10% Recovery Fee to the debt?'
  • (If employee) 'To avoid being incorrectly paid and having a 10% Recovery Fee imposed, it is important that you declare all your employment income correctly on your reporting day.'
  • (if self-employed): To avoid being incorrectly paid and having a 10% Recovery Fee imposed, it is important that you update your self-employment details (sole trader/partnership/Private trust or company) if there is a significant change to your business income or assets

If the customer verbally gives evidence about why they did not correctly declare their earnings, record this in the intervention DOC. It may be used later when deciding if the Recovery Fee will apply.

If the customer is working and they get DSP or CP, they are subject to allowable hours rules

Check that the number of hours they are working does not exceed the allowable limits:

  • For DSP customers, see Commencing or returning to work Disability Support Pension (DSP)
  • For CP customers, see Carer Payment (CP) and Carer Allowance (CA) carer undertakes paid employment, self-employment, voluntary work, education or training activities
  • For CP customers, see where the care receiver commences or returns to work

Record discussion in the intervention DOC.

11

Notification obligations + Read more ...

It is important to remind the customer of their notification obligations.

This relates to how long a customer has to notify a change in their circumstances. For customers who are notification reporters, also see Reporting overview.

Customers are also obliged to advise Centrelink if there are changes to their financial and non-financial assets.

Requesting verification + Read more ...

If the customer cannot provide the information verbally during the initial phone conversation:

  • give the customer a chance to source the information
  • advise the customer
    • they must call back within the negotiated time (preferably no more than 14 days) with the information
    • if they fail to call back within that time, their payments may stop. Refer to section 63 and section 64 of the Social Security (Administration) Act 1999

If documented verification is required, give the customer the chance to provide suitable documents before contacting any third parties.

Mandatory evidence

In addition to all verbal updates obtained, policy dictates that mandatory documented evidence is also required for the following triggers:

  • FDC cases as income needs to be verified
  • Undisclosed companies as Modules and supporting documents are required
  • Undisclosed investment properties as a Module R and supporting documents are required. If the property is sold, there is no need for a Mod R and verbal updates can be accepted. See the UII Trigger and Discussion Resource for more information

Is verification of income and/or assets required?

  • Yes, customer or third party verification of income is required. See Requesting verification for UII
  • No, all the information has been received verbally, see Assessing and finalising UII
