This page contains information about providing business income estimates for Farm Household Allowance (FHA).

Parts of this process are completed by Smart Centre FHA processing staff.

1

Service Officer responsibilities + Read more ...

  • Understand and explain the importance of the business income estimates being accurate and up to date. Customers must not provide a false estimate of their farm or non-farm business income for any reason
  • Provide information about income sources to be included in each business income estimate but not determine the amount for the customer
  • Reconciliation process

Having a reasonable and accurate estimate of the customer’s business income will support accurate assessment for entitlements and reduce the risk of overpayments.

The business income estimates must :

  • reflect the farm enterprise’s and any directly related farm businesses financial circumstances for the whole financial year
  • reflect the non-farm business circumstances for the whole financial year
  • be determined by the customer. Any discrepancies or unreasonable estimates must be discussed with the customer to see if the amount must be changed

2

Providing or updating estimates + Read more ...

When claiming FHA, customers must provide an estimate of their business income for the current financial year. The estimate may be based on a projected profit and loss statement, or the latest income tax return.

The estimate must be recorded accurately even if the amount is nil or a loss.

If the customer advises their new financial year estimate will be the same as that currently assessed, the estimate must be updated from 1 July of the new financial year.

Business income estimates may be updated at any time, particularly when there has been a change in circumstances such as a downturn in business or change in stock value.

The estimate is used to calculate FHA payment rate during the financial year.

A customer's actual business income is reconciled to their estimate after the end of the financial year during the Business income reconciliation process. The reconciliation process applies to 30 June 2020.

Customers can update their business income estimate by:

  • Calling the Farmer Assistance hotline on 132 316. Transfer the customer's call if they want to update their estimate
  • Lodging a document online. The document should list all sources of income and expenditure for the farm business enterprise for the full financial year, and contain an explanation for any significant change
  • Lodging a document in person:
    • Scan the document and upload to Document Tools on the customer record
    • Select Yes to hand-off for further action and include 'FHA business income estimate, send to FHH' in the Notes section to transfer the activity to the Rural Processing team
  • Lodging a document by post. Scanned documents will be added to the national queue for classification as an unstructured document. Processing staff must be aware that unstructured documents on an FHA record could be related to farm business income estimates.

Note: customers cannot update their farm business income estimate online via myGov or their Centrelink online account.

3

View current estimate + Read more ...

To view the current FHA business income estimate:

  • On or after 14 November 2020, farm business income estimates may be recorded on:
    • REBS/BUS, and/or
    • Farm Business Income (FBI) FHA Trust and Company task in Process Direct
  • Prior to 14 November 2020, see Farm Business Income (FBI) FHA Estimate task in Process Direct

Current and previous estimates are displayed.

Note: if there is no estimate recorded, review the claim and supporting documentation to establish the estimate for the relevant financial year.

See Table 2, Assessing and recording farm business income estimates.

4

Business income estimates + Read more ...

Estimates must include business income for the financial year to date, plus expected business income and expenditure for the rest of the financial year.

If a farmer has more than one farm business, or a single farm business with entities, which are:

  • not necessarily related (such as a farm and a petrol station), profits from one cannot be offset by losses from the other
  • necessarily or directly related (for example, the related farm business cannot operate without the farm enterprise), profits from the related farm income can be offset by losses from the other

To calculate their total farm or non-farm business income they need to add all sources of income and then subtract allowable business deductions.

The online claim and Claim for Farm Household Allowance (SU694) asks customers to list sources of income, non-variable expenses and variable expenses separately.

5

Income and expenses to be included + Read more ...

Business income estimates must include:

  • All income derived from the activities of the farm enterprise and any directly related business. This includes money from the sale of livestock but may exclude money (or a portion of the amount) received from the forced disposal of livestock (see Item 7 for more information). If income is not derived from the activities of the farm enterprise or any directly related business, it is non-farm income and should not be included in the farm business income estimate
  • Expenses incurred as a result of carrying on the farm enterprise, so far as they related to the farm or any directly related business
  • The value of trading stock on hand, noting that if the value at the end of the tax year is:
    • greater than at the start of that tax year, the customer's profit from business must include the difference in these values
    • less than at the start of that tax year, the customer's profit from business must be reduced by the difference in these values

Non–farm business

  • All income derived from the activities of the non-farm business can only be offset by expenses directly related to that non-farm business
  • Treat unrelated fields of activity as separate sources of income
  • Asses each non- farm business operation in its own right

For more information about allowable deductions, see Business deductions.

6

Concessions and losses not to be included + Read more ...

The following are not allowable for FHA farm business income estimates:

  • losses from non-farm business (note: if a farmer advises of such losses, they are to be recorded and will be automatically assessed)
  • investments in Farm Management Deposits
  • income averaging
  • provisions to defer tax
  • capital expenditure deductions
  • superannuation contributions for the sole trader/ partner
  • obsolescence
  • borrowing costs
  • donations to charities
  • depreciation expenses
  • Small business entity depreciation expense
  • prior year losses

7

Forced disposal of livestock + Read more ...

When updating the farm business income estimate after a customer advises they have received income from the forced disposal of livestock:

  • include the net income received from the forced disposal of livestock that will not be deposited into a Farm Management Deposit (FMD), and
  • exclude the amount of the exemption applied to the amount intended or deposited into an FMD

See Assessing income for Farm Household Allowance (FHA).

Trust or Company structure + Read more ...

Does the customer operate their business in a Trust or Company (T&C) structure?

    • Income Tax Return (ITR) and financial statement if the customer advises the estimate will be the same as the previous financial year tax return

4

Check all estimates are reasonable + Read more ...

Generally, if a customer provides a total estimate and the calculations are correct, the estimate is considered reasonable. However, the customer must agree to make any alteration to the estimate if errors or omissions are identified.

When the estimate differs substantially from the income shown on the latest financial statements provided, for the estimate to be considered reasonable the customer must explain the variation, e.g.

  • reduced livestock numbers due to drought
  • reduced fruit volumes due to storm
  • disease or pest outbreak
  • change in commodity price
  • change in climatic conditions
  • restructure of the business
  • increase in estimate regardless of amount

If an explanation is not provided and the assessor considers the estimate is not reasonable, request a profit and loss statement.

Is the estimate reasonable?

  • Yes, go to Step 7
  • No, go to Step 5

5

Ask customer to revise estimate + Read more ...

Ask the customer to correct any errors or omissions.

If the provided estimate is verbal, seek the customer's agreement to revise their estimate immediately.

If a document has been lodged, contact the customer to discuss the estimate and seek their agreement to revise the estimate verbally.

If reasonable estimate cannot be obtained issue a Request for Information (RFI) letter to request a reasonable estimate and record details on a DOC.

Has the customer provided a reasonable estimate as requested?

  • Yes, go to Step 7
  • No, go to Step 6

6

Estimate not considered reasonable + Read more ...

If a reasonable estimate is not provided within the notified timeframe:

  • For FHA claims, reject the claim reason 'FSD'
  • If FHA is current and the customer's current estimate is not considered reasonable and the timeframe for providing the estimate has expired:
    • suspend FHA payments reason FRC
    • record details on a DOC including details of the estimate provided and any contact with the customer

Procedure ends here.

7

Cancelled due to income + Read more ...

Has it been identified the new estimate may cancel FHA due to income?

Yes:

  • If the customer is on the phone offer them the opportunity to give a new estimate
  • If the customer confirms the estimate is:
    • Correct, code the estimate. See Step 1 in Table 3
    • Incorrect, the customer can revise the estimate verbally or if related to a trust and company they will need to provide new documentation as another CAO assessment is needed. Refer to Step 2 in Table 2
  • If the customer is not on the phone, make genuine attempts to contact to advise them of cancellation and give them the opportunity to give a new estimate. If contact is successfule and the customer confirms the estimate is:
    • correct, code the estimate. See Step 1 in Table 3
    • incorrect, the customer can revise the estimate verbally or if related to a trust and company they will need to provide new documentation as another CAO assessment is needed. See Step 2 in Table 2
  • If the customer is unable to be contacted:
    • Record the details in the FHA Phone Contact Attempted Fast Note.
    • Code the estimate, See Step 1 in Table 3

No: See Step 1 in Table 3

5

Recording reasonable estimate prior to 14 November 2020 + Read more ...

To update the estimate in Process Direct

  • Open the customer record
  • Enter 'FBI' in Super Key to launch the Farm Business Income (FBI) task
  • In the Farm Business Income Estimate subtask - select Add to insert a new estimate and update
    • net estimate for the person
    • date of event (DOV)
    • For new claims, the date FHA payments will start. If the prefilled date of event is incorrect, select Edit and enter the correct date
    • For current customers providing a revised estimate, the DOV must be earlier than 14/11/2020
    • financial year details
  • Do not code the end date field. The latest estimate provided must have an end date of 31/12/9999. Any previous entries will have the end date automatically adjusted when a new estimate is recorded. Do not edit any data from previous estimates

Review the estimate details and finalise the update:

  • Select Assess
  • In the Update Header Data enter:
    • Receipt Date - the date the customer notifies
    • Channel - the channel the information was received
  • Select Save

After completing the Farm Business Income (FBI), check for and complete any Change of Circumstances (CoC) activities. These activities may be located on the Activities List (AL) and the Interaction History tab in Customer First.

Record the income estimate on REBS under the relevant sole trader or partnership farm business. The DOV recorded on REBS is from the date the new estimate is effective. For coding assistance, see Code the Business Details (BUS) screen.

Record the details in the FHA Business Income Update Fast Note.

Go to Step 6

6

Farm Business Losses + Read more ...

Farm business losses will automatically assess from 14 November 2020, if the farm business income is less than zero.

If the customer and partner request to change the way their loss is apportioned, see Assessing income for Farm Household Allowance (FHA).

Go to Step 7

7

Reassess FHA payments + Read more ...

If the updated estimate results in a rate or entitlement change, a new Social Service Plan (SSP) will be created for the customer (and their partner). This SSP will be approved once the activity is finalised in Process Direct.

Note: to view the partners SSP, select the Relations tile before finalising the activity.

If the estimate is for:

  • the current financial year, the estimate will be used to assess the rate of FHA payments from the date the estimate was provided
  • the next financial year, the estimate will be used to assess the rate of FHA payments from 1 July of the new financial year

If the business income estimate results in a nil rate of payment, the customer's FHA payments will automatically cancel reason INC. Record a cancellation in the FHA Cancelled Fast Note.

An automatic letter is sent to the customer to advise any change in their FHA payments.
