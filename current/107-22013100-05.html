Contact Details - Consultation and Referrals

Table 2

Consultation and Referrals

Instructions

Australian Taxation Office (ATO) Garnishee

Consult with ATO Garnishee Liaison Officers if an ATO Garnishee causes concern and the issue cannot be resolved locally.

The Australian Taxation Office (ATO) may confirm with the Liaison Officer that an incorrect tax garnishee has occurred. In these cases, a garnishee repayment needs to be returned to the ATO. Special instructions exist for the return of the funds and will be actioned by the Liaison Officer. See Tax garnishees to recover debts for further information.

Bankruptcy

Refer to a Bankruptcy Officers in the Collections Administration Team if a:

  • hardship refund request is received
  • dividend over recovered the debt
  • repayment source over recovered a debt written off under:
    • Bankruptcy Pending (BRP)
    • Bankruptcy Awaiting Estate (BRT)
    • Bankruptcy Discharged, No Further Estate/No Fraud (BRD), or
    • Debt Agreement Current (DAC)

To refer hardship refund requests:

  • consult via phone with a Bankruptcy Officer in the Collections Administration Team
  • the Bankruptcy Officer may recommend warm transferring the customer to their team

To refer over recovered work items:

  • add the ISIS Activity Keyword to the Activity on AL
  • see Is a refund of repayments payable to a bankrupt customer

BPAY

Refer customers to their financial institution if they request a hardship refund for a BPAY repayment. The customer can lodge a claim to reverse the repayment.

  • Email Finance Officers in the Collections Administration Team to enquire about unusual BPAY repayments and identified BPAY claims

Credit card and direct debit

Refer customers to their Financial Institution if they request a hardship refund for a credit card and direct debit. The customer can lodge a claim to reverse the repayment.

Department of Veterans' Affairs (DVA) Withholdings

Consult with DVA Liaison Officers if withholdings from a DVA payment causes concern and the issue cannot be resolved locally.

External Collection Agencies (ECA)

Customers can ask about repayments on a debt currently with an ECA. They can call or be transferred to the relevant agency (ARL Collect Pty Ltd, Milton Graham or Probe Group). This includes if the customer requests a hardship refund. For contact details see, External Collection Agents (ECA) - debt collection agencies.

Consult with ECA Liaison Officers about an over recovered work item by emailing the Collection Agent Liaison Team.

See: External Collection Agents (ECA).

Family Assistance offsetting

Transfer the customer to Families and Parenting if they request a hardship refund for an amount offset from:

  • Family Tax Benefit (FTB) reconciliation top-ups and lump sum claim entitlements (including FTB supplements)
  • Family Tax Benefit (FTB) instalment arrears
  • Child Care Subsidy (CCS) entitlements delivered at reconciliation
  • Child Care Benefit (CCB) reconciliation top-ups and lump sum claim entitlements
  • Child Care Rebate (CCR) entitlements delivered at reconciliation
  • Tax refunds (excluding tax refunds received through garnishee action)

Approval for the hardship refund must be recorded on the file before a refund is actioned. The approval comes from the National Families and Childcare Level 2 Policy Helpdesk.

Debt staff can email the Level 2 Policy Helpdesk (Families and Childcare) to seek FAO Offsetting Level 2 support.

See Debt offsetting during family assistance reassessment, reconciliation and claim processing.

Garnishee of Compensation Payment

Refer to Compensation Officers in the Compensation Recovery Team if a:

  • hardship refund request is received
  • garnishee of compensation payment over recovered the debt

To refer hardship refund requests:

  • consult via phone with a Compensation Officer
  • the Compensation Officer may recommend warm transferring the customer to their team.

To refer over recovered work items:

  • reallocate an activity to the region code (CTN) with the keywords DORMFU and DORCMP
  • recategorise a Work Item to ZIAM_CMM_DBT

Proceed with the hardship or over recovered refund if approval from a Compensation Officer is already recorded on the file.

See Compensation garnishees to recover debts and Garnishee customer does not agree with decision.

Report Suspected Fraud

Where any type of fraud is suspected, go to Report Suspected Fraud and Corruption - Intranet to complete and submit a Suspected Fraud form.

Work Items administered by specialist teams

Debt staff can refer Work Items to specialist teams by:

  • re-categorising Work Items in Work Optimiser
  • emailing Workforce Strategy Analytics and Reporting
  • re-allocating Work Items for case managed debts to the relevant team in Intensive Debt Collections

Unusual repayments

A repayment may be unusual and cause concern. Attempt to resolve the issue locally. If unable to resolve, consult with a Finance Officer in the Collections Administration Team.
