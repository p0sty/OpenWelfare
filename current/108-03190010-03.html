4

Payments that are compensatory in nature + Read more ...

  • Criminal injuries under a statutory scheme
  • Death of a relative. For example, deceased dependant payments paid to a surviving partner. See Assessment of income for Centrelink payments
  • Discrimination
  • Unfair dismissal
  • Work place harassment

Assess these payments as ordinary income.

For CRT, record that these payments are not compensation in a Fast Note and code the payments on the Other Income (OIN) screen.

Code payments on the OIN screen

Process Direct

  • Go to the OIN screen
  • Select Add New Row
  • On Create Other Income, complete:
    • Income Type: OTH - OTHER
    • Description: income source
    • Reference: the reference number or claim number if known
    • Start Date: the start date of the income source
    • Frequency: select
    • Gross Amount: the amount of income
    • Select Save
  • Complete the Receipt Date and Channel fields, select Save
  • Select Assess and address any errors/warnings
  • Selecting Assess again
  • Check payment correctness on ELD, Select Finish
  • Record details of the update
  • Select Finalise to complete the transaction

Customer First

  • Go to OIN and code
  • Type: OTH
  • Description: enter the type of payment and source
  • Reference: the reference number or claim number
  • Event Date: date of event
  • Gross Amount $: amount of other income
  • Freq: how often the customer receives the amount of income, for example, 2WE or MTH

5

Sickness and accident insurance policies + Read more ...

These include:

  • Personal injury income protection
  • Trauma insurance

Payments from sickness and accident insurance policies are ordinary income when:

  • the customer paid or contributed to the premium, and
  • there is no offset clause or an offset clause that is not invoked against a social security pension or benefit

Note: If the employer has made contributions to the policy and the off-set clause is invoked, treat the payments as compensation. See Step 8.

Are payments to be treated as ordinary income?

  • Yes, CRT must be coded on OIN. Go to Step 7
  • No, payments are treated as compensation. Go to Step 8

6

Payments made by a superannuation fund + Read more ...

When payments are made by a superannuation fund, CRT must:

  • code payments on OINS
  • record that these payments are not compensation in a Fast Note
  • finalise the CMS record

Note: do not send any letters auto generated by the system.

Go to Step 7.

Customer First: + Read more ...

    • Type: OTH
    • Description: enter the type of payment and source
    • Reference: enter the reference or claim number
    • Start Date: date of event for the change or start date of an assessment period
    • Gross Amount $: amount of income

  • Record that these payments are not compensation in a Fast Note

8

Periodic payments to be treated as compensation + Read more ...

Record the compensation on the Compensation Management System (CMS) in the customer's record. See Treating periodic compensation payments as a direct deduction or income.

Any periodic compensation received by the customer may be a direct deduction from the rate of their CAP. If this prevents them being paid and they have a partner, then an excess periodic compensation amount may be calculated and transferred to the partner as other ordinary income.

For information on how to record the details of the customer's compensation, see Role of staff outside Compensation Teams.
