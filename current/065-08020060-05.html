Risk profiling criteria

Table 5

Risk Category

Reasons for review profiling

High risk XHI

A review has not been completed in the last 12 months.

The HEAS balance is greater than zero, and any one of the following criteria is met:

  • The HEAS security property has an assessable asset value on the Real Estate/Business Summary (REBS) screen greater than zero, and the customer or partner is getting payments for:
    • Age Pension
    • Disability Support Payment, or
    • Carer Payment
  • The mortgage deduction field in REBS for the HEAS security is greater than zero
  • The deduction field on the HEAS Details (PLS/HEAS) screen is greater than zero
  • The preceding real estate indexation file for the postcode of any property as the HEAS security shows a reduction in value
  • The current market value of a property linked as security has reduced
  • The customer’s ownership of the property has reduced in the review period
  • The customer has appointed a new nominee or power of attorney in the review period

From 1 July 2022, new risk review indicators apply to current and new HEAS customers that:

  • get fortnightly loan payments, or
  • are in a HEAS advance period, or
  • have a HEAS loan balance above zero

These new risk review indicators are:

  • The customer is at risk of having the No Negative Equity Guarantee (NNEG) applied. A review for the partner is also created
  • A customer regularly takes the full HEAS advance payment

Medium risk XMD
  • A review has not been done in the last 2 years, and
  • the HEAS balance is greater than zero, and
  • there are multiple HEAS security properties

Low risk XLO

The HEAS balance is greater than zero, and a review has not been done for at least 3 years.
