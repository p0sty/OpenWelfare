Special circumstances - family and domestic violence and the work test + Read more ...

Customers who do not meet the DAP work test are asked in their online claim if there were any special circumstances that affected their ability to meet the work test. If a customer indicates they were affected by family and domestic violence, a priority keyword (PPLFDV) is automatically added to the claim work item.

The customer must provide evidence of employment confirming they would have worked during the work test period. The customer's employer may not know why the customer was unable to work, and the customer is not expected to tell their employer.

A social worker referral needs to be completed when allocated a family and domestic violence claim.

Before sending a social working referral, check the customer's employment evidence lodged with their claim to assess if sufficient. For evidence requirements, see Paid Parental Leave scheme Work Test.

If not sufficient, attempt to contact the customer by phone to request the missing claim information. Issue a written request for information for the employment evidence (even if verbally discussed with customer) using Request Documents.

Before calling, use Desktop Electronic Messaging Capability (DEMC) to advise the customer they will receive a phone call.

If the customer needs help from a Social Worker to get the relevant employment evidence, include this in the referral DOC/Notes.

To create a referral to a Social Worker:

  • Within the DAP claim select Referral
  • Select the following options:
    • Referral type: Social Worker Referral
    • Referral reason: Family and Domestic Violence
    • Referral Detail: Third Party Verification
  • Include the following in Additional Information:
    • Customer has been referred for a social work assessment for DAP/PPL FDV special circumstances
    • Date of Contact:
    • Work Test Period (13mths):
    • Referring Officer (Logon ID):
    • Additional Information (if required): please consider any relevant information regarding contact with the customer e.g. most appropriate contact number, availability and any other information including safety and wellbeing concerns. E.g. Mrs Smith is available Monday and Tuesday between 9-1:30pm on mobile number 0444 444 444. Customer has advised not to leave a message

Add a Note to the customer's record:

  • Select category: Claim
  • Copy and paste into the fields:
    • Summary: PPL/DAP Spec Circ work test
    • Content:
      Customer claimed <PPL><DAP> on <date of receipt> via <claim type – eg online claim, FA080 etc>
      13-month work test period is <start date of work test period> to <end date of work test period>.
      Customer does not meet work test but advised of special circumstances under s 24B(2)(a) of the Paid Parental Leave Rules 2021.
      Customer has <provided><not provided> evidence of <circumstances> / <employment> with their claim
    • Referred for social worker assessment
    • Claim placed on hold until <date hold is due to end>

Annotate progress of claims to refer staff to PPL/DAP Spec Circ work test DOC on the record.

Due to privacy and safety concerns, do not use the term 'family and domestic violence' on the customer's record. Instead, use the phrase s 24B(2)(a) of the Paid Parental Leave Rules 2021. For example, the social worker assessment will advise the customer meets/does not meet the requirement per s 24B(2)(a) of the Paid Parental Leave Rules 2021. Fast Notes for grant/reject/NEF claims must also refer to this section and not 'family and domestic violence'. Note: this does not apply to the Social Work Referral text as this does not populate onto the customer's record.

Place the claim On hold with reason Referral - Social Worker for 21 days.

When the social worker assessment has been completed, they will take the claim off hold and annotate PPL/DAP Spec Circ work test DOC with the outcome and return it for processing/allocation. Once the assessment has been completed, continue processing the claim.

Further information required + Read more ...

Proof of birth is required to finalise a pre-birth DAP claim

  • A customer can provide a fully completed FA081 to finalise a pre-birth DAP claim. Customers can upload the completed FA081 via upload documents. If upload documents cannot be used, the customer can provide the document by post or in person at a service centre. If the DAP customer is unable to lodge the fully completed FA081, other acceptable proof of birth must be provided (for example, birth certificate)
  • If the customer has uploaded the back page of the FA081 only, contact the customer to request the fully completed FA081 be uploaded or provided or alternative proof of birth (for example, birth certificate)
  • If the customer has lodged an incomplete FA081, print all pages of the incomplete scanned FA081 lodged and send a Request for Information (RFI) as per Information requests for FTB, PPL and DAP claims and Add Newborn activities
  • A biological father not partnered to the birth mother can claim DAP for their child if they meet the eligibility requirements. These customers need to provide proof of birth for the child. Where the birth mother has not provided proof of birth and the customer cannot provide the back page of the FA081 they will need to provide other acceptable proof of birth to verify the child's birth

Proof of birth lodged without a claim

  • Where the customer has not lodged a claim and has only lodged the completed FA081, the back page of the FA081 or alternative proof of birth, a claim for payment will need to be completed before the customer can be assessed

Timeframes for return of documents/information

Customers are to provide:

  • Requested documents/information within 14 days (21 days for remote area or overseas customer)
  • Tax File Numbers (TFNs) and/or bank account details within 28 days

Hold the claim for a minimum of 22 days to allow extra time for mail delivery and for customers to respond.

10

DAP may be paid for more than one of the children + Read more ...

If there is a significant delay between the births of the children from the same pregnancy, for example 5 days apart, the births may be considered separate birth events and not part of a multiple birth. In these instances, DAP may be payable for each child.

The customer must provide additional evidence to support their claims before the claim for the subsequent child can be assessed. Evidence could include a letter from the hospital or midwife where the children were delivered and confirming the children were born during separate birthing events and not as part of a multiple birth. If this has not been provided, contact the customer to request the evidence and any other outstanding information, see Requesting information (CLK). Desktop Electronic Messaging Capability (DEMC) can be used to advise the customer of receiving a phone call.

  • If necessary, request for additional evidence as per Information requests for FTB, PPL and DAP claims and Add Newborn activities
  • The claim will be automatically placed on hold in Process Direct
  • The new claim activity on the AL screen in Customer First will also need to be placed on hold manually for the appropriate timeframe

Service Officers must refer these cases to the Level 2 Policy Helpdesk with all relevant details for investigation.

If the Level 2 Policy Helpdesk team:

  • confirms the child is eligible for DAP and the claim is rejecting MCR:
    • navigate to the PPL Exemption (P1EX) screen, and
    • select Exemption Reason: Multiple children accepted
  • does not confirm DAP eligibility, go to Step 11

Residency Assessment + Read more ...

If the customer notifies they are overseas or departing to go overseas within 21 days of lodging their claim, handoff to Families International Claims (FIC). See Pre-processing checks for standalone and combined claims for family assistance and/or Paid Parental Leave payments.

To check if the customer is overseas:

  • Go to the RSIMME/CHIMME screen
  • Check and update Country of Birth if required
  • Update any travel documents on the Immigration Enquiry table
  • Select Request Immigration Data

The customer's Australian residence qualification will be automatically assessed when Assess is selected, based on the residence information recorded.

Residence information must still be recorded if the claim is to be rejected for a reason other than residence. If residence details are not recorded, the system will override any other rejection reason and the claim will reject for a residence related reason.

For help recording residence details, see Residence and Portability screens.

The new claim process will attempt a datalink with the Department of Home Affairs. If successful, the datalink will automatically record a customer's visa and movement information from 1 September 1994, and grants of Australian citizenship where available.

Check the assessment and ensure DAP is only granted when the customer has been residentially qualified for every day in the DAP period. Where the assessment result is incorrect, the details must be investigated through normal escalation processes. For example, to National ICT Referral Officer (NIRO) or Service Support Officer (SSO).
