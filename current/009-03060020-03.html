Invitation letters for CTAT + Read more ...

The CTAT invitation letter issued to eligible carers (online or via post) explains how to complete and submit the CTAT application. Forms are not included with the invitation letter.

2

CTAT online task, forms and lodgement due dates + Read more ...

The CTAT is not a ‘new claim’. It is a streamlined process to support an Adult Disability Assessment Tool (ADAT) assessment process.

Online Task

All carers must be encouraged to complete the CTAT online when possible. The carer question set is completed online, rather than completing a form. Supporting documents and an SA332A can be uploaded within the task.

Forms

If a carer is unable or unsuitable to complete the online CTAT they must lodge 2 forms by the due date:

  • Child to adult transfer for Carer Payment and/or Carer Allowance (SA432), and
  • Carer Payment and/or Carer Allowance Medical Report for a person 16 years or over (SA332A)

Lodgement due date

A CTAT must be submitted online, or paper forms lodged, before the:

  • child is 16 years for CA Health Care Card (HCC) only
  • child is 16 years 3 months for CP and CA
  • child is 18 years for CP (terminal), and
  • end of the eligibility period for CP short term/episodic

Note: For HCC only CA (child) carers, when the care receiver reaches 15 years and 9 months, an invitation letter is issued. This letter advises the carer of loss of entitlement to a HCC when the care receiver turns 16 years. CA (adult) does not include a HCC for the care receiver. If the carer is no longer entitled to CA (child), the care receiver may be entitled to an ex-CA (child) HCC.

If the transfer is not submitted by the time the child turns 16 years and 3 months (or 18 if terminal) their CA and/or CP will stop.

A CTAT transfer will be backdated to the date of cancellation of the child-level entitlement if the:

  • carer submitted their transfer application before the due date
  • the application was not processed by Services Australia until after the due date, and
  • the carer is qualified

If the CTAT forms are lodged after CP/CA has cancelled and it appears the carer was given incorrect or insufficient information, refer the case to the Carer Helpdesk for advice on deemed date of lodgement.

Note - Exceptions: CTAT submission exceptions apply if a carer cannot complete an online CTAT if, when starting their CTAT, they state they are in a crisis situation or caring for someone with a terminal illness. Tell carers who meet the exception criteria to upload/provide all Required tasks as soon as possible.

6

Missing documents + Read more ...

If documents are missing, tell the carer:

  • what documents are required
  • when they need to submit the documents. Late submission may result in:
    • delays in processing their CTAT, and/or
    • their payment being cancelled
  • once the documents have been provided, they need to submit the CTAT. Payment cannot usually be restored if the documents are lodged after CP/CA has been cancelled

Carers can provide documents by:

  • uploading online, or
  • taking them to their nearest service centre to be scanned to their record

If a carer states on the CTAT form that the care receiver will not continue to live with them:

  • for paper forms, complete a SA381 - Carer Allowance Questionnaire - Carer not living with the person being cared for. Note: the carer must return the SA381 before finalising the CTAT
  • for online CTATs, complete the questions as part of the CTAT application

Note: when documents are uploaded outside of the CTAT task, by either the carer or a Service Officer, they must be marked as 'provided' in the CTAT integrated review activity and the status updated to 'accepted.' If this is not done the carer will not be able to submit the CTAT.

Tell the carer:

  • if the online transfer is not submitted by the due date, CP and/or CA will be cancelled
  • for paper forms, if only 1 form is sent back, they must supply the missing form before the due date or payment(s) may cancel
  • if the missing form is sent back after payment(s) have cancelled, standard ‘review of decision’ rules apply

7

Backdating CTAT + Read more ...

The CTAT can only be backdated to the cancellation date if all the following apply:

  • CP and/or CA has cancelled due to the child turning 16 (HCC only), 16 years and 3 months (or 18 years if terminal)
  • the carer submitted an application before the due date, and
  • the carer qualifies under adult provisions

If all of these are not met, the carer will need to re-claim by:

  • submitting an CP/CA online claim, or
  • have an Assisted Customer Claim (ACC) run

See Claiming Carer Payment (CP) and/or Carer Allowance (CA).

If the CTAT forms are lodged after CP/CA has cancelled and it appears the carer was given incorrect or insufficient information, refer the case to the Carer Helpdesk for advice on deemed date of lodgement.

8

CTAT submitted without required documentation + Read more ...

The carer must provide supporting documents when submitting their CTAT. These include:

  • SA332A (medical report)
  • carers identity, if not confirmed

A carer can submit a CTAT without documents if they meet the exception criteria. Do not reject a CTAT submitted under an exception circumstance:

  • until 14 days have passed, and
  • the carer’s child entitlement is current

Note: do not reject a CTAT FRC. Place on hold until the care receiver’s qualification date. The CP/CA will auto-cancel on this date.

9

Assessing the carer and Treating Health Professional (THP) questionnaires + Read more ...

If the carer or THP has:

  • ticked more than 1 answer, accept the answer that results in the highest Adult Disability Assessment Tool (ADAT) score
  • not answered a care question, contact the carer to give them the opportunity to provide an answer. Do not contact when:
  • the ADAT produces a qualifying score, and
  • there are unanswered questions which have no bearing on the carer’s qualification for CP or CA. Record details on a DOC on carer’s record

A functional assessment rating is calculated for each portion of the ADAT. For example, the carer portion and the THP portion. The THP score must reach the required level before taking the carer's score into account. The care receiver may qualify a carer for CP and/or CA if the:

  • THP score reaches the required level, and
  • total ADAT score is met for the payment

10

Short term/episodic care period + Read more ...

Carers who receive CP for a child with a short term or episodic condition can submit a CTAT. However the CTAT must be processed as a new claim and should only be processed once the short term or episodic medical certificate has expired.

Note: the CTAT or new claim can be backdated to the date of cancellation of the child-level entitlement if CP has cancelled as a result of the child turning 16 years and 3 months (or 18 years if terminal) and the carer sent in their transfer application before the due date and qualifies under the adult provisions.

110

Temporary absence + Read more ...

If the care receiver is in a period of temporary absence at the adult transition determination date, eligibility is not impacted. Exceptions to this for CP are if the respite absence quota will be exceeded:

  • before care receiver's 16th birthday (or 18th under terminal care provisions). The payment will cancel when the respite exceeds the quota. The carer will receive a notification letter
  • after their 16th birthday but before 28 days + Entitlement Period End Date (EPED) from the system date. The transition to adult payment will be granted

There will be a prompt to contact the carer if the child is future assessed but the respite absence quota will exceed:

  • before their 16th birthday (or 18th under terminal care provisions), and
  • this date is greater than 28 days + EPED from the system date

Check CTAT requirements are met + Read more ...

Care receiver getting CA for another adult care receiver

If the carer claims CA for an adult care receiver, the care receiver cannot also qualify for CA for another adult.

Before granting, rejecting or cancelling any CA payments, additional information will be required from the carer and the care receiver to clarify the circumstances and determine if the care receiver has ceased providing care.

More than one care receiver

A person may qualify for CA for up to 2 adults with a disability who meet the CA (adult) criteria. They may also qualify for each dependent child with a disability that meets the CA (child) criteria, regardless of the number of children.

Identity

Care receivers subject to CTAT:

  • Are not required to prove their identity. If the care receiver provides identity documents see Alternative Identity
  • Must not have a GD review applied. If there is an Identity review in the Identity Confirmation dashboard in Process Direct, cancel it
  • See process in Alternative Identity to complete identity requirements - Coding Known to Centrelink (KC)

Note: if a Service Officer codes identity for the carer resulting in their identity being confirmed, an identity task will display as a Required task within the CTAT. When the Identity Status is Confirmed, the outstanding task must be updated to Provided. This action will allow the transfer to be finalised.

Check requirements and supporting documents are met for:

  • Australian residence (where applicable)
  • medical reports provided
  • other information or documents required to assess the CTAT

Conduct Service Offer Interview (SOI) + Read more ...

Before the CTAT is finalised, conduct a Service Offer Interview (SOI) for all CTAT activities.

Make genuine attempts to contact the customer and/or nominee.

Include the following steps in the SOI to advise the carer:

  • the outcome of the CTAT (continuation of CA payment under adult provisions or cancellation of CA)
  • the CA HCC cancels immediately upon assessment of the CTAT
  • options for the child to claim a payment in their own right if they have not already done so or confirm receipt of claim and explain KPI.
    For example, Disability Support Pension or Youth Allowance if they have not already done so, or confirm
  • options for the child to claim, if they have not already done so, the Ex-Carer Allowance HCC if they are a full time student or a Low Income Health Care Card (LIC). Where relevant, confirm receipt of claim and KPI
  • option to become a nominee for their child , to support them to claim any payments and/or concessions and engaging with the agency
  • if the child will be making a claim for a payment or card in their own right, they must complete the Identity Confirmation process. Also advise the carer of the outcome for the Carer Payment as a result of the CTAT assessment

Additionally, if the child is available to speak with, discuss:

  • creating a myGov account and linking services
  • an Assisted Customer Claim (ACC) for Ex-Carer HCC, or offer a warm transfer to the main business line for the ACC to be completed

Note: while the decision to continue payment of CA under the adult provisions is not an adverse decision, the transfer from CA (child) to CA (adult) results in the immediate cancellation of the CA HCC.

If the carer advises:

  • they have medications due for their child in the coming days,
  • and expresses concern about how they will afford the full price scripts, and
  • the child has NOT lodged an Ex-carer HCC claim, advise the carer:
    • the CA HCC cancels as soon as the CTAT is processed
    • the CTAT work item can be held for a maximum of 1 week to allow the carer time to purchase any medications using the CA HCC whilst it is still current. If the carer accepts this, the CTAT work item must be held to user for a maximum of 1 week

If it is evident that it will be difficult for the child to attend the service centre for Identification Confirmation, consider Alternative Identity provisions. Where appropriate, this can be actioned over the phone.

Finalise the CTAT + Read more ...

Select Finish and check the details of the displayed document:

  • the outcome must match the benefit type and grant. For example, benefit type and grant or cancellation outcome, customer name, care receiver name, CRN and ADAT/THP scores
  • key ‘Grant date’, and
  • key notes

Update the finalisation document with the correct outcome. If the CTAT review is for the combined CP/CA and the outcome is grant for 1 benefit, cancel the other:

  • select Finalise
  • close all other opened documents
  • to process the CTAT, select the relevant scanned documents

Note: close all documents that are open in Customer First.

Select Complete to update the provisional data to Confirmed.

The CTAT is finalised and CTAT work item closed.

2

Temporary Cessation of Care (TCC) and hospitalisation rules following transition from CP/CA child to adult + Read more ...

Carers who transition to CP (adult) are entitled to a Pensioner Concession Card (PCC). The total hospitalisation absences are reset to zero when the care receiver qualifies the carer for CP/CA (adult).

Adult care receivers have a 63 day limit per calendar year for hospitalisation absences. There is no limit for hospitalisation absences for child care receivers. There is a 63 day limit each calendar year for respite absences.

Note: where the child care receiver qualifies the carer for CP (adult) and/or CA (adult), the system will assess the accumulated respite absences for the calendar year. Unlike hospitalisation, respite provisions are not reset to zero.

3

Payment cancelled due to non-return of the CTAT + Read more ...

Carers who are determined as eligible under adult provisions must, before the child level of entitlement is cancelled, submit a CTAT online or lodge a CTAT paper form for the payment to continue.

If payments have cancelled, carers will need to lodge a new claim.

Note: carers who lodged CTAT paper forms before the child level of entitlement is cancelled do not have to complete a new claim.

Refer cases to the Carer Helpdesk if the carer is lodging online and:

  • has difficulties submitting the CTAT by the due date, or
  • assumes that the CTAT has been submitted but failed to select the submit button

The Carer Helpdesk will determine if the CTAT submission can be accepted.
