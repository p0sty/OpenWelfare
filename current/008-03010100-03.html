3

Reassess Rejected Claim - for reason FSD/FRC/POI/IME/FRP or GCD + Read more ...

If staff receive a Reassess Rejected Claim Fast Note, review previous claim to make sure the claim meets the requirements to reopen.

Do not reopen and reassess a DSP claim where:

  • the request is made more than 13 weeks after rejection notice given, or
  • not all requested information has been provided within 13 weeks of the rejection notice
    • if any of these occur, see Requested documentation not provided resulting in rejection of new claim, for action required
  • the DSP claim was rejected for any other reason, see Reviews and Appeals

Where the claim can be reopened and reassessed, action required depends on previous rejection reason. If the claim was previously rejected for reason:

  • FSD/FRC or POI:
    • reindex claim
    • review previous rejection DOC to determine action required to progress claim
    • go to Table 3
  • FRP (Failed to Report):
    • Review customer circumstances to confirm customer is eligible to DSP from original date of claim
    • Check customer has provided all information for the outstanding reporting periods
    • Reindex claim, remove reporting requirements, code employment income for outstanding reporting periods and finalise claim
    • After finalising claim, place the customer on reporting type EAN, see Reporting screens
  • IME:
    • Reindex claim and refer to MAT via Medical Assessment Team Referral
    • Use the Referral reason 'New evidence'
    • Procedure ends here until MAT has been completed
    • When MAT is completed, go to Table 3 to review MAT and stream claim
  • GCD:
    • refer to Disability Medical Assessment (DMA) appointment outcome to reopen and progress claim

Close the Reassess Rejected claim Fast Note when required action has been taken.

Duplicate/Re-provided claims + Read more ...

Duplicate claims (where a second claim is submitted within 13 weeks of a previous claim)

Claims for DSP can be submitted at any time. If a customer lodges a DSP claim within the qualification period (for example, 13 weeks) of a previously submitted DSP claim, Service Officers must determine how each claim should be progressed.

Attempt to contact the customer/nominee to establish their reason or intent in lodging a second claim.

Customers can also choose to withdraw the subsequent claim. See Cancel or Withdraw an online claim and Withdrawal of claims. Tell the customer that when a claim is withdrawn, they cannot:

  • ask for the claim to be reinstated
  • appeal the decision

Do not withdraw a claim if the customer has not been contacted. If contact is not successful, or the customer wishes to pursue assessment of the subsequent claim, progress claim per normal process. Go to Step 2.

For help with multiple claims, or where the customer has attended a JCA/DMA supporting grant, for the second DSP Claim, Service Officers can contact Level 2 Policy Helpdesk. See Resources page.

If the customer is seeking reassessment of a previously rejected claim, see Initial contact about a decision and the review of decision process. Go to Step 2.

Re-provided claims (where customer has lodged the same DSP paper claim form)

A re-provided claim can be considered a legal claim, if the claim is complete, and meets the claim lodgement requirements, See Claim lodgement of Centrelink claims.

Action required depends on the status of the original DSP claim and contact with the customer/nominee to confirm the reason for lodging the second claim. A claim cannot be cancelled or withdrawn, without the customer/nominee agreeing to it.

Review the DSP Claim Progress Doc/ Notes to determine the status of original claim.

Where the original DSP Claim is still in progress, and the customer has either:

  • mailed, lodged or faxed the claim form after uploading, or uploaded multiple times, or
  • returned the completed claim form (to address missing information and/or signature) within the requested time frame, see Claims received that are incomplete or incorrect:
    • Contact the customer/nominee and confirm reason for lodging the second claim
    • If the customer agrees, cancel the second (later) DSP Claim SOA in Process Direct - Not Required
    • Annotate the second DSP Claim Progress DOC to advise claim has been marked as Not Required and include why
    • Progress the original DSP Claim, and annotate DSP Claim Progress DOC

Where the original DSP claim was rejected in the last 13 weeks:

  • Try to contact the customer/nominee to establish their reason for re-lodging the claim
  • If the customer lodged the claim seeking a review of original claim decision, they can withdraw the second claim and either ask for a:
    • reassessment of the original claim (if rejection reason was FRC/FSD/POI/IME or GCD), see Request to reassess a rejected claim, or
    • SME Explanation or Formal review (for any other rejection reason), see Reviews & Appeals
  • If a customer/nominee cannot be contacted, or they do not agree to withdraw the second claim, the claim must be progressed, as normal, go to Step 2

Where the re-provided claim is lodged more than 13 weeks after previous rejection:

  • If the claim is complete, and meets the definition of a legal claim, progress the claim as normal, go to Step 2

For help with re-provided claims, contact Level 2 Policy Helpdesk.

Staff must determine if the customer has a clear ability to work at least 15 hours per week in open employment.

  • Consider the circumstances before progressing a claim where MAT recommend:
  • manifest medical eligibility
  • there is a current and valid assessment supporting DSP eligibility, or
  • the customer requires a JCA referral, and
  • the customer is working at least 15 hours per week

9

Querying a MAT recommendation + Read more ...

If a Service Officer has a query or concern with the MAT recommendation about clear error(s) or omission(s) only, refer the query to MAT. Examples include:

  • not all fields in the SA479 completed
  • SA479 not uploaded (when required)
  • incorrect referral reason. For instance, SA479 indicates recommendation is for a new claim when in fact it is for an appeal
  • incorrect date of claim, or
  • no MAT Recommendation DOC recorded, or
  • MAT recommend manifest but have not assessed where CITW occurred (where applicable)
  • MAT have recommended 'Current and Valid' but assessment was completed under the incorrect version of the Impairment Tables

If the MAT issue can be escalated by the Service Officer:

  • run the Streaming and Progress DOC script. Select 'Policy' as the on hold reason
  • update the DSP new claim Progress DOC to advise the details of issue with the MAT Recommendation
  • make sure DSP claim and Progress DOC are on hold for 14 days
  • use Fast Note - select Auto text, use Disabilities > Claims > Template - DSP MAT Recommendation Query > Report Incomplete
  • add details of query. For example, 'Assessment to determine if CITW occurred in Australia is required.'
  • wait for MAT to complete the review. Once completed, go to Step 10

Any other concerns, for example issues related to sensitive information that are not to be recorded on a DOC, must be escalated to a Service Support Officer (SSO):

  • run the Streaming and Progress DOC script. Select 'Policy' as the on hold reason
  • update the DSP new claim Progress DOC to advise the details of issue with the MAT Recommendation
  • make sure the DSP claim and Progress DOC are on hold for 14 days
  • apply hold to user
  • refer the case to the SSO via the Direct Referral to SSO web form, use escalation type 'Check JCA/ESAt/DMA Report'
  • wait for the query to be reviewed. Once reviewed, go to Step 9

Finalise streaming the DSP new claim + Read more ...

Service Officers are to review MAT and reviewed query.

If no further MAT referral is required:

  • update the DSP new claim Progress DOC with receipt and outcome of the query
  • take the NCL activity off hold
  • finalise the SSO enquiry in the escalation inbox

If further MAT referral is required:

  • create an open work item. Use Fast Note - select Auto text, use Disabilities > Claims > Template - DSP MAT Recommendation Query
  • edit the Fast Note auto text as appropriate. High level information about the reason for escalation must be included in the DOC. For example, manifest eligibility reason selected in the report is inconsistent with the contents of the report
  • update the DSP new claim Progress DOC advising a new referral to MAT has been actioned and processing staff are to refer to the most recent UNSPOC/SA479 or MAT Query referral DOC for outcome
  • make sure the claim and progress DOC remain on hold
  • finalise the SSO enquiry in the escalation inbox

Note: do not include sensitive information in the DOC, such as details of medical conditions and manifest codes. Instead, email this information to the Medical Assessment Team and the processing Service Officer. The email address for MAT is listed on the Assessment Services contact details. Make sure the escalation reason is clear for someone who may not have reviewed the report.

If the SSO sends an email to the Medical Assessment Team, they:

  • are not to send the DSP MAT Recommendation Query Fast Note as this duplicates the query
  • are to continue to manage the case and not finalise the enquiry in the escalation inbox. Once a response from MAT is received, the SSO enquiry can be finalised

MAT will:

  • review the escalation request, this may include revising the original recommendation and completing a new report
  • upload the new report via Document Tools or annotate the MAT referral request DOC if no new assessment completed
  • reply to the SSO email to advise of the outcome of the query, and
  • take the NCL activity off hold
