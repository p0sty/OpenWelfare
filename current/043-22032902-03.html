Launch ADMS + Read more ...

For information on requesting access to ADMS and launching ADMS see the Resources page.

2

Locating claims + Read more ...

Select View Claim Applications in the Actions section of the home screen.

Use the Search box to locate a claim using any of the following:

  • Claim application ID
  • ABN
  • Business name, or
  • Apprentice name

Select the claim to display the Claim Details page.

Note: if an employer is eligible for an AWS for more than one apprentice, search using the ABN or business name to display claims for all of the employerâ€™s apprentices.

The Claim Details page displays:

  • Claim details, supplied by the AASN provider
  • Claim question responses, entered by the employer
  • Claim status
  • Claim period
  • Comments
  • Service Notes

3

Claim status + Read more ...

Claims in ADMS display as:

  • Ready
  • Expired
  • Future
  • Ineligible
  • Draft
  • Submitted
  • Approved
  • Verified
  • Assessment
  • Returned
  • Rejected, or
  • Withdrawn

Only claims with a status of Assessment will allocate to Service Officers for verification or to make outbound call attempts.

See the ADMS claim status table on the Resources page for a description of each claim status.

4

Claim period + Read more ...

The claim period displayed refers to the wages paid period the employer is claiming for.

See Verifying evidence for Apprenticeship Wage Subsidy (AWS) claims.

Employers claiming an AWS are able to claim from their effect date, once a claim has been created:

  • For BAC and CAC, employers are able to submit claims the day after the end of the quarterly claim period. Claims may be created up to 15 days following this date
  • For PWS, employers are able to submit claims the day after the wage period being claimed concludes

5

Comments + Read more ...

Two types of messages display in Comments, under the Action heading:

  • ADMS_System messaging is a record of actions taken by the ADMS automation process. These comments indicate evidence that requires verification
  • Service Officer comments are saved after entering text into the Comments for Employer field, when marking claims as Eligible, Ineligible or Requires more information. Comments for:
    • Eligible claims are not visible to employers
    • Ineligible or Requires more information are visible to employers

6

Service Notes + Read more ...

Service Notes are accessed by selecting the heading on the sidebar, located on the right-hand side of the screen, and contain information documented by Service Officers about:

  • the assessment of an AWS claim
  • conversations with employers, contacting their claim

Record a Service Note every time:

  • Outbound contact is made or attempted, after an AWS claim is allocated to a Service Officer for evidence verification, or
  • Inbound enquiry is received from an employer
  • Claim is escalated to DEWER via the AWSE line or the Contact card web form

Service Notes are only visible to the agency, Department of Employment and Workplace Relations (DEWR) staff and AASN providers.
