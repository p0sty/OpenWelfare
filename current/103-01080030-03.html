Part 1 financial vulnerability test + Read more ...

There are two parts to the process of assessing exemptions for customers with dependent children.

Part one of the exemption process requires a customer to demonstrate there have been no indications of financial vulnerability during the 12 month period ending immediately before the date of the exemption application.

The principles for determining that no indications of financial vulnerability were present during the previous 12 months are that the customer:

  • Has been applying appropriate resources to meet their and their dependents priority needs
  • Had stable payment patterns and budgeting practices and is meeting priority needs from their income support and family assistance payments
  • Had control over their money and was not subject to financial exploitation
  • Did not regularly require urgent funds to pay for foreseeable costs, or did not frequently change their income support pay dates. Consideration is given to the reason for seeking the urgent payment

For more information about the Financial Vulnerability test, see Conducting the financial vulnerability test for an exemption from Income Management for customers with dependent children.

2

Part 2 participation requirements + Read more ...

Part two of the exemption process requires a customer with dependent children of compulsory school age for the purposes of Income Management or younger to demonstrate they are undertaking responsible parenting.

This is done by meeting attendance or participation requirements relating to education, health care and/or other activities.

3

12 month period for exemptions + Read more ...

Any exemption granted will remain valid for a period of 12 months.

At the end of the 12 months the customer will be advised they are eligible for Income Management and must contact Services Australia for an initial assessment. The customer can reapply for an exemption from Income Management at this time and will have access to the same engagement and grace periods as any other 'new' customer.

Although exemptions are not reviewed during the 12 month period, it may be reassessed prior to the end of the 12 month exemption period in cases where an individual's circumstances have significantly changed. See the Resources page for examples of when a review of an exemption would be appropriate.

4

Determining responsible parenting - child of compulsory school age + Read more ...

The principles for determining responsible parenting in relation to each child of compulsory school age for the purposes of Income Management require one of the following to be met:

  • School attendance (less than 5 satisfactory absences):
    • The child is enrolled in and attending school
    • Has no more than 5 absences for each of the previous 2 school terms that are not satisfactory to the person responsible for the operation of the school, and
    • The customer provides evidence of school attendance. See Step 1 in the Evidence required for activity table
  • School attendance (five or more unsatisfactory absences):
    • The child is enrolled in and attending school
    • Has 5 or more absences that are not satisfactory to a person responsible for the operation of the school, and
    • The customer is taking reasonable steps to ensure the child attends school as required. For example, they have taken action to stop truancy by addressing bullying at school
  • Home schooling:
    • The child is approved to receive home based schooling, or other alternative schooling arrangement, in accordance with the relevant state or territory legislation, and
    • The customer must provide evidence of home schooling. See Step 2 in the Evidence required for activity table
  • Special circumstances:
    • If there are special circumstances preventing participation in schooling, the customer must provide evidence of special circumstances for non-enrolment and attendance. See Step 3 in the Evidence required for activity table

In the case of changed care arrangements, a foster parent or dependent carer should not be held responsible for attendance patterns prior to the time the child entered their care. School attendance should be considered only from the time the child commences care with the foster parent or dependent carer.

5

Determining responsible parenting - child under compulsory school age + Read more ...

The principles for determining responsible parenting in relation to each child under compulsory school age for the purposes of Income Management are:

  • The child is participating in the required number and kind of activities relating to their intellectual, physical or social development
  • This depends on the child's age as follows.

Child aged 0 - 36 months

  • At least two Health Related Activities

Child aged 36 - 48 months

  • At least two Health Related Activities, and
  • At least one Engagement Activity

Child aged 48 months to compulsory school age for the purposes of Income Management

  • At least two Health Related Activities, and
  • Regular participation in preschool, or
  • At least two other Engagement Activities. One of these activities to be a formal activity requiring evidence such as child care

In cases where a child under compulsory school age for the purposes of Income Management has significant disabilities, three Health Related Activities would be appropriate, due to the difficulty of finding appropriately supported social activities.

Evidence of significant disabilities would need to be provided to the agency in the form of a Doctor's Certificate or Treating Doctor's Report (TDR). The Income Management Exemption and Assessment Team (IMEAT) are the primary decision makers in granting an exemption in these circumstances.

Note: Additional updates are required for customers impacted by the Healthy Start for School initiative. If they lodge evidence to support their Income Management exemption application which proves the child has completed a relevant health check since turning 3, their health check status should also be updated. For more information, see Healthy Start for School.

6

Health Related Activities - Immunisation and health checks + Read more ...

The following are the acceptable Health Related Activities for children under compulsory school age for the purposes of an exemption from Income Management:

Age appropriate level of immunisation

  • In accordance with Family Assistance Act section 6, the immunisations are scheduled at:
    • 2 months
    • 4 months
    • 6 months
    • 12 months
    • 18 months, and
    • 4 years
  • Or one of the following:
    • A child has a medical reason not to have a particular vaccination
    • A child has had a disease and has a natural immunity
    • A particular vaccine is unavailable
    • A child is on a 'catch up' vaccination schedule for a range of reasons

For a link to the forms and the Australian Immunisation Register (AIR) Immunisation Schedule see the Resources page.

The customer needs to provide evidence of immunisation.

Regular child health checks

  • As set out in the personal health and immunisation record as published by the Northern Territory Government. This requires health checks at:
    • Up to 10 days
    • 4 weeks
    • 2 months
    • 6 - 8 months
    • 12 months
    • 18 months
    • 2 years 6 months, and
    • 3 - 4 years

The customer needs to provide evidence of regular child health checks.

Regular health checks or immunisation requirements not met

If a customer has not met the regular health checks or immunisation requirements listed above they should not be granted an exemption in the first instance.

If the customer reapplies for an exemption, the customer must provide evidence showing they have undertaken a full child health check along with all required catch up immunisations to qualify for an exemption.

Participation in a program

The child is participating in a program of speech therapy, occupational therapy, physical therapy or other approved therapy. The customer needs to provide evidence from a health professional.

Participation in this case means reasonable participation as assessed by the health professional.

7

Engagement Activities - Approved child care, preschool and school + Read more ...

Approved Child Care

Child is participating in approved child care for at least 8 hours per week.

  • Approved child care is child care approved by the Government for Child Care Subsidy.
  • The customer must provide evidence of approved child care. The following are approved:
    • Long day care
    • Family day care
    • Occasional care
    • In home care

Preschool

Child is regularly attending preschool. The customer must provide evidence of preschool.

Age Appropriate Activities

Child is engaged in age appropriate socialisation, learning, or physical activities, including kinder gym, creche and playgroup. No formal evidence is required. See Evidence of engagement in age appropriate socialisation, learning or physical activities.

School

If the child is under compulsory school age and attending school full time, this will be accepted as evidence of an engagement activity. Attendance at school must be verified, refer to Evidence of School attendance.

Requirement to provide evidence + Read more ...

In the first instance, the customer is expected to obtain evidence of their child's participation in health and engagement related activities. The agency has the discretion to accept as suitable or not.

In circumstances where the customer is having legitimate difficulties obtaining the required evidence, assistance may be provided or alternative options may be considered by the Income Management Exemption and Assessment Team (IMEAT).

See:

  • Evidence of school attendance
  • Evidence of home schooling
  • Evidence of special circumstances for non-enrolment and attendance
  • Evidence of immunisation
  • Evidence of regular health checks, or visits to medical providers
  • Evidence of approved Child Care
  • Evidence of preschool
  • Evidence of engagement in age appropriate socialisation, learning or physical activities

2

Grace period to gather evidence + Read more ...

Customers, with dependent children, not currently income managed but eligible for Income Management, are given a 28 day grace period to gather the evidence required to be granted an exemption.

This means, once the customer applies for an exemption, they will have 28 days to gather the required information before Income Management is applied.

This grace period is in addition the 28/56 day engagement period.

At the end of the grace period, if the exemption has not been granted, the customer is given an additional engagement period to complete an initial assessment for Income Management before Auto Income Management is applied.

Customers who are already income managed and are applying for an exemption, have 28 days to complete their application. At the end of the 28 days, if the required evidence has not been provided, the application will be rejected and Income Management will continue to apply.

See the Resources page for an example.

Evidence required for activity

Table 3:

Item

Description

1

Evidence of school attendance + Read more ...

Evidence of attendance at school is taken from the child's school report which, in the Northern Territory, includes the total number of absences.

Where the school reports show there have been more than 5 absences in either of the last two terms or semester, the customer will be asked to obtain evidence directly from the school. This is to determine whether the absences were satisfactory to the person responsible for the operation of the school.

The school report, together with other evidence provided by the school, would be considered definitive.

  • Parent/principal carer would not be able to provide evidence to the agency where the school has wrongly reported their child's attendance
  • If the parent/principal carer felt the school had misrepresented their child's attendance, they should be advised to follow this up with the school, and seek to have an amended report issued or a letter from the school correcting the information

Customers with children attending a Northern Territory Department of Education school

  • The parent/principal carer has to request the child's attendance report for Income Management exemption purposes
  • The individual student attendance record will be produced by school staff and forwarded to the Principal of the school
  • When the Principal approves the report it is emailed to the Income Management Exemption and Assessment Team (IMEAT) and where possible the parent/principal carer will be cc'd into the email
  • It is the responsibility of parent/principal carer to follow up with the agency to make sure the report has been received
  • Where a parent/principal carer disputes the record, they must contact the School Enrolment and Attendance Team and lodge their dispute
  • Burden of proof rests with the parent/principal carer
  • Where a parent/principal carer can prove their child was present, a school can change the record
  • Where a parent/principal carer attains a medical certificate or other evidence for dates marked with a 'U' or 'X' code after a 10 week period, the school will not change the record as this is not evidence of the timely notification of the absence
  • See the Resources page for a link to the contact details

2

Evidence of home schooling (including school of the air) + Read more ...

Evidence of home schooling for the Northern Territory (NT) will be:

  • The written consent letters provided by the NT Department of Education, or
  • A letter from School of the Air

These letters are valid for twelve months.

Approval to provide home schooling from the NT Department of Education is contingent on and, therefore, provides evidence of, satisfactory progress of the child in the preceding year.

An application for exemption can be granted at any stage of that twelve month period and would be reviewed at the end of twelve months.

3

Evidence of special circumstances for non-enrolment and attendance + Read more ...

In circumstances where school enrolment and attendance is not appropriate for a child, the customer must obtain and provide a letter from the relevant state or territory Education Authority stating that schooling is not appropriate for the child.

IMEAT are the primary decision makers in granting an exemption in these circumstances where evidence is provided. If evidence is not supplied, any request for an exemption due to special circumstances is to be referred to the Level 2 Income Management Policy helpdesk for assessment.

4

Evidence of immunisation + Read more ...

The following is evidence of immunisation:

  • Child's Personal Health Record (issued at the time of the child's birth) with details of vaccinations received, or
  • Letter from a qualified health professional confirming:
    • A child has had a disease and has a natural immunity
    • A particular vaccine is unavailable
    • A child is exempt from immunisations on the grounds of a medical condition (can only be approved by a General Practitioner)
    • A child is on a 'catch up' vaccination schedule and reason for changed schedule, or
  • The current methods used to confirm eligibility for Child Care Subsidy. See Immunisation requirements for Child Care Subsidy (CCS)

5

Evidence of regular health checks, or visits to medical providers + Read more ...

The customer will need to provide evidence of the child's health check via one of the following:

  • A practitioner's certificate with the child's name and the date of the appointment
  • A child's Personal Health Record which is issued at the time of the child's birth, or
  • A copy of the customer's Medicare record, showing the child attended a medical appointment. Medicare customers are able to request this information from Medicare

Note: evidence lodged to support a child's immunisation, does not equate to a health check. Separate evidence must be provided as outlined above. See the Resources page for example of evidence requirements.

6

Evidence of approved child care + Read more ...

The following are approved:

  • Long day care
  • Family day care
  • Occasional care
  • In home care

The customer must show evidence their child is attending approved child care for at least 8 hours per week.

7

Evidence of preschool + Read more ...

The customer must show evidence their child is attending preschool.

8

Evidence of engagement in age appropriate socialisation, learning or physical activities + Read more ...

Engagement in:

  • Age appropriate socialisation
  • Learning, or
  • Physical activities

requires the customer seeking the exemption to provide information to the agency showing the child/ren are engaging in these activities.

Where no formal activities are available in the customer's location, Service Officers should hold appropriate discussions with the customer and clearly document the type of activity the child/ren are engaging in, and the approximate frequency. Details of any informal age appropriate socialisation, learning or physical activities must be clearly documented on the customer's record.

The parent/principal carer will be expected to disclose to the Service Officer information about the activity the child is participating in.

Formal evidence is not required for these activities.

If there is any doubt about the activities available to the child or about the child's actual participation in these activities, IMEAT will refer to specialist officers, such as an Indigenous Service Officer (ISO), Multicultural Service Officer (MSO) or social worker, or may engage Remote Servicing Teams to further investigate the child's participation.

9

Seeking an exemption due to special circumstances + Read more ...

An exemption from Income Management should not be refused because the reason for non-participation in health or engagement activities is due to circumstances outside of the customer's control. For example, limited or no suitable activities in the area. These applications are to be referred to the Level 2 Income Management Policy helpdesk for assessment.

Before referring an exemption due to special circumstances to the helpdesk, IMEAT will:

  • Investigate the reason the child/ren are not able to participate in the relevant health or engagement activities
  • Obtain evidence to support the application (for example, verbal or written confirmation from third parties/organisations, written statement from customer etc.)
  • Scan the evidence onto the customer's record and/or record all relevant information in a DOC, and
  • Refer the Customer Reference Number (CRN) to the Level 2 Income Management Policy helpdesk for assessment

See the Resources page for a link to the Level 2 Income Management Policy helpdesk webform.
