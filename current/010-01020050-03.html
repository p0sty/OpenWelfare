This page contains information on the commonly used modules and forms in relation to students and Australian Apprentices.

Forms / Modules - A to E

Issuing appropriate modules and additional forms A to E

Table 1: This table describes a list of forms/modules from A to E which are used in conjunction with the claim forms for ABSTUDY, Assistance for Isolated Children (AIC), Austudy, Pensioner Education Supplement (PES) and Youth Allowance (YA) or forms which are issued when particular information is required.

Component

Form or Module

Accommodation

Rent Certificate (SU523) + Read more ...

Issued to customers who have changed their accommodation details. See the Resources page for a link to forms on the Services Australia website.

Additional information (ABSTUDY)

SY023 (ABSTUDY Student Additional Information) + Read more ...

Issue to ABSTUDY students and Australian Apprentices who may have missed completing relevant and important questions on their specific claim form. May also be issued to students and Australian Apprentices when they advise of a major change in their circumstances.

Assurance of Support

Module AR (Assurance of Support) + Read more ...

Issue to customers who have entered Australia under an Assurance of Support .This form is not available to the public.

Business (sole trader) or partnership

Module F (Business details) + Read more ...

Issue to YA and Austudy customers who are or were, immediately prior to claiming, involved in a sole trader business or partnership.

Compensation

Module C (Compensation and Damages) + Read more ...

Issue to customers who have claimed compensation.

Employment

Seasonal, Contract and Intermittent Work Details (SU496) + Read more ...

Issue to customers who have engaged in season, contract or intermittent work in the six months prior to lodging a claim.

Forms /Modules - F to P

Issuing appropriate modules and additional forms F to P

Table 2: This table describes a list of forms/modules beginning from F to P and describes when to issue these particular forms/ modules in conjunction with the claim forms for ABSTUDY, Assistance for Isolated Children (AIC), Austudy, Pensioner Education Supplement (PES) and Youth Allowance (YA), to enable a customer to provide supplementary information to their claim.

Component

Form or Module

Fares Allowance

ABSTUDY + Read more ...

ABSTUDY Fares Allowance claim (SY032)

Issue to ABSTUDY customers who have undertaken a journey as a full-time student for which they are eligible for fares allowance.

Austudy, PES and YA

Application for Fares Allowance tertiary students only (SY005)

Issue to tertiary students who have undertaken a journey for which they are eligible for Fares Allowance.

Income and Assets

Module iA - (Income and Assets) + Read more ...

Issue to customers:

  • for details of income or assets not previously listed on the claim form
  • to advise of a change in personal or partner assets

Tailored online income and assets form available only if the customer has deferred an income and/or asset category in a new claim lodged online.

Independence

Parent(s)/Guardian(s) additional details for Youth Allowance or ABSTUDY customers (A2115) + Read more ...

Issue to customers who are applying for independence as they have self-supported themselves through employment, and the family home is in a Regional or Remote area.

Marginal Distance Confirmation (AIC)

Assistance for Isolated Children (AIC) - Marginal Distance Confirmation (SY069) + Read more ...

Issued to AIC applicants who are required to obtain verification of the distance and travel time to the student's nearest state school and transport service.

Medical Statement for Student Special Needs (AIC)

Assistance for Isolated Children (AIC) Medical Statement - Student Special Needs (SY099) + Read more ...

Issue to AIC applicants who are required to obtain medical evidence to support their claim that the student has a special need.

Organisation details (AIC)

Assistance for Isolated Children (AIC) - Organisation Details (SY067) + Read more ...

Issue to AIC organisation applicants for a student in order to obtain information about their care and responsibility, financial institution account details and tax file number.

Overseas

Module O (Residence in Australia and other countries) + Read more ...

Issue to customers who have lived overseas or who were born overseas.

Parental income

Module JY (Parent(s)/Guardian details for the Base Tax Year and Current Tax Year) for Dependent Youth Allowance and ABSTUDY customers + Read more ...

Issue to ABSTUDY and YA customers who are required to provide parental income information for the base tax year and/or the current tax year.

Note: the Advise Parental Income Service can be used by the Parent/Guardian to declare details for the Base Tax Year and Current Tax Year online. This service is only available to parent(s)/guardian(s) of a YA claimant, or for a YA or ABSTUDY customer's parent/guardian when lodging information for a parental income reassessment.

Partner details

Module P (Partner Details) + Read more ...

Issue to customers who have a partner. See Separation.

Private Company

Module PC (Private Company) + Read more ...

Issue to customers who have an involvement in a private company.

Private Trust

Module PT (Private Trust) + Read more ...

Issue to customers who have an involvement in a private trust.

Forms/Modules - Q to Z

Issuing appropriate modules and additional forms Q to Z

Table 3: This table describes a list of forms/modules beginning from Q to Z and describes when to issue these particular forms/ modules in conjunction with the claim forms for ABSTUDY, Isolated Children (AIC), Austudy, Pensioner Education Supplement (PES) and Youth Allowance (YA) so that a customer’s entitlement can be accurately assessed.

Component

Form or Module

Real estate

Module R (Real Estate Details) + Read more ...

Issue to customers who own real estate other than their principal home.

Note: when triggered from an online new claim, Mod R can be launched from the What You Must Do page and lodged online.

Separation

Module S (Separation details) + Read more ...

Issue to customers who have separated from their partner. See Partner details.

There are key tasks a customer should consider when separating to keep their personal information safe. See Separating Safely - protecting personal details.

Short Term Boarding Allowance (AIC)

Assistance for Isolated Children (AIC) - Application for Short Term Boarding Allowance (SY068) + Read more ...

Issue to AIC applicants to allow them to claim AIC boarding allowance for the student for additional short term board periods.

Study details

Module ST (Study Details) + Read more ...

Issue to customers who are full-time students needing to provide new study details and who are unable to provide information verbally.

Unreasonable to live at home

Unreasonable to live at home + Read more ...

For over 18 year olds issue:

  • SY015 - Youth Allowance or ABSTUDY 'Unreasonable to live at home' Statement by Young Person
  • SY016 - Youth Allowance or ABSTUDY 'Unreasonable to live at home' Statement by Parent(s)/Guardians(s)

Note: if the Statement by young person form (SY015) and Statement by Parent(s)/Guardian(s) form (SY016) align as determined by the UTLAH Over 18 Assessment team, the Statement by a Third Party form (SY017) is not required and should not be requested to grant UTLAH (O18).
The SY017 is only requested if the Statement by young person form (SY015) and Statement by Parent(s) / Guardian(s) form (SY016) do not align or if a parent is refusing or failing to provide a statement, there is doubt about the reason the young person is unable to live at home, then the Statement by a Third Party form (SY017) will be requested by the UTLAH Over 18 Assessment team in order to process UTLAH
