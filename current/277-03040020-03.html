Investigate change in care

Step

Action

1

Care notification that does not result in change to cost percentage + Read more ...

Open the Change in care macro. The macro helps to:

  • guide and record the customer conversation
  • calculate the reported care percentage, and
  • document the outcome

For a change in care notification that does not result in a change to the cost percentage, an investigation is only required in the following circumstances:

  • the notifying customer seeks to have the change reflected, despite understanding that it will not affect the cost percentage, or
  • the care is based on a written care arrangement, or
  • the care has ceased to be in line with a written care arrangement, or
  • there are other circumstances that warrant making a care decision. Circumstances that may warrant further consideration include:
    • if the notification is for an increase to the other partyâ€™s care
    • a history of escalations, objections and dissatisfaction, or
    • Family and Domestic Violence issues

Note: a warning message will display when a customer notifies of a care change via online services. The message advises the customer the care change information provided will not change their assessment. By continuing with the care change, the customer is indicating they understand this, but are seeking to have the change reflected in their child support assessment.

If none of the above circumstances exist, proceed to making a refusal decision.

Otherwise, continue investigating care.

2

Customer contact + Read more ...

Consider information from both customers when determining:

  • the date of event
  • percentage of care, and
  • type of care decision for each reported care change

Make 1 attempt to contact the customer on all available numbers, including their place of employment before using the macro to send the Care contact letter.

Customer who receive their communication via:

  • surface mail, give them 21 days to reply
  • online channel, give them 10 days to reply

Make at least 2 further contact attempts after sending the care contact letter if:

  • there are known family or domestic violence issues
  • there is debt, but there is no known collection avenue
  • the customer is identified as Culturally and Linguistically Diverse (CALD)
  • the customer is identified as Indigenous and/or Remote
  • it is deemed that there are risks or impacts that indicate additional contact is required to support the parent or carer, or to achieve an outcome

If initial attempts to contact the other customer by phone are unsuccessful, go to Step 7.

Give each customer a chance to comment on relevant information gathered and/or provided by the other customer to confirm:

  • date of event
  • care period
  • percentage of care
  • date of effect
  • care type

Discuss all care information provided by the notifying customer with the other customer. Explain how any new information may affect their percentage of care and their assessment. This gives them the chance to respond and ensure the decision is just.

Establish and clarify the specific details of the care change (particularly the Date of Event and pattern of care) by asking open ended questions:

  • What is the actual care that is occurring?
  • When did the change actually occur?
  • When did you last have overnight care?
  • Why did the care change?
  • How was the care change discussed with the other parent?
  • Who is aware of the arrangements?
  • Which child/ren does the change relate to?
  • Is the change intended to be permanent?
  • If the care change is temporary, what is the period of the change?

Note: for WA Ex-nuptial children, if the change was late notified after 14 May 2019, the system needs to be enhanced to treat these customers correctly. For more information see WA Child Support ex-nuptial cases Process page Table 3.

Resources has an example on care investigation.

3

Review information + Read more ...

If the other customer confirms the change in care then proceed to make a decision, see Check and process care table in Child support care outcomes and decision making (post 1 July 2010).

If there is a disagreement over care arrangements, refer to Integrated Care in the Customer Referral Guidelines.

Prior to referring the case to Integrated Care:

  • Gather all relevant information from the customer/s regarding the details of the new care pattern, including:
    • details of when the care changes (date of event)
    • the pattern of care

If the customer is on the phone, ask them to send evidence to support their care change. Ask the customer to upload their evidence via Child Support self service. Tell the customer an Integrated Care Service Officer will call them to discuss the disagreed process.

Exception - referrals to the Integrated Care Team

Service Officer in specialised areas who manage disagreements about care, for example Personalised Services, Complaints, New Customers (if there is a pending registration) or a RACs officer are not required to refer the customer to the Integrated Care Team. These officers will retain ownership of the change in care notification.

4

Care decision that may impact Family Tax Benefit (FTB) + Read more ...

If either customer receives FTB for the child, the care decision:

  • is sent to Centrelink, and
  • may affect any Centrelink benefits or payments they receive

Explain to the customer that:

  • Child Support sends the care decision to Centrelink
  • the customer should ensure their relationship and income details are correct with Centrelink. This will reduce the risk of incorrect Centrelink payments when the care decision is transferred. Update any required information, for example, income, in Cuba before finalising the care decision
  • the date of effect for FTB may be different to the date used by the Child Support. They should give all date of event details to Child Support, even if it was more than 28 days ago
  • Centrelink will advise them in writing of any impact on their Centrelink entitlements

Offer to cold transfer the customer to Centrelink if they need:

  • more information, or
  • to update their relationship or income details

5

Documentation + Read more ...

Save the output document from the Change in care macro in the Care window notepad.

If unable to determine a clear event date after discussions with both customers, go to Step 6.

If unable to contact the other customer, go to Step 7.

Otherwise, go to Step 8.

6

Unable to determine the event date + Read more ...

Attempt to obtain information from both customers to determine when the change in care occurred. If they cannot agree on the date, support them by questioning and investigating potential dates to establish the correct date of event.

If this is unsuccessful, ask the customer to send evidence that demonstrates the date of event and refer the change in care to Integrated care, go to Step 3.

7

Unable to contact the other customer + Read more ...

Conduct searches to source new contact details, see External searches guide (CS).

If there is a new contact number, go to Step 2.

If there is a pending care and the other customer is on tracing, see Customer location (Tracing) (CS).

If the parent is living overseas, they respond within a 'reasonable time' if:

  • they respond within the 28 days allowed in the notice plus
  • a reasonable time for postage to and from the country. This is usually an extra 14 days

See Australia Post website for postage and delivery times.

If there is no response to the 'Care contact letter', base the decision on the best information available.

If there is any reason to doubt the information available, seek evidence from the notifying parent to support their claims.

Situations where we may seek evidence include:

  • Notifying party advises that the other party is likely to disagree with the change
  • History of disagreed/disputed care
  • History of care objections/complaints
  • Care change notification is unclear or inconsistent with other information available.
  • Any other information available that casts doubt over the information received

If there is no reason to doubt the information provided by the notifying parent, there may be no need to seek evidence prior to making a decision.

Situations where evidence may not be required include:

  • Care change notification is clear and there is no history of disagreed care or objections/complaints
  • Notifying party advises that the other party is likely to agree to the change
  • Notifying party has advised their own care has reduced
  • Notifying customer advises that one of the parties has moved a considerable distance away from the other and there is an address change verifying this

If there is a care arrangement, see Step 6 in Calculate and determine care percentage in Child support care outcomes and decision making (post 1 July 2010).

8

Request further evidence + Read more ...

Where necessary, contact customers to request evidence to support their claims. Allow additional time as appropriate to the channel where the evidence will be lodged.

Discuss with each customer any evidence or claim considered in the care decision. This includes evidence and information:

  • provided by either customer
  • provided by a third party
  • obtained from a Child Support initiated search

Do not consider evidence or claims if a customer or third party does not want the evidence or claim discussed or made available to all customers.

If there is a disagreement over care arrangements, refer to Integrated Care in the Customer Referral Guidelines.

If the customers do not agree on the current care percentage details provided to Child Support, refer the customer to the Integrated Care Team.

Document all conversations, contact attempts and information gathered in the Care window notepad.

If all information available has been gathered relevant to determining the care percentage, see Step 1 in the Calculate and determine care percentage table in Child support care outcomes and decision making (post 1 July 2010).
