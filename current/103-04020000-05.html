Forms

Table 1

Item

Description

1

International bank account form (AUS178)

This form is available for customers to provide their overseas bank details.

This form is commonly known as an OSDD (Overseas Direct Deposit) form.

There are 40 forms to cater for overseas customers. There are numerous versions of this form because overseas account details vary in format depending on:

  • the country
  • the payment method used (such as: ACH, WWR with local currency, WWR with US intermediary)
  • the information needed (SWIFT or local bank code, account number or IBAN)

The forms reflect this.

There are also various language versions.

The correct form to use for a country can be found on the Services Australia website.

The forms are also available for staff on the intranet (see Online Forms).

2

Authorising a person or organisation to enquire or act on your behalf form

The appointed payment nominee does not need to be in Australia to receive payments on behalf of the customer living outside Australia. They can be located outside Australia.

Exception: if the nominated person/organisation is in a payment sanctioned country, they cannot then receive Centrelink payments on behalf of the customer.

To arrange for another person or organisation to receive payments on the customer's behalf, the customer and payment nominee must complete a form.

There are 2 forms available, depending where the payment nominee is. For payment nominees:

  • in Australia – the SS313 form
  • outside Australia – the AUS221 form (including the AUS221GR – English/Greek version, AUS221IT – English/Italian version)

The form can be:

  • lodged online, or
  • returned by fax, mail or in person

The correct form to use for a country can be found on the Services Australia website.

The forms are also available for staff on the intranet (see Online Forms).

Exemption reasons

A person paid in an ACH country may be exempt from receiving their Centrelink payment by direct deposit in exceptional circumstances. These include:

  • Customer is aged, frail or has a disability
  • Customer resides in a remote regions
  • Temporary grace period needed to open bank account

5

Departing overseas and requesting temporary suspension

The customer may request their Australian payment to be suspended while they organise an overseas bank account. Staff can do this, but they must make sure the details of the customer's request are recorded on a DOC.

  • Ask the customer to give an estimated time they think they will be able to provide the overseas bank account details. Record a review DOC for just after this time to make sure bank account details are returned
  • If the customer has not already been suspended/cancelled for reason DOS, suspend the customer's payment for reason OTH. This will avoid payments being issued to a closed bank account
