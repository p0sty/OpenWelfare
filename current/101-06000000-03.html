Concession cards information

Table 1

Step

Action

1

Eligible payment + Read more

Is the customer:

  • Receiving a pension, or
  • Aged 60 or over and in continuous receipt of income support payment for more than 39 weeks, or
  • A single principal carer who is receiving JobSeeker Payment (JSP) or Youth Allowance (YA), or
  • A person assessed by an Employment Services Assessment (ESAt) as having a partial capacity to work, who is either receiving JSP or YA, or
  • A Parenting Payment Partnered (PPP) customer who was granted on or after 1 July 2006 and has been assessed as having a partial capacity to work by an ESAt?
    • Yes, go to Step 2
    • No, go to Step 3

2

Pensioner Concession Card (PCC) + Read more ...

The customer is eligible for a PCC. This is issued every 2 years, to coincide with the customer's birth anniversary month.

Check the Action Date: field on the Concession Card Issue Summary (CCIS) screen to see when the card was last issued.

For more information, see Pensioner Concession Card (PCC). Procedure ends here.

3

Determine customer circumstances + Read more ...

Is the customer:

  • A person whose Disability Support Pension (DSP) has been cancelled within the last 12 months due to earnings or employment more than 30 hours per week, or
  • A person who is no longer qualified for Parenting Payment Single (PPS) because their youngest child has turned 8 on or after 1 January 2014, or
  • A single principal carer who is either receiving JobSeeker Payment (JSP) or Youth Allowance (YA) or whose JSP/YA has been cancelled due to a return to work, or
  • Assessed by an Employment Services Assessment (ESAt) as having a partial capacity to work and whose JSP/YA has been cancelled due to a return to work
    • Yes, go to Step 4
    • No, go to Step 5

4

Retaining of concession card + Read more ...

  • Cancelled DSP customers retain their PCC for 40 weeks immediately following a 12 week employment income nil rate period
  • PPS customers retain eligibility for the PCC for a period of 12 weeks if they are no longer qualified for PPS on or after 1 January 2014 because their youngest child has turned 8
  • Cancelled JSP or YA customers with a partial capacity to work as assessed by an ESAt will retain their PCC for 12 months
  • Cancelled JSP or YA customers who are single principal carers retain their PCC for 12 weeks. They will qualify for the Health Care Card (HCC) for the remainder of the 26 week period
  • Check the Concession Card Issue Summary (CCIS) screen to see when card last issued

For more information, refer to:

  • Pensioner Concession Card (PCC) and
  • Extended Entitlement to Concession Cards

5

Customer circumstance that may affect entitlement to concession card + Read more ...

  • Do any of the following apply to the customer?
    • Receiving an eligible income support payment
    • Receiving the maximum rate of Family Tax Benefit (FTB) Part A as fortnightly instalments (or would except for the Maintenance Income Test)
    • Qualified for maximum FTB Part A except that they only have care of a Regular Care Child
    • Receiving FTB for a foster child
    • Receiving Carer Payment (CP) for short-term or episodic conditions
    • Receiving Carer Allowance (child)
    • Receiving Mobility Allowance (MOB)
    • A former recipient of MOB which cancelled due to the customer becoming a funded participant of the National Disability Insurance Scheme (NDIS)
  • Yes, go to Step 6
  • No, go to Step 1 in Table 2

6

Automatically issued HCC's + Read more ...

The customer is eligible for a Health Care Card (HCC) which is automatically issued.

Check the Action Date: field on the CCIS screen to see when last card was issued.

Note: customers whose MOB has been cancelled due to them becoming a funded participant of the NDIS will retain their eligibility for their Mobility Allowance HCC. Manual concession override processing is required to enable this continuation of entitlement. See Cancellation and Period of Grace for Mobility Allowance (MOB).

For more information, see Health Care Card (HCC). Procedure ends here.

Concession cards issued to Carers, Foster Carers and retirees not on Age Pension

Table 2

Step

Action

1

Is the customer a child care receiver? + Read more ...

  • Yes, they may be entitled to a HCC if their carer claims Carer Allowance. See Coding a claim for Carer Allowance (CA) where the care receiver is under 16. Procedure ends here
  • No, go to Step 2

2

Is the customer an ex-CA care recipient? + Read more ...

  • Yes, they may be entitled to the ex-CA HCC. For how to claim, see Ex-Carer Allowance (child) Health Care Card (EHC). Procedure ends here
  • No, go to Step 3

3

Is the customer a foster carer? + Read more ...

  • Yes, they may be entitled to a HCC for their foster child. For how to claim, see Health Care Cards (HCC) for foster children
  • No, go to Step 4

4

Over Age Pension age customer not on income support + Read more ...

Is the customer of Age Pension age or over, but doesn't qualify for the pension due to assets or residency, and their income is below the Commonwealth Seniors Health Card (CSHC) limits?

  • Yes, go to Step 5
  • No, go to Step 6

5

Possible entitlement for the CSHC + Read more ...

A claim must be completed. See Claiming Commonwealth Seniors Health Card (CSHC).

For more information, see Commonwealth Seniors Health Card (CSHC) - concession card.

Note: a customer may hold a CSHC and Low Income Health Care Card at the same time.

Procedure ends here.

6

Low Income Health Care Card + Read more ...

Does the customer have a low income, which is below the income threshold for the Low Income Health Care Card (LIC) or are they receiving ABSTUDY, Austudy or YA as a full-time student or an Australian apprentice? For the LIC threshold, see the Centrelink rate charts. A link to this can be found in the Resources page.

  • Yes, go to Step 7
  • No, customer is not eligible for LIC. Procedure ends here

7

Customer possible entitlement for a LIC and claim process + Read more ...

For how to claim, see Claiming a Low Income Health Care Card (LIC).

For information on eligibility and the income test, see Low Income Health Care Card (LIC).

Concession cards returned to Services Australia

Table 3

Step

Action

1

Concession card returned + Read more ...

When a concession card is returned determine if the customer is still eligible for the card, go to the CCIS screen.

Is the customer still eligible for this concession card?

  • Yes, go to Step 2
  • No, go to Step 3

2

Has the card been reissued recently? + Read more ...

Review the CCIS screen to determine if the card has been recently reissued.

Was the card recently reissued?

  • Yes, go to Step 3
  • No, run the Concession Card reissue guided procedure, then go to Step 3

Note: for more information, See How to reissue concession cards.

3

Document the outcome + Read more ...

Record a DOC that includes:

  • The type of concession card returned, including the expiry date
  • Action taken. For example was a replacement card issued
  • How the returned card will be disposed of. Go to Step 4

Go to Step 4

4

Dispose of concession card + Read more ...

The returned card(s) must be disposed of using a secure process.

Is there a Digital Secure Bin available?

  • Yes, use the Digital Secure Bin to dispose of the returned concession card
  • No:
    • shred or cut up the returned concession card
    • place the card in a sealed envelope/box in the Internal Services Australia mail service, addressed to 'MDC - Mail Distribution Centre'
    • place in a courier bag
    • MDC will dispose of the card/s securely
