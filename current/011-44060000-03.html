Belgium

Table 1

Step

Description

1

Eligibility + Read more ...

Eligible visitors from Belgium can enrol in Medicare and be issued with a Medicare card.

To be eligible for Medicare the applicant must have been residing (living) in Belgium before arriving in Australia and insured in the Belgian health system, this also includes:

  • Applicants travelling on a student visa
  • Australian citizens that have been residing outside of Australia for 5 years or more and are not returning to Australia to reside

If the applicant:

  • is a diplomat, go to Step 2
  • holds are retiree visa subclass 405, 410, go to Step 3
  • is Defence Force personal, see the Defence force personnel and medical visa holders table
  • holds a Medical visa subclass 602, see the Defence force personnel and medical visa holders table

If the applicant is not one of the above, go to Step 4.

2

Diplomats eligibility + Read more ...

Diplomats from Belgium and their families are eligible for full Medicare cover (they are issued with a green Medicare card rather than a yellow RHCA card). Unlike green Medicare cards, an entitlement end date must be added for the duration of their visa.

Documents required:

  • completed Medicare enrolment application form (MS004)
  • passports
  • visa

Note: the diplomat and accompanying family must have evidence of their diplomatic status, this will be notated on (either or both) their passport and/or visa.

All identity documents must be verified in DVS. See Register a customer for Medicare in the Consumer Directory Maintenance System (CDMS) for the process.

Action required by: Medicare Eligibility trained Service Officers.

For a diplomatic Medicare enrolment, key the following fields:

  • Entitlement Type: Diplomat - Belgium
  • RHCA Country: Belgium
  • Entitlement Start date: date of arrival into Australia
  • Entitlement End date: duration of visa

Procedure ends here.

3

Retirees - visa subclass 405 or 410 + Read more ...

Holders of the following Retiree visas are not eligible for Medicare under a RHCA:

  • Investor Retirement visa subclass 405, or
  • Retiree visa subclass 410 issued after 1 December 1998, or
  • Retiree visa subclass 410 issued before 1 December 1998 where the applicant opted for Medicare levy exemption in lieu of eligibility under a RHCA (this arrangement is outlined under a Ministerial Order)

Note: the pre 1 December 1998 arrangements for holders of visa subclass 410 only apply if the RHCA was in force at that time, for more information, see Ministerial orders and Medicare eligibility.

However, there are some exceptions in which a retiree visa holder may be eligible for Medicare under a RHCA.

Exceptions:

Applicants that hold a retiree visa 405 or 410 may be eligible under a RHCA if 1 of the following applies:

  • Applicant holds either visa subclass 405 or 410 (issued after 1 December 1998) and exceptional circumstances apply. More information about the criteria used when making an assessment for exceptional circumstances can be found on the Services Australia website. See Resources page for a link

Note: these applications must be escalated to Medicare Eligibility Local Peer Support (LPS)

  • Applicant has lodged an application for permanent residency under the Aged Parent category (visa subclass 103 or 804). The parent visa application supersedes the existing visa subclass; however, they would still need to meet the eligibility criteria under the specific RHCA
  • Applicant has lodged an application for permanent residency under the Contributory Parent visa see: Ministerial orders and Medicare eligibility

Procedure ends here.

4

Determining residency + Read more ...

To determine if the applicant was living in Belgium before arriving in Australia, service officers must:

  • ask the applicant of their last place of residency, or
  • refer to Medicare enrolment MS004 - questions:
    • Q14 - Previous country of residence (before arriving in Australia)
    • Q15 - How long were you residing in that country?

For Medicare purposes, an applicant’s country of residence would be the country they have been living in for a period of 6 months or more before arriving in Australia.

A Belgian citizen who was not a resident of Belgium for 6 months or more before arriving in Australia is not eligible.

5

Documents required for enrolment in Medicare under RHCA + Read more ...

Belgian and non-Belgian citizens (including Australian citizens living in Belgium)

  • completed Medicare enrolment application form (MS004)
  • current passport
  • evidence of a valid visa, and
  • either of the following:
  • evidence of valid insurance with the Belgian Health System, or
  • valid European Health Insurance card (EHIC) bearing the initials 'BE'

Note: Australian citizens (who are travelling on an Australian passport) are not issued with a visa.

If no evidence of visa

If the applicant does not have evidence of their visa (excluding Australian citizens), they may have an Electronic Travel Authority (ETA). In this case, Medicare eligibility should only be issued for 3 months from arrival date.

If the applicant insists they have a visa for a longer period, access VEVO (Visa Entitlement Verification Online) to verify visa information.

6

Recording entitlement details in CDMS (not previously enrolled in Medicare) + Read more ...

Register a customer for Medicare in the CDMS must be followed where the applicant has not been previously enrolled in Medicare.

Note: all identity documents must be verified in DVS. See Register a new customer table.

Key the following fields:

  • Entitlement Type: RHCA (Belgium)
  • RHCA Country: Belgium
  • Entitlement Start Date: date of arrival into Australia
  • Entitlement End Date: entitlement ends when the first of these expires:
    • Belgium Health Insurance Card
    • European Health Insurance Card (EHIC), or
    • Visa

If the applicant is previously enrolled in Medicare (has an existing Medicare number) and is:

  • not an Australian citizen, go to Step 8
  • an Australian citizen, go to Step 9

Note:

  • if the entitlement end date is less than 3 months, go to Step 10
  • if the applicant has a visa that allows them to stay and work in Australia for 4 years or longer, it is acceptable to issue a Medicare card for the period of the visa if it does not go past the expiry date of their Belgium health cover or EHIC
  • Entitlement End Reason: Entitlement ceased

7

Recording eligibility documents in CDMS + Read more ...

For a new Medicare enrolment, eligibility documents must be added in CDMS, under document type.

Eligibility Document: passport

Reference Text: applicant's passport number

Evidence of eligibility under the Belgian health system - either of the below documents is acceptable:

Eligibility Document:

  • RHCA - European Health Insurance Card (EHIC), or
  • RHCA - evidence of national health insurance

Reference Text:

  • EHIC expiry date, for example, EHIC expires - 01/12/2018 or
  • National health insurance expiry date, for example Health Insurance expires - 01/12/2018

Reference Date:

  • processing date

8

Extending RHCA eligibility to existing Medicare enrolment (non-Australian citizens) + Read more ...

If the applicant has previously had RHCA entitlement (is not an Australian citizen) and they want to extend their eligibility, amend the existing end date only where there is no break in entitlement, that is:

  • they have not left the country
  • their new visa was granted before their previous one expired
  • they have a valid EHIC or National Health Insurance

If identity has been DVS on the initial enrolment, DVS is not needed to extend eligibility. To confirm, check comments in CDMS on Personal tab.

If the identity document is not recorded, verify in DVS. See Register a new customer table.

To change the existing entitlement end date, under Eligibility Status, select the Amend option.

Where there has been a break in entitlement and the applicant meets the eligibility criteria, add a new entitlement under the Entitlement tab - New/Additional Entitlement. All eligibility documents must be recorded under Documents. Follow Steps 6 and 7.

The applicant must provide:

  • completed Medicare enrolment application form (MS004)
  • current passport
  • evidence of a new valid visa, and
  • confirmation that they still meet the RHCA criteria by holding:
    • a valid European Health Insurance Card (EHIC) with initials 'BE', or
    • valid Belgian National Health Insurance

See: Amend entitlement details in CDMS

9

Extending RHCA eligibility to existing Medicare enrolment (Australian citizens) + Read more ...

  • For Australian citizens that have been residing outside of Australia for 5 years or more and are not returning to Australia to reside

If identity has been DVS on the initial enrolment, DVS is not needed to extend eligibility. To confirm, check comments in CDMS on Personal tab.

If the identity document is not recorded, verify in DVS. See Register a new customer table.

  • Service Officers must:
  • confirm the applicant’s initial departure date from Australia
  • amend entitlement details in CDMS

Updating entitlement details in CDMS

Amend existing entitlement details, under Eligibility Status:

  • select the eligibility status button
  • select the Amend option tab
  • add an Entitlement End Date: the date should be 5 years from the applicant’s initial departure date from Australia
  • Entitlement End Reason: select Entitlement ceased
  • Validate and Update

Add new RHCA entitlement

Note: If only one person is changing their entitlement type to RHCA, they must be transferred off the card onto a card of their own.

Under New/Additional Entitlement:

  • Entitlement type: select RHCA Belgium from the dropdown list
  • RHCA country: select Belgium from the dropdown list
  • Entitlement Start Date: date of arrival into Australia
  • Entitlement End Date: entitlement ends when the first of these expires:
  • Belgium Health Insurance Card
  • European Health Insurance Card (EHIC)

Note: Australian citizens (travelling on an Australian passport) are not issued with a visa and may not be able to provide a National Health Insurance card. In this situation, the applicant must provide 2 residency documents from Belgium. Entitlement would be for 3 months. If they provide travel itinerary, you can use the date of their departure.

If the Entitlement End Date is less than 3 months, go to Step 10

  • Entitlement End Reason: select Entitlement Ceased from the dropdown list
  • Update My Health Record tab, see Step 5 in Register customer’s Medicare entitlement
  • select Validate
  • select Documents
  • add all the eligibility documents provided
  • select Return
  • Validate and Update
  • add Group comment: ‘Australian citizens residing in RHCA country’
  • Validate and Update

10

Entitlement less than 3 months + Read more ...

If the applicant's entitlement is for less than 3 months, the Production Control check box must be ticked in CDMS which will pre populate the address for the Service Officer. This will prevent a Medicare card from being produced, but still allows the person to make a claim. See Register a customer for Medicare in the CDMS.

Attempt to call the customer once to tell them of the Medicare card number. If unsuccessful send a Z2590 Your Medicare card details letter.

Retirees - visa subclass 405 or 410 + Read more ...

Holders of the following visas are not eligible for Medicare under a RHCA.

  • Investor Retirement visa subclass 405, or
  • Retiree visa subclass 410 issued after 1 December 1998, or
  • Retiree visa subclass 410 issued before 1 December 1998 where the applicant opted for Medicare levy exemption in lieu of eligibility under a RHCA (this arrangement is outlined under a Ministerial Order)

Note: the pre 1 December 1998 arrangements for holders of visa subclass 410 only apply if the RHCA was in force at that time. See Ministerial orders and Medicare eligibility

However, there are some exceptions in which a retiree visa holder may be eligible for Medicare under a RHCA.

Exceptions:

Applicants that hold a retiree visa 405 or 410 may be eligible under a RHCA if 1 of the following applies:

  • Applicant holds either, a visa subclass 405, or 410 (issued after 1 December 1998) and exceptional circumstances apply. More information about the criteria used when making an assessment for exceptional circumstances can be found on the Services Australia website. See Resources page for a link
    • these applications must be escalated to Medicare Eligibility Local Peer Support (LPS)
  • Applicant has lodged an application for permanent residency under the Aged Parent category (visa subclass 103 or 804). The parent visa application supersedes the existing visa subclass, however, they would still need to meet the eligibility criteria under the specific RHCA
  • Applicant has lodged an application for permanent residency under the Contributory Parent visa see: Ministerial orders and Medicare eligibility

Defence force personnel + Read more ...

Medical expenses for foreign defence force personnel residing in Australia are covered by their defence force. However, they can enrol in Medicare together with their spouse and dependants if from a country that has an RHCA with Australia. They are then covered under Medicare for medically necessary treatment to the extent of the RHCA that applies.

British defence force personnel

British defence force personnel and their dependants on exchange transfer with Australian defence services are eligible for enrolment under the RHCA.

The normal criteria for enrolment under RHCA arrangements must be met. The Australian Defence Service Medical Cover covers the personnel on exchange, except when they attend a medical practitioner of their own choice rather than attend an Australian defence services doctor. This may relate to injuries or illness while not on duty.

2

Medical visa (subclass 602) + Read more ...

Visa issued outside Australia

A person entering Australia with a Medical Treatment visa subclass 602 who is a resident from an RHCA country can enrol in the Medicare program but is not entitled to claim benefits for treatment related to the medical condition for which the visa was issued.

Visa issued in Australia

If a person residing in Australia is issued with a medical visa (commonly subclass 602) and they are from an RHCA country, they remain eligible for Medicare enrolment. There is no restriction to benefits for this group as they are generally elderly and too ill to return to their home country. To enrol, a current passport and valid visa are required.

Action required by Service Officers

If it is identified that an applicant holds a medical visa, advise the applicant they will be asked when lodging claims to produce a letter from a doctor or provide other evidence of the condition they are seeking treatment for. This advice is to be given at the time of enrolment.

Details of the medical condition relating to the issue of the visa must be keyed in the Comments field on CDMS.
