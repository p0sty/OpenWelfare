2

Payment on Advice requests + Read more ...

Payment on Advice information is obtained from schools/boarding institutions by the following method:

  • Scheduled statistical analysis system (SAS) requests produce lists of ABSTUDY Away from Home students. Students must be ABSTUDY (CUR)rent on the first Friday of the relevant school term to be selected in the SAS
  • Students are loaded under the relevant school/boarding institution on the Third Party Information Summary - Organisation (TPISO) screen
  • An email is sent to all schools/boarding institutions that are required to report student commencement dates in order to receive the first ABSTUDY boarding instalment for eligible students. Note: if the school does not have an email address, the letter is posted to them
  • The letter provides a One Time Access Code (OTAC) to institutions to report this information via the Education Institutions eService (the eService)
  • Institutions must respond by the third Friday of the school term or ABSTUDY boarding instalment entitlements will be suspended pending a commencement date for each student

See the Resources page for a link to the eService and for a copy of the Payment on Advice OTAC letter.

3

One Time Access Code (OTAC) rules + Read more ...

An OTAC:

  • is unique to a school/boarding institution, the term/year and the ABSTUDY Payment on Advice process
  • will allow multiple entries to the eService but will expire once information is submitted to Services Australia

Schools/boarding institutions can save information in the eService. Encourage them to continually save data as entered.

The ABSTUDY Payment on Advice Student Input screen will allow all information to be completed. However, a timeout message will display after 15 minutes of non-keying. If this occurs:

  • the information coded in the eService will be lost, and
  • the school/boarding institution will need to reuse their OTAC to log back into the eService and re-code the information

Once the data has been submitted to Services Australia:

  • no further changes can be made to the data
  • any amendments will need to be submitted to Services Australia using the SY106 - First or last day of attendance for ABSTUDY Away from home students form. This form is available on the Services Australia website

See the Resources page for a copy of the Payment on Advice OTAC letter.

If Services Australia issues a new OTAC (if OTAC was lost or a new OTAC is issued for the next term), then the previous OTAC will expire.

4

Accessing the Third Party Information Summary - Organisations (TPISO) screen + Read more ...

The TPISO screen can be used to confirm:

  • the status of a Payment on Advice request for a particular school/boarding institution, term and year
  • a student was on the Payment on Advice request sent to a particular school/boarding institution
  • a school/boarding institution has submitted the Payment on Advice information to Services Australia

To access the school/boarding instructions Payment on Advice request, complete the following fields on the TPISO screen:

  • In Environment B, code 'TPISO' in the Nxt: field
  • Select One Time Access Code from the Application Type drop down box
  • Select ABY Payment on Advice from the Business Function: field
  • The Information Type will pre-populate with KEY load for POA OTAC
  • Use the Date from and Date to field to narrow the search and identify a particular term. This field is not mandatory
  • Narrow the search by selecting processed, unprocessed or store forward in the Status: field
  • Select the Extra Search Components drop down box and select the school/boarding institution state or territory under State: field
  • Enter the organisation Customer Reference Number (CRN) into the Internal Search CRN: field to find a particular school/boarding institution
  • Select Search
  • Look under the Description tab to locate the school/boarding institution
  • If the Status: is processed, Services Australia has received Payment on Advice information from the school/boarding institution. Select the KEY load for POA OTAC to show the responses submitted by the institution, see Step 2 in Table 2
  • If the Status: is store forward, Services Australia is awaiting Payment on Advice information from the school/boarding institution. The organisation has commenced updating records, saved the data but has not submitted the information. Select the Key load for OTAC process to confirm the student had a Payment on Advice request sent to the school/boarding institution. See Step 6 in Table 2
  • If the Status: is unprocessed, Services Australia is awaiting commencement dates for ABSTUDY Away from Home students from the boarding institution. Select the KEY load for POA OTAC to confirm the student had a Payment on Advice request sent to the school/boarding institution. See Step 6 in Table 2
  • If the Status: is processed, Services Australia has received Payment on Advice information from the school/boarding institution. Select the KEY load for POA OTAC to show the responses submitted by the institution. See Step 2 in Table 2

Has the school/boarding institution received their OTAC?

  • Yes, advise the school/boarding institution to log onto the Education Institutions eService to complete the commencement dates for ABSTUDY students, go to Step 5
  • No, to reissue a new OTAC to the school/boarding institution, go to Step 6

Where the issue with the eService cannot be resolved, advise the provider to contact the Centrelink Confirmation eServices (CCeS) helpdesk. Support is available from 8:30 am to 5:00 pm AEST, Monday to Friday excluding public holidays.

Where there is an issue with receiving the OTAC, send an email to the team. Include the details of the errors the school is experiencing. See the ABSTUDY School Term Allowance (STA) entry on the ABSTUDY AIC contact details page.

5

Education Institutions eService (the eService) + Read more ...

The school/boarding institution will use the OTAC to log into the eService.

In the eService, the school/boarding institution will be presented with a 'workflow' that requires the following information to be completed:

  • contact details
  • student commencement status - 'has the student commenced study?'
  • student commencement date - if the student:
    • has commenced, the date of commencement must be provided
    • has not commenced, a date must not be entered

The Education Institutions eService system also enables the school/boarding institution to:

  • add additional students to the Payment on Advice list, and
  • provide comments (if required) for any student

When Payment on Advice information is submitted to Services Australia, the school/boarding institution will be presented with the 'Institution Receipt Screen'. This screen allows the school/boarding institution to print their Payment on Advice responses (commencement date and comments) for their records.

6

Payment on Advice not submitted by a school/boarding institution + Read more ...

If a school/boarding institution has not responded to the Payment on Advice request by C.O.B. of the third Friday of the school term:

  • all ABSTUDY students who require their commencement date to be confirmed by that institution will have their ABSTUDY payments suspended, and
  • the ABSTUDY boarding instalment will not be issued

On the fourth Monday of each school term, these Intention to Board (ITB) students will:

  • be automatically suspended (SUS-ITB) from the start of the term entitlement period, and
  • have a DOC coded on their record

If a school/boarding institution returns Payment on Advice information after the automated suspension occurs:

  • a STArted activity will show on the student record to be actioned by the Smart Centre Processing team
  • these activities are allocated via Customer First Workload Allocation as a high priority and will contain a keyword of POAY and POARES

To restore students suspended as a result of the automated process, for:

  • ABSTUDY, see Restoration of ABSTUDY payments
  • EPA, see Restoration of Pensioner Education Supplement (PES) and ABSTUDY PES

If the automated suspension fails (due to other activities on the record):

  • a STArted activity will show on the student record to be actioned by the Smart Centre Processing team
  • these started activities are allocated via Customer First Workload Allocation as a high priority and will contain a keyword of POASUS and ACTABY

Finalise the suspension activities for all ABSTUDY students by C.O.B. that day. This will ensure the ABSTUDY boarding instalment is not released.

If a suspension activity is unable to be completed, a manual cancellation may be required. Complete the following on the Benefit Action (BA) screen:

  • Svc Rsn field - ABY
  • Action field - CAN
  • Reason field - ITB
  • Effect Date field - the first date of current term entitlement period

Finalise action on the Assessment Results (AR) screen.

Contact each school/boarding institution to:

  • follow up the non-return of the Payment on Advice information, and
  • inform them that non-provision of student commencement dates will result in cessation of payment for all students listed at the school/board location

Procedure ends here.

  • is not known at the school/boarding institution, see Step 1 in Table 3

  • will be boarding at another location for the term, see Step 3 in Table 3
  • will commence studies at the school/boarding institution after the third Friday of term, see Step 6 in Table 3

  • did commence at the school/boarding institution in the term, but has subsequently ceased prior to the issue of the first ABSTUDY boarding instalment payment, see Step 8 in Table 3
  • will not commence at the school/boarding institution, see Step 9 in Table 3

Student will commence on campus study at the school/boarding institution after the third Friday of Term + Read more ...

A student who is only eligible to away from home entitlements from the date the student will commence living away from home in the term if they:

  • have not yet commenced, and
  • the school/boarding institution has advised will commence after the third Friday of term

Students unable to commence on time due to circumstances beyond their control will be eligible to be paid once:

  • Impacts are confirmed, and
  • The student begins study on campus in a face to face environment

Evidence to confirm other impacts can include (but is not limited to) the following:

  • Medical Certificate
  • Statement from independent 3rd party
  • Letter/verbal confirmation from school or board provider
  • relevant state/territory websites confirming natural disaster

For more details see, Calculating the commencement date of away from home rate for ABSTUDY.

Advise the school/boarding institution that payments will not be released until the school/boarding institution has confirmed that the student has actually commenced.

Student commenced at the school/boarding institution, but ceased study prior to the ABSTUDY boarding instalment payment + Read more ...

If a school/boarding institution advises a student did commence at the board location, but has subsequently left the boarding institution:

  • the school/boarding institution is still entitled to have a proportion of the first ABSTUDY boarding instalment payment
